# Creating Care Plans

Produces goal-oriented, actionable care plans.

## Workflow

1. **Assess** current state — active problems, functional status, patient goals
2. **Set** SMART goals — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
3. **Plan** interventions — link each intervention to a goal
4. **Assign** responsibility — who does what (patient, provider, specialist, caregiver)
5. **Schedule** evaluation points — when to assess progress
6. **Anticipate** barriers and contingencies

## Output Template

```markdown
## Care Plan: [Primary Condition]

### Patient Goals (in their words)
- "[What matters to the patient]"

### Problem List (Prioritized)
| Priority | Problem | Status |
|----------|---------|--------|

### Goals & Interventions

#### Goal 1: [SMART Goal]
| Intervention | Responsible | Frequency | Start Date |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| [Action] | [Who] | [How often] | [When] |

**Evaluation**: [Metric] by [Date]
**If Not Met**: [Contingency plan]

### Patient Education
- [Key self-management instructions]

### Follow-Up Schedule
| Date | Provider | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
```

## Key Rules

- Goals must be patient-centered, not just provider-centered
- Every intervention needs a responsible party
- Include contingency plans for goals not met
- Address social determinants if relevant (transportation, cost, support system)
- Chronic disease plans should include self-management components
