# Clinical Risk Score Criteria

## HEART Score (Chest Pain Risk Stratification)

| Criterion | 0 Points | 1 Point | 2 Points |
|-----------|----------|---------|----------|
| **H**istory | Slightly suspicious | Moderately suspicious | Highly suspicious |
| **E**CG | Normal | Non-specific ST changes | Significant ST deviation |
| **A**ge | < 45 | 45-64 | ≥ 65 |
| **R**isk factors | None | 1-2 risk factors | ≥ 3 or h/o atherosclerosis |
| **T**roponin | ≤ normal limit | 1-3x normal | > 3x normal |

**Interpretation**: 0-3 Low risk (1.7% MACE) → Consider discharge; 4-6 Moderate (12%) → Admit, observe; 7-10 High (65%) → Early invasive strategy

## Wells Score — Pulmonary Embolism

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Clinical signs/symptoms of DVT | +3.0 |
| PE is #1 diagnosis or equally likely | +3.0 |
| Heart rate > 100 | +1.5 |
| Immobilization ≥ 3 days or surgery in prior 4 weeks | +1.5 |
| Previous PE or DVT | +1.5 |
| Hemoptysis | +1.0 |
| Malignancy (treatment within 6 months or palliative) | +1.0 |

**Interpretation**: ≤ 4 PE unlikely (check D-dimer); > 4 PE likely (imaging indicated)

## Wells Score — DVT

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Active cancer | +1 |
| Paralysis/paresis or recent cast of lower extremity | +1 |
| Recently bedridden > 3 days or major surgery within 12 weeks | +1 |
| Localized tenderness along deep venous system | +1 |
| Entire leg swollen | +1 |
| Calf swelling > 3 cm compared to asymptomatic side | +1 |
| Pitting edema confined to symptomatic leg | +1 |
| Collateral superficial veins (non-varicose) | +1 |
| Previously documented DVT | +1 |
| Alternative diagnosis equally or more likely | -2 |

**Interpretation**: ≤ 1 Low probability; 2 Moderate; ≥ 3 High probability

## CHA₂DS₂-VASc (Stroke Risk in Atrial Fibrillation)

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| **C**ongestive heart failure | +1 |
| **H**ypertension | +1 |
| **A**ge ≥ 75 | +2 |
| **D**iabetes mellitus | +1 |
| **S**troke/TIA/thromboembolism history | +2 |
| **V**ascular disease (prior MI, PAD, aortic plaque) | +1 |
| **A**ge 65-74 | +1 |
| **S**ex category (female) | +1 |

**Interpretation**: 0 (male) or 1 (female) = Low risk, no anticoagulation; ≥ 2 = Anticoagulation recommended

## HAS-BLED (Bleeding Risk)

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| **H**ypertension (uncontrolled, >160 systolic) | +1 |
| **A**bnormal renal/liver function (1 point each) | +1 or +2 |
| **S**troke history | +1 |
| **B**leeding history or predisposition | +1 |
| **L**abile INR (TTR < 60%) | +1 |
| **E**lderly (> 65) | +1 |
| **D**rugs (antiplatelets, NSAIDs) or alcohol (1 point each) | +1 or +2 |

**Interpretation**: ≥ 3 = High bleeding risk — does NOT contraindicate anticoagulation, but requires closer monitoring

## CURB-65 (Pneumonia Severity)

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| **C**onfusion (new onset) | +1 |
| **U**rea > 7 mmol/L (BUN > 19 mg/dL) | +1 |
| **R**espiratory rate ≥ 30 | +1 |
| **B**lood pressure (systolic < 90 or diastolic ≤ 60) | +1 |
| Age ≥ **65** | +1 |

**Interpretation**: 0-1 Outpatient; 2 Short inpatient/supervised outpatient; 3-5 Inpatient, consider ICU if 4-5

## MELD-Na (Liver Disease Severity)

**Formula**: MELD = 10 × (0.957 × ln(Creatinine) + 0.378 × ln(Bilirubin) + 1.120 × ln(INR) + 0.643)

MELD-Na adjusts for serum sodium: MELD-Na = MELD + 1.32 × (137 − Na) − 0.033 × MELD × (137 − Na)

| MELD Score | 3-Month Mortality |
|-----------|------------------|
| < 10 | 1.9% |
| 10-19 | 6.0% |
| 20-29 | 19.6% |
| 30-39 | 52.6% |
| ≥ 40 | 71.3% |

## Glasgow Coma Scale

| Response | Score Range |
|----------|------------|
| **Eye Opening**: Spontaneous (4), To voice (3), To pain (2), None (1) | 1-4 |
| **Verbal**: Oriented (5), Confused (4), Inappropriate (3), Incomprehensible (2), None (1) | 1-5 |
| **Motor**: Obeys commands (6), Localizes pain (5), Withdrawal (4), Flexion (3), Extension (2), None (1) | 1-6 |

**Total**: 3-15. Severe TBI ≤ 8, Moderate 9-12, Mild 13-15
