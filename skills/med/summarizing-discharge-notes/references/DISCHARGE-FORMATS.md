# Discharge Format Variations

## Standard Hospital Discharge Summary

Most common format. Sections vary by institution but typically include:

| Section | Required | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Patient Demographics | Yes | Name, DOB, MRN, dates of admission/discharge |
| Attending Physician | Yes | Primary responsible provider |
| Principal Diagnosis | Yes | Primary reason for admission |
| Secondary Diagnoses | Yes | Comorbidities, complications |
| Procedures | If applicable | With dates and operators |
| Hospital Course | Yes | Narrative of stay |
| Discharge Medications | Yes | Complete list with changes noted |
| Discharge Condition | Yes | Stable, improved, etc. |
| Discharge Disposition | Yes | Home, SNF, rehab, etc. |
| Follow-Up Instructions | Yes | Appointments, activity, diet |

## VA (Veterans Affairs) Format

VA discharge summaries follow VHA Directive 1907 and typically include:

- **Problem-oriented format** — organized by active problem rather than chronologically
- **Standardized medication reconciliation** — BCMA (Bar Code Medication Administration) format
- **Community care coordination** — referrals to VA community-based outpatient clinics (CBOCs)
- **Mental health screening** — PHQ-2/PHQ-9 results often included regardless of admission reason
- **Service connection** — notes may reference service-connected conditions for disability tracking

Key differences from civilian hospitals:
- Uses VA-specific terminology (e.g., "fee basis" for external care)
- Includes Veteran's preferred pharmacy location
- Follow-up often split between VA and non-VA providers

## Academic Medical Center Format

Typically more detailed, often structured for resident education:

- **Detailed hospital course** — often organized by system (cardiovascular, pulmonary, renal, etc.)
- **Consultant notes referenced** — each consulting service's recommendations summarized
- **Teaching points** may be embedded (remove these from patient summaries)
- **Resident/attending co-signatures** — note the attending as the authoritative source
- **Often longer** — 3-5 pages vs. 1-2 for community hospitals

## Community Hospital Format

Usually more concise:

- **Shorter hospital course** — narrative style, 1-2 paragraphs
- **May combine sections** — assessment and plan in one block
- **Fewer consultants** — often single-provider management
- **Template-driven** — EHR templates with auto-populated fields (verify accuracy of auto-populated data)

## Emergency Department Discharge

Not a traditional discharge summary, but often needs summarization:

- **MDM (Medical Decision Making)** section contains the key reasoning
- **Disposition section** contains discharge instructions
- **Often lacks medication reconciliation** — may only list new prescriptions
- **Return precautions** are critical — extract these explicitly
- **Follow-up typically less specific** — "follow up with PCP in 2-3 days"

## Behavioral Health Discharge

Additional sections beyond standard medical discharge:

- **Safety assessment** at discharge (suicidal/homicidal ideation screening)
- **Legal status** (voluntary vs. involuntary, any holds)
- **Substance use assessment** and treatment plan
- **Aftercare plan** — therapy appointments, support groups, crisis resources
- **Medication changes** — psychiatric medications often adjusted during stay; document titration schedule
