# Writing Prior Authorizations

Builds compelling medical necessity arguments for PA submissions.

## Workflow

1. **Identify** PA requirements — what the payer needs
2. **Document** clinical history — diagnosis, severity, functional impact
3. **Show** step therapy compliance — what was tried and why it failed
4. **Cite** evidence — guidelines, literature supporting the requested treatment
5. **Argue** medical necessity — why this specific treatment, why now
6. **Format** for submission — payer-specific form + letter of medical necessity

## Output Template

```markdown
## Prior Authorization Request

### Patient Information
[Demographics, insurance ID, diagnosis codes]

### Requested Service
[Drug/procedure/service with specific codes]

### Clinical Justification

#### Diagnosis & Severity
[Clinical condition with impact on function/quality of life]

#### Treatment History (Step Therapy)
| Treatment | Dates | Outcome | Reason Discontinued |
|-----------|-------|---------|-------------------|

#### Why This Treatment Is Medically Necessary
1. [Clinical argument]
2. [Guideline support]
3. [Patient-specific factors]

#### Supporting Evidence
- [Guideline citation]
- [Relevant clinical data]

### Peer-to-Peer Contact
[Provider available for peer-to-peer review: name, phone, availability]
```

## Key Rules

- Address payer-specific criteria directly — match their checklist
- "Failed" means inadequate response OR intolerable side effects — document which
- Include objective measures of severity (lab values, functional scores, imaging)
- Cite current clinical guidelines by name and year
- Always offer peer-to-peer review availability
- Keep the letter under 2 pages — concise arguments win
