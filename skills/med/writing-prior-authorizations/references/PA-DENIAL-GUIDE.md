# Prior Authorization Denial Reasons and Appeal Strategies

## Most Common Denial Reasons

| Denial Reason | Frequency | Appeal Strategy |
|--------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Medical necessity not established** | ~40% | Stronger clinical documentation, guideline citations, functional impact data |
| **Step therapy not completed** | ~25% | Document all prior treatments with specific failure reasons and dates |
| **Not a covered benefit** | ~10% | Verify benefit category, check medical vs. pharmacy benefit, request exception |
| **Incorrect coding** | ~10% | Recode with correct diagnosis/procedure codes, resubmit |
| **Missing information** | ~10% | Resubmit with complete documentation promptly |
| **Out of network** | ~5% | Network adequacy argument, continuity of care exception |

## Appeal Levels

| Level | Timeframe | Who Reviews | Success Rate |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Internal Appeal (1st) | 30-60 days | Payer medical director | ~40-50% |
| Internal Appeal (2nd) | 30-60 days | Different payer reviewer | ~20-30% |
| External/Independent Review | Varies by state | Independent reviewer (IRO) | ~40-60% |
| State Insurance Department | Varies | State regulator | Varies |

## Writing Effective Appeal Letters

### Structure
1. **Opening**: State the denial, date, reference number, and that you are appealing
2. **Clinical summary**: Diagnosis, severity, functional impact (1-2 paragraphs)
3. **Step therapy documentation**: What was tried, exact dates, why it failed/was contraindicated
4. **Medical necessity argument**: Why THIS treatment is needed for THIS patient
5. **Evidence**: Guideline citations (name, year, specific recommendation)
6. **Conclusion**: Request for approval, peer-to-peer availability

### Effective Phrases
- "Clinical guidelines from [Organization, Year] recommend [treatment] for patients who [match this patient's profile]"
- "Patient has failed [X] previous treatments over [Y] months, specifically: [list with dates and outcomes]"
- "Objective measures demonstrate severity: [specific values — lab results, functional scores, imaging findings]"
- "Without this treatment, the patient faces [specific clinical consequence]"

### Phrases to Avoid
- "The patient really needs this" (subjective, not clinical)
- "I've been treating patients for 20 years" (credential argument, not evidence)
- "This is the only option" (usually untrue; state why alternatives are inadequate)

## Urgent/Expedited PA

Request expedited review when:
- Standard timeline would seriously jeopardize life, health, or ability to regain maximum function
- Document urgency with specific clinical indicators
- Most payers must respond to urgent PAs within 72 hours (some states: 24 hours)

## Peer-to-Peer Review Tips

- Prepare a 2-minute summary before the call
- Have the chart, guidelines, and denial letter in front of you
- Ask the reviewer to state their specific clinical concern
- Address their concern directly with evidence
- If denied verbally, still request written denial to preserve appeal rights
- Document the call: date, time, reviewer name, what was discussed
