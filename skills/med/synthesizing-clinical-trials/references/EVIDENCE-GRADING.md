# Evidence Grading Reference

## GRADE Framework (Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development and Evaluation)

The standard for rating quality of evidence in clinical guidelines.

### Evidence Quality Levels

| Level | Definition | Starts As |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **High** | Very confident the true effect is close to the estimate | RCTs |
| **Moderate** | Moderately confident; true effect likely close to estimate | — |
| **Low** | Limited confidence; true effect may differ substantially | Observational studies |
| **Very Low** | Very little confidence in the estimate | Case reports, expert opinion |

### Factors That Lower Quality

| Factor | Description | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Risk of bias | Methodological flaws | Unblinded study, high dropout |
| Inconsistency | Unexplained heterogeneity across studies | Effect varies widely between trials |
| Indirectness | Population/intervention/outcome mismatch | Studied in adults, applying to children |
| Imprecision | Wide confidence intervals | CI crosses clinically meaningful thresholds |
| Publication bias | Selective reporting | Funnel plot asymmetry, missing small negative trials |

### Factors That Raise Quality

| Factor | Description | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Large effect | Large magnitude of effect | RR > 2 or < 0.5 with no confounders |
| Dose-response | Gradient present | Higher dose → larger effect |
| Confounders | All plausible confounders would reduce effect | Effect persists despite biases against it |

## Jadad Score (Oxford Quality Scoring)

Simple 0-5 scale for RCT quality.

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Randomization described | +1 |
| Randomization method appropriate | +1 |
| Double-blinding described | +1 |
| Double-blinding method appropriate | +1 |
| Withdrawals/dropouts described | +1 |
| Randomization method inappropriate | -1 |
| Blinding method inappropriate | -1 |

**Score interpretation**: 0-2 = Low quality, 3-5 = High quality

## Cochrane Risk of Bias (RoB 2)

Seven domains assessed as Low / Some concerns / High risk:

1. Bias from randomization process
2. Bias due to deviations from intended interventions
3. Bias from missing outcome data
4. Bias in measurement of the outcome
5. Bias in selection of the reported result
6. Overall bias assessment
7. (Additional domain for cluster-randomized trials)

## Recommendation Strength

| Strength | Meaning | Phrasing |
|----------|---------|----------|
| **Strong** | Benefits clearly outweigh risks | "We recommend..." |
| **Conditional/Weak** | Benefits probably outweigh risks, but less certain | "We suggest..." |

A strong recommendation with low-quality evidence means: "We're sure this is the right thing to do, but the evidence base is weak."
A weak recommendation with high-quality evidence means: "The evidence is good, but the decision depends on patient values/context."
