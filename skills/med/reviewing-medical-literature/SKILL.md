# Reviewing Medical Literature

Systematic approach to clinical evidence review.

## Workflow

1. **Frame** clinical question using PICO
2. **Strategize** search — databases, MeSH terms, filters
3. **Screen** results — inclusion/exclusion criteria
4. **Appraise** critically — study design, bias, validity
5. **Synthesize** findings — summarize evidence with quality assessment
6. **Apply** — clinical bottom line with evidence strength

## Output Template

```markdown
## Literature Review: [Clinical Question]

### PICO
- **P** (Population): [Who]
- **I** (Intervention): [What treatment/exposure]
- **C** (Comparison): [Against what]
- **O** (Outcome): [What is measured]

### Search Strategy
- **Databases**: [PubMed, Cochrane, EMBASE, etc.]
- **Terms**: [Key search terms and MeSH headings]
- **Filters**: [Date range, study type, language]
- **Results**: [X articles identified → Y included after screening]

### Evidence Summary
| Study | Design | N | Key Finding | Quality |
|-------|--------|---|-------------|---------|

### Critical Appraisal
[Strengths and limitations of the evidence base]

### Evidence Grade
[GRADE: High / Moderate / Low / Very Low]

### Clinical Bottom Line
[Actionable summary for clinical decision-making]
```

## Key Rules

- Always start with PICO — vague questions produce vague answers
- Systematic reviews and RCTs > observational studies > expert opinion
- Report both positive and negative findings
- Note publication bias risk
- Distinguish between absence of evidence and evidence of absence
- Date-limit searches only when there's a reason to (e.g., new drug approval date)
