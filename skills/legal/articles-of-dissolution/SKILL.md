# Articles of Dissolution

Drafts a state-compliant Articles of Dissolution (or Certificate of Dissolution) ready for filing with the applicable Secretary of State to legally terminate a corporation's existence.

## Prerequisites

1. **Articles of Incorporation** — exact legal name, incorporation date, state file/ID number
2. **Bylaws** — voting thresholds, notice requirements, officer authority
3. **Board authorization** — meeting minutes or written consent with date, quorum confirmation, vote count
4. **Shareholder approval** — meeting minutes or written consent with date, shares outstanding, shares voting for/against
5. **State of incorporation** — to apply correct statutory authority and mandatory disclosures
6. **Intended effective date** — upon filing or a specified future date

## Output Structure

### 1. Document Header
- Title: "Articles of Dissolution" or "Certificate of Dissolution" per state convention
- Statutory citation: cite the state's General Corporation Law or Business Corporation Act section governing voluntary dissolution [VERIFY section number for jurisdiction]

### 2. Corporate Identification

| Field | Source |
|---|---|
| Exact legal name | State records / Articles of Incorporation |
| Date of incorporation | Articles of Incorporation |
| State file / corporate ID number | Secretary of State records |
| Any DBAs or assumed names | Corporate records |

### 3. Board Authorization
- Date of board meeting or written consent
- Confirmation of proper notice or waiver
- Vote count (quorum confirmed; resolution adopted)
- Reference to specific board resolution authorizing dissolution

### 4. Shareholder Approval

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Approval method | Shareholder meeting or written consent in lieu |
| Date of meeting / consent | From minutes or consent document |
| Shares outstanding (entitled to vote) | From stock ledger |
| Shares voted in favor | From minutes or consent |
| Shares voted against / abstaining | From minutes or consent |
| Required threshold met | Confirm per state law + governing docs |

> Note: Required threshold varies by state—commonly a majority or two-thirds of outstanding shares; confirm against state statute and the corporation's articles. [VERIFY]

### 5. Effective Date
- **Upon filing**: State that dissolution is effective upon filing and acceptance by the Secretary of State.
- **Delayed effective date**: Specify exact future date; confirm it falls within any statutory maximum delay (commonly up to 90 days). [VERIFY by state]

### 6. State-Specific Mandatory Disclosures

Include whichever of the following the filing jurisdiction requires:

- [ ] Statement that all debts, obligations, and liabilities have been paid or adequately provided for
- [ ] Statement that remaining assets have been or will be distributed to shareholders per their rights
- [ ] Designation of agent for service of process during wind-up period (name + address)
- [ ] Tax clearance certificate obtained (some states require prior to filing) [VERIFY]
- [ ] Confirmation no known claims exist or describe claims-handling procedure

### 7. Execution Block

```
IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the undersigned officer of [Corporation Name], duly authorized
by resolution of the Board of Directors, executes these Articles of Dissolution as
of [Date].

[Corporation Name]

By: ___________________________
    [Printed Name]
    [Title: President / Secretary / Authorized Officer]
```

- If state requires **verification under penalty of perjury**, add: *"I declare under penalty of perjury that the foregoing is true and correct."*
- If state requires **notarization**, append standard notarial acknowledgment block for the filing jurisdiction.

## Guidelines

- **Name must match exactly**: Any discrepancy from the Secretary of State's records causes rejection; pull the name directly from official state records.
- **Governing law varies materially**: Delaware, California, Nevada, and other states have distinct procedures, forms, and prerequisites—always confirm current statutory requirements before finalizing. [VERIFY]
- **Tax clearance**: Some states (e.g., California, New Jersey) require tax clearance before dissolution is accepted; confirm with the state revenue agency timeline. [VERIFY]
- **Wind-up period**: Dissolution does not extinguish pre-existing liabilities; note that the corporation continues to exist for wind-up purposes under most state statutes. [VERIFY]
- **Written consent vs. meeting**: If shareholder written consent was used, confirm it was executed by holders of the requisite percentage and that notice of the consent was provided to non-consenting shareholders as required by state law. [VERIFY]
- Do not include substantive legal advice on tax consequences, creditor rights, or successor liability—flag those issues for separate counsel review.
