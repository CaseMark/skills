# Pour-Over Will

Drafts a pour-over will that transfers the testator's residuary estate to an existing revocable living trust, ensuring unified distribution for assets not titled in the trust at death.

## Prerequisites

1. **Testator information** — full legal name (as on ID), city/state of domicile
2. **Trust details** — exact legal name of trust, date of trust agreement, settlor/grantor name
3. **Fiduciary nominees** — Personal Representative (executor) + successors, with full names and relationships
4. **Guardian nominees** (if minor children) — guardian of person and/or estate, with names, addresses, relationships
5. **Specific bequests** (if any) — property descriptions, beneficiary names
6. **Applicable state law** — execution requirements, number of witnesses, self-proving affidavit statute, pour-over statute

## Output Structure

### Article I — Declaration

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Testamentary intent | Explicit "Last Will and Testament" declaration |
| Revocation | Revoke all prior wills and codicils |
| Domicile | City and state of residence |
| Capacity | Sound mind, legal age, voluntary, no undue influence |

### Article II — Fiduciary Appointments

| Role | Include |
|------|---------|
| Personal Representative | Full name, relationship; successor(s) if primary unable/unwilling |
| Guardian of Person | Full name, address, relationship (minor children only) |
| Guardian of Estate | Same or separate individual; state reasoning if split |
| Powers | Broad administration without court supervision (if state permits) |
| Bond | Waiver provision for all nominated fiduciaries |

### Article III — Payment of Debts and Expenses

Checklist for coverage:

- [ ] Legally enforceable debts
- [ ] Funeral and burial expenses
- [ ] Costs of administration
- [ ] Estate taxes — specify: apportioned among beneficiaries OR paid from residuary
- [ ] Secured debts — specify: beneficiary takes subject to encumbrance OR paid off
- [ ] Priority of payments
- [ ] PR discretion to contest or compromise claims

### Article IV — Specific Bequests and Tangible Personal Property

- Draft specific bequests before residuary pour-over if testator desires
- Reference separate written statement/list if permitted under state law (UPC § 2-513 or equivalent) [VERIFY]
- Address:
  - **Ademption** — property no longer in estate at death
  - **Lapse** — beneficiary predeceases testator
  - **Anti-lapse statute** applicability

### Article V — Pour-Over of Residuary Estate

Core clause must include:

```
I give, devise, and bequeath all the rest, residue, and remainder of my estate,
whether real, personal, or mixed, wherever situated, to the then-acting Trustee
of the [TRUST NAME], dated [TRUST DATE], established by [SETTLOR NAME],
as amended from time to time, to be held, administered, and distributed
in accordance with the terms of said trust and not as a testamentary trust.
```

| Issue | Required Language |
|-------|-------------------|
| Trust amendments | "as amended from time to time" — post-execution amendments recognized |
| Trust revocation/invalidity | Contingent residuary beneficiary or alternative distribution scheme |
| Statutory compliance | Conform to Uniform Testamentary Additions to Trusts Act (UTATA) or state equivalent [VERIFY] |
| Trust existence | Confirm trust in existence at will execution (required in most jurisdictions) |

### Article VI — Execution and Attestation

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Witnesses | Minimum per state law (typically 2; 3 in some states) |
| Attestation clause | Witnesses confirm: (1) observed signing, (2) testator declared it as will, (3) appeared of sound mind, (4) no constraint/undue influence, (5) signed in presence of testator and each other |
| Self-proving affidavit | Include if state permits; requires notarization; cite applicable statute |
| Signature lines | Testator + all witnesses: printed name, address, date, location |
| Page numbering | "Page ___ of ___" on each page |

## Guidelines

- **Domicile controls**: State of domicile governs validity and interpretation — verify all provisions against that state's probate code
- **"As amended" language is critical**: Without it, only the original trust terms apply; most states honor this under UTATA
- **Pour-over assets pass through probate**: Unlike trust-funded assets — advise client this is a safety net, not a probate-avoidance tool
- **No substantive trust terms in the will**: Distribution is governed by the trust instrument exclusively
- **Tangible property lists**: If referencing a separate list, confirm state permits incorporation by reference for items described with reasonable certainty
- **Tax apportionment**: Default state rules vary significantly — always specify intent explicitly
- **Format**: Professional headings, numbered articles/sections, clear signature blocks; ready for attorney review and client execution
- **Placeholders**: Use `[BRACKETED CAPS]` for all client-specific information to be filled in
