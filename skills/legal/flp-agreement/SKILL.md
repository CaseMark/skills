# Family Limited Partnership (FLP) Agreement

Drafts a legally enforceable FLP agreement that withstands IRS scrutiny under IRC §2036 while achieving wealth consolidation, intergenerational transfer with valuation discounts, and senior-generation control.

## Prerequisites

1. **Governing documents** — existing estate plans, trusts, prior partnership agreements
2. **Asset inventory** — legal descriptions, appraisals, encumbrances, tax basis for all contributed property
3. **Family details** — full legal names, addresses, relationships, ages of all partners
4. **Valuation reports** — qualified appraisals meeting IRS substantiation requirements
5. **State of formation** — chosen jurisdiction's Revised Uniform Limited Partnership Act provisions

## Pre-Draft: Document Extraction

Before drafting, search uploaded documents for:
- Client names, addresses, family relationships
- Asset descriptions, legal property descriptions, valuations
- Existing trust structures, custodial arrangements
- Mortgage/encumbrance details, tax basis information
- Prior estate planning documents

## Output Structure

### Article I — Formation & Purpose

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Entity name | Must comply with state naming requirements, identify as FLP |
| State of formation | Specify RULPA or equivalent statute |
| Principal office | Full street address |
| Registered agent | Name and address |
| Term | Perpetual or specified (50–99 years typical) |

**Purpose clause** must articulate legitimate business objectives beyond tax avoidance:
- Consolidated professional asset management
- Multi-generational wealth preservation
- Orderly business succession
- Asset protection from creditor claims
- Family unity through shared investment objectives

Enumerate authorized asset classes (real estate, securities, closely-held interests, mineral rights, IP, etc.) based on client's actual portfolio.

### Article II — Partners & Interests

| Role | Typical Interest | Rights |
|---|---|---|
| General Partner(s) | 1–2% | Full management authority |
| Limited Partner(s) | 98–99% | Economic rights only, no management control |

For each partner specify: full legal name, mailing address, percentage interest, class (GP/LP).

For minors/beneficiaries, specify holding mechanism:
- Direct ownership
- UTMA custodial account (identify custodian)
- Trust (identify trust name, trustee, date)

### Article III — Capital Contributions

For each contributed asset, document:

| Asset Type | Required Details |
|---|---|
| Real property | Legal description, appraised FMV, encumbrances, valuation method |
| Marketable securities | Shares/units, issuer name, valuation method, trading status |
| Closely-held interests | Entity name, ownership percentage, valuation method, transfer restrictions |
| Tangible personal property | Itemized description, qualified appraisal |
| All assets | Contributing partner's tax basis, assumed liabilities |

**Capital accounts** — maintain per Treas. Reg. §1.704-1(b)(2)(iv):
- Increased by: cash contributions, FMV of property contributions, allocated income/gain
- Decreased by: cash distributions, FMV of property distributions, allocated loss/deduction

Include provisions for:
- Additional contributions (voluntary/mandatory)
- Consequences of failure to contribute (dilution)
- No right to demand return of capital or withdraw except as specified

### Article IV — Management & Fiduciary Framework

**General Partner powers** (sole and absolute discretion):
- Acquire, manage, improve, dispose of partnership property
- Enter contracts, borrow funds, pledge assets
- Hire employees and professional advisors
- Make investment/reinvestment decisions
- Determine distribution policy and timing
- File tax returns, make tax elections
- Maintain insurance

**Fiduciary duties:**
- Duty of loyalty, duty of care, good faith and fair dealing
- State any permitted modifications under applicable statute
- Note: modifications cannot be "manifestly unreasonable"

**Exculpation:** No liability for good-faith business judgments; liable only for willful misconduct, bad faith, knowing legal violations, or gross negligence.

**Indemnification:** Partnership indemnifies GPs except for willful misconduct, bad faith, or gross negligence. Include expense advancement and D&O insurance authority.

**Limited partner safe harbors** — LP may without losing limited liability:
- Serve as officer/employee of partnership
- Consult with and advise GPs
- Attend meetings, vote on specified matters
- Engage in competitive business activities

**Multi-GP governance** (if applicable):
- Voting thresholds (unanimous, majority, supermajority by category)
- Meeting/notice/quorum requirements, written consent procedures
- Deadlock resolution (mediation → arbitration → buy-sell)
- GP removal (supermajority LP vote), successor designation

### Article V — Tax Allocations & Distributions

**Allocations** per IRC §704(b) and Treas. Reg. §1.704-1(b):
- Pro rata by percentage interest (or special allocations if applicable)
- Must have substantial economic effect
- Required regulatory provisions:
  - Minimum gain chargeback
  - Qualified income offset
  - Gross income allocation for deficit capital accounts

**Distributions** — at GP sole and absolute discretion considering:
- Cash flow, liquidity, working capital needs
- Debt service, loan covenants
- Capital expenditures, operating expenses
- Tax liability distributions
- Long-term investment objectives

**Tax distributions:**
- Quarterly/annually based on allocated taxable income × highest marginal rate (federal + state)
- Treated as advances against future distributions
- Subject to available cash flow limitations

**Tax matters partner / Partnership representative:**
- Designate under IRC §6231 / BBA centralized audit regime
- Authority: represent in audits, make elections, extend SOL, settle
- Accounting method, depreciation methods, fiscal year (typically calendar)
- Annual Form 1065, Schedule K-1 delivery, financial statements

### Article VI — Transfer Restrictions

**General restriction:** No transfer (sale, gift, assignment, pledge, involuntary) without prior written GP consent, withholdable in sole and absolute discretion. Violation renders transfer null and void.

**Right of first refusal procedure:**
1. Partner receives bona fide third-party offer → written notice to partnership + all partners
2. Partnership: 30 days to purchase at same price/terms
3. If declined: other partners pro rata, additional 30 days
4. If all decline: partner may complete third-party sale within specified period; otherwise restart

**Buy-sell triggers:** Death, divorce, bankruptcy, insolvency, unauthorized transfer, loss of LP status through management participation.

**Valuation methodology:**
- Independent appraisal (specify appraiser selection: each party selects one; if >X% divergence, two appraisers select third)
- Appraiser qualifications: accredited, experienced in partnership valuations
- Standard: FMV (willing buyer/willing seller, no compulsion, reasonable knowledge)
- Address applicability of minority interest and lack-of-marketability discounts
- Costs allocation, binding effect, dispute resolution

**Permitted transfers** (still require GP consent):
- Gifts to family members (spouses, lineal descendants/ancestors, siblings)
- Transfers to trusts for family benefit
- Transfers per QDRO in divorce
- Testamentary transfers to family members
- "Family member" defined consistent with IRC §2704

Transferees must execute joinder agreement and take subject to all restrictions.

**Charging order provisions:**
- Creditor obtains only right to receive declared distributions
- No management rights, inspection rights, voting rights, or liquidation rights
- Partnership may treat creditor as assignee for tax purposes (phantom income exposure)

### Article VII — Dissolution & Continuation

**Dissolution triggers:**
- Written consent of all GPs
- Supermajority consent (75–80% of all interests)
- Sale of substantially all assets
- Judicial decree

Death/withdrawal/bankruptcy of GP does **not** cause dissolution if successor GP designated or appointed by LPs.

**Winding up priority:**
1. Partnership creditors (including partner-creditors for loans)
2. Return of capital per positive capital account balances
3. Remaining assets per percentage interests or positive capital accounts

**Continuation election:** Partners holding requisite interest may elect to continue rather than liquidate. Specify voting threshold, successor GP appointment, withdrawal valuation for non-continuing partners.

**Withdrawal restrictions:** Prohibit or heavily restrict (GP consent required, year-end only, lengthy notice period) to support valuation discounts.

### Article VIII — Administrative & Miscellaneous

**Records maintained at principal office:**
- Current partner list with addresses and interests
- Certificate of LP and amendments
- Partnership agreement and amendments
- Financial statements (current + 3 years)
- Tax returns (current + 6 years)

**Inspection rights:** Partners may inspect during business hours with reasonable notice for partnership-related purposes. GPs may restrict access to confidential/privileged information.

**Amendment:**
- Material adverse changes to LP economic rights → affected LP or supermajority consent
- Administrative amendments → GP only, no LP consent required
- Cure ambiguities / comply with law → GP only

**Meetings:** Called by GPs or upon written request of 25–33% of LP interests. Include notice requirements, quorum (majority of interests), written consent procedures, remote participation.

**Dispute resolution:** Negotiation → mediation (AAA or agreed mediator) → binding arbitration (AAA Commercial Rules). Specify location, cost allocation, binding nature.

**Standard provisions:**
- Governing law (formation state, no conflict-of-law)
- Severability, entire agreement, waiver (written only)
- Notice provisions (addresses, methods: personal delivery / certified mail / email with confirmation)
- Counterparts, binding on heirs/successors/permitted assigns

**Tax qualification statement:** Partnership intended to qualify as FLP for federal estate/gift tax purposes; interpret consistently; partnership has economic substance and legitimate business purpose beyond tax avoidance.

### Execution & Exhibits

**Signature blocks** for each GP and LP:
- Individual: name, date, acknowledgment of reading/understanding, opportunity to consult advisors
- Entity (trust/LLC/corp): authorized representative, title, authority statement

**Notarization** if required by state law (filing with secretary of state or recording in real property records).

**Required exhibits:**

## Guidelines

1. **IRS §2036 compliance is paramount** — every provision must support economic substance and legitimate business purpose; avoid structures that appear as mere paper restructurings
2. **Document actual asset transfers** — ensure deeds recorded, stock ledgers updated, bank accounts established in partnership name, EIN obtained
3. **Ancillary filings required:** Form 709 (gift tax) with full disclosure for gifted interests; qualified appraisals for contributed property and gifted interests
4. **Ongoing substance requirements:** separate bank account, annual returns, regular investment activity, partner meetings with minutes, actual distributions
5. **State law variations:** Verify formation state's RULPA provisions for fiduciary duty modification limits, charging order exclusivity, and safe harbor activities
6. **Securities compliance:** Partnership interests are unregistered securities — include appropriate legends and transfer restrictions referencing Securities Act of 1933
7. **Never guarantee tax outcomes** — present discount strategies as subject to IRS challenge; recommend client obtain independent tax counsel opinion letter
8. **Flag gaps** — if critical client information (names, assets, valuations) is unavailable, mark with `[INSERT]` and note in a summary of missing items
