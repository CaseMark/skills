# Volunteer Waiver and Release of Liability Agreement

Drafts a litigation-tested volunteer waiver that balances maximum liability protection with enforceability under state law.

## Prerequisites

1. **Organization info** — full legal name, corporate designation (e.g., "California nonprofit public benefit corporation"), principal address
2. **Activity description** — specific volunteer activities, locations (fixed/variable/outdoor), and program duration
3. **Governing state** — determines enforceability scope, conspicuousness requirements, and minor release rules
4. **Minor participation** — whether volunteers under 18 will be accepted
5. **Existing policies** — prior incident reports, volunteer handbooks, or activity-specific safety protocols

## Output Structure

### 1. Header & Party Identification
- Document title in bold caps: **VOLUNTEER WAIVER AND RELEASE OF LIABILITY AGREEMENT**
- Organization: full legal name + corporate designation + principal address
- Volunteer fields: full legal name, address, phone, email

### 2. Covered Activities & Scope

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Activity types | Enumerate specifically (event setup, outdoor conservation, construction, animal care, client services, administrative, etc.) |
| Locations | Fixed address, multi-site region, outdoor/wilderness, or variable |
| Temporal scope | One-time event / program period / ongoing (specify term) |
| Extended coverage | Training, cleanup, org-sponsored volunteer events, org-provided transport |

### 3. Risk Disclosures

Include specific categories matched to actual activities:

- **Physical work**: lifting injuries, tool/equipment cuts, slips/trips/falls, overexertion, repetitive strain
- **Outdoor**: heat exhaustion, hypothermia, lightning, wildlife/insects/plants, uneven terrain, water hazards
- **Client-facing**: communicable disease exposure, agitated individuals, psychological stress from vulnerable populations
- **Premises**: structural defects, inadequate lighting/security, third-party conduct
- **Catch-all**: "risks are not exhaustive; injuries ranging from minor to catastrophic, including permanent disability or death, are possible"

### 4. Assumption of Risk

Volunteer must affirmatively represent:
- Participation is voluntary and uncoerced; may withdraw at any time
- Had opportunity to ask questions and received satisfactory answers
- Physically and mentally capable; no undisclosed medical conditions creating undue risk
- Accepts personal responsibility for fitness to participate

### 5. Release of Liability

**Released parties**: Organization (by legal name) + officers, directors, trustees, employees, agents, contractors, other volunteers, successors, assigns, affiliates — in official and individual capacities.

**Released claims**:
- Personal injury, bodily injury, illness, disease, death
- Property damage, loss, theft
- Emotional distress, psychological injury
- Economic and consequential losses
- Legal theories: negligence, breach of contract/warranty, strict liability, premises liability

**Scope language**: "whether arising from the negligence of the Released Parties or otherwise, to the fullest extent permitted by law" + "whether now known or unknown, arising in the past, present, or future."

**Enforceability guardrails** [VERIFY per governing state]:
- Release extends to ordinary negligence only
- Do NOT draft release for gross negligence, recklessness, willful misconduct, intentional torts, statutory rights, or fraud — may void entire agreement in some jurisdictions
- Confirm state conspicuousness requirements (font size, placement, formatting)

**Explicit acknowledgment block** (bold/boxed above signature):
> "I understand that by signing this Agreement, I am giving up my right to sue the Organization and the Released Parties for injuries or damages I may suffer, even if caused by their negligence."

### 6. Indemnification

Volunteer agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless all Released Parties from claims arising from:
- Volunteer's participation in activities
- Volunteer's breach of this agreement
- Volunteer's negligent, reckless, or intentional acts/omissions
- Third-party claims related to volunteer's conduct

Includes attorneys' fees and court costs. Note: limited practical enforceability against volunteers but deters frivolous claims.

### 7. Medical Authorization

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Emergency contacts | Primary and alternate (name, relationship, phone) |
| Medical disclosures | Conditions, allergies, current medications, physical limitations |
| Treatment authorization | First aid, EMS, ambulance, hospital care |
| Financial responsibility | Volunteer solely responsible for all medical costs |
| Information sharing | Authorizes disclosure to emergency responders and treating physicians |
| Release | Organization released from liability for good-faith emergency decisions |

### 8. Standard Legal Provisions

- **Governing law**: State where org is principally located or activities primarily occur
- **Venue**: Exclusive jurisdiction in designated county/state courts
- **Severability**: Invalid provisions modified minimally; remainder survives
- **Integration**: Entire agreement; modifications require signed writing
- **Jury waiver**: Consider if permissible under governing state law [VERIFY]
- **Counsel opportunity**: Volunteer acknowledges right to consult attorney before signing

### 9. Minor Volunteer Provisions (if applicable)

Separate section: **"Parent/Guardian Consent and Release for Minor Volunteers"**

- Parent/guardian affirms legal authority to consent and waive minor's rights
- Contains identical risk disclosures, assumption of risk, release, and indemnification
- Parent/guardian signature + printed name + date + relationship to minor

> ⚠️ **[VERIFY]** Parental releases on behalf of minors are enforceable in some states and void as against public policy in others. Even where release is unenforceable, document still establishes informed consent and assumption of risk.

### 10. Signature Block

```
Printed Name: _______________________________

Signature: __________________________________  Date: ____________

[ ] I have read and understand this Agreement
[ ] I understand I am giving up the right to sue
[ ] I am signing voluntarily and without inducement
[ ] I have received a copy of this signed Agreement
```

Consider witness signature line or notarization for high-risk activities or strict-scrutiny jurisdictions.

## Guidelines

- **Conspicuousness**: Bold, capitalize, or box the release clause and key acknowledgments; courts may invalidate releases buried in dense text
- **No gross negligence release**: Limit release to ordinary negligence; overbroad releases can void the entire agreement
- **State-specific versions**: If multi-state operations, draft to most restrictive state or create jurisdiction-specific versions
- **Non-waivable claims**: Never attempt to release civil rights violations, fraud, or intentional torts
- **Periodic review**: Flag document for annual legal review as state law on volunteer waivers evolves
- **Minors**: Always consult governing state law before relying on parental release to bar a minor's claim
