# Area Development Agreement

Drafts a franchise area development agreement granting exclusive territorial rights for phased multi-unit development, balancing brand control with developer protections.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party information** — full legal names, entity types, states of organization, principal addresses, parent company relationships
2. **Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD)** — current Item 22 (development agreement terms), Item 5 (fees), Item 12 (territory)
3. **Territory definition** — county lines, zip codes, MSA boundaries, or metes-and-bounds description; maps if available
4. **Development schedule** — number of units, phased opening deadlines, total term
5. **Fee structure** — area development fee, per-unit franchise fee, royalty rates, marketing fund contributions, credit/offset terms
6. **Existing franchise agreement form** — the unit-level FА that each opened location will execute
7. **Term sheet or LOI** — any pre-negotiated terms, special conditions, or side letters

## Output Structure

### Document Architecture

| Section | Key Contents |
|---|---|
| **Recitals** | System description, developer qualifications, FDD acknowledgment, mutual intent |
| **Definitions** | Territory, Development Schedule, Unit, Gross Revenues, Confidential Information, Marks, System Standards, Commencement Date |
| **Territorial Grant** | Exclusivity scope, reserved rights (company-owned, e-commerce, national accounts), exclusivity conditions |
| **Development Schedule** | Exhibit cross-ref, per-phase unit counts + deadlines, cumulative requirements |
| **Default & Remedies** | Graduated consequences for missed milestones, cure periods, force majeure extensions |
| **Financial Terms** | Development fee, per-unit franchise fee (with multi-unit credit), royalties, marketing fund, payment mechanics, late fees, audit rights |
| **Franchisor Obligations** | Training, operations manual, site approval (15–30 day response), marketing support, field consulting, vendor programs |
| **Developer Obligations** | Site selection criteria, training completion, operational compliance, insurance, record-keeping |
| **Representations & Warranties** | Authority, financial capacity, FDD receipt, no conflicting obligations, mark ownership |
| **Confidentiality** | Broad definition, survival post-term, security measures, permitted disclosures |
| **Non-Compete / Non-Solicitation** | In-term + post-term (1–3 years), geographic limits, competing business definition, injunctive relief authorization |
| **Term & Renewal** | Initial term aligned to schedule (3–10 years), renewal conditions, notice periods |
| **Termination** | Curable vs. non-curable defaults, cure periods (30–60 days), post-termination obligations |
| **Dispute Resolution** | Governing law, mediation-first, arbitration (AAA Commercial Rules), injunctive relief carve-outs |
| **Administrative** | Integration, amendment, severability, notices, independent contractor, assignment, force majeure, indemnification |
| **Exhibits** | A: Development Schedule; B: Territory Description/Map; C: Form Franchise Agreement; D: Guaranty (if applicable) |

### Definitions Drafting Checklist

- [ ] **Territory** — use objective boundaries (county, zip, MSA, streets/highways); never vague ("greater metro area")
- [ ] **Development Schedule** — reference Exhibit A; define what constitutes "opened" (grand opening after full system compliance, not soft open)
- [ ] **Gross Revenues** — all revenue streams; exclude only sales tax collected/remitted; specify treatment of delivery fees, catering, gift cards
- [ ] **Confidential Information** — ops manual, methods, formulas, customer data, supplier terms, financial data, marketing strategies

### Territorial Grant Framework

```
Grant: Exclusive right to develop [X] units within Territory per Development Schedule
Reserved rights (negotiate):
  □ Franchisor company-owned units
  □ Alternative distribution channels (e-commerce, grocery, wholesale)
  □ National/institutional accounts serviced in Territory
  □ Franchisor acquisition of competing systems with existing Territory locations

Exclusivity conditions:
  □ Tied to Development Schedule compliance
  □ Loss of exclusivity for undeveloped sub-areas on milestone default
  □ ROFR for adjacent territories (if negotiated)

Clarification: Development rights ≠ operating rights; each unit requires separate FA execution
```

### Default & Remedy Escalation

| Trigger | Remedy | Cure Period |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| Single milestone missed | Written notice + cure opportunity | 60–90 days |
| Repeated milestone failures | Loss of exclusivity for undeveloped sub-territory | 30 days notice |
| Persistent non-compliance | Territory reduction | 30 days |
| Material/incurable default | Termination | Immediate |

**Force majeure extensions**: Require prompt written notice + documentation; cover acts of God, pandemic, government permitting delays, war/terrorism.

### Financial Terms Structure

| Fee Type | Typical Structure | Notes |
|----------|-------------------|-------|
| Development fee | Lump sum or milestone installments | Non-refundable; specify credit vs. separate consideration |
| Per-unit franchise fee | Standard fee minus multi-unit discount | Payable at each FA execution |
| Royalties | [X]% of Gross Revenues | Weekly/monthly; specify EFT requirement |
| Marketing fund | [X]% of Gross Revenues | National + local; reporting obligations |
| Late fees | [X]% + interest at lesser of [X]% or max legal rate | Accrues from due date |
| Audit rights | Franchisor may audit books on [X] days notice | Developer pays audit costs if underreporting >2% |

### Termination Framework

**Non-curable (immediate termination):**
- Bankruptcy/insolvency
- Criminal conviction of developer/principals
- Abandonment of development efforts
- Material misrepresentation in obtaining agreement
- Repeated defaults after prior cure opportunities

**Curable defaults (30–60 day cure):**
- Single missed development deadline
- Operational standards breach
- Insurance lapse
- Reporting/payment delinquency

**Post-termination obligations:**
- [ ] Immediate loss of territorial development rights
- [ ] Existing unit FAs continue under their own terms
- [ ] De-identification of partially developed sites
- [ ] Return of all confidential materials
- [ ] Non-compete and confidentiality survive per their terms
- [ ] Cooperation on transition of in-progress development

### Dispute Resolution Sequence

1. **Mandatory mediation** — good faith, costs shared equally, 30–60 day window
2. **Arbitration** — AAA Commercial Rules; 1 arbitrator (< $500K), 3 arbitrators (≥ $500K); seat at franchisor HQ
3. **Carve-outs for injunctive relief** — confidentiality breach, non-compete violation, mark infringement, IP misappropriation

## Guidelines

- **FTC Franchise Rule**: All fees must match FDD Items 5 and 22; development agreement terms must be disclosed in Item 22
- **State registration**: Verify franchise registration status in registration states (CA, HI, IL, IN, MD, MI, MN, NY, ND, RI, SD, VA, WA, WI) before execution [VERIFY]
- **State relationship laws**: CA, HI, IL, IN, IA, MI, MN, MS, NE, NJ, WA, WI impose restrictions on termination, non-renewal, and transfer that override contractual terms [VERIFY]
- **Non-compete enforceability**: Varies dramatically by state; CA broadly prohibits post-term non-competes; tailor geographic scope and duration to governing law
- **Forum selection / arbitration limits**: Some state franchise laws (CA, IL, MN, WA) void out-of-state forum clauses or mandatory arbitration for in-state franchisees [VERIFY]
- **Independent contractor**: Reinforce throughout; avoid operational control language that could create joint-employer liability under NLRB/state standards
- **Do not** guarantee developer profitability or include earnings projections outside FDD Item 19
- **Do not** draft provisions waiving state franchise law protections where prohibited
- **Mark all statutory citations** with [VERIFY] unless confirmed from source documents
