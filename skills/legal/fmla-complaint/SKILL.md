# FMLA Violation Complaint

Drafts a federal complaint alleging employer interference with or retaliation for exercise of FMLA rights under 29 U.S.C. § 2601 et seq.

## Prerequisites

1. **Employment records** — offer letter, pay stubs, timesheets establishing 12-month tenure and 1,250+ hours
2. **Leave documentation** — request correspondence, medical certifications, employer notices (or absence thereof)
3. **Adverse action evidence** — termination letter, demotion notice, disciplinary records, performance reviews
4. **Medical records** — healthcare provider certifications, treatment records establishing serious health condition
5. **Employer FMLA policy** — handbook provisions, posted notices, training materials
6. **Witness information** — names, titles, contact info for corroborating witnesses
7. **Damages documentation** — pay stubs, benefits statements, out-of-pocket medical expenses, tax returns

## Output Structure

### I. Caption & Parties

| Element | Requirements |
|---------|-------------|
| Complainant | Full legal name, address, contact info, employee ID, job title, department, exact employment dates |
| Employer | Legal business name, DBAs, principal address, registered agent, FEIN if available |
| Forum | Federal district court (28 U.S.C. § 1331) or DOL Wage and Hour Division |

### II. FMLA Eligibility (29 U.S.C. § 2611)

Establish all three prongs with documentary support:

| Prong | Requirement | Evidence |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Duration | 12+ months employed (need not be consecutive) | Employment records, offer letter |
| Hours | 1,250+ hours in preceding 12 months (§ 2611(2)(A)(ii)) | Pay stubs, timesheets — show calculation by pay period |
| Worksite size | 50+ employees within 75-mile radius for 20+ workweeks (§ 2611(2)(B)(ii)) | Public filings, company records; note if in employer's exclusive knowledge |

- Preemptively address close-call defenses (gaps in employment, borderline hours, multi-location aggregation)

### III. Qualifying Reason (29 U.S.C. § 2612(a)(1))

Identify which statutory category applies:

| Category | Statute |
|----------|---------|
| Birth/newborn care | § 2612(a)(1)(A) |
| Adoption/foster placement | § 2612(a)(1)(B) |
| Care for spouse/child/parent — serious health condition | § 2612(a)(1)(C) |
| Employee's own serious health condition | § 2612(a)(1)(D) |
| Military qualifying exigency | § 2612(a)(1)(E) |

For serious health conditions, map to 29 CFR § 825.113 categories:
- Inpatient care
- Continuing treatment: 3+ days incapacity + provider treatment
- Chronic serious health condition
- Permanent/long-term incapacity
- Multiple treatments (restorative surgery or condition requiring treatment)

### IV. Notice & Certification Timeline

Build a chronological table:

| Date | Event | Regulatory Requirement | Compliance? |
|------|-------|----------------------|-------------|
| | Employee notice to employer | 30 days if foreseeable (29 CFR § 825.302); ASAP if not (§ 825.303) | |
| | Employer eligibility notice | Within 5 business days (29 CFR § 825.300(b)) | |
| | Employer rights/responsibilities notice | With eligibility notice (29 CFR § 825.300(c)) | |
| | Certification request | Per 29 CFR § 825.305; 15 days to return | |
| | Certification submitted | Complete/sufficient per § 825.305(d) | |
| | Deficiency notice (if any) | Written, 7 days to cure (§ 825.305(c)) | |
| | Designation notice | Within 5 business days (29 CFR § 825.300(d)) | |

Each missed step = potential independent violation.

### V. Violations — Causes of Action

Organize by legal theory, numbered as separate counts:

**Interference (29 U.S.C. § 2615(a)(1)):**

| Violation Type | Key Regulation |
|---------------|----------------|
| Outright denial of leave | § 2612(a)(1) |
| Discouragement through threats | § 2615(a)(1) |
| Counting FMLA leave under no-fault attendance policy | 29 CFR § 825.220(c) |
| Failure to maintain health benefits during leave | § 2614(c)(1) |
| Failure to restore to same/equivalent position | § 2614(a)(1); 29 CFR § 825.214(a) |
| Requiring work during leave | § 2612 |
| FMLA leave as negative factor in employment decisions | 29 CFR § 825.220(c) |
| Failure to provide required notices | § 2619; 29 CFR § 825.300 |

**Retaliation (29 U.S.C. § 2615(a)(2)):**

For each adverse action, establish causation through:
- [ ] Temporal proximity to protected activity
- [ ] Comparator evidence (similarly situated non-FMLA employees treated better)
- [ ] Direct statements linking action to FMLA usage
- [ ] Pattern of escalating discipline post-leave
- [ ] Pretext (shifting or unsupported justifications)

For every violation: specify date, actors (name + title), precise action/omission, consequence to employee, and cite the specific statutory/regulatory provision violated. Use direct quotations from documents where available.

### VI. Evidence Inventory

Organize by category:

- [ ] Employment records (offer letter, contract, job description, personnel file)
- [ ] Time/attendance records (timesheets, payroll, scheduling)
- [ ] Leave correspondence (emails, letters, texts — with dates and participants)
- [ ] Medical documentation (certifications, provider letters, discharge summaries)
- [ ] Employer FMLA policy (handbook, posted notices, intranet)
- [ ] Employer notices (eligibility, rights/responsibilities, designation)
- [ ] Performance/discipline records (pre- and post-leave)
- [ ] Comparative evidence (treatment of non-FMLA employees)
- [ ] Documents in employer's exclusive possession (identify for discovery)

### VII. Witness List

| Name | Title/Relationship | Anticipated Testimony | Credibility Basis |
|------|-------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| | | | |

Include: supervisors, HR personnel, coworkers, healthcare providers, family members.

### VIII. Relief (29 U.S.C. § 2617(a)(1))

| Category | Basis | Calculation Method |
|----------|-------|--------------------|
| Reinstatement | § 2614(a)(1) | Same or equivalent position; or front pay if infeasible |
| Back pay | § 2617(a)(1)(A)(i)(I) | Wage rate × lost period, minus interim earnings |
| Lost benefits | § 2617(a)(1)(A)(i)(II) | Health premiums, retirement contributions, other fringes |
| Out-of-pocket medical | § 2617(a)(1)(A)(i)(II) | Itemized expenses from coverage loss |
| Liquidated damages | § 2617(a)(1)(A)(iii) | Equal to lost wages + benefits; mandatory unless employer proves good faith (29 CFR § 825.400(c)) |
| Attorney's fees & costs | § 2617(a)(3) | Mandatory for prevailing plaintiffs |
| Prejudgment interest | Applicable federal/state rate | From date each loss incurred |
| Post-judgment interest | 28 U.S.C. § 1961 | Federal statutory rate |
| Injunctive relief | Equitable authority | Compliant policies, training, notice posting |
| Record expungement | Equitable authority | Remove FMLA-tainted discipline/evaluations |

Provide itemized calculations with supporting documentation for all quantifiable damages.

### IX. Verification & Signature

- Verification under penalty of perjury (28 U.S.C. § 1746 for federal court)
- Complainant signature block (name, date, contact)
- Attorney signature block (name, bar number, firm, address, contact)
- Rule 11 certification (well-grounded in fact and law, no improper purpose)

## Guidelines

- **Forum selection**: Assess federal court vs. DOL Wage and Hour Division based on client's strategic goals, desired remedies, and speed
- **Statute of limitations**: 2 years from violation; 3 years if willful (29 U.S.C. § 2617(c))
- **Burden shifting**: Employee establishes prima facie case; employer bears burden on affirmative defenses (§ 2617(a)(3))
- **Liquidated damages**: Presumptively mandatory — frame violations to foreclose good-faith defense
- **No preemption**: FMLA does not preempt state family/medical leave laws providing greater protections — note any applicable state claims
- **Numbered paragraphs**: Use sequentially numbered paragraphs per FRCP standards
- **Tone**: Professional and objective; present facts favorably without inflammatory language
- **Anticipate defenses**: Address eligibility challenges, legitimate business reasons, and certification deficiency arguments proactively
- **Discovery roadmap**: Flag documents in employer's exclusive possession for formal discovery
- Cite all regulatory provisions precisely — do not paraphrase CFR section numbers
- Mark any uncertain citations with [VERIFY]
