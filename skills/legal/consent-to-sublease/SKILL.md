# Landlord's Consent to Sublease

Drafts a consent document that formally authorizes a commercial tenant to sublease premises to a third party while preserving all landlord rights under the master lease.

## Prerequisites

1. **Executed Master Lease** — full legal names of landlord and tenant, execution date, premises description, lease term, and the specific section requiring landlord consent for subleasing
2. **Proposed or executed Sublease** — subtenant's full legal name, portion of premises subleased (entire or partial with sq. ft./suite), sublease term (commencement and expiration), permitted use
3. **Tenant's formal consent request** — confirming compliance with any Master Lease notice requirements
4. **Any Master Lease conditions precedent** — financial statements, use restrictions, tenant improvement requirements, insurance certificates, security deposit adjustments

## Output Structure

### Document Skeleton

| Section | Contents |
|---|---|
| **Title** | "LANDLORD'S CONSENT TO SUBLEASE" (all caps) |
| **Execution Date** | Must align with or precede sublease commencement |
| **Recitals** | Three recitals (see below) |
| **Operative Consent** | Consent clause + five protective provisions |
| **Additional Protections** | Subordination, cure rights, attornment |
| **Signature Blocks** | Landlord, Tenant ("Agreed and Accepted"), Subtenant |
| **Exhibit** | Attach complete copy of sublease |

### Recitals

Draft three recitals:

1. **Master Lease** — Landlord and Tenant full legal names (as they appear in the lease), execution date, complete premises description, lease term
2. **Sublease** — Subtenant full legal name, specific premises portion, sublease execution date, sublease term (confirm it does not extend beyond Master Lease term)
3. **Consent Requirement** — Cite specific Master Lease section requiring consent; note if consent "shall not be unreasonably withheld"; confirm tenant's formal request

### Operative Provisions

Draft these five core provisions:

- [ ] **Limited Consent** — Consent applies only to this specific sublease (attach as exhibit). Explicitly: no waiver of right to approve/disapprove future transfers; each subsequent transfer requires separate written consent.

- [ ] **Master Lease Continuation** — Master Lease remains in full force, unmodified. Tenant remains primarily and fully liable for all obligations (rent, maintenance, insurance, covenants). Sublease does not diminish, release, or discharge any tenant liability. Landlord retains all rights and remedies against tenant for defaults by tenant or subtenant.

- [ ] **No Privity with Subtenant** — No contractual relationship or privity of estate between landlord and subtenant. Subtenant cannot enforce Master Lease provisions against landlord, cannot claim third-party beneficiary status. Landlord has no direct obligations to subtenant except as imposed by law.

- [ ] **Subtenant Bound by Master Lease** — Subtenant must comply with all applicable Master Lease terms, conditions, covenants, rules, and regulations. Use must conform to permitted use. Any Master Lease violation by subtenant constitutes a tenant default.

- [ ] **Rent Procedures** — Landlord looks solely to tenant for all rent/additional rent. No obligation to accept payment directly from subtenant. Any payment received from subtenant: credited to tenant's account, does not create landlord-tenant relationship or waive tenant obligations. Tenant liable for full Master Lease rent regardless of subtenant payment.

### Additional Protective Provisions

Include as applicable:

- [ ] **Subordination** — Sublease expressly subject and subordinate to Master Lease; termination of Master Lease automatically terminates sublease without landlord liability
- [ ] **Landlord's Cure Rights** — Landlord may cure subtenant defaults and charge costs to tenant
- [ ] **Attornment** — Landlord may require subtenant to attorn to landlord upon Master Lease termination
- [ ] **Amendment Notice** — Tenant must provide landlord copies of any sublease amendments
- [ ] **No Further Transfers** — Subtenant may not further sublease or assign without landlord consent
- [ ] **Cross-Default** — Any sublease default constitutes a Master Lease default

### Signature Blocks

**Landlord:**
- Full legal name, signature line, date, printed name and title of authorized signatory

**Tenant** (headed "AGREED AND ACCEPTED"):
- Full legal name, signature line, date, printed name and title
- Acknowledgment of continued full liability under Master Lease

**Subtenant** (headed "ACKNOWLEDGED AND AGREED"):
- Full legal name, signature line, date, printed name and title
- Acknowledgment: bound by Master Lease terms; no direct landlord-subtenant relationship created

## Guidelines

- Verify all party names, dates, and premises descriptions match exactly across Master Lease, Sublease, and this Consent
- Confirm sublease term does not extend beyond Master Lease expiration
- Check whether the jurisdiction requires notarization or witnesses for enforceability
- Review Master Lease for any additional conditions precedent (increased security deposit, modified insurance requirements, signage restrictions)
- If Master Lease states consent "not to be unreasonably withheld," ensure conditions imposed are commercially reasonable
- Use defined terms consistently throughout; match capitalization conventions from the Master Lease
- This is a U.S. general template — flag any state-specific requirements (e.g., statutory notice periods, recording obligations) with `[VERIFY]`
