# Tenant Improvement Work Letter

Drafts the lease exhibit that controls TI scope, approvals, construction standards, allowance funding, and closeout.

## Prerequisites

1. **Lease terms**: defined terms, notice clause, permitted use, delivery condition, rent commencement, defaults, insurance, and exhibits list.
2. **Premises data**: address, suite, rentable SF, base building condition, landlord work, building standards.
3. **Allowance terms**: total and per-SF TI allowance, eligible costs, retainage, reimbursement method, unused allowance treatment.
4. **Delivery schedule**: plan submittal deadlines, review periods, start date, substantial completion date, delay rules.
5. **Contractor model**: landlord or tenant GC, approved list or pre-qualification, supervision rights, site rules.
6. **Compliance inputs**: applicable codes, ADA, permits, union labor rules, building rules, lien law mechanics.

- Insurance and risk allocation

| --- | --- | --- |

| Materials and finishes | Yes | Building standard or better |

## Guidelines

- Use the lease’s defined terms and cross-reference relevant lease sections.
- State which document controls if there is a conflict between lease and work letter.
- Specify approval timeframes and that approval is not unreasonably withheld.
- Allocate permitting and compliance duties explicitly to one party.
- Tie disbursement to lien waivers and proof of payment.
- Include a retainage percentage and release conditions.
- Clarify treatment of unused allowance and overages.
- Define substantial completion with objective criteria.
- Address mechanics’ lien removal obligations and indemnity.
- Avoid adding scope beyond tenant improvements unless the lease contemplates it.
