# Third-Party Special Needs Trust

Drafts an irrevocable third-party Special Needs Trust that supplements government benefits for a disabled beneficiary using assets belonging to someone other than the beneficiary, with no Medicaid payback obligation at termination.

## Prerequisites

1. **Grantor information** — full legal names, relationship to beneficiary, state of residence
2. **Beneficiary details** — full legal name, date of birth, nature of disability, current benefit programs (SSI, Medicaid, SSDI, etc.), specific supplemental needs
3. **Trustee designation** — individual vs. corporate fiduciary, successor trustees
4. **Funding plan** — immediate funding (asset schedule), or deferred (life insurance, retirement account beneficiary designations, pour-over from will)
5. **Remainder beneficiaries** — primary and contingent, with shares/percentages
6. **Jurisdiction** — state-specific trust execution and Medicaid rules

## Output Structure

### Document Sections

| Section | Contents |
|---|---|
| **Preamble** | Trust name, grantor, trustee, beneficiary, date, recitals of intent |
| **Article I — Purpose & Intent** | Supplemental-only purpose statement; benefits preservation declaration |
| **Article II — Irrevocability** | Irrevocable from execution; no amendment except as permitted by law |
| **Article III — Distribution Standards** | Sole and absolute discretion; supplemental needs categories; prohibited distributions |
| **Article IV — Trustee Powers & Duties** | Investment, hiring advisors, tax authority, recordkeeping |
| **Article V — Trustee Compensation & Succession** | Fee schedule or reasonable compensation; resignation/removal; successor appointment |
| **Article VI — Administration & Accounting** | Annual accountings, tax filings (EIN), trust situs |
| **Article VII — Termination & Remainder** | Terminates at beneficiary's death; NO Medicaid payback; remainder to designated beneficiaries |
| **Article VIII — Tax Provisions** | Grantor vs. non-grantor trust election; gift tax treatment; tax allocation authority |
| **Article IX — General Provisions** | Severability, governing law, spendthrift, anti-alienation |
| **Execution** | Grantor signature (notarized), trustee acceptance, notary acknowledgment |
| **Schedule A** | Initial trust assets (if funded at execution) |

### Purpose Statement Template

```
This Trust is established solely to provide for the supplemental and extra
needs of the Beneficiary beyond those provided by any local, state, or
federal government program. It is the Grantor's intent that Trust assets
shall NOT supplant, replace, impair, or reduce any public benefits to which
the Beneficiary is or may become entitled.
```

### Permissible Distribution Categories

- Medical/dental care not covered by Medicaid
- Therapy, rehabilitation, habilitation services
- Education, vocational training, tutoring
- Entertainment, recreation, travel, companionship
- Electronic equipment, communication devices, assistive technology
- Personal care attendants (beyond public benefit coverage)
- Vehicle purchase/modification, transportation costs
- Clothing, personal items, quality-of-life enhancements
- Legal, financial, and benefits planning services
- Trust-owned residence expenses (see housing rules below)

### Prohibited Distributions (SSI In-Kind Support & Maintenance)

| Prohibited | Compliant Alternative |
|---|---|
| Cash directly to beneficiary | Pay vendors/providers directly |
| Food purchases for beneficiary | Use ABLE account if available; accept ISM reduction if justified |
| Shelter costs paid to/for beneficiary | Trust purchases and holds title to residence; trustee pays carrying costs from trust |
| Rent paid to beneficiary's landlord | Structure as trust-owned housing or accept ISM reduction with cost-benefit analysis |

> **Trustee instruction**: Before any distribution that could affect benefits, consult a public benefits specialist. Document the analysis in trust records.

### Remainder Distribution Clause Template

```
Upon the death of the Beneficiary, the Trustee shall distribute remaining
Trust assets to the following remainder beneficiaries, free of any obligation
to reimburse any governmental entity for benefits provided to the Beneficiary
during the Beneficiary's lifetime:

  [Remainder beneficiary designations with shares and contingencies]
```

### Execution Requirements

- Grantor signature with notarization (best practice even where not required by state law)
- Trustee acceptance signature
- Witnesses per state requirement (if applicable)
- Notary acknowledgment with venue and seal

## Guidelines

- **No Medicaid payback**: This is a third-party trust — explicitly disclaim any payback obligation. This distinguishes it from first-party/self-settled SNTs under 42 U.S.C. § 1396p(d)(4)(A) [VERIFY].
- **Irrevocability is mandatory** for benefits protection; state this unambiguously.
- **Discretionary language must be absolute** — beneficiary has no enforceable right to demand distributions. Use "sole and absolute discretion."
- **Spendthrift clause required** — trust assets are not assignable by the beneficiary and not reachable by creditors.
- **State variations**: Verify trust execution formalities, investment standards (UPIA adoption), and state Medicaid manual treatment of third-party SNTs for the relevant jurisdiction.
- **Gift tax**: If grantor wants annual exclusion eligibility, a Crummey power may be needed — flag this for attorney review. Otherwise, contributions use lifetime exemption.
- **Grantor vs. non-grantor trust**: Grantor trust status may be advantageous during grantor's lifetime for income tax purposes; address the toggle explicitly.
- **ABLE account coordination**: If beneficiary qualifies (disability onset before age 26), consider provisions authorizing trustee to fund an ABLE account (up to annual limit) for food/shelter flexibility.
- **Consistent naming**: Use defined terms ("Grantor," "Trustee," "Beneficiary," "Trust") consistently throughout; verify all cross-references.
- **Do not draft** first-party/self-settled SNT provisions (payback, age-65 restrictions) — those require a separate skill.
