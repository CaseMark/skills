# Seller Consulting Agreement

Draft a seller-to-buyer consulting agreement that operationalizes post-closing knowledge transfer while aligning with the purchase documents.

## Prerequisites

1. **Transaction documents**: asset/stock purchase agreement, schedules, disclosure lists, earnout/holdback terms.
2. **Service scope brief**: topics, deliverables, frequency, and transition timeline.
3. **Compensation inputs**: rate/retainer structure, expense policy, payment workflow.
4. **Restrictive covenants**: any non-compete/non-solicit already in deal documents.
5. **IP/data context**: ownership expectations for work product and access to systems.
6. **Governing law/venue**: preferred state and dispute forum.

## Output Structure / Process

**A. Term Sheet Inputs (capture first)**

| Item | Drafting Prompt | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Parties | Legal names, entity types, addresses, signers | Seller is consultant; Buyer is client |
| Effective Date | Closing date or specified date | Tie to transaction if required |
| Term | Start/end, renewal mechanics | 6–24 months typical |
| Scope | Specific services + exclusions | Avoid operational control language |
| Deliverables | Meetings, intros, docs, training | Define measurable outputs |
| Time/Location | Hours, availability windows, remote/on-site | Include travel expectations |
| Compensation | Retainer/hourly/project | Define billable time |
| Expenses | Reimbursable categories + pre-approval | Cap or per diem if needed |
| Confidentiality | Definition + exceptions | Cross-reference deal NDA |
| IP/Work Product | Work-for-hire or assignment | Address pre-existing IP |
| Restrictions | Non-compete/non-solicit | Align with purchase agreement |
| Liability | Cap, exclusions, indemnity | Carve-outs for confidentiality |
| Disputes | Negotiation, arbitration/litigation | Injunctive relief carve-out |
| Notices | Methods + addresses | Email notice if allowed |

**B. Clause Checklist (include each heading)**

1. Parties; Effective Date; Recitals referencing transaction.
2. Definitions (Confidential Information, Services, Work Product).
3. Scope of Services; Deliverables; Availability; Location.
4. Standard of Performance; No Authority to Bind Client.
5. Independent Contractor; Taxes and Benefits.
6. Compensation; Invoicing; Payment Terms; Late Fees.
7. Expenses; Approval; Supporting Documentation.
8. Term; Renewal; Termination for Cause; Termination for Convenience.
9. Effect of Termination; Final Invoice; Return of Materials.
10. Confidentiality; Permitted Disclosures; Notice of Legal Process.
11. Non-Use/Non-Disclosure; Data Security; Return/Destruction.
12. Work Product Ownership; IP Assignment; License Back if required.
13. Representations and Warranties (authority, compliance, non-infringement).
14. Indemnification (consultant and client); Defense control; mitigation.
15. Limitation of Liability; No Consequential Damages; carve-outs.
16. Restrictive Covenants alignment with purchase agreement.
17. Governing Law; Venue; Dispute Resolution; Injunctive Relief.
18. Notices; Assignment; Entire Agreement; Amendment; Severability; Counterparts; E-signatures.

**D. Optional Schedules**

| Schedule | Use When |
| --- | --- |
| Schedule A: Services | Complex deliverables or milestones |
| Schedule B: Rates/Retainer | Multiple rates or blended services |
| Schedule C: Expense Policy | Detailed travel/expense rules |
| Schedule D: Non-Compete/Non-Solicit | If not in purchase agreement |

## Guidelines

- Align term, restrictions, and confidentiality with the purchase agreement to avoid conflicts.
- Avoid language granting the consultant managerial authority unless explicitly required.
- If compensation is tied to earnout/holdback, mirror the purchase agreement mechanics.
- Define billable time and minimum billing increments if hourly.
- Carve out confidentiality and IP from liability caps where appropriate.
- Include a clear return/destruction obligation for client materials at termination.
- For arbitration, specify seat, rules, number of arbitrators, and fee allocation.
- Confirm any jurisdiction-specific limits on non-compete/non-solicit enforceability.
- Use [VERIFY] for any statutory citations added by the drafter.
