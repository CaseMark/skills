# Articles of Incorporation

Drafts a filing-ready U.S. Articles of Incorporation tailored to the jurisdiction of incorporation, capital structure, and stakeholder requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Jurisdiction of incorporation** — state selected (Delaware, Wyoming, home state, etc.)
2. **Proposed corporate name** — including designator; note any name reservation number/expiration
3. **Business purpose** — general commercial or specialized (professional corp, regulated industry)
4. **Capital structure intent** — founder-only common, VC-backed (blank check preferred), dual-class, S-corp
5. **Registered agent** — name and physical street address in state of incorporation
6. **Incorporator** — name and mailing address
7. **Supporting documents** — term sheets, cap tables, shareholder agreements, formation questionnaires, business plans

## Output Structure / Process

### Article I — Corporate Name
State the exact legal name with required designator. Common designators by state:

| Form | Abbreviations |
|------|---------------|
| Corporation | Corp. |
| Incorporated | Inc. |
| Company | Co. |
| Limited | Ltd. |

Confirm name is distinguishable from existing registrations; note reservation number if applicable.

### Article II — Purpose
- **Default (recommended):** broad general purpose — "to engage in any lawful act or activity for which corporations may be organized under [State] law"
- **Narrow purpose required when:** VC documents restrict deployment of capital to specific sectors; regulated industries (banking, insurance, healthcare, professional services) with mandatory statutory language
- Include catch-all: power to acquire/dispose property, issue securities, contract, sue and be sued

### Article III — Registered Agent and Office
- Must be an individual resident of the state OR a commercial registered agent authorized in-state
- Address must be a **physical street address** (no P.O. boxes)
- Include: street number, street name, suite/unit, city, county (if required), state, ZIP

### Article IV — Capital Structure

#### Simple Common Stock (founder/closely held)
| Element | Guidance |
|---------|----------|
| Authorized shares | 10M–100M shares; leave headroom for ESOP (10–20% fully diluted) and future rounds |
| Par value | $0.001 or $0.0001 (nominal); affects Delaware franchise tax — lower = less tax under authorized shares method |
| Voting | 1 vote per share |
| Dividends | Board discretion, pro rata |
| Liquidation | Pro rata after creditors |

#### Blank Check Preferred (VC-backed)
Authorize board to establish series without shareholder vote. Board powers to specify per series:
- [ ] Dividend rate; cumulative or non-cumulative
- [ ] Liquidation preference amount; participating or non-participating
- [ ] Conversion ratio into common; anti-dilution adjustments (weighted-average or full ratchet)
- [ ] Optional/mandatory redemption; redemption price
- [ ] Votes per share; any class voting rights
- [ ] Protective provisions triggering class vote

Authorize preferred shares ≥ common shares authorized (commonly 1:1 ratio).

#### Dual-Class Common (founder control)
| Class | Votes/Share | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Class A | 1 | Public/investor shares |
| Class B | 10 | Founder shares; specify conversion to Class A on transfer |

State whether classes vote together (general matters) or separately (class-specific rights).

#### S-Corp Compliance Check
If S-election planned: single class of stock only; ≤100 shareholders; eligible shareholder types only (no non-resident aliens, no entity shareholders except certain trusts and estates).

### Article V — Incorporator
Name and mailing address. Role is ministerial — signs and files; may conduct initial organizational meeting.

### Article VI — Optional Provisions (include as applicable)

| Provision | Include When |
|-----------|-------------|
| Director exculpation | Always — eliminates monetary liability for duty-of-care breaches (not loyalty, bad faith, intentional misconduct, unlawful distributions) |
| Indemnification | Always — directors and officers, to the fullest extent permitted by law |
| Supermajority voting | Investors or founders require enhanced protection for mergers, asset sales, dissolution, or charter amendments |
| Board size/classification | Staggered board desired for takeover defense or investor negotiation |
| Preemptive rights | Shareholders require right to maintain pro rata ownership on new issuances |
| No preemptive rights | Default for VC-backed; state expressly if jurisdiction implies them |
| Close corporation status | Jurisdiction recognizes close corp; shareholders want shareholder-managed structure |
| Professional corporation | Licensed services (law, medicine, accounting); include: authorized services, licensure requirement for shareholders, transfer restrictions |
| Perpetual existence | Standard; omit duration limitation unless expressly required |

### Execution Block
- Declaration citing applicable state corporation statute
- Incorporator signature line with printed name
- Date of execution
- Notarization block if required by jurisdiction (verify — most states do not require)

### Post-Filing Checklist (include as guidance section in deliverable)
- [ ] Organizational meeting: adopt bylaws, elect directors/officers, authorize share issuance, authorize bank accounts, ratify pre-incorporation agreements
- [ ] Obtain federal EIN (IRS online — immediate)
- [ ] Issue shares with consideration documented (board resolution + subscription agreement)
- [ ] File initial report if required by state
- [ ] Foreign qualification if operating in states other than state of incorporation
- [ ] Industry-specific licenses/permits before commencing operations
- [ ] Annual report and franchise tax calendar reminders

## Guidelines

- **Jurisdiction-specific rules**: Delaware DGCL, California Corp Code, Wyoming, etc. differ on par value franchise tax, required provisions, and permissive provisions — verify each filing state's current statute
- **Par value and franchise tax**: In Delaware, the authorized shares method taxes on number of authorized shares; assumed par value capital method may yield lower tax for high-asset companies — [VERIFY current rates with Delaware Division of Corporations]
- **Blank check preferred**: Do not enumerate specific preferred series in the Articles for VC-backed companies; leave to board resolutions and Certificates of Designation filed at each round
- **Exculpation scope**: State law limits vary — confirm jurisdiction permits elimination of duty-of-care liability and identify any exceptions [VERIFY per state]
- **Professional corporations**: Confirm state-specific licensure and ownership restrictions; some states prohibit non-licensed shareholders entirely
- **No legal advice on S-election**: Note that S-corp qualification requires IRS Form 2553 post-filing; structural compliance in Articles is necessary but not sufficient
- **Do not include**: Bylaw-level operational details (meeting procedures, officer titles, quorum requirements) — these belong in bylaws, not the charter
- **Conflicts**: If term sheet or shareholder agreement provisions conflict with proposed charter terms, flag for client resolution before filing
