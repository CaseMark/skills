# Settlement Agreement

Drafts a binding settlement agreement that memorializes all negotiated terms with enforceability, clarity, and finality.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party information** — full legal names, capacities, addresses, authority to bind
2. **Dispute background** — case caption, court, claims at issue, relevant dates
3. **Negotiated terms** — monetary amounts, payment schedules, non-monetary obligations
4. **Confidentiality requirements** — scope, permitted disclosures, remedies
5. **Pending litigation details** — docket number, dismissal preferences (with/without prejudice)
6. **Governing contract(s)** — if dispute arises from a contract, review for forum-selection, choice-of-law, fee-shifting

## Output Structure

### Document Skeleton

| Section | Key Contents |
|---|---|
| **Title & Preamble** | "Settlement Agreement and Mutual Release"; party names, addresses, defined as "Party A" / "Party B" |
| **Recitals** | Background of dispute (neutral framing — no admissions); mutual desire to resolve; adequate consideration acknowledged |
| **Definitions** | Defined terms on first use or in standalone section; capitalize consistently |
| **Payment Terms** | Amount, schedule, method, wire instructions, late-payment consequences, acceleration on default |
| **Mutual Releases** | Scope (known/unknown claims), carve-outs, effective date |
| **Confidentiality** | Protected information, permitted disclosures (legal/tax advisors, regulatory, subpoena), breach remedies |
| **Non-Admission** | Express statement: settlement ≠ admission of liability |
| **Representations & Warranties** | Authority to execute, no assignment of claims, accuracy of information |
| **Dismissal Mechanics** | Which party files, timeline, with/without prejudice, cost allocation |
| **Tax Provisions** | Responsibility for tax consequences, 1099 reporting, indemnification for tax liability |
| **General Provisions** | Governing law, dispute resolution (mediation → arbitration → litigation), severability, integration, amendment, counterparts, attorney's fees |
| **Signature Blocks** | Name, title, date, notarization/witness lines if required |
| **Exhibits** | Payment schedule, form of dismissal/stipulation, property descriptions |

### Release Drafting Checklist

- [ ] Define released claims with specificity (causes of action, time period, subject matter)
- [ ] State whether release covers **known and unknown** claims
- [ ] If unknown claims included, add statutory waiver:

```
Each Party expressly waives any rights under California Civil Code
§ 1542, which provides: "A general release does not extend to claims
that the creditor or releasing party does not know or suspect to
exist in his or her favor at the time of executing the release and
that, if known by him or her, would have materially affected his or
her settlement with the debtor or released party."
```

- [ ] Adapt waiver language for applicable jurisdiction's equivalent statute
- [ ] Identify carve-outs (e.g., rights under the agreement itself, indemnification obligations, future conduct)
- [ ] Confirm release is mutual unless parties agreed otherwise

### Payment Terms Template

```
Section __: Payment Terms

(a) [Payor] shall pay [Payee] the total sum of $[AMOUNT] ("Settlement
    Amount"), payable as follows:
    (i)   $[AMOUNT] within [X] business days of the Effective Date;
    (ii)  $[AMOUNT] on or before [DATE]; ...

(b) Payments shall be made by [wire transfer/certified check] to:
    [Account details or delivery address]

(c) If any payment is not received within [X] days of its due date,
    the entire remaining balance shall become immediately due and
    payable, together with interest at [X]% per annum.
```

## Special Compliance Requirements

### ADEA / Age Discrimination Claims

If releasing age-discrimination claims under the Older Workers Benefit Protection Act (29 U.S.C. § 626(f)):

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Knowing & voluntary | Plain-language explanation of rights waived |
| Consideration | Must exceed what employee is already entitled to |
| Written advisement | Advise to consult attorney |
| Consideration period | 21 days (individual) or 45 days (group/RIF) |
| Revocation period | 7 days after execution |
| Specific reference | Must specifically reference ADEA claims |

### Minors / Incapacitated Persons

- Court approval required in most jurisdictions — include provision conditioning effectiveness on court order
- Identify guardian ad litem or conservator as signatory

### Structured Settlements

- If annuity or periodic payments: identify funding mechanism, qualified assignment if applicable, IRC § 104(a)(2) considerations

## Guidelines

- **Neutral framing**: Recitals must not contain language attributable as admissions — use "disputed claims," "alleged," "without conceding"
- **No ambiguity in releases**: Vague releases invite future litigation; enumerate released claims or use comprehensive "any and all" with clear carve-outs
- **Dismissal timing**: Tie dismissal filing to payment completion, not execution date, unless parties agree otherwise
- **Confidentiality carve-outs**: Always permit disclosures required by law, regulation, or court order; include notice-and-opportunity-to-object mechanism for subpoenas
- **Integration clause**: Must be airtight — "This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements, whether written or oral"
- **Counterparts / e-signature**: Include counterparts clause; confirm e-signature validity under E-SIGN Act / UETA if parties will sign electronically
- **Jurisdiction-specific**: Verify governing law requirements — some states have specific enforceability rules for settlement agreements (e.g., Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 664.6 for stipulated settlements)
- **Tax allocation**: If settlement covers multiple claim types (e.g., lost wages vs. emotional distress), allocate amounts explicitly — tax treatment differs under IRC § 104
