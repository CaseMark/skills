# Change in Control Agreement

Drafts a complete, tax-compliant U.S. executive Change in Control Agreement with double-trigger severance, equity acceleration, and 280G/409A provisions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Executive profile** — name, title, reporting structure, base salary, target bonus (% or $), outstanding equity awards (type, vesting schedule, performance metrics)
2. **Company profile** — full legal name, state of incorporation, entity type, parent/subsidiary structure, public/private status
3. **Existing agreements** — employment agreement, equity plan documents, any prior CIC or severance agreements
4. **Benchmarking data** (if available) — peer agreements, comp committee philosophy, proxy disclosures, shareholder guidelines
5. **Key deal terms** — severance multiple, protection period length, 280G treatment election, benefits continuation period

## Output Structure

### Article I — Definitions

| Term | Standard Formulation |
|------|---------------------|
| **Change in Control** | Three-prong: (1) >50% voting power acquisition by any person/group (per §§13(d)/14(d) of Exchange Act); (2) merger/consolidation where pre-transaction shareholders own <50% of surviving entity; (3) sale of all or substantially all assets. Optional 4th prong: incumbent board majority lost via proxy contest. |
| **Cause** | (a) Felony conviction or guilty/no-contest plea; (b) willful, continued failure to perform duties after written notice + 30-day cure; (c) willful conduct materially injurious to company; (d) breach of fiduciary duty; (e) material written policy violation after notice + cure. |
| **Good Reason** | (a) Material diminution in authority, duties, or responsibilities; (b) material reduction in base salary or target bonus not applicable to all similarly situated executives; (c) principal office relocation >[35–50] miles without consent. **Procedural**: executive provides written notice within [60–90] days of first awareness → company has [30]-day cure period → executive must terminate within [30–60] days after uncured condition or right is waived. |
| **Qualifying Termination** | Termination by company without Cause OR by executive for Good Reason, occurring during the Protection Period. |
| **Protection Period** | [12–24] months following Change in Control. Consider 3–6 month pre-CIC tail for terminations at acquirer's request in connection with the transaction. |

### Article II — Severance Benefits

Upon a Qualifying Termination:

| Benefit | Standard Terms |
|---------|---------------|
| **Cash Severance** | Lump sum = [1x–3x] × (Base Salary + Target Annual Bonus). Salary measured at higher of pre-termination or pre-CIC rate. |
| **Pro-Rated Bonus** | Optional. Prorated (days employed ÷ 365) × target or actual performance. Specify payment timing and 409A treatment. |
| **Equity Acceleration** | 100% of all unvested awards (options, SARs, RSAs, RSUs, PSAs, PSUs) vest immediately. Performance awards: vest at [target / maximum / actual measured through CIC date]. Post-termination exercise window: [12–24] months from termination or original expiration, whichever is earlier — supersedes contrary plan or award terms. |
| **Benefits Continuation** | Company-paid COBRA for [12–24] months or until comparable coverage through subsequent employer. Premiums may be taxable — consider gross-up or cash-in-lieu structure. |
| **Mitigation** | Executive has no duty to mitigate; benefits not offset by subsequent earnings or retirement benefits. |

**Payment Timing / §409A:**
- Pay on Day 60 post-separation; if 60-day window spans two tax years, pay in the second year
- **Specified employee delay**: if executive is a "specified employee" of a publicly traded company, delay nonqualified deferred compensation payments until the first business day following the 6-month anniversary of separation from service

### Article III — Conditions to Benefits

- **Release requirement:** Executive must execute (and not revoke) a general release within:
  - 21 days for executives under age 40
  - 45 or 60 days for executives age 40+ (OWBPA); 7-day statutory revocation period must expire
- **Scope:** All employment-related claims (Title VII, ADEA, FLSA, state analogs, common law wrongful termination). Carve-outs: workers' comp, UI benefits, D&O indemnification, rights under this agreement itself.
- **Property return:** All company devices, property, and confidential information
- **Post-termination cooperation:** Transition assistance; company reimburses reasonable out-of-pocket expenses; compensate for more than de minimis time

### Article IV — Section 280G / Parachute Payments

Select **one** approach:

| Approach | Description | Market Status |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| **No Gross-Up** | Executive bears full §4999 excise tax. No reduction or company payment. | **Current standard** |
| **Best Net (Modified Cutback)** | Payments reduced to 2.99× base amount only if executive's after-tax amount with reduction exceeds after-tax amount with full payment and excise tax. | Common middle ground |
| **Full Gross-Up** | Company pays additional amount making executive whole for §4999 excise tax and taxes on the gross-up (pyramiding calculation). | Legacy only; ISS/Glass Lewis disfavor |

If Best Net applies, specify reduction order: cash severance first → then equity (latest-vesting first); within each category, reduce §409A-covered amounts before exempt amounts.

**Calculation mechanics:** Nationally recognized accounting firm or tax counsel, company-selected and company-paid; determination final and binding absent manifest error; detailed supporting calculations delivered to both parties.

### Article V — Section 409A Compliance

- Each payment is a **separate payment** for §409A purposes
- "Termination of employment" requires a §409A **separation from service**
- Reimbursements: (a) expenses eligible in one year do not affect other years; (b) reimbursement made by end of following calendar year; (c) right may not be liquidated or exchanged
- Savings clause: parties cooperate in good faith to amend any provision causing §409A additional tax while preserving economic intent; company makes no warranty as to tax treatment and has no indemnification obligation

### Article VI — General Provisions

| Provision | Standard Terms |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Governing Law** | State of incorporation or HQ, without conflicts-of-law principles |
| **Dispute Resolution** | Arbitration (JAMS or AAA employment rules) or litigation; specify venue and cost allocation |
| **Non-Disparagement** | Mutual; exceptions for statements required by law or made in legal proceedings |
| **Supersession** | Supersedes prior CIC/severance agreements; employment agreement, equity awards, and restrictive covenants survive except as expressly modified |
| **Assignment** | Executive: no assignment without consent. Company: may assign to successor; must require successor assumption; failure to obtain assumption triggers immediate vesting and payment |
| **Amendment / Waiver** | Written instrument signed by both parties; waiver of one provision not waiver of others |
| **Severability** | Invalid provision modified to minimum extent necessary; remainder survives |
| **Notices** | Written; hand delivery, overnight courier, or certified mail; effective upon receipt |
| **Offset** | Company may offset only undisputed amounts; 10-day prior written notice required |

## Guidelines

- **Double trigger is the standard**: Avoid single-trigger equity acceleration for public companies; ISS and Glass Lewis routinely vote against it
- **280G modeling**: Run parachute payment calculations before selecting approach; full gross-up is rarely defensible in new agreements
- **§409A**: Treat each payment separately; do not rely on short-term deferral exemption for amounts that are clearly contingent on a CIC event
- **OWBPA**: Strictly observe 21/45/60-day consideration periods and 7-day revocation for executives age 40+; non-compliance invalidates the ADEA release
- **Equity plan coordination**: Confirm the agreement's acceleration and extended exercise provisions expressly supersede conflicting plan or award agreement terms; some plans require separate committee action to override
- **Pre-CIC termination tail**: Limit to terminations "at the request of" or "in connection with" a contemplated transaction — avoid overbroad language that captures unrelated terminations
- **Public companies**: Obtain compensation committee approval; confirm compliance with shareholder-approved guidelines before finalizing multiples or 280G treatment
- **State law**: California, New York, and other states impose additional limits on non-competes and may affect enforceability of restrictive covenant conditions; local WARN, mini-COBRA, and wage payment laws require separate review
