# Opinion of Seller's Counsel (APA)

Drafts a closing opinion letter from seller's counsel in an asset purchase transaction, conforming to ABA Legal Opinion Accord standards and current market practice.

## Prerequisites

1. **Asset Purchase Agreement** — fully executed, with all exhibits, schedules, and disclosure letters
2. **Ancillary agreements** — TSA, non-competes, escrow agreements, IP assignments, bills of sale
3. **Seller organizational documents** — articles/certificate of formation, bylaws/operating agreement, all amendments
4. **Authorization records** — board/manager resolutions, shareholder/member consents (if required)
5. **Good standing certificate** — current, from seller's state of organization
6. **Lien/litigation searches** — UCC, judgment, and litigation search results
7. **Officer's certificate** — factual representations from seller's officers
8. **Consents and approvals** — governmental, lender, and third-party consents obtained
9. **APA opinion requirements** — specific section of the APA specifying scope, addressees, and reliance permissions

## Output Structure

### 1. Letterhead & Addressee Block

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| Firm name, address, phone, fax | Firm records |
| Date | Closing date per APA |
| Addressee(s) | Buyer entity + buyer's counsel (exact legal names from APA) |

### 2. Introductory Paragraph

- Reference APA by full title, execution date, and all parties with legal names and jurisdictions of organization
- State role as seller's counsel
- Confirm delivery as condition precedent to closing under APA § [specify]
- Define scope boundaries

### 3. Documents Examined

Organize into categories:

- **Transaction documents** — APA, all ancillary agreements
- **Organizational documents** — articles/certificate, bylaws/operating agreement, amendments
- **Corporate records** — board resolutions, shareholder/member consents, minutes, ledgers
- **Government/third-party** — good standing certificates, tax clearances, regulatory approvals, lender consents
- **Searches** — UCC, litigation, judgment, IP registrations
- **Officer/official certificates** — describe with particularity

### 4. Assumptions & Qualifications

**Standard assumptions:**
- Genuineness of signatures; authenticity of originals; conformity of copies
- Legal capacity of natural persons
- Power, authority, and valid execution by all non-client parties
- Accuracy of officer and official certificates
- No mutual mistake of fact

**Jurisdictional scope:**
- Limited to laws of seller's state of organization + U.S. federal law
- Expressly disclaim all other state and foreign law

**Excluded matters** (unless specifically engaged):
- Tax consequences
- Environmental compliance
- ERISA / employee benefits
- IP validity or enforceability
- Antitrust analysis
- Industry-specific regulatory compliance

**Standard qualifications:**
- Bankruptcy, insolvency, reorganization, moratorium, fraudulent transfer laws
- General equitable principles (materiality, reasonableness, good faith, unconscionability) — whether at law or in equity
- Equitable remedies (specific performance, injunctive relief) subject to judicial discretion

### 5. Opinions

Draft each as a separately numbered paragraph:

| # | Opinion | Key Basis |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Organization & standing** — Seller is duly organized, validly existing, in good standing under laws of [state] | Org docs + good standing certificate |
| 2 | **Power & authority** — Seller has requisite power to own/operate assets and to execute/deliver/perform under APA and ancillary docs | Org docs, APA |
| 3 | **Due authorization** — All necessary corporate/entity action taken; no further proceedings required | Board resolutions, shareholder consent (if applicable) |
| 4 | **Due execution & delivery** — APA and ancillary docs duly executed and delivered by seller | Execution copies |
| 5 | **Enforceability** — Each transaction document constitutes legal, valid, binding obligation enforceable per its terms (subject to § 4 qualifications) | Legal analysis |
| 6 | **No conflicts** — Execution/delivery/performance does not violate: (a) org docs, (b) applicable law, (c) material agreements/orders | Org docs, searches, officer cert |
| 7 | **Consents & approvals** — All required governmental and third-party consents obtained and in effect | Consent files |
| 8 | **Securities exemption** (if applicable) — No registration required under Securities Act of 1933 or state securities laws | Facts + representations |
| 9 | **HSR compliance** (if applicable) — Required filings made; waiting periods expired/terminated | Filing records |
| 10 | **No litigation** — To knowledge after due inquiry, no pending or threatened actions challenging the transaction or materially impairing seller's ability to perform | Officer cert, litigation searches |

### 6. Closing & Reliance Limitations

- Opinions solely for benefit of named addressees in connection with this transaction
- No reliance by other persons without prior written consent
- Opinions as of date of letter only; no obligation to update
- If APA requires lender reliance — add consent language with appropriate limitations
- Execute with firm name, partner signature, printed name, title, bar admissions

## Guidelines

- **Extract all specifics from uploaded matter documents** — party names, dates, jurisdictions, consent requirements, APA opinion section references. Do not use placeholders where facts are available.
- **ABA conformity** — Follow the ABA Legal Opinion Accord and Third-Party Legal Opinion Reports (including the Accord and related Bar Association reports) for standard formulations
- **"Knowledge" qualifiers** — Define "knowledge" explicitly (typically: actual knowledge of lawyers who participated in the transaction, after inquiry of specified officers)
- **"Material" qualifiers** — Use only where APA permits; over-qualification undermines opinion value
- **Negative assurance vs. opinions** — Do not conflate; use negative assurance only where expressly requested and appropriate
- **Backstop with officer certificates** — Factual predicates for no-conflict and no-litigation opinions should rest on identified officer certificates
- **Jurisdiction-specific issues** — Check for state-specific opinion requirements (e.g., Delaware entity opinions have specific formulations; California requires attention to long-arm and franchise tax issues)
- **Do not opine beyond scope of engagement** — If excluded matters arise in APA requirements, flag for attorney review rather than expanding scope
- **Mark uncertain citations** — Use `[VERIFY]` for any statutory or regulatory citation not confirmed from source materials
