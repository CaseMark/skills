# Subordination, Non-Disturbance, and Attornment Agreement (SNDA)

Drafts a tri-party SNDA that subordinates a tenant's lease to a lender's mortgage lien while securing non-disturbance protections and establishing attornment obligations.

## Prerequisites

1. **Lease agreement** — full executed lease with all amendments, including legal names, premises description, term, rent schedule, renewal/extension options, and any existing subordination or non-disturbance language
2. **Loan documents** — promissory note, mortgage/deed of trust, and loan agreement with lender name, principal amount, interest rate, legal description, and recording information
3. **Chronological relationship** — whether lease was executed before or after mortgage recording (determines subordination necessity)
4. **Party authority** — organizational documents confirming signatory authority for all three parties

## Output Structure

### 1. Document Review & Extraction

Extract and cross-verify from all documents:

| Element | Source | Verify |
|---|---|---|
| Party legal names & addresses | Lease + Loan docs | Names match exactly |
| Property legal description | Lease + Mortgage | Consistent across docs |
| Leased premises (suite, SF, APN) | Lease | Within mortgaged property |
| Lease dates (commencement, expiration) | Lease | Current and accurate |
| Renewal/extension options | Lease | Note all options |
| Rent amounts & schedule | Lease | Current period confirmed |
| Loan amount, rate, terms | Note/Loan agreement | Match mortgage |
| Mortgage recording info (book/page, date, county) | Mortgage | Recorded correctly |
| Financing type | Loan docs | New / refi / modification |
| Security deposit amount | Lease | Actual amount held |
| TI allowance / free rent status | Lease | Performed or remaining |

Flag any discrepancies for clarification before drafting.

### 2. Recitals

Structure recitals to establish:
- Each party's exact legal name, entity type, and principal address
- Property common address + full legal description (including APN, county, state)
- Lease identification: execution date, parties, premises (suite/unit, SF), term, recorded memorandum (if any)
- Loan identification: execution date, principal amount, mortgage recording info (instrument #, book/page, date, recorder's office)
- Chronological priority statement (lease before/after mortgage)
- Statement that lender requires subordination as loan condition; tenant requests non-disturbance as consideration
- Mutual intent to establish rights, obligations, and priorities

### 3. Subordination Provisions

Draft subordination covering:
- Lease and all tenant rights subordinate to mortgage lien and all advances
- Extends to renewals, modifications, consolidations, replacements, extensions, and increases (including future advances, obligatory and optional)
- Covers full loan amount as increased from time to time (principal, interest, fees, costs, expenses)
- Effective regardless of lease/mortgage chronology
- Remains effective through lease amendments/extensions/renewals (with lender consent for material changes)
- Tenant acknowledgment that subordination is material inducement for loan
- **Conditionality**: subordination effective simultaneously with non-disturbance covenant (standard modern approach — confirm parties' intent)
- Multi-parcel provisions if applicable (partial releases, cross-collateralization)

### 4. Non-Disturbance Provisions

| Topic | Standard Treatment |
|---|---|
| Core covenant | Lender will not disturb tenant's possession if tenant not in default beyond notice/cure periods |
| Default definition | Monetary failure + material non-monetary failure, each beyond applicable cure periods |
| Prior landlord liability | Successor landlord NOT liable for prior landlord acts/omissions |
| Prepaid rent | Successor not bound by rent paid >1 month in advance |
| Offsets/defenses | Successor not subject to offsets or counterclaims against prior landlord |
| Prior landlord obligations | Successor not required to perform/pay obligations accruing before succession |
| Security deposit | Honored only to extent actually received by successor or credited in acquisition |
| TI allowance / free rent | Address whether successor assumes unperformed concessions |
| Runs with land | Binds lender's successors, assigns, foreclosure purchasers, deed-in-lieu grantees |
| Lease continuation | Continues as direct lease with successor landlord per original terms, subject to SNDA modifications |

### 5. Attornment Provisions

- Tenant unconditionally attorns to any successor landlord (foreclosure, deed in lieu, or other enforcement)
- Self-operative upon succession — no further instruments required
- Tenant to execute confirmation instrument within [10–30] days if requested (consistent with lease and SNDA, no increased obligations)
- Failure to execute confirmation does not affect automatic attornment
- All rent and charges paid to successor at designated address
- Lease continues on all original terms except as modified by SNDA
- All tenant rights, options, and privileges preserved subject to non-disturbance limitations

### 6. Representations & Warranties

**From Landlord:**
- Lease in full force and effect; no modifications except as scheduled
- Authority to execute SNDA (duly authorized)
- No existing defaults by landlord or tenant
- Rent current; none prepaid beyond current month (except as disclosed)
- No assignment of lease or rents except to lender

**From Tenant:**
- Lease (with scheduled amendments) is entire agreement for premises
- No defenses, offsets, or counterclaims against rent payment
- Possession accepted; premises comply with lease requirements
- No assignment or subletting except as disclosed
- No notice of prior lease/rent assignment except to lender
- All conditions precedent satisfied; TI/concession status confirmed
- Entity status and authority to execute

**From Lender:**
- Authority to execute SNDA and grant non-disturbance covenant
- Status as holder/beneficiary of mortgage

### 7. Lender Cure Rights

- Tenant must send landlord-default notice to lender simultaneously with (or promptly after) landlord notice
- Tenant may not terminate lease or exercise offset/deduction without giving lender notice and cure opportunity

| Default Type | Lender Cure Period |
|---|---|
| Monetary | 30 days after expiration of landlord's cure period |
| Non-monetary | 60–90 days (or longer if cure requires possession, provided lender commences within initial period and diligently pursues) |

- Lender may (but is not obligated to) enter premises to cure; tenant shall permit entry and cooperate
- Lender cure does not constitute lease assumption or ongoing obligation (unless/until lender succeeds to landlord's interest)

### 8. Lease Modification Restrictions

- No modification, amendment, termination, surrender, rent reduction, payment extension, or offset grant without lender's prior written consent
- Exceptions: ministerial amendments; modifications increasing tenant obligations or improving lender security
- Unauthorized modifications void as against lender; lease continues on original terms
- Address whether exercise of existing lease options (renewal, expansion, contraction) requires lender consent

### 9. Administrative Provisions

Include standard provisions:
- **Notices**: Full addresses with attention lines; delivery by personal delivery, certified/registered mail, overnight courier, email (if agreed); effective upon receipt/refusal or 3 business days after mailing; change-of-address mechanism
- **Governing law**: State where property is located
- **Jurisdiction**: Exclusive venue in state/federal courts in property's county; consent to personal jurisdiction; waiver of venue/forum non conveniens objections
- **Counterparts**: Multiple counterparts; electronic/PDF signatures valid
- **Amendments**: Written instrument signed by all three parties required
- **Waivers**: Written, signed by party against whom enforced; no continuing waiver
- **Severability**: Invalid provisions ineffective only to extent of invalidity; reformation clause
- **Conflict resolution**: SNDA controls over lease on SNDA subject matters
- **Successors & assigns**: Binding on all successors and assigns
- **Recordation**: Any party may record; parties cooperate in execution of recordation documents

### 10. Execution & Signature Blocks

Signature blocks for all three parties with:
- Signature line, printed name, title/capacity, date
- Entity-appropriate execution (corporate officers + seal or authority certificate; LLC manager/member; partnership general partners)
- Notarial acknowledgments in form required by property-state law (required if recording)

## Guidelines

- Subordination should be **conditioned on** non-disturbance — confirm this is parties' intent before making self-executing
- Security deposit and TI allowance treatment are the most frequently negotiated provisions — present options to user
- Verify state-specific notarial acknowledgment requirements before finalizing
- Cross-check all defined terms for consistent usage throughout
- Include table of contents if document exceeds 10 pages
- All cross-references, exhibits, and schedules must be verified for accuracy
- Flag any discrepancies between lease and loan documents before proceeding with draft
- Recording requirements vary by jurisdiction — confirm county recorder requirements
