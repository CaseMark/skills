# Consent Judgment and Injunction

Drafts an enforceable consent judgment with injunctive relief that memorializes a negotiated resolution while preserving the court's enforcement authority.

## Prerequisites

1. **Complaint and pleadings** — full party names, capacities, case number, court/division
2. **Settlement agreement or term sheet** — monetary amounts, payment schedules, admissions/denials, scope of injunctive relief
3. **Correspondence between counsel** — draft terms, negotiation history
4. **Governing contracts** — any underlying IP licenses, assignments, or agreements at issue
5. **Local court rules** — formatting, e-filing requirements, consent judgment approval procedures for the specific jurisdiction

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Court name | Full name with division; match local formatting rules |
| Case number | Exactly as docketed |
| Party names | Match original complaint; include designations (Plaintiff/Defendant) |
| Document title | "Consent Judgment and Injunction" or "Agreed Judgment and Permanent Injunction" per local practice |

### 2. Recitals (WHEREAS Clauses)

Include findings establishing:
- [ ] Subject-matter and personal jurisdiction
- [ ] Nature of claims and defenses (neutral framing)
- [ ] Voluntary agreement to resolve without trial
- [ ] Representation by competent counsel
- [ ] Understanding of binding nature
- [ ] Statutory/procedural authority for entry (cite applicable FRCP or state equivalent)
- [ ] Whether resolution is on the merits or without merits adjudication

### 3. Substantive Judgment Terms

For each term, state with specificity:

**Monetary:**
- Award amount, payment schedule, interest rate, default consequences
- Costs/fees allocation — each party bears own, or shifting provisions

**Non-Monetary:**
- Property transfers, document deliveries, specific performance obligations
- Concrete deadlines and performance standards
- Admissions or denials of liability (if any) — state unambiguously

### 4. Injunctive Relief

Draft with Rule 65(d) specificity requirements:

| Component | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| Prohibited conduct | Specific, measurable actions the enjoined party must not take |
| Required conduct | Exact actions, by whom, by when, to what standard |
| Geographic scope | Define precisely |
| Duration | Permanent or time-limited; state modification standard |
| Compliance monitoring | Reporting requirements, inspection rights, third-party oversight if applicable |
| Enforcement | Contempt procedures, modification/dissolution process |

> **Critical**: Avoid vague language ("shall not engage in unfair practices"). Every prohibition or requirement must be concrete enough for a contempt proceeding.

### 5. Releases and Waivers

- [ ] Mutual release scope — identify released claims, causes of action, and parties with specificity
- [ ] Known and unknown claims — include Cal. Civ. Code § 1542 waiver or equivalent if applicable [VERIFY]
- [ ] Appeal waiver — explicit if agreed
- [ ] Carve-outs — claims against non-parties, unrelated matters, or enforcement of the judgment itself
- [ ] Adequate consideration acknowledged

### 6. Jurisdiction Retention

```
The Court retains continuing jurisdiction to:
(a) enforce the terms of this Consent Judgment and Injunction;
(b) resolve disputes regarding interpretation or compliance;
(c) modify the injunction upon proper showing of changed circumstances;
(d) adjudicate motions for contempt or other enforcement proceedings.
```

Also address:
- Notice requirements before enforcement motions
- Meet-and-confer obligations
- Prevailing-party fees in enforcement actions
- Expedited relief procedures for imminent violations

### 7. Execution and Approval

**Signature blocks:**
- All parties — with authority-to-bind language
- Attorneys of record — name, bar number, contact information
- Date lines for each signatory

**Judicial approval section:**
- Proposed findings: fair, reasonable, voluntary, entered with advice of counsel
- Separate approval line and date for the court
- Comply with e-filing requirements (e.g., `/s/` signature format if required)

### 8. Formatting

- Numbered paragraphs throughout
- Full caption on page 1; abbreviated caption on subsequent pages
- Certificate of service if required by local rules
- Consistent defined terms; proper citation format

## Guidelines

- **Negotiated resolution framing** — unless parties agree otherwise, recitals should state this is not an adjudication on the merits
- **FRE 408 awareness** — do not include settlement negotiation details that could be used against either party in related proceedings
- **Enforceability check** — verify all terms comply with applicable substantive law and public policy; courts can refuse to enter consent judgments with unconscionable terms
- **IP-specific considerations** — for trademark cases, address mark usage going forward; for patent cases, address license-back provisions; for trade secret cases, ensure injunction scope doesn't function as an unenforceable non-compete
- **Jurisdiction-specific requirements** — some courts require separate proposed orders, Tunney Act procedures (antitrust) [VERIFY], or minor/incapacitated party court approval
- **Do not** include boilerplate integration clauses that conflict with the court's retained jurisdiction
- **Do not** draft release language that inadvertently releases claims the parties intend to preserve
