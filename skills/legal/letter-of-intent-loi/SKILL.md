# Letter of Intent (LOI)

Drafts and outputs a transaction LOI that advances execution while preserving negotiating flexibility.

## Prerequisites

1. Verified party identities, legal entity forms, and governing jurisdictions.
2. Transaction type and structure (asset sale, stock sale, merger, investment, JV, option).
3. Agreed economics: purchase/investment amount, payment schedule, earn-outs, escrows, caps.
4. Commercial milestones: signing target, exclusivity window, due diligence period, closing date.
5. Conditions precedent list and responsibility allocation (regulatory approvals, financing, consents).
6. Desired binding terms and non-binding defaults (confidentiality, no-shop, expenses, governing law).
7. Known constraints: antitrust, sector regulations, lender or board approvals, approvals committees.
8. Preferred execution method (wet signature vs electronic, counterparts).

## Output Structure / Process

Use this sequence and produce an audit-ready LOI in business-letter format.

### 1) Deal Intake Validation

- [ ] Party names and addresses complete and consistent.
- [ ] Transaction purpose stated in one sentence with no ambiguity.
- [ ] Economic terms fully defined and internally consistent.
- [ ] Timeline and sequencing mapped to CPs.
- [ ] Definitive documents identified (stock purchase agreement, merger agreement, ancillary docs).
- [ ] Binding clause set explicitly chosen by user.
- [ ] Default legal governing law selected and jurisdictional basis confirmed.

### 2) Clause Matrix

| Section | Required | Purpose | Binding?
|---|---|---|---|
| Date / Parties / Purpose | Required | Identify transaction and intent | Non-binding |
| Transaction Overview | Required | Define target, structure, included/excluded assets | Non-binding |
| Principal Terms | Required | Price, structure, adjustments, timing, conditions | Non-binding unless stated |
| Conditions Precedent | Required | Due diligence, approvals, financing, third-party consents | Non-binding |
| Definitive Agreement Process | Required | Negotiate and execute within target date | Mixed |
| Exclusivity / No-Shop | Optional | Prevent other buyer/investor talks | Usually binding |
| Confidentiality | Optional/Required | Protect information and process | Usually binding |
| Expenses / Costs | Optional | Allocation of diligence/transaction fees | Usually binding |
| Liability Limitation / No-Action Clause | Optional | Clarify no commitment absent closing | Usually binding |
| Governing Law / Disputes | Required | Choice of law and forum | Binding |
| Signatures / Counterparts | Required | Execution mechanics | Binding as specified |

### 3) Drafting Rules

- Default rule: treat LOI as non-binding except explicitly designated provisions.
- Required always include:
  - A plain-language binding-effect section.
  - Explicitly carved-out binding clauses list.
  - No-liability clause for failure to close.
  - Good-faith negotiation language for definitive agreements.
- Keep CPs objective, verifiable, and assignable to each side.
- Do not embed tax opinions; add a placeholder for tax allocations.

### 4) Template Output

```text
[Date]
[Party A Name]
[Party B Name]

RE: Letter of Intent – Proposed [Transaction Type]

[Opening: parties + transaction purpose]

1. Transaction Overview
2. Transaction Structure
3. Principal Terms
4. Conditions Precedent
5. Binding Commitments (Confidentiality / Exclusivity / Costs / Governing Law)
6. Definitive Agreements and Timeline
7. Termination and Liability
8. Miscellaneous (integration, notices, counsel, no assignment)
9. Signature Blocks
```

### 5) Quality Gate Before Finalizing

- Check defined terms glossary for consistency (Transaction, Closing Date, Effective Date, CPs).
- Ensure all amounts have currency, basis, and payment schedule.
- Confirm non-compete references are only included if jurisdictionally supportable and requested.
- Verify any public-company-sensitive language has legal/compliance review placeholder.

## Guidelines

- Use clean drafting style; avoid overcommitment language in non-binding portions.
- Preserve negotiation flexibility: definitive agreement controls if conflict.
- Do not omit remedies for breach of expressly binding clauses.
- For heavily regulated sectors, require counsel review notes on approvals and filing requirements.
- For cross-border transactions, flag foreign-law and conflict-of-laws risk for localized counsel review.
- If anti-circumvention concern exists, add explicit carve-outs and enforcement language for exclusivity.
- Preserve separate NDA references and hierarchy (if already signed, state LOI is supplemental).
