# GMP Standard Operating Procedure

Produces a GMP-compliant SOP that is inspection-ready and operationally executable.

## Prerequisites

1. **Process scope** — product type, dosage form, facility classification, markets.
2. **Applicable regs** — FDA CGMP, ICH, WHO, PIC/S, EU GMP, local requirements.
3. **QMS context** — document numbering, approval matrix, related SOPs.
4. **Equipment list** — IDs, calibration/maintenance/qualification status.
5. **Record systems** — paper vs electronic, Part 11 status.
6. **Inspection history** — FDA 483s, warning letters, CAPA.
7. **SME/approver list** — production, QA, QC, engineering, management.

## Output Structure / Process

**1) Document Control Page (required)**  
Use this table and fill all fields.

| Field | Required Content |
|---|---|
| SOP Title | Precise scope (process/area) |
| SOP ID | Follow site numbering convention |
| Version | Numeric or semantic |
| Effective Date | Approved use date |
| Supersedes | Prior SOP ID/version |
| Prepared By | Name, role, date |
| Reviewed By | QA, SMEs |
| Approved By | QA/Management |
| Distribution | Controlled locations |
| Related SOPs | IDs and titles |
| Electronic Signatures | Part 11 status |

**2) Purpose and Scope (template)**  
```
Purpose:
This SOP establishes controlled steps for [process] to ensure GMP compliance under [reg citations].

Scope:
Applies to [areas, equipment, product types, personnel]. Excludes [exclusions with rationale].
Interfaces: [related SOP IDs].
```

**3) Regulatory Basis (cite and verify)**  
List only applicable regulations and guidance. Use [VERIFY] if unsure.

| Authority | Citation |
|---|---|
| FDA CGMP | 21 CFR Parts 210 and 211 |
| Electronic records | 21 CFR Part 11 |
| ICH | Q7 (API) [VERIFY], Q9, Q10 |
| WHO | TRS GMP guidance [VERIFY] |
| PIC/S | GMP Guide [VERIFY] |
| EU | EU GMP Guide + Annexes (as applicable) [VERIFY] |

**4) Definitions and Abbreviations**  
Provide GMP-aligned definitions. Use authoritative sources and keep consistent with site QMS.  
Include: CPP, CQA, deviation, OOS, OOT, batch record, validation, qualification, calibration.

**5) Responsibilities Matrix**

| Role | Duties | Decisions/Approvals | Records Owned |
|---|---|---|---|
| Production | Execute SOP steps | Stop work for deviations | Batch records |
| QA | Approve SOP, review deviations | Release/closure authority | QA review logs |
| QC | Testing, sampling | OOS disposition | Lab records |
| Engineering | Maintenance/calibration | Equipment readiness | PM/calibration logs |
| Management | Resource allocation | Final approval | Training matrix |

**6) Materials, Equipment, and Utilities**

| Item | ID/Spec | Qualification Status | Calibration/PM | Acceptance Criteria |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Equipment | Asset ID | IQ/OQ/PQ status | Frequency | Tolerance |
| Materials | Grade/spec | Supplier status | Storage | COA required |
| Utilities | Water/HVAC/gases | Monitoring | Limits | Action levels |

**7) Procedure (step-by-step)**  
Use a step table. Each step must specify role, action, records, and acceptance.

| Step | Role | Action | CPP/CQA | Hold Point | Records |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Production | [imperative action] | [range] | QA if required | [record] |
| 2 | QC | [sample/test] | [spec] | QA review | [record] |
| 3 | QA | [verification] | N/A | Release | [record] |

Rules for procedure steps:  
- Include decision points and stop-work criteria.  
- Define acceptance ranges for CPPs/CQAs.  
- State required PPE/cleanroom behavior where applicable.  
- Require immediate documentation at time of action.

**8) In-Process Controls and Monitoring**

| Control | Frequency | Method | Acceptance | Action if Out of Range |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Control] | [per batch/time] | [method] | [spec] | [action] |

**9) Deviations, OOS/OOT, and CAPA (checklist)**  
- Document deviation within same shift or defined timeframe.  
- Quarantine affected material/lots.  
- Initiate investigation with root cause analysis.  
- Assess impact on product quality and patient safety.  
- Implement CAPA with effectiveness checks.  
- QA disposition and release decision documented.

**10) Documentation and Records**

| Record | Owner | System | Review Timeline | Retention |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Batch record | Production | Paper/eQMS | [timeframe] | Per 21 CFR 211.180 [VERIFY] |
| Calibration log | Engineering | Paper/eQMS | [timeframe] | [policy] |
| Training record | QA/HR | LMS/eQMS | [timeframe] | [policy] |

Part 11 controls if electronic: validation, audit trails, access control, e-signatures.

**11) Training and Qualification**  
- Initial training before execution.  
- Retraining on revision.  
- Qualification requirements for roles with critical steps.  
- Training effectiveness documented.

**12) Change Control and Revision History**

| Version | Effective Date | Change Summary | Rationale | Approvers |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1.0 | [date] | Initial release | [reason] | [names] |

**13) References**  
List all cited standards with version/date. Mark uncertain items with [VERIFY].

**14) Appendices**  
Include controlled templates as appendices:  
- SOP execution checklist  
- Batch record or log sheet template  
- Deviation report template  
- In-process control log  
- Equipment cleaning log

## Guidelines

- Use imperative, unambiguous language; avoid advisory phrasing.  
- Verify all citations and current versions; mark uncertain ones with [VERIFY].  
- Align scope and responsibilities with actual organizational roles.  
- Maintain segregation between production and QA/QC functions.  
- Ensure record retention meets the strictest applicable requirement.  
- Add jurisdiction-specific annexes when distributing outside the U.S.
