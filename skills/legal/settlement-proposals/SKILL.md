# Settlement Proposals Summary

Produces a chronological record of settlement negotiations, proposals, and agreements from litigation case files.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case documents** — demand letters, offer letters, counteroffers, mediation summaries, email exchanges discussing settlement terms
2. **Executed agreements** (if any) — final settlement agreements, releases, dismissal stipulations
3. **Case posture context** — key rulings, discovery status, expert reports, trial date

## Output Structure

### 1. Matter Overview

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Caption | Parties, court, case number |
| Current phase | Discovery / pre-trial / settlement |
| Key dates | Filing, trial date, settlement deadlines |
| Parties at table | Who is negotiating, counsel identified |

### 2. Negotiation Chronology

For each substantive communication, create an entry:

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Date | Date of proposal/communication |
| From → To | Proposing party → receiving party |
| Type | Initial demand / offer / counteroffer / mediator's proposal |
| Key monetary terms | Gross amount, fees/costs allocation, net to client |
| Non-monetary terms | Injunctive relief, behavioral commitments, structural changes |
| Conditions/contingencies | Deadlines, approvals, performance conditions |
| Response | Accepted / rejected / countered (with date) |
| Source | Document name, page/paragraph cite |

Use a separate entry for each substantive proposal. Preserve exact dollar figures and quoted language for critical terms.

### 3. Payment Structure (if applicable)

For any proposal involving structured payments:

- Lump sum vs. installment breakdown
- Payment schedule with dates and amounts
- Interest rate and calculation method
- Security or guarantees for future payments
- Distinguish: gross settlement / attorney's fees / costs / net to client

### 4. ADR Summary

If mediation or other ADR was used:

- Process type, date(s), neutral identified
- Mediator's proposals (if any, and if not privileged)
- Session dynamics to the extent documented and not subject to mediation privilege
- Outcome of each session

### 5. Strategic Context

Note developments that influenced negotiations:

- Court rulings on dispositive or significant motions
- Discovery revelations shifting case strength
- Expert opinions or reports
- Changes in either party's litigation posture
- External pressures (publicity, business relationships, costs)

### 6. Settlement Evaluation

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Probability of success on merits | High / moderate / low with basis |
| Damages range at trial | Low–high estimate |
| Remaining litigation costs | Estimated through trial |
| Time to resolution (trial vs. settlement) | Comparison |
| Non-economic factors | Publicity, relationships, emotional toll |
| Comparable settlements | Similar cases/jurisdictions if available |

### 7. Executed Agreement Terms (if settled)

- Parties' obligations and performance deadlines
- Scope of releases and dismissals
- Confidentiality provisions
- Post-settlement dispute resolution mechanisms
- Unusual or particularly favorable provisions
- Ambiguities or provisions requiring future attention
- Implementation status and outstanding obligations
- Case dismissal status

### 8. Status & Next Steps

**If ongoing:** Pending decisions, upcoming deadlines, client recommendations, open items.

**If resolved:** Implementation checklist, outstanding post-settlement obligations, final disposition status.

## Guidelines

- **Chronological order** — always present negotiations in time sequence
- **Cite precisely** — document name, date, page/paragraph for every term referenced
- **Quote critical language** — preserve exact wording for key provisions rather than paraphrasing
- **Objectivity** — present terms without advocacy; reserve evaluation for the Strategic Context and Evaluation sections
- **Confidentiality** — if settlement is confidential, mark the document accordingly and ensure compliance with confidentiality obligations
- **Multi-audience clarity** — must be detailed enough for counsel, understandable for clients, and defensible if reviewed by courts (class actions, minors, incapacitated persons) or insurers
- **Tables and charts** — use comparison tables for multiple proposals; use schedules for structured payments
- **Currency and dates** — consistent formatting throughout (e.g., $1,250,000.00; January 15, 2026)
- **Mediation privilege** — do not disclose privileged mediation communications; flag any content that may implicate privilege concerns
