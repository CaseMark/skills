# Informed Consent Form — Clinical Trial

Drafts a regulatory-compliant ICF covering all 21 CFR 50.25 required elements, written in plain language suitable for research subjects.

## Prerequisites

1. **Protocol details** — study title, protocol number, phase (I/II/III/IV), sponsor name, IND number if applicable
2. **Investigational product** — name, mechanism, dosing, route of administration, known risk profile
3. **Study design** — randomization rationale, arms (including placebo), visit schedule, total duration
4. **Site information** — principal investigator name/title, institution, IRB name and contact
5. **Risk/benefit data** — preclinical and prior clinical data supporting known risk frequencies and severities
6. **Compensation/injury policy** — what sponsor covers for research-related injury; any participation payment

### 2. Study Information

**Purpose**
- Plain-language description of the disease/condition under study
- What the study is designed to learn; hypothesis being tested
- Trial phase and how this fits into the research continuum
- Approximate number of subjects (site-level and total)
- Statement: *"This is a research study, not standard medical treatment."*

**Procedures** (chronological)
- Screening eligibility assessments
- Randomization explanation: probability of each arm, including placebo odds
- Visit-by-visit table:

| Visit | Timing | Duration | Procedures |
|---|---|---|---|
| Screening | Week −2 | ~2 hrs | Labs, physical exam, eligibility review |
| Baseline | Day 1 | ~3 hrs | IP administration, vitals, ECG |
| Follow-up | Weeks 4, 8, 12 | ~1 hr each | Labs, safety assessments |
| End of Study | Week 16 | ~2 hrs | Final assessments, unblinding |

- Flag procedures that are research-only vs. standard-of-care
- Note any special preparation requirements (fasting, hold medications, etc.)

**Risks and Discomforts**

Organize by category:

| Risk Category | Description | Frequency | Severity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Investigational product | [e.g., nausea, hepatotoxicity] | [common/rare] | [mild/serious] |
| Study procedures | [e.g., venipuncture bruising] | Common | Mild |
| Placebo exposure | Risk of forgoing active therapy | N/A | [study-specific] |
| Reproductive risks | [teratogenicity if applicable] | Unknown | Potentially serious |
| Privacy/psychosocial | Time burden, incidental findings | Low | Mild |

- Include: *"There may be unknown risks. You will be told of any new information that may affect your decision to continue."*
- State what medical care and compensation are available if research-related injury occurs; specify what is **not** covered.

**Potential Benefits**
- Direct benefit to subject: state honestly whether benefit is possible, probable, or not expected
- If benefit possible: probability estimate from prior data; note it is not guaranteed
- Societal benefit: knowledge advancement, future patient impact
- Payments/free study medication: list but note these are not medical benefits
- Close with: *"Choosing not to participate will not affect your access to standard medical care."*

### 3. Subject Rights

**Confidentiality**
- Data protections in place (coded identifiers, encrypted storage, limited access)
- Who may access identifiable records:
  - Research team
  - IRB / Ethics Committee
  - FDA and other regulatory authorities
  - Study sponsor / monitors / auditors
  - Data Safety Monitoring Board (if applicable)
- Circumstances requiring disclosure (mandatory reporting, court order)
- Publication policy: individual subjects will not be identified
- Data retention period and disposition at study close
- **HIPAA Authorization** (if applicable): describe scope of PHI use/disclosure; note authorization is required for study participation

**Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal**
- Participation is entirely voluntary; refusal or withdrawal does not affect medical care or benefits
- Subject may withdraw at any time without penalty
- Process to withdraw: contact [PI name] at [phone]
- Data collected prior to withdrawal may be retained unless subject requests deletion and feasibility permits
- Investigator/sponsor/IRB may remove subject without consent if: safety concern, protocol non-compliance, or study termination
- Subject will be informed of new findings that may affect willingness to continue

### 4. Consent Statement (first person)

> *"I have read this consent form (or had it read to me). The study has been explained to me. I have had the opportunity to ask questions and have received satisfactory answers. I understand that participation is voluntary and I may withdraw at any time without penalty. I agree to take part in [Study Title], Protocol [Number]."*

## Guidelines

- **Reading level**: Target ≤ 8th grade; avoid jargon; define technical terms on first use
- **Required elements**: Verify all 21 CFR 50.25(a) basic elements and any applicable 50.25(b) additional elements are present before IRB submission
- **No exculpatory language**: Do not include any waiver of legal rights or release of liability for negligence (21 CFR 50.25(a)(8) prohibition)
- **Therapeutic misconception**: Explicitly distinguish research from treatment; do not overstate likelihood of direct benefit
- **Placebo disclosure**: If placebo arm exists, explain probability and risk of forgoing active therapy
- **Vulnerable populations**: Add required protections if enrolling pregnant women, children, prisoners, or cognitively impaired subjects (21 CFR Parts 50 Subparts C, D)
- **Formatting**: ≥12pt font, clear section headings, page numbers, version/date footer on every page; typical length 8–15 pages depending on complexity
- **IRB submission**: Final ICF must be approved by the IRB before use; retain IRB-stamped version as the operative document
- **[VERIFY]**: Confirm current ICH E6(R3) GCP requirements align with your jurisdiction's adoption status before finalizing
