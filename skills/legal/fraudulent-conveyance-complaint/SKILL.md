# Fraudulent Conveyance Complaint

Drafts a litigation-ready complaint to avoid a debtor's fraudulent asset transfer and recover value for creditors under UFTA, UVTA, or state equivalents.

## Prerequisites

1. **Creditor's claim** — nature of debt, date incurred, amount, whether reduced to judgment
2. **Transfer details** — asset description (with legal description if real property), transfer date, transferee identity, stated and actual consideration
3. **Debtor financial condition** — evidence of insolvency at time of transfer (balance sheet, cash-flow, or unreasonably small capital)
4. **Badges of fraud** — insider relationship, retention of control, concealment, timing relative to litigation, adequacy of consideration
5. **Jurisdiction** — governing statute (UFTA, UVTA, or state-specific); federal vs. state court; whether filed as adversary proceeding in bankruptcy

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption
- Court name, district/county, case number placeholder
- Full legal names of all plaintiffs and defendants
- Title: "Complaint for Fraudulent Conveyance" or "Complaint to Avoid Fraudulent Transfer"
- FRCP 10 (federal) or applicable local rules (state)

### 2. Parties

| Party | Required Allegations |
|---|---|
| Plaintiff-creditor | Legal name, entity type, basis for standing, nature and amount of debt |
| Debtor-transferor | Identity, entity type, insider status if applicable |
| Transferee | Identity, consideration paid, knowledge of debtor's intent |
| Subsequent transferees | Downstream recipients; good-faith purchaser status if asserted |

### 3. Jurisdiction & Venue

- **Federal diversity**: 28 U.S.C. § 1332 — plead citizenship of each party, amount > $75,000
- **Federal bankruptcy**: 28 U.S.C. §§ 157, 1334 — adversary proceeding under FRBP 7001
- **State court**: cite jurisdictional statute and applicable amount-in-controversy threshold
- **Personal jurisdiction**: residence, principal place of business, situs of property, or where transfer executed
- **Venue**: defendant's residence, where cause of action arose, or where property located

### 4. Factual Allegations

Plead in numbered paragraphs, chronologically:

1. Origin, nature, and amount of plaintiff's claim; date debt incurred; judgment status
2. Whether claim is pre-transfer or post-transfer (determines available theories)
3. Debtor's financial condition at time of transfer (plead all that apply):
   - Liabilities exceeded assets at fair valuation (balance sheet insolvency)
   - Unable to pay debts as they came due (cash-flow insolvency)
   - Transfer left debtor with unreasonably small capital
4. Transfer description: asset, date, transferee, stated consideration, fair market value
5. Badges of fraud:

| Badge | Factual Allegation |
|---|---|
| Insider transfer | Identify relationship (family member, officer, affiliate, controlled entity) |
| Retention of control | Debtor continued to use, possess, or control transferred asset post-transfer |
| Concealment | Transfer not recorded, disclosed, or reported to creditors |
| Transfer of substantially all assets | Describe scope of asset depletion relative to debtor's total estate |
| Inadequate consideration | State FMV vs. consideration actually received |
| Timing | Transfer occurred [X days] before/after debt incurred or suit filed |
| Prior pattern | Prior transfers or history of asset-shifting |

6. Transferee's actual or constructive knowledge of debtor's fraudulent intent (if applicable)

### 5. Causes of Action

#### Count I — Actual Fraudulent Transfer
- Statutory basis: UVTA § 4(a)(1) [VERIFY state codification]
- Elements: (1) transfer by debtor; (2) with actual intent to hinder, delay, or defraud any creditor
- Incorporate badges of fraud and any direct evidence of intent (debtor statements, patterns)
- Available to both present and future creditors

#### Count II — Constructive Fraudulent Transfer (Insolvency)
- Statutory basis: UVTA § 4(a)(2) [VERIFY state codification]
- Elements: (1) transfer without reasonably equivalent value; (2) debtor was insolvent at time or became insolvent as a result
- Plead in the alternative to Count I

#### Count III — Constructive Fraudulent Transfer (Insider Antecedent Debt) *(if applicable)*
- Statutory basis: UVTA § 5(b) [VERIFY state codification]
- Elements: (1) transfer to insider; (2) for antecedent debt; (3) debtor insolvent at time; (4) insider had reasonable cause to believe debtor was insolvent

#### Additional Claims *(as applicable)*
- Conspiracy to defraud creditors (multiple coordinating parties)
- Aiding and abetting (knowing third-party assistance)
- Successor liability (transferee assumed debtor's business operations)
- Lis pendens (real property — file with complaint to provide record notice)

### 6. Prayer for Relief

- [ ] Avoidance of the transfer to the extent necessary to satisfy plaintiff's claim
- [ ] Recovery of transferred property or its value from transferee
- [ ] Money judgment against debtor for underlying debt (if not already obtained)
- [ ] Money judgment against transferee to extent of asset value received
- [ ] Attachment, garnishment, or appointment of receiver to preserve assets pending judgment
- [ ] Preliminary injunction against further dissipation *(if imminent risk)*
- [ ] Pre- and post-judgment interest at applicable statutory rate
- [ ] Costs of suit
- [ ] Attorney's fees *(if permitted by statute or fee-shifting provision — verify jurisdiction)*
- [ ] Such other and further relief as the court deems just and proper

### 7. Signature Block & Verification

- Attorney: name, bar number, firm, address, phone, email; FRCP 11 or state-equivalent certification
- **Verification**: many jurisdictions require verification in fraudulent conveyance actions, particularly when seeking provisional remedies (attachment, TRO) — confirm local rules; some require notarization, others accept unsworn declaration under penalty of perjury

## Guidelines

- **UFTA vs. UVTA**: Confirm governing statute — most states have adopted UVTA (2014) but effective dates vary; [VERIFY] current state codification before citing section numbers
- **Statute of limitations**: UVTA § 9 — 4 years from transfer for actual fraud; 4 years (or 1 year after discovery, whichever is later) for constructive fraud [VERIFY state equivalent]
- **Bankruptcy intersection**: 11 U.S.C. § 548 reaches transfers within 2 years of petition date; trustee may also invoke state UFTA/UVTA via § 544(b) to capture transfers within longer state limitations periods
- **Good-faith transferee defense**: A transferee who took for value and in good faith may retain the transfer — plead facts negating good faith where known; affects remedy (avoidance vs. money judgment)
- **Reasonably equivalent value**: Distinct from fair market value — courts apply totality-of-circumstances analysis; inadequate price alone is not per se constructive fraud without insolvency
- **Provisional remedies**: If dissipation risk is imminent, prepare TRO/preliminary injunction motion for concurrent filing; allege irreparable harm and likelihood of success on the merits
- **Insider definition**: UVTA § 1(8) defines insiders broadly — includes relatives, general partners, directors, officers, and affiliates [VERIFY state definition]
