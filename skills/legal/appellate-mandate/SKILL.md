# Appellate Mandate

Drafts the formal order issued by an appellate court that concludes the appeal and directs the trial court to implement the appellate decision.

## Prerequisites

1. **Appellate opinion/judgment** — the final decision with exact disposition language
2. **Case caption** — all party names and designations (appellant/appellee/petitioner/respondent) as they appear in the appellate record
3. **Case numbers** — both appellate and trial court docket numbers
4. **Procedural history** — rehearing petitions, certiorari filings, stay orders, extensions of time
5. **Remand instructions** — any specific directives from the appellate opinion on scope of further proceedings

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption & Identification

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Court name | Full formal name of issuing appellate court |
| Receiving court | Full formal name of trial court |
| Party designations | Mirror appellate record exactly |
| Case numbers | Both appellate and trial court numbers |
| Opening recital | "This mandate is issued pursuant to this Court's judgment dated [date]..." |

### 2. Incorporation of Decision

- Attach or incorporate certified copy of the appellate opinion/judgment
- Reference opinion by case name, court, date, and reporter citation if published

### 3. Directive Section

Use standard formulations matching the actual disposition:

```
IT IS ORDERED that the judgment of the [trial court] is [AFFIRMED / REVERSED / REVERSED AND REMANDED / MODIFIED / DISMISSED].
```

For remands, specify with particularity:

| Remand Type | Required Specificity |
|---|---|
| New trial | Whether all issues or only specified matters |
| Entry of judgment | In whose favor and on what basis |
| Partial remand | Which issues are foreclosed vs. open |
| Conditional (e.g., remittitur) | Exact conditions and implementation steps |
| Damages/remedies | Any calculation instructions or caps from opinion |

Extract and incorporate verbatim any limiting language from the opinion regarding scope of proceedings on remand.

### 4. Effective Date & Procedural Conditions

Calculate issuance date per applicable appellate rules:

| Jurisdiction Type | Typical Auto-Issuance Period |
|---|---|
| Federal (FRAP 41) | 7 days after time to petition for rehearing expires |
| Most state courts | 21–30 days after final judgment |

Address in sequence:
1. Whether rehearing petition was filed → if denied, state date of denial
2. Whether certiorari or discretionary review was sought → note any stay of mandate
3. Whether stay has been lifted or review denied → include dates
4. Any extensions of time that tolled the issuance period
5. Any order for expedited issuance

### 5. Certification & Authentication

```
CERTIFICATION

The undersigned Clerk of [Court Name] hereby certifies that the
foregoing is a true and correct copy of the judgment/opinion of this
Court entered on [date] in the above-captioned matter, and that this
mandate is issued in accordance with [applicable rule].

[SEAL]

_______________________________
[Name], Clerk of Court
Date of Issuance: [date]
```

Include filing instructions for trial court clerk:
- Docketing requirements
- Service obligations on parties
- Any required acknowledgment or action timeline

## Guidelines

- **Verbatim precision**: Copy disposition language and remand instructions exactly from the appellate opinion — do not paraphrase
- **Delineate authority**: Explicitly state which matters are conclusively determined (beyond trial court reconsideration) vs. which remain open
- **Cross-reference all identifiers**: Verify every case number, party name, and date against the appellate record
- **Jurisdiction-specific rules**: Confirm the mandate issuance timeline under the governing appellate rules (federal vs. state variations)
- **Conditional directives**: Capture any if/then conditions from the opinion (e.g., "new trial unless plaintiff accepts remittitur of $X within Y days")
- **No interpretive gloss**: The mandate is a ministerial document — do not add analysis or characterization beyond what the opinion states
- **Mark uncertain citations**: Use `[VERIFY]` for any rule or statutory citation not confirmed against the source
