# Depository Account Agreement

Drafts a regulation-compliant depository account agreement governing the financial institution–depositor relationship for checking, savings, money market, or CD accounts.

## Prerequisites

1. **Account type** — checking, savings, money market, CD, or specialized deposit product
2. **Institution details** — name, charter state, federal regulator (OCC / FDIC / Federal Reserve)
3. **Account features** — interest-bearing (yes/no), tiered rates, minimum balance thresholds, overdraft protection options
4. **Fee schedule** — maintenance fees, overdraft/NSF fees, ATM fees, wire fees, and all other applicable charges
5. **EFT services offered** — ATM, debit card, ACH, online/mobile banking, bill pay, remote deposit capture
6. **Arbitration policy** — binding arbitration required or jury trial waiver (if any)
7. **Governing state law** — state where institution is chartered or account is maintained
8. **Existing agreements or policies** (if any) — upload for extraction of institution-specific terms

## Output Structure

### 1. Account Type and Interest Terms

- Account classification and product description
- For interest-bearing accounts:

| Element | Required Disclosure |
|---|---|
| APY | Current rate as a percentage |
| Calculation method | Simple or compound |
| Compounding frequency | Daily / monthly / quarterly / annually |
| Balance method | Minimum daily / average daily / collected |
| Rate adjustment | Index/benchmark tied to rate, advance notice period |

- Minimum balance thresholds: measurement method (daily minimum / average daily / average monthly), consequences of breach (fee assessment, rate reduction, reclassification)
- Tiered balance structures with rate or fee-waiver triggers

### 2. Funds Availability Policy (Reg CC)

**Standard availability schedule:**

| Deposit Type | Availability |
|---|---|
| Electronic direct deposits, wire transfers | Next business day |
| Cash deposited in person to employee | Next business day |
| On-us checks | Next business day |
| U.S. Treasury checks; USPS money orders | Next business day (first $225 only) |
| Federal Reserve / Home Loan Bank checks | Next business day (first $225 only) |
| State/local government checks (conditions met) | Next business day (first $225 only) |
| Cashier's, certified, teller's checks (conditions met) | Next business day (first $225 only) |
| Local checks | By second business day |
| Non-local checks | Up to fifth business day |

**Exception holds (Reg CC § 229.13):**

| Exception | Maximum Hold |
|---|---|
| New accounts (first 30 days after opening) | Up to 9 business days |
| Large deposits (>$5,525 in one day; excess amount only) | Up to 7 business days |
| Repeated overdrafts (≥6 days overdrawn in prior 6 months, or overdrawn ≥$5,525 on ≥2 days) | Up to 7 business days |
| Redeposited returned checks | Up to 5 business days |
| Reasonable cause to doubt collectibility | Up to 5 business days |

- Hold notice requirements: timing (at deposit or by next business day); content (amount held, availability date, reason for hold)
- Business day definition: excludes Saturdays, Sundays, federal holidays; cut-off time no earlier than 2:00 p.m. for branch deposits

### 3. Electronic Fund Transfer Disclosures (Reg E)

**EFT services to describe:** ATM, POS debit (PIN and signature), ACH credits/debits, online/mobile banking, preauthorized recurring transfers, remote deposit capture, telephone banking

**Unauthorized transfer liability limits:**

| Notification Timing | Depositor Liability Cap |
|---|---|
| Within 2 business days of learning of loss or theft | Lesser of $50 or actual unauthorized transfers |
| 3–60 days after statement transmission | Up to $500 plus unauthorized transfers occurring after 2-day mark |
| After 60 days of statement transmission | Potentially unlimited for transfers occurring after 60-day period |

**Error resolution procedure:**

| Step | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Depositor notice | Oral or written; oral is effective without written confirmation |
| Institution investigation | Complete within 10 business days (20 business days for new accounts ≤30 days old, POS transactions, or foreign-initiated transfers) |
| Provisional credit | Within 1 business day of determining error occurred, or within 10 business days if investigation ongoing |
| Written explanation (if no error found) | Required; must state depositor's right to request documents relied upon |

- Transaction limits: disclose daily ATM withdrawal limit, daily POS limit, online/mobile transfer limits
- **Reg E opt-in requirement**: Institution may NOT assess overdraft fees on ATM and one-time debit card transactions without affirmative, separate opt-in consent

### 4. Fee Structure

| Fee Category | Disclosure Requirements |
|---|---|
| Monthly maintenance | Dollar amount; posting date; waiver conditions (minimum balance, direct deposit, relationship balance, age-based) |
| Overdraft | Per-item amount; daily maximum; transaction processing order used (largest-to-smallest, chronological, etc.) |
| NSF / returned item | Per-item amount; daily maximum |
| Overdraft protection | Linked savings transfer fee; LOC interest rate and draw fee; credit card cash advance fee |
| ATM (in-network) | Fee amount or "no fee" |
| ATM (out-of-network) | Institution fee plus operator surcharge (disclosed separately) |
| Foreign transactions | Percentage or flat fee on foreign-currency or foreign-network transactions |
| Wire transfers | Incoming domestic; outgoing domestic; international (separate line items) |
| Stop payment | Fee amount; 6-month duration; renewal procedure |
| Returned deposited item | Per-item fee |
| Account research | Hourly rate; minimum charge |
| Dormancy / inactivity | Fee amount; inactivity trigger period |
| Early account closure | Fee amount; applicable window (commonly 90–180 days) |

- Fee modification notice: 30 days' advance notice for increases or new fees (verify state law for longer periods); delivery method; continued use constitutes acceptance

### 5. Account Ownership and General Terms

**Ownership structures:**
- **JTWROS**: Surviving owner receives full ownership upon co-owner's death; each owner has full transactional authority without co-owner consent or notice
- **Tenants in common**: Each owner's interest passes to their estate upon death; specify fractional ownership if applicable

**Key operational provisions:**

| Provision | Content |
|---|---|
| Right of setoff | Institution may apply account funds to depositor debts without prior notice; subject to federal benefit payment prohibitions (31 CFR Part 212) [VERIFY] |
| Statement review duty | Monthly (transaction accounts); quarterly (savings unless activity triggers monthly); 30-day window to report unauthorized signatures or alterations; 60 days for other errors; 1-year absolute bar |
| Check processing order | Institution may process in any order; disclose chosen method; verify governing state law before recommending largest-to-smallest |
| Stale-dated checks | Institution not required to pay checks presented more than 6 months after date |
| Stop payments | 6-month duration; must be renewed; fee applies; institution liability if paid despite valid stop order |
| Account amendment | 30 days' notice for most changes; 21 days for EFT term changes; immediate for security-related changes |
| Account closure | Depositor may close at any time (subject to early closure fee if applicable); institution may close with 10–30 days' notice; immediate for fraud, illegal activity, or safety threats |
| Escheatment | 3–5 years inactivity (state law controls); institution notifies depositor before escheating; depositor may reclaim from state unclaimed property division |
| Death or incompetency | Institution freezes account pending documentation; requires letters testamentary or probate order; honors POD/trust designations if applicable |

### 6. Regulatory Compliance Provisions

Incorporate and cross-reference the following:

| Regulation / Law | Provision Required |
|---|---|
| BSA / AML | Institution may report transactions to federal authorities; reserves right to delay, refuse, or reverse transactions to comply |
| USA PATRIOT Act (CIP) | Customer identification program; institution may request additional documentation or information |
| OFAC | Prohibition on transactions with sanctioned individuals, entities, or countries |
| FATCA | Foreign account certification where foreign indicia present |
| IRS Form W-9 | TIN certification; backup withholding status |
| FinCEN Beneficial Ownership (CDD Rule) | Certification required for legal entity customers [VERIFY current threshold and form requirements] |

### 7. Execution and Acknowledgments

**Signature block elements:**
- Full legal name (as appearing on government-issued ID), handwritten signature, date
- For business accounts: signer's title or capacity; corporate resolution or certificate of authority authorizing account opening and designating signers

**Required acknowledgments checklist:**
- [ ] Receipt of funds availability policy disclosure (Reg CC)
- [ ] Receipt of electronic fund transfer disclosure (Reg E)
- [ ] Receipt of Truth in Savings disclosure (Reg DD)
- [ ] Receipt of fee schedule
- [ ] Overdraft protection enrollment with explicit Reg E opt-in consent (ATM/debit)
- [ ] Electronic banking services with security responsibility acknowledgment
- [ ] Debit card issuance with card terms acceptance
- [ ] Online/mobile banking with security procedure acceptance
- [ ] W-9 certification of TIN and backup withholding status
- [ ] ESIGN Act / state UETA compliance confirmed for electronic execution

## Guidelines

- **Reg DD APY**: Must appear in close proximity to interest rate disclosure; use exact regulatory terminology
- **Reg E opt-in**: ATM and one-time debit overdraft coverage requires clear, separate written consent — do not bundle into general agreement acceptance language
- **Arbitration clauses**: CFPB arbitration rulemaking has been historically volatile; verify current enforceability in governing jurisdiction [VERIFY]
- **Processing order**: Some states restrict or prohibit largest-to-smallest processing; verify governing state law before including
- **Federal benefit setoff**: Prohibited against Social Security, VA, and other federal benefit deposits under 31 CFR Part 212 [VERIFY applicable payment types]
- **Reg D savings limits**: Federal enforcement of the 6-transfer limit has been suspended; confirm institution's current contractual policy
- **Escheatment periods**: Vary by state (commonly 3–5 years); always verify the governing state's unclaimed property statute
- **Electronic disclosures**: Reg CC, Reg E, and Reg DD disclosures may only be delivered electronically after satisfying ESIGN Act § 101(c) consumer consent requirements
- Cite all regulations in Bluebook format; verify effective dates and recent amendments via CFPB, OCC, FDIC, or Federal Reserve sources before finalizing
