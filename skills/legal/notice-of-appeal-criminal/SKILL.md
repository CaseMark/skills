# Notice of Appeal — Criminal

Drafts a procedurally compliant Notice of Appeal that initiates appellate jurisdiction in a criminal matter, preserving all appellate rights within strict filing deadlines.

## Prerequisites

1. **Judgment entry / sentencing order** — with clerk's file-stamp date (not oral announcement date)
2. **Docket sheet** — trial court case number in exact format
3. **Indictment / information** — charges, statutory citations, counts
4. **Party names** — as they appear in official court records, including aliases
5. **Jurisdiction identification** — federal circuit or state appellate court and its local rules
6. **Appellant custody/contact info** — facility name, inmate number, or home address
7. **Counsel information** — or confirmation appellant is pro se

If the judgment entry or docket sheet is missing, request them before drafting — do not guess dates or case numbers.

## Critical Deadlines

| Jurisdiction | Deadline | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Federal | 14 days from judgment entry | FRAP 4(b)(1)(A) |
| State (typical) | 30–60 days | Varies by state appellate rule |

Missing the deadline by one day = permanent loss of appellate rights with no equitable remedy. Always calculate and state the deadline explicitly in the document.

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption

- **Appellate court**: full legal name per that court's rules (e.g., "United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit")
- **Trial court case number**: exact format from docket
- **Appellate case number**: "Case No. (To Be Assigned)"
- **Title**: `NOTICE OF APPEAL` — bold, centered, all caps
- **Reference line**: trial court name, county/district, case number

### 2. Party Identification

**Appellant (Defendant):**
- Full legal name matching judgment exactly, including suffixes, aliases (a/k/a)
- Current location if incarcerated (facility name, city, state)
- Mailing address with inmate number if applicable
- Phone number

**Appellee (Prosecution):**
- Formal designation exactly as in trial court caption:
  - State: "The State of [X]" / "The People of the State of [X]" / "The Commonwealth of [X]"
  - Federal: "United States of America"
- Prosecuting office with full address, phone, assigned ADA/AUSA name if known

### 3. Statement of Appeal

Draft using this template, adapting to jurisdiction:

```
Defendant-Appellant [FULL NAME] hereby appeals to the [APPELLATE COURT]
from the [Judgment Entry of conviction and sentence / Sentence / Order denying
post-conviction relief] entered by the [TRIAL COURT] on [DATE],
in Case No. [NUMBER], the Honorable [JUDGE NAME] presiding.
```

Specify scope explicitly:
- Conviction + sentence, or sentence only, or specific counts
- If limited: "Appellant does not challenge the underlying conviction on [Count/Charge]."

### 4. Basis for Appellate Jurisdiction

- Appeal as of right from final judgment: cite applicable statute/rule
- If leave required (e.g., guilty plea appeal, interlocutory order): reference the order granting leave with date and case number
- If prosecution appeal: include certification that appeal is not for delay and the legal question is substantial

### 5. Procedural History

Concise summary including:
- Trial type (jury / bench / guilty plea) and dates
- Plea terms if applicable (negotiated agreement, dismissed counts)
- Each count of conviction: offense, statutory citation, degree/class
- Sentence per count: imprisonment, fines, restitution, supervision terms, special conditions
- Aggregate sentence
- Presiding judge(s) at each stage if different

### 6. Grounds for Appeal

Present as numbered list. Required specificity varies by jurisdiction — when in doubt, include more detail. Common grounds:

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Sufficiency of evidence | No rational trier of fact could find elements proven beyond reasonable doubt |
| Jury instruction error | Misstatement of law, omitted elements, refused defense instructions |
| Evidentiary error | Improper admission (hearsay, 4th Amend., Confrontation Clause, FRE 403/404(b)) or exclusion of defense evidence |
| Prosecutorial misconduct | Improper argument, burden shifting, commenting on silence, Brady violation |
| Ineffective assistance | Strickland — deficient performance + prejudice (often better suited for post-conviction) |
| Constitutional violations | 4th, 5th, 6th Amendment; due process; speedy trial |
| Sentencing error | Exceeds statutory max, Apprendi/Blakely violation, guideline miscalculation, disproportionate sentence |

Do not include frivolous claims — attorney signature certifies good faith (FRAP 38 / state equivalent).

### 7. Transcript Request

State one of:
- **Complete transcript**: list all proceedings by date (trial, sentencing, suppression hearings, etc.)
- **Partial transcript**: specify which proceedings and explain why others are excluded
- **No transcript**: explain that appeal raises purely legal questions resolvable from written record

### 8. Indigency Certification (if applicable)

If appellant cannot pay costs, include or attach:
- Request for appointed appellate counsel (cite state statute, e.g., Douglas v. California)
- Request for transcript at public expense
- Financial affidavit / Affidavit of Indigency
- Certification that appeal is in good faith and not for delay

### 9. Certificate of Service

State:
- Each party/office served (prosecutor, trial court clerk, co-defendants if applicable)
- Method of service (personal, U.S. mail, certified mail, electronic filing)
- Date of service

### 10. Signature Block

**If represented by counsel:**
- "Respectfully submitted"
- Signature line, printed name, bar number
- Firm/office name, full address, phone, email, fax

**If pro se:**
- Signature line, printed name, current address, phone
- Date line

## Guidelines

- **Judgment entry date**: Use the clerk's file-stamp date, not the oral pronouncement date or judge's signature date. This controls the appeal deadline.
- **Name consistency**: Party names must match the trial court record exactly throughout — discrepancies create jurisdictional defects.
- **Formatting**: Comply with local rules on font (typically 12-pt serif), margins (typically 1"), spacing, and page numbering. Check for cover page or color-coded cover requirements.
- **Attachments**: Some jurisdictions require a copy of the judgment be attached. Include as Exhibit A if required.
- **Extensions**: If time was extended, reference the extension order with date and attach a copy.
- **Guilty plea appeals**: May be limited in scope or require leave to appeal — verify jurisdiction-specific restrictions.
- **Pro se filers**: Held to same procedural standards, though courts may construe filings liberally. Note any e-filing accommodations.
- **Timeliness calculation**: Include explicit calculation in the document when filing is near the deadline.
- **Preservation**: In jurisdictions that limit review to issues stated in the notice, err on the side of including all potential grounds.
