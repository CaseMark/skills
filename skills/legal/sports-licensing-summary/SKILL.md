# Sports Licensing Agreement Summary

Produces a structured summary of a sports licensing agreement (merchandise, broadcasting, or endorsement) capturing all material terms needed for legal and business decision-making.

## Prerequisites

1. **Full executed agreement** — including all schedules, exhibits, and amendments
2. **Agreement type identified** — merchandise licensing, broadcasting rights, athlete/team endorsement, or hybrid
3. **Any referenced league rules or CBA provisions** that constrain agreement terms

## Output Structure

### 1. Agreement Overview

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Parties | Full legal names + roles (licensor, licensee, agent) |
| Effective Date | Start date |
| Term | Duration + renewal/extension mechanics |
| Agreement Type | Merchandise / Broadcasting / Endorsement / Hybrid |
| Licensed Rights | See type-specific detail below |

**Licensed rights by type:**

- **Merchandise** — Exact IP licensed (logos, player names/likenesses, event branding), product categories, territory, exclusivity
- **Broadcasting** — Media rights (TV, streaming, radio, digital), territory, exclusivity, content restrictions, blackout provisions, sublicensing rights
- **Endorsement** — Scope of promotional obligations, products/services endorsed, name/image/likeness usage rights, social media obligations

### 2. Financial Terms

| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Upfront Payments | Fixed fees, signing bonuses |
| Guaranteed Minimums | Annual or term minimums |
| Royalties | Rate, calculation methodology, gross vs. net definition |
| Performance Bonuses | Triggers (sales thresholds, viewership, milestones) |
| Advances | Amount, recoupment terms |
| Revenue Sharing | Split percentages, qualifying revenue definition |
| Payment Schedule | Frequency, reporting deadlines |
| Audit Rights | Scope, frequency, cost allocation |

Flag any ambiguity in gross vs. net revenue definitions.

### 3. Obligations & Performance Requirements

**Licensee obligations:**
- [ ] Minimum sales/broadcast commitments
- [ ] Quality control standards (merchandise) or production values (broadcast)
- [ ] Marketing/promotion spend minimums
- [ ] Reporting requirements (sales, distribution, audience metrics)

**Licensor obligations:**
- [ ] IP value maintenance
- [ ] Marketing support / promotional appearances
- [ ] Category or territory exclusivity grants
- [ ] Approval rights (designs, ad materials, broadcast content)

### 4. Legal Protections & Compliance

| Provision | Summary |
|-----------|---------|
| IP Warranties | Ownership representations, infringement indemnification |
| Termination for Cause | Grounds, cure periods, notice requirements |
| Termination without Cause | Available to whom, notice period |
| Post-Termination | Inventory sell-off, content removal, final payments |
| Morals Clause | Triggers, remedies (especially endorsement deals) |
| Confidentiality | Scope, duration, carve-outs |
| Non-Compete / Exclusivity | Restrictions on competing arrangements |
| Dispute Resolution | Arbitration vs. litigation, governing law, venue |

### 5. Industry-Specific Provisions

Check and document where applicable:

- [ ] League rules limiting agreement terms or requiring approval
- [ ] CBA restrictions on player-related deals
- [ ] FCC compliance requirements (broadcasting)
- [ ] Consumer protection compliance (merchandise)
- [ ] Digital content protection / DRM requirements
- [ ] Social media restrictions or obligations
- [ ] Competing brand association restrictions

### 6. Key Dates & Deadlines

List all contractual dates requiring monitoring:
- Renewal option exercise deadlines
- Performance threshold measurement periods
- Reporting due dates
- Approval response windows
- Termination notice periods

### 7. Risk Assessment

Conclude with 3-5 bullet points identifying the most significant risks:
- Ambiguous performance standards
- One-sided termination rights
- Inadequate IP protections
- Commercially unfavorable financial terms under downside scenarios
- Regulatory or league-rule compliance gaps

## Guidelines

- Define gross vs. net revenue precisely — flag if the agreement is ambiguous on this point
- For endorsement deals, scrutinize morals clause breadth and image/likeness usage scope
- Always note whether exclusivity is category-specific or blanket
- Flag provisions that may conflict with applicable league rules or CBAs
- Indicate where full contract language should be consulted rather than relying on summary
- Mark any referenced statutes or rules with `[VERIFY]` if not confirmed in the agreement text
