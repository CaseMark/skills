# Investment Advisory Agreement

Drafts a U.S.-compliant Investment Advisory Agreement for registered investment advisers, satisfying federal and state securities law disclosure and contract requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Adviser details** — legal entity name, registration status (SEC or state), CRD number, principal office
2. **Client details** — full legal name(s), account type (individual, joint, trust, entity), investment objectives, risk tolerance, time horizon
3. **Form ADV Part 2A & 2B** — current brochure and brochure supplements
4. **Fee schedule** — AUM percentages, tier breakpoints, billing frequency, minimum fees if any
5. **Custodian** — identified qualified custodian or selection process description
6. **Service scope** — discretionary vs. non-discretionary authority; ancillary services (financial planning, etc.)
7. **Proxy voting policy** — adviser's written policy if adviser will accept proxy voting authority

## Output Structure

### 1. Cover Page
Agreement title, client legal name, adviser legal name, effective date, and table of contents (if >5 pages).

### 2. Services

| Element | Required Content |
|---|---|
| Authority type | Explicitly state discretionary or non-discretionary; explain practical implications |
| Portfolio management | Construction methodology, asset allocation, rebalancing frequency, review cadence |
| Ancillary services | Financial planning, retirement, estate coordination — only if in scope |
| Out-of-scope | Tax prep, legal drafting, accounting excluded; referrals to qualified professionals |

### 3. Fees & Billing

| Element | Required Content |
|---|---|
| Advisory fee | Annual % of AUM; tiered schedule with breakpoints and worked dollar examples |
| Calculation basis | Beginning / ending / average daily balance — specify which |
| Billing frequency | Quarterly in advance, quarterly in arrears, monthly, or annual |
| Payment method | Direct debit (written authorization required) or invoice |
| Pro-ration | Mid-period account opens/closes, significant deposits/withdrawals |
| Third-party costs | Custodial fees, commissions, fund expense ratios — clearly distinguished from advisory fee |
| Minimums | Minimum account size and/or minimum annual fee if applicable |

### 4. Custody & Brokerage

- Identify qualified custodian (SEC Rule 206(4)-2 [VERIFY current rule number])
- Confirm adviser holds no physical custody; assets held in client's name at custodial institution
- Disclose soft dollar arrangements, resulting conflicts, and how best execution is maintained
- Disclose referral fees or revenue-sharing arrangements with custodians or service providers
- Confirm client receives account statements directly from custodian at least quarterly

### 5. Risk Disclosures

Enumerate applicable risks with client acknowledgement:

- [ ] Market risk (overall market declines)
- [ ] Interest rate risk (bond value sensitivity)
- [ ] Credit / default risk
- [ ] Liquidity risk
- [ ] Concentration risk (if applicable to strategy)
- [ ] International / currency risk (if applicable)
- [ ] Alternative asset risk (if applicable)
- [ ] No guarantee of performance or return of principal; past performance not indicative of future results

### 6. Regulatory Disclosures & Acknowledgements

| Requirement | Provision |
|---|---|
| Form ADV delivery | Client received Part 2A and 2B ≥48 hours before execution (Rule 204-3) |
| Free-look right | Client may terminate without penalty within 5 business days if ADV not delivered in advance |
| Annual update right | Client entitled to updated ADV within 120 days of adviser's fiscal year-end |
| Electronic signatures | Valid under ESIGN Act / UETA; client consent to electronic business included |

### 7. Proxy Voting

**Option A — Adviser votes proxies:**
- Reference adviser's written proxy voting policies (copy provided or available on request)
- Describe voting methodology (proxy advisory service, independent analysis, etc.)
- Explain conflict-of-interest handling (e.g., issuer is also an advisory client)
- Describe how client can obtain information on how proxies were voted

**Option B — Client retains proxy authority:**
- Adviser has no obligation or authority to vote proxies, make recommendations, or act on corporate actions
- Client receives proxy materials directly from custodian or transfer agent

Address class actions, tender offers, and rights offerings — state responsibility for each explicitly.

### 8. Term, Termination & Amendment

| Provision | Standard Language |
|---|---|
| Term | Effective on execution; continues until terminated by either party |
| Termination | Written notice; specify notice period (typically 30 days) |
| Fees on termination | Pro-rate; refund prepaid unearned advisory fees promptly |
| Amendment | Material changes require written mutual consent; fee increases require affirmative client consent |
| Assignment | Requires client consent per IAA § 205(a)(2); define assignment to include transfer of controlling interest |

### 9. General Provisions

- Governing law (specify state)
- Severability
- Integration / entire agreement supersedes prior oral or written agreements
- Confidentiality and client privacy (Regulation S-P [VERIFY])
- Limitation of liability to the extent permitted by law
- Dispute resolution / mandatory arbitration (if adviser firm policy)

### 10. Signature Block

| Signatory Type | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Individual | Full legal name as on account registration + date |
| Joint account | All account holders sign; each confirms receipt of disclosures |
| Entity (trust / LLC / corp) | Authorized representative + title + representation of binding authority |

Include dated acknowledgement that client has received, read, and understands the IAA and all Form ADV disclosures.

## Guidelines

- **Fiduciary standard:** All provisions must reflect duty to act in client's best interest; disclose and manage all conflicts of interest
- **Rule 206(4)-3 [VERIFY]:** If solicitor arrangements exist, include required cash solicitation disclosures
- **State registration:** For state-registered advisers, verify state-specific contract and disclosure requirements — some states impose additional obligations beyond SEC rules
- **No guarantee language:** Exclude any language that could be construed as guaranteeing returns, promising specific outcomes, or limiting fiduciary obligations
- **Search uploaded docs first:** Extract existing Form ADV, prior IAA, fee schedules, and client data from uploaded documents before drafting
- **Length target:** 8–15 pages; use numbered sections and table of contents for longer agreements
- **Annual maintenance:** Flag for re-execution or client acknowledgement whenever material terms change or Form ADV is materially amended
