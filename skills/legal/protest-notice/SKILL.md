# Notice of Intent to Protest Award

Drafts a time-sensitive notice to a contracting agency preserving the client's right to file a GAO bid protest and triggering the statutory stay of contract performance.

## Prerequisites

1. **Solicitation/contract number** and full procurement title
2. **Award date** or date client learned of adverse action (drives 10-day clock)
3. **Contracting Officer identity** — name, title, agency, address (if available)
4. **Client identification** — legal name, DBA, UEI/DUNS, offeror status
5. **Debriefing materials** (if received) — evaluation scores, narrative, comparative strengths/weaknesses
6. **Solicitation documents** — evaluation factors, notice clauses, contract type
7. **Basis of protest** — at least a preliminary identification of the procurement error(s)

## Timeliness Rules

| Trigger | Deadline | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Adverse award decision known | 10 days from knowledge | FAR 33.103(e); 4 C.F.R. § 21.2(a)(2) |
| Post-debriefing protest | 10 days from debriefing date | 4 C.F.R. § 21.2(a)(2) |
| Solicitation impropriety apparent pre-close | Before proposal due date | 4 C.F.R. § 21.2(a)(1) |
| Formal protest after this notice | 10 days from notice filing | 4 C.F.R. § 21.2(a)(3) |

**These deadlines are jurisdictional — missing them is fatal.**

## Output Structure

### 1. Header Block

```
[Firm Letterhead]

[Date]

VIA [EMAIL/CERTIFIED MAIL/HAND DELIVERY]

[Contracting Officer Name & Title, or "Contracting Officer"]
[Agency Name]
[Office/Division]
[Address]

RE:   Notice of Intent to Protest Award
      Solicitation No. [NUMBER]
      [Procurement Title]
      Award Date: [DATE] / Date of Knowledge: [DATE]
```

### 2. Opening — Purpose & Identification

- State this is a formal Notice of Intent to Protest under FAR 33.103
- Identify protester: full legal name, UEI, offeror status
- Identify counsel: name, firm, bar admissions, contact info

### 3. Interested Party Standing

Establish standing under 31 U.S.C. § 3551(2) and 4 C.F.R. § 21.0(a)(1):

| Element | What to Show |
|---|---|
| Timely proposal | Client submitted a conforming proposal |
| Direct economic interest | Client is next in line or had substantial chance of award |
| Competitive prejudice | Identified errors directly affected client's competitive position |

Strengthen with: competitive range inclusion, incumbent status, debriefing inconsistencies, technical acceptability rating.

### 4. Protest Grounds

State each ground with enough specificity to provide notice, but preserve detailed arguments for the formal protest. Organize by category:

**Evaluation errors:**
- Misapplication of stated evaluation criteria
- Unstated evaluation factors
- Inconsistent treatment between offerors
- Arbitrary/unsupported evaluation judgments

**Solicitation defects:**
- Ambiguous or unduly restrictive requirements
- OCI issues, bundling violations, set-aside eligibility problems

**Procedural violations:**
- Improper discussions, unequal exchanges
- Procurement integrity violations

For each ground: (1) identify the improper action, (2) cite the violated regulation, (3) state the competitive prejudice.

### 5. Timeliness Statement

Affirmatively state:
- Date client learned of adverse action
- That this notice is filed within 10 days of that date
- If post-debriefing: the debriefing date and computation

### 6. Stay of Performance Request

Invoke automatic stay under FAR 33.104(c) and 31 U.S.C. § 3553(d):

- Demand immediate suspension of contract performance
- Note override requires written agency head finding of "urgent and compelling circumstances"
- Request written confirmation that stay is in effect
- If applicable, flag irreversibility of performance, switching costs, or proprietary information risks
- For task orders under existing vehicles, cite applicable stay provisions separately

### 7. Reservation of Rights & Requests

- Formal protest will follow within 10 days per 4 C.F.R. § 21.2(a)(3)
- Reserve right to supplement grounds after reviewing agency report
- Request complete procurement file (all proposals, evaluation materials, source selection documents)
- Express openness to corrective action / ADR while preserving all rights

### 8. Signature Block & Certificate of Service

```
Respectfully submitted,

[Signature]
[Name], [Title]
[Bar Admissions]
[Firm Name]
[Address / Email / Phone]
Date: [DATE]

CERTIFICATE OF SERVICE

I certify that on [DATE], a copy of this Notice of Intent to Protest
was served on [Contracting Officer Name] at [Agency] via [method].

[Signature]
```

## Guidelines

- **Length**: 2–4 pages. Detailed enough for notice requirements; save full arguments for the formal protest.
- **Tone**: Professional and assertive. No inflammatory language — preserve settlement posture.
- **Do not** prematurely disclose full legal theory or proprietary competitive analysis.
- **Do not** omit the timeliness statement — it is jurisdictional.
- **Always** include the explicit stay request with statutory citation.
- **Always** calculate and prominently note the formal protest filing deadline.
- **Format**: Single-spaced, double-space between paragraphs, 12pt Times New Roman or equivalent.
- **Task order protests** may have different stay rules — flag and research if applicable.
- **Commercial item procurements** may have modified protest procedures — verify applicability.
