# Complaint for Copyright Infringement

Drafts a litigation-ready federal complaint asserting copyright infringement claims under the Copyright Act, establishing jurisdiction, ownership, and infringement with Twombly/Iqbal plausibility.

## Prerequisites

1. **Copyright registration(s)** — registration number(s), effective date(s), and certificate copies (or pending application details); registration is a filing prerequisite under 17 U.S.C. § 411(a)
2. **Ownership chain** — creation, work-for-hire agreement, or assignment/transfer documents establishing plaintiff's title
3. **Party information** — full legal names, states of incorporation/citizenship, principal places of business for all parties
4. **Infringement facts** — specific acts, dates, locations/URLs, and method of infringement; access evidence; similarity analysis
5. **Willfulness evidence** (if any) — prior notice, admissions, identical errors, or forensic evidence
6. **Statute of limitations check** — all alleged acts must be within 3 years of discovery (17 U.S.C. § 507(b))
7. **Target district** — confirm local rules for caption format and filing requirements

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption
```
UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
[DISTRICT NAME]

[PLAINTIFF FULL NAME],          Case No.: ___________
    Plaintiff,
v.
[DEFENDANT FULL NAME],
    Defendant.

COMPLAINT FOR COPYRIGHT INFRINGEMENT
JURY TRIAL DEMANDED
```

### 2. Parties

| Element | Required Allegations |
|---|---|
| Plaintiff | Legal status, state of incorporation/citizenship, PPB, relationship to work (author, employer, assignee) |
| Defendant | Legal status, state of incorporation/citizenship, PPB or residence, business activities establishing personal jurisdiction |
| Online defendants | Describe websites/platforms; allege access, downloads, or distribution within district |

### 3. Jurisdiction & Venue

- **Subject matter:** Action arises under Copyright Act, 17 U.S.C. § 101 et seq.; federal question jurisdiction under 28 U.S.C. §§ 1331 and 1338(a); Court has original and exclusive jurisdiction
- **Venue:** 28 U.S.C. § 1391 and § 1400(a) — allege defendant resides in district, or substantial part of events occurred in district, or defendant subject to personal jurisdiction here

### 4. The Copyrighted Work(s)

For each work, allege:
- [ ] Title and category under 17 U.S.C. § 102 (literary, musical, pictorial, audiovisual, sound recording, etc.)
- [ ] Date of creation and first publication (if applicable)
- [ ] Creative elements establishing originality
- [ ] Plaintiff owns all rights under 17 U.S.C. § 106 (reproduce, distribute, display, perform, make derivatives)
- [ ] Copyright Registration No. ___, effective date ___; certificate attached as Exhibit ___
- [ ] Compliance with all statutory formalities
- [ ] Chain of title if acquired by assignment or work-for-hire

### 5. Count I — Copyright Infringement (17 U.S.C. § 501)

**Incorporate** all preceding paragraphs by reference.

**Two-part infringement test:**
1. **Access** — defendant had actual access OR work was widely disseminated
2. **Substantial similarity** — defendant's work is substantially similar to plaintiff's protectable expression (distinguish ideas/facts/scènes à faire from protected expression)

**Exclusive rights violated** (check all that apply):
- [ ] Reproduction — 17 U.S.C. § 106(1)
- [ ] Distribution — 17 U.S.C. § 106(3)
- [ ] Public display — 17 U.S.C. § 106(5)
- [ ] Public performance — 17 U.S.C. § 106(4)
- [ ] Derivative works — 17 U.S.C. § 106(2)

**Willfulness** (if applicable): Allege defendant knew or should have known conduct constituted infringement, or acted with reckless disregard, or continued after notice. Required for enhanced statutory damages under § 504(c)(2) and fees under § 505.

**Damages:**
- Actual damages + defendant's profits not included in actual damages (17 U.S.C. § 504(b)); OR
- Statutory damages: $750–$30,000 per work; up to $150,000 per work if willful (17 U.S.C. § 504(c)) — election before final judgment

**Injunctive relief:** Allege ongoing infringement causing irreparable harm with no adequate remedy at law; seek preliminary and permanent injunction under 17 U.S.C. § 502.

### 6. Prayer for Relief

Request:
- (a) Preliminary and permanent injunction against further infringement (17 U.S.C. § 502)
- (b) Actual damages and defendant's profits (§ 504(b)), or statutory damages up to $150,000/work if willful (§ 504(c)), at plaintiff's election
- (c) Costs and reasonable attorney's fees (17 U.S.C. § 505)
- (d) Impoundment and destruction of infringing copies and means of reproduction (17 U.S.C. § 503)
- (e) Pre- and post-judgment interest
- (f) Such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper

## Guidelines

- **Paragraph discipline:** Number consecutively; one set of circumstances per paragraph (FRCP 10(b))
- **Plausibility standard:** Allege sufficient facts to make claims plausible, not merely conceivable — *Twombly*, 550 U.S. 544; *Iqbal*, 556 U.S. 662
- **Registration prerequisite:** Confirm registration is effective (or § 411(a) exception applies) before filing; attach certificates as exhibits
- **SOL:** Confirm all alleged acts are within 3 years of plaintiff's discovery (17 U.S.C. § 507(b))
- **FRCP 11:** All factual contentions must have evidentiary support; legal contentions must be warranted by existing law or nonfrivolous argument
- **Online infringement:** For digital/web defendants, specifically plead forum contacts (downloads, transmissions, ad revenue) to anchor personal jurisdiction and venue
- **Damages election:** Do not make the statutory damages election in the complaint — preserve until before final judgment
- **Local rules:** Verify caption format, exhibit labeling, and page limits in target district before filing
