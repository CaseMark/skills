# EEOC Charge of Discrimination

Drafts a jurisdictionally sufficient, fact-driven EEOC Charge of Discrimination that satisfies administrative exhaustion and preserves the right to sue.

## Prerequisites

1. **Complainant info** — full legal name, address, phone, email, job title, hire date, termination date (if applicable)
2. **Respondent info** — legal entity name, address, employee count, HR/legal contact
3. **Timeline documents** — adverse action notices, performance reviews, disciplinary records, emails, texts
4. **Protected class evidence** — basis for discrimination (race, sex, age, disability, etc.) with supporting facts
5. **Comparator/witness information** — names, titles, treatment differentials
6. **Filing deadline check** — date of last discriminatory act to confirm timeliness

## Output Structure

### 1. Jurisdiction & Timeliness Check

| Factor | Rule |
|--------|------|
| Title VII / ADA / GINA threshold | ≥ 15 employees (payroll method, 20+ calendar weeks) |
| ADEA threshold | ≥ 20 employees |
| Filing deadline — non-deferral state | 180 days from discriminatory act |
| Filing deadline — deferral state | 300 days (state with FEPA worksharing agreement) |
| Complainant age (ADEA) | Must be ≥ 40 at time of adverse action |
| Continuing violation | Document last act; hostile environment claims use final incident date |

Identify whether a parallel state agency dual-filing is appropriate to preserve state claims and extend the federal deadline to 300 days.

### 2. Complainant Section

- Full legal name, address, phone, email
- Employment status: current / former employee / applicant
- Job title, department, supervisor, hire date, separation date
- Protected class membership (stated as fact, not legal conclusion)
- Prior EEOC/FEPA charge number if applicable

### 3. Respondent Section

- Legal entity name (not trade name/DBA)
- Principal business address and phone
- HR director or registered agent as contact
- Employee count and coverage statute(s)
- FEIN if available
- Parent/subsidiary or joint employer relationships if relevant

### 4. Basis of Discrimination

Check all that apply and specify the theory:

| Statute | Basis |
|---------|-------|
| Title VII | Race, color, religion, sex, national origin |
| Title VII (sex) | Sexual harassment (quid pro quo / hostile environment), pregnancy, sexual orientation/gender identity |
| ADEA | Age (complainant ≥ 40) |
| ADA | Actual disability / record of impairment / regarded as; discriminatory treatment, failure to accommodate, or harassment |
| GINA | Genetic information |
| EPA | Sex-based wage discrimination |
| Retaliation | Prior charge, opposition to discrimination, participation in investigation |

For ADA: specify whether claim involves actual disability (substantially limits major life activity), record of impairment, or regarded-as theory.

### 5. Adverse Action & Dates

List each discrete act with exact date:
- Termination / constructive discharge
- Demotion / reduction in pay or responsibilities
- Failure to promote (identify position and qualifications)
- Denial of transfer, training, or benefits
- Retaliatory action (identify protected activity and temporal gap)
- Harassing conduct (date of each significant incident; date of last act for timeliness)

### 6. Factual Narrative

Structure chronologically:

1. **Background** — hire date, role, performance baseline
2. **Protected class / activity** — how/when respondent knew
3. **Discriminatory conduct** — specific acts, direct quotes, dates, actor names
4. **Comparators** — name, title, protected class status, preferential treatment received
5. **Employer knowledge & response** — complaints made, to whom, date, outcome
6. **Adverse action** — decision-maker, stated reason, pretext evidence
7. **Harm** — economic losses, emotional distress, dates

For harassment: establish severity or pervasiveness, unwelcomeness, employer knowledge, and failure to take prompt remedial action.
For accommodation: document request, interactive process (or failure thereof), employer response, and why accommodation was reasonable.
For retaliation: establish protected activity → employer knowledge → adverse action with temporal proximity or direct evidence.

### 7. Relief Requested

- **Back pay** — lost wages, bonuses, benefits from date of violation through resolution (with prejudgment interest)
- **Front pay** — if reinstatement not feasible
- **Reinstatement** — to same or equivalent position with restored seniority/benefits
- **Compensatory damages** — out-of-pocket losses, emotional distress, mental anguish
- **Punitive damages** — if malice or reckless indifference (Civil Rights Act of 1991 caps):

| Employer size | Cap |
|---------------|-----|
| 15–100 employees | $50,000 |
| 101–200 employees | $100,000 |
| 201–500 employees | $200,000 |
| 500+ employees | $300,000 |

- **Injunctive relief** — policy changes, training, monitoring, notice posting
- **Attorney's fees and costs**
- **Such other relief as the Commission deems just and proper**

### 8. Verification Block

Per 29 C.F.R. § 1601.12 — include sworn verification:

> "I have read the above charge and it is true to the best of my knowledge, information, and belief."

Signature block: charging party name, date, and (if represented) attorney name, bar number, firm, address, phone, email.

## Guidelines

- **File on Form 5 or via EEOC Public Portal**; retain proof of filing (certified mail return receipt or electronic confirmation)
- **Preserve all bases** — assert each protected characteristic supported by facts; omitted bases cannot be added post-charge without amendment
- **Avoid legal conclusions** in the narrative; state facts and let the law follow
- **Continuing violation doctrine** arguments must be expressly stated if any acts fall outside the filing window
- **Dual-file** with state FEPA where available to preserve state law claims
- **Do not identify witnesses by name** if doing so creates safety concerns; use descriptive identifiers instead
- Punitive damages not available against government employers under Title VII [VERIFY state-specific equivalents]
- ADEA does not provide compensatory or punitive damages — liquidated damages (double back pay) available for willful violations [VERIFY]
