# Bluebook Citation Format

Quick-reference for Bluebook citation rules in court documents (Bluepages practitioner format).

## Case Citations

| Form | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Full | *Party v. Party*, Vol. Reporter Pg., Pincite (Court Year) | *Smith v. Jones*, 123 F.3d 456, 458 (9th Cir. 2020) |
| Short | *Party*, Vol. Reporter at Pincite | *Smith*, 123 F.3d at 459 |
| Id. | *Id.* at Pincite | *Id.* at 460 |
| Subsequent history | Append with comma | *Smith v. Jones*, 123 F.3d 456 (9th Cir. 2020), *aff'd*, 540 U.S. 100 (2003) |

**Rules:**
- Italicize case names, including *v.*
- Omit "Inc.," "Ltd.," "Co." when other party-description words are present (R. 10.2.1(h))
- Include pincites for all propositions
- Abbreviate court names per T.7; omit court if obvious from reporter (e.g., U.S. Reports → Supreme Court)
- Use *Id.* only when citing the immediately preceding authority

## Statutes & Constitutions

| Type | Format |
|------|--------|
| Federal statute | 42 U.S.C. § 1983 (2018) |
| State statute | Cal. Civ. Code § 1542 (West 2020) |
| U.S. Constitution | U.S. Const. amend. XIV, § 1 |
| State constitution | Cal. Const. art. I, § 7 |

- Use "§" (single) or "§§" (range) — always with a space after
- Include code year in parenthetical for statutes
- For amended statutes, cite current version unless historical version is relevant

## Rules & Regulations

| Type | Format |
|------|--------|
| Federal civil rule | Fed. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6) |
| Federal evidence rule | Fed. R. Evid. 702 |
| Federal appellate rule | Fed. R. App. P. 28(a) |
| Federal regulation | 29 C.F.R. § 1630.2(j) (2020) |
| Federal Register | 85 Fed. Reg. 12,345 (Mar. 2, 2020) |

## Secondary Sources

| Type | Format |
|------|--------|
| Restatement | Restatement (Second) of Contracts § 90 (Am. L. Inst. 1981) |
| Treatise | 5 Wright & Miller, *Federal Practice and Procedure* § 1216 (3d ed. 2004) |
| Law review | Sunstein, *On the Expressive Function of Law*, 144 U. Pa. L. Rev. 2021, 2025 (1996) |
| A.L.R. | Annotation, *Title*, 100 A.L.R.5th 1 (2002) |
| Black's Law Dict. | *Term*, Black's Law Dictionary (12th ed. 2024) |

## Introductory Signals

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| [none] | Direct support; source states the proposition |
| *See* | Clearly supports; inference required |
| *See also* | Additional support; less directly on point |
| *Accord* | Two or more sources state same rule; cite one, *accord* the others |
| *Cf.* | Analogous support by analogy |
| *Compare . . . with* | Comparison of authorities illustrates the point |
| *But see* | Directly contrary |
| *But cf.* | Contrary by analogy |
| *See generally* | Background or general support |

- Signals are italicized. Capitalize only when beginning a citation sentence.
- Order of authorities within each signal follows R. 1.4 (constitutions → statutes → cases by court hierarchy → secondary sources).

## Short-Form Rules

- **Cases:** Use *Id.* for immediately preceding source; otherwise party short form + reporter pincite.
- **Statutes:** Use "§" alone after full cite in same discussion: § 1983.
- **Supra/Hereinafter:** Use *supra* for secondary sources (not cases or statutes): Sunstein, *supra*, at 2030.

## Guidelines

- Always pincite — never cite a source without a specific page or section
- Use parenthetical explanations for non-obvious relevance: (holding that . . .)
- Place weight-of-authority parentheticals after the cite: (en banc); (per curiam); (5-4 decision)
- Distinguish *citation sentences* (ending with period, separate from text) from *citation clauses* (embedded, set off by commas)
- When in doubt on abbreviations, consult Bluebook tables T.6 (case names), T.7 (court names), T.10 (geographical terms)
