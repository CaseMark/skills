# Clinical Trial Agreement

Draft a protocol-specific CTA that governs study conduct, compliance, data rights, payments, and risk allocation.

## Prerequisites

1. Executed or near-final study protocol with number, version date, phase, endpoints, and schedule of events
2. Investigator Brochure or device manual and current safety profile
3. Draft budget and payment schedule with FMV rationale
4. Site policies on contracting, IP, publication, and indemnification
5. Regulatory status: IND or IDE holder, IRB process, DSMB plan (if any)

## Output Structure / Process

1. Complete the intake table and confirm missing fields before drafting.

| Intake Field | Required | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sponsor legal name and address | Yes | Include parent or funding affiliate if applicable |
| Institution legal name and address | Yes | Include hospital or academic entity if contracting party |
| Principal Investigator | Yes | Individual signature needed? |
| Protocol title/number/version | Yes | Attach as Exhibit A |
| Study phase and design | Yes | Randomized, blinded, placebo, etc. |
| Enrollment target for site | Yes | Min, max, or good-faith estimate |
| IND/IDE holder and number | Yes | Identify sponsor vs institution |
| Investigational product | Yes | Drug/device/biologic and comparators |
| Central lab and vendors | If applicable | Include data flows |
| Privacy model | Yes | HIPAA authorization vs waiver, de-identification plan |
| Insurance limits | Yes | Sponsor and institution coverage levels |
| Publication plan | Yes | Single-site vs multicenter |
| Governing law and venue | Yes | State selection |
| Compensation model | Yes | Per-subject, milestone, hybrid |
| Closeout and retention | Yes | Record retention period |

2. Draft the agreement using this section map and required content.

| Section | Required Content |
| --- | --- |
| Parties and Recitals | Identify all parties; reference protocol; acknowledge regulatory framework |
| Definitions | Study, Protocol, Investigational Product, AE/SAE (21 CFR 312.32), GCP, Study Data, Confidential Information |
| Study Conduct | Incorporate protocol; amendment process; investigator independence; facilities and staffing |
| Sponsor Obligations | Supply product; training; monitoring; regulatory submissions; safety updates |
| Site/Investigator Obligations | IRB approval; informed consent (21 CFR 50.27); records; protocol adherence; safety reporting |
| Data and Records | Source data access; CRF completion; retention period |
| Budget and Payment | FMV structure; invoicing; screen failures; early withdrawals; withholding rights |
| Confidentiality and Privacy | Confidentiality exceptions; HIPAA compliance (45 CFR Parts 160, 164); de-identification |
| IP and Publications | Ownership of data and inventions; review windows; multicenter restrictions |
| Indemnification and Insurance | Mutual indemnity; product liability; insurance limits; additional insureds |
| Term and Termination | For cause and convenience; subject safety; wind-down; survival |
| Dispute Resolution | Negotiation, mediation, arbitration or courts; injunctive relief |
| Admin | Notices, assignment, amendment, severability, independent contractor |

3. Use the compliance checklist to validate all regulatory and ethics obligations are covered.

- [ ] IRB approval required before study start and for amendments
- [ ] Informed consent process and documentation meets 21 CFR Part 50
- [ ] IRB requirements aligned with 21 CFR Part 56
- [ ] IND or IDE responsibilities aligned with 21 CFR Part 312
- [ ] SAE reporting timelines defined and sponsor notification within 24 hours
- [ ] GCP reference to ICH E6(R2) and applicable laws
- [ ] Record retention period specified (minimum 2 years after FDA approval or discontinuation)
- [ ] HIPAA compliance and minimum necessary disclosure
- [ ] Disclosure of conflicts and exclusion/debarment status
- [ ] Anti-kickback and Stark Law compliance for compensation

4. Tailor risk allocation based on product and population risk profile.

- [ ] High-risk product triggers higher insurance limits and enhanced safety monitoring
- [ ] Vulnerable populations require explicit consent safeguards
- [ ] Invasive procedures require explicit injury and compensation language

5. Attach exhibits.

- Exhibit A: Protocol
- Exhibit B: Budget and payment schedule
- Exhibit C: Schedule of events or procedures (if separate)

## Guidelines

- Use exact protocol identifiers and dates; do not paraphrase key protocol constraints.
- Define AE and SAE per 21 CFR 312.32; mark [VERIFY] if deviating.
- Do not promise sponsor control over investigator medical judgment.
- Keep publication review windows reasonable and time-bounded (typically 30 to 60 days).
- Ensure compensation is FMV and not tied to outcomes or referrals.
- If state law mandates clinical trial injury coverage, add state-specific language. Use [VERIFY] if unsure.
- Preserve subject safety obligations after termination, including follow-up and reporting.
