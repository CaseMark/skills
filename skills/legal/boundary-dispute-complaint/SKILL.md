# Boundary Dispute Complaint

Drafts a plaintiff-side complaint to judicially establish the true boundary line between adjoining properties, with optional trespass/encroachment claims.

## Prerequisites

1. **Plaintiff's deed** — legal description (metes and bounds, lot/block, or recorded plat reference)
2. **Defendant's deed** — adjoining parcel legal description
3. **Survey** — professional survey findings, surveyor name, date, and recording info (if obtained)
4. **Encroachment details** — type, dimensions, and date of discovery (if applicable)
5. **Jurisdiction** — state and county where the real property is located (venue basis)
6. **Pre-suit notice** — whether defendant was notified before filing

## Output Structure

### Caption
- Court name, case number placeholder, plaintiff and defendant full legal names
- Follow local court formatting rules for the filing jurisdiction

### Parties & Jurisdiction
| Element | Required Allegation |
|---|---|
| Subject matter jurisdiction | Real property dispute; amount in controversy (if threshold required) |
| Personal jurisdiction | Defendant's contacts with forum state |
| Venue | Real property located in [County], [State] |

### Factual Allegations (Numbered Paragraphs)

1. **Plaintiff's property** — full legal description per deed/survey; recording info
2. **Defendant's property** — full legal description; recording info; relationship to plaintiff's parcel
3. **Boundary dispute** — how parties' positions differ; reference to deeds, historical conveyances, surveys, or physical markers
4. **Survey findings** — surveyor's conclusion as to true boundary location; methodology
5. **Discrepancies** — conflicting deed language, ambiguous historical conveyances, or inconsistent prior surveys
6. **Encroachment** *(if applicable)* — structure/improvement type, dimensions, extent of intrusion, date discovered, whether defendant was notified

### Counts

**Count I — Quiet Title to Boundary Line**
- Seek judicial determination and declaration of the true boundary line
- Allege entitlement under state quiet title statute [VERIFY applicable state statute]
- Incorporate deed interpretation principles, survey evidence, and applicable boundary-determination standards

**Count II — Trespass** *(if encroachment present)*
- Allege defendant's placement/maintenance of improvements on plaintiff's land constitutes ongoing trespass
- Damages: diminution in property value, loss of use and enjoyment, remediation costs
- Relief: compensatory damages + mandatory injunction requiring encroachment removal

### Prayer for Relief

1. Declaratory judgment establishing true and correct boundary line
2. Order requiring defendant to recognize and respect judicially determined boundary
3. Mandatory injunctive relief requiring removal of encroachments *(if applicable)*
4. Compensatory damages for trespass and property harm *(if applicable)*
5. Costs and attorney's fees *(if authorized by statute or contract — [VERIFY jurisdiction]*
6. Such other relief as the court deems just and proper

### Exhibits (Incorporated by Reference)
- Exhibit A: Plaintiff's deed
- Exhibit B: Survey/plat
- Exhibit C: Photographs of encroachment *(if applicable)*

## Guidelines

- Use numbered paragraphs for all factual allegations
- Keep tone factual and professional — avoid inflammatory characterizations of defendant's conduct
- All legal descriptions must be reproduced verbatim from source documents
- Quiet title claims typically require joinder of all persons with an interest in the property — confirm whether lienholders or prior owners must be named [VERIFY per state rules]
- Some states require a lis pendens filed concurrently — flag for attorney review
- Complaint must be sufficiently detailed to survive a 12(b)(6) motion; avoid conclusory allegations unsupported by factual predicates
