# Certificate of Incumbency

Drafts a Secretary's Certificate of Incumbency certifying current officers, directors, and signing authority for authorized third-party reliance in corporate transactions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Formation documents** — articles of incorporation or certificate of formation confirming exact legal entity name including corporate designator (Inc., Corp., Ltd., LLC, L.L.C.)
2. **Current bylaws or operating agreement** — to be attached as Exhibit A; note any amendments
3. **Authorizing board resolutions or written consents** — with adoption dates; confirm valid notice, quorum, and voting thresholds were met
4. **Officer/director roster** — full legal names, exact titles, dates of election or appointment
5. **Third-party requirements** — bank checklists, prescribed forms, or specific certifications (FinCEN, sanctions, TIN, etc.)
6. **Transaction description** — purpose, named recipient, and whether multiple closings or draw-downs are anticipated

## Output Structure

### Header
```
CERTIFICATE OF INCUMBENCY
[EXACT LEGAL ENTITY NAME — match articles of incorporation verbatim]
```

### Certifying Officer Introduction
Secretary or Assistant Secretary certifies by full name and title that they:
- Hold office by valid election or appointment
- Are duly authorized to execute this certificate on behalf of the corporation
- Affirm the corporation is validly existing and **in good standing** under the laws of [State] as of the certificate date

### Numbered Certification Paragraphs

| # | Certification | Key Content |
|---|--------------|-------------|
| 1 | Governing Documents | Bylaws/Operating Agreement attached as **Exhibit A** are true, correct, complete, and currently in effect (identify any amendments) |
| 2 | Authorizing Resolutions | Each resolution identified by adoption date; description of authority granted; certify resolution remains **in full force and effect, unamended and unrescinded** |
| 3 | Incumbency | Tabular officer/director list (see below) |
| 4 | Authority Scope | Signing authority limitations, dollar thresholds, dual-signature or committee-approval requirements |
| 5 | Purpose & Reliance | Named third party, transaction description, explicit reliance authorization |
| 6 | Temporal Scope | Certificate speaks as of execution date; address notification obligations or re-certification intervals for ongoing/multi-closing transactions |

### Reliance Paragraph
> [CORPORATION NAME] hereby authorizes and directs [THIRD PARTY NAME] to rely fully upon all certifications, representations, and information contained in this Certificate in connection with [DESCRIPTION OF TRANSACTION].

### Execution Block
```
IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the undersigned has executed this Certificate as of the ___ day of __________, 20___.


_________________________________
[Printed Name]
Secretary / Assistant Secretary
[Corporation Name]

                    [CORPORATE SEAL]
```

Include a notarial certificate (acknowledgment or jurat as appropriate to the jurisdiction) if required by the third party. Note apostille or consular legalization requirements for international use.

### Exhibit Assembly

| Exhibit | Document |
|---------|---------|
| A | Bylaws / Operating Agreement (complete, with all amendments) |
| B | Board Resolutions / Written Consents |
| C+ | Additional attachments in order referenced |

Each exhibit: labeled cover page referencing this Certificate; Secretary signs or initials each page or includes an incorporated-by-reference certification statement.

## Guidelines

- **Exact legal name is critical** — any variance from the articles of incorporation may cause rejection; verify corporate designator spelling
- **Personal liability exposure** — certifying officer may face personal liability for material misstatements; corporation may be estopped from denying authority certified here
- **Resolution validity** — confirm proper notice (or valid written waiver), quorum satisfaction, and required voting thresholds before certifying resolutions as validly adopted
- **Disclose all authority limitations** — affirmatively state dollar caps, dual-signature rules, or committee prerequisites; omitting apparent-authority traps creates legal risk
- **Cross-reference consistency** — entity name, officer titles, and authorized actions must match across all closing deliverables (good standing certificate, legal opinion, transaction documents)
- **Bank-specific add-ons** — financial institutions commonly require FinCEN beneficial ownership certification, taxpayer identification number, absence-of-bankruptcy representation, or sanctions/AML compliance language; review any checklist before finalizing
- **Multi-closing transactions** — for revolving facilities or staged closings, address whether the corporation must provide notice of material changes or deliver updated certificates at specified intervals
- **Jurisdiction** — U.S. state corporate law governs; applicable notice, quorum, and voting rules vary by state — verify against the incorporating state's statute [VERIFY]
