# Parole Hearing Summary

Distills parole hearing transcripts and exhibits into a structured, standalone record for attorney reference and future hearing preparation.

## Prerequisites

1. **Hearing transcript(s)** — full or partial transcript of the parole proceeding
2. **Exhibits and documentary evidence** — disciplinary records, psych evaluations, program certificates, parole plans, risk assessments, support letters
3. **Victim impact statements** — written or oral, if available
4. **Prior hearing records** — previous decisions, denial reasons, conditions (if applicable)

## Output Structure

### 1. Case Identification Header

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Inmate Name / ID | Full name, DOC or BOP number |
| Offense of Conviction | Charge(s), conviction date, case number |
| Current Sentence | Term, minimum/maximum, time served |
| Hearing Date & Location | Date, facility, hearing type (initial/subsequent) |
| Board Panel | Names and titles of board members |
| Participants | Inmate, counsel, victims/representatives, witnesses, officials |

### 2. Procedural Matters

- Opening statements or preliminary rulings
- Continuances, objections, or procedural issues raised

### 3. Inmate Testimony

Summarize with attention to these specific areas:

- [ ] Acceptance of responsibility
- [ ] Expressions of remorse
- [ ] Account of offense circumstances
- [ ] Institutional conduct and disciplinary history
- [ ] Rehabilitative programming (substance abuse, mental health, anger management)
- [ ] Educational / vocational achievements
- [ ] Post-release plan: housing, employment, family support, supervision
- [ ] Board Q&A — capture substance of board questions and inmate responses

### 4. Victim Impact

- Key points from victim or representative statements
- Ongoing impact of the crime described
- Public safety concerns raised
- Position on parole suitability (support / oppose / neutral)

### 5. Witness Testimony

- Summary of each witness (family, employers, counselors, experts)
- Relationship to inmate and substance of testimony
- Board reaction or questioning of witnesses

### 6. Documentary Evidence

| Exhibit | Type | Key Content | Board Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| e.g., Ex. A | Psych evaluation | Low recidivism risk per [instrument] | Questioned methodology |
| e.g., Ex. B | Program certificates | Completed X, Y, Z programs | Noted positively |

Include: disciplinary records, risk assessments, parole plans, expert reports, support letters.

### 7. Board Decision

**If DENIED:**
- Specific reasons cited
- Statutory/regulatory factors referenced
- Next eligible hearing date
- Recommendations for inmate before next hearing

**If GRANTED:**
- Proposed release date
- Conditions of parole imposed
- Special supervision requirements
- Reporting obligations

**If CONTINUED/DEFERRED:**
- Information or actions required before final determination
- Timeline for follow-up

### 8. Strategic Analysis

- Key factors that influenced the board's decision
- Unresolved issues affecting future hearings
- Recommendations for counsel preparing subsequent proceedings
- Comparison to prior hearing outcomes (if applicable)

## Guidelines

- **Objectivity** — report testimony and evidence without advocacy framing; distinguish between direct testimony, documentary evidence, and board commentary
- **Quotations** — use direct quotes sparingly, only when specific language is critical to understanding board reasoning or inmate admissions
- **Length** — target 3–8 pages depending on hearing complexity
- **Factual basis** — every assertion must trace to the hearing record; flag anything inferred with `[INFERRED]`
- **Section headings** — maintain all section headings even if a section is "N/A" to preserve scanability
- **Confidentiality** — note any sealed portions or redacted testimony; do not reconstruct sealed content
