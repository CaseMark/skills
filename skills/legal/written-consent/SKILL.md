# Written Consent in Lieu of Meeting

Drafts a legally sufficient written consent enabling directors or shareholders to take formal corporate action without a meeting.

## Prerequisites

1. **Entity identification** — full legal name, jurisdiction of incorporation, entity type (corporation, LLC)
2. **Governing documents** — bylaws or operating agreement, especially consent-procedure provisions (notice periods, approval thresholds, waiting periods)
3. **Consent type** — board consent vs. shareholder consent
4. **Signatory roster** — current directors (board consent) or shareholders with share counts, classes, and ownership percentages (shareholder consent)
5. **Proposed actions** — specific resolutions to be approved, with supporting transaction documents if applicable
6. **Applicable statute** — confirm the authorizing provision (e.g., DGCL §141(f) for board, §228 for shareholders)

## Output Structure

### Document Components

| Section | Requirements |
|---|---|
| **Title** | Centered, bold: `WRITTEN CONSENT OF THE [BOARD OF DIRECTORS/SHAREHOLDERS] OF [ENTITY NAME] IN LIEU OF [ANNUAL/SPECIAL] MEETING` |
| **Preamble** | Cite specific statutory authority (code, section, subsection); reference bylaws/OA provisions; state whether unanimous or majority consent required; specify effective date |
| **Recitals** | WHEREAS clauses: identify each signatory by name/title/shares; state factual background and business rationale; address prerequisites, conditions precedent, conflicts of interest |
| **Resolutions** | Numbered, each beginning with `RESOLVED,` — see resolution drafting rules below |
| **Execution clause** | Counterparts permitted; electronic signature authorization (cite E-SIGN Act or state equivalent if applicable); filing directive to secretary |
| **Signature blocks** | Board: name + "Director" + date line. Shareholders: name + shares held (number, class, % of outstanding) + date line |
| **Secretary's certificate** | Attestation that consent was duly executed, filed with corporate records, and remains in effect |

### Resolution Drafting Rules

- **Contracts/transactions**: identify counterparty by full legal name, all material terms, monetary amounts; attach agreement as numbered exhibit rather than approving "in substantially the form presented"; specify scope of officer authority to make changes
- **Officer authorizations**: name authorized officers, document categories, monetary limits, individual vs. joint authority, time/transaction limitations
- **Equity issuances**: exact number, class/series, price or valuation method, recipient identity, vesting/transfer restrictions, form of consideration, securities law compliance confirmation, preemptive rights clearance
- **Governing document amendments**: include full amendment text or attach as exhibit with incorporation by reference; identify exact section amended; confirm supermajority or other procedural compliance
- **Financial transactions**: maximum principal, material terms (rate, maturity), collateral/security interests, lender identity, delegation parameters for final negotiation

### Consent Threshold Reference

| Jurisdiction | Board Consent | Shareholder Consent |
|---|---|---|
| Delaware (DGCL) | Unanimous required — §141(f) | Majority of voting power sufficient — §228 (unless charter requires more) |
| Model Business Corp. Act states | Unanimous required — §8.21 | Unanimous required — §7.04 (unless articles authorize less) |
| Delaware LLC Act | Per operating agreement — §18-404 | Per operating agreement — §18-302 |

> Always verify the specific entity's charter/bylaws for stricter-than-statutory requirements.

## Guidelines

- **Extract information from uploaded documents first** — search organizational documents, prior minutes, bylaws, cap tables, and stock ledgers before requesting information from the user
- **Effective date precision** — some jurisdictions tie effectiveness to the last required signature; others to filing. State this explicitly in the preamble
- **Fundamental actions** (mergers, dissolution, charter amendments) may require unanimous consent even where majority consent is otherwise permitted — verify per jurisdiction
- **Related-party transactions** — include recitals confirming disinterested-director approval, independent valuation, or other conflict-clearance procedures as required
- **Downstream obligations** — flag any disclosure obligations (securities laws, exchange listing rules), regulatory filings, or third-party consent requirements triggered by the approved actions
- **Defined terms** — capitalize consistently; define at first use or in a definitions section for complex consents
- **Cross-references** — verify all internal cross-references and exhibit numbering before finalizing
- **If information is missing or ambiguous**, surface the issue to the user rather than guessing — the consent must be legally sufficient, not merely formally complete
