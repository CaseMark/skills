# Antitrust Investigation Summary

Deliver a decision-ready antitrust investigation brief with evidence-linked chronology, legal theories, and business risk.

## Prerequisites

1. Regulatory correspondence or process documents (CIDs, subpoenas, Second Requests, notices, meeting minutes).
2. Internal investigation materials (interview memos, forensic reviews, privilege log entries).
3. Market and competitive data (market shares, competitors, pricing, entry barriers).
4. Public sources (press, filings, complaints, consent decrees, agency statements).
5. Current status inputs (deadlines, productions, negotiations, upcoming meetings).

## Output Structure / Process

1. Build the key-facts table first and resolve gaps.
2. Draft the summary using the template below, keeping each section concise and source-cited.
3. If multiple authorities or jurisdictions are involved, create one subsection per authority and a consolidated risk section.

| Field | Required Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|

**Citation Standard**
- Attach a source tag to every factual assertion, e.g., `[source: CID-2024-05-12]` or `[source: Memo-Interview-JD-001]`.
- If a fact is inferred, label it as inference and cite the source used.

## Guidelines

- Use exact dates and agency names; do not paraphrase procedural instruments.
- Separate allegations from proven facts; do not speculate.
- Flag missing or uncertain facts explicitly; use `[VERIFY]` for uncertain statutes or foreign-law citations.
- For merger-related matters, state the asserted theory of harm and any divestiture or behavioral remedy signals.
- Include any parallel proceedings (state AGs, EC, CMA, or other competition authorities) as separate subsections.
- Maintain privilege labels and confidentiality headers in the document.
