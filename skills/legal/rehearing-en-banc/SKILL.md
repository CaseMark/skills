# Petition for Rehearing En Banc

Drafts a petition seeking the extraordinary remedy of full appellate court reconsideration of a panel decision under FRAP 35 or state equivalents.

## Prerequisites

1. **Panel opinion** — including any concurrences or dissents signaling division
2. **Prior briefs and record** — appellant/appellee briefs, appendix, relevant record excerpts
3. **Conflicting authority** — identified Supreme Court, circuit, or intra-circuit precedent in tension with the panel decision
4. **Court-specific rules** — local rules for the target circuit/state court (formatting, word limits, special certifications)
5. **Filing deadline confirmed** — 14 days after entry of judgment under FRAP 35(c); state deadlines vary (14–30 days)

## Threshold Analysis

Before drafting, determine which FRAP 35(a) ground applies:

| Ground | Standard | What to Show |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| **Precedent conflict** | Panel decision conflicts with Supreme Court or circuit precedent | Side-by-side comparison of incompatible holdings on the same legal question |
| **Exceptional importance** | Question of exceptional importance to the law | Broad impact beyond the parties — affects a class of litigants, statutory scheme, or recurring legal question |

> Both grounds require more than panel error. Even clear error does not justify en banc review absent conflict or exceptional importance.

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption
- Full court name, case number (including consolidated numbers)
- All parties with appellate designations
- Title: "Petition for Rehearing En Banc"
- Comply with circuit-specific formatting (margins, font, spacing per local rules)

### 2. Introduction (300–400 words max)
- Open with the specific FRAP 35(a) ground
- If conflict: identify the courts/panels in conflict, the exact legal question, and quote conflicting language
- If exceptional importance: identify the broader impact — class of litigants, statutory interpretation, constitutional implications
- Make the case self-evident within two paragraphs

### 3. Statement of the Case (500–800 words)
- Streamlined procedural history — only developments relevant to the en banc petition
- Facts strategically framed to show why this case cleanly presents the legal question
- Do NOT relitigate every factual dispute or recount the full trial record

### 4. Issues Presented (1–3 maximum)
- Each issue as a discrete yes/no question of law
- Frame to highlight conflict or exceptional importance
- Pure legal questions preferred over fact-bound applications
- Courts are skeptical of petitions relitigating every panel ruling — be selective

### 5. Argument (target: within court's word/page limit)

For each issue, structure as:

```
A. Threshold: Why En Banc Review Is Warranted
   - Conflict argument: side-by-side comparison of holdings
     OR
   - Exceptional importance: concrete evidence of broad impact

B. Merits: Why the Panel Erred
   - Legal analysis showing the panel's reasoning is wrong
```

**Conflict arguments checklist:**
- [ ] Precise identification of conflicting decisions
- [ ] Quoted language from both panel opinion and conflicting authority
- [ ] Holding-level conflict (not mere factual differences or dicta)
- [ ] Explanation of resulting uncertainty for practitioners/lower courts

**Exceptional importance checklist:**
- [ ] Categories of cases or litigants affected
- [ ] Impact on statutory/regulatory scheme
- [ ] Case volume or frequency of the recurring question
- [ ] Scholarly commentary or amicus interest if available

**Tone requirements:**
- Acknowledge the high bar explicitly
- Do not simply reassert rejected arguments
- Do not challenge factual findings or credibility determinations
- Address vehicle problems candidly if present

### 6. Conclusion (50–100 words)
- Request: grant petition, vacate panel decision, decide en banc or remand
- No new arguments or emotional appeals

### 7. Required Certifications

| Certificate | Contents |
|-------------|----------|
| **Compliance** | Word count (excluding caption, TOC, TOA, certificates, signature block); typeface (typically 14-pt proportional); word processing software used |
| **Service** | Date, method (CM/ECF for federal), each party/counsel with contact information |
| **Signature block** | Attorney signature, printed name, bar number, firm, address, phone, email, date; verify admission to the appellate court bar |

## Guidelines

- **Deadline is jurisdictional** — count from date of judgment entry (not opinion date if different); account for weekends/holidays under applicable rules; FRAP 35(c) deadline cannot be extended
- **Word/page limits** — FRAP 35(b)(2): 3,900 words or 15 pages; check circuit-specific variations
- **Vehicle assessment** — ideal cases present clean legal questions on developed records without procedural complications or alternative grounds for decision
- **Citation accuracy** — verify every citation against the original source; use pinpoint cites; indicate alterations with brackets and omissions with ellipses; Bluebook format
- **Self-contained document** — judges who did not sit on the panel must understand the petition without prior familiarity with the case
- **Research the court** — en banc grant rates vary dramatically by circuit (some < 1%); consider court composition and jurisprudential dynamics
- **State equivalents** — verify applicable state rule, deadline, and any procedural differences from FRAP 35
