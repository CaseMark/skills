# Quality Control Provisions (Trademark License Exhibit)

Drafts an exhibit to a Trademark License Agreement establishing quality standards, monitoring procedures, and compliance obligations that preserve the licensor's trademark rights under the Lanham Act, 15 U.S.C. § 1127.

## Prerequisites

1. **Executed or draft Trademark License Agreement** — including defined terms, exhibit numbering, territory, licensed marks, and product categories
2. **Brand standards / style guide** — Pantone/CMYK values, approved mark forms, trademark designations (™ / ®)
3. **Approved pre-production samples** (if any) — serving as the definitive quality benchmark
4. **Applicable regulatory framework** — FDA, CPSC, FTC, or industry-specific standards relevant to the licensed products
5. **Quality metrics** — material specs, durability requirements, performance criteria already negotiated

## Output Structure

### Document Header

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Exhibit designation | Next sequential letter/number per agreement structure |
| Title | "Quality Control Standards and Procedures" |
| Integration clause | "This Exhibit is attached to and made part of that certain Trademark License Agreement dated [DATE] by and between [LICENSOR] ('Licensor') and [LICENSEE] ('Licensee'). Capitalized terms have the meanings given in the Agreement unless otherwise defined herein." |

### Section 1: Foundational Quality Standards

- Universal application to all products, services, packaging, and materials bearing the licensed marks
- Statement that compliance is a **material term** of the Agreement
- Quality floor: must meet or exceed quality of licensor's comparable products under the same marks
- Reference to approved pre-production samples as the definitive benchmark

**Regulatory Compliance Checklist** (include all that apply):

- [ ] FDA (food, cosmetics, devices)
- [ ] CPSC (consumer product safety)
- [ ] FTC (advertising, labeling)
- [ ] Environmental / import-export regulations
- [ ] Industry-specific certifications

Licensee bears sole responsibility; must maintain all licenses/permits; must promptly notify licensor of any regulatory inquiry or enforcement action.

**Quality Metrics Table** (adapt to product category):

| Category | Metric Examples |
|----------|----------------|
| Materials | Thread count, material grade, ingredient purity |
| Workmanship | Stitching, finish consistency, assembly precision |
| Durability | Wash cycles, wear resistance, shelf life |
| Performance | Functionality standards, safety margins, efficacy |
| Aesthetics | Color matching (Pantone), design fidelity, brand presentation |

### Section 2: Trademark Usage Standards

- Marks displayed only in approved forms, sizes, colors, typefaces, placements per brand standards manual
- Proper trademark designations (™ for common law; ® for registered)
- No alteration, modification, distortion, or abbreviation without prior written consent

**Prohibited Uses:**

1. Connection with unlawful, obscene, or objectionable content
2. Comparative advertising or competitor disparagement
3. Registration or attempted registration of marks or confusingly similar variations
4. Use in corporate names, domain names, or social media handles without authorization
5. Sublicensing except as expressly permitted
6. All advertising, packaging, and promotional materials require same approval process as product samples

### Section 3: Sample Submission & Approval

**Submission Scope** — all items before production, distribution, or commercial use:
- Finished products (all variations)
- Primary and secondary packaging, shipping containers
- Labels, hang tags, instruction manuals
- Advertising/promotional materials (print, digital, video, social)
- POS displays and marketing collateral

**Submission Requirements:**

| Parameter | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| Format | Physical samples (3 per variation) AND/OR high-res digital per licensor spec |
| Lead time | 30–60 days before intended production/launch |
| Review period | 15–30 business days from receipt of complete submission |
| Approval form | Written only; oral approvals insufficient |

**Outcomes:** Approve (may be conditional) · Request revisions · Reject

**Key Limitations on Approval:**
- Does not waive right to inspect production items
- Does not waive other Agreement rights/remedies
- Revocable if production materially differs from approved samples
- Both parties retain approved samples as quality standard

### Section 4: Inspection & Monitoring Rights

- Licensor may inspect licensee's facilities (manufacturing, warehouses, distribution, retail) upon **5–10 business days' notice** (immediate access for suspected material violations, safety concerns, or regulatory issues)
- Access to personnel, manufacturing/QC records, production logs, testing results
- Right to take random samples at no charge for testing (lab analysis, performance, safety, comparative, consumer perception)

**Cost Allocation:**

| Scenario | Costs Borne By |
|----------|---------------|
| Compliant inspection | Licensor |
| Non-compliant inspection + follow-up | Licensee |

**Licensee's Own QC Obligations:**
- Maintain documented QC procedures and regular testing protocols
- Designate a QC officer as primary liaison
- Submit periodic quality reports (quarterly or as specified) covering compliance status, issues identified/corrected, and production volumes

### Section 5: Non-Compliance & Remediation

**Graduated Cure Framework:**

| Severity | Cure Period | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Minor (no safety/brand risk) | 15–30 days | Cosmetic deviations, minor labeling errors |
| Serious | 5–10 days | Material quality shortfall, unapproved mark usage |
| Critical (safety/regulatory/severe brand harm) | Immediate; no cure period | Safety defects, regulatory violations |

**Adequate Cure Requires:**
1. Detailed corrective action plan approved by licensor
2. Cessation of production/distribution of non-conforming products
3. Destruction or rework of non-conforming inventory (documented)
4. Enhanced QC procedures to prevent recurrence
5. New samples demonstrating correction

**Failure to Cure → Licensor Remedies** (cumulative):
- Suspension (partial or full)
- Termination per Agreement terms
- Monetary damages for mark/goodwill harm
- Injunctive relief

### Section 6: Product Recall & Market Withdrawal

- Licensor may require immediate cessation of production/distribution and recall in its sole discretion
- Scope ranges from targeted lot/SKU recall to comprehensive public recall

**Licensee Recall Obligations:**
1. Notify all distributors, retailers, and channel partners
2. Consumer notification via channels directed by licensor
3. Establish return/refund/replacement process
4. Retrieve recalled products from all channels (documented)
5. Destroy/dispose of recalled products to prevent re-entry; certify destruction

**All recall costs borne exclusively by Licensee** — notification, logistics, destruction, refunds, licensor oversight costs.

**Indemnification:** Licensee defends, indemnifies, and holds harmless licensor and affiliates from all claims, liabilities, damages, and expenses (including attorneys' fees) arising from non-conforming products, quality deficiencies, regulatory violations, recalls, or breach of these provisions. Survives termination.

### Section 7: Acknowledgments

Licensee expressly acknowledges:
1. Quality control is essential to trademark validity and enforceability under federal and state law
2. Licensor's trademark rights depend on ability to control nature and quality of goods sold under the marks; failure may result in abandonment
3. These provisions are **material terms**; material breach entitles licensor to immediate termination and all available remedies
4. Licensee has expertise, resources, and operational capability to meet these standards
5. Licensee will not challenge validity/enforceability of licensed marks or claim ownership
6. All goodwill from licensee's use vests exclusively in licensor
7. Upon termination, licensee has no residual rights in marks or associated goodwill

## Guidelines

- Use "shall" for obligations, "may" for discretionary rights, "will" for future events
- Match all defined terms exactly to the principal License Agreement
- Define any new terms upon first use in this exhibit
- Use numbered sections/subsections; avoid vague cross-references ("above," "herein")
- Verify exhibit does not conflict with main agreement terms, remedies, or termination provisions
- Adapt regulatory compliance and quality metrics sections to the specific product category
- If industry-specific standards exist (ISO, ASTM, etc.), incorporate by reference
- Include specific Pantone/CMYK values and approved mark artwork references where available from brand guidelines
