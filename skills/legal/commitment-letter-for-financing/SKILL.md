# Commitment Letter for Financing

Drafts a lender's commitment letter that bridges preliminary negotiations and final loan documentation, with binding economic terms and enumerated conditions precedent to funding.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party details** — full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions of formation, and principal places of business for lender, borrower, any guarantors, and co-borrowers
2. **Loan economics** — commitment amount (fixed or maximum), interest rate structure (fixed/variable, index, margin), term, amortization schedule, balloon provisions, and fee schedule
3. **Transaction type** — commercial real estate acquisition, construction, business expansion, or other (drives collateral and condition requirements)
4. **Collateral description** — asset type, estimated value, location/identification, and any existing liens
5. **Proposed closing date** — for commitment expiration deadline and funding timeline

## Output Structure

### 1. Header / Parties Block
- Commitment date and lender letterhead placeholder
- Full legal names, entity types, and jurisdictions for all obligated parties
- Binding vs. non-binding designation — specify which provisions survive regardless of overall character

### 2. Financial Terms

| Term | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Commitment Amount | Fixed sum or maximum; note if multiple disbursements |
| Interest Rate | Fixed or variable; index (SOFR/Prime/other) + margin; floor if applicable |
| Loan Term | Maturity date; amortization period; balloon payment amount/date |
| Repayment | Payment frequency; P&I or interest-only period(s) |
| Use of Proceeds | Stated purpose; any restrictions on fund deployment |
| Fees | Commitment fee (refundable/non-refundable), origination, unused line, prepayment penalty/yield maintenance |
| Funding Date(s) | Anticipated date; multi-draw schedule if applicable |

### 3. Conditions Precedent to Funding

**Due Diligence & Valuation**
- [ ] Satisfactory completion of legal, financial, environmental, and operational due diligence
- [ ] Acceptable appraisal/valuation of collateral
- [ ] No material adverse change in borrower's financial condition, operations, or collateral value

**Documentation**
- [ ] Execution of definitive loan documents (note, mortgage/deed of trust, security agreement, guaranty)
- [ ] Perfection of security interests in all collateral
- [ ] Organizational documents and good standing certificates (all relevant jurisdictions)
- [ ] Evidence of requisite corporate/partnership approvals and signatory authority
- [ ] Legal opinion from borrower's counsel (enforceability, authority, no conflicts with existing obligations)
- [ ] Title insurance commitment (real estate transactions)

**Third-Party & Regulatory**
- [ ] Evidence of adequate property and liability insurance (lender named as additional insured/mortgagee)
- [ ] Required third-party consents, subordination agreements, or intercreditor arrangements
- [ ] Applicable regulatory approvals or permits

**Financial Condition**
- [ ] Delivery of current financial statements and projections acceptable to lender
- [ ] Evidence of required equity contribution or other funding sources

### 4. Representations & Warranties

Borrower represents as of commitment date and as of closing:

| Category | Key Representations |
|----------|---------------------|
| Organizational | Legal existence, good standing, authority to borrow, no conflicts with existing agreements |
| Financial | Accuracy of financial statements; no undisclosed material liabilities |
| Legal | No material pending/threatened litigation; no existing defaults |
| Compliance | Tax compliance; environmental compliance; all required permits obtained |
| Collateral | Ownership; absence of undisclosed liens; property condition (RE transactions) |
| No MAC | No material adverse change since most recent financial statement date |

### 5. Interim Borrower Obligations (Commitment Period)

- Maintain existing insurance; preserve collateral value
- Provide updated financial information upon lender request
- No additional indebtedness without lender consent
- No sale, transfer, or further encumbrance of collateral
- Prompt notice to lender of any material adverse developments

### 6. Expiration, Termination & Cost Allocation

- **Expiration**: Fixed date and time; automatic termination unless extended by written agreement
- **Lender termination rights**: Failure to satisfy conditions precedent; MAC event; borrower breach of interim covenants; material inaccuracy of representations
- **Fee retention**: Specify non-refundable amounts and circumstances for forfeiture
- **Cost allocation**: State which party bears legal fees, due diligence costs, appraisal fees, and title insurance premiums regardless of whether transaction closes

### 7. General Provisions

- Governing law (lender's jurisdiction or collateral situs)
- Forum selection clause (exclusive or non-exclusive venue)
- Confidentiality (surviving termination)
- Explicit identification of provisions that survive expiration or termination (fees, confidentiality, governing law)
- Counterpart execution / e-signature authorization

### 8. Signature Block

- All parties: name, title, entity name, date
- Note if corporate seal or attestation required
- Separate execution page; attach schedules/exhibits for collateral descriptions or form documents to be delivered at closing

## Guidelines

- **Usury compliance**: Verify interest rate against applicable state usury limits before finalizing; commercial exemptions vary by jurisdiction [VERIFY current thresholds for target state]
- **MAC definition**: Define "material adverse change" with objective, measurable standards where possible to prevent closing disputes
- **Fee enforceability**: Non-refundable commitment fees are generally enforceable but must be clearly disclosed; verify against consumer protection statutes if borrower is an individual
- **Non-binding provisions**: Clearly label non-binding terms; enumerate surviving binding provisions (fees, confidentiality, exclusivity) with explicit language
- **SOFR rate references**: Use current SOFR conventions and Credit Spread Adjustment language for variable-rate loans; avoid any LIBOR reference [VERIFY applicable SOFR term conventions for loan type]
- **Environmental**: For real estate collateral, condition on receipt of acceptable Phase I ESA; Phase II if Phase I reveals recognized environmental conditions
- **Target length**: 5–15 pages depending on transaction complexity
