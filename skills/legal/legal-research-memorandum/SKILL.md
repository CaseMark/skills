# Legal Research Memorandum

Generates a formal research memo addressing a legal question with comprehensive authority analysis and practical recommendations.

## Prerequisites

- Clearly defined legal question or issue
- Relevant jurisdiction(s)
- Key facts of the matter
- Any controlling or relevant authorities already identified

## Workflow

### 1. Frame the Issue

State the legal question precisely. Identify the jurisdiction, applicable body of law (federal, state, regulatory), and any sub-issues that must be resolved to answer the primary question.

### 2. Research Controlling Authority

Identify binding authority: constitutional provisions, statutes, regulations, and appellate decisions from the controlling jurisdiction. Organize by hierarchy and recency.

### 3. Analyze Persuasive Authority

Survey persuasive sources: decisions from other jurisdictions addressing the same issue, treatises, law review articles, and Restatements. Note trends across jurisdictions.

### 4. Construct Analysis

For each position, present: the strongest supporting authority with pinpoint citations, factual distinctions from the present matter, counterarguments and their supporting authority, and an assessment of likely outcome.

### 5. Deliver Conclusion and Recommendations

State the most probable outcome with confidence level. Recommend specific actions: litigation strategy adjustments, settlement considerations, contractual protections, or further research needed on unresolved sub-issues.

## Pitfalls

- Citing overruled or superseded authority without checking current validity
- Presenting only favorable authority without addressing counterarguments
- Failing to distinguish binding from persuasive authority in the analysis
