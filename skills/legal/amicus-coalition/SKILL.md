# Amicus Coalition Management

Operationalizes governance, drafting, conflict resolution, sign-off, and filing for multi-organization coalition amicus briefs in appellate courts.

## Prerequisites

1. **Docket info** — court, case number, caption, all deadlines
2. **Parties' briefs** — opening, response, reply (as available); key orders (cert grant, scheduling, consolidation)
3. **Forum rules** — amicus brief rules (word/page limits, disclosure requirements, consent/leave rules, cover formatting, e-filing specs). **Must be retrieved and verified per forum; flag if unverifiable** `[VERIFY]`
4. **Coalition roster** — full legal names, jurisdiction of incorporation, preferred display names, entity types (corp, nonprofit, association)
5. **Approval pathways** — authorized signatory per org, internal review chain, required lead time
6. **Red lines** — positions each org cannot support, factual assertions it cannot endorse
7. **Counsel-of-record details** — names, bar numbers, addresses, phone, email; pro hac vice needs; filing system (CM/ECF, SCOTUS e-filing, paper)
8. **Funding/authorship disclosure inputs** — ask every member: (a) did party or party's counsel author any part? (b) did anyone other than amici/counsel fund preparation or submission? (Required by FRAP 29(a)(4)(E) / Supreme Court Rule 37.6)
9. **Posture** — supporting which party, or neither; consent status or need for motion for leave

**Do not proceed until all prerequisites are collected or gaps are flagged to the user.**

## Process

### Step 1: Governance & Timeline

| Element | Action |
|---|---|
| Single-pen authority | Lead counsel controls final narrative and legal integrity; coalition provides input, not co-drafting |
| Authority map | For each org: who can commit to join, who approves final text, who can make public statements |
| Backward timeline | Build from filing deadline; include concept outline → near-final draft → final proof → sign-off cutoff |
| Hard cutoff | Orgs without sign-off by cutoff date are excluded — no "pending approval" listings |

Send a **Workflow Memo** to all members at kickoff:

```
To meet the filing deadline, please provide:
(1) One consolidated comment set by [DATE/TIME]
(2) Written authorization from an authorized approver to join by [DATE/TIME]
Absent authorization by that time, your organization will not be listed.
```

### Step 2: Position Alignment

Before any full draft:

1. Gather each org's desired emphasis (economic, technical, historical, policy)
2. Cross-check against parties' briefs to eliminate duplication
3. Produce **Issue-Framing Memo** (1–2 pages):
   - Proposed argument headings
   - Key factual propositions (each with reliable citation)
   - Proposed use of social science / technical materials
   - Brief's posture
4. Solicit red lines in writing:

```
Please confirm whether your organization can be listed in support of the
argument that [X]. Identify any positions you cannot endorse now.
```

### Step 3: Draft Management

| Rule | Rationale |
|---|---|
| One official draft, one sender | Prevents version proliferation |
| Naming convention: `AmicusCoalition_Brief_Draft[N]_YYYYMMDD` | Version integrity |
| One point person per org, one consolidated comment set | Avoids conflicting markups |
| Triage comments into: (a) legal/argument, (b) factual/citation, (c) policy/branding | Legal clarity governs over branding preferences |
| All internal markup is confidential under Model Rule 1.6 | Do not forward outside coalition without permission |
| Late new sections must include equal-length deletion proposal | Enforces word-limit discipline |

### Step 4: Conflict Resolution

**Diagnose conflict type:**

| Type | Resolution |
|---|---|
| **Core** (org cannot be associated with the legal position) | Narrow the position, modularize language, or allow withdrawal of subset |
| **Expressive** (disagreement on emphasis/phrasing) | Favor legal clarity and court credibility |

**Compromise patterns:**

- Statutory avoidance: *"This Court can and should resolve on statutory grounds; if it reaches constitutional issues, the proper framework is…"*
- Narrow consensus: *"Amici take no position on the precise outer bounds of [test], but submit that under any plausible formulation, [party] prevails here."*
- Procedural framing: Reframe around procedural deficiency rather than substantive policy validity

**Escalation protocol:** If conflict threatens core thesis or timing → written issue log (contested text, each org's position, proposed resolution, decision deadline) → escalate to lead attorney → coordinate with supported party's counsel if needed (maintaining confidentiality boundaries). Treat issue log as privileged work product.

**Do not** draft language suggesting consensus where none exists (Model Rule 3.3 risk).

### Step 5: Sign-Off Protocol

**Two-step authorization:**

1. **Join authorization** on near-final draft — written, from authorized person:

```
On behalf of [Organization], I confirm I am authorized to approve participation
and that [Organization] joins the amicus brief in [Case Name], substantially in
the form circulated on [DATE]. [Organization] authorizes counsel to make
non-substantive edits (formatting, citations, typos) prior to filing.
```

2. **Final confirmation** on filing version (if time allows)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Written only | No verbal approvals |
| Verify signatory authority | Policy staff enthusiasm ≠ institutional authorization |
| Track entity names precisely | Distinguish association from its foundation, etc. |
| Re-confirm if substantive changes made after sign-off | |
| Default to exclusion if cutoff missed | |

### Step 6: Technical Presentation

**Cover & caption** — verify forum-specific rules for:

- How amici are listed on cover
- Caption format
- Corporate disclosure requirements (FRAP 26.1: parent corp + any publicly held corp owning ≥10% stock)

**Cover format options** (verify against forum rules `[VERIFY]`):

```
BRIEF OF [LEAD AMICUS] AND [N] OTHER ORGANIZATIONS AS AMICI CURIAE
IN SUPPORT OF [PARTY]
```
```
BRIEF OF AMICI CURIAE [CATEGORY, e.g., TECHNOLOGY COMPANIES AND
TRADE ASSOCIATIONS] IN SUPPORT OF [PARTY]
```

**Master roster table:**

| Legal Name | Display Name | Acronym | Entity Type | Corp Disclosure Required? |
|---|---|---|---|---|

**Interest of Amici section:**
- Synthesize by category, not enumerate individually
- Use appendix for individual descriptions if list is long
- Avoid promotional descriptors ("leading," "premier")

**Disclosures checklist:**

- [ ] FRAP 29(a)(4)(E) / Rule 37.6 authorship and funding statement — for every member
- [ ] FRAP 26.1 corporate disclosure — for every corporate amicus
- [ ] Consent of parties stated, or motion for leave attached

**Consistency check:** Amicus list must match across cover, interest statement, signature block, and disclosure statement.

### Step 7: Filing

- [ ] Verify consent/leave requirements for specific forum (FRAP 29 requirements vary by circuit) `[VERIFY]`
- [ ] If motion for leave required, draft and file in advance with proposed brief attached
- [ ] Confirm service requirements (all parties, method, paper copies)
- [ ] Final cite-check — every case citation verified against official reporters
- [ ] All listed amici have timely written authorization
- [ ] Counsel info complete; bar admissions satisfy forum requirements
- [ ] All disclosures included and accurate
- [ ] PDF technical requirements met (bookmarks, fonts, OCR, color covers per forum)
- [ ] Word/page count within limits

**Internal filing certification:**

```
As of [TIMESTAMP]:
- Written authorization received from: [LIST]
- Removed for lack of timely sign-off: [LIST]
- Final PDF: [WORD COUNT/PAGE COUNT]
- Disclosures: included and verified
- Filed/served via: [METHOD]
```

## Jurisdiction Notes

| Forum | Key Requirements |
|---|---|
| **Federal circuits** | FRAP 29; ~6,500-word limit (half of party brief) `[VERIFY per circuit]`; local rules on covers, e-filing, corporate disclosures; D.C. Circuit particularly strict on non-duplication |
| **U.S. Supreme Court** | Rule 37 (amicus briefs), Rule 33.1 (booklet format), green cover for merits amicus; Rule 37.3 timing strictly enforced; use earlier internal cutoffs |
| **State appellate** | Varies dramatically; many require motion for leave; different disclosure regimes; e.g., Cal. Rule of Court 8.520(f) for Cal. Supreme Court `[VERIFY]` |
| **Admin tribunals / specialized courts** | Unique rules or customs; require rule-based confirmation, not inference from prior filings |

**Always retrieve and cite current official rules for the specific forum. Flag all unverifiable procedural statements.**

## Guidelines

- **Ethics:** Treat each amicus as client for conflicts (Model Rules 1.7–1.10) and confidentiality (1.6); verify conflicts before accepting org into coalition
- **Candor:** Do not attribute positions to orgs that have not approved them (Model Rule 3.3); do not include unverifiable factual claims — flag with `[VERIFY]`
- **Citations:** Every non-record factual claim needs a pinpoint citation to a reliable source; flag any unverifiable citation for attorney review
- **Adversarial lens:** Before filing, ask whether opponent could move to strike based on misstatements, undisclosed funding, noncompliance, or overreaching tone
- **Unique contribution test:** Brief must say something the parties' briefs do not; if it merely repeats party arguments, restructure
- **All outputs require attorney review before filing**
