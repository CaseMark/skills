# Stock Option Grant Agreement

Drafts a binding stock option grant agreement governing issuance of ISOs or NQSOs, integrated with the company's equity incentive plan and corporate governance framework.

## Prerequisites

1. **Equity incentive plan** — adopted plan document with current amendments
2. **Board/committee authorization** — resolution specifying grant date, share count, exercise price, recipient
3. **Corporate documents** — certificate of incorporation, bylaws (authorized shares, stock classes, issuance restrictions)
4. **Recipient's service agreement** — employment/consulting agreement with any equity, acceleration, or post-termination provisions
5. **Cap table context** — stockholders' agreement, investor rights agreement, ROFR/co-sale/drag-along obligations
6. **Valuation** — 409A valuation (private) or closing price (public) establishing FMV as of grant date
7. **Insider trading / equity admin policies** if any

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption & Recitals

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Title | "Stock Option Grant Agreement" |
| Grant Date | Exact date from board resolution |
| Company | Full legal name + state of incorporation |
| Optionee | Full legal name |
| Plan Reference | Exact plan title, adoption date, last amendment date |
| Incorporation | "Subject to and governed by the Plan" |

### 2. Definitions

Draft precise definitions for each:

| Term | Key Requirements |
|---|---|
| Option | State ISO (§422) or NQSO; if ISO, include savings clause converting excess to NQSO |
| Shares | Class of stock (typically common) |
| Continuous Service | Employee/director/consultant; whether parent/subsidiary service counts |
| Exercise Price | Per-share price + FMV methodology used |
| Vesting Commencement Date | Grant date, hire date, or other specified date |
| Cause | Dishonesty, fraud, felony conviction, fiduciary breach, material policy violation |
| Change in Control | Merger, asset sale, stock sale, board composition change — specify threshold percentages |
| Disability / Death | As defined in plan or agreement |

### 3. Grant Terms

- [ ] Number of shares and exercise price per share
- [ ] ISO: exercise price ≥ 100% FMV (110% for >10% shareholders)
- [ ] NQSO: exercise price ≥ FMV (§409A compliance)
- [ ] Maximum term: 10 years (5 years for >10% shareholder ISOs)

### 4. Vesting Schedule

Standard four-year template (customize as needed):

```
Cliff:       12 months — 0% vests before first anniversary
Year 1:      25% vests on first anniversary of Vesting Commencement Date
Years 2–4:   Remaining 75% vests in 36 equal monthly installments
Condition:   Continued Service through each vesting date
```

For performance-based vesting: specify measurable milestones, verification method, and certification process.

**Acceleration provisions:**

| Trigger | Type | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Change in Control alone | Single-trigger | Specify % or full acceleration |
| CiC + qualifying termination within 12–18 months | Double-trigger | Specify % or full acceleration |
| Death or Disability | Single-trigger | Per plan terms |
| Involuntary termination w/o Cause (no CiC) | If applicable | Per employment agreement |

Confirm alignment with any separate employment/severance/CiC agreement.

### 5. Exercise Procedures

**Payment methods (include all that apply):**
- Cash or check
- Broker-assisted cashless exercise
- Net exercise / share withholding
- Stock swap (previously owned shares)

**Post-exercise delivery:**
- Book-entry or certificate issuance
- Private company: securities law legend + stockholders' agreement legend
- Public company: market stand-off / lock-up obligations

### 6. Termination Matrix

| Scenario | Unvested Options | Vested Options — Exercise Period |
|---|---|---|
| Cause | Immediately terminate | Immediately terminate |
| Voluntary resignation | Immediately terminate | 30–90 days (not beyond max term) |
| Involuntary w/o Cause | Terminate (unless acceleration) | 90 days (or longer per agreement) |
| Death | Terminate (unless acceleration) | 12 months; exercisable by estate/beneficiary |
| Disability | Terminate (unless acceleration) | 12 months |

**Change in Control treatment:**
1. If option assumed/substituted → vesting continues per original schedule (+ acceleration terms)
2. If not assumed/substituted → full vesting immediately prior to closing; limited exercise window
3. Cash-out permitted → cancellation for payment equal to spread (exercise price vs. FMV)
4. All CiC provisions must comply with §409A

### 7. Tax Provisions

**NQSO:**
- Ordinary income at exercise = FMV − exercise price
- Subject to income tax withholding + employment taxes
- Withholding methods: cash, share withholding, sell-to-cover
- Exercise is condition-precedent on satisfying withholding

**ISO:**
- No ordinary income at exercise if holding periods met (2 years from grant + 1 year from exercise)
- AMT adjustment = spread at exercise
- Disqualifying disposition notice requirement to company
- Include explanation of holding period requirements

**Reporting:** W-2 (employees), 1099 (consultants), Form 3921 (ISO exercises), Form 3922 (ESPP)

### 8. Securities Law Compliance

- [ ] Shares unregistered; issued under §4(a)(2), Rule 701, or Reg D exemption
- [ ] Investment representations: acquiring for investment, not distribution; understands risk; access to information
- [ ] Transfer restriction: non-transferable except by will or intestacy
- [ ] Reference stockholders'/ROFR/co-sale/drag-along agreements
- [ ] §16 officers/directors: ensure Rule 16b-3 compliance (board or non-employee director committee approval)

### 9. Administrative Provisions

- [ ] Governing law: state of incorporation, no conflicts principles
- [ ] Severability clause
- [ ] Plan controls over agreement in event of conflict; plan administrator interpretations are final
- [ ] Amendment: written + signed by both parties; company may amend unilaterally for legal compliance (§409A, securities, tax)
- [ ] Notice mechanics: delivery method + deemed-received timing
- [ ] No employment guarantee / at-will acknowledgment
- [ ] Data privacy consent for plan administration and third-party transfers
- [ ] International addendum placeholder for non-US optionees

### 10. Execution & Exhibits

**Signature blocks:**
- Company: authorized officer (CEO/CFO), title, date
- Optionee: signature, printed name, date

**Optionee acknowledgment section:**
- [ ] Receipt of plan document and summary
- [ ] Opportunity to consult tax/legal advisors
- [ ] Understanding that option is subject to both agreement and plan

**Exhibit A — Grant Summary:**

```
Grant Date:                  [DATE]
Number of Option Shares:     [NUMBER]
Exercise Price Per Share:     $[PRICE]
Option Type:                  [ISO / NQSO]
Vesting Commencement Date:   [DATE]
Vesting Schedule:            [CLIFF + MONTHLY/QUARTERLY]
Option Expiration Date:      [DATE]
```

Exhibit incorporated by reference; signed/initialed by both parties.

## Guidelines

- Always confirm option type (ISO vs. NQSO) — tax treatment diverges significantly
- ISO annual vesting limit: $100,000 FMV (measured at grant) first exercisable per year; excess converts to NQSO [IRC §422(d)] [VERIFY]
- §409A: exercise price must never be below FMV at grant for NQSOs; discounted options trigger immediate taxation + 20% penalty
- For private companies, require current 409A valuation (safe harbor: independent appraisal within 12 months)
- >10% shareholder ISOs: 110% FMV exercise price + 5-year max term [IRC §422(c)(5)] [VERIFY]
- Do not draft tax advice as legal advice — include disclaimer directing optionee to personal tax advisor
- Cross-check acceleration terms against all existing agreements to prevent conflicts
- If company has outstanding preferred stock, confirm common stock class and any anti-dilution or protective provisions affecting option shares
