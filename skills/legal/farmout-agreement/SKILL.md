# Farmout Agreement

Draft a recordable farmout agreement that defines earning obligations, earned interests, and post-earn operations.

## Prerequisites

1. Party identities, entity types, formation jurisdictions, addresses, and signing authority.
2. Lease schedule with recording data, current working interest and net revenue interest, and all burdens.
3. Legal description and contract area map, including depth/formation limits.
4. Earning obligations: well count, location, depth/formation, commencement deadline, completion standard, dry hole rules, substitute well rules.
5. Cost responsibility: pre-earn (typically 100% Farmee) and post-earn cost sharing.
6. JOA form and version year, operator designation, and negotiated deviations.
7. AMI parameters if applicable: boundaries, term, offer mechanics, exclusions.
8. Title examination window, title standard, cure timeline, and remedies.
9. Regulatory and recording requirements for the state where the lands sit.

## Output Structure / Process

1. Validate inputs.
Required fields checklist:
- Contract area description and exhibit references.
- WI/NRI math and burdens.
- Earning well location and depth criteria.
- Deadlines and force majeure triggers.
- Operator and JOA form/version.

2. Build exhibits.
Exhibit list:
- Exhibit A: Lease schedule with recording data and WI/NRI by lease.
- Exhibit B: Legal description and contract area map.
- Exhibit C: Earning well location (plat or coordinates).
- Exhibit D: JOA form and any addenda.
- Exhibit E: AMI map and description (if used).
- Exhibit F: Form of Assignment and Bill of Sale.
- Exhibit G: Memorandum of Farmout (if recording summary only).

3. Draft agreement sections in order.
| Section | Must include |
| --- | --- |
| Parties and Recitals | Purpose, prior LOI/term sheet status, intent to earn interest. |
| Definitions | Contract Area, Earning Well, Earning Date, Commencement, Completion, WI, NRI, AMI. |
| Grant/Farmout | Interest to be earned, burdened WI/NRI, title standard. |
| Lands/Depths | Horizontal and vertical limits, formation scope. |
| Earning Obligations | Well count, location, depth, deadlines, reporting, force majeure. |
| Cost Allocation | Pre-earn costs, post-earn cost sharing, payout triggers. |
| JOA Integration | Form/version, operator, conflict clause. |
| Title Examination | Access, defect notice, cure period, remedies. |
| AMI | Area, term, offer mechanics, exclusions. |
| Reassignment/Reversion | Failure to earn, surrender after earning, P&A duties. |
| Regulatory/Recording | Filing responsibilities, recordable form, memo vs full. |
| Reps/Warranties | Defensible title, authority, no undisclosed burdens. |
| Indemnity/Insurance | Operational liability allocation, environmental split. |
| Miscellaneous | Law, venue, notices, assignment limits, counterparts. |

4. Insert clause templates as needed.

```text
Earning Obligation.
Farmee shall drill [one] Earning Well on [Legal Description/Exhibit C] to a minimum [TVD/MD] of [depth] or to the [formation], and shall commence drilling no later than [date]. "Commence" means [spud/move rig/construct location]. Farmee shall complete drilling by [date]; "complete" means [reach TD/run casing/complete for production/first production in paying quantities]. A dry hole that reaches the required depth and tests the target formation [does/does not] satisfy the Earning Obligation. If the Earning Well is lost due to [mechanical failure/conditions beyond control], Farmee may drill a substitute well within [time] on [same unit/approved location] to satisfy the Earning Obligation.
```

```text
Interest Earned.
Upon satisfaction of the Earning Obligation, Farmor shall assign to Farmee a [__]% Working Interest and [__]% Net Revenue Interest in the Contract Area, subject to existing burdens shown on Exhibit A. If Farmor cannot convey the stated NRI due to title defects or undisclosed burdens, Farmee may elect: (a) proportionate reduction in WI/NRI; (b) Farmor cure within [__] days; or (c) terminate as to affected lands.
```

```text
Reassignment/Reversion.
If Farmee fails to timely commence or complete the Earning Obligation, all rights granted hereunder terminate and revert to Farmor without further action, and Farmee shall plug and abandon any well commenced and restore the surface in accordance with applicable law and lease obligations. After earning, if Farmee elects to surrender or ceases operations and no wells are producing, Farmee shall reassign its interest to Farmor within [__] days using Exhibit F.
```

```text
Title Examination and Cure.
Farmee may examine title within [__] days of the Effective Date. Farmor shall provide access to all title instruments and curative materials. Title standard is [defensible/marketable] title. Farmee shall deliver written defects within [__] days after examination; Farmor shall have [__] days to cure. If uncured, Farmee may elect proportionate reduction, cure at its expense and offset against payments, or terminate as to affected lands.
```

5. Final checks.
Checklist:
- Reconcile WI/NRI with burdens and include proportionate reduction mechanics.
- Define Commencement and Completion with objective milestones.
- State dry hole treatment and substitute well rights.
- Confirm JOA conflict clause and effective date.
- Include recordable acknowledgments and notary blocks.

## Guidelines

- Use "defensible title" unless the client explicitly requires marketable title.
- Align WI/NRI with all burdens; avoid silent NRI shortfalls.
- Define deadlines, force majeure, and reporting with objective triggers.
- Address dry hole treatment and substitute well mechanics explicitly.
- Make the farmout control over JOA for conflicts; specify JOA form/version year.
- Decide whether to record full agreement or memorandum; include recordable acknowledgments.
- Allocate environmental liabilities by period of ownership/operation.
- Disclaim geology and production representations; include "as is, where is" language.
- Verify state-specific regulatory filings and recording formalities; mark uncertain items as [VERIFY].
