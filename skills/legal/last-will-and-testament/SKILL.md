# Last Will and Testament Drafting

Produces a complete probate-ready testament that maps client intent to enforceable will language and state compliance requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. Confirm testator identity, capacity, age, and domicile state at execution.
2. Collect full family map: spouse(s), children (including adopted/stepchildren), minor status, and estranged/excluded persons.
3. Identify governing agreements: marriage agreements, prior wills/codicils, trusts, POA, and beneficiary designations.
4. Gather asset/liability inventory: real estate, business interests, personalty, digital assets, debts, funeral directives, tax preferences.
5. Select fiduciaries: primary and alternate personal representative; guardian(s) for minors (person and estate if split).
6. Confirm testamentary rules needed for the state: witnessing, witness disqualifications, notarization, self-proving standards [VERIFY].
7. Map risks and policy preferences: spendthrift concerns, anti-lapse treatment, common-disaster clauses, potential contests.
8. Decide revocable revocation strategy and compatibility with non-probate plans (trusts, TOD/POD, joint tenancies).

## Output Structure / Process

### 1) Drafting Inputs

| Intake Area | Required Outputs | Common Failure to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Identity | Full legal names, addresses, relationships, domicile | Misspelled names and outdated identity details |
| Family | All children and minor care needs; step/adopted distinctions | Future child/predeceased child disputes |
| Fiduciaries | Executor + alternate; guardian of person/estate | Single executor with no backup |
| Property | Specific gifts list + residue universe | Omitted assets causing intestacy spillover |
| Contingencies | Death-before-testator branches and survivorship periods | Gifts failing under predecease or simultaneous death |
| Tax/Costs | Debt, tax, administration-payment sequence | Hidden tax liabilities and creditor disputes |
| Non-Probate Coordination | Trust triggers, account beneficiary tags, ownership structures | Conflicting transfers or partial revocations |

### 2) Required Will Structure

| Section | Must Include | Draft Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Revocation | Revokes prior wills/codicils | Single clear sentence of supersession |
| Funeral/legacy statements | Optional memorial choices | Keep non-binding where desired |
| Executor powers | Appointment + alternates, authority scope, bond choice | Include broad statutory powers or explicit limits |
| Guardianship | Guardian of person/estate with alternates | Include reasoned notes only if strategically useful |
| Debts and expenses | Funeral/just debts/admin costs ordering | Align with state default rules |
| Specific bequests | Itemized gifts with contingencies | Add anti-ademption fallback where useful |
| Residuary clause | Full residual distribution, percentages/classes | Must include fallback for predeceasing takers |
| Per stirpes/capita | Choice and default mechanics | Explain in plain language in parent clause |
| Distribution protections | No-contest clause (jurisdictional check), survivorship period | Many states restrict [VERIFY] |
| Executor powers | Sale, investment, retention, settlement authority | Preserve liquidity options |
| Finalization | Signature block, witnesses, notary, self-proving affidavit | Match state witness count/order [VERIFY] |

### 3) Execution Package

```text
LAST WILL AND TESTAMENT OF: [Testator Name]
Date: [Execution Date]
State of Domicile: [State]

1. Revocation
2. Executor and Personal Representative
3. Guardianship of Minors
4. Payment of Expenses, Debts, and Taxes
5. Specific Bequests
6. Residual Estate
7. Contingent Distribution Scheme
8. Powers of Executor
9. Final Provisions
10. Signature, Attestation, Self-Proving Affidavit
```

### 5) Output Formatting Requirements

- Use numbered clause order to preserve clarity.
- Insert all legal names as `[full legal name]`.
- Keep bequests in two-column format when complex: beneficiary / gift.
- Flag all uncertain legal assumptions in a dedicated section.
- Provide a clean signature block with witness and notary placeholders.

## Guidelines

- Do: use state-specific statutory authority references sparingly and only where required.
- Do: add contingencies for predeceased or disabled beneficiaries.
- Do: include clear alternate fiduciary/guardian chains.
- Do: include coordination notes for trusts, joint property, beneficiary designations, and tax elections.
- Don’t: give tax avoidance assurances; only identify assumptions and election impacts.
- Don’t: use unresolved placeholders in the final will body (`[TBD]`); require explicit values.
- Don’t: draft no-contest clauses as default in uncertain states; validate before inclusion [VERIFY].
- Prefer attorney review where elective-share, pretermitted-heir, or community-property rules materially alter distribution.
