# Director and Officer Questionnaire

Drafts a structured D&O questionnaire to collect disclosures required for SEC filings, proxy statements, conflict assessments, and transaction due diligence.

## Prerequisites

1. **Company profile** — public vs. private, exchange listing (NYSE/Nasdaq), regulated industry status
2. **Prior questionnaires** — existing D&O questionnaires, proxy statements, Form 10-K biographical sections
3. **Governance documents** — articles of incorporation, bylaws, code of ethics, related party transaction policy, insider trading policy
4. **Disclosure baseline** — most recent proxy statement, current director/officer roster, committee assignments
5. **Purpose** — annual proxy, IPO prep, M&A due diligence, or general governance record

## Output Structure

### Cover Page & Introduction
- Company name, questionnaire purpose (e.g., "2025 Annual Proxy Statement"), submission deadline, designated contact
- Confidentiality notice: information may be disclosed in SEC filings, shared with auditors/outside counsel, provided to transaction counterparties
- Continuing obligation: respondents must report material changes within **5 business days** throughout service and for a reasonable period thereafter

### Key Definitions (embed in document)

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Immediate family | Spouse, domestic partner, parents, stepparents, children, stepchildren, siblings, in-laws, household members (excluding tenants/employees) |
| Beneficial ownership | Direct + indirect ownership including voting/investment power per Rule 13d-3; shares in trusts, family member holdings, controlled entity holdings |
| Related party transaction | Any transaction exceeding applicable threshold where a related person has a direct or indirect material interest (Reg S-K Item 404) |
| Material | Information a reasonable shareholder would consider important; err toward disclosure when uncertain |

### Section I — Personal & Identifying Information
- Full legal name (as on passport/driver's license); former names, maiden names, aliases (past 10 years)
- Business address (for public filings); residential address (confidential)
- Business email, mobile number; date of birth, citizenship/residency status
- Tax ID/SSN (limited use: tax reporting, background verification — store under data security controls)
- Family relationships with other directors, officers, >5% shareholders, or nominees (Item 401(d))

### Section II — Professional Background & Qualifications

Current role: title, appointment date, reporting relationships, material role changes in past fiscal year; for executive officers, existence and material terms of any employment agreement.

Employment history — **10 years** (5 years required by Item 401(e); 10 years recommended for due diligence):

| Employer | Title | Dates (MM/YYYY) | Principal Responsibilities | Departure Reason |
|----------|-------|-----------------|---------------------------|------------------|
| | | | | |

- Education: institution, degree, field, graduation date, honors
- Professional licenses and bar admissions: jurisdiction, license #, current status, any disciplinary history (suspensions, revocations, grievances)
- Qualifications narrative: expertise relevant to board service (financial reporting, risk, cybersecurity, M&A, regulatory compliance, industry knowledge) — supports proxy biographical disclosure per Item 401(e)

### Section III — Board Service & External Positions

All current and prior directorships, trusteeships, advisory board roles, and officer positions — **past 10 years**:

| Organization | Role | Dates | Public/Private | Committee Service | Leadership Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| | | | | | |

- Public companies: include ticker, exchange, principal business
- Audit committee financial expert designation (Item 407(d)(5)) — required in Form 10-K
- Lead independent director, non-executive chair, presiding director roles
- Current public company board/committee attendance record
- Industry association, trade group, or standard-setting organization service

**Adverse Events During Tenure** (Item 401(f) — past 10 years; serious matters disclose regardless of timing):

- [ ] Bankruptcy (Ch. 7, 11, 13), receivership, or insolvency proceedings at any organization where respondent served
- [ ] SEC or other regulatory enforcement actions or consent orders
- [ ] Financial statement restatements for material noncompliance
- [ ] Material litigation judgments or settlements
- [ ] Regulatory sanctions or bars from service
- [ ] Decision not to stand for reelection or pre-term resignation (explain circumstances)

### Section IV — Conflicts of Interest & Related Party Transactions

*(Reg S-K Item 404 — threshold [VERIFY: currently $120,000] for public companies)*

**Business Relationships** — disclose for respondent, immediate family members, or controlled entities:

| Transaction Type | Counterparty | Amount/Value | Terms vs. Arm's-Length | Approval Body |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Goods/services purchase or sale | | | | |
| Consulting/advisory/professional services | | | | |
| Lease or license of property or IP | | | | |
| Loan, guarantee, or credit arrangement | | | | |
| Charitable contributions (where respondent/family serve as director/officer) | | | | |

**Competing Interests:**
- Service as director, officer, employee, consultant, or significant investor in a competitor or related-industry company
- Personal financial interest in company customers, suppliers, distributors, lenders, or potential acquisition targets
- Corporate opportunities pursued individually without board presentation or approval

**Indebtedness:**
- Loans from company to respondent or family members (Note: SOX §402 prohibits company loans to executive officers of public companies [VERIFY current exemptions]; disclose any grandfathered pre-SOX loans with principal amount, rate, maturity, collateral)
- Company guarantees of respondent or family member obligations

**Independence-Impairing Relationships:**
- Employment at or financial interest in company's independent auditors (within past 3 years)
- Interlocking directorates: executive officer of company serving on a board where respondent is also an executive officer
- Significant personal relationships (social, charitable, club) with other directors, officers, or key service providers that could affect perceived independence

### Section V — Legal Proceedings & Regulatory Matters

*(Item 401(f) — past 10 years; certain matters disclose regardless of timing)*

**Criminal Matters:**
- Convictions, guilty pleas, nolo contendere pleas, deferred prosecution agreements (exclude minor traffic violations not involving DUI)
- Pending charges or active investigations by federal, state, or local authorities
- For each: specific charges, jurisdiction/court, date, sentence imposed, current probation/parole status

**Civil Litigation (as named party):**
- Claims involving fraud, misrepresentation, breach of fiduciary duty, securities violations, or banking/commodities law violations
- Litigation arising from service as director, officer, trustee, or fiduciary of any organization
- For each: nature of claims, court/case number, parties, resolution or current status, monetary judgment or settlement amount, any finding of wrongdoing

**Regulatory & Administrative Proceedings:**
- SEC, DOJ, state securities regulators, Federal Reserve, OCC, FDIC, FDA, FINRA, stock exchange investigations or enforcement actions
- Findings of violation, consent orders, monetary penalties, censures, suspensions, or bars from serving as director, officer, investment adviser, broker, or dealer
- Bar disciplinary proceedings, grievances, or sanctions (attorneys); CPA board or PCAOB proceedings (accountants); other professional licensing actions
- SEC Wells notices or comment letters raising significant conduct issues
- Involvement in financial statement restatements, material internal control deficiencies, or accounting irregularities

*Disclose regardless of outcome; company counsel assesses materiality. Non-disclosure creates greater risk than over-disclosure.*

**Transactions & Arrangements in Company Securities:**

## Guidelines

- **Regulated industries**: Add enhanced sections for Financial Services (Federal Reserve/OCC compliance, FINRA licenses), Healthcare/Pharma (FDA, DEA), Defense (security clearances, government contracting), Energy as applicable
- **Public vs. private**: Item references and thresholds apply as written for public companies; for private companies contemplating IPO or M&A, collect information in public-company format proactively
- **10-year look-back**: Required for Item 401(f) legal proceedings; recommended for employment history and board service (exceeds the 5-year minimum for Item 401(e) biographical disclosure)
- **Independence**: Cross-reference NYSE/Nasdaq listing standards applicable to the company; questionnaire data feeds formal independence determinations
- **Annual updates**: Structure for efficient year-over-year refresh — flag stable biographical data vs. fields requiring fresh review each cycle
- **Privacy**: SSN/DOB collected under data security controls; reference CCPA and applicable state privacy statutes in confidentiality notice; limit access to personnel with legitimate business need
- **Materiality standard**: When uncertain whether to disclose, err toward disclosure — counsel evaluates significance; failure to disclose creates securities law exposure
