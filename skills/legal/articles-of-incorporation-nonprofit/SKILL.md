# Articles of Incorporation — Non-Profit Corporation

Drafts a complete, filing-ready Articles of Incorporation for a U.S. non-profit corporation satisfying state formation requirements and the IRS organizational test under IRC § 501(c)(3).

## Prerequisites

1. **Corporate name** — confirmed available via Secretary of State entity search
2. **State of incorporation** — determines controlling statute, mandatory provisions, fee schedule, and formatting rules
3. **Registered agent** — full legal name and physical street address in state of incorporation; written consent if state requires
4. **Incorporators** — full legal names and mailing addresses (typically 1–3; check state minimum)
5. **Mission and programs** — intended activities, beneficiaries, geographic scope (sufficient detail for Form 1023 review)
6. **Governance model** — membership vs. directorship; director count/range; officer titles; term lengths
7. **Effective date** — immediate upon filing or delayed (confirm state maximum lead time)

## Output Structure

Draft articles in sequence. Verify each provision against the state's non-profit corporation statute before finalizing.

---

### Article I — Corporate Name
```
The name of the corporation is [FULL LEGAL NAME].
```
- Include state-required designator (e.g., "Inc.", "Corp.", "Incorporated") if mandated
- Confirm name does not imply commercial, political, or non-charitable character
- Re-confirm availability immediately before filing; name reservation may be needed

---

### Article II — Registered Office and Registered Agent
```
The address of the initial registered office of the corporation is
[STREET ADDRESS, CITY, STATE, ZIP]. The name of its initial registered
agent at such address is [FULL LEGAL NAME].
```
- PO boxes not permitted; must be a physical in-state address
- Agent must maintain regular business hours; failure risks administrative dissolution or default judgment
- Attach agent consent form if required by state

---

### Article III — Purposes

Lead with IRS organizational test language, then add specific mission:
```
This corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, educational, religious,
and/or scientific purposes within the meaning of IRC § 501(c)(3), as amended, or
the corresponding provision of any future United States Internal Revenue law.

Specifically, the corporation's purposes include: [DESCRIBE PROGRAMS, TARGET
POPULATION, GEOGRAPHIC SCOPE — sufficient for Form 1023 scrutiny].
```
- All stated purposes must fall within § 501(c)(3) enumerated categories (charitable, religious, educational, scientific, literary, public safety testing, amateur sports, prevention of cruelty to children/animals)
- Avoid language implying substantial commercial activity, private benefit, or non-exempt purposes
- Breadth should accommodate program evolution without requiring amendment
- Reference IRS Pub. 557 for model language by exempt-purpose category

---

### Article IV — Tax-Exemption Requirements *(irrevocable — do not soften or remove)*

All three blocks are required to satisfy Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(b) and (c):

**Private Inurement Prohibition**
```
No part of the net earnings of the corporation shall inure to the benefit of,
or be distributable to, its members, directors, officers, or other private
persons, except that the corporation is authorized to pay reasonable
compensation for services rendered and to make payments in furtherance of
the purposes in Article III.
```

**Lobbying / Political Activity Limitation**
```
No substantial part of the activities of the corporation shall be the carrying
on of propaganda or otherwise attempting to influence legislation. The corporation
shall not participate in or intervene in (including publishing or distributing
statements) any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate
for public office.
```

**Dissolution Clause**
```
Upon dissolution, assets shall be distributed for one or more exempt purposes
within the meaning of IRC § 501(c)(3), or to the federal government or a state
or local government for a public purpose. Any assets not so disposed of shall
be disposed of by a court of competent jurisdiction of the county in which the
corporation's principal office is then located, exclusively for such purposes.
```

---

### Article V — Membership and Governance

**No-member (directorship) model — most common for operating charities:**
```
The corporation shall have no members. Any action otherwise requiring member
approval shall require only approval of the board of directors.
```

**If members exist:** specify classes, admission criteria, dues, voting rights, matters requiring member vote (dissolution, merger, amendment of Articles), and termination procedures per state statute.

**Board of Directors:**
```
The affairs of the corporation shall be managed by a board of directors
consisting of not less than [MIN] nor more than [MAX] directors, with the
exact number fixed from time to time by board resolution. Directors shall
serve [TERM]-year terms.
```
- Address vacancy-filling and removal procedures in compliance with state law
- State any director qualifications, constituency requirements, or independence standards

**Officers:** Include at minimum President/Chair, Secretary, Treasurer. State whether elected by board or appointed.

---

### Article VI — Incorporators
```
The name and address of each incorporator is as follows:
  [Full Name], [Street Address, City, State, ZIP]
```
Include signature block for each incorporator; add notary acknowledgment if state requires.

---

### Article VII — Duration and Effective Date
```
The duration of the corporation shall be perpetual.
These Articles of Incorporation shall become effective [upon filing /
on [SPECIFIC DATE]].
```

---

### Article VIII — Additional Provisions *(recommended)*

**Director Liability Limitation:**
```
A director shall not be personally liable for monetary damages for breach of
fiduciary duty except for: breach of the duty of loyalty; acts not in good faith
or involving intentional misconduct or a knowing violation of law; or any
transaction from which the director derived an improper personal benefit.
```

**Indemnification:**
```
The corporation shall indemnify directors, officers, employees, and agents to
the fullest extent permitted by [STATE] law against expenses (including
attorneys' fees), judgments, fines, and amounts paid in settlement incurred in
connection with any proceeding arising from their service.
```

**Amendment Procedure:**
```
These Articles may be amended by affirmative vote of [X]% of directors then in
office, provided that no amendment to Article III or Article IV shall adversely
affect the corporation's qualification under IRC § 501(c)(3).
```

## Guidelines

| Topic | Rule |
|---|---|
| § 501(c)(3) irrevocability | Article IV provisions cannot be amended or removed without jeopardizing exempt status — flag any client request to weaken them |
| Private benefit doctrine | Purpose statement must not imply substantial private benefit; see Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(d) |
| Excess benefit transactions | § 4958 conflict-of-interest and compensation policies belong in bylaws; note to client during formation |
| State variance | Mandatory provisions, name designators, registered agent rules, fee schedules, and formatting requirements differ by state — verify at the Secretary of State website for each jurisdiction |
| Name availability | Re-confirm immediately before filing; some states require a separate name reservation filing |
| IRS Pub. 557 | Primary reference for model purpose language and exempt-purpose category descriptions |
| Post-incorporation checklist | Flag as next steps: EIN (IRS Form SS-4), Form 1023 or 1023-EZ, state income tax exemption application, state AG charitable trust registration, charitable solicitation registration |
| Formatting | Check Secretary of State for margin requirements, font rules, page numbering, cover sheet, and acceptable payment methods |
