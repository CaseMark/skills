# Healthcare Power of Attorney

Drafts a jurisdiction-compliant HCPOA that designates an agent, defines scope of authority, captures principal directives, and satisfies state execution formalities.

## Prerequisites

1. **Principal information** — full legal name, address, date of birth
2. **Agent information** — full legal name, address, contact; confirm eligibility under applicable state law
3. **Successor/alternate agent** — same details if designated
4. **State jurisdiction** — governs statutory form, witnesses, notarization, and mandatory language
5. **Healthcare preferences** — principal's wishes on life-sustaining treatment, ANH, CPR, palliative care, organ donation
6. **Any religious/moral directives** or limitations on agent authority

## Guidelines

- **Verify statutory form**: Several states (CA, NY, TX, FL, etc.) have mandatory or model forms — use or adapt as required [VERIFY each state]
- **HIPAA gap**: An HCPOA without explicit HIPAA language may be rejected by providers; always include
- **Co-agent conflicts**: If co-agents appointed, specify joint vs. independent authority
- **Healthcare provider immunity**: Note state immunity provisions for good-faith compliance to aid provider acceptance
- **Organ donation**: Some states require a separate anatomical gift form; confirm whether HCPOA alone suffices
- **Do not** include provisions barring comfort/palliative care where prohibited by state law
- **Recommend** principal retain original; provide copies to agent, primary physician, and hospital of choice
