# Endowment Fund Investment and Spending Policy Statement

Drafts a Board-ready governance instrument for 501(c)(3) endowment management compliant with UPMIFA or applicable state law.

## Prerequisites

1. **Organization basics** — legal name (as in articles), state of incorporation, fiscal year end, governing state law (UPMIFA or other)
2. **Endowment structure** — total AUM, number of funds, fund categories (true/term/quasi-endowments), pooled vs. separate management, any donor-imposed investment restrictions
3. **Investment profile** — external advisors/managers (names, scope), risk tolerance, ESG/mission-related restrictions, prohibited investments, experience with alternatives (PE, hedge, real assets)
4. **Governance structure** — Investment Committee existence, composition, meeting cadence, staff roles (CFO, Treasurer, ED), delegation authority
5. **Spending preferences** — target spending rate (%), averaging period (12 or 20 quarters), underwater fund philosophy, budget dependence on endowment income
6. **Special circumstances** — unusual donor restrictions, pending gifts, legal matters, prior policy conflicts

### Underwater Endowment Policy Options

| Approach | Description | Use When |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Full suspension | No spending until fund restored above historic dollar value | Conservative; donor-sensitive environments |
| Proportional reduction | Spending scaled to current value / historic dollar value | Balances mission needs with stewardship |
| Continued spending | Normal rate applied after UPMIFA prudence analysis | Operationally dependent organizations |

### Roles & Responsibilities Summary

| Party | Authority |
|-------|-----------|
| Board of Directors | Ultimate fiduciary; approves policy, IC appointments, material amendments |
| Investment Committee | Recommends policy changes; selects/monitors managers; reviews performance; reports to Board |
| Staff (CFO/Treasurer) | Implements decisions; processes transactions; maintains records; prepares reports |
| External Managers/Consultants | Discretionary/advisory authority as delegated; subject to IPS guidelines |

## Guidelines

- **UPMIFA factors** — spending decisions must consider: duration/preservation of fund, purposes of organization and fund, general economic conditions, effects of inflation/deflation, expected returns, other organizational resources, and gift instrument [VERIFY state-specific adoptions]
- **Delegation limits** — document any discretionary authority granted to investment managers in writing; Board retains ultimate accountability
- **Quasi-endowments** — clearly distinguish board-designated funds (unilaterally revocable) from donor-restricted endowments; separate accounting required
- **IRS compliance** — excess business holdings and jeopardizing investments rules under IRC §§ 4943–4944 apply to private foundations; confirm 501(c)(3) public charity status before relying on more permissive standards
- **Legal review required** — final document must be reviewed by legal counsel for alignment with state UPMIFA enactment, governing documents, and any existing gift agreements
- **Jurisdiction caveat** — a minority of states have not adopted UPMIFA; confirm governing statute before drafting [VERIFY current state adoption map]
- **No generic placeholders** — replace all bracketed fields with organization-specific data before presenting draft for Board adoption
