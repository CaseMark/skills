# M&A Due Diligence Review

Analyzes data room contents at scale to identify risks and deal-critical issues.

## Prerequisites

1. **Data room** — target company documents in vault
2. **Deal parameters** — type, value, key risk areas, buyer priorities
3. **Checklist** — corporate, contracts, IP, litigation, regulatory, financial, employment, real property, environmental, tax

## Workflow

### 1. Inventory
Catalog against checklist. Identify missing categories. Prioritize by materiality.

### 2. Contracts
Change of control provisions, assignment restrictions, customer/supplier concentration, revenue at risk, non-competes.

### 3. Risks
Litigation (pending/threatened), IP (ownership gaps, licenses), Employment (key persons, benefits), Regulatory (permits, investigations), Financial (off-balance-sheet, related party).

### 4. Report
Executive summary with recommendation. Findings by category. Material issues for negotiation. Info gaps for follow-up. Recommended reps & warranties.

## Pitfalls
- Volume ≠ thoroughness — deep analysis beats document counting
- Start with largest contracts and highest-value obligations
- Missing documents are red flags
- Change-of-control triggers in key contracts can destroy post-close value
