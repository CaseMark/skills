# ALTA Settlement Statement

Drafts a mathematically balanced, ALTA-compliant settlement statement allocating all transaction costs between buyer and seller at closing.

## Prerequisites

1. **Purchase and Sale Agreement** — confirmed sales price, earnest money amount, seller concessions, cost allocation provisions
2. **Title Commitment** — title insurance premiums, endorsements, exception items requiring payoff
3. **Loan Documents** — new loan amount, lender fees, prepaid items required by lender
4. **Payoff Statements** — existing mortgage(s) and lien balances with per diem interest rates
5. **Closing/Disbursement Dates** — both dates required for proration calculations
6. **Tax Records** — current year tax amount, payment status (arrears vs. advance), last paid date
7. **HOA Documents** — dues, transfer fees, special assessments
8. **Commission Instructions** — total amount and split between brokerages

## Output Structure

### Header Block

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Document Title | ALTA SETTLEMENT STATEMENT |
| Settlement Date | Date documents are signed |
| Disbursement Date | Date funds are distributed |
| Settlement Agent | Name, firm, address, phone |
| Property Address | Street address + legal description reference |
| Buyer/Borrower | Full legal name(s) |
| Seller | Full legal name(s) |

---

### Summary of Seller's Transaction

**Credits to Seller**
- Gross sales price
- Seller financing provided (if any)
- Prorated items in seller's favor
- Earnest money (if seller-held)

**Debits Against Seller**
- Existing mortgage payoff(s) + accrued per diem interest
- Second liens and judgment payoffs
- Real estate commissions (listing + selling side)
- Seller-paid closing costs per contract
- Owner's title insurance premium (if seller-paid per contract/local custom)
- Transfer taxes allocated to seller
- Recording fees allocated to seller
- Repair credits / seller concessions to buyer
- HOA dues, transfer fees, estoppel fees through closing
- Prorated property taxes (seller's share through closing date)

**→ Net: Cash Due to Seller** (or Cash Required from Seller if negative)

---

### Summary of Buyer's Transaction

**Debits to Buyer**
- Gross sales price
- Lender origination fees, points, underwriting fees
- Appraisal, credit report, flood certification fees
- Prepaid interest (closing date through month-end)
- Initial escrow deposit (tax + insurance reserves)
- Hazard insurance premium (first year if lender-required)
- Owner's title insurance premium (if buyer-paid)
- Lender's title insurance premium
- Title endorsements
- Recording fees (deed + mortgage/deed of trust)
- Transfer taxes allocated to buyer
- HOA transfer fees, working capital contributions
- Prorated items owed to seller (prepaid taxes, etc.)

**Credits to Buyer**
- Earnest money deposit(s)
- New loan proceeds
- Seller concessions / closing cost credits
- Assumed loans or seller financing
- Prorated items in buyer's favor

**→ Net: Cash Required from Buyer** (or Cash Due to Buyer if negative)

## Proration Calculations

| Charge Type | Method |
|---|---|
| Annual charges (property taxes, HOA annual dues) | 365-day year; seller responsible through day before disbursement |
| Monthly charges (HOA monthly dues) | Actual days in month |
| Existing mortgage interest | Per diem rate from payoff statement |
| Prepaid lender interest | Daily rate × days from closing through month-end |

**Tax proration note**: Confirm whether jurisdiction collects in arrears (seller credits buyer for elapsed year) or in advance (buyer credits seller for prepaid period). If current year bills are not yet issued, use prior year as estimate and mark `[ESTIMATE — SUBJECT TO FINAL TAX BILL]`.

---

## Balance Verification Checklist

- [ ] Seller credits − Seller debits = Cash to Seller
- [ ] Buyer debits − Buyer credits = Cash from Buyer
- [ ] Loan proceeds + Cash from Buyer = Sales price + all buyer-side charges
- [ ] All payoff figures match current payoff statements to the penny
- [ ] All prorations use confirmed disbursement date (not estimated)
- [ ] All lender fees match Closing Disclosure line-for-line (if applicable)
- [ ] All title charges match title company invoice
- [ ] Commission amounts match executed commission instructions
- [ ] Earnest money appears as buyer credit only (not duplicated as seller debit)

## Guidelines

- Mark unconfirmed figures `[ESTIMATE — CONFIRM BEFORE CLOSING]`
- All amounts to two decimal places; no rounding mid-calculation
- Allocate transfer taxes per contract; default to jurisdictional custom if silent
- Recording fee allocation (deed vs. mortgage) varies by state — follow contract, then local custom
- If simultaneous issue title rates apply (owner's + lender's policies), confirm premium split with title company before using standard schedule rates
- The ALTA Settlement Statement does not replace the CFPB Closing Disclosure on residential transactions with consumer mortgage financing — both may be required
- Payoff statements typically expire in 30 days; do not finalize until current payoff is confirmed
