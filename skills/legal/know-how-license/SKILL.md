# Know-How License Agreement

Drafts a commercially balanced agreement governing the transfer and licensing of confidential technical knowledge, processes, and trade secrets — information deriving value from secrecy rather than patent disclosure.

## Prerequisites

1. **Transaction documents** — term sheets, LOIs, prior agreements, correspondence with negotiated deal terms
2. **Party information** — full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions of formation, principal places of business
3. **Technical description** — functional description of the know-how sufficient to define scope without disclosing secrets
4. **Commercial terms** — exclusivity, territory, field of use, financial structure, milestones
5. **Regulatory context** — export control applicability, industry-specific regulations (FDA, environmental, etc.)

## Document Review

Before drafting, extract from uploaded documents:

| Extract | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Party identities | Legal names, entity types, jurisdictions |
| Know-how description | Technical scope, functional categories — preserve negotiated language |
| Financial terms | Upfront fees, milestones, royalty rates, minimums |
| Exclusivity & territory | Exclusive/sole/non-exclusive, geographic scope, field of use |
| Special provisions | Regulatory requirements, cross-border issues, university-to-industry terms |
| Existing relationships | Prior agreements to reference or supersede |

Adjust drafting approach based on context: university-to-industry, cross-border, regulated technology, startup vs. established entity dynamics.

## Output Structure

Draft the agreement with these sections in order:

### 1. Preamble & Recitals
- Full party identification with entity type, jurisdiction, principal place of business
- Recitals establishing: licensor ownership of proprietary know-how, trade secret status, licensee's desire for rights, mutual intent — avoid promotional language
- Reference background context (prior collaborations, strategic objectives)

### 2. Definitions

Define precisely — ambiguity destroys enforceability and trade secret protection:

| Term | Drafting Notes |
|------|---------------|
| Know-How / Licensed Technology | Specific enough to enforce, flexible enough for updates; use confidential technical schedule if needed |
| Licensed Territory | Geographic precision; subcategories if rights vary by region |
| Licensed Field / Field of Use | Applications, industries, product categories with clear boundaries |
| Confidential Information | Broad capture + standard exclusions (public domain, independent development, prior knowledge, third-party receipt) |
| Improvements / Derivative Know-How | Scope: modifications only vs. related developments |
| Net Sales | Royalty base inclusions/exclusions, bundled product valuation, affiliate transfer pricing |
| Affiliate | Related entities that may exercise rights |
| Milestone events | Objective criteria for triggering payments/obligations |

### 3. Grant of License

- [ ] Specify exclusive / sole / non-exclusive — define what each means in context
- [ ] Enumerate rights: use, manufacture, have-made, sell, distribute, create derivatives, sublicense
- [ ] Field-of-use restrictions with clear boundary definitions
- [ ] Territorial scope covering manufacturing, use, sale, import; address cross-border treatment
- [ ] Improvements: ownership, grant-back terms (exclusive/non-exclusive, royalty-bearing/free), joint improvements
- [ ] Licensor reservations: research rights, existing obligations, competing technology development
- [ ] Sublicensing: permitted scope, approval rights, required sublicense terms, termination treatment

### 4. Financial Terms

Structure based on deal type:

| Component | Provisions |
|-----------|-----------|
| Upfront fees | Amount, timing, conditions precedent, creditability against royalties |
| Milestones | Defined events, objective criteria, amounts, payment timing |
| Running royalties | % of Net Sales or per-unit; bundled product allocation; third-party license stacking discounts |
| Minimum annual royalties | Amount per contract year; consequence of shortfall (conversion, termination, or pay-up) |
| Payment mechanics | Currency, FX rates, frequency, method, late interest rate |
| Records & audit | 3-5 year retention; annual audit by independent CPA; cost-shifting if underpayment exceeds threshold % |

### 5. Confidentiality & Trade Secret Protection

Critical — failure here destroys trade secret status:

- [ ] Standard of care: at least same as own most sensitive information, no less than reasonable care
- [ ] Physical, technical, and administrative safeguards required
- [ ] Permitted disclosures: need-to-know basis only, written confidentiality agreements required from recipients
- [ ] Maintain written disclosure log; provide to licensor on request
- [ ] Compelled disclosure: prompt notice, cooperate on protective orders, minimum disclosure
- [ ] Survival: indefinite (until public domain through no breach)
- [ ] Return/destruction on termination with officer certification
- [ ] Address residual knowledge: either cessation + destruction, or continued use subject to ongoing confidentiality and royalties

### 6. Technology Transfer & Support

- Documentation deliverables: format, scope, timeline
- Training: sessions, duration, location, attendees, cost allocation
- Ongoing technical support: scope, response times, limitations
- Manufacturing transfer assistance, process validation, regulatory support
- Quality control: standards, sampling, reporting, facility inspections
- Updates/improvements: obligation to provide, included vs. additional fee

### 7. Diligence & Performance (Especially for Exclusive Licenses)

- Commercially reasonable efforts obligation
- Specific milestones with deadlines (development, regulatory, first commercial sale, sales targets)
- Consequences for failure: conversion to non-exclusive, termination, compulsory sublicensing
- Periodic progress reports: frequency, content, timing
- Consultation rights vs. licensee discretion

### 8. Representations & Warranties

**Licensor represents:**
- Authority to enter agreement and grant license
- Ownership/control of licensed know-how
- Trade secret status maintained
- No known third-party infringement (knowledge qualifier)
- Disclosure of known limitations/defects

**Licensee represents:**
- Authority to enter agreement
- Lawful use, regulatory compliance
- Technical and financial capability
- Export control and anti-corruption compliance

**Disclaimers (conspicuous text):**
- AS-IS for functionality, accuracy, completeness, fitness
- No warranty of non-infringement; licensee responsible for FTO analysis
- Disclaim implied warranties of merchantability and fitness for particular purpose

### 9. Indemnification

| Party | Covers |
|-------|--------|
| Licensee indemnifies | Use, modification, manufacture, sale of products; product liability; combination with other tech; failure to follow instructions |
| Licensor indemnifies | Third-party IP claims re: know-how as provided (often limited to rep/warranty breach) |

Procedures: prompt notice, indemnifier controls defense, indemnified party may participate at own cost, no settlement admitting liability without consent, cooperation obligation. Late notice relieves only to extent of material prejudice.

### 10. Limitation of Liability

- Mutual consequential damages waiver (conspicuous text)
- Aggregate liability cap (e.g., multiple of fees paid in preceding 12 months)
- **Carve-outs from cap/waiver:** confidentiality breach, unauthorized use beyond scope, indemnification obligations, willful misconduct, gross negligence, fraud

### 11. IP Ownership

- Licensor retains all rights in licensed know-how
- Licensee acquires only expressly granted license rights
- Improvement ownership: licensor-developed → licensor; licensee-developed → licensee (subject to grant-back); joint → per agreement
- Patent prosecution (if applicable): control, costs, consultation rights, geographic scope decisions
- Enforcement: primary enforcer, notice obligations, cost/recovery allocation, step-in rights

### 12. Term & Termination

| Trigger | Provisions |
|---------|-----------|
| Term | Perpetual, fixed, or until terminated; renewal mechanics |
| Convenience | Advance notice period (30-180 days); consider restricting for exclusive licenses |
| Material breach | Written notice specifying breach + cure period (30-60 days) |
| Incurable breach | Immediate termination: confidentiality breach, unauthorized use, non-payment of undisputed amounts |
| Insolvency | Automatic on bankruptcy, receivership, assignment for benefit of creditors |
| Diligence failure | Conversion to non-exclusive or termination |
| Change of control | Termination right, especially if acquirer is competitor |

### 13. Post-Termination

- [ ] Cessation of use (immediate or wind-down period with defined scope and continued royalties)
- [ ] Sublicense treatment: automatic termination, survival if compliant, or conversion to direct license
- [ ] Return/destruction of all materials with written certification
- [ ] Accrued payments immediately due; audit rights survive
- [ ] Survival clause: confidentiality, payment obligations, indemnification, liability limitations, dispute resolution, governing law

### 14. Regulatory & Export Control

- Compliance with EAR, ITAR, and equivalent foreign laws
- Licensee responsible for export licenses/approvals
- No transfer to prohibited destinations/entities/persons
- Industry-specific compliance (FDA, environmental, safety) — allocate responsibility for regulatory approvals

### 15. General Provisions

Include: entire agreement/integration, amendment (written only), assignment restrictions (consent required; affiliate/successor exceptions), notices (addresses, delivery methods, effectiveness), severability (modify-or-sever), waiver (written only, instance-specific), independent contractor disclaimer, counterparts/e-signatures (E-SIGN Act compliance), force majeure (excludes payment obligations; termination right if prolonged), further assurances.

### 16. Signature Blocks & Schedules

- Signature blocks with name, title, date; authority representation
- Prepare exhibits: confidential technical description, documentation list, payment schedule, milestone chart, quality specs, form sublicense/NDA

## Guidelines

- **Calibrate to deal size**: high-value/complex → exhaustive schedules and quality control; simple methodology transfer → streamlined
- **Protect trade secret status**: every confidentiality provision must satisfy applicable trade secret law requirements (DTSA, UTSA)
- **Bracket open terms**: use `[AMOUNT]`, `[PERIOD]`, `[JURISDICTION]` for terms requiring party input
- **Flag jurisdictional issues**: note where provisions need adjustment for non-US jurisdictions
- **No implied licenses**: expressly reserve all rights not granted
- **Conspicuous disclaimers**: use bold or caps for warranty disclaimers and liability limitations
- Export control provisions are mandatory for any technology that could be dual-use
- Address residual knowledge problem explicitly — this is the most litigated post-termination issue in know-how licenses
- If royalty stacking is possible, include anti-stacking or reduction provisions
- For exclusive licenses, always include diligence requirements with objective milestones
