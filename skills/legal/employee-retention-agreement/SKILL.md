# Employee Retention Agreement

Drafts a binding retention agreement balancing the employer's need for talent continuity with enforceable, fair terms for the key employee during a critical business transition.

## Prerequisites

1. **Employee data** — full legal name, title, department, hire date, base salary, bonus structure, equity holdings, reporting structure
2. **Employer data** — complete legal entity name, state of incorporation, principal place of business, relevant parent/subsidiary relationships
3. **Existing agreements** — current employment agreement, offer letter, equity plan documents, prior severance or retention arrangements, restrictive covenant agreements
4. **Transaction context** — if M&A-related: term sheet, board resolutions, authorized retention budget, anticipated closing date
5. **Jurisdiction** — employee's primary work state (drives restrictive covenant enforceability)
6. **Incentive parameters** — cash amount or formula, equity type/amount, vesting schedule, retention period start/end dates

## Output Structure

### 1. Recitals
- Articulate the specific business rationale (M&A integration, leadership transition, critical product cycle, competitive threat)
- Frame retention payment as **new consideration** beyond existing duties to address enforceability challenges
- Acknowledge employee's unique skills, institutional knowledge, and cost of replacement

### 2. Retention Incentive Structure

| Element | Drafting Requirements |
|---|---|
| **Cash (fixed)** | Gross dollar amount; withholding acknowledgment; installment dates if applicable |
| **Cash (formula)** | Base salary multiple or performance metric with calculation methodology |
| **Equity** | Reference governing plan (name + date); award type; grant terms; attach grant agreement as exhibit |
| **Hybrid** | Define each component separately; address interaction of vesting timelines |

**Tax/409A provisions:**
- Employer withholds federal, state/local income tax, FICA
- Employee bears ultimate tax liability
- Classify payment as either: (a) **short-term deferral** (paid within 2½ months of year-end in which vested), or (b) compliant **409A deferred compensation** with fixed schedule or permissible distribution event
- Define "separation from service" per 409A standard (reasonably anticipated reduction to <50% of prior 36-month average services)
- For **specified employees** (key executives of public companies): include 6-month payment delay for separation-from-service triggered payments

### 3. Retention Period

- Specify exact start date and end date (not ranges)
- Define "continuous employment": address FMLA leave, paid leave, STD — whether counted or tolled
- State whether employee must be employed **through** the final day or **on** a date certain
- If performance conditions beyond tenure apply, include: measurable objective criteria, evaluator, evaluation timing, documentation required, dispute resolution process

### 4. Termination Scenarios

| Scenario | Default Treatment |
|---|---|
| Voluntary resignation | Full forfeiture of unvested amounts; specify required notice period |
| Termination for Cause | Forfeiture of all unvested (and optionally vested-but-unpaid) amounts |
| Termination without Cause | Pro-rata or accelerated vesting (specify fraction or formula) |
| Constructive Discharge | Treat as termination without Cause; define triggering conditions (material duty reduction, >X% comp reduction, relocation >Y miles) |
| Death | Pay vested amounts to estate; consider accelerating unvested amounts |
| Disability | Reference company LTD plan definition or specify incapacity period; mirror death treatment |
| Change in Control | Specify **single-trigger** (transaction alone accelerates) vs. **double-trigger** (transaction + qualifying termination required) |

**Cause definition must include** (non-exhaustive): felony conviction, willful misconduct/gross negligence, material breach of fiduciary duties, unauthorized disclosure of confidential information, material policy violations.

### 5. Restrictive Covenants

| Covenant | Scope | Key Jurisdiction Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Confidentiality** | Trade secrets: indefinite; other confidential info: specified term | Uniform across all states |
| **Non-compete** | Geographic area + duration (typically 6–24 months by seniority) | CA, ND, OK: prohibited or severely restricted; FL, TX: enforceable if reasonable; include reformation/blue-pencil clause |
| **Non-solicitation (employees)** | 12–24 months post-termination | Broadly enforceable; tailor to seniority |
| **Non-solicitation (clients)** | Clients with whom employee had material contact or confidential info; 12–24 months | Tailor to actual client exposure |
| **Non-disparagement** | Mutual; carve out legally required truthful statements |  |

- Include **severability/reformation** clause authorizing courts to narrow overbroad covenants to maximum enforceable scope
- Note: some jurisdictions void rather than reform unreasonable covenants — flag for governing-state review

### 6. 280G/4999 Golden Parachute Analysis

If ERA payments combined with other change-in-control compensation may exceed **3× base amount** (triggering 280G):
- **Gross-up provision**: employer pays additional amounts to make employee whole for 20% excise tax — use for senior executives with strong leverage
- **Cutback provision**: payments reduced to 2.99× base amount to avoid excise tax entirely — typical default
- Specify who performs the 280G calculation (independent accountants), binding effect of calculation, and timing

### 7. Standard Provisions

- **Integration**: ERA supersedes prior retention arrangements; supplements (does not replace) employment agreement/offer letter except on direct conflict
- **Amendment**: written, signed by both parties
- **Assignment**: employee rights non-assignable; employer may assign to M&A successor who assumes all obligations
- **Notice**: certified mail, overnight courier, or email with confirmation; specify addresses and deemed-receipt timing
- **Dispute resolution**: specify litigation or arbitration (JAMS/AAA); carve out immediate injunctive relief for restrictive covenant breaches; address fee-shifting and cost allocation
- **Governing law**: employer's primary state or employee's work state; note mandatory employment law protections may override choice-of-law
- **Counterparts/e-signatures**: permitted; reference ESIGN Act compliance
- **Waiver**: non-cumulative; course-of-dealing waiver not implied

### 8. Representations & Acknowledgments

Employee represents:
- No conflicting prior employer agreements (non-compete, NDA, non-solicit)
- Voluntary execution; opportunity to consult counsel
- Awareness of restrictive covenant scope and post-employment implications
- Disclosure of any prior restrictive covenants to employer

### 9. Signature Block

- Employer: authorized officer (CEO, CFO, or CHRO) with actual authority per bylaws/board resolution; include title and date
- Employee: printed name, title, date
- List and attach all exhibits (equity grant agreement, vesting schedule, performance milestones)

## Guidelines

- **Verify authority**: confirm signing officer's actual authority before execution; unauthorized signatures may render agreement voidable
- **409A**: when in doubt, default to short-term deferral structure — it is the simplest safe harbor
- **Non-compete**: always confirm employee's primary work state before including; do not include CA non-competes without flagging unenforceability [VERIFY current state law for recent legislative changes]
- **280G**: flag for separate tax counsel review on any M&A-related ERA with senior executives
- **Drafting style**: use defined terms (capitalize on first definition), numbered sections, active voice; avoid "witnesseth," "party of the first part," and other archaisms
- **Consideration**: ensure retention payment is framed as new consideration for obligations beyond existing duties — critical for enforceability in states requiring independent consideration
