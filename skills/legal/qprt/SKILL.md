# Qualified Personal Residence Trust (QPRT) Agreement

Drafts an IRC §2702-compliant irrevocable QPRT that transfers a personal residence to remainder beneficiaries at a reduced gift tax value while the grantor retains rent-free occupancy for a specified term.

## Prerequisites

1. **Parties** — Full legal name and address of grantor, initial trustee, and all remainder beneficiaries (with DOB, relationship, SSN/TIN, and percentage share)
2. **Property** — Complete legal description from current deed (metes-and-bounds or lot/block), street address, tax parcel number, county, and state; confirmation of principal vs. qualified secondary residence (Treas. Reg. §25.2702-5(c)(2)(i))
3. **Valuation** — Qualified appraisal of FMV as of anticipated transfer date
4. **Planning parameters** — Grantor's age, health/longevity factors, desired retained term (years), and applicable IRC §7520 rate (transfer month or either of the two preceding months)
5. **Existing estate plan** — Prior gifts, lifetime exemption usage, will/revocable trust, GST exemption allocation history
6. **Governing law state** — Typically state of situs; note if grantor domicile differs

## Output Structure / Process

### Article I — Preamble & Definitions

- Identify grantor, trustee, execution date, and governing law state
- Declare trust irrevocable; recite intent to create a QPRT under Treas. Reg. §25.2702-5(c)
- Define: *Residence, Retained Interest Term, Term Expiration Date, Remainder Beneficiaries, §7520 Rate, Qualified Personal Residence*

### Article II — Trust Property & Permitted Holdings

| Item | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Exhibit A | Verbatim legal description from deed; all easements and appurtenances |
| Cash holdings | ≤ 6 months' operating expenses (taxes, insurance, maintenance, trustee fees) + improvement/replacement reserve (Treas. Reg. §25.2702-5(c)(5)(ii)) |
| Sale of residence | Proceeds must purchase replacement qualified residence within 2 years; excess → grantor distribution or GRAT conversion for remainder of term |
| Casualty/destruction | Insurance proceeds applied to repair/replacement; if not feasible → reversion to grantor or GRAT conversion |

### Article III — Retained Interest Term & Grantor's Rights

- State exact term (years), commencement date, and calculated Term Expiration Date
- Grantor's retained rights: exclusive rent-free occupancy, use of fixtures/furnishings, right to exclude others; right is personal and non-transferable
- Grantor responsible for ordinary maintenance; trustee oversees overall property condition
- Third-party rental permitted only if grantor continues to use property as personal residence for the greater of 14 days/year or 10% of rented days (Treas. Reg. §25.2702-5(c)(2)(i))
- **Estate tax disclosure:** If grantor dies before Term Expiration Date, full date-of-death FMV included in gross estate under IRC §2036(a); gift tax benefit eliminated

### Article IV — Post-Term Occupancy

- Upon term expiration, grantor has no occupancy right absent a bona fide arm's-length lease at fair market rent
- Rent must be paid in cash (check/ACH); document with formal written lease
- Failure to pay FMR → IRC §2036(a)(1) estate tax inclusion risk
- Optional trustee/beneficiary discretion to permit reduced-rent occupancy; document that such election causes estate tax inclusion

### Article V — Remainder Beneficiaries & Distribution

- List each beneficiary: name, address, DOB, relationship, SSN/TIN, percentage interest (must total 100%)
- Contingent remainder: deceased beneficiary's share → descendants per stirpes (or alternate mechanism)
- Election: outright distribution vs. continuing trust (continuing trust provisions: occupancy rights among multiple beneficiaries, rent allocation, buy-sell mechanism, expense allocation)
- Buy-sell / deadlock: trustee authority to order appraisal and compel sale if beneficiaries cannot agree within [X] days of term expiration

### Article VI — Trustee Powers, Duties & Limitations

**Authorized:**
- Maintain, repair, and improve residence; engage contractors
- Pay property taxes, assessments, insurance premiums timely
- Maintain property/casualty insurance (replacement-cost coverage) and liability insurance
- Hold cash within Treas. Reg. §25.2702-5(c)(5)(ii) limits

**Prohibited during Retained Interest Term:**
- Distribute principal or income to anyone other than grantor (except trust operating expenses)
- Sell or encumber residence without grantor's prior written consent; any sale must comply with replacement/GRAT conversion rules

**Fiduciary duties:** loyalty, impartiality between grantor and remaindermen, duty to inform; annual written accounting (receipts, disbursements, property value, activities)

### Article VII — Trustee Succession & Removal

- Name successor trustee(s) to assume upon grantor's death, resignation, or incapacity
- Incapacity defined as: certification by two licensed physicians or appointment of guardian/conservator
- Resignation: 60-day written notice; removal by [majority beneficiary vote / cause only] after term expiration
- Grantor's removal power during term: limited to replacement with independent trustee (avoid IRC §2036 inclusion)

### Article VIII — Tax Status & Reporting

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Income tax status | Grantor trust (IRC §§671–679) during retained term; all items on grantor's Form 1040 |
| Gift tax | Grantor files Form 709 for transfer year; taxable gift = FMV − actuarial value of retained term (§7520 rate + IRS actuarial tables, Pub. 1457) |
| GST exemption | If remaindermen include skip persons, consider GST exemption allocation on Form 709; allocated on full FMV, not discounted gift value |
| Death during term | Trustee cooperates with executor; full FMV at date of death included in gross estate (IRC §2036(a)); beneficiaries receive stepped-up basis |
| Survival of term | Beneficiaries receive carryover basis = grantor's adjusted basis + gift tax paid on appreciation (IRC §1015) |

### Article IX — Termination Events & Distribution

| Event | Result |
|---|---|
| Natural expiration (grantor survives) | Trustee executes deed transferring title; delivers final accounting, all records, and basis documentation |
| Grantor dies during term | Trustee notifies beneficiaries; property passes per trust terms; cooperates with estate administration; estate tax inclusion applies |
| Disqualifying event (non-use, improper sale) | Reversion to grantor OR conversion to GRAT (annuity per Treas. Reg. §25.2702-3); include conversion mechanics and annuity calculation |

Trustee duties continue until all property distributed, tax returns filed, and administration complete.

### Article X — Administrative & Miscellaneous

- **Governing law:** State of situs (or grantor domicile if more favorable); disputes in courts of designated jurisdiction
- **Severability:** Invalid provisions reformed to minimum extent; primary purpose of §2702 qualification controls interpretation
- **Amendment:** No amendments altering beneficial interests; trustee may make administrative amendments to maintain QPRT qualification or correct scrivener's errors
- **Notices:** Written; personal delivery, certified mail (RRR), or overnight courier; effective on receipt or 3 business days after mailing

### Article XI — Spendthrift & Creditor Protection

- No beneficiary may voluntarily or involuntarily transfer, assign, pledge, or encumber trust interest
- Creditors of beneficiaries cannot reach trust assets by attachment, garnishment, or execution
- Grantor's retained occupancy interest may be reachable by grantor's creditors in some jurisdictions during term; post-term, grantor has no trust interest
- Address exception creditors (child support, alimony, tax authorities) per applicable state law

### Article XII — Execution & Exhibits

**Signature blocks:**
- Grantor: acknowledges irrevocability and tax consequences
- Trustee (if not grantor): acceptance of fiduciary duties
- Two disinterested witnesses (non-beneficiaries) per applicable state law
- Notarial acknowledgment sufficient for deed recordation (include seal, commission expiration)

**Required exhibits:**
- **Exhibit A** — Verbatim legal description from deed
- **Exhibit B** — Remainder beneficiaries with complete identifying information and percentage interests

### Ancillary Documents & Post-Execution Checklist

- [ ] Deed conveying residence from grantor individually to grantor as trustee of QPRT (comply with local recording requirements; include transfer tax declaration/exemption)
- [ ] Form 709 filed by April 15 of following year (or with extension); attach trust agreement and appraisal
- [ ] Update property/casualty insurance to name trust as insured
- [ ] Notify property tax authority of ownership change (most jurisdictions: no reassessment for grantor trust transfers)
- [ ] Obtain new liability insurance policy in trust name
- [ ] Establish arm's-length lease and rental payment protocol if post-term occupancy anticipated
- [ ] Trustee begins annual accounting records from transfer date

## Guidelines

- **§7520 rate selection:** Use the transfer-month rate or either of the two preceding months — select whichever maximizes the retained interest value (higher rate = larger retained interest deduction = smaller taxable gift)
- **Term length:** Must not exceed grantor's actuarial life expectancy; shorter terms reduce mortality risk but also reduce gift tax savings
- **Residence type:** Only one principal residence and one secondary residence may be held in QPRTs simultaneously per Treas. Reg. §25.2702-5(c)(2)(i)
- **Grantor-trustee:** Permissible; use independent successor trustee to avoid §2036 issues on removal
- **Post-term rent:** Must be FMR, actually paid, and documented — failure is the most common QPRT compliance failure
- **GST planning:** Consult before allocating exemption; allocation is based on full FMV, not the discounted gift amount
- **Basis tracking:** Maintain grantor's adjusted basis records throughout term; critical for beneficiary income tax planning on future sale
- **[VERIFY]** Rev. Proc. 2003-42 safe harbor language requirements — confirm IRS has not issued superseding guidance
- **Jurisdiction caveat:** State-specific recording, transfer tax, and homestead exemption rules vary; confirm local requirements before executing deed
