# Answer to Harassment Complaint

Drafts a responsive Answer on behalf of a defendant employer in employment harassment litigation, preserving all available defenses and establishing the client's factual and legal positions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Complaint** — all numbered paragraphs, attached exhibits, and referenced documents
2. **Anti-harassment policies** — written policy, training records, complaint procedures, acknowledgment forms
3. **Investigation file** — any internal investigation triggered by plaintiff's complaint (or lack thereof)
4. **Personnel records** — plaintiff's employment dates, title, reporting chain, disciplinary history
5. **Alleged harasser's records** — supervisory authority, tangible action authority, employment status
6. **EEOC charge** — filing date, charge scope, right-to-sue letter date

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption
Mirror the complaint's caption exactly: court name, case number, party names, and title (`DEFENDANT'S ANSWER TO COMPLAINT`). Include jury demand if client elects; add local-rule designations (case track, line numbers) as required.

### 2. Paragraph-by-Paragraph Responses

For each numbered paragraph, use one of three responses:

| Response | When to Use |
|---|---|
| **Admit** | Indisputably true facts: corporate existence, employment dates, existence of policies |
| **Deny** | False allegations, legal conclusions ("severe," "pervasive," "unwelcome," "harassing") |
| **Lack sufficient knowledge** | Plaintiff's subjective experiences, statements outside defendant's presence — but use sparingly for facts defendant should know |

**Drafting rules:**
- Parse compound paragraphs — admit true portions, deny the rest specifically
- When complaint quotes or references a document, admit the document's existence; deny plaintiff's characterization
- Never blanket-deny entire paragraphs containing mixed assertions
- Keep responses consistent with documents to be produced in discovery

### 3. Affirmative Defenses

Assert each defense in a separately numbered paragraph. Waiver applies to defenses not pleaded.

| Defense | Key Elements |
|---|---|
| **Failure to state a claim** | Conduct not severe or pervasive enough to alter conditions of employment; isolated incidents, offhand comments, or petty slights insufficient |
| **Faragher-Ellerth** (supervisor, no tangible action) | (1) Employer exercised reasonable care: written policy, regular training, accessible complaint channels, prompt investigation; (2) Plaintiff unreasonably failed to use available remedial procedures |
| **Statute of limitations** | EEOC charge: 180 days (non-deferral state) or 300 days (deferral state) from each discrete act; suit: 90 days from right-to-sue letter; state claims: 1–3 years (jurisdiction-specific) [VERIFY state-specific period] |
| **Continuing violation** | Discrete acts outside limitations period not recoverable even under continuing-violation theory |
| **No protected characteristic nexus** | Plaintiff cannot establish conduct was because of sex, race, or other protected class |
| **Failure to exhaust** | Claims not included in EEOC charge scope are unexhausted |
| **No compensable damages** | Plaintiff suffered no cognizable harm, or harm caused by independent intervening factors |
| **Failure to mitigate** | Plaintiff failed to take reasonable steps to reduce damages |
| **After-acquired evidence** | Post-separation discovery of misconduct warranting discharge regardless of alleged harassment [VERIFY jurisdiction's application to harassment] |
| **Release/waiver** | Assert if any settlement agreement or release exists |
| **Statutory coverage** | Assert lack of jurisdiction if employer falls below Title VII/state-law employee threshold |

Include a reservation to assert additional defenses as discovery reveals new facts (receptiveness varies by jurisdiction).

### 4. Prayer for Relief

- Dismissal of the complaint with prejudice
- Judgment for defendant on all claims
- Costs of suit
- Attorney's fees (Title VII fee-shifting for prevailing defendant where frivolous [VERIFY standard]; contractual basis if applicable)
- Such other and further relief as the court deems just

### 5. Signature Block & Service

Include: attorney name, bar number, firm, address, phone, email, filing date. Attach **Certificate of Service** stating date and method of service on plaintiff's counsel.

## Guidelines

- **Supervisor vs. co-worker distinction**: Faragher-Ellerth is available only where alleged harasser is a supervisor who took no tangible employment action; co-worker harassment requires different negligence standard
- **Tangible employment action**: Discharge, demotion, or undesirable reassignment defeats Faragher-Ellerth — pivot to different defenses if present
- **No argument in pleading**: Strike editorial commentary; the Answer is not a brief
- **Internal consistency check**: Confirm all paragraph responses align with asserted affirmative defenses before filing
- **Local rules**: Verify page limits, font, spacing, margin, and line-numbering requirements for the specific court
- **Amendment**: Note any facts learned post-filing that may require an amended answer; check local deadline for amendment as of right
