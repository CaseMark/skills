# Demand Package Generation

Compiles evidence, calculates damages, and generates settlement demand packages.

## Prerequisites

1. **Medical summary** — completed medical record chronology (see medical-record-summarization)
2. **Billing records** — all medical bills, both paid and outstanding
3. **Lost wages documentation** — employment records, tax returns, employer statements
4. **Liability evidence** — police reports, photos, witness statements, expert reports

## Workflow

### 1. Calculate Economic Damages

| Category | Sources |
|---|---|
| Past medical expenses | Bills, EOBs, lien amounts |
| Future medical expenses | Treating physician projections, life care plans |
| Lost wages (past) | Pay stubs, employer letter, tax returns |
| Lost earning capacity | Vocational expert opinion, career trajectory |
| Property damage | Repair estimates, diminished value |

### 2. Assess Non-Economic Damages

- Pain and suffering narrative based on medical records and client testimony
- Loss of enjoyment of life — specific activities impacted
- Emotional distress — documented treatment (therapy, medication)
- Compare to jury verdicts in similar cases in the jurisdiction

### 3. Assemble Package

Structure:
1. Demand letter with liability analysis and damages summary
2. Medical chronology and provider summary
3. Itemized damages calculation
4. Supporting exhibits (bills, records, photos, reports)
5. Specific demand amount with response deadline

### 4. Quality Check

- Verify all bills are accounted for and totals match
- Confirm liability facts are supported by evidence in the package
- Ensure demand amount is defensible based on comparable verdicts
- Store complete package in vault

## Pitfalls

- **Don't inflate damages** — adjusters spot padding instantly; it hurts credibility
- **Include all medical bills** — missing a $200 bill looks sloppy and invites scrutiny
- **Know your jurisdiction** — damage caps, comparative fault rules, and collateral source rules vary
- **Set realistic deadlines** — too short looks aggressive; too long loses urgency
