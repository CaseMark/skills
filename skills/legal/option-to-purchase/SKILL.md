# Option to Purchase Real Estate

Drafts a unilateral option contract granting the Optionee an exclusive right to purchase real property under predetermined terms within a defined period.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party information** — full legal names, entity types, addresses, organizational jurisdiction, authority/capacity documentation (POA, corporate resolution)
2. **Property documentation** — current deed with legal description, survey, known encumbrances/easements/CCRs, title commitment or preliminary report
3. **Deal terms** — purchase price (fixed or formula), option consideration amount, option period, credit-toward-purchase treatment, financing structure
4. **Governing jurisdiction** — state where property is located (controls recording requirements, mandatory disclosures, statutory language)

## Output Structure

### Section 1: Parties

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Optionor | Full legal name (as on deed), entity type, address, state of organization |
| Optionee | Full legal name, entity type, address, organizational jurisdiction |
| Representatives | If acting via POA or agent, state authority and reference instrument |
| Assignability | State whether option is assignable or personal to named Optionee |
| Capacity reps | Include representations of authority, especially for entity parties |

### Section 2: Property Description & Title

- Full legal description (lot, block, subdivision, county, state) + street address
- Schedule of known encumbrances, easements, CCRs, existing mortgages/liens, HOA obligations
- Title standard: marketable and insurable, free of encumbrances except those Optionee accepts
- Title commitment delivery obligation and cost allocation during option period
- Included fixtures/personal property/appurtenances; excluded items Optionor will remove

### Section 3: Option Period

| Provision | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Commencement | Specific date |
| Expiration | Specific date and time (e.g., 5:00 PM local time) with time zone |
| Time of the essence | State whether global or provision-specific |
| Extensions | Conditions, additional consideration, automatic extension triggers (force majeure, title defects) |
| Maintenance payments | If lengthy period, specify periodic payments to keep option alive and consequences of non-payment |

### Section 4: Option Consideration

- **Amount**: State dollar figure
- **Refundability**: Designate non-refundable except for Optionor breach or title failure
- **Payment timing**: Lump sum at execution or installment schedule
- **Credit**: Whether applied toward purchase price upon exercise or purely cost of the option right
- **Receipt acknowledgment**: Include in document for evidentiary completeness

### Section 5: Purchase Price & Payment

- Fixed price or formula/mechanism (e.g., FMV by defined appraisal methodology)
- Payment structure:

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Earnest money | Amount, timeline post-exercise, form (wire/certified funds) |
| Option consideration credit | Yes/no, amount |
| Purchase price allocation | Real property vs. personal property (tax purposes) |
| Seller financing (if any) | Rate, amortization, balloon, security instruments |

- **Contingencies** — for each, specify deadline and consequence of non-satisfaction:
  - Financing (with specific loan terms)
  - Inspection (timeframe for inspection and objection)
  - Appraisal
  - Environmental assessment

### Section 6: Exercise Mechanics

- **Notice method**: Personal delivery, certified mail (RRCR), overnight courier, email — or combination
- **Delivery address(es)** and authorized recipient
- **Effective date**: Upon sending vs. upon receipt (critical near expiration)
- **Accompanying deposit**: Whether earnest money must accompany exercise notice
- **Partial exercise**: Permitted or not (multi-parcel options)
- **Failure to exercise**: Automatic termination of all option rights

**Attach as Exhibit**: Form of Exercise Notice template.

### Section 7: Closing Procedure

| Item | Specification |
|------|---------------|
| Closing deadline | 30–90 days post-exercise; extension terms |
| Location/method | In-person or escrow; mutually agreed venue |
| Optionor deliverables | Warranty deed, bill of sale, title affidavit, FIRPTA cert, lead-paint disclosure, estoppel certs (if leased) |
| Optionee deliverables | Certified funds, loan documents, assumption agreements |
| Prorations | Property taxes, HOA fees, utilities, rents — as of closing date |
| Cost allocation | Title insurance, recording fees, transfer taxes, survey, attorney fees |
| Walk-through | Immediately pre-closing; procedure if material condition change |

### Section 8: Optionor Covenants During Option Period

- [ ] Maintain property in current condition
- [ ] Maintain insurance coverage
- [ ] Pay taxes and assessments when due
- [ ] No additional encumbrances without Optionee consent
- [ ] No leases beyond specified terms without Optionee consent
- Remedies for covenant violation: termination with return of consideration, or injunctive relief

### Section 9: Default & Remedies

**Optionor breach (refuses to convey after valid exercise):**
- Specific performance
- Monetary damages (including return of option consideration + consequential damages)
- State whether Optionee must elect or may pursue concurrently

**Optionee breach (exercises but fails to close):**
- Retention of option consideration + earnest money as liquidated damages
- Right to pursue actual damages if exceeding liquidated amounts
- Specific performance if breach is remediable
- Termination + freedom to sell to third parties

**Non-exercise expiration:** Automatic termination; Optionor free to sell/encumber without restriction.

### Section 10: General Provisions

- Governing law: state where property is located
- Integration clause; amendments require written mutual consent
- Dispute resolution: specify litigation/mediation/arbitration; venue and jurisdiction
- Prevailing party attorney fees (if permitted under applicable state law)
- Severability, waiver, binding on heirs/successors/permitted assigns
- Counterpart execution
- General notice provisions (beyond exercise notice)

### Signature & Recording

- Signature blocks with date lines, printed names, titles for entity reps
- Notary acknowledgment blocks if required or advisable under state law
- If recording in land records: comply with local formatting, font size, and statutory language requirements

## Guidelines

- Use consistent defined terms throughout (e.g., "Option Period" — never "Exercise Period" as synonym)
- Define all capitalized terms on first use or in a definitions section
- Plain English; avoid archaic legalese while maintaining legal precision
- Number all sections/subsections for cross-reference accuracy
- Verify internal consistency of dates, dollar amounts, and party names
- Confirm compliance with state-specific mandatory disclosures and recording requirements
- Attach exhibits: legal description, survey, form of exercise notice, form of deed
- Mark any jurisdiction-specific citation with `[VERIFY]` if uncertain of current statutory text
