# Appellee's Response Brief

Drafts an appellate response brief defending the trial court's judgment and arguing for affirmance.

## Prerequisites

1. **Appellant's opening brief** — all issues raised, authorities cited, standards of review claimed
2. **Trial court's judgment/order** — written decision, oral ruling transcripts, findings of fact and conclusions of law
3. **Appellate record** — trial transcripts, exhibits, pleadings, motions, docket entries
4. **Notice of appeal** — filing date, docket numbers (appellate and trial court)
5. **Applicable court rules** — formatting, word/page limits, citation format, filing deadlines, service requirements
6. **Governing contract(s) or relevant documents** — if applicable to underlying claims

## Output Structure

### Cover Page

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Court name | Full official name of appellate court |
| Case caption | All parties with appellate designations |
| Docket numbers | Both appellate and trial court |
| Document title | "Brief of Appellee" or jurisdiction-specific variant |
| Counsel info | Name, bar number, firm, address, phone, email |
| Additional | Word count certification, oral argument notation per local rules |

### Required Sections (in order)

1. **Table of Contents** — Argumentative headings with page numbers; generate after substantive sections are complete
2. **Table of Authorities** — Categorized (cases, statutes, constitutional provisions, rules, secondary sources); alphabetized within categories; Bluebook format unless local rules specify otherwise; include all page references
3. **Jurisdictional Statement** — Cite specific statutory/constitutional basis for appellate jurisdiction; state judgment date, NOA filing date, confirm timeliness; address finality if at issue
4. **Statement of the Case**
   - Procedural history: nature of action, claims/defenses, key rulings, trial court's reasoning
   - Statement of facts: record-supported, every assertion cited; correct/supplement appellant's omissions or mischaracterizations
5. **Summary of Argument** — 1–3 pages; substantive preview of each responsive argument; emphasize standard of review, deference owed, and absence of reversible error
6. **Argument** — See detailed framework below
7. **Conclusion** — Single paragraph; state precise relief requested (typically: "affirm the judgment")
8. **Certificate of Compliance** — Word/page count, typeface, method of calculation
9. **Certificate of Service** — Method, date, parties served

### Argument Framework

For **each issue** raised by appellant, follow this structure:

```
## [Argumentative Point Heading as Complete Sentence]

### Standard of Review
- State applicable standard (de novo / abuse of discretion / substantial evidence / clearly erroneous)
- Cite jurisdiction-specific authority articulating the standard
- Explain burden on appellant and deference owed to trial court

### Legal Framework
- Controlling precedent, statutes, constitutional provisions
- Build from general principles → specific applications

### Application to Record
- Connect law to specific record evidence (cite transcript pages, exhibit numbers)
- Highlight facts appellant omitted or minimized
- Show trial court's findings are supported by substantial evidence

### Rebuttal of Appellant's Arguments
- Address each sub-argument directly
- Distinguish appellant's cited authorities on material facts
- Identify: mischaracterizations of record, misapplications of law, logical gaps,
  failure to address controlling authority, failure to show prejudice/harm

### Policy Considerations (if applicable)
- Finality, predictability, proper allocation of trial/appellate authority
- Consequences of adopting appellant's proposed rule
```

**Point heading examples:**
- GOOD: "The Trial Court Properly Granted Summary Judgment Because Appellant Failed to Produce Evidence of Any Genuine Dispute of Material Fact"
- BAD: "Summary Judgment"

### Standard of Review Quick Reference

| Standard | Deference Level | Appellant's Burden | Key Language |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Abuse of discretion | High | Show decision was arbitrary, capricious, or manifestly unreasonable | "Range of reasonable choices" |
| Clearly erroneous | High | Show definite and firm conviction of mistake | "Not clearly erroneous if supported by any evidence" |
| Substantial evidence | High | Show no reasonable mind could accept evidence as adequate | "More than a scintilla" |
| De novo | None | Show legal error | Still argue trial court's reasoning was sound |

## Guidelines

- **Record fidelity is paramount** — every factual assertion must have a specific record citation (page, line number); never mischaracterize, omit material facts, or take quotes out of context
- **Mirror appellant's issue order** unless reordering is strategically superior (e.g., threshold/dispositive issues first, strongest arguments first)
- **Engage directly** with appellant's arguments — never ignore them; silence may be treated as concession
- **Distinguish unfavorable authority** on material factual differences; show cases are not controlling, have been limited/superseded, or actually support affirmance when read fully
- **Verify all cited authorities** remain good law; check for negative treatment, superseding legislation
- **Correct appellant's standard of review** if they claim de novo where deferential review applies — this is often outcome-determinative
- **Preserve alternative grounds** for affirmance supported by the record, even if not relied on by the trial court
- **Harmless error** — when appropriate, argue that even if error occurred, it was harmless and did not affect the outcome
- **Tone** — professional, confident, respectful; avoid ad hominem, hyperbole, or inflammatory language; let the record and law speak
- **Formatting** — comply strictly with all local rules on margins, typeface (typically 14pt proportional or 12pt monospaced), line spacing, word/page limits
- **Mark any uncertain citation with [VERIFY]**
