# OCI Mitigation Plan

Drafts a comprehensive FAR 9.5-compliant Organizational Conflict of Interest Mitigation Plan for federal government contractors.

## Prerequisites

1. **Contractor identity** — full legal name, corporate structure, parent/subsidiary relationships, UEI, CAGE code
2. **Procurement details** — solicitation/contract number, agency, scope of work, contract type, performance period
3. **Prior government work** — relevant contracts, advisory roles, information access that may create conflicts
4. **Correspondence** — any prior OCI-related communications with the contracting officer
5. **Organizational relationships** — teaming agreements, subcontractors, equity interests, consulting arrangements

## Information Extraction

Before drafting, extract from uploaded documents:

| Data Point | Source |
|---|---|
| Corporate structure & affiliates | Organizational docs, SAM registration |
| Solicitation scope & evaluation criteria | RFP/RFQ, SOW, PWS |
| Prior contracts creating potential conflicts | Award documents, task orders, correspondence |
| Non-public information accessed | Prior performance records, internal assessments |
| Personnel with dual exposure | Org charts, staffing plans |
| Prior OCI communications | CO letters, proposal representations |

## Output Structure

### Section 1: Executive Summary

- Contractor identification (name, UEI, CAGE, corporate family)
- Procurement identification (solicitation #, agency, scope)
- Legal basis for submission (CO request / proposal requirement / voluntary disclosure)
- Reference prior OCI correspondence with dates
- Summary table of identified conflicts and proposed mitigations

### Section 2: Conflict Identification & Materiality

Analyze each FAR 9.5 conflict category:

| Category | FAR Basis | Key Questions |
|---|---|---|
| **Unequal Access to Information** | FAR 9.505-4 | What non-public info was obtained? Through which contracts? Is it still competitively useful? |
| **Impaired Objectivity** | FAR 9.505-3 | Does contractor advise on matters where it has financial interest in outcomes? Any equity/teaming with benefiting entities? |
| **Biased Ground Rules** | FAR 9.505-1, 9.505-2 | Did contractor draft specs, SOW, or evaluation criteria for this procurement? Could prior work shape requirements to favor contractor? |

For each identified conflict, document:
1. Factual narrative with specific contracts, dates, personnel
2. Government interests at risk
3. Competitive implications
4. Materiality rating (substantial vs. theoretical)
5. Whether appearance of impropriety alone warrants mitigation

### Section 3: Mitigation Strategies

Tailor measures to each conflict. Use the following toolkit:

**Organizational Firewalls / Information Barriers:**
- Physical separation (dedicated facilities, restricted areas)
- Electronic controls (separate networks, access-controlled systems, restricted distribution lists)
- Administrative controls (separate reporting chains, NDAs with enforcement provisions)
- Personnel assignment criteria and firewall boundary definitions
- Breach detection and response protocols

**Personnel Recusal / Disqualification:**
- Objective criteria triggering recusal (prior access, relationships, financial interests)
- Documentation and supervisory approval process
- Backfill procedures to maintain performance quality
- Communication of restrictions to affected individuals

**Disclosure & Transparency Commitments:**
- Categories of information subject to ongoing disclosure
- Notification timeframes after triggering events
- Regular reporting cadence (even absent triggers)
- Government recipients and format requirements

**Business Activity Restrictions** (if firewalls insufficient):
- Non-competition commitments for follow-on procurements
- Information use restrictions
- Divestiture or relationship termination commitments
- Scope reductions eliminating problematic activities

### Section 4: Implementation & Compliance

**Governance:**
- Designate OCI Compliance Officer by title, reporting relationship, authority, and independence from program management
- Direct access to executive leadership; authority to halt activities

**Training Program:**

| Audience | Content | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Contract personnel | FAR OCI principles, specific restrictions, firewall rules | Initial + annual refresher |
| Supervisors/managers | Monitoring obligations, breach escalation | Initial + annual |
| Business development | Restrictions on information use, competition limits | Initial + as needed |
| New hires to affected programs | Full onboarding OCI training | At assignment |

- Signed acknowledgments and comprehension assessments required
- Targeted training when plan updates or new conflicts emerge

**Monitoring & Audit:**
- Active verification (not just self-reporting): access logs, recusal records, firewall integrity checks
- Compliance review frequency (monthly/quarterly/event-triggered)
- Documentation: training records, disclosure logs, investigation reports
- Audit methodology: internal audit, external consultants, or Compliance Officer review

**Periodic Certifications to CO:**
- Defined intervals (quarterly recommended)
- Affirm plan effectiveness, disclose violations/remediations, flag changed circumstances
- Senior official certification with due diligence process

**Change Management:**
- Define "material change" triggers (reorgs, personnel moves, new business relationships, scope changes)
- Prompt notification to CO within specified timeframe
- Written assessment of OCI implications before implementing modifications

**Corrective Action:**
- Immediate containment → investigation → root cause analysis → discipline → systemic fix
- Full disclosure to CO with remediation plan

### Section 5: Binding Certification

Draft certification by authorized corporate officer including:

```
CERTIFICATION

I, [Name], [Title], of [Contractor Legal Name], hereby certify that:

1. The foregoing OCI analysis is complete, thorough, and accurate to the
   best of my knowledge after reasonable inquiry;
2. All material facts bearing on potential conflicts have been disclosed
   without omission or misrepresentation;
3. [Contractor] commits to implementing the mitigation measures exactly
   as described herein;
4. This plan, upon government acceptance, constitutes a binding
   contractual obligation;
5. Non-compliance may result in: termination for default, payment
   withholding, suspension/debarment, referral for investigation,
   and all other available remedies.

_____________________________
Name:
Title:
Organization:
Date:
```

- Certifying official must have actual binding authority (corporate officer, division president, or equivalent)
- If contractor is a subsidiary, assess whether parent company ratification is appropriate

## Guidelines

- **Regulatory foundation**: FAR 9.504 (CO responsibilities), FAR 9.505 (representations), FAR 9.505-1 through 9.505-4 (specific conflict types), agency-specific supplements (DFARS, NFS, etc.)
- **No boilerplate**: Every mitigation measure must be specifically tailored to the identified conflict — generic firewalls without factual grounding will be rejected
- **Appearance matters**: Mitigate conflicts that create appearance of impropriety even if actual harm is unlikely
- **Temporal scope**: Address both performance-period and post-performance residual conflicts
- **Tone**: Professional, candid, appropriately formal — avoid defensive posturing
- **Audience**: Contracting officers, agency counsel, IG offices, potentially GAO
- **Dual purpose**: Document must satisfy legal/regulatory requirements AND serve as operational implementation guide
- **Agency-specific rules**: Flag when DoD (DFARS), NASA (NFS), or other agency supplements impose additional OCI requirements [VERIFY specific supplement citations against current regulations]
