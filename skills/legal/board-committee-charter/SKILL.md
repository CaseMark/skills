# Board Committee Charter — Audit & Compensation

Drafts a comprehensive board committee charter establishing Audit and Compensation Committees tailored to the company's regulatory posture, exchange listing, and governance needs.

## Prerequisites

1. **Company profile** — public/private status, stock exchange (NYSE/NASDAQ/other), state of incorporation, industry sector
2. **Existing governance documents** — articles of incorporation, bylaws, existing charters, board resolutions, code of ethics
3. **Organizational context** — ownership structure, capitalization, international operations, pending transactions (IPO, M&A)
4. **Special circumstances** — recent restatements, internal control deficiencies, regulatory investigations, compensation controversies

## Output Structure

Draft a single charter document with the following sections:

### I. Preamble & Authority

- Statement of board adoption pursuant to bylaws and applicable law
- Relationship to board: committees exercise delegated authority; ultimate fiduciary duty remains with full board

### II. Purpose Statements

| Committee | Core Purpose |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Audit** | Independent oversight of financial integrity, internal controls, audit functions, compliance systems, and financial/operational risk |
| **Compensation** | Alignment of executive compensation with shareholder interests, strategic objectives, talent retention, and appropriate risk balance |

### III. Composition & Qualifications

**Audit Committee:**

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Minimum size | 3 members (4-5 for complex organizations) |
| Independence | All members independent under exchange listing standards + SEC Rule 10A-3 |
| Financial expert | At least 1 member qualifying under SEC definition (GAAP understanding, audit experience, internal controls knowledge) |
| Prohibited relationships | No compensatory fees beyond director pay; no affiliate relationships; cooling-off periods apply |

**Compensation Committee:**

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Independence | All members independent under listing rules |
| SEC Rule 16b-3 | All members qualify as "non-employee directors" |
| IRC §162(m) | "Outside director" status if preserving deductibility |
| Preferred experience | Executive compensation, human capital management, senior leadership |

**Appointment procedures:**
- Nominated by Nominating/Governance Committee (or full board) annually
- Define term lengths (indefinite with annual reappointment vs. fixed/staggered)
- Chair designation with enhanced responsibilities: agenda-setting, board liaison, meeting leadership

### IV. Authority & Resources

**Audit Committee powers:**
- [ ] Appoint, compensate, retain, oversee, and terminate independent auditor (auditor reports to committee, not management)
- [ ] Pre-approve all audit and permitted non-audit services (may delegate to chair below threshold, subject to ratification)
- [ ] Retain independent counsel, accountants, forensic investigators without board approval; company funds as committee determines
- [ ] Unrestricted access to personnel, facilities, books, records, and information systems
- [ ] Authority to conduct investigations and compel management cooperation

**Compensation Committee powers:**
- [ ] Set CEO performance goals, evaluate performance, determine CEO compensation
- [ ] Approve compensation for all executive officers (may delegate sub-CEO to CEO with committee ratification)
- [ ] Approve employment agreements, severance, change-in-control provisions
- [ ] Retain and oversee compensation consultants with sole selection/termination authority
- [ ] Assess consultant independence per listing standard factors (other services, fee %, conflicts, relationships, stock ownership)
- [ ] Administer equity incentive plans, approve grants, interpret plan provisions

### V. Meeting Procedures

| Element | Audit Committee | Compensation Committee |
|---------|----------------|----------------------|
| Minimum frequency | 4x/year (aligned with quarterly earnings) | 2x/year |
| Additional meetings | Audit planning, year-end, internal control assessments | Equity grants, employment agreements, annual review |
| Quorum | Majority of members | Majority of members |
| Action | Majority vote at quorum, or unanimous written consent | Same |
| Minutes | Detailed: attendees, matters, deliberations, decisions, basis for judgments | Same |

**Executive session requirements:**

- Audit Committee: regular sessions excluding management; separate private sessions with independent auditor, internal auditor, and CFO at least annually
- Compensation Committee: no officers present during deliberations/votes on their own compensation; annual session with independent consultant without management
- Management (CEO, CFO, GC) may attend by invitation but not during votes on matters where personally interested

### VI. Audit Committee Responsibilities

**Financial reporting oversight:**
- Review annual audited and quarterly financial statements with management and auditor before public release (including MD&A)
- Evaluate: accounting principles applied, significant judgments/estimates, new pronouncements, alternative treatments, disclosure completeness
- Review auditor communications per auditing standards (critical policies, alternative treatments, quality of principles)
- Review management representation letters

**Independent auditor oversight:**
- Annual evaluation of qualifications, performance, and independence
- Obtain auditor report on: internal quality controls, quality review issues, governmental investigations, all company relationships affecting independence
- Evaluate lead audit partner; consider firm rotation

**Internal audit oversight:**
- Approve internal audit charter, annual plan, budget, staffing
- Approve appointment/replacement of internal audit head
- Review reports, management responses, corrective action status
- Assess effectiveness, independence, and organizational stature

**Compliance & risk:**
- Oversee legal/regulatory compliance program effectiveness
- Establish whistleblower procedures (confidential/anonymous) per SOX §806
- Review and approve/ratify related party transactions per applicable rules
- Oversee enterprise risk management for financial risk exposures

### VII. Compensation Committee Responsibilities

**Compensation philosophy & strategy:**
- Articulate and annually review: program objectives, element weighting, fixed vs. variable balance, time horizons, equity vs. cash mix, pay-performance relationship

**CEO compensation:**
- Set annual goals in consultation with board → evaluate performance → determine compensation in executive session without CEO → report to full board

**Executive officer compensation:**
- Review total compensation competitiveness, performance metrics appropriateness, short/long-term incentive balance
- Approve all material changes: base salary, bonus targets/payouts, LTI awards, special arrangements
- Approve employment agreements, severance, CIC provisions; assess cost under all termination scenarios
- Ensure compliance with IRC §409A; include appropriate restrictive covenants (non-compete, non-solicit, non-disparagement)

**Equity compensation:**
- Oversee plan design; recommend plans for shareholder approval
- Approve individual grants; establish grant timing policies to prevent opportunistic timing around MNPI
- Monitor burn rate, overhang, and dilution levels

**Risk assessment:**
- Annual assessment of whether compensation policies create material adverse risk
- Identify risk-encouraging features: uncapped payouts, steep cliffs, highly leveraged metrics, short periods
- Ensure mitigants: balanced scorecards, multiple metrics, caps, clawbacks, stock ownership requirements, holding periods

**Succession planning:**
- Oversee management development and succession for CEO and key executives
- Review plans annually; assess readiness of potential successors
- Coordinate with full board on CEO succession (emergency and planned)

**Proxy disclosure (public companies):**
- Review and approve CD&A for proxy statement
- Review compensation tables and narrative disclosure
- Prepare Compensation Committee Report

### VIII. Reporting to the Board

Each committee reports at the next board meeting following each committee meeting. Chairs highlight significant issues, concerns, and items requiring board action.

**Escalation triggers (immediate board notification):**
- Audit: material weaknesses, potential management fraud, significant regulatory violations, whistleblower matters
- Compensation: arrangements creating significant financial obligations, conflicts of interest, reputational risks

### IX. Self-Assessment & Charter Review

- Annual performance self-evaluation (confidential questionnaires, activity-vs-charter gap analysis, peer benchmarking)
- Annual charter review for legal/regulatory currency
- Report evaluation results and improvement recommendations to full board
- Ongoing director education in committee domain areas

## Guidelines

- **Public vs. private**: For private companies, SOX and exchange listing requirements do not apply directly but serve as best-practice benchmarks. Scale provisions accordingly.
- **Regulatory citations to verify**: SOX §§201, 206, 301, 806; SEC Rule 10A-3; SEC Rule 16b-3; IRC §162(m); IRC §409A [VERIFY current applicability of §162(m) post-TCJA]
- **Exchange-specific tailoring**: NYSE and NASDAQ independence definitions differ — confirm which standard applies and use the more stringent if dual-listed
- **Industry overlays**: Financial institutions (banking regs, Fed guidance, FDIC), healthcare (FDA, fraud/abuse, HIPAA), energy (environmental, safety, reserves) — expand committee responsibilities as needed
- **State law alignment**: Confirm committee authority provisions comply with state of incorporation (DGCL §141(c) for Delaware) regarding permissible delegation and reserved board powers
- **Drafting tone**: Clear, precise legal language accessible to non-specialist directors; active voice; defined terms used consistently; include definitions section if needed
- **Document elements**: Title, adoption date, board approval reference; table of contents for longer charters; professional formatting with numbered sections
- **Cross-reference check**: Verify alignment with bylaws, articles, other governance policies before finalizing
