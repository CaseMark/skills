## State-analog reminders

The federal statutes above set the floor. State analogs frequently exceed federal minimums:

- **CWA**: state-issued NPDES (most states are authorized); state water-quality standards typically more stringent on wetlands and listed waters.
- **RCRA**: most states are authorized to administer; California's HSC regime is broader.
- **CERCLA**: state Superfund / VCP programs (CT, NJ, MA, MI, NY, others).
- **TSCA**: state chemical-restriction programs (CA Prop 65, Maine LD 1503 PFAS, Washington Safer Products).
- **CAA**: state SIPs; California's CARB authority preserved.

## Civil penalty pointer

EPA adjusts civil penalty maximums annually under the Federal Civil Penalties Inflation Adjustment Act of 1990 (28 U.S.C. § 2461 note), as amended by the 2015 Improvements Act. **Do not embed specific dollar amounts in deliverables.** Cite **40 CFR § 19.4** as the authoritative table and pull the current value at draft time.

A frozen baseline (the values published in the Jan 8, 2025 inflation adjustment rule) is recorded in `consent-decree-epa/references/AUTHORITY-STATUS.md` for historical reference only. Updated values are published each January in 40 CFR § 19.4.

## Verification methodology

For each citation in this matrix, the verification ladder is:

1. eCFR (`ecfr.gov`) — for current regulatory text.
2. Cornell LII / U.S. Code — for statutory text.
3. Federal Register — for promulgation history.
4. Agency websites (EPA, FWS, NMFS, CEQ historical, USACE) — for guidance and program-specific detail.
5. Supreme Court / federal-circuit opinions — for case-law-driven interpretive shifts.

Record the verification date for any item changed since the previous review.
