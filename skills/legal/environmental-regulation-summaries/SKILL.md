# Environmental Regulation Summary

Identifies and summarizes applicable environmental laws, compliance obligations, and permitting requirements for a defined industry, activity, or project.

## Prerequisites

1. **Industry or activity** — sector (manufacturing, energy, construction, agriculture, mining, waste management, other)
2. **Jurisdiction** — federal only, specific state(s), local, or multi-jurisdictional
3. **Environmental media involved** — air emissions, water discharge, hazardous waste, land use, wildlife, or combination
4. **Project description** (if applicable) — type, scale, operational activities that trigger regulatory review

## Output Structure

### 1. Executive Overview
- Top 3–5 most critical applicable regulations
- Imminent compliance deadlines or high-penalty exposure areas
- Any recent or pending regulatory changes affecting the operations

### 3. Compliance Obligations by Media

**Air**
- Applicable emission standards (MACT, NSPS, SIP requirements)
- Permit type required (Title V, minor source, synthetic minor)
- Monitoring, recordkeeping, and reporting (MRR) obligations

**Water**
- NPDES individual vs. general permit applicability
- Stormwater (SWPPP, CGP, MS4 if applicable)
- §404/§401 certification triggers

**Waste**
- Generator category (LQG / SQG / VSQG) and resulting obligations
- Manifest, storage time limits, designated facility requirements
- Universal waste applicability

**Land / Site**
- Brownfield considerations, voluntary cleanup programs
- Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure (SPCC) plan triggers
- Emergency Planning and Community Right-to-Know Act (EPCRA) Tier I/II reporting thresholds

### 4. Permitting Roadmap

| Permit | Lead Agency | Typical Timeline | Pre-Application Step |
|---|---|---|---|
| Title V Air | State agency / EPA Region | 12–18 months | Emission inventory |
| NPDES Individual | State / EPA | 6–18 months | Discharge characterization |
| §404 Individual | Army Corps | 12–24+ months | Alternatives analysis |
| Hazardous Waste TSD | EPA / State | 18–36 months | Part A/B application |

### 5. Compliance Checklist

- [ ] All applicable permits identified and obtained or applied for
- [ ] Monitoring and recordkeeping systems operational
- [ ] Annual/semi-annual/quarterly reports calendared
- [ ] Emergency response plan current (SPCC, RMP if applicable)
- [ ] Employee training documented
- [ ] Environmental management system (EMS) in place
- [ ] Regulatory change tracking process established

## Guidelines

- **Jurisdiction layering**: State standards frequently exceed federal minimums; always check state analog statutes and regulations
- **Strict liability**: CERCLA and many state cleanup laws impose liability regardless of fault or intent — flag this prominently for clients
- **Citizen suits**: CAA, CWA, RCRA, and ESA all authorize private enforcement — note where third-party litigation risk exists
- **Verify citations**: Confirm current penalty amounts, thresholds, and rule status before client delivery; EPA frequently adjusts civil penalties for inflation
- **Overlap conflicts**: Identify where multiple permits govern the same activity and whether integrated permitting is available
- **Mark all uncertain statutory citations** with `[VERIFY]`
- Do not render legal opinions on ultimate compliance status; recommend engagement with environmental counsel for high-risk or complex matters
