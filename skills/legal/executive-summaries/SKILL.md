# Executive Summary

Condenses complex legal documents into a standalone, decision-ready summary for senior stakeholders.

## Prerequisites

1. **Source materials** — full document(s) to be summarized (contracts, opinions, reports, filings, etc.)
2. **Audience** — specify recipient type: senior lawyer, business executive, or client (determines technical depth and emphasis)
3. **Purpose** — decision to be made, meeting being prepared for, or action being considered

## Output Structure

Produce a document artifact with a descriptive title identifying subject matter and document type. Default length: 1–3 pages. Use clear headings, short paragraphs, and selective bold for critical points. Favor structured prose over excessive lists.

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| **Overview** | One-paragraph statement of what the document addresses and why it matters |
| **Background** | Essential facts only — parties, dates, key amounts, governing instruments |
| **Key Issues** | Central legal questions or issues at stake |
| **Findings & Analysis** | Material conclusions, holdings, arguments, or risk factors; cite sections/exhibits for critical facts |
| **Conclusions** | Bottom-line results; reflect nuance if underlying document is uncertain or presents multiple views |
| **Recommendations / Next Steps** | Specific actions; call out deadlines and risks prominently |

## Audience Calibration

| Audience | Emphasis |
|---|---|
| Senior lawyer | Technical detail, legal citations, procedural posture |
| Business executive | Business implications, financial exposure, decision impact |
| Client | Plain-language explanation of what findings mean for their situation and what to do next |

## Guidelines

- **Accuracy**: Do not overstate conclusions, minimize risks, or inject opinions absent from source materials
- **Deadlines & risks**: Feature prominently with proposed mitigation where identified
- **Privilege**: Mark summary appropriately if it contains privileged material; characterize information with confidentiality in mind
- **Completeness**: Summary must stand alone — a reader should not need the underlying documents for basic comprehension
- **Brevity discipline**: Omit procedural detail and background unless directly relevant to the decision at hand
