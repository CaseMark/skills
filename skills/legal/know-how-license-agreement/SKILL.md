# Know-How License Agreement

Draft a comprehensive know‑how license agreement for confidential technical information and trade secrets.

## Prerequisites

1. **Parties & authority** — legal names, entity type, jurisdiction, signatory authority.
2. **Know‑how scope** — technical description, boundaries, confidentiality level, exhibits.
3. **Field & territory** — permitted uses, industries, geographies.
4. **Exclusivity** — exclusive, sole, or non‑exclusive; sublicensing stance.
5. **Economics** — upfront fee, royalties, milestones, minimums, audit terms.
6. **Transfer package** — documentation, training, support, timelines.
7. **Diligence** — milestones, reporting, commercialization obligations.
8. **Compliance** — export controls, regulatory approvals, industry rules.
9. **Prior agreements** — existing NDAs, collaboration, or IP licenses.

- [ ] Sublicensing: consent, flow‑downs, termination effects.
- [ ] Improvements/derivatives: ownership and grant‑back.
- [ ] Consideration: upfront, milestones, royalties, minimums.
- [ ] Reports and records; audit rights.
- [ ] Confidentiality and trade secret safeguards.
- [ ] Tech transfer: deliverables, training, support.
- [ ] Diligence and performance milestones.
- [ ] Representations, warranties, disclaimers.
- [ ] Indemnification and procedures.
- [ ] Limitation of liability and carve‑outs.
- [ ] IP ownership and enforcement.
- [ ] Term, termination, wind‑down.

- Year 1: $[amount]

**Confidentiality & Trade Secret Controls**

- [ ] Care standard at least reasonable and not less than own highest‑value info.
- [ ] Access limited to need‑to‑know personnel under written NDA.
- [ ] Physical/technical/administrative safeguards.
- [ ] Compelled disclosure: notice + protective order + minimum disclosure.
- [ ] Survival: perpetual or until public domain without breach.
- [ ] Return/destroy materials; officer certification.

**Improvements & Ownership Decision Table**

| Scenario | Ownership | License Back |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Licensor‑only improvements | Licensor | Included / separate fee |
| Licensee‑only improvements | Licensee | Grant‑back terms |
| Joint improvements | Joint / by inventorship | Cross‑license scope |

**Termination Matrix**

| Trigger | Cure | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Material breach | 30–60 days | Termination |
| Non‑payment | Short cure | Termination or conversion |
| Confidentiality breach | None | Immediate |
| Insolvency | 30–90 days | Automatic |
| Diligence failure | N/A or short | Convert or terminate |

**Exhibits / Schedules**

- Exhibit A: Confidential know‑how description.
- Exhibit B: Tech transfer deliverables and timeline.
- Exhibit C: Milestones and reporting format.
- Exhibit D: Royalty report template.

## Guidelines

- Do not over‑describe the know‑how in the agreement body; put sensitive detail in confidential exhibits.
- Exclusivity requires diligence milestones or minimums.
- Royalty base must be mechanically auditable; define deductions tightly.
- If export‑controlled technology is involved, include explicit export control language and required licenses.
- Use bold or caps for warranty disclaimers and limitation of liability to improve enforceability.
- For international deals, confirm governing law and arbitration seat; flag any choice of law concerns.
- Mark any uncertain citations with `[VERIFY]`.
