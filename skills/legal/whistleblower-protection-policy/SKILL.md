# Whistleblower Protection Policy

Drafts a complete, board-adoptable whistleblower policy with implementation-ready placeholders and US-focused compliance guardrails.

## Prerequisites

1. **Organization profile**: legal name, entity type, public/private/nonprofit status, governing state, and reporting contacts.
2. **Legal applicability matrix**: determine whether SOX/Dodd-Frank/OSHA/False Claims Act/state whistleblower statutes are applicable.
3. **Existing governance stack**: code of conduct, employee handbook, volunteer/board policies, grievance procedure, investigation policy.
4. **Administration model**: who receives reports, who investigates, and who oversees outcomes (C-suite, board committee, etc.).
5. **Operational preferences**: desired channels (email, portal, hotline, written/ verbal), required tone, and word-count target.
6. **Signature/board requirements**: adoption authority, signatory roles, effective-date mechanics, review cadence.
7. **Scope decisions**: whether routine HR complaints are routed elsewhere and how to define exceptions.

## Output Structure / Process

1. **Input normalization and risk scoping**
   - Validate missing inputs and emit a short clarifying checklist before drafting.
   - Identify mandatory versus discretionary protections based on jurisdiction and entity type.

2. **Generate a fixed sectioned policy in this order**
   - 0. Title block and version
   - 1. Introduction and commitment
   - 2. Covered concerns and reporting responsibility
   - 3. Reporting procedures and channels
   - 4. Investigation and resolution process
   - 5. Anti-retaliation protections
   - 6. Confidentiality and limits
   - 7. Good-faith reporting / false reports
   - 8. Administration and governance
   - 9. External legal rights and non-waiver
   - 10. Adoption and effective date

3. **Section-level requirements**

| Section | Required inputs | Must include |
|---|---|---|
| 1 Purpose/Commitment | Org name + ethical commitment | Scope statement, non-retaliation promise, protected intent |
| 2 Covered concerns | Concern taxonomy + exclusions | Serious misconduct only + explicit non-coverage examples |
| 3 Reporting | Contacts + escalation hierarchy | normal path, override path, anonymous option, placeholders |
| 4 Investigation | Investigation owner model | triage, assignment, timeline expectations, updates |
| 5 Retaliation | Disciplinary authority | strict prohibition, protected conduct examples, sanctions |
| 6 Confidentiality | Privilege/confidentiality boundaries | need-to-know limits, legal exceptions, expectation management |
| 7 Good faith | Standard of belief | reasonable belief language, no certainty burden, bad-faith consequences |
| 8 Administration | Board/committee + records practice | governance roles, secure records, training, periodic review |
| 9 External rights | Applicable statutes | supplement-not-replace, no internal-only condition |
| 10 Adoption | Board process | effective date, signature block, supersession language |

4. **Template placeholders to include exactly**
   - `[Organization Name]`, `[State of Formation]`, `[Board Chair]`, `[Executive Director/CEO]`
   - `[Primary Contact]`, `[Alternative Contact]`, `[Hotline URL/Number]`
   - `[Adoption Date]`, `[Effective Date]`, `[Review Cycle]`

5. **Citation discipline**
   - Use law references only when clearly supported.
   - For uncertain scope, append `[VERIFY]` and ask for jurisdictional confirmation.
   - Mandatory examples:
     - `[VERIFY] SOX Section 806 applicability to publicly traded entities`
     - `[VERIFY] Dodd-Frank retaliation channels and jurisdiction-specific standards`
     - `[VERIFY] OSHA and state statute overlap requirements`

6. **Policy template output format**
- Use clear section headings and numbered subsections.
- Keep prose first, with short 3-5 item bullets only where needed.
- End each section with implementation-ready fill-ins, not drafting notes.

```text
[Organization Name] WHISTLEBLOWER PROTECTION POLICY
Version: [v__] | Effective Date: [Date] | Effective for: [Scope entities]

1. INTRODUCTION AND COMMITMENT
...

10. ADOPTION
[Organization Name] Board adopted this policy on [Adoption Date].
Signatures:
[Board Chair]  _____________________  Date: ________
[CEO/Executive Director] _____________________  Date: ________
```

## Guidelines

- Use plain legal language; define reporting, good faith, retaliation, and reportable conduct once and reuse terms consistently.
- Prioritize protection language for good-faith reporters regardless of substantiation outcome.
- Keep lists concise: no more than 5 bullets per section unless replaced by a table.
- Do not state that internal reporting is a legal prerequisite for external agency reporting.
- Add explicit limits on confidentiality (need-to-know, lawful disclosure, legal process, audit/defense needs).
- Include both public-company and nonprofit/private-entity branches where statute relevance differs.
- Require annual or semiannual policy review, training completion, and board/committee reporting metrics.
- Include anti-frivolous-reporting language, but clearly distinguish it from negligent or honest mistakes.
- Finish with non-legal-advice disclaimer: “This policy is internal guidance and does not replace legal counsel for specific rights.”
