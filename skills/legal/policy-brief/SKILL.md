# Public Policy Brief: Legislative Impact Analysis

Produces a balanced, actionable policy brief that distills complex legislation into structured impact analysis for diverse stakeholders.

## Prerequisites

1. **Legislation text** — full bill text, enrolled version, or enacted law
2. **Legislative history** (if available) — committee reports, fiscal notes, floor statements, amendment records
3. **Supplemental materials** (if available) — CBO/CRS analyses, agency impact statements, stakeholder comment letters

## Output Structure

### 1. Executive Summary (2–3 paragraphs)

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Policy area | Subject matter and governing domain |
| Problem addressed | The specific issue the legislation targets |
| Primary mechanism | How the law operates to achieve its goals |
| Key takeaway | Net expected impact in one sentence |

### 2. Background & Context

- Policy problem and triggering events
- Prior legislative attempts or existing law being modified
- Political context: sponsors, coalition dynamics, amendment history
- Relevant comparable policies in other jurisdictions

### 3. Key Provisions

Break down each major provision in plain language:

| Provision | Description | Effective Date | Mandatory/Discretionary | Sunset? |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|------------------------|---------|
| § ___ | ... | ... | ... | ... |

Flag permanent vs. phased implementation and any delegated rulemaking authority.

### 4. Impact Analysis

Analyze across four dimensions:

**Economic**
- Effects on businesses (by size/sector), government budgets, individuals/households
- Include quantitative projections from fiscal notes or CBO scores where available

**Social**
- Affected communities and populations
- Equity and distributional effects
- Public health, education, or safety implications

**Legal & Regulatory**
- Constitutional considerations (if relevant)
- Federalism implications (state/federal interaction)
- Relationship to existing statutory and regulatory framework
- New compliance obligations or enforcement mechanisms

**Implementation**
- Responsible agencies and resource requirements
- Timeline and phasing
- Administrative capacity concerns

### 5. Stakeholder Positions

| Stakeholder | Position | Rationale |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| ... | Support/Oppose/Mixed | ... |

Present viewpoints without advocacy. Note coalition alignments.

### 6. Risks & Open Questions

- Unintended consequences
- Areas of genuine uncertainty
- Implementation bottlenecks
- Litigation risk
- Questions that depend on future rulemaking or appropriations

### 7. Sources

List key sources consulted (legislation text, committee reports, agency analyses, academic studies).

## Guidelines

- **Objectivity is paramount** — inform, do not persuade; acknowledge legitimate debate on all sides
- **Plain language** — define technical or legal terms on first use; use concrete examples for abstract mechanisms
- **Distinguish fact from projection** — label estimates, forecasts, and contested claims explicitly
- **Target length** — 3–6 pages; prioritize efficient consumption by time-constrained readers
- **Jurisdiction** — default to U.S. federal framework; note state-level interactions where relevant
- **Citations** — mark any statutory citation not drawn directly from provided materials with [VERIFY]
- **Audience calibration** — write for a mixed audience of policy professionals and informed non-specialists
