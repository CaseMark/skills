# Expert Witness Deposition

Produces a structured playbook to take, defend, and preserve a record for expert depositions and Daubert/Frye admissibility decisions.

## Prerequisites

1. Confirm governing standard (federal Daubert versus Frye/state variant).
2. Identify objective: **exclude**, **limit**, **impeach**, or **defend** the expert.
3. Collect full Rule 26(a)(2) disclosure package, CV, report, listed materials, and all known prior testimony/publications.
4. Confirm jurisdiction, protective orders, and any pending Daubert motion status.
5. Determine whether counsel wants an attack plan, defense plan, or both.
6. Define witness role, party role, and whether direct or hostile tactics are expected.

## Output Structure / Process

### 1) Mode and Deliverable Matrix

| Mode | Core goal | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| Taking | Challenge qualifications, methodology, facts basis, and opinions | Deposition outline + targeted question set + record-capture log |
| Defending | Prepare expert to testify clearly and withstand Daubert attacks | Mock-exam plan + response models + objection protocol |
| Mixed | Build litigation strategy and witness readiness in one run | Two-side workbook: attack issues mapped to defense counters |

### 2) Mandatory Intake Checklist

```text
DEPOSITION WORKSET
- Case caption, judge, deposition type, jurisdiction
- Expert report and addenda
- Rule 26(a)(2)(B) report details:
  - opinions, bases, data, exhibits, compensation
- Expert CV and publication list
- Prior testimony transcripts (if obtainable)
- Written and electronic exhibits expected
- Pending motions (Daubert/exclusion/limitation)
- Prior discovery rulings affecting objections and scope
```

### 3) Taking an Expert Deposition (Questioning Framework)

1. Use fixed questioning order:
   - Qualifications → engagement/compensation → reviewed materials → methodology → each opinion → application to facts → prior positions.
2. For each topic, force narrow answers, then expand with one follow-up.
3. For every opinion capture:
   - exact stated opinion
   - basis and data
   - assumptions
   - vulnerability rating (high/medium/low)
   - whether it supports exclusion, limitation, or impeachment.
4. Maintain live Daubert tracker keyed to reliability factors.

| Topic | Key asks | Daubert/Frye value |
|---|---|---|
| Qualifications | scope, recency, actual hands-on experience | Limits admissible field |
| Engagement/compensation | retention chain, total fees, repeat retainers | Bias/credibility weight |
| Materials reviewed | who provided, what omitted, assumptions made | Factual foundation strength |
| Methodology | testing, standards, peer review, error rate | Gatekeeping reliability |
| Opinions | precision, certainty, alternatives, contradictions | Helpfulness to trier of fact |
| Prior work | prior testimony/publications | Impeachment and consistency |

### 4) Opinion Lock-In Template

```text
Opinion Block
1) State your exact opinion, no shorthand.
2) What facts/data support it?
3) What methodology produced it?
4) What assumptions did you make?
5) What changes facts first invalidate it?
6) Are there equally valid alternatives?
7) Are there any opinions in this case not in your report?
```

### 5) Daubert/Frye Motion Readiness Checklist

| Evidence point | Record evidence required |
|---|---|
| Methodology not testable/peer-reviewed | direct witness admissions, publications, expert literature |
| No accepted standards | identify missing protocols, calibration, reproducibility gaps |
| No known error rate | ask calculation methodology and validation |
| Not generally accepted in field | expert rebuttal sources, authoritative treatise contrast |
| Unreliable application to facts | omitted or contradictory facts, wrong assumptions |
| Unsupported opinions | missing data linkage and analytic steps |

### 6) Defending an Expert Deposition (Protection Framework)

1. Three-session prep: opinions, methodology, mock hostile cross.
2. Enforce “explain, don’t advocate.”
3. Rehearse scope boundaries:
   - stick to disclosed opinions,
   - avoid legal conclusions,
   - concede limits explicitly.
4. Prep for prior-consistency attacks:
   - identify each prior statement that can be distinguished by time, facts, or standards.
5. Preserve protected channels:
   - draft work-product and strategy communications,
   - finalize what can be answered under Rule 703/fact basis demands.
6. Build concise objection playbook for counsel during deposition.

```text
Defense Brief Packet
- Disclosed-opinion index (report section → witness answer)
- Methodology defense notes (standards, peer support, replication)
- Contradiction-response matrix (prior testimony/publication)
- Bias question mitigation language
```

### 7) Objection and Privilege Handling Matrix (US Federal baseline)

| Issue | Preserve with | Typical use |
|---|---|---|
| Draft materials | work-product / communications objections [VERIFY] | limit production/evasive scope |
| Counsel-assistance details | privilege framing where recognized [VERIFY] | protect preparation strategy |
| Factual assumptions | relevance / foundation objections | force precision before opinion expansion |
| Expert scope | opinions beyond report | cap surprise testimony |

## Guidelines

1. Do not let counsel ask for unconstrained legal conclusions from the expert.
2. Do not assume Frye applies in all states; flag jurisdiction explicitly [VERIFY].
3. Do produce record citations for each critical admission used in dispositive motions.
4. Do track every non-asked/avoided answer as preservation evidence.
5. Do separate admissibility attacks into **exclusion**, **limitation**, and **weight** outcomes.
6. Do keep the expert in lane: technical explanation only, no advocacy.
7. Do not overstate the strength of Daubert/Frye standards; tie every argument to record facts.
8. Do end with a concise hearing-ready summary for the deposition officer and lead counsel.
