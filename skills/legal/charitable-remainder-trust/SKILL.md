# Charitable Remainder Trust (CRT)

Execution-ready CRT agreement that qualifies under IRC 664 and applicable state law.

## Prerequisites

1. Trust type election: CRAT or CRUT, and whether NICRUT or NIMCRUT applies.
2. Parties and roles: settlor, trustee(s), income beneficiary(ies), and charitable remainder beneficiary(ies) or selection authority.
3. Funding assets list with FMV, valuation date, and transfer documents; identify any restricted or problematic assets.
4. Payout terms: percentage or annuity amount, frequency, and term (life/lives or term of years not exceeding 20).
5. Actuarial inputs to satisfy the 10% remainder test and any CRAT probability test [VERIFY].
6. Governing law state, venue, and execution formalities (notary, witnesses, real property requirements).

## Output Structure / Process

Use article-style numbering and include defined terms.

**Trust Type Selection**

| Item | CRAT | CRUT |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Payout base | Fixed annuity based on initial FMV | Annual % of annual FMV |
| Additional contributions | Prohibited | Permitted |
| Valuation | One-time initial | Annual valuation date |
| Variants | Standard | Standard, NICRUT, NIMCRUT |

**Core Agreement Sections**

| Section | Required Content |
| --- | --- |
| Declaration | Identify settlor, trustee, beneficiaries; state intent to qualify under IRC 664 and applicable Treasury regs (§1.664-1 to §1.664-4). |
| Irrevocability | Expressly irrevocable; no amendment except to maintain qualification or correct scrivener’s error. |
| Funding | Asset description, valuation date, transfer warranties, acceptance by trustee, restrictions on problematic assets. |
| Payout Clause | CRAT annuity or CRUT unitrust; 5%–50% payout range; frequency; start date. |
| Net Income Limit | NICRUT or NIMCRUT election if applicable; define “net income” and makeup mechanics. |
| Term | Life/lives or term of years not exceeding 20; termination trigger. |
| Remainder | Identify qualified charities or selection mechanism; contingency for disqualified/defunct charities. |
| Trustee Powers | Investment authority, valuation procedures, professional retention, accounting, and reporting. |
| Tax Compliance | Prohibited transactions, UBTI avoidance, and qualification safeguards. |
| Accounting | Annual statements to income beneficiaries and remainder charities. |
| Amendment | Limited to qualification maintenance and scrivener’s error, no change to beneficial interests. |
| Termination | Final accounting; remainder distribution in cash or in kind at trustee discretion. |
| Execution | Signature blocks, acknowledgments, notary, witnesses if required. |

**Payout Math (Insert in Clause or Appendix)**

```text
CRAT: Annual annuity = Initial FMV × Payout %
CRUT: Annual unitrust amount = Annual FMV × Payout %
Remainder test: PV of remainder >= 10% of initial FMV
Term: Life/lives or <= 20 years
```

**Charitable Beneficiary Clause**

Include one of the following structures and add contingencies:

| Structure | Requirements |
| --- | --- |
| Named charities | Full legal names, addresses, EINs; confirm 501(c)(3) status. |
| Selection authority | Identify selector; require selection only among qualified 501(c)(3) organizations at distribution. |
| Cy pres | Permit substitution with similar charitable purpose if a named charity is ineligible or defunct. |

**Compliance Checklist (Insert as internal drafting checklist)**

1. IRC 664(d)(1) or 664(d)(2) structure identified.
2. Payout between 5% and 50% of FMV.
3. Term does not exceed 20 years if term-based.
4. 10% remainder test satisfied.
5. CRAT: no additional contributions; annual payout fixed.
6. CRUT: annual valuation date defined.
7. Prohibited transaction and self-dealing prohibitions included (IRC 4941-4945 as applicable) [VERIFY].
8. UBTI avoidance clause and trustee duty to maintain qualification [VERIFY].

## Guidelines

- State explicitly that the trust is intended to qualify under IRC 664 and Treasury Regs §1.664-1 to §1.664-4.
- Do not permit amendment that changes income or remainder interests.
- Include trustee duty to avoid self-dealing and other prohibited acts; add qualified exception language for administrative corrections [VERIFY].
- Flag any contributed asset that could cause UBTI or other tax issues; require trustee discretion to decline or dispose.
- If a CRAT, do not allow additional contributions or variable payout adjustments.
- If a CRUT, define the annual valuation date and permissible valuation method.
- Use [VERIFY] where local law or specialized tax tests are uncertain.
