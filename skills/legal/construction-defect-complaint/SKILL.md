**Construction Defect Complaint**

Draft a litigation-ready construction defect complaint that pleads defect facts, preserves prerequisites, and survives early dismissal.

**Prerequisites**
1. Property identification: full address, legal description, ownership chain, acquisition date(s)
2. Project documents: contracts, scopes of work, plans/specs, change orders, warranties, completion dates
3. Defect evidence: photos, inspection notes, expert reports, repair estimates, testing results
4. Communications: notices, responses, repair attempts, inspection access logs
5. Jurisdictional prerequisites: pre-suit notice statutes, opportunity-to-repair requirements, certificate-of-merit rules if applicable
6. Limitations analysis: statute of limitation, statute of repose, discovery dates, tolling facts
7. Defendant roster: legal entity names, roles, licensing info, registered agents, relationship to plaintiff

**Output Structure / Process**
Use this section order and content.

| Section | Required Content | Exhibits |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Caption | Court, parties, case number placeholder, document title | None |
| Parties | Plaintiff ownership/standing; each defendant’s role and contract chain | Optional contracts |
| Jurisdiction/Venue | Subject-matter basis, personal jurisdiction facts, venue facts | None |
| Background | Project scope, dates, contract price, completion, warranties | Contract, warranty |
| Defect Allegations | System-by-system defects with discovery date, manifestation, and causal link | Photos, reports |
| Pre-Suit Compliance | Notice dates, method, response/repair attempts, inspection access | Notice letters |
| Damages | Repair costs, loss of use, diminution, consequential damages | Estimates |
| Causes of Action | Separate counts with element-matched facts | None |
| Prayer | Relief tailored to claims and statutes | None |
| Jury Demand | If requested | None |
| Verification | If required by local rules | Verification |

Use this defect-allegation template for each system or location.

```text
Defect: [Component/system]
Defendant(s): [Entity + trade role]
Observed condition: [Specific defect]
Discovery date: [Month/Year]
Manifestation: [Leak/crack/failure pattern]
Cause: [Improper install/material defect/design error, if supported]
Resulting damage: [Water intrusion, mold, structural movement, etc.]
Supporting evidence: [Report/Photo/Estimate ID]
```

Claim selection checklist (include only supported claims):

- Breach of Contract: contract + obligation + breach facts + damages
- Breach of Express Warranty: warranty terms + breach facts + timely notice
- Breach of Implied Warranty: habitability/workmanship/fitness + latent defect facts + damages
- Negligence: duty + breach of standard of care + causation + damages
- Negligence Per Se: identified code/standard + violation facts + causation
- Products Liability/Strict Liability: product defect + commercial seller + causation + damages
- Misrepresentation/Fraud: who/what/when/where + reliance + damages
- Statutory Consumer/Construction Act: elements and preconditions specific to jurisdiction

Pleading standards:

- If in federal court, plead enough factual detail to satisfy plausibility
- If pleading fraud or misrepresentation, satisfy heightened particularity

Damages model checklist:

- Repair and remediation costs (itemized)
- Consequential damages (loss of use, relocation, storage)
- Diminution in value (if not fully repairable)
- Pre- and post-judgment interest if authorized
- Attorney’s fees and costs if contract/statute allows

**Guidelines**
- Do verify pre-suit notice and opportunity-to-repair compliance; plead dates, methods, and responses
- Do allege each defendant’s role with specificity; avoid group pleading where possible
- Do align each claim to jurisdictional elements; omit claims barred by economic loss doctrine unless independent duty or property damage is alleged
- Do address statute of repose and limitations with clear discovery facts
- Do attach exhibits strategically; avoid attaching documents that undermine allegations
- Don’t assert code violations without a factual basis or expert support
- Don’t plead warranty disclaimers unless necessary to address known defenses
- Don’t include personal injury damages unless supported by evidence and causation
- Note jurisdiction-specific requirements: pre-suit mediation, right-to-repair acts, expert certification, or special pleadings may apply
