# Legal Opinion Letter

Drafts a formal legal opinion letter for M&A and corporate transactions with jurisdiction-specific analysis, calibrated conclusion language, and complete qualifications.

## Prerequisites

1. **Transaction description** — structure, parties, purpose (M&A, financing, regulatory, etc.)
2. **Governing documents** — agreements, corporate records, certificates, resolutions
3. **Jurisdictions** — governing law and jurisdictions requiring analysis
4. **Opinion recipient** — client only or named third-party relying parties
5. **Specific questions** — enumerated legal issues the opinion must address
6. **Exclusions** — matters outside scope (tax, securities, antitrust, etc.)

## Output Structure

### Header Block
```
[Firm Letterhead]
[Date]

[Recipient Name, Title]
[Recipient Address]

Re: [Matter Name / Transaction Description]
```

### 1. Introduction
- Identify the attorney-client or professional relationship
- Name the specific transaction or matter
- State the purpose of the opinion in one paragraph

### 2. Scope of Opinion
- List the specific legal questions being answered (numbered)
- Explicitly exclude out-of-scope matters:

| Excluded Matter | Note |
|---|---|
| Tax consequences | Advise client to consult tax counsel |
| Securities law compliance | Advise client to consult securities counsel |
| Antitrust / regulatory | Note if applicable |
| Business judgment / wisdom | Outside legal analysis |
| Laws of non-covered jurisdictions | Identify which jurisdictions are covered |

### 3. Documents Reviewed
- Enumerated list with full titles, execution dates, parties, and relevant sections

### 4. Factual Background
- Material facts only, organized chronologically or by transaction component
- Distinguish clearly:
  - **Verified**: facts confirmed through document review (cite document, section, page)
  - **Represented**: facts accepted as client representations
  - **Assumed**: facts assumed for purposes of the opinion (flag prominently)

### 5. Assumptions
Standard assumptions to include (tailor as needed):

- [ ] Document signatures are genuine and authorized
- [ ] Documents reviewed are authentic and complete; no undisclosed amendments
- [ ] Signatories had requisite corporate authority
- [ ] No oral modifications to written agreements
- [ ] Entities are duly formed and in good standing
- [ ] No insolvency proceedings pending or contemplated
- [ ] No undisclosed facts that would affect the analysis

### 6. Legal Analysis
For each question in Scope:

```
[Issue Heading]

Applicable law: [cite statute/regulation/common law rule with full citation]

[Explain rule → apply to facts → address counterarguments or ambiguities]

Where authority is split or unsettled, acknowledge directly and explain 
the basis for preferring one line of authority.
```

**Citation standards:**
- Statutes: current version; note any pending amendments [VERIFY if recent]
- Cases: verify not overruled; note binding vs. persuasive; pinpoint cite
- Regulations: current CFR section and effective date
- Agency guidance: note persuasive (not binding) weight

### 7. Opinion / Conclusions

Use calibrated language matched to confidence level:

| Confidence Level | Language |
|---|---|
| High (clear law, undisputed facts) | "It is our opinion that…" |
| Moderate (some ambiguity) | "It is our opinion that it is more likely than not that…" |
| Predictive (court outcome) | "We believe a court would likely conclude that…" |
| Conditional | "Assuming [X], it is our opinion that…" |

Address each question from Scope in parallel numbered structure.

### 8. Qualifications and Limitations

- [ ] Opinion rendered as of the date of the letter; no duty to update
- [ ] Based solely on laws of [specified jurisdictions] as of the opinion date
- [ ] Does not address laws of any other jurisdiction
- [ ] Reliance limited to: [named recipient(s) / client only]
- [ ] May not be distributed or relied upon by others without written consent
- [ ] Subject to: equitable principles, bankruptcy/insolvency law, public policy
- [ ] Subject to: general principles limiting availability of specific remedies

### 9. Signature Block
```
[Attorney Full Name]
[Bar Admission: Jurisdiction, Bar No.]
[Title / Firm Name]
[Address, Phone, Email]
```
If firm opinion: clarify signing capacity and whether opinion is institutional.

## Guidelines

- **Scope discipline**: Never let the opinion drift beyond enumerated questions; unsolicited opinions on adjacent issues create unintended liability
- **Certainty calibration**: Excessive hedging is as problematic as overstatement — use the table above deliberately
- **Citation verification**: Do not cite cases or statutes from memory; mark uncertain citations `[VERIFY]`
- **Third-party reliance**: Identify relying parties by name; time-limit reliance where appropriate
- **Updating obligation**: Expressly disclaim any duty to update unless separately engaged
- **Local counsel**: If opining on a jurisdiction where the drafter is not admitted, note the basis for confidence (local counsel consultation, research, etc.)
- **Length**: Typically 3–10 pages depending on complexity; do not pad with background law that adds no analytical value
