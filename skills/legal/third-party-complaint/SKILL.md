# Third-Party Complaint (Impleader)

Drafts a complete FRCP 14 / state-equivalent third-party complaint establishing derivative liability against an impleaded party.

## Prerequisites

1. **Original complaint** — full copy for attachment as Exhibit A and incorporation by reference
2. **Basis for derivative liability** — contracts with indemnification clauses, insurance policies, warranties, or facts supporting common-law indemnity/contribution
3. **Third-party defendant identity** — proper legal name, registered agent, address for service
4. **Jurisdictional facts** — citizenship of all parties (if diversity), contacts for personal jurisdiction, venue basis
5. **Timing status** — whether filing is within 14 days of original answer (no leave required) or leave of court is needed

## Output Structure

### Caption & Introduction

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Court | Full court name, division |
| Case number | Original case number |
| Party designations | Original Plaintiff; Defendant **and Third-Party Plaintiff**; Third-Party Defendant |
| Title | "THIRD-PARTY COMPLAINT" |
| Opening paragraph | Identifies FRCP 14(a) / state rule; states purpose of shifting/sharing liability |

### Section I: Procedural Posture

- Summarize original complaint: nature of claims, legal theories, relief sought
- State: "The Complaint is attached as Exhibit A and incorporated by reference"
- Note date of original answer and timeliness of third-party filing

### Section II: Jurisdiction & Venue

| Type | Address |
|---|---|
| Subject matter | Federal question / diversity (confirm complete diversity + amount in controversy) or supplemental under 28 U.S.C. § 1367 |
| Personal jurisdiction | Contacts, consent, or long-arm statute basis |
| Venue | Proper under 28 U.S.C. § 1391 or applicable state rule |

### Section III: Factual Allegations

Present chronologically. Must establish:

- [ ] Relationship between Defendant/TPP and Third-Party Defendant (contractual, business, insurer-insured)
- [ ] Specific agreements creating indemnification/defense/warranty obligations — quote key language
- [ ] Third-Party Defendant's acts or omissions causing/contributing to Plaintiff's alleged harm
- [ ] Notice given to Third-Party Defendant (dates, method, demands made, response or non-response)
- [ ] Causal chain: TPD conduct → Defendant's exposure → Plaintiff's claims

### Section IV: Counts

Structure each as a separate numbered count. Select applicable theories:

| Count | Theory | Key Elements |
|---|---|---|
| Contractual Indemnification | Express indemnity clause | Quote provision; show claims fall within scope; show conditions precedent met |
| Common Law Indemnification | Derivative/vicarious liability only | Defendant's liability is purely passive; TPD is actual wrongdoer |
| Contribution | Shared fault | Both parties at fault; seek apportionment per comparative fault principles |
| Breach of Contract/Warranty | Failure to perform | Identify obligation, breach, causation to Defendant's exposure |
| Subrogation | Defendant already paid | Payment to Plaintiff; TPD bears ultimate responsibility |

For each count:
1. Incorporate relevant factual paragraphs by reference
2. State elements of the cause of action
3. Identify governing law
4. Conclude: "Defendant is entitled to judgment against Third-Party Defendant on this Count"

### Section V: Prayer for Relief

```
WHEREFORE, Defendant and Third-Party Plaintiff respectfully requests:

(a) Judgment against Third-Party Defendant for all sums adjudged
    against Defendant in favor of Plaintiff, including any settlement;
(b) Full indemnification or contribution for compensatory and, if
    applicable, punitive damages;
(c) Defense costs, reasonable attorney's fees, and litigation expenses
    [if supported by contract or statute — cite basis];
(d) Declaratory judgment that Third-Party Defendant is obligated to
    defend and indemnify Defendant;
(e) Pre- and post-judgment interest at the applicable rate;
(f) Costs of prosecuting this Third-Party Complaint;
(g) Such other relief as the Court deems just and proper.
```

### Signature Block & Verification

Standard attorney signature block with Rule 11 / state equivalent certification.

## Guidelines

- **Timing**: If filed within 14 days of serving the original answer, no leave of court required (FRCP 14(a)(1)). After that, must obtain leave by motion.
- **Service**: Serve Third-Party Defendant with the third-party complaint AND the original complaint plus all prior pleadings (FRCP 14(a)(1)).
- **Derivative liability only**: FRCP 14 requires the third-party claim be derivative of the original claim — independent claims against TPD belong in a separate counterclaim/crossclaim or must satisfy independent jurisdictional grounds.
- **State variations**: Many states track FRCP 14 but check local timing rules, leave requirements, and contribution statutes (e.g., UCFA states vs. joint-and-several jurisdictions).
- **Supplemental jurisdiction**: Third-party claims typically fall under 28 U.S.C. § 1367 — but confirm no statutory exception applies.
- **Contract language**: Always quote indemnification provisions verbatim; do not paraphrase trigger language or scope limitations.
- **Rule 11 compliance**: Every factual allegation must have evidentiary support or be likely to after reasonable investigation.
- **Party consistency**: Use "Defendant and Third-Party Plaintiff" on first reference, then "Defendant/TPP" or "Third-Party Plaintiff" consistently throughout.
