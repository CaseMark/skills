# Intercreditor Agreement (Lien Priority)

Allocate collateral control, remedies, and payment rights between first lien and second lien creditors.

## Prerequisites

1. First lien credit agreement and security documents, including collateral description and obligations schedule.
2. Second lien credit agreement and security documents, including collateral description and obligations schedule.
3. Parties list (agents, lenders, borrower), execution blocks, and notice addresses.
4. Negotiated business points for standstill length, enforcement control, DIP/cash collateral positions, permitted payments, and amendment thresholds.
5. Governing law, forum, and service of process preferences.

**Article Outline**

1. Article I - Definitions and construction.
2. Article II - Lien priority and subordination.
3. Article III - Enforcement rights and standstill.
4. Article IV - Payment waterfall and turnover.
5. Article V - Bankruptcy and insolvency.
6. Article VI - Amendments and restrictions on second lien documents.
7. Article VII - Representations and warranties.
8. Article VIII - Covenants and cooperation.
9. Article IX - Miscellaneous (governing law, jurisdiction, notices, waiver of jury trial, successors).

**Required Clauses Checklist**

- Absolute priority of first lien over all shared collateral, regardless of timing of perfection.
- Subordination is continuing until Discharge of First Lien Obligations.
- Exclusive enforcement control by first lien agent during standstill.
- Turnover obligation for any junior receipts.
- Proceeds applied first to first lien obligations, then second lien.
- Prohibition on junior objections to first lien enforcement actions, subject to negotiated carve-outs.
- Bankruptcy subordination under 11 U.S.C. 510(a).
- Restrictions on amendments to second lien documents that worsen senior position.
- Notice mechanics and amendment consent requirements.

## Guidelines

- Use lien subordination language unless the parties also intend debt subordination; align the waterfall and turnover accordingly.
- Define "Discharge of First Lien Obligations" to include cash collateralization or backstop of contingent LC/hedging exposure.
- Keep defined terms consistent across the agreement and cross-reference credit documents precisely.
- If permitting junior payments, limit to scheduled interest and fees when no first lien default exists.
- In bankruptcy provisions, confirm 11 U.S.C. 510(a) enforceability and address cash collateral, DIP financing, relief from stay, and plan voting rights.
- Address post-petition interest and proceeds allocation with correct statutory references (e.g., 11 U.S.C. 552(b)(1) [VERIFY]).
- Confirm UCC Article 9 perfection status and whether after-acquired property and proceeds are included.
- If borrower is a party, include covenants not to direct payments contrary to the waterfall.
- Confirm whether junior creditors may file proofs of claim or seek adequate protection after standstill expiry, and draft carve-outs narrowly.
- Ensure notices, amendment consent, and assignment provisions match the deal structure and agent roles.
