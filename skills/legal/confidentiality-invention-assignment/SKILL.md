# Confidentiality and Invention Assignment Agreement

Creates an execution-ready agreement protecting trade secrets and assigning work-related IP while complying with state and federal limits.

## Prerequisites

1. Company legal name, entity type, and address.
2. Worker identity, role, status (employee or contractor), and start/effective date.
3. Governing law and primary work location(s).
4. Business description, core products/services, and sensitive information categories.
5. Any existing agreements to supersede or incorporate by reference.
6. Decision on restrictive covenants and their scope, duration, and geography.
7. Prior inventions list for Exhibit A and any required statutory notices.

## Output Structure / Process

**Document Outline**

| Section | Core Requirements |
| --- | --- |
| Title and Effective Date | Clear title (PIIA/CIIA) and effective date. |
| Parties | Exact legal names and addresses; role labels (Company, Employee/Contractor). |
| Recitals | Access to confidential info, IP creation expectations, legitimate business interests. |
| Definitions | Confidential Information; Inventions; Company Business; Trade Secrets. |
| Confidentiality Obligations | Use-only-for-company, no disclosure, reasonable care, no unauthorized copying. |
| Exceptions | Public domain, prior possession, third-party lawful receipt, independent development. |
| Term | Trade secrets: indefinite; other confidential info: defined period. |
| Return/Destruction | Immediate return, delete copies, certify compliance. |
| DTSA Immunity Notice | Required notice text. |
| Invention Assignment | Present assignment + promise to assign; scope triggers. |
| Prior Inventions | Exhibit A disclosure; representation if none. |
| Disclosure and Cooperation | Prompt written disclosure; cooperation for filings; post-termination compensation. |
| Work Made for Hire | Copyright ownership plus backup assignment. |
| Moral Rights | Waiver where allowed. |
| Restrictive Covenants (Optional) | Non-solicit, non-compete, tolling, blue-pencil. |
| Consideration | Employment, continued employment, or separate consideration. |
| General Provisions | Governing law, forum/arbitration, entire agreement, severability, notices. |
| Acknowledgments | Review, counsel opportunity, voluntariness, reasonableness, DTSA notice. |
| Signatures | Employee/contractor and authorized company signatory. |
| Exhibits | Exhibit A (Prior Inventions); Exhibit B (CA 2870 text if applicable). |

**Clause Templates**

DTSA immunity notice:
```text
Employee is hereby notified that under the Defend Trade Secrets Act, an individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or state trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is made (i) in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney, and solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (ii) in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.
```

Present assignment:
```text
Employee hereby assigns and agrees to assign to Company all right, title, and interest worldwide in and to all Inventions that: (a) relate to Company’s business or actual or demonstrably anticipated research or development; (b) result from work performed for Company; or (c) are created using Company equipment, supplies, facilities, time, or trade secret information.
```

California 2870 carve-out:
```text
This Agreement does not require assignment of any invention that qualifies fully under California Labor Code Section 2870. A copy of Section 2870 is attached as Exhibit B.
```

Prior inventions schedule:
```text
Exhibit A — Prior Inventions
Employee lists all inventions, original works, developments, or improvements created before employment that relate to Company’s business and are excluded from assignment. If none, Employee indicates “None.”
```

Return and certification:
```text
Upon termination or request, Employee shall return all Company property and Confidential Information and certify in writing that no copies are retained, including from personal devices and cloud accounts.
```

**Restrictive Covenants Decision Checklist**

- [ ] Non-compete is prohibited in CA, ND, OK except narrow statutory exceptions.
- [ ] Non-solicit duration and scope align with role and jurisdiction.
- [ ] Consideration is adequate for post-employment restrictions.
- [ ] Geographic scope matches actual business footprint and employee territory.
- [ ] Blue-pencil clause included only if the jurisdiction enforces reformation.

**Exhibits**

- Exhibit A: Prior Inventions Disclosure.
- Exhibit B: California Labor Code 2870 text (if CA applies).

## Guidelines

- Keep definitions broad enough for coverage but narrow enough for enforceability.
- Use present-tense assignment language and include a backup “agree to assign.”
- Include jurisdiction-specific carve-outs for employee inventions; do not overreach.
- Avoid non-competes in prohibited states; prefer narrowly tailored non-solicit.
- Provide post-termination cooperation compensation when required or advisable.
- Ensure choice-of-law and forum clauses do not waive mandatory worker protections.
- Mark any uncertain statutory citations with `[VERIFY]` before finalizing.
