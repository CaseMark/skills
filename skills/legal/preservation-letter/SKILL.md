# Document Preservation Letter

Drafts a formal preservation notice creating an unambiguous record of the opposing party's duty to preserve evidence, tailored to the specific matter's custodians, data sources, and time period.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case identification** — parties, case caption/docket (if filed), or description of anticipated claims
2. **Key facts** — relevant dates, transactions, relationships, and events establishing the dispute
3. **Known custodians** — employees, contractors, agents, or third parties likely possessing relevant information
4. **Data sources** — known IT systems, communication platforms, cloud services, retention policies used by recipient
5. **Temporal scope** — relevant time period for preservation (start date through present)

## Output Structure

### Letter Format

Use formal business correspondence: law firm letterhead, date, recipient block (full name/title/org/address), re-line with matter description.

### Required Sections

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| **Opening Notice** | Identify counsel, client, nature of dispute. If filed: case caption, court, docket. If pre-suit: state client reasonably anticipates litigation. Cite FRCP 37(e) or state equivalent. |
| **Matter Description** | Sufficient factual context to enable recipient to identify relevant custodians and sources. Do not disclose litigation strategy. |
| **Preservation Scope** | Categorized preservation instructions (see table below) |
| **Implementation Instructions** | Litigation hold procedures (see checklist below) |
| **Spoliation Consequences** | Legal consequences of non-compliance |
| **Response Requirements** | Written acknowledgment deadline (5–10 business days), confirmation of hold implementation, contact information |
| **Closing** | Formal closing, full signature block with bar admissions |

### Preservation Scope Categories

Draft specific instructions for each applicable category:

| Category | Includes |
|---|---|
| **Paper documents** | Correspondence, contracts, memoranda, handwritten notes, reports, financials, invoices, drafts, working papers |
| **Electronic communications** | Email, text messages, instant messages, social media, voicemails — across all platforms and devices including personal devices/accounts used for business |
| **ESI** | Word processing files, spreadsheets, databases, presentations, calendars, contacts, task lists — in native format with metadata intact |
| **Cloud/storage** | Cloud services, shared drives, backup systems, archived data, servers, mobile devices, tablets, laptops |
| **Physical evidence** | Products, equipment, machinery, vehicles, or other tangible items (if applicable) |
| **Audio/visual** | Recordings, photographs, videos, surveillance footage, multimedia |

Emphasize: metadata must be preserved intact; no format conversions; personal devices/accounts included if used for business purposes.

### Litigation Hold Implementation Checklist

Instruct the recipient to:

- [ ] Immediately suspend all routine document destruction, auto-deletion, email purging, and retention schedules
- [ ] Issue written litigation hold notice to all identified custodians (by name and position)
- [ ] Notify IT department to disable auto-deletion, preserve backups, protect systems from maintenance/upgrades that could cause data loss
- [ ] Preserve all new documents/information created after receipt that relate to the matter
- [ ] Maintain records of preservation efforts (hold notices, IT protocols, related communications)
- [ ] Continue hold until matter is fully resolved or written release is provided

### Spoliation Consequences

Address these consequences with appropriate firmness:

- Court sanctions under FRCP 37(e) ranging from monetary penalties to adverse inference instructions
- Dismissal of claims/defenses or default judgment in egregious cases
- Contempt findings
- Sanctions available even for negligent destruction; intentional destruction may trigger punitive sanctions and criminal liability
- Reliance on routine business practices is not a valid defense once litigation is reasonably anticipated

## Guidelines

- **Tone**: Professional courtesy paired with unmistakable firmness about legal obligations
- **Scope calibration**: Tailor preservation categories to the actual dispute — do not include inapplicable categories (e.g., physical evidence in a pure contract case)
- **Temporal scope**: Define explicitly with start and end dates
- **No strategy disclosure**: Provide enough context for compliance without revealing legal theories
- **Ongoing duty**: Emphasize preservation is continuous, not one-time
- **Questions ≠ delay**: Note that questions about scope do not excuse or delay compliance
- **Jurisdiction**: Default to federal standards (FRCP 37(e)); adapt to state rules if specified
- **Verify all citations**: Confirm any cited case law or statutory references are accurate; mark uncertain citations with [VERIFY]
