# Deed of Gift

Drafts a complete, recordable Deed of Gift conveying real or personal property without consideration, compliant with applicable state execution and recording requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Donor/Donee info** — full legal names (as on official ID), addresses, any representative capacity (trustee, executor, corporate officer)
2. **Property description** — for real property: complete legal description from prior recorded deed (lot/block, subdivision, metes and bounds, or government survey), prior deed recording reference (book, page, instrument number, date); for personal property: serial number, VIN, account number, or other unique identifier
3. **Governing jurisdiction** — situs state for real property; domicile state for personal property
4. **Encumbrances** — any existing liens, mortgages, or restrictions on the property
5. **Relationship between parties** — if relevant to establishing donative intent

## Output Structure

### Document Sections

| Section | Required Content |
|---|---|
| **Title & Date** | "Deed of Gift"; execution date; governing law jurisdiction |
| **Parties** | Donor (grantor) and donee (grantee): full legal name, address, capacity; entity type + authorized rep if applicable |
| **Recitals** | Donor's voluntary donative intent; relationship or motivation; any background facts; jurisdiction-specific recital language if required |
| **Granting Clause** | Conveyancing words appropriate to jurisdiction (e.g., "give, grant, and convey"); explicit statement of gift without consideration; present-tense transfer |
| **Property Description** | Complete legal description verbatim from prior deed; prior deed recording reference; for personal property: all unique identifiers |
| **Declarations** | Irrevocability; donor's authority and legal capacity; title warranty or quitclaim (per jurisdiction preference); identification of any encumbrances |
| **Acceptance** | Donee's express acceptance (required in some jurisdictions for gift to be complete) |
| **Execution** | Donor signature line + printed name; date; witness lines if required (see table below) |
| **Notarial Acknowledgment** | Statutory acknowledgment language per state; notary signature, seal, commission expiration |

### Jurisdiction Execution Matrix

| Requirement | Varies By State — Research Required |
|---|---|
| Witnesses | Number (0–2), qualification (disinterested), placement |
| Notarization | Mandatory vs. optional; certificate of acknowledgment vs. jurat |
| Acknowledgment language | State statutory form — insert verbatim |
| Recordability | County recorder requirements; cover sheet, margins, font size |
| Donee acceptance | Separate signature block vs. incorporated recital |

### Granting Clause Template

```
[DONOR FULL NAME], ("Donor"), for donative purposes and without consideration,
does hereby give, grant, bargain, sell, and convey to [DONEE FULL NAME], ("Donee"),
the following described property [located in [COUNTY], [STATE]]:

[COMPLETE LEGAL DESCRIPTION]

Being the same property conveyed to Donor by [PRIOR GRANTOR] by deed dated
[DATE], recorded [DATE] in [BOOK/VOL] [PAGE/INSTRUMENT NO.], [COUNTY] records.
```

## Guidelines

- **Gift tax flag**: Gifts exceeding the annual exclusion ($18,000/donee for 2024 [VERIFY current IRS threshold]) may require Form 709; advise donor to consult a tax professional for high-value transfers
- **Irrevocability**: Once delivered and accepted, a deed of gift is generally irrevocable — confirm donor understands this before execution
- **Entity donors**: Verify corporate/LLC/trust authority to make gifts; may require board resolution or trust agreement review
- **Personal property**: A deed of gift is one method; some assets (vehicles, securities, bank accounts) transfer more cleanly via title/account-level mechanisms — flag alternatives if appropriate
- **Recording**: Real property gift deeds should be recorded in the county where property is located to provide constructive notice and complete transfer; confirm recording fees and transfer tax exemptions (gifts are often exempt but require an affidavit)
- **Capacity**: If donor is elderly or has cognitive concerns, consider adding explicit capacity recitals or obtaining contemporaneous capacity documentation
- **Cite jurisdiction-specific statutes** for acknowledgment language; do not use generic boilerplate without verifying current state requirements
