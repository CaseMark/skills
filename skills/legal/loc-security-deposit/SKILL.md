# Irrevocable Standby Letter of Credit — Security Deposit

Drafts a standalone irrevocable standby LOC that secures tenant obligations under a commercial lease, compliant with UCC Article 5, ISP98, and state security deposit statutes.

## Prerequisites

1. **Executed lease agreement** — with security deposit amount, notice clauses, cure periods, renewal options, and forum-selection provisions
2. **Party details** — full legal names, addresses, and tax IDs for landlord/beneficiary, tenant/applicant, and issuing bank (including branch, charter info, authorized signatories)
3. **Property description** — address and legal description as stated in the lease
4. **State security deposit statute** — confirm whether the jurisdiction imposes caps, interest accrual requirements, or special conditions on LOC alternatives to cash deposits
5. **Bank requirements** — issuing bank's signature authority rules, transfer fee schedule, and standard LOC reference numbering convention

## Output Structure

### Document Header

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Title | "IRREVOCABLE STANDBY LETTER OF CREDIT — SECURITY DEPOSIT" |
| Reference No. | Bank's standard LOC numbering |
| Issuance Date | Date of execution |
| Governing Rules | "Issued subject to ISP98 and UCC Article 5 as adopted in [Bank's State]" |
| Lease Reference | Full lease date, property address, landlord name, tenant name — matched exactly to lease |

### Section 1: Parties

- **Issuing Bank**: Legal name, principal office, branch, charter/regulatory info, authorized signatories (name + title)
- **Beneficiary (Landlord)**: Legal name per title/lease, notice address, tax ID
- **Applicant (Tenant)**: Legal name, co-tenants/guarantors if any
- If property management company involved or ownership may transfer, address notice routing

### Section 2: Credit Amount & Purpose

- State amount in **numerals and words**; reconcile with lease deposit requirement
- Adjust for state-mandated interest accrual if applicable
- Purpose statement: secures tenant's full performance — rent, maintenance, restoration (reasonable wear and tear excepted)
- Include independence principle: bank's obligation is **documentary only**, independent of landlord-tenant disputes

### Section 3: Drawing Conditions

Beneficiary draws by presenting to issuing bank's designated office:

| Document | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Sight draft | Drawn on issuing bank for amount not exceeding available balance |
| Beneficiary's signed statement | On letterhead, certifying: (a) tenant default or damage, (b) specific lease provision breached or damage description, (c) amount claimed |
| Notice certification | Dates and methods of all required default notices sent to tenant per lease and applicable law |
| Supporting documents (optional) | Copies of default notices, final account statements, damage assessments — draft to avoid imposing investigative burden on bank |

- Presentation: hand delivery or overnight courier with proof of receipt
- Examination period: **[5] business days** after receipt of complying documents (per ISP98)
- Bank examines facial compliance only — no duty to investigate underlying facts

### Section 4: Expiration, Renewal & Termination

- **Initial expiry**: [30–60] days after lease termination date
- **Evergreen clause** (if lease has renewal options): auto-extends for successive 1-year periods unless bank gives **written non-renewal notice ≥ 60 days** before current expiry
- **Termination procedure**: beneficiary returns original LOC + signed release confirming full tenant performance → bank obligations discharged
- Ensure expiry accommodates state statutory deadlines for security deposit return/itemized statements

### Section 5: Transferability

- [ ] Transferable / [ ] Non-transferable (select per deal terms)
- If transferable:
  - Current beneficiary submits: written transfer request + original LOC + evidence of property transfer or lease assignment
  - Bank issues amended LOC or transfer certificate within **[10] business days**
  - Transfer fee: **[amount/percentage]** payable by [applicant/beneficiary]
  - Bank relies on facial regularity of transfer documents — no duty to investigate validity
  - State whether partial transfers permitted or single complete transfer only
- Must comply with UCC § 5-112 transfer requirements

### Section 6: Partial Draws & Reinstatement

- [ ] Partial draws permitted / [ ] Full draw only
- If partial draws permitted:
  - Each draw statement must specify exact amount and confirm it ≤ available balance
  - Bank notifies both parties of remaining balance after each draw
  - **Reinstatement**: applicant may restore to original amount by [paying fees + providing documentation] within **[15] days** of draw, or LOC remains permanently reduced

### Section 7: Governing Law & Disputes

- LOC governed by ISP98 and UCC Article 5 of **[Bank's State]**, without conflict-of-law principles
- Underlying lease governed separately per its own choice-of-law clause
- Exclusive jurisdiction: state and federal courts in **[Bank's jurisdiction]**
- Applicant irrevocably submits to jurisdiction; waives inconvenient forum objection

### Section 8: Execution

- Signature blocks for **two** authorized bank officers (name, title, date)
- Bank certification: signatories are duly authorized; instrument is valid, binding, enforceable obligation of issuing bank
- Optional beneficiary acknowledgment of receipt (not a condition of effectiveness)
- Safekeeping instructions: beneficiary must present original for draws or amendments

## Guidelines

- **Independence principle**: Never draft conditions that require the bank to evaluate underlying lease disputes or investigate facts beyond the presented documents
- **State law check**: Verify jurisdiction's security deposit statute — some states (e.g., CA, NY, MA) impose specific requirements on LOC alternatives including caps, interest, and return timelines [VERIFY specific state statutes at time of drafting]
- **ISP98 Rule 1.06**: Standby is irrevocable unless it expressly states otherwise — include explicit "irrevocable" language regardless
- **UCC § 5-108**: Standard of strict compliance for document examination — drawing conditions must be unambiguous and facially determinable
- **UCC § 5-112**: Governs transfer rights — ensure transfer provisions don't conflict
- **Avoid investigative burdens**: Drawing documents should never require the bank to verify external facts, contact third parties, or interpret lease provisions
- **Negotiate draw triggers carefully**: Overly broad triggers invite wrongful draws; overly narrow triggers undermine the security purpose — balance per deal context
- **Always confirm**: Bank's internal LOC issuance requirements, signature authority limits, and whether the bank requires its own standard form or accepts custom drafts
