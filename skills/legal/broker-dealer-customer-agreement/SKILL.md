# Broker-Dealer Customer Agreement

Drafts a regulation-compliant customer agreement establishing the full contractual relationship between a registered broker-dealer and its customers.

## Prerequisites

1. **Firm registration details** — SEC registration (Form BD), FINRA CRD number, state registrations, dual-registrant status
2. **Business model** — execution-only vs. full-service, proprietary platforms, order routing, PFOF arrangements, principal trading
3. **Product catalog** — all security types offered (equities, fixed income, options, mutual funds, ETFs, penny stocks, alternatives)
4. **Fee schedule** — commissions by product, account fees, margin rates, revenue sharing, 12b-1 fees
5. **Existing documentation** — current account applications, margin agreements, options agreements, privacy policy, Form CRS
6. **Regulatory history** — exam findings, deficiency letters, enforcement actions, complaint trends

## Output Structure

### Section 1: Party Identification & Account Establishment

| Element | Requirements |
|---------|-------------|
| Broker-dealer ID | Legal name (per SEC registration), principal office, SEC reg number, FINRA CRD |
| Customer ID (individuals) | Full legal name, residential address, DOB, SSN/TIN, citizenship — per CIP Rule (USA PATRIOT Act §326) |
| Customer ID (entities) | Legal name, jurisdiction, principal office, TIN, beneficial owners (≥25% or control persons) |
| Account types | Cash (Rule 15c3-3), margin (Reg T), retirement (ERISA/IRC), joint (JTWROS vs. TIC) |
| KYC/Suitability data | Investment objectives, risk tolerance, financial situation, liquidity needs, time horizon, experience — per FINRA Rules 2090/2111 |
| Firm reserved rights | Reject applications, request additional documentation, restrict activities, close accounts |

### Section 2: Service Relationship & Obligations

**Capacity disclosure** — State precisely whether firm acts as:
- Execution-only broker (no advice, no recommendations)
- Recommendation-making broker-dealer (subject to Reg BI)
- Dual registrant (delineate when acting as BD vs. IA under Advisers Act of 1940)

**Reg BI four-component framework** (if firm makes recommendations):

| Obligation | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Disclosure | Material facts about relationship and conflicts |
| Care | Reasonable diligence; reasonable basis to believe recommendation is in customer's best interest |
| Conflict of interest | Policies to identify, disclose, and mitigate conflicts |
| Compliance | Written policies and procedures |

**Product catalog** — Enumerate all offered products with applicable special rules:

| Product | Special Requirements |
|---------|---------------------|
| Listed/OTC equities | Standard disclosures |
| Penny stocks (<$5, unlisted) | SEC Rule 15g-2 risk disclosure document, quotation disclosure, compensation disclosure, monthly statements |
| Options | Separate options agreement; ODD delivery; approval tiers by strategy; FINRA Rule 2360 |
| Mutual funds | Share class disclosure (A/B/C); revenue sharing conflicts; 12b-1 fee disclosure |
| Leveraged/inverse ETFs | Daily reset compounding risk; short-term trading suitability only |
| Margin lending | Separate margin agreement; Reg T/FINRA 4210 compliance |
| Day trading | Pattern day trader rules; $25K minimum equity; special risk disclosure |
| Reg S securities | Representation letters; transfer restrictions |

### Section 3: Trading Authorization & Order Handling

**Order entry methods** — Specify each channel (phone, web, mobile, automated) with:
- Authentication procedures (MFA, biometrics, security questions)
- Customer responsibility for credential security
- Telephone recording disclosure
- Error reporting procedures

**Best execution obligations:**
- Duty to seek most favorable terms reasonably available
- Factors: price, speed, likelihood of execution/settlement, order size, total cost
- Not a guarantee of best possible price on every trade

**Order routing disclosure (SEC Rule 606):**
- Market makers, ECNs, exchanges, proprietary desk
- Payment for order flow arrangements — disclose existence and conflict
- Quarterly Rule 606 reports available

**Order types and risks:**

| Order Type | Risk |
|-----------|------|
| Market | No price protection; execution may differ from last quote |
| Limit | May not execute if market doesn't reach price |
| Stop | Becomes market order when triggered; no price protection |
| Stop-limit | May not execute if market gaps through limit |
| GTC | Remains active; customer must monitor for continued intent |

**Discretionary authority** (if applicable) — FINRA Rule 3260:
- Written grant required; may be limited by scope
- Heightened supervisory review
- Revocable at any time by written notice
- All trades must remain consistent with customer profile

### Section 4: Fee & Compensation Disclosure

Draft full transparency provisions covering:

| Category | Detail Required |
|----------|----------------|
| Commissions | Schedules by security type; minimums; negotiability |
| Markups/markdowns | Fixed income principal trading; customer right to request prevailing market price |
| Account fees | Maintenance, inactivity, paper delivery, closing/transfer (ACATS), wire, returned items — exact amounts or reference to fee schedule |
| Margin interest | Base rate, spread, calculation method (daily/monthly), posting schedule, variable rate disclosure, tiered rates by balance |
| PFOF | Per-share or spread-based; conflict disclosure; Rule 606 reference |
| Fund compensation | Revenue sharing, 12b-1, sales loads by share class; conflict disclosure |
| Underwriting | Selling concessions; new issue incentive conflict |
| Proprietary products | Affiliated fund/product conflicts |
| Cross-reference | Reg BI disclosure document and Form CRS |

### Section 5: Margin Lending

**Regulatory framework:**

| Requirement | Source | Standard |
|------------|--------|----------|
| Initial margin | Reg T (Federal Reserve) | 50% of purchase price |
| Maintenance margin | FINRA Rule 4210 | 25% of current market value (minimum) |
| House requirements | Firm policy | Typically 30-50%+; may change without notice |
| Special requirements | Firm policy | Security-specific based on volatility/liquidity |

**Include concrete example:**
> Customer buys $10,000 stock on margin → deposits $5,000, borrows $5,000. If stock drops to $6,000 → equity = $1,000 (16.7% of market value) → maintenance call triggered.

**Firm rights in margin accounts:**
- Charge interest per disclosed schedule
- Lend/hypothecate customer securities (per Rule 15c3-3) to extent of debit balance
- Fully paid securities: no lending without express consent

**Margin call provisions:**
- Timeframe: typically 2-5 business days (firm reserves right to require immediate action)
- Methods to meet: cash deposit, securities deposit, position liquidation
- **Liquidation rights (PROMINENT LANGUAGE):** Firm may sell securities without prior notice or consent; firm has sole discretion over which securities, timing, and method; firm not required to minimize loss or optimize tax consequences; customer not entitled to choose securities or demand delay

**Bold-faced risk warnings** (require initial/separate signature):
- Losses can exceed initial investment
- Customer may owe more than deposited
- Firm can force sale over customer's objection
- Customer cannot choose which securities are sold
- Margin calls may come with little response time

### Section 6: Risk Disclosures & Acknowledgments

Draft product-specific risk disclosures:

| Risk Category | Key Points |
|--------------|------------|
| Market risk | All securities can lose value; past performance ≠ future results |
| Liquidity risk | Wide spreads; may not sell at desired price; acute for thinly traded securities |
| Equity risk | Earnings expectations; company-specific events; common stock subordinate to all debt |
| Options risk | Time decay; total premium loss; unlimited risk on uncovered writes; leverage |
| Fixed income | Interest rate risk (inverse price relationship); credit/default risk; call risk; inflation risk |
| Penny stock | Illiquidity; manipulation; limited information; potential total loss |
| Leveraged/inverse ETFs | Compounding deviation over >1 day; daily monitoring required |
| International | Currency risk; political risk; different accounting/disclosure standards; limited legal recourse |

**Required acknowledgments** (signature/initial):
- Read and understood agreement and risk disclosures
- Received ODD (options), penny stock disclosure (if applicable)
- Securities can lose value including total loss of principal
- Solely responsible for decisions in non-discretionary accounts
- Should not invest funds needed for near-term expenses

### Section 7: Corporate Actions & Account Maintenance

- **Dividends/interest** — credited on payable date; reliance on issuer/agent data
- **Mandatory actions** (mergers, splits, spin-offs) — processed per issuer terms
- **Voluntary actions** (tenders, rights offerings, exchanges) — notice provided; customer must respond by deadline; default action on non-response
- **Proxy voting** — materials forwarded per SEC rules; firm may vote routine matters for non-responsive customers; cannot vote non-routine matters without instruction
- **Securities lending** (if applicable) — voting rights lost while on loan; tax treatment of substitute payments may differ; securities may not be immediately available for sale
- **Fractional shares** — state policy (maintain, round, or liquidate)
- **Confirmations** — sent T+1; must review immediately
- **Statements** — monthly/quarterly; must review for errors
- **Error reporting deadlines** — trade confirmations: 5 business days; statements/unauthorized transactions: 30 days; late reporting may limit remedies

### Section 8: Privacy & Data Security

Draft per SEC Regulation S-P:

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Information collected | PII, financial data, transaction history, investment profile, third-party sources |
| Uses | Account servicing, regulatory compliance (IRC §6045, BSA), fraud prevention, service improvement |
| Sharing — service providers | Clearing firms, custodians, technology vendors, printers |
| Sharing — regulators | SEC, FINRA, FinCEN, state regulators, courts (subpoena) |
| Sharing — affiliates | Related financial entities (subject to opt-out) |
| Sharing — non-affiliates | Marketing purposes only with opt-out right |
| Opt-out procedures | Phone, written request, or website; remains effective until revoked |
| Security measures | Encryption (transit/rest), access controls, MFA, penetration testing, employee training, incident response |
| Breach notification | Per applicable state laws; notice of breach nature, data affected, remediation steps |
| Disclaimer | No absolute security guarantee; industry-standard measures maintained |

### Section 9: Amendment, Assignment & Termination

**Amendments:**
- Firm may amend with 30 days' written notice
- Continued account use = acceptance
- Customer may reject by closing account within notice period
- Regulatory/security changes may take effect with shorter or no notice

**Assignment:**
- Customer: no assignment without firm's written consent
- Firm: may assign in mergers, acquisitions, affiliate transfers; assignee assumes all obligations; customer receives notice

**Termination:**

| Scenario | Procedure |
|----------|-----------|
| Customer-initiated | Settle outstanding trades; pay all balances; instruct asset disposition (ACATS transfer 5-7 days, DRS, liquidation) |
| Firm-initiated | Minimum balance violations; agreement breaches; suspicious activity; failure to provide required documentation; death/incapacity |
| Firm's post-termination rights | Liquidate to satisfy debts; retain assets as lien; charge closing fees; customer liable for any deficiency |
| Records | Retained per SEC Rule 17a-4 (6 years post-close); copies available for reasonable fee |

### Section 10: Mandatory Arbitration

**FINRA-required disclosure (use bold/caps formatting):**

> BY SIGNING THIS AGREEMENT, THE PARTIES AGREE THAT ALL CONTROVERSIES SHALL BE RESOLVED BY BINDING ARBITRATION. ALL PARTIES GIVE UP THE RIGHT TO SUE IN COURT, INCLUDING THE RIGHT TO A TRIAL BY JURY. ARBITRATION AWARDS ARE GENERALLY FINAL AND BINDING. THE ABILITY TO HAVE A COURT REVERSE OR MODIFY AN AWARD IS VERY LIMITED. DISCOVERY IS GENERALLY MORE LIMITED THAN IN COURT. ARBITRATORS NEED NOT EXPLAIN THEIR AWARD UNLESS A JOINT REQUEST FOR AN EXPLAINED DECISION IS SUBMITTED AT LEAST 20 DAYS BEFORE THE FIRST HEARING. THE PANEL TYPICALLY INCLUDES A MINORITY OF INDUSTRY-AFFILIATED ARBITRATORS. TIME LIMITS MAY APPLY.

**Scope:** All controversies — account transactions, agreement terms, recommendations, fees, account handling; all legal theories (contract, tort, statute)

**Forum:** FINRA Dispute Resolution (primary); NYSE or AAA by agreement

**Location:** Customer's state of residence or nearby

**Panel composition:**
- Claims >$100K: three arbitrators; majority public (non-industry)
- Claims ≤$100K: single arbitrator
- Party participation in selection via ranked strike lists

**Costs:** Filing fees per FINRA schedule; shared arbitrator fees; each party bears own attorneys' fees unless panel awards otherwise

**Finality:** Very limited judicial review — vacatur only for fraud/corruption, evident partiality, or arbitrators exceeding powers; no merits review

**Preserved rights:** Customer may still file complaints with SEC, FINRA, state regulators

**Governing law:** State law of [firm's principal office / customer's residence]; federal securities laws preempt where applicable; Federal Arbitration Act governs arbitration clause; severability provision

### Section 11: Execution & Acknowledgments

**Customer representations:**
- Full legal capacity and authority
- Legal age; no legal disability
- All application information accurate and complete
- Understands investment risk including loss of principal
- Received all required regulatory disclosures (FINRA investor materials, BCP summary)

**Entity-specific representations:**
- Signatory properly authorized (bylaws, partnership agreement, trust instrument)
- Agreement doesn't violate governing documents
- All necessary approvals obtained (board resolutions, partner consents)
- Entity duly organized and in good standing
- Supporting documentation attached (resolutions, certificates, trust certifications)

**Signature requirements:**

| Account Type | Signatures Required |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Individual | Customer signature, printed name, date |
| Joint | All account holders; specify JTWROS or TIC |
| Entity | Authorized representative with title; second signature if applicable |
| Firm | Representative signature or acceptance by account activation |

**E-signature compliance** (E-SIGN Act / UETA):
- Click-to-agree, typed name, or touchscreen signature accepted
- Same legal effect as handwritten
- Customer consents to electronic business conduct and delivery
- Right to withdraw e-delivery consent (paper fees may apply)
- Hardware/software requirements disclosed
- Withdrawal doesn't affect prior signatures

**Integration clause:** Agreement + incorporated documents (fee schedule, margin agreement, options agreement, privacy policy) = entire agreement; supersedes all prior agreements/representations

**Non-waiver:** Firm's failure to enforce any provision doesn't waive future enforcement

## Guidelines

- Research current SEC releases, FINRA regulatory notices, and enforcement actions before drafting
- Tailor all provisions to the firm's actual business model — do not include provisions for services not offered
- Ensure consistency across all customer-facing documents (Form CRS, Reg BI disclosure, fee schedule, privacy policy)
- Use defined terms consistently; define on first use or in definitions section
- Use clear headings and numbered sections for easy reference in disputes and compliance reviews
- All statutory and rule citations should be verified against current law [VERIFY any citation you are uncertain about]
- Margin and arbitration risk warnings must be prominent (bold, caps, or separate signature/initial)
- Penny stock provisions trigger only if firm offers penny stocks (SEC Rules 15g-2 through 15g-9)
- State law variations may affect enforceability of certain provisions — flag jurisdiction-specific issues
- Agreement must be comprehensible to retail investors with limited financial sophistication
