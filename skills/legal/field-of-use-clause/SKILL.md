# Field of Use Restriction Clause

Drafts an enforceable Field of Use Restriction Clause limiting a licensee's exploitation of licensed IP to a defined permitted field.

## Prerequisites

1. **Licensed IP identification** — patent numbers, patent claims, know-how description, or trade secret scope
2. **Permitted field definition** — industry, application, customer type, geography, or combination
3. **Restricted fields** — express list of excluded uses or markets (inferred if not provided)
4. **License structure** — exclusive vs. non-exclusive; sublicense rights; term and milestones
5. **Party information** — licensor, licensee, any existing licenses or third-party rights that constrain scope

## Output Structure

### 1. Definitions

| Term | Drafting Notes |
|------|---------------|
| **Licensed Technology** | Incorporate patent numbers, claim references, or technical spec language verbatim |
| **Field of Use** | Delineate by one or more of: industry sector, therapeutic area, application method, customer class, geography |
| **Permitted Applications** | Affirmative list of approved uses |
| **Restricted Fields** | Exclusive list of prohibited uses; "including but not limited to" construction |
| **Licensed Products/Services** | Tie to Permitted Applications; define whether improvements are included |

**Delineation approaches by sector:**

| Sector | Typical Field Definition Method |
|--------|---------------------------------|
| Pharma/biotech | Therapeutic indication, species (human vs. veterinary), delivery route |
| Software | Deployment type (SaaS, on-prem), end-user category (educational, commercial), geography |
| Manufacturing | Product category, production volume thresholds, end-use application |
| Semiconductors | Device class, performance tier, market segment |

---

### 2. Grant of License

```
Licensor hereby grants to Licensee a [exclusive/non-exclusive], non-transferable license 
under [Patent Nos. ___ / the Licensed Technology] solely within the Field of Use to:
  (a) make, have made, use, sell, offer for sale, and import Licensed Products; and
  (b) [practice the Licensed Methods] [use the Licensed Know-How],
subject in each case to the terms and conditions of this Agreement. All rights not 
expressly granted are reserved by Licensor.
```

- Specify whether license extends to continuations, divisionals, and foreign counterparts
- Address improvements: whether licensee's field-of-use improvements are licensed back
- Sublicense rights: if permitted, require sublicensees to be bound by identical field restrictions

---

### 3. Restrictions on Use

```
Licensee shall not, directly or indirectly:
  (a) use the Licensed Technology for any purpose outside the Field of Use;
  (b) supply Licensed Technology or components thereof to any third party with knowledge 
      or reason to know such party will use them outside the Field of Use;
  (c) grant sublicenses, assign, or otherwise transfer rights to use the Licensed 
      Technology outside the Field of Use;
  (d) take any action through Affiliates, contractors, or third-party arrangements 
      intended to circumvent the Field of Use restriction.
```

---

### 4. Monitoring, Reporting, and Audit Rights

- **Records retention**: Licensee maintains records of all uses for [3–5] years
- **Periodic reports**: Quarterly/annual compliance reports documenting uses by application and market; tie to royalty statements if applicable
- **Audit rights**: Licensor may audit books and operations on [30-day] notice, [once per year], at Licensor's expense unless material discrepancy found (>5%), then at Licensee's expense
- **Cooperation obligation**: Licensee provides reasonable access to facilities, personnel, and systems

---

### 5. Remedies for Breach

| Remedy Type | Provision |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Termination** | Immediate upon material breach (unauthorized field use is deemed material); [30-day] cure for minor/inadvertent violations |
| **Enhanced royalties** | [2–3×] the applicable royalty rate on revenues from unauthorized field use |
| **Disgorgement** | Profits derived from exploitation in Restricted Fields |
| **Liquidated damages** | If included, must be reasonable estimate of harm (not punitive) — tie to lost royalty opportunity |
| **Injunctive relief** | Expressly preserved; parties acknowledge breach causes irreparable harm for which money damages are inadequate |
| **Post-termination** | Licensee ceases all use in Restricted Fields; returns/destroys Licensed Technology; assigns IP developed through unauthorized use to Licensor |
| **Survival** | Field-of-use and confidentiality obligations survive termination |

---

### 6. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

- **Governing law**: [State] — favor jurisdictions with developed licensing case law (NY, DE, CA)
- **Dispute resolution**: Arbitration (AAA or JAMS) recommended for complex technology; specify panel with relevant technical expertise
- **Carve-out**: Licensor retains right to seek TRO/preliminary injunction in any court of competent jurisdiction notwithstanding arbitration clause
- **Venue**: Designate for any court proceedings
- **Attorneys' fees**: Each party bears own unless position found substantially unjustified

---

## Guidelines

- **Specificity controls**: Vague field definitions invite litigation; prefer enumerated application lists over broad descriptors
- **Indirect circumvention**: Always include affiliate/contractor anti-circumvention language
- **Sublicense pass-through**: Any permitted sublicense must incorporate field restrictions verbatim or by reference
- **FRAND considerations**: If licensed patents are subject to FRAND commitments, field-of-use restrictions may conflict — [VERIFY] applicability
- **Antitrust**: Overly broad field restrictions in cross-licenses or standard-essential patents can raise § 1 Sherman Act concerns — [VERIFY] current DOJ/FTC guidance
- **Patent exhaustion**: Field-of-use restrictions in patent licenses are generally enforceable post-*Quanta* only for non-authorized uses; structure carefully — [VERIFY] *Quanta Computer, Inc. v. LG Electronics, Inc.*, 553 U.S. 617 (2008)
- **Jurisdiction**: US-centric; EEA licenses require review under EU technology transfer block exemption (TTBER) [VERIFY]
