# CDA Certified Claim

Produce a CDA-compliant claim package that preserves appeal rights and supports entitlement and quantum.

## Prerequisites

1. Executed contract and all modifications, including disputes/changes clauses.
2. Contracting officer name, title, office address, and submission method.
3. Chronology of events with source documents.
4. Damages data with source records (cost reports, invoices, payroll, schedules).
5. Authorized company official to sign certification (if claim > $100,000).

## Output Structure / Process

Use this structure and template. Replace bracketed fields.

**1) Header**
- Claimant: full legal name and address (as in contract)
- Contracting Officer: name, title, agency, office address
- Contract Number: including mods
- Contract Award Date
- Date of Claim Submission
- Subject: "Certified Claim under the Contract Disputes Act (CDA)"

**2) Executive Summary (5–10 lines)**
- Contract purpose, type, period of performance
- Claim type(s): change / differing site conditions / delay / breach
- Relief requested: definite sum and/or time extension
- Request for final decision

**3) Contract Background**
Include contract facts in a table.

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Contract type | [FFP / CPFF / T&M] |
| Scope | [brief scope] |
| POP | [start–end] |
| Value | [base + options] |
| Key clauses | [list clause numbers/titles] |

**4) Statement of Claim (Definite Relief)**
- Monetary claim: `$[exact amount]` (no ranges or estimates).
- Time extension: `[exact days]` calendar/work days (if requested).
- Identify claim theory and triggering government act/omission.

**5) Factual Narrative (Chronological)**
Use a dated table with exhibit cites.

| Date | Event | Personnel | Exhibit |
|---|---|---|---|
| [YYYY-MM-DD] | [event] | [names/titles] | [Ex. #] |

**6) Entitlement (Legal/Regulatory Basis)**
Cite applicable authority and map elements to facts.

| Theory | Elements | Contract/FAR/Statute | Facts Satisfied |
|---|---|---|---|
| Change | [element list] | [FAR 52.243-1 / 52.243-2 or clause] |
| Differing Site Conditions | [Type I/II elements] | FAR 52.236-2 |
| Delay/Suspension | [elements] | [Suspension clause] |
| Other | [elements] | [authority] |

Required authority anchors:
- Contract Disputes Act, 41 U.S.C. §§ 7101–7109.
- FAR Part 33 (Disputes and Appeals).
- FAR 33.211 (final decision requirements) [VERIFY].
- If citing case law, include full case name, citation, and holding in 1 sentence.

**7) Quantum (Damages)**
Present a summary table, then detail by category.

| Cost Category | Basis | Amount | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Direct labor | [hours x rate] | $ | [Ex. #] |
| Materials | [qty x unit] | $ | [Ex. #] |
| Equipment | [rental/owned] | $ | [Ex. #] |
| Subcontracts | [COs/invoices] | $ | [Ex. #] |
| Indirects | [rate x base] | $ | [Ex. #] |
| Delay/overhead | [method] | $ | [Ex. #] |
| Productivity loss | [method] | $ | [Ex. #] |
| CDA interest | [rate + period] | $ | [Ex. #] |
| **Total** |  | **$[total]** |  |

For delay/home-office overhead, specify method (e.g., Eichleay) and inputs.
For interest, cite 41 U.S.C. § 7109.

**8) Certification (Required if > $100,000)**
Insert exact statutory text without modification:

"I certify that the claim is made in good faith; that the supporting data are accurate and complete to the best of my knowledge and belief; that the amount requested accurately reflects the contract adjustment for which the contractor believes the Government is liable; and that I am duly authorized to certify the claim on behalf of the contractor."

Signature block:
- Name
- Title
- Company
- Date

Note: For claims ≤ $100,000, omit certification but still request a final decision.

**9) Demand for Final Decision**
State: "This is a claim under the CDA and requests a final decision by the Contracting Officer."

Include CDA appeal preservation language: 41 U.S.C. § 7104 [VERIFY].

**10) Exhibits**
Provide an index and cite each exhibit in the narrative and quantum.

| Exhibit | Description | Date | Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|

**11) Delivery**
State delivery method and attach proof of receipt (mail, courier, or email). Keep a complete copy.

## Guidelines

- Use a definite sum; no ranges or "to be determined."
- Map every factual assertion to an exhibit.
- Keep tone factual and non-argumentative.
- Ensure claim is addressed to the proper contracting officer.
- If citing case law, verify jurisdiction (ASBCA, CBCA, COFC, Fed. Cir.).
- Mark uncertain citations with `[VERIFY]`.
