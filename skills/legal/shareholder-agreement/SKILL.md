# Shareholder Agreement

Drafts a company-specific shareholder agreement aligned to the charter, bylaws, and applicable state law.

## Prerequisites

1. Jurisdiction of incorporation and entity type (corp vs. close corp or statutory variants).
2. Charter and bylaws (current versions with amendments).
3. Current cap table by class/series with ownership percentages and special rights.
4. List of shareholders and their roles (founder, investor, employee, passive).
5. Desired governance model and approval thresholds.
6. Transfer/exit objectives (liquidity controls, buy-sell triggers).
7. Dispute resolution preference (court vs. arbitration).
8. Restrictive covenant policy and enforceability constraints.

**Core Clause Checklist**

- Parties, recitals, effective date, hierarchy vs. charter/bylaws.
- Definitions: Affiliate, Transfer, Permitted Transferee, Fair Market Value, Change of Control, Cause, Good Reason.
- Capitalization schedule with class/series rights.
- Preemptive rights or explicit waiver.
- Board composition and designation rights with threshold maintenance.
- Shareholder voting rights by class and written consent.
- Meeting notice, quorum, remote participation.
- Information rights and inspection.
- ROFR and process timelines.
- Co-sale (tag-along) and drag-along mechanics.
- Permitted transfers and joinder requirement.
- Involuntary transfer treatment (death/divorce/bankruptcy).
- Valuation method and appraisal dispute mechanics.
- Payment terms: down payment, note terms, interest.
- Restrictive covenants and confidentiality (if enforceable).
- Dispute resolution ladder and carve-outs.
- Exit events, IPO termination, dissolution.
- Notices, amendments, severability, waiver.
- Assignment and third-party beneficiary disclaimer.
- Spousal consent if applicable.

**Clause Templates (Insert and tailor)**

```text
[Hierarchy]
This Agreement supplements the Charter and Bylaws. If conflict: Charter controls; then this Agreement; then Bylaws, to the extent permitted by law.

[ROFR Notice]
Selling Shareholder delivers written notice stating: proposed transferee, number/class of shares, price, terms, and copies of offers. Company has [30] days to elect. If declined, non-selling shareholders have [30] days to elect pro rata, with oversubscription rights.

[Tag-Along]
If holders of >[50]% propose a Transfer to a third party, each other shareholder may participate pro rata on identical terms by giving notice within [10] days.

[Drag-Along]
Upon approval by holders of >[X]% of outstanding voting power, all shareholders must sell on the same terms, subject to pro rata escrow/indemnity capped at proceeds.

[Valuation]
Fair Market Value determined by [method]. If appraisal: parties choose appraiser within [15] days, else appoint by [ASA/JAMS]. Appraisal completed within [60-90] days. Discounts for lack of control/marketability: [include/exclude].

[Note Terms]
Deferred purchase price paid over [3-5] years at [AFR + X%], prepayable without penalty, secured by pledge of purchased shares, default accelerates.

[Restrictive Covenants]
Non-competition and non-solicitation apply to [active shareholders] for [12-24] months in [defined territory], limited to [defined business]. Confidentiality survives [3-5] years; trade secrets indefinitely.

[Dispute Resolution]
Notice → executive meeting within [15] days → mediation within [60] days → [arbitration/court]. Equitable relief available for confidentiality, noncompete, transfer restrictions.
```

**Schedules / Exhibits**

- Schedule A: Capitalization Table.
- Schedule B: Shareholder Contact Information.
- Exhibit 1: Form of Joinder.
- Exhibit 2: Form of Promissory Note.
- Exhibit 3: Spousal Consent (if applicable).

**Quality Assurance Checklist**

- Defined terms used consistently; no undefined caps.
- Cross-references accurate.
- Charter/bylaws conflict check completed.
- Transfer restrictions comply with state statute requirements.
- Noncompete and nonsolicit enforceability confirmed.
- Arbitration clause and carve-outs are consistent.
- Signature blocks match entity authority.

## Guidelines

- Confirm state corporate statute requirements for enforceability of transfer restrictions and shareholder agreements.
- Avoid unreasonable restraints on alienation; document legitimate business purpose for restrictions.
- Limit restrictive covenants to enforceable scope; exclude jurisdictions where noncompetes are restricted unless narrowed to protect trade secrets.
- If founders/employee shares vest, coordinate with equity plan and tax counsel for 83(b) timing.
- Ensure securities law compliance for private transfers and legends on certificates/records.
- Use spousal consent in community property states or where marital rights may attach.
- Do not override mandatory charter provisions or statutory protections for minority shareholders.
- Use a conspicuous jury trial waiver only if enforceable in the jurisdiction.
