# Franchise Confidentiality / NDA

Protect franchisor proprietary information during franchise evaluation while preserving due diligence access.

## Prerequisites

1. **Parties**: Franchisor legal name, state of organization, principal place of business; prospective franchisee entity or individual details.
2. **Disclosure scope**: What will be shared (operations manual excerpts, FDD, financials, vendor lists, recipes, training materials).
3. **Timeline**: Anticipated disclosure dates and evaluation window.
4. **Jurisdiction**: Governing law and venue preferences (typically franchisor home state).
5. **Business intent**: Confirm NDA is pre-franchise and does not guarantee a franchise award.

## Output Structure / Process

**A. Draft order (use numbered sections)**

1. **Parties and Recitals**
2. **Definitions**
3. **Exclusions**
4. **Permitted Purpose / Use**
5. **Confidentiality Obligations**
6. **Restrictions and Non-Contact**
7. **Required Disclosures by Law**
8. **Return / Destruction**
9. **Term and Survival**
10. **Remedies**
11. **No License / No Obligation / No Reliance**
12. **Governing Law / Venue / Jury Waiver / Arbitration (if any)**
13. **Miscellaneous**
14. **Signatures**

**B. Core clause checklist**

- Recitals: evaluation of possible franchise relationship; no obligation to grant franchise; NDA is separate from FDD or franchise agreement confidentiality.
- Definition of Confidential Information:
  - Operations manuals, training materials, system standards.
  - Financial performance representations, pro formas, unit economics.
  - Marketing strategies, advertising assets, brand guidelines.
  - Supplier lists, pricing, vendor relationships.
  - Trade secrets, recipes, formulas, processes, know-how.
  - Business model, operational systems, existence of the opportunity.
  - All forms of disclosure (written, oral, electronic, visual) before/after execution.
  - No requirement to mark as confidential if apparent by context.
- Exclusions (burden on recipient; documented proof):
  - Publicly available without breach.
  - Rightfully possessed pre-disclosure.
  - Independently developed without use.
  - Rightfully received from third party without breach.
  - Legal compulsion only with advance notice and cooperation for protective order.
- Use restrictions:
  - Sole purpose: evaluate franchise opportunity.
  - Care standard: at least reasonable care and no less than recipient's own.
  - Disclosure only to advisors with need-to-know and confidentiality obligation.
  - No reverse engineering or derivation.
  - No contact with franchisor franchisees, suppliers, or customers without written consent.
  - No copies except as necessary; copies remain franchisor property.
  - Breach notice and cooperation obligations.
- Return/destruction:
  - Return or certified destruction within 5 business days of request or termination.
  - Include certification of destruction.
- Term/survival:
  - Trade secrets: perpetual or as long as trade secret status maintained.
  - Other confidential info: 3–5 years from last disclosure or termination, whichever is later.
  - Survival for return, remedies, governing law.
- Remedies:
  - Injunctive relief and specific performance without bond.
  - Monetary damages, disgorgement, attorneys’ fees and costs.
  - Liability for agents/advisors.
  - Optional liquidated damages only if state law permits and reasonable.
- No license / no obligation / no reliance:
  - No IP license or franchise rights granted.
  - No obligation to disclose or enter franchise agreement.
  - Recipient relies on its own investigation; no warranties on accuracy/completeness.
  - No partnership/joint venture/agency.
- Governing law / venue:
  - Franchisor home state law and exclusive venue.
  - Consent to personal jurisdiction and waiver of inconvenient forum.
  - Optional arbitration carve-out allowing court injunctive relief.
- Miscellaneous:
  - Entire agreement, amendments in writing, severability, waiver, assignment limits.
  - Notice methods and addresses.

**C. Clause template skeleton (edit placeholders)**

```text
1. Purpose. Recipient will use Confidential Information solely to evaluate a potential franchise relationship with Discloser.
2. Confidential Information. "Confidential Information" means ...
3. Exclusions. Confidential Information does not include information Recipient can prove by contemporaneous written records that ...
4. Obligations. Recipient shall (a) protect..., (b) limit access..., (c) not reverse engineer..., (d) not contact...
5. Compelled Disclosure. If Recipient is required by law..., Recipient will give prompt notice and cooperate...
6. Return/Destruction. Within five (5) business days..., Recipient will return or certify destruction...
7. Term. Obligations begin on Effective Date and continue... Trade secrets remain protected as long as they are trade secrets.
8. Remedies. Recipient acknowledges irreparable harm and agrees to injunctive relief without bond...
9. No License / No Obligation / No Reliance. Nothing herein grants any license or obligates Discloser...
10. Governing Law; Venue. This Agreement is governed by the laws of [State]. Exclusive jurisdiction in [County, State] courts...
11. Miscellaneous. Entire agreement; amendments; severability; waiver; assignment; notices.
```

## Guidelines

- Confirm consistency with franchise disclosure timing and the FTC Franchise Rule [VERIFY].
- Avoid overbroad restrictions that may be unenforceable under state law; keep scope tied to evaluation purpose.
- Keep defined terms consistent and capitalized.
- Make exclusions proof-based and document-dependent.
- Ensure advisors receiving information are bound to confidentiality.
- Do not allow blanket legal-compulsion disclosure without notice and protective order cooperation.
- If using arbitration, preserve immediate court access for injunctive relief.
- Include explicit non-contact with existing franchisees, suppliers, and customers.
- Ensure no IP license or franchise rights are implied.
