# Cash Collateral Motion

Drafts a motion for authorization to use cash collateral under 11 U.S.C. § 363 — one of the most critical first-day pleadings in bankruptcy.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case information** — debtor name, case number, chapter, court, judge
2. **Prepetition debt** — secured creditor(s), loan documents, amounts owed, collateral descriptions
3. **Cash collateral** — sources (accounts receivable, bank accounts, inventory proceeds, rents)
4. **Proposed budget** — 13-week cash flow projection with line-item detail
5. **Adequate protection** — what the debtor proposes to offer the secured creditor
6. **Prepetition liens** — UCC filings, mortgages, security agreements, intercreditor agreements
7. **Urgency** — why interim relief is needed before a final hearing

## Output Structure

### Introduction and Relief Requested

- Identify debtor, case, and chapter
- State that debtor seeks authority to use cash collateral under § 363(c)(2)
- Specify interim and final hearing relief
- Preview adequate protection package

### Background

- Debtor's business operations and why cash collateral is essential
- Nature and amount of prepetition secured debt
- Description of collateral, including cash collateral
- Secured creditor's lien position and priority
- Events leading to the bankruptcy filing

### Jurisdiction and Authority

- 28 U.S.C. §§ 157, 1334 (bankruptcy jurisdiction)
- 11 U.S.C. § 363(c)(2) (use of cash collateral with consent or court order)
- 11 U.S.C. § 363(e) (adequate protection)
- Bankruptcy Rule 4001(b) (procedural requirements)
- Local rules for cash collateral motions

### Cash Collateral Description

- Identify all cash and cash equivalents constituting cash collateral
- Trace to secured creditor's collateral package
- Bank accounts, deposit accounts, proceeds of inventory/receivables
- Rents and profits from real property (if applicable)

### Need for Cash Collateral

- Why debtor cannot operate without using cash collateral
- Immediate payroll, vendor, and operating obligations
- Consequences of not authorizing use (business shutdown, job losses, asset deterioration)
- No alternative financing available on better terms

### Proposed Budget

- 13-week cash flow projection as exhibit
- Line-item categories: payroll, rent, utilities, insurance, critical vendors, professional fees
- Variance reporting obligations (typically ±10-15%)
- Budget modification procedures

### Adequate Protection

Propose package based on facts (§ 363(e)):

- **Replacement liens** — on same priority on post-petition assets of the same kind
- **Superpriority claims** — § 507(b) administrative expense claim
- **Cash payments** — periodic interest or principal payments
- **Equity cushion** — value of collateral exceeds debt (if applicable)
- **Reporting** — financial reporting, budget variance reports, access to books and records
- **Insurance** — maintenance of insurance on collateral
- **Additional protections** — milestones, operating covenants, termination events

### Proposed Interim and Final Orders

**Interim order** (typically entered at first-day hearing):
- Authorization for limited period (usually 2-3 weeks until final hearing)
- Budget compliance requirements
- Adequate protection effective immediately
- Scheduling of final hearing

**Final order**:
- Extended authorization period
- Full adequate protection package
- Termination events and remedies
- Carve-out for professional fees (debtor's counsel, committee counsel, UST fees)
- Reservation of rights for all parties

### Carve-Out Provisions

- Professional fee carve-out (amount and structure)
- UST quarterly fees
- Clerk fees
- Committee professionals
- Triggers that reduce or eliminate carve-out

## Guidelines

- File as emergency/first-day motion with request for interim relief
- Comply with Bankruptcy Rule 4001(b)(1)(B) — highlight specified provisions
- Address any intercreditor agreement issues
- If multiple secured creditors, address relative priorities
- Budget must be realistic — inflated budgets destroy credibility
- Adequate protection must be meaningful — not just lip service
- Include a declaration from a debtor representative supporting the motion
- Proposed order should be a separate document
