# FERC Market-Based Rate Tariff

Drafts a complete, eTariff-ready FERC Market-Based Rate Tariff authorizing an entity to sell wholesale electric energy, capacity, and ancillary services at market-based rates under FERC jurisdiction.

## Prerequisites

1. **Seller identity** — full legal name, state of incorporation, principal place of business
2. **FERC identifiers** — docket number, MBR authorization order date, FERC-assigned codes
3. **Category status by region** — Category 1 or 2 designation for each FERC-defined market (Northeast, Southeast, Southwest, Central, Northwest, California)
4. **Authorization conditions** — MW caps, affiliate restrictions, behavioral/structural remedies, geographic limitations from the FERC order
5. **Asset inventory** — generation assets (capacity, fuel type, location), transmission assets, affiliate holdings
6. **Organized market participation** — ISO/RTO memberships (full legal entity names) if applicable

## Output Structure

### 1. Preamble
- Seller legal name, organizational details, all FERC-assigned identifiers
- Docket number and authorization order date; cite Order No. 697 as foundational authority
- Effective date: [DATE CERTAIN] or date of FERC acceptance, whichever is later
- Statement of wholesale sales authority and acknowledgment of FERC's ongoing oversight jurisdiction

### 2. Regulatory Compliance Commitments (18 CFR Part 35)

| Obligation | Trigger | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Maintain category status | Ongoing | Continuous |
| Change-in-status notification | Ownership, asset, or affiliate changes | 30 days |
| Horizontal market power screen compliance | Delivered price test + pivotal supplier analysis | Ongoing |
| Vertical market power compliance | Transmission/fuel control concerns | Ongoing |
| Affiliate codes of conduct | As applicable per authorization order | Per Commission order |
| Organized market participation | If required by authorization | Per RTO/ISO tariff |

### 3. Market Power Mitigation & Operational Constraints
- Generation capacity caps by market (specify MW limits per FERC order)
- Affiliate transaction restrictions (Standards of Conduct compliance)
- Behavioral remedies (price caps, if imposed) or structural remedies (divestitures, if imposed)
- Geographic scope: identify each authorized balancing authority area, ISO, or RTO by full legal name
- Cross-reference specific factual findings in authorization order supporting each limitation

- **Category 1**: rebuttable presumption of no horizontal market power (passed indicative screens)
- **Category 2**: case-specific showing required despite failing ≥1 screen; enhanced reporting and operational limitations apply

### 5. Ongoing Reporting Obligations

| Filing | System | Frequency / Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Market power analysis update | FERC eFiling | Triennial, or within 30 days of material status change |
| Electric Quarterly Report (EQR) | FERC EQR system | Within 30 days of each calendar quarter end |
| Change-in-status report | FERC eFiling | Within 30 days of triggering event |
| Asset appendix update | FERC eFiling | Upon acquisition or disposition of generation or transmission assets |

Triggering events for change-in-status reports: mergers, acquisitions, reorganizations, affiliate relationship changes, or asset transactions affecting market power analysis in any region.

*Format, timing, and content requirements governed by 18 CFR § 35.42.*

### 6. General Terms and Conditions
- **Rate-setting**: rates established via voluntary bilateral contracts or FERC-approved organized market participation; subject to filed rate doctrine and FERC authority to investigate unjust/unreasonable charges
- **Non-discrimination**: sales on non-discriminatory basis consistent with market-based rate authority
- **Dispute resolution**: FERC complaint procedures (18 CFR Part 206); contractual arbitration permitted to the extent consistent with Commission jurisdiction
- **Force majeure**: events beyond reasonable control; notice and mitigation obligations required
- **Liability**: limitations consistent with FERC precedent and industry practice
- **Creditworthiness**: seller may impose collateral requirements on purchasers
- **Termination/amendment**: contract termination procedures; tariff amendments via FERC eFiling

## Guidelines

- **eTariff formatting**: Section numbering must be hierarchical (1.0, 1.1, 1.1.1); include seller name and tariff designation in headers/footers on every page
- **Citations**: Use complete citations to FERC orders and 18 CFR Part 35; Bluebook or FERC citation conventions
- **Placeholders**: Flag all client-specific gaps with `[INSERT]` tags; deliver with a cover memo identifying each placeholder and any unusual conditions requiring client attention
- **Category 2 sellers**: Must include a case-specific market power showing; flag enhanced reporting requirements prominently throughout
- **Jurisdictional limits**: No provision may impermissibly restrict FERC's regulatory authority or create barriers to competitive market entry
- **Target length**: 12–25 pages depending on operational complexity and number of authorized markets
- **Verify before filing**: Order No. 697 (2007), Order No. 697-A (2008), Order No. 860 (2019) [VERIFY current amendment status]; 18 CFR § 35.42 [VERIFY current CFR text]; confirm no intervening Commission orders have modified applicable requirements
