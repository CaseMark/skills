# Forensic Accounting Investigation Report

Deliver a court-ready forensic accounting report that traces irregularities, quantifies impact, and documents evidentiary support.

## Prerequisites

1. Engagement scope and objectives from counsel or client.
2. Core financial records and data sources (see Data Sources table).
3. Case chronology and allegation summary.
4. Any privilege or confidentiality instructions.

## Output Structure / Process

Use this section order and include all required elements.

1. Title Page and Confidentiality
2. Executive Summary
3. Background and Objectives
4. Scope and Limitations
5. Methodology
6. Factual Findings
7. Analysis and Damages
8. Conclusions
9. Recommendations (if requested)
10. References, Standards, and Qualifications
11. Appendices and Exhibit Index

Title Page template:

```text
Forensic Accounting Investigation Report
Matter: [Matter Name]
Prepared for: [Engaging Party / Counsel]
Prepared by: [Name, Credentials, Firm]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Confidentiality: [e.g., Confidential - Attorney-Client Privileged / Work Product]
```

Executive Summary requirements:

- Time period examined
- Allegations or issues investigated
- Key findings with quantified amounts
- Parties or accounts implicated
- Bottom-line conclusion in neutral language

## Guidelines

- Separate facts from inferences; label estimates and ranges explicitly.
- Cite every material assertion to a specific exhibit or data source.
- Quantify impact using exact figures when possible; disclose rounding.
- Avoid legal conclusions; stay within accounting expertise.
- Disclose limitations and missing records that affect certainty.
- Maintain neutral tone; no advocacy language.
- If privileged or work-product framing is required, state it on the title page and in a footer.
- Use plain language for non-accountants; define technical terms in a glossary appendix.
- Flag any uncertain legal authority with `[VERIFY]`.
