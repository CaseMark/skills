# Security Agreement (Granting Lien)

Drafts an enforceable Security Agreement creating a valid first-priority security interest in specified collateral under UCC Article 9.

## Prerequisites

1. **Underlying loan documents** — loan agreement, promissory note, or credit facility with principal amount, interest rate, maturity date
2. **Party identification** — exact legal names as registered with Secretary of State, entity type, organizational jurisdiction, principal place of business
3. **Collateral details** — asset descriptions, serial numbers, locations, account numbers as applicable
4. **Corporate authority** — organizational documents confirming signatory authority to grant security interest
5. **Existing lien search** — UCC search results showing prior filings against debtor's collateral

## Output Structure

### 1. Preamble & Parties

| Element | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Debtor name | Exact legal name per Secretary of State records (Inc. vs. Incorporated matters for perfection) |
| Secured Party name | Full legal name, entity type, jurisdiction |
| Individual debtors | Include last 4 SSN or DL number for filing/searching |
| Entity debtors | Organizational ID number, jurisdiction of organization |
| Addresses | Principal place of business for each party |

### 2. Recitals

- Reference underlying obligation by date, type, and principal amount
- State voluntary grant of security interest
- Confirm UCC Article 9 governance in specified jurisdiction
- Establish consideration and intent (supports validity if challenged)

### 3. Grant of Security Interest

**Granting clause template:**
> Debtor hereby grants to Secured Party a continuing security interest in all of the Debtor's right, title, and interest in and to the Collateral described below.

**"Obligations" definition must include:**
- Current principal debt
- Accrued and future interest, fees, costs of collection, attorneys' fees
- Modifications, extensions, renewals, refinancings
- Future advances (if intended — note subordination risk with intervening lienholders)

### 4. Collateral Description

Must "reasonably identify" collateral per UCC § 9-108. Use UCC classifications plus specific identifiers:

| Collateral Type | Required Detail |
|---|---|
| Equipment | Make, model, serial number, location |
| Inventory | Type, location; explicitly state after-acquired if floating lien intended |
| Accounts receivable | Nature of accounts; include proceeds, collections, supporting obligations |
| Investment property | Specific securities, account numbers, issuing entities |
| General intangibles | IP registrations, contract rights, license agreements |

**Always include proceeds clause:**
> All proceeds, products, offspring, rents, profits, accessions, substitutions, and replacements of and to the Collateral, and all insurance proceeds and claims relating thereto.

**After-acquired property** (if intended): "whether now owned or hereafter acquired by Debtor."

- Avoid "all personal property" unless genuinely a blanket lien; even then, supplement with specific categories
- Description must work for both the agreement and UCC-1 financing statement

### 5. Representations & Warranties

**General:**
- [ ] Good and marketable title, free of liens except scheduled permitted liens
- [ ] Duly organized, validly existing, in good standing
- [ ] Full power and authority to execute and grant security interest
- [ ] No violation of other agreements, laws, regulations, or court orders
- [ ] Legal name, org ID, and jurisdiction of organization are accurate (affects UCC filing adequacy)

**Collateral-specific:**
- Equipment: condition, operability, regulatory compliance
- Accounts: genuine, enforceable, no defenses/offsets/counterclaims, arising from bona fide transactions

### 6. Covenants

**Affirmative:**
- Maintain collateral in good condition and repair
- Insure against casualty loss; name Secured Party as loss payee / additional insured
- Pay taxes and assessments when due
- Maintain legal existence and good standing
- Deliver periodic financial statements and collateral reports
- Comply with applicable laws

**Negative:**
- No sale, transfer, lease, or further encumbrance without prior written consent
- No change to legal name, org structure, or jurisdiction without advance notice
- No relocation of collateral outside specified boundaries without consent
- No action diminishing collateral value

**Tailor to collateral type:** inventory → minimum levels + regular reports; equipment → maintenance schedules + usage restrictions.

### 7. Events of Default

| Default Type | Cure Period |
|---|---|
| Payment default | None (immediate) |
| Breach of representation/warranty | None (immediate) |
| Covenant breach (technical) | 10–30 days after notice |
| Bankruptcy/insolvency/receivership | None (immediate) |
| Cross-default (loan agreement or material third-party agreements) | Per underlying agreement |
| Material adverse change in financial condition | None |
| Judgment exceeding threshold amount | Per negotiated threshold |
| Loss, theft, or material damage to collateral | None |

### 8. Remedies

Upon default, Secured Party may:

1. **Accelerate** — declare all obligations immediately due and payable
2. **Possess** — take possession by self-help (without breach of peace) or judicial process; enter debtor's premises; require debtor to assemble collateral at convenient location
3. **Dispose** — sell at public or private sale, commercially reasonable manner per UCC Article 9; Secured Party may credit bid at public sale (private sale only if customarily sold on recognized market)
4. **Collect** — collect accounts directly from account debtors; notify account debtors to pay Secured Party

**Application of proceeds (in order):**
1. Reasonable expenses of collection/disposition (including attorneys' fees)
2. Satisfaction of secured obligations
3. Surplus to debtor or entitled parties

- Debtor liable for any deficiency
- Remedies are cumulative; exercise of one does not waive others

### 9. Governing Law & Administrative Provisions

| Provision | Content |
|---|---|
| Governing law (contract) | Party-selected state law |
| Governing law (security interest) | UCC as adopted in debtor's location state (governs perfection and priority) |
| Jurisdiction/venue | Exclusive jurisdiction in designated state and federal courts; debtor consents to personal jurisdiction, waives inconvenient forum |
| Jury waiver | Include for commercial transactions between sophisticated parties |
| Severability | Invalid provisions severed; remainder enforceable |
| Amendments | Written and signed by both parties only; no oral modifications |
| Notices | Written; specify delivery methods and designated addresses |
| Waiver | No waiver by delay; no partial exercise precludes further exercise |
| Assignment | Debtor prohibited without consent; Secured Party may freely assign |
| Entire agreement | Merger clause covering agreement + exhibits + underlying loan documents |
| Counterparts | Multiple counterparts; electronic signatures if appropriate |
| Further assurances | Debtor must execute additional documents for perfection (UCC-1s, amendments, continuations) |

### 10. Execution

| Party Type | Signature Requirements |
|---|---|
| Corporation | Authorized officer with title |
| LLC | Manager or authorized member per operating agreement |
| Partnership | General partner with binding authority |
| Individual | Simple signature; consider spousal signature if marital property rights apply |
| Notarization | Not required for pure personal property; **required** if collateral includes fixtures or agreement filed in real property records |

## Guidelines

- Verify debtor's legal name against Secretary of State records before finalizing — minor variations defeat perfection priority
- Cross-check all defined terms, cross-references, and party names for internal consistency
- Confirm collateral description matches any schedules/exhibits and underlying term sheet
- Ensure description is sufficient for both the security agreement and UCC-1 financing statement
- Flag any gaps in critical information (debtor legal name, collateral specifics, obligation terms) before drafting
- Review against term sheet or commitment letter to confirm all negotiated terms are incorporated
- All UCC citations reference the uniform version; confirm adoption specifics in the applicable state
