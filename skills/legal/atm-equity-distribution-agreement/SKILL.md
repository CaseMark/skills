# ATM Equity Distribution Agreement

Drafts a senior-level, market-standard Equity Distribution Agreement for an At-The-Market offering program compliant with Rule 415(a)(4), Regulation M, and applicable exchange listing rules.

## Prerequisites

1. **Issuer information** — exact legal name, state of incorporation, principal place of business
2. **Registration statement** — Form S-3 file number, effective date, maximum aggregate offering amount available
3. **Securities** — class, par value, proposed ATM program maximum (must not exceed shelf availability)
4. **Placement agent(s)** — full legal name(s), FINRA registration status, negotiated commission rate (market: 2–3%)
5. **Exchange** — principal listing market (NYSE / Nasdaq) and any 20%-cap shareholder approval status
6. **Existing arrangements** — prior ATM agreements, lock-ups, or co-manager arrangements

## Document Structure

| Section | Key Contents |
|---|---|
| Parties & Recitals | Full party names; shelf registration citation (form, file no., effective date); Rule 415(a)(4) characterization |
| Definitions | See table below |
| Agency Appointment | Non-exclusive; commercially reasonable efforts; not an underwriter; sub-agent consent requirements |
| Sale Mechanics | Placement notice process; volume caps; daily confirmations; T+2 settlement; DTC delivery |
| Compensation | Commission % of gross proceeds; expense allocation; wire instructions |
| Representations & Warranties | See checklist below |
| Issuer Covenants | See checklist below |
| Indemnification & Contribution | Mutual indemnification; defense procedures; contribution cap = commissions received |
| Conditions Precedent | Initial closing deliverables; ongoing per-placement conditions |
| Termination | Mutual notice termination; immediate termination triggers; automatic termination events |
| General Provisions | NY or DE governing law; jury waiver; notices; integration; no assignment; severability; counterparts; confidentiality; force majeure |

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition Standard |
|---|---|
| **Shares** | Class + par value + maximum aggregate offering amount ($) |
| **Placement Notice** | Written notice specifying: max shares, floor price, sales period |
| **Floor Price** | Minimum per-share price; agent may not sell below without issuer instruction |
| **Commission** | % of gross sales proceeds per transaction (2–3% market range) |
| **Applicable Time** | Time of each sale (disclosure adequacy measured at this moment) |
| **Trading Day** | Day exchange is open per principal market rules |
| **VWAP** | Volume-weighted average price with specified calculation methodology |
| **Issuance Cap** | Exchange rule limit — NYSE/Nasdaq generally require shareholder approval for issuances exceeding 20% of outstanding shares at below-market prices [VERIFY current exchange rules] |
| **Suspension Notice / Period** | Mechanism for issuer to halt sales (MNPI, blackout, registration gap) |
| **Time of Sale Information** | Full disclosure package available to investors at time of each sale |

## Sale Mechanics

**Placement Notice Process**
- Issuer delivers written notice → agent uses commercially reasonable efforts to sell within specified parameters
- Notice must specify: max shares, floor price, sales period
- Agent provides daily trade confirmations by agreed cutoff time: shares sold, gross proceeds, net proceeds after commission, VWAP

**Volume Limitations**
- Daily cap: typically ≤ 25% of average daily trading volume over trailing reference period
- Program cap: total shares/dollars may not exceed shelf registration availability; track against exchange listing limits

**Settlement**
- T+2 via DTC; shares delivered through issuer's transfer agent to DTC for agent's account

**Mandatory Suspension Triggers**
- Issuer possesses MNPI not yet publicly disclosed
- Quarterly earnings blackout periods
- Registration statement not effective or stop order in effect or threatened
- Exchange trading suspension or regulatory halt

## Representations & Warranties Checklist

**Corporate**
- [ ] Duly organized, validly existing, in good standing
- [ ] Full corporate power and authority; all necessary corporate action taken
- [ ] Agreement is valid, binding, enforceable (subject to bankruptcy/insolvency/equity carve-outs)
- [ ] No conflicts with charter, bylaws, material agreements, or applicable law
- [ ] No required consents or approvals outstanding

**Registration Statement & Disclosure**
- [ ] Registration statement effective; no stop order issued or threatened; no proceedings pending
- [ ] Registration statement, prospectus, and supplements comply with Securities Act and rules thereunder
- [ ] No material misstatement or omission as of each applicable time (standard 10b-5 language)
- [ ] All required Exchange Act filings current and timely made

**Capitalization**
- [ ] Authorized and outstanding shares (all classes) stated accurately
- [ ] Shares, when issued and paid for per agreement, will be validly issued, fully paid, non-assessable
- [ ] No preemptive rights, co-sale rights, or rights of first refusal (or all waived/complied with)
- [ ] Options, warrants, convertibles disclosed with dilutive impact noted

**Financial**
- [ ] Financial statements present fairly in all material respects; prepared on consistent GAAP basis; comply with applicable SEC accounting requirements
- [ ] No material adverse change since most recent financials (except as disclosed in prospectus)

**Compliance**
- [ ] All material permits, licenses, and authorizations held
- [ ] Compliance with all applicable laws except where non-compliance would not have a material adverse effect

**Litigation**
- [ ] No undisclosed pending or threatened proceedings required to be disclosed or that could reasonably be expected to have a material adverse effect

## Issuer Covenants Checklist

**Registration Maintenance**
- [ ] Use best efforts to maintain registration statement effectiveness throughout term
- [ ] Provide agent advance copies of all proposed amendments/supplements before filing
- [ ] Not file any amendment to which agent reasonably objects on legal compliance or disclosure grounds

**Reporting & Disclosure**
- [ ] Furnish agent all Exchange Act filings, earnings releases, and material shareholder communications
- [ ] Timely file all required Exchange Act reports; maintain exchange listing; file additional-share listing applications

**Notification Obligations**
- [ ] Immediately notify agent of any event causing prospectus to contain misstatement or omission → promptly file supplement or amendment
- [ ] Notify agent of: earnings releases, material contracts, management or board changes, regulatory inquiries, material litigation, any other event constituting MNPI

**Blackout Periods**
- [ ] Suspend sales when possessing undisclosed MNPI
- [ ] Suspend during standard quarterly earnings blackout windows
- [ ] Specify suspension notice form, timing, and resumption procedures

**Due Diligence**
- [ ] Reasonable access to management, facilities, and books and records for agent due diligence

**Ongoing Deliverables** (at program launch and quarterly / on agent request)
- [ ] Officer's certificate (CEO + CFO): reps & warranties true; covenants performed; no stop order; no MAC
- [ ] Issuer counsel opinion: corporate authority, valid issuance, no conflicts, securities law compliance
- [ ] Issuer counsel negative assurance letter: registration statement / prospectus disclosure
- [ ] Auditor comfort letter: financial statements and selected financial data in customary form

## Indemnification & Contribution

**Issuer → Agent Indemnification**
- Scope: losses, claims, damages, liabilities, and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) arising from any misstatement or omission in the registration statement, prospectus, supplements, or issuer free writing prospectuses
- Exception: losses arising from agent-furnished written information included in offering documents

**Agent → Issuer Indemnification**
- Scope: losses arising from agent-furnished written information (typically narrow)

**Defense Procedures**
- Indemnified party must give prompt written notice; delay excuses indemnitor only if materially prejudicial
- Indemnifying party may assume defense with counsel reasonably satisfactory to indemnified party
- Indemnified party may participate at own expense; at indemnifying party's expense if actual or potential conflict of interest
- No settlement without indemnified party's consent unless settlement includes unconditional release and no admission of fault or wrongdoing
- No indemnified party settlement without indemnifying party's consent where indemnification is sought

**Contribution**
- Available when indemnification is unavailable (policy, nature of liability, or otherwise)
- Allocated by: relative benefits received (issuer proceeds vs. agent commissions) + relative fault + equitable considerations
- **Agent contribution cap = total commissions actually received under the agreement**

## Conditions Precedent

**Initial Closing**
- Fully executed agreement
- Issuer counsel opinion (authority, valid issuance, no conflicts, securities law compliance)
- Issuer counsel negative assurance letter (disclosure)
- Auditor comfort letter (customary form)
- Officer's certificate (CEO + CFO)
- Exchange listing approval for program shares (subject to official notice of issuance)
- Confirmation: registration statement effective, no stop order issued or threatened

**Each Placement Notice / Settlement Date**
- All reps and warranties true and correct in all material respects as of notice date and each settlement date
- All covenants performed; all conditions satisfied
- No stop order issued or threatened; registration statement current and effective
- No MAC since most recent financials included in prospectus
- Shares not suspended or delisted; no suspension or delisting threatened
- Updated comfort letters and opinions as required (quarterly minimum or on agent request)
- Any additional certificates or documents reasonably requested by agent

## Termination

**Mutual Notice Termination**
- Either party: written notice; effective 5–10 business days after delivery (allows wind-down of pending transactions)

**Immediate Termination Triggers (Agent)**
- Trading in shares suspended or materially limited; exchange-wide suspension
- Stop order issued or SEC proceeding initiated
- Material adverse change in issuer's business or financial condition
- Material breach of issuer representation, warranty, or covenant
- Material adverse change in national or international financial markets making offering inadvisable or impracticable

**Automatic Termination Events**
- All registered shares sold; shelf registration availability exhausted
- Registration statement expires or terminates
- Shares delisted from principal exchange
- Bankruptcy petition filed by or against issuer

**Post-Termination Obligations**
- Complete settlement and pay commissions on all transactions executed prior to termination
- Final accounting and reconciliation of all transactions, commissions, and expenses
- Survival: indemnification/contribution (indefinitely or applicable statute of limitations); confidentiality
- Return or destruction of confidential information; agent ceases use of issuer name and trademarks

## Guidelines

- **Rule 415(a)(4)**: All sales must qualify as at-the-market offerings into an existing trading market at other than a fixed price; verify this characterization is preserved throughout, including any block transaction provisions
- **Regulation M**: Address restrictions on agent's market activities during distribution periods; confirm agent's compliance obligations [VERIFY applicable subrules with current SEC guidance]
- **Exchange 20% Cap**: Confirm issuer's shareholder approval status for issuances potentially exceeding 20% of outstanding shares at below-market prices (NYSE Listed Company Manual § 312 / Nasdaq Rule 5635) [VERIFY current rule text]
- **Form S-3 Eligibility**: Confirm issuer meets public float (generally ≥ $75M) and reporting history requirements; Baby Shelf Rule limits primary offerings for issuers with public float < $75M to 1/3 of public float in any 12-month period [VERIFY current SEC rules]
- **No Underwriter Characterization**: Agent acts on commercially reasonable efforts basis, not as principal purchaser; preserve this throughout to avoid Securities Act § 11 underwriter liability exposure
- **Shelf Tracking**: Build in mechanism for issuer and agent to track shares sold against program maximum and remaining shelf availability
- **FINRA**: Confirm agent is registered broker-dealer; filing requirements under FINRA Rule 5110 may apply to ATM programs [VERIFY]
- **Governing Law**: Default to New York; consider Delaware if issuer preference or where charter-related issues are central
- **Survival**: Expressly state that indemnification survives termination; specify period (statute of limitations recommended)
- **Concurrent Programs**: If issuer has multiple ATM agreements or other equity compensation programs, track aggregate issuances against exchange caps
