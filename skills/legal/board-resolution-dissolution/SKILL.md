# Board Resolution to Dissolve Corporation

Drafts a unanimous written consent of the Board of Directors authorizing voluntary dissolution, preceding shareholder approval and Secretary of State filings.

## Prerequisites

1. **Corporation details** — exact legal name per articles of incorporation, state of incorporation, date of formation
2. **Director roster** — full names of all current directors (unanimous consent requires all)
3. **Effective date** — intended date for the consent action
4. **Plan of Liquidation** — draft or outline of the Plan of Complete Liquidation and Dissolution (attached as exhibit)
5. **Shareholder approval method** — special meeting vs. written consent; applicable voting thresholds; multi-class stock voting requirements
6. **State statute** — dissolution and written consent provisions for the state of incorporation (e.g., DGCL §§141(f), 275 for Delaware)

## Output Structure

### 1. Header Block
- Document title: "ACTION BY UNANIMOUS WRITTEN CONSENT OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS OF [CORPORATION NAME]"
- State of incorporation, effective date of consent
- Statement that written consent is authorized in lieu of a meeting under [State] [Statute §___] [VERIFY]

### 2. Preamble
- Identifies all directors by name; confirms they constitute the full board
- Affirms this consent has the same force and effect as a unanimous vote at a duly called meeting

### 3. Core Resolutions (each beginning with "RESOLVED,")

| # | Resolution | Key Language Points |
|---|-----------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Dissolution Advisability** | Board has determined dissolution is advisable and in the best interests of the corporation and its shareholders |
| 2 | **Adopt Plan of Liquidation** | Approves Plan of Complete Liquidation and Dissolution in the form attached as Exhibit A; incorporated by reference |
| 3 | **Submit to Shareholders** | Directs submission to shareholders for approval; specifies method (special meeting or written consent); references applicable vote threshold |
| 4 | **Officer Authority – Shareholder Approval** | Authorizes officers to call meetings, prepare/distribute notices and proxy/consent materials, engage counsel and advisors |
| 5 | **Officer Authority – Post-Approval Filing** | Conditioned on shareholder approval: authorizes filing Certificate/Articles of Dissolution with Secretary of State; wind-up actions |
| 6 | **Wind-Up Authorizations** | Satisfy/provision all known liabilities; liquidate assets per Plan; distribute remaining assets per shareholder rights/preferences; file final tax returns and regulatory filings |
| 7 | **General Authority** | Omnibus authority to execute all documents, certificates, instruments, and agreements necessary to effectuate dissolution |
| 8 | **Ratification** | Ratifies and approves all prior actions taken by officers in furtherance of dissolution |

### 4. Execution Block
- Counterparts clause (directors may execute separate identical copies)
- Individual signature blocks per director: signature line, printed name, "Director" title, date
- Statement of effectiveness (upon execution by all directors, or specified date)
- Optional: corporate secretary attestation block for minute book filing

## Guidelines

- **Unanimous consent** — all seated directors must sign; confirm no vacancies exist
- **Statute citation** — replace placeholder with current state code section; verify DGCL §141(f) equivalent for non-Delaware entities [VERIFY jurisdiction-specific]
- **Multi-class stock** — if corporation has preferred or multiple share classes, confirm whether class voting is required for dissolution approval
- **Contractual restrictions** — flag any credit agreements, material contracts, or regulatory licenses that condition or restrict dissolution; may require third-party consents before filing
- **Tax elections** — resolution does not substitute for IRC §331/332 liquidation tax analysis; note that final federal and state tax returns are required
- **Exhibit A consistency** — Plan of Liquidation terms must align with officer authorizations granted in the resolutions; avoid conflicts
- **Do not include** reasons for dissolution in operative resolutions unless required by state law — business judgment is presumed
- **Format** — formal legal document style; paginate; include document title and date in footer; suitable for corporate minute book
