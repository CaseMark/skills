# Directed Share Program

Drafts a legally compliant DSP agreement governing share allocation, purchase, and settlement for designated participants in a public offering.

## Prerequisites

1. **Issuer information** — exact legal name, state of incorporation, SEC registration statement form type and file number
2. **Offering details** — type of securities, aggregate offering size, anticipated pricing range or public offering price
3. **DSP parameters** — number or percentage of shares allocated to DSP, per-participant limits
4. **Underwriter information** — names and roles of lead and co-underwriters; designated broker-dealer (if any)
5. **Participant categories** — eligible classes (employees, directors, business associates, family members, etc.)
6. **Lock-up terms** — duration, scope, permitted exceptions
7. **Timing** — anticipated pricing date, settlement timeline

If any of the above are unavailable, identify the gaps and request them before drafting.

## Output Structure

### 1. Introduction & Disclaimers

| Element | Required Content |
|---|---|
| Issuer identification | Full legal name, offering description, aggregate size |
| Program purpose | Opportunity to purchase at public offering price via underwriter allocation |
| No-guarantee disclaimer | Allocations not guaranteed; subject to company/underwriter discretion |
| Modification rights | Program may be modified or terminated prior to pricing |
| Prospectus primacy | Shares offered only pursuant to final prospectus; EDGAR reference |

### 2. Eligibility

- **Eligible categories**: current/former employees, officers, directors, subsidiaries; bona fide business associates and service providers; family members of eligible persons
- **Invitation-only**: participation strictly by invitation; company retains sole discretion over invitations and allocations
- **Ineligible persons**: regulatory sanction subjects; residents of jurisdictions where offering cannot legally be made; persons required to register as broker-dealers to participate
- **Non-retaliation**: employment/service relationship not contingent on participation decision

### 3. Allocation Methodology

| Topic | Provisions |
|---|---|
| Indication of interest | Format, required information, submission deadline |
| DSP pool size | Shares available (number or % of total); may be increased/decreased |
| Per-participant cap | Maximum shares or dollar amount per participant |
| Allocation factors | DSP demand level, primary offering demand, participant relationship, regulatory considerations |
| Oversubscription | Pro-rata reduction, tiered allocation, or full discretion — specify method |
| No entitlement | Participants have no contractual, legal, or equitable right to any allocation |

### 4. Pricing, Payment & Settlement

**Pricing**
- All DSP shares offered at public offering price; no discount, commission rebate, or preferential pricing
- State whether participants pay underwriting discounts/commissions and applicable rate

**Payment**
- Acceptable methods: wire transfer (immediately available funds) or certified/cashier's check
- Payment deadline: [X] business days following allocation notification
- Consequences of non-payment: allocation canceled, shares reallocated, participant liable for resulting losses

**Settlement**
- Delivery timeline following offering close
- Book-entry form via Depository Trust Company (DTC)
- Brokerage account designation process
- Fractional share treatment (round up / round down / cash)
- Excess payment refund procedure

### 5. Underwriting Relationship & Registration

- DSP conducted pursuant to underwriting agreement; DSP shares part of underwriters' firm commitment allocation
- Underwriters have no obligation to allocate specific shares; retain discretion to modify or terminate
- Registration statement reference: form type, file number, filing date
- Prospectus access: EDGAR / underwriter distribution
- Designated broker-dealer role (if applicable): account opening, order submission, settlement
- Compliance confirmation: FINRA Rule 5130 (restrictions on purchase/sale of initial equity public offerings); Regulation M [VERIFY applicability to specific participant categories]

### 6. Risk Disclosures

- Prominent directive to review "Risk Factors" in prospectus
- Highlight: no prior public market, price volatility risk, possible decline below offering price
- Lock-up agreement terms:
  - Duration: [X] days from effective date
  - Scope: sales, pledges, hedges, and derivative transactions
  - Permitted exceptions: estate planning transfers to family/trusts (with conditions)
- Affiliate/control person resale: Rule 144 restrictions continue post-lock-up [VERIFY current Rule 144 volume and manner-of-sale limitations]
- Forward-looking statements disclaimer
- No investment, tax, or legal advice; recommend independent advisor consultation
- Regulation M anti-manipulation compliance confirmation; participants prohibited from manipulative practices

### 7. Indemnification & Liability

**Participant indemnification of company/underwriters** — covers:
- Claims arising from participant's DSP participation
- Breach of participant representations or obligations
- Participant securities law violations
- Unauthorized resale or transfer

**Participant acknowledgments:**
- Independent investment decision based solely on prospectus review
- Company/underwriters not acting as fiduciaries or advisors
- Opportunity to ask questions and obtain additional information

**Liability limitations:**
- No consequential, indirect, incidental, punitive, or special damages
- Maximum liability capped at amount paid by participant for shares
- Participant solely responsible for securities law compliance on purchase and resale

### 8. Execution & Governing Terms

**Participant acknowledgments checklist:**
- [ ] Received and reviewed prospectus including risk factors
- [ ] Understands DSP terms, restrictions, and limitations
- [ ] Agrees to comply with lock-up and all applicable requirements
- [ ] Acknowledges investment risks and accepts responsibility
- [ ] Consulted own advisors or knowingly elected not to

**Signature blocks:** participant; authorized company representative; underwriter representative (if applicable)

**Boilerplate provisions:**

| Provision | Standard Position |
|---|---|
| Governing law | State of issuer incorporation |
| Exclusive jurisdiction | Specify court system or binding arbitration |
| Amendment | Written consent of company and underwriters; notice to participants |
| Waiver | No waiver constitutes continuing waiver |
| Severability | Invalid provisions severed; remainder continues |
| Entire agreement | DSP documentation supersedes all prior understandings |

### 9. Exhibits

- **Exhibit A** — Indication of Interest Form (participant info, shares requested)
- **Exhibit B** — Acknowledgment and Agreement Form
- **Exhibit C** — Lock-Up Agreement
- **Exhibit D** — Payment and Account Setup Instructions

## Guidelines

- All DSP statements must be accurate and non-misleading; document may be subject to SEC review and Securities Act §11 / Exchange Act §10(b) liability [VERIFY]
- Cross-reference prospectus for detailed disclosures; DSP must also stand alone as a binding agreement
- Verify FINRA Rule 5130 eligibility for each participant category before finalizing eligibility provisions [VERIFY]
- Confirm applicable state blue sky law compliance for jurisdictions where participants are located
- Use defined terms consistently; capitalize upon first use
- Include table of contents if document exceeds ten pages
- Deliver with cover memo: key terms summary, provisions requiring negotiation, information gaps, open legal/regulatory issues
