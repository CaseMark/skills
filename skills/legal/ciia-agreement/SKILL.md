# Confidentiality and Invention Assignment Agreement (CIIA)

Drafts an enforceable CIIA that protects company IP and trade secrets while complying with state invention assignment statutes and federal trade secret law.

## Prerequisites

1. **Company information** — legal entity name, state of incorporation, principal address
2. **Employee/contractor information** — full legal name, residential address, role, start date
3. **Governing jurisdiction** — state law governing enforceability (affects invention carve-outs and restrictive covenants)
4. **Company IP profile** — nature of proprietary information, R&D activities, industry sector
5. **Existing agreements** — any prior confidentiality, employment, or equity agreements to reconcile
6. **Restrictive covenant preferences** — whether to include non-solicitation/non-compete (with awareness of jurisdiction limits)

## Pre-Drafting Research

Before drafting, research in uploaded documents for:

| Look for | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Existing NDAs or CIIAs | Match company style, identify gaps |
| Corporate formation docs | Verify exact legal entity name |
| Employee offer letter | Reconcile compensation, role scope |
| Industry/competitive info | Calibrate scope of restrictions |
| Prior litigation on similar agreements | Avoid known weaknesses |

Identify the governing jurisdiction and research:
- Invention assignment limitation statutes (see table below)
- Restrictive covenant enforceability standards
- Additional consideration requirements for post-hire agreements

## Document Structure

### 1. Preamble and Parties

- Full legal entity name (from formation docs), entity type, principal address
- Employee full legal name, residential address
- Effective date (establishes obligation start and time-based calculation reference)
- If contractor: adjust terminology throughout; note some jurisdictions apply different enforceability standards

### 2. Recitals

Frame business justification for restrictions — employee access to confidential information, potential IP creation, legitimate business interests. Incorporate company-specific facts (R&D investment, competitive threats) from uploaded documents.

### 3. Confidential Information

**Definition** — all information (written, oral, electronic, visual) not generally known to the public that provides competitive advantage. Enumerate:

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Technical/trade secrets | Formulas, algorithms, source code, processes, methods |
| Business | Strategic plans, pricing, financial projections, customer/supplier lists |
| Product/service | Specs, designs, roadmaps, R&D activities |
| Personnel | Compensation data, org structures |
| Relationships | Customer/supplier identity, deal terms |

**Exclusions** (standard trade secret law carve-outs):
- Publicly available (not through employee breach)
- Rightfully possessed prior to disclosure (documented)
- Received from third party without confidentiality breach
- Independently developed without use of company information (documented)

**Obligations**:
- Strict confidence; use solely for company duties
- No disclosure without written authorization
- Reasonable care standard (no less than care for own confidential info)
- No copying, removal, reverse engineering except as required for duties

**Duration**:
- Trade secrets: indefinite (as long as secrecy maintained)
- Non-trade-secret confidential info: 3–5 years post-termination (calibrate to industry lifecycle)

**Return/destruction**: all materials in any form returned immediately upon termination or request; written certification of compliance required. For remote/BYOD workers: add specific deletion provisions for personal devices, cloud accounts, personal email.

### 4. Permitted Disclosures

**DTSA Whistleblower Immunity Notice** (required for agreements entered/updated after May 11, 2016):

> An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or state trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is made (i) in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney, and solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (ii) in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.

Also state: nothing prohibits reporting potential law violations to government agencies or cooperating with government investigations.

### 5. Invention Assignment

**Definition of "Inventions"**: all ideas, inventions, discoveries, improvements, original works, designs, processes, software, algorithms, know-how — whether or not patentable/copyrightable, whether alone or jointly, during employment.

**Assignment clause**:
- Use **present assignment** language ("hereby assigns") for immediate vesting
- Include **backup "agrees to assign"** for jurisdictions not recognizing present assignment of future rights
- Scope: inventions that (a) relate to company's current or anticipated business/R&D, (b) result from use of company resources/time/trade secrets, or (c) relate to assigned work

**Jurisdiction-Specific Invention Carve-Outs**:

| State | Statute | Key Limitation |
|---|---|---|
| California | Labor Code §2870–2872 | Cannot require assignment of inventions developed entirely on own time without company resources, unless related to company business or resulting from company work. **Must attach full §2870 text as exhibit.** |
| Delaware | 19 Del. C. §805 | Similar own-time/own-resources carve-out |
| Illinois | 765 ILCS 1060/2 | Employee retains rights to inventions made entirely on own time without company resources |
| Kansas | K.S.A. 44-130 | Own-time/own-resources carve-out |
| Minnesota | Minn. Stat. §181.78 | Own-time/own-resources; must notify employee of statute |
| North Carolina | N.C.G.S. §66-57.1 | Own-time/own-resources carve-out |
| Utah | Utah Code §34-39-3 | Employment inventions act limitations |
| Washington | RCW 49.44.140 | Own-time/own-resources carve-out |

[VERIFY] — confirm current statutory citations for each state at time of drafting.

For multi-state employers: include carve-outs for all applicable jurisdictions or create state-specific addenda.

**Prior Invention Disclosure (Exhibit A)**:
- Employee lists all pre-existing inventions related to company business
- Checkbox: "No prior inventions to disclose"
- If none listed/checked: representation that no prior inventions exist

**Disclosure Obligation**: employee must promptly disclose in writing all inventions conceived during employment, regardless of believed assignability. Company reviews and confirms ownership determination in writing.

**Cooperation**: employee assists with IP filings, prosecution, enforcement at company expense. Post-termination: reasonable compensation for time plus expense reimbursement.

**Power of Attorney**: irrevocable, coupled with interest, to execute IP documents if employee unavailable/refuses. Limited to documents for already-assigned IP.

**Works of Authorship**:
- Designate as "works made for hire" under Copyright Act
- Backup assignment of all rights if work-for-hire doctrine doesn't apply
- Moral rights waiver to extent permitted by law

### 6. Restrictive Covenants

**Threshold decision**: evaluate jurisdiction before including.

| Covenant Type | Typical Duration | Key Considerations |
|---|---|---|
| Employee non-solicitation | 12 months | Limit to employees with whom departing employee worked/supervised/had confidential info about |
| Customer non-solicitation | 12–24 months | Limit to customers during final 12–24 months with whom employee had material contact; competitive activities only |
| Non-competition | 6–12 months | Most scrutinized; must be narrow in scope, duration, geography |

**Jurisdiction bars on non-competes**: California, North Dakota, Oklahoma — do not include non-compete (narrow exceptions for business sale/partnership dissolution). Many states increasingly restricting; check current law.

**Required elements for enforceability**:
- Reasonable scope, duration, geographic reach
- Protects legitimate business interest
- Adequate consideration (some states require independent consideration for post-hire agreements)
- Reformation/blue-pencil clause (but draft reasonable from outset — some jurisdictions void rather than reform)
- Tolling provision for breach period (where permitted)

**Define key terms**: "solicit" includes direct/indirect; "competitive" defined by reference to company's actual products/services; "customers" defined by recency and employee contact.

### 7. General Provisions

Include standard provisions:

| Provision | Key Points |
|---|---|
| Governing law | Specified state, without conflict of laws principles; verify enforceability of choice-of-law in employment context |
| Forum selection / arbitration | Exclusive venue; if arbitration: specify rules (e.g., AAA Employment Rules), location, binding nature |
| Entire agreement | Supersedes prior confidentiality/IP agreements; carve out offer letter, handbook, equity agreements |
| Severability | Invalid provisions modified to minimum extent or severed; remainder survives |
| Amendment | Written, signed by both parties; no waiver by course of dealing |
| Assignment | Company may assign to successors; employee may not assign |
| Notice | Writing required; personal delivery, email, overnight courier, certified mail |
| Survival | Confidentiality, IP assignment, restrictive covenants survive termination |
| Counterparts | Electronic signatures valid |

### 8. Acknowledgments

Employee acknowledges:
- Read and understood entire agreement; adequate time to consider
- Opportunity to consult independent legal counsel
- Voluntary execution without duress
- Restrictions reasonable and necessary; adequate consideration received
- Breach would cause irreparable harm; company entitled to injunctive relief
- Receipt of DTSA immunity notice
- (California) Receipt of Labor Code §2870 copy

### 9. Signature Blocks and Exhibits

- **Employee**: signature, printed name, date
- **Company**: signature, printed name, title (officer-level authority required), date
- **Exhibit A**: Prior Inventions Disclosure form
- **Exhibit B** (California): Full text of Labor Code §2870

## Guidelines

- **Jurisdiction first**: always identify governing jurisdiction before drafting; it controls invention carve-outs, restrictive covenant enforceability, and consideration requirements
- **No non-competes in CA/ND/OK** unless narrow statutory exception applies
- **DTSA notice is mandatory** for agreements entered/updated after May 11, 2016
- **California §2870 exhibit is mandatory** — not optional for CA-governed agreements
- **Present + backup assignment language** — use both "hereby assigns" and "agrees to assign" for maximum enforceability
- **Calibrate restrictions to role** — senior executives may support broader covenants; junior employees require narrower scope
- **Additional consideration**: if agreement is post-hire, verify whether jurisdiction requires independent consideration beyond continued employment
- **Mark uncertain citations** with [VERIFY] and flag for attorney review
- **Reconcile with existing agreements** — explicitly state whether this supersedes or supplements prior NDAs/CIIAs
- **Professional formatting**: consistent headings, numbered sections, page numbers, signature blocks on page with substantive text
