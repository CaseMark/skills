# Invasive Testing Consent Letter (Phase II)

Issues a limited, conditional consent letter that authorizes narrowly defined invasive environmental testing under an existing agreement.

## Prerequisites

1. Executed main agreement and all amendments with notice and consent mechanics.
2. Recipient’s written request and current work plan, H&S plan, and restoration plan.
3. Site plan/survey with proposed locations and utility information.
4. Contractor/consultant identities, qualifications, and licenses if required.
5. Insurance certificates and endorsements required by the main agreement.
6. Proposed schedule and restoration security (bond/LOC/escrow) terms.

## Output Structure / Process

Inputs Checklist:

| Item | Required | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Agreement name/date/parties | Yes | Use defined terms and notice addresses. |
| Property description | Yes | Address or legal description. |
| Authorized work plan | Yes | Convert into hard limits. |
| Exhibit A locations | Yes | Coordinates or fixed references. |
| Work window and hours | Yes | Tie to agreement. |
| Insurance and security | Yes | Condition precedent to entry. |
| Reporting/data control | Yes | Include consultation period. |

Drafting Steps:

1. Identify the main agreement, parties, property, and defined terms; incorporate by reference and state no amendment except as expressly stated.
2. Grant limited, conditional consent for “Authorized Work” only; align revocation/suspension with the main agreement.
3. Define scope with numeric caps, methods, depths/diameters, and prohibited activities.
4. Fix locations with Exhibit A; define relocation radius and no-go zones.
5. Set timing, notice, access logistics, and site conduct rules.
6. Allocate reporting/data ownership, confidentiality, split samples, and regulatory notice process.
7. Specify restoration, IDW handling, and same-day safety stabilization.
8. Require restoration security with clear draw mechanics and timelines.
9. Reaffirm indemnity, insurance, and as-is entry; avoid narrowing existing protections.
10. Add lien protection, no waiver, counterparts, and execution conditions.

Core Clause Checklist:

- Limited consent under the main agreement; no implied rights.
- Authorized Work definition with explicit exclusions.
- Exhibit A with precise locations and relocation limits.
- Utility locate compliance and private utility clearance.
- Work window, hours, notice, escorts, staging, and cleanup.
- Reporting/data delivery deadlines and confidentiality.
- Consultation period before any regulator disclosure; statutory exceptions.
- Restoration obligations, standards, and deadlines.
- IDW handling and generator designation [VERIFY].
- Restoration security and draw conditions.
- Indemnity reaffirmation and environmental-specific scope.
- Insurance requirements including PLL coverage [VERIFY].
- Lien prevention and release obligations.
- Conditions precedent to entry and suspension rights.

Template Skeleton:

```text
[Date]

Re: Limited Consent for Invasive Testing at [Property]

Pursuant to Section [__] of [Agreement Name] dated [__] (the “Agreement”) between [Grantor] and [Recipient], Grantor grants Recipient limited consent to perform only the “Authorized Work” described below. Capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings in the Agreement. Except as expressly stated, the Agreement remains in full force.

Authorized Work: [number] soil borings, max diameter [__], max depth [__], method [__], samples [__]. No monitoring wells, test pits, pavement cutting, vegetation removal, or work within [__] feet of utilities or structures.

Locations: Only at Exhibit A. Relocation > [__] feet requires written approval.

Timing/Access: Work Window [start–end]. Hours [__]. [__] business days’ notice. Site rules, escorts, and staging areas apply.

Utilities/Safety: Recipient must comply with applicable one-call requirements and private utility clearance. Recipient proceeds at its sole risk.

Reporting/Data: Deliver all logs and lab data within [__] business days. No disclosure to any government authority or third party without Grantor’s prior written consent except as required by law. Provide [__] business days’ consultation before any required report.

Restoration/IDW: Same-day stabilization. Final restoration within [__] days. Recipient responsible for IDW handling and disposal in compliance with law [VERIFY]. Property must be left lien-free.

Security: Provide restoration security in amount $[__], form [__], deliverable before entry. Grantor may draw after [__] days’ notice for reasonable restoration costs.

Indemnity/Insurance: Indemnity and insurance in Agreement Section [__] apply to Authorized Work and all contractors. PLL coverage with limits $[__] required [VERIFY].

Conditions Precedent: Consent effective only upon receipt of signed acceptance, insurance, security, and locate confirmation.

[Signature blocks for Grantor, Recipient, Consultant acknowledgment]
```

## Guidelines

- Use the exact defined terms, notice addresses, and execution mechanics from the main agreement.
- Never grant “Phase II testing” broadly; enumerate methods, counts, and limits.
- Do not incorporate a work plan “as modified in the field” without written approval controls.
- Avoid admissions about site conditions or utility accuracy; use “as-is” and no-representation language.
- Include a consultation period for regulatory reporting and note statutory exceptions.
- Mark regulatory citations and ASTM standard versions with `[VERIFY]` if not confirmed.
- State-specific rules for one-call, well abandonment, reporting, and liens require counsel confirmation.
- If required inputs are missing, issue a conditional consent that withholds entry until delivered.
