# Demand Package Compilation

Assembles a litigation-ready demand package—cover letter, demand letter, and indexed exhibits—that advances settlement negotiations and signals readiness to file.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case facts** — incident/breach narrative, dates, all party names and roles
2. **Damages documentation** — medical bills, invoices, wage records, repair estimates
3. **Evidence inventory** — photos/video, witness statements, expert reports, contracts, correspondence
4. **Legal theories identified** — duties breached, applicable statutes or common law claims
5. **Settlement demand figure** — client-approved amount with response deadline

## Output Structure

### 1. Cover Letter
Brief transmittal identifying matter, parties, and purpose of the package.

### 2. Demand Letter

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| Introduction | Client identity, adverse party, and basis for claim |
| Statement of Facts | Chronological narrative with exhibit references (Ex. 1, Ex. 2…) |
| Legal Theories | Duty, breach, causation, damages — one paragraph per theory |
| Damages Summary | Itemized table (see below) with total demand figure |
| Liability Analysis | Why liability is clear; preemptively address key weaknesses |
| Demand & Deadline | Specific dollar amount; reasonable response deadline (typically 30 days) |

## Pre-Finalization Checklist

- [ ] All referenced exhibits included and labeled consistently with demand letter citations
- [ ] Every factual assertion supported by an exhibit in the package
- [ ] Damages figures accurate and cross-referenced to source documents
- [ ] Dates, names, and party designations consistent throughout
- [ ] Total demand stated clearly with response deadline
- [ ] No attorney work product, privileged communications, or internal strategy included
- [ ] Medical records HIPAA-compliant; written patient authorization confirmed before transmission
- [ ] Sensitive PII redacted where not essential to evidentiary value
- [ ] Package paginated, bookmarked (PDF), and exhibit-indexed for easy navigation

## Guidelines

- **Tone**: Professional and factual — let the evidence carry persuasive weight; avoid inflammatory language
- **Jurisdiction**: U.S. only; verify applicable statute of limitations and statutory notice requirements for the governing state before setting the response deadline
- **Punitive damages**: Include only where conduct meets the applicable state legal standard [VERIFY jurisdiction-specific threshold and pleading requirements]
- **HIPAA**: Confirm written patient authorization before including medical records in any package transmitted to adverse parties
- **Privilege**: Exclude all attorney-client communications and work product — the package becomes opposing counsel's property upon transmission
- **Self-contained**: Package must require no supplementation; recipient should be able to evaluate the full claim without follow-up
