# Stipulated Protective Order for Confidential Information

Drafts a comprehensive federal Stipulated Protective Order governing designation, handling, and disposition of confidential discovery materials under FRCP 26(c).

## Prerequisites

1. **Case identifiers** — court, district, case number, judge, party names
2. **Nature of dispute** — trade secret, commercial, IP, healthcare, financial, etc.
3. **Anticipated confidential material types** — trade secrets, financials, source code, PII, PHI, customer data, pricing, strategy docs
4. **Industry-specific regulations** — HIPAA, FERPA, financial privacy, if applicable
5. **Local rules** — district-specific requirements for protective orders and sealed filings
6. **Party preferences** — single-tier vs. multi-tier; any prior confidentiality disputes

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption & Preamble

- Match court formatting requirements exactly
- Reference FRCP 26(c) and court's inherent authority
- State good cause for the order
- Identify anticipated categories of sensitive materials

### 2. Definitions

| Term | Coverage |
|------|----------|
| Confidential Information | Trade secrets, proprietary strategy, customer data, financials, pricing, technical specs, source code, marketing plans, PII — scoped to case specifics |
| Designating Party | Any party or non-party producing discovery who designates materials |
| Receiving Party | Any party or non-party receiving designated materials |
| Qualified Person | Individuals authorized to access confidential materials |
| Outside Counsel | Attorneys not employed by any party |
| Retained Expert | Consultants engaged for this litigation |
| Competitive Decision-Maker | Individuals with regular competitive decision-making duties |

### 3. Designation Tiers

**Default: Two-tier system** (adjust to single-tier for simple cases or three-tier for highly sensitive matters).

| Tier | Label | Access Scope |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Tier 1 | CONFIDENTIAL | Litigation team + parties under safeguards |
| Tier 2 | ATTORNEYS' EYES ONLY (AEO) | Outside counsel + approved experts only; excludes parties and employees |

**Marking procedures by format:**

| Format | Procedure |
|--------|-----------|
| Paper | Stamp/label each page with designation legend |
| ESI | Mark in filename, first page, or metadata; address native files, databases, spreadsheets |
| Deposition testimony | Designate on the record OR in writing within 14–30 days of transcript receipt; all testimony treated as AEO until designation period expires |

### 4. Access Rights

**CONFIDENTIAL tier access:**
- Outside counsel, associated attorneys, paralegals, support staff, contract attorneys
- In-house counsel (non-competitive-decision-makers) who execute acknowledgments
- Retained experts/consultants with signed acknowledgments
- Court, court staff, court reporters
- Parties (consider limiting to review in counsel's presence)

**AEO tier access:**
- Outside counsel and associated attorneys only (exclude in-house counsel unless criteria met)
- Retained experts/consultants with detailed acknowledgments
- Court and personnel
- **Explicitly exclude**: parties, employees, officers, directors, agents
- Safety valve: motion to court for specific disclosure upon showing substantial need

**Acknowledgment form must include:**
- [ ] Identification of the litigation
- [ ] Confirmation of receipt and understanding of the order
- [ ] Agreement to be bound by all provisions
- [ ] Submission to court jurisdiction for enforcement
- [ ] Acknowledgment that violation may result in sanctions/contempt
- [ ] Counsel retains originals; copies available to opposing counsel on request

### 5. Use Limitations & Court Filings

- Use restricted solely to prosecuting, defending, or resolving this litigation (including appeals)
- Prohibit business, commercial, competitive, or unrelated use
- **Sealed filings**: Designation alone does not authorize filing under seal
  - Party must comply with local rules and file a motion to seal
  - Materials filed provisionally under seal pending ruling
  - Court makes independent determination; no presumption from designation

### 6. Challenge Procedure

```
Step 1 → Written notice identifying challenged material + reasons
Step 2 → Good-faith meet-and-confer (minimum 14 days; longer for voluminous materials)
Step 3 → If unresolved → motion to court
         Burden: on designating party to show good cause
Step 4 → Challenged material retains designation until court rules or designation withdrawn
Optional → Prevailing challenger may recover reasonable expenses including fees
```

### 7. Inadvertent Disclosure & FRE 502(d)

- Inadvertent production does NOT waive privilege (attorney-client, work product, or other)
- **Clawback protocol:**
  1. Designating party provides written notice of inadvertent production
  2. Receiving party has 5 business days to return, sequester, or destroy all copies/extracts/summaries
  3. Receiving party must not use or disclose; must retrieve any further disseminations
  4. Delete from databases, litigation support systems, and devices
- **Include express FRE 502(d) language**: disclosure under this order does not waive privilege in this or any other federal or state proceeding

### 8. Non-Party Protections

- Non-parties may designate using same tiers and procedures
- Subpoenaing party must provide copy of order with subpoena and notify of designation rights
- Extended designation period for non-parties (30 days after production)
- Non-parties may participate in challenge proceedings regarding their materials

### 9. Final Disposition

- Within 60–90 days after final termination (all appeals exhausted):
  - Return all confidential materials to designating party, OR
  - Certify in writing that all copies/extracts/summaries destroyed
- **Archival exception**: counsel may retain one copy of court filings, transcripts, exhibits, and work product for professional responsibility purposes, subject to continuing confidentiality obligations
- "Final termination" = conclusion of all proceedings including post-trial motions, appeals, and remands
- Obligations survive case termination unless modified by court order
- Subsequent litigation use requires designating party consent or court order after notice and hearing

### 10. Modification & Enforcement

- Modification by written stipulation of all parties (court-approved) or by court order after motion
- Meet-and-confer required before modification motions
- Court retains continuing jurisdiction for enforcement
- Available remedies: contempt, monetary sanctions, evidentiary sanctions, terminating sanctions
- Enforceable against parties and non-parties who receive confidential information
- Immediate notification duty upon learning of any violation or threatened violation

### 11. Signature Blocks

- Signature lines for counsel for all parties (stipulation)
- "SO ORDERED" block with judge signature line and date
- Include notice of presentment or agreed/contested statement if required by local rules

## Guidelines

- **Check local rules**: Some districts have model protective orders — adopt or reconcile with them
- **Number all paragraphs** for easy citation and cross-reference
- **Maintain terminological consistency** throughout
- **Verify cross-references** between sections before finalizing
- **Scope definitions narrowly enough** to prevent overdesignation while covering legitimate interests
- **Tailor to industry**: weave in HIPAA, FERPA, or financial privacy requirements where applicable
- **Anticipate disputes**: the order should resolve common discovery friction (overdesignation, access requests, inadvertent production) without requiring constant court intervention
- Do not fabricate local rule numbers or citation specifics — mark with [VERIFY] if uncertain
