# Hearing Preparation Summary

Single document that orients counsel, frames issues, and provides a fast in-hearing reference.

## Prerequisites

1. Hearing notice or order with date, time, forum, and scope.
2. Operative pleadings and any amendments.
3. Key motions, briefs, and oppositions for the hearing.
4. Docket sheet and relevant prior orders or minute entries.
5. Evidence set: exhibits, declarations, discovery excerpts, transcripts.
6. Witness list with contact info and prior statements.
7. Applicable rules and authorities (statutes, rules, leading cases) or a request to research them.

## Guidelines

- Use source citations for every factual assertion and quote.
- Separate facts from arguments; label advocacy explicitly.
- Do not assume law or local rules; verify and mark uncertainties with `[VERIFY]`.
- Prefer tables and checklists over narrative.
- Keep to the hearing scope; omit unrelated case history.
- Note jurisdiction and forum at the top; adjust standards accordingly.
