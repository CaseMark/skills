# Customer Identification Program (CIP) Policy

Produce an exam-ready CIP policy that operationalizes identity collection, verification, screening, recordkeeping, and governance.

## Prerequisites

1. **Institution profile** — charter type, products, channels, footprint, risk assessment
2. **Current AML/BSA program** — related policies (CDD/EDD, SAR, sanctions)
3. **Account taxonomy** — definitions of "account" and product coverage
4. **Existing procedures** — onboarding workflows and system capabilities
5. **Regulatory references** — Section 326, 31 CFR 1020.220, applicable interagency guidance

## Output Structure / Process

**1) Policy Header**
- Effective date, version, approving authority, owner

**2) Purpose and Authority**
- Cite Section 326 and 31 CFR 1020.220
- State CIP is mandatory and integrated with BSA/AML

**3) Scope and Definitions**
- Covered accounts, channels, customer types
- Exclusions (narrowly defined; cite specific rule basis if used)
- Define “customer,” “account,” and “beneficial owner” as applicable

**4) Minimum Information Collection**

| Customer Type | Required Data | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Individual | Name, DOB, residential address, ID number | Physical address required; mailing address optional |
| Entity | Legal name, principal place of business, EIN/TIN, formation docs | Identify authorized signers |
| Non‑U.S. | Passport/foreign ID number + country | Verify per risk-based standards |

**5) Verification Methods**

| Method | When Used | Evidence to Record |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Documentary | Default | Document type, issuer, number, expiration |
| Non‑documentary | If no suitable documents or higher risk | Source, date, result, analyst notes |

**Documentary checklist**
- Document appears genuine and unaltered
- Photo/description reasonably matches customer
- Name/DOB/address match customer-provided data
- Document is unexpired or institution-defined acceptable exception

**Non‑documentary options**
- Database/consumer report checks
- Contact at verified address/phone
- References from other financial institutions
- Additional corroborating documents

**6) Entity and Beneficial Ownership**
- Verify legal existence (formation docs, good standing, registry)
- Verify identity of individuals with authority to act
- Collect/verify beneficial ownership per CDD Rule (31 CFR 1010.230) [VERIFY]

**7) Discrepancy Resolution**
- Document mismatch and remediation steps
- Escalate unresolved discrepancies
- Refuse or restrict account when identity cannot be verified
- Consider SAR referral when warranted

**8) Government List Screening**
- Screen all customers against OFAC lists prior to account opening
- Document list sources, tool, match logic, and clearance
- Escalation and blocking procedures for true matches
- No customer notice of sanctions match or blocking

**9) Risk-Based Enhancements**
- Define risk factors and EDD triggers
- Additional verification, senior approval, transaction limits
- Periodic review cadence by risk tier

**10) Recordkeeping**
- Retain CIP records for 5 years after account closure
- Maintain retrievable records for exam and law enforcement requests
- Storage format and custody responsibility

**11) Customer Notice**
- Provide CIP notice at or before account opening
- Channel-specific delivery methods

**Sample notice (edit for channel)**

```text
To help the government fight the funding of terrorism and money laundering, federal law requires all financial institutions to obtain, verify, and record information that identifies each person who opens an account. We will ask for your name, address, date of birth, and identification number, and may ask to see your driver’s license or other identifying documents.
```

**12) Third‑Party Reliance**
- Written contract, certification of compliant CIP, due diligence
- Ongoing monitoring and audit rights
- Institution retains ultimate responsibility

**13) Training and Governance**
- Initial and annual training for relevant staff
- CIP owner (BSA/AML Officer) duties and reporting
- Independent testing cadence and board reporting

**14) Review and Change Control**
- Annual review minimum
- Board approval for material changes
- Version history and distribution

## Guidelines

- Use “reasonable time” for verification timing; define exact SLAs in procedures.
- Avoid expanding exemptions beyond regulatory text; document rationale.
- Align with broader AML, sanctions, and CDD policies.
- Mark any jurisdiction-specific deviations and approvals.
- If citing guidance, ensure currency; tag uncertain sources as [VERIFY].
