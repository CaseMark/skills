# Fairness Hearing Notice

Drafts a class action settlement notice informing class members about a proposed settlement and the fairness hearing, compliant with FRCP 23(e) and due process requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Settlement agreement** — all material terms
2. **Class definition** — as certified or proposed for settlement purposes
3. **Preliminary approval order** — court's order approving notice and setting hearing
4. **Notice plan** — approved distribution methods (mail, email, publication, website)
5. **Key dates** — opt-out deadline, objection deadline, fairness hearing date and location
6. **Claims process** — if claims-made settlement, the claim form and procedures
7. **Class counsel information** — contact details for class members' questions

## Output Structure

### Header and Summary

- Case name, number, and court
- Bold summary: "A proposed settlement has been reached. You may be affected."
- Plain-language description of who is a class member
- **Key dates box** prominently displayed:

| Action | Deadline |
|--------|----------|
| Submit a claim | [date] |
| Opt out (exclude yourself) | [date] |
| File an objection | [date] |
| Fairness hearing | [date, time, location] |

### What Is This Case About?

- Plain-language description of the claims (no legal jargon)
- What the plaintiffs alleged
- What the defendant's position was
- Why the parties are settling

### Who Is Included?

- Class definition in plain language
- How to determine if you are a class member
- Contact information for questions about class membership

### Settlement Terms

- **Total settlement value**
- **What class members will receive** — per-member amount or formula
- Payment timing and method
- Non-monetary relief (if any — policy changes, injunctive relief)
- **Claim process**: how to file a claim, deadline, required documentation
- What happens if you don't file a claim (claims-made vs. automatic distribution)

### Your Options

Present three clear choices:

**Option 1: Submit a Claim** (if claims-made)
- How to submit
- Deadline
- What you'll receive

**Option 2: Exclude Yourself (Opt Out)**
- What it means (you keep your individual right to sue but get nothing from the settlement)
- How to opt out (written request, required content, where to send)
- Deadline
- Consequences of opting out

**Option 3: Object**
- What it means (you remain a class member but tell the court you disagree)
- How to object (written objection, required content, where to file/send)
- Deadline
- May appear at the fairness hearing (personally or through counsel)

**If You Do Nothing**
- You will be bound by the settlement
- You will receive [benefit, if automatic] / [nothing, if claims-made without filing]
- You will release the claims described in the settlement

### Attorney's Fees and Costs

- Amount class counsel will request
- How fees will be paid (from settlement fund or separately by defendant)
- Amount of service awards requested for class representatives

### Release of Claims

- Plain-language description of what claims are being released
- Scope of the release
- Warning that released claims cannot be pursued later

### The Fairness Hearing

- Date, time, and location (including courtroom)
- What the court will consider
- Whether class members may appear and speak
- That the hearing may be rescheduled — how to get updated information

### Contact Information

- Settlement website URL
- Toll-free phone number
- Class counsel contact information
- Settlement administrator address
- Court clerk (for reviewing filed documents)

## Guidelines

- Write at a **6th-8th grade reading level** — class members are not lawyers
- Use short sentences and simple words
- Bold key dates and deadlines
- Include a "frequently asked questions" format where possible
- Avoid legal jargon — if you must use a legal term, define it
- The notice is a due process document — it must be accurate and complete
- Include both English and any additional languages required by the preliminary approval order
- Comply exactly with any specific notice requirements in the preliminary approval order
- Consider ADA accessibility for digital notice formats
