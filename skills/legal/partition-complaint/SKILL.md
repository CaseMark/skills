# Complaint for Partition of Real Property

Draft a partition complaint that pleads jurisdiction, ownership, property description, and grounds for partition in kind or by sale.

## Prerequisites

1. Governing state partition statute and local rule requirements.
2. Recorded deed(s) and full legal description of the property.
3. Current ownership breakdown with percentage or fractional interests.
4. Encumbrances and lienholder information.
5. Property facts supporting partition in kind or partition by sale.
6. Prior attempts to resolve the dispute, if any.

## Output Structure / Process

Use the structure below and keep allegations in numbered paragraphs.

### Required Facts Intake

| Item | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Court and county | Venue where the property is located |
| Parties | Full legal names, capacity, addresses, entity info |
| Co-ownership basis | Purchase, inheritance, gift, divorce, etc. |
| Tenancy type | Tenants in common or joint tenants |
| Property | Street address, APN, county |
| Legal description | Metes/bounds or lot/block/subdivision |
| Ownership interests | Percentages or fractional shares |
| Encumbrances | Mortgages, liens, easements, amounts |
| Property use | Residential/commercial, improvements |
| Partition type | In kind or by sale with supporting facts |
| Prior efforts | Negotiations, demands, ADR if required |

### Pleading Outline

1. **Caption**  
Identify court, parties, and title: "Complaint for Partition of Real Property."

2. **Introduction and Nature of Action**  
State this is an action for partition under the governing statute.

3. **Jurisdiction and Venue**  
Allege subject matter jurisdiction, personal jurisdiction via ownership in forum, and venue in the county where the property sits.

4. **Parties**  
Allege each party's identity and relationship to the property. For entities: formation state and principal place of business. For estates: personal representative or heirs.

5. **Property Description**  
Include street address, APN, and full legal description. Attach legal description as Exhibit A when required.

6. **Ownership Interests**  
Allege tenancy type and specific ownership shares. State acquisition method and dates.

7. **Encumbrances and Contributions**  
List recorded liens and material encumbrances. Allege disproportionate payments or improvements if seeking accounting or credits.

8. **Grounds for Partition**  
Allege inability to agree on disposition and why partition is necessary.

9. **Partition Type**  
Plead whether partition in kind is feasible. If seeking sale, allege material injury or impracticability of physical division with supporting facts.

10. **Statutory Authority and Compliance**  
Cite the state partition statute and plead satisfaction of any preconditions such as notice or ADR.

11. **Prayer for Relief**  
Request determination of interests, partition in kind or sale, appointment of a referee/commissioner if applicable, accounting, costs, and other relief.

12. **Verification and Signature**  
Include verification if required and attorney or pro se signature block.

### Partition Type Checklist

Use these allegations when supporting partition by sale:

- Physical division is impracticable due to size, configuration, access, or utilities.
- Single structure or improvements cannot be divided without destroying value.
- Zoning or subdivision rules prevent lawful division.
- Division would materially diminish value compared to sale as a whole.
- Fractional interests make equitable division infeasible.

### Prayer for Relief Menu

| Relief | Include When |
| --- | --- |
| Partition in kind | Physical division is feasible and equitable |
| Partition by sale | In kind causes material injury or impracticable |
| Appointment of referee/commissioner | Statute or local practice requires |
| Accounting | Dispute over rents, profits, taxes, or improvements |
| Costs and fees | Authorized by statute, contract, or equity |
| Sale procedure | Court-supervised sale and distribution |

## Guidelines

- Join all co-owners and necessary lienholders; identify unknown claimants per local rules.
- Use the deed’s legal description verbatim; attach as an exhibit when required.
- Plead tenancy type precisely; do not assume joint tenancy without record support.
- Avoid conclusory statements; tie partition-by-sale allegations to concrete property facts.
- Check local rules for verification, special notices, or pre-filing ADR.
- Do not include settlement communications or privileged material in the complaint.
- If seeking fees or credits, plead statutory or contractual basis.
