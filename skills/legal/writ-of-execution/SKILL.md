# Writ of Execution

Drafts a writ of execution authorizing law enforcement to seize and liquidate assets to satisfy an outstanding money judgment.

## Prerequisites

1. **Judgment** — certified copy with amount, date entered, court, case number
2. **Debtor information** — full legal name, aliases, addresses, known assets
3. **Asset identification** — bank accounts, real property, vehicles, business assets, accounts receivable
4. **Prior enforcement** — any partial payments, prior writs, garnishments
5. **Exemption analysis** — debtor's likely exemption claims by jurisdiction
6. **Jurisdictional requirements** — state-specific writ form, filing requirements, sheriff/marshal procedures

## Output Structure

### Application for Writ of Execution

- Case caption and judgment information
- Original judgment amount, date, and court
- Credits and payments received
- Accrued post-judgment interest (rate per statute or contract, calculation)
- Recoverable costs (filing fees, service costs, prior enforcement costs)
- **Net amount due** (judgment + interest + costs − credits)
- Request to clerk to issue writ

### Writ of Execution

Per jurisdiction-specific form:
- Directed to sheriff/marshal of the county where assets are located
- Judgment creditor and debtor identification
- Amount to collect (net amount due)
- Instructions to levy on debtor's non-exempt property
- Return date for writ

### Levy Instructions to Sheriff/Marshal

Specify assets and enforcement methods:

| Asset Type | Enforcement Method | Key Requirements |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Bank accounts | Bank levy | Account numbers, bank branches, hold period |
| Real property | Record abstract of judgment, then levy | Property description, address, recorder's office |
| Vehicles | Keeper levy or seizure | VIN, registration, storage arrangements |
| Business assets | Till-tap or keeper levy | Business address, hours of operation |
| Wages | Earnings withholding order (separate from writ) | Employer information, pay schedule |
| Personal property | Seizure and sale | Description, location |

### Exemption Considerations

Common debtor exemptions to anticipate:
- Homestead exemption (amount varies dramatically by state)
- Motor vehicle exemption
- Tools of trade
- Retirement accounts (ERISA-qualified generally fully exempt)
- Social Security and public benefits
- Wildcard exemption
- Head of household wage protection

## Multi-Jurisdiction Enforcement

If debtor's assets are in a different state:
1. Domesticate the judgment (Uniform Enforcement of Foreign Judgments Act or registration)
2. Obtain writ from the domesticated court
3. Direct levy in the state where assets are located
4. Comply with that state's exemption laws

## Guidelines

- Verify judgment is final (not stayed pending appeal)
- Check for bankruptcy filing — automatic stay bars enforcement (11 U.S.C. § 362)
- Renewal: judgments expire — check statute (typically 10 years, renewable)
- Abstract of judgment should be recorded in every county where debtor owns real property
- Coordinate timing of levies — hit multiple assets simultaneously if possible
- Consider supplemental proceedings (debtor examination) to discover assets
- Track all costs meticulously — recoverable as part of enforcement
