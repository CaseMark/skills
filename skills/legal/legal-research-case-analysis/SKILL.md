# Legal Research & Case Law Analysis

Systematically researches case law and produces cited analysis to support litigation strategy.

## Prerequisites

1. **Research question** — specific legal issue, jurisdiction, and relevant facts
2. **Legal databases** — access to case law sources (court opinions, statutes, regulations)
3. **Existing case materials** — current briefs, complaints, or motions for context

## Workflow

### 1. Define Search Strategy

- Break the legal issue into searchable components
- Identify controlling jurisdiction and courts
- List key terms, statutes, and legal standards at issue

### 2. Search and Retrieve

- Search case law databases for relevant opinions
- Upload promising cases to vault for analysis
- Cast a wide net first, then narrow by relevance and recency

### 3. Analyze Precedent

For each relevant case:
- Extract the holding and reasoning
- Identify key facts and how they compare to the current matter
- Note whether it's binding or persuasive authority
- Flag any cases that cut against the client's position

### 4. Produce Research Memo

- Organize by legal issue, not chronologically
- Lead with strongest authorities
- Address adverse authority and distinguish it
- Include full citations in proper format

## Pitfalls

- **Check currency** — cases get overruled; verify each citation is still good law
- **Don't ignore adverse authority** — courts expect candor; hiding bad cases backfires
- **Jurisdiction matters** — persuasive authority from other circuits ≠ binding authority
- **Distinguish facts carefully** — surface similarity isn't enough; analyze the specific facts that drove each holding
