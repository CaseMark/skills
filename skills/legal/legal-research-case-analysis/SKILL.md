# Legal Research & Case Law Analysis

Systematically researches case law and produces cited analysis for litigation strategy.

## Prerequisites

1. **Research question** — specific legal issue, jurisdiction, relevant facts
2. **Legal databases** — access to case law sources
3. **Existing case materials** — briefs, complaints, or motions for context

## Workflow

### 1. Define Strategy
Break issue into searchable components. Identify controlling jurisdiction. List key terms, statutes, and standards.

### 2. Search and Retrieve
Search for relevant opinions. Upload promising cases to vault. Cast wide, then narrow by relevance and recency.

### 3. Analyze
Per case: extract holding and reasoning, identify key facts, note binding vs. persuasive authority, flag adverse cases.

### 4. Produce Memo
Organize by legal issue. Lead with strongest authorities. Address adverse authority. Include full citations.

## Pitfalls
- Verify each citation is still good law — cases get overruled
- Don't ignore adverse authority — courts expect candor
- Distinguish persuasive from binding authority
- Analyze specific facts that drove holdings, not surface similarity
