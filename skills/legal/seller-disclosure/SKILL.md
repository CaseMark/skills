# Seller Disclosure Statement

Drafts a legally compliant residential Seller Disclosure Statement that satisfies state and federal requirements while protecting sellers from post-closing liability.

## Prerequisites

1. **Property identification** — legal description, APN, full address, county
2. **Seller information** — full legal names of all title holders
3. **State jurisdiction** — determines statutory disclosure requirements, format, and delivery timeframes
4. **Available documents** — inspection reports, repair invoices, HOA docs, surveys, environmental reports, prior disclosures
5. **Property age** — if pre-1978, federal lead-based paint disclosure is mandatory

## Process

### Step 1: Document Extraction

Search uploaded documents for:

| Data Point | Sources |
|---|---|
| Seller names (as on title) | Deed, title report |
| Property ID (APN, legal description) | Tax records, title report |
| Known defects & repair history | Inspection reports, invoices, correspondence |
| Environmental conditions | Environmental reports, FEMA maps, radon tests |
| HOA details | CC&Rs, HOA statements, assessment notices |
| Permits & code compliance | Building permits, certificates of occupancy |
| Easements & encumbrances | Title report, survey |

### Step 2: Document Header

Include:
- All sellers' full legal names
- Complete property identification (address, APN, legal description)
- Cite governing state disclosure statute
- Seller knowledge-and-belief attestation
- Buyer notice: disclosure ≠ warranty; independent inspections recommended

### Step 3: Structural & Systems Disclosures

For each system, disclose: **current condition, age, known defects, repair history (contractor + date + warranty), materials of concern**.

| System | Key Disclosure Points |
|---|---|
| **Foundation** | Cracks, settlement, water intrusion, past repairs + warranties |
| **Roof** | Age, composition, leak history (locations + dates), warranty status |
| **Plumbing** | Pipe materials (flag polybutylene/galvanized), leaks, sewer vs. septic (last pump date) |
| **Electrical** | Panel amperage, aluminum wiring, GFCI presence, code violations |
| **HVAC** | Equipment age, fuel type, maintenance history, deficiencies |
| **Water heater** | Age, capacity, fuel type, condition |

Cite specific inspection reports or invoices by name and date.

### Step 4: Environmental & Federal Disclosures

**Lead-based paint (pre-1978 properties — mandatory under 42 U.S.C. § 4852d):**
- Disclose known/unknown presence of lead-based paint or hazards
- Note buyer receives EPA pamphlet "Protect Your Family from Lead in Your Home"
- Include 10-day inspection right (unless parties negotiate otherwise)

**Additional environmental items:**

| Hazard | Disclosure Requirements |
|---|---|
| Asbestos | Location, testing/abatement history, scope of work |
| Radon | Test results or statement that no testing conducted |
| Flooding | Chronological event history: date, source, damage extent, insurance claims, remediation; FEMA SFHA status |
| Underground storage tanks | In use, abandoned, or removed; soil contamination; remediation |
| Mold/moisture | Locations, water damage history, remediation efforts |
| Hazardous waste proximity | Federal/state database listings, former industrial use |
| Natural hazards | Earthquake fault zones, seismic hazard areas, wildfire zones (per state requirements) |

### Step 5: Legal Encumbrances & Title

- **Easements** — purpose, location, practical impact (utility vs. access vs. conservation)
- **Boundary disputes** — nature, parties, resolution status
- **HOA** — name, management contact, assessment amounts, special assessments, pending litigation, violations
- **Zoning** — classification, non-conforming uses/structures
- **Permits** — pulled permits, final inspection status, certificates of occupancy; flag unpermitted work explicitly
- **Code violations** — notices, compliance orders, corrective measures, current status
- **Use restrictions** — rental, age, occupancy limitations from CC&Rs or local ordinances

### Step 6: Neighborhood & Area Conditions

Disclose material facts affecting value or desirability:
- Noise sources (airports, highways, railroads, commercial/industrial)
- Proximity to waste facilities, agricultural operations
- Planned public improvements (road widening, utility work, development proposals)
- Upcoming special assessments with estimated costs/timing
- Deaths on property — **check state statute** (requirements vary: timeframe limits, type restrictions, or prohibitions)
- Any other fact a reasonable buyer would consider material

### Step 7: Certification & Execution

**Seller certification must include:**
- Truth and correctness attestation (under penalty of perjury)
- Acknowledgment of duty to amend if new material facts arise before closing
- Statement that disclosure is not a warranty
- Statement that disclosure does not substitute for buyer inspections

**Signature block requirements:**
- Signature line for every person with ownership interest
- Printed name + date for each seller
- Separate buyer acknowledgment section (receipt + right to inspect)

**Delivery timing:** State the statutory delivery deadline (typically 3–10 days before or after contract execution). Note buyer rescission rights for late delivery.

### Step 8: Assembly & Review

- [ ] Every section completed — no blanks (use "N/A" with seller initials for inapplicable items)
- [ ] All referenced documents attached as numbered exhibits with table of contents
- [ ] Page numbers + footer (property address + disclosure date) on every page
- [ ] State-specific mandatory disclosures verified against statutory checklist
- [ ] Language is factual and objective — not promotional or minimizing
- [ ] Lead-based paint disclosure included if pre-1978
- [ ] Federal, state, and local requirements all addressed

## Guidelines

- **State variation is significant** — always identify the governing state statute before drafting; disclosure requirements, format, delivery timing, and rescission rights differ materially across jurisdictions
- **"Unknown" is a valid answer** — do not fabricate conditions; clearly distinguish between "not present," "unknown," and "not inspected"
- **Err on the side of over-disclosure** — undisclosed material defects create post-closing liability; disclosed items rarely do
- **Never advise sellers to conceal** — disclosure obligations are non-negotiable; ethics rules and state statutes prohibit material omission
- **Ongoing duty** — remind sellers that amendments are required if conditions change before closing
- **Verify all statutory citations** against current state law [VERIFY]
