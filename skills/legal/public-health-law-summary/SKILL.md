# Public Health Law Summary

Produces accessible, actionable summaries of legislation or case law affecting public health policy for government agencies, healthcare providers, and policy advisors.

## Prerequisites

1. **Source identification** — official statutory text, case opinion, or regulatory action to summarize
2. **Jurisdiction** — confirm federal, state, or local scope
3. **Audience** — specify primary readers (agency staff, clinicians, policy advisors, general public)

## Output Structure

### 1. Header Block

| Field | Legislation | Case Law |
|-------|------------|----------|
| Full name/title | Statute title | Full case name |
| Enacting/deciding body | Legislature + chamber | Court name + level |
| Date | Enactment/effective date | Decision date |
| Jurisdiction | Federal/state/local | Circuit/district/state |
| Citation | Bill no., public law no., statutory cite (Bluebook) | Case cite, docket no. (Bluebook) |
| Procedural posture | N/A | Appeal status, procedural history |

All citations must use **Bluebook format** with links to authoritative sources (congress.gov, state legislature sites, court PACER/opinions).

### 2. Background & Context

- Public health crisis, policy gap, or litigation prompting the action
- Prior legal framework being modified or interpreted
- Key stakeholders and interests at stake
- Keep to 1–2 paragraphs max

### 3. Core Legal Analysis

**For legislation:**
- Principal provisions in plain language
- Defined terms and their scope
- New authorities granted or restrictions imposed
- Funding mechanisms and appropriations

**For case law:**
- Holding — state precisely, then explain in plain language
- Reasoning — key legal principles applied
- Concurrences/dissents — only if they signal future doctrinal shifts

### 4. Public Health Areas Affected

Tag all that apply and briefly explain impact:

- [ ] Disease surveillance & reporting
- [ ] Vaccination requirements
- [ ] Environmental health standards
- [ ] Healthcare access & coverage
- [ ] Emergency powers
- [ ] Privacy / health data (HIPAA, state equivalents)
- [ ] Health equity & social determinants
- [ ] Controlled substances / behavioral health
- [ ] Food & drug safety

### 5. Practical Implications

| Stakeholder | Key Impacts |
|-------------|-------------|
| Government agencies | New duties, authorities, compliance deadlines |
| Healthcare providers | Clinical practice changes, reporting obligations |
| Patients / public | Rights, access, protections |
| Regulated entities | Compliance requirements, penalties |

Flag ambiguities requiring further regulatory guidance.

### 6. Scope & Limitations

- Populations, geographies, and conditions **covered vs. excluded**
- Sunset provisions or expiration dates
- Pending challenges or anticipated amendments
- Conflicts with existing law — identify which provisions control
- **For cases:** precedential scope (binding vs. persuasive, party-specific vs. general)

### 7. Implementation Details

**Legislation:**
- Effective date and phased implementation schedule
- Responsible agencies and rulemaking timelines
- Reporting and enforcement mechanisms

**Case law:**
- Finality — subject to further appeal?
- Remand instructions
- Agency compliance obligations post-decision

### 8. Forward-Looking Analysis

- Fit within broader trends (federalism, individual rights vs. collective health, health equity)
- Likely regulatory or legislative responses
- Action items for affected stakeholders
- Related pending legislation or litigation to monitor

## Guidelines

1. **Plain language first** — define legal terms on first use; connect legal concepts to public health practice
2. **Bluebook citations required** — verify every citation against authoritative sources; mark unverified citations with `[VERIFY]`
3. **Objectivity** — present analysis without advocacy; note competing interpretations where they exist
4. **Scannable format** — use headings, tables, and checklists; busy professionals must find answers quickly
5. **No legal advice** — frame as informational summary, not counsel
6. **Jurisdiction matters** — always distinguish federal from state authority; note preemption issues
7. **Link to sources** — provide URLs to official text (government websites, court opinion databases)
