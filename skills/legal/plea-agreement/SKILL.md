# Plea Agreement

Drafts an enforceable plea agreement that formalizes the negotiated resolution of criminal charges between defendant and prosecution.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case information** — case number, court, presiding judge, full caption
2. **Charge sheet** — all original charges with statute citations and max penalties
3. **Negotiated terms** — which charges defendant pleads to, which are dismissed, sentencing concessions
4. **Factual basis** — agreed facts establishing each element of the plea offense(s)
5. **Defendant information** — full legal name, aliases, DOB
6. **Cooperation terms** (if any) — debriefing, testimony, ongoing obligations

## Output Structure

Draft the agreement with the following sections in order:

### 1. Parties & Jurisdiction

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Defendant | Full name, aliases/AKAs, DOB, case number |
| Prosecution | Office name (USAO / DA), assigned prosecutor |
| Court | Court name, district/division, presiding judge |
| Jurisdictional basis | Statutory authority |

### 2. Charges & Plea Specification

- Table of **all original charges**: count number, statute, offense description, max penalty
- Table of **plea charges**: which counts defendant pleads to (guilty or nolo contendere)
- List of **dismissed/not-pursued charges**
- If charge reduction: state both original and reduced charge explicitly
- Include statement that plea is voluntary and made with full understanding of consequences

### 3. Factual Basis

Stipulated facts establishing every element of each plea offense:

- [ ] Date, time, location of offense
- [ ] Defendant's conduct constituting the crime
- [ ] Victims, co-conspirators, relevant parties
- [ ] Mental state / intent as required by statute
- [ ] Aggravating or mitigating circumstances relevant to sentencing

Write so defendant can acknowledge as true. Note: this stipulation binds defendant and may be used in subsequent proceedings.

### 4. Sentencing Recommendations

Specify the agreement type:

| Type | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Rule 11(c)(1)(A) | Specific charge dismissal/non-prosecution agreement |
| Rule 11(c)(1)(B) | Non-binding recommendation — court may reject |
| Rule 11(c)(1)(C) | Binding specific sentence — defendant may withdraw if court rejects |

For state cases, identify the equivalent rule.

Include as applicable:
- [ ] Incarceration term or probation recommendation
- [ ] Fines, restitution amounts, payment schedule
- [ ] Supervised release / probation conditions
- [ ] Forfeiture terms
- [ ] Guideline calculation agreements, departure/variance positions
- [ ] Government's sentencing posture (advocate / silent / joint recommendation)
- [ ] Defendant's right to request different sentence

### 5. Rights Waived & Retained

**Rights waived:**
- Right to jury trial and to confront witnesses
- Right to remain silent / privilege against self-incrimination
- Right to compulsory process for defense witnesses
- Right to appeal (specify scope: sentence, conviction, or both)
- Right to post-conviction relief (specify exceptions: ineffective assistance, prosecutorial misconduct)
- Speedy Trial Act rights

**Rights expressly retained:**
- Right to challenge illegal sentence
- Right to challenge voluntariness of plea
- Any other negotiated retained rights

Include confirmation defendant discussed waivers with counsel and understands implications.

### 6. Additional Terms

Address as applicable:
- [ ] Cooperation obligations (truthful testimony, debriefings, law enforcement cooperation)
- [ ] Related civil/administrative proceeding agreements
- [ ] Media contact restrictions
- [ ] Breach provisions — consequences for each party's breach
- [ ] Government's right to reinstate dismissed charges upon defendant's breach

### 7. Acknowledgments

Defendant affirms:
- [ ] Plea is voluntary — no force, threats, or undisclosed promises
- [ ] Reviewed agreement thoroughly with counsel; understands all terms
- [ ] Satisfied with counsel's representation
- [ ] Understands nature of charges and potential penalties
- [ ] Mentally competent; clear mind; not under influence of substances
- [ ] Agreement constitutes entire understanding (integration clause)

## Guidelines

- Use plain language accessible to a layperson; retain terms of art only where legally necessary
- Number all paragraphs for easy cross-reference at plea colloquy
- Verify internal consistency — dismissed charges, retained rights, and breach provisions must align
- Confirm compliance with **Fed. R. Crim. P. 11** (federal) or state equivalent; check local court rules for additional requirements
- For state cases, adapt Rule 11 references to applicable state rule and cite correctly
- Never include terms beyond the scope of what the parties have actually negotiated
- Flag any ambiguity in sentencing terms — courts scrutinize vagueness at plea hearings
- If cooperation agreement, ensure adequate protections for defendant (use immunity, safe harbor provisions)
