# Personal Guaranty of Franchisee

Drafts an enforceable personal guaranty binding individual principals to all franchisee obligations, providing maximum credit enhancement for the franchisor.

## Prerequisites

1. **Franchise Agreement** — executed or to-be-executed, with date, parties, territory/location, key financial terms
2. **Franchisee entity details** — legal name, jurisdiction of organization, entity type, ownership structure
3. **Guarantor information** — full legal names (as on government ID), residential addresses, relationship to franchisee entity
4. **Governing jurisdiction** — state law governing the guaranty; note community property states for spousal consent
5. **Franchisor preferences** — exclusive vs. non-exclusive jurisdiction, notarization requirements, jury waiver policy

## Pre-Drafting Research

Before drafting, extract from uploaded documents:

| Item | Source |
|---|---|
| All obligation categories (fees, royalties, advertising, technology, training, transfer, indemnification, post-termination) | Franchise Agreement |
| Notice clauses, cure periods, renewal/extension terms | Franchise Agreement |
| Spousal consent requirement | Governing state law (community property states: AZ, CA, ID, LA, NV, NM, TX, WA, WI) |
| Notarization/witnessing requirements | Governing jurisdiction |
| Enforceability of suretyship defense waivers | Governing jurisdiction case law |
| Jury waiver enforceability | Governing jurisdiction |

## Output Structure

### Document Sections

Draft the following sections in order:

#### 1. Header & Preamble
- Title: **PERSONAL GUARANTY** (centered, bold, all caps)
- Effective date, guarantor(s) full legal name(s) + addresses, franchisor name + address
- Present-tense commitment language: "does hereby unconditionally and irrevocably guarantee"
- Inducement statement: guaranty is material condition to franchisor entering the Franchise Agreement

#### 2. Recitals (WHEREAS Clauses)
- Reference Franchise Agreement with specificity (date, parties, location/territory)
- Guarantor's relationship to franchisee (principal/shareholder/member/officer)
- Inducement: franchisor would not enter Franchise Agreement absent this guaranty
- Guarantor's familiarity with franchisee's financial condition and the Franchise Agreement
- Consideration acknowledgment → "NOW, THEREFORE..." transition

#### 3. Scope of Guaranty
Enumerate all guaranteed obligation categories:

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Initial fees | Franchise fee, training fees, opening costs |
| Continuing payments | Royalties, advertising fund, technology fees |
| Purchase obligations | Products, supplies, equipment from designated suppliers |
| Operational costs | Audit costs, inspection fees, accounting/legal fees for underreporting |
| Transfer-related | Transfer fees, costs of proposed transfers |
| Breach/default | Liquidated damages, indemnification obligations |
| Post-termination | De-identification, continuing royalties on inventory sell-off, lease obligations |
| Collection costs | Attorneys' fees, court costs |

- Extend to renewals, extensions, modifications (with or without guarantor's knowledge/consent)
- Cover obligations accruing after default or termination

#### 4. Guaranty of Payment and Performance (Not Collection)
- No requirement to first proceed against franchisee or exhaust remedies
- Primary, direct, immediate obligation — guarantor treated as principal debtor
- Joint and several liability with franchisee
- Franchisor may pursue guarantor without prerequisite actions

#### 5. Waivers of Defenses

Draft comprehensive waivers of:

- [ ] Notice of acceptance, default, dishonor, acceleration, protest
- [ ] Defense based on failure to pursue franchisee or exhaust collateral
- [ ] Release/discharge of franchisee or other guarantors
- [ ] Modifications/amendments/extensions to Franchise Agreement
- [ ] Compromise or forbearance with franchisee
- [ ] Franchisee bankruptcy/insolvency
- [ ] Franchisee disability, incapacity, lack of authority
- [ ] Invalidity/unenforceability of franchisee's obligations
- [ ] Statute of limitations defenses
- [ ] All other suretyship defenses

State guarantor's obligations are independent of franchisee's obligations.

#### 6. Continuing Guaranty & Reinstatement
- Remains effective until all obligations indefeasibly paid and performed in full
- Automatic reinstatement if any payment is rescinded, avoided, or returned (including bankruptcy preference avoidance)
- Continues through renewals/extensions unless franchisor provides written release
- Survives transfer of franchised business unless express written release

#### 7. Joint and Several Liability (Multiple Guarantors)
- Each guarantor liable for full amount
- Franchisor may proceed against any guarantor without joining others
- Waiver of contribution, reimbursement, and subrogation rights among guarantors until all obligations satisfied
- Release of one guarantor does not affect others

#### 8. Representations and Warranties

Guarantor represents:
- Adequate financial resources independent of franchise income
- Has read and understands Franchise Agreement and FDD
- Has had opportunity to consult legal/financial/tax counsel
- No conflicts with existing agreements, orders, or laws
- Guaranty is legal, valid, and binding
- All financial information provided is true, accurate, and complete; no material adverse change

#### 9. Financial Disclosure Covenants
- Current personal financial statement at execution
- Annual updates within 30 days of calendar year-end
- Updates on request with 10 days' notice
- Authorization for franchisor to obtain credit reports
- Prompt notice (10 days) of: material adverse change, bankruptcy filing, judgments/liens exceeding $[threshold], defaults on material obligations

#### 10. Events of Default & Remedies

**Default triggers:** franchisee default under Franchise Agreement | guarantor breach | guarantor bankruptcy/insolvency | assignment for benefit of creditors | material adverse change (franchisor's reasonable judgment) | death/incapacity | dissolution

**Remedies:** immediate acceleration | demand for full payment | specific performance | setoff rights | recovery of enforcement costs (attorneys' fees, court costs, collection fees) | all other legal/equitable remedies

- Remedies cumulative, not exclusive
- No waiver by delay; waivers must be written and signed

#### 11. Subordination
- All franchisee indebtedness to guarantor subordinated to franchisor's claims
- Guarantor may not accept payments from franchisee while obligations remain outstanding
- Payments received in violation held in trust for franchisor
- Waiver of subrogation, reimbursement, and contribution against franchisee until all obligations satisfied

#### 12. Jurisdiction, Venue & Procedural
- Consent to exclusive jurisdiction in [franchisor's preferred county/state]
- Consent to service by certified/registered mail
- **JURY TRIAL WAIVER** — all caps, conspicuous (verify enforceability in governing jurisdiction)

#### 13. Governing Law & Interpretation
- Governing state law without conflicts-of-law principles
- Amendments only by written instrument signed by franchisor
- No modification by course of dealing or trade usage

#### 14. Miscellaneous
- **Severability** — invalid provisions modified to minimum extent or severed
- **Binding effect** — binds heirs, executors, administrators, successors, assigns
- **Assignment** — franchisor may assign without consent; guarantor may not assign
- **Cumulative security** — supplements (does not replace) other guaranties or security
- **Entire agreement** — supersedes prior negotiations on subject matter

#### 15. Acknowledgment of Understanding
Guarantor specifically acknowledges:
- Guaranty is material inducement; franchisor would not proceed without it
- Valuable consideration received through franchisor entering Franchise Agreement
- Opportunity to consult counsel; fully understands obligations
- Voluntary execution without duress
- Franchise businesses involve significant risk; guarantor may face substantial liability
- Adequate financial resources independent of franchise income
- Important legal rights being waived (enumerate key waivers)

#### 16. Signature Block

For each guarantor:
```
IN WITNESS WHEREOF, Guarantor has executed this Guaranty
as of the date first written above.

Signature: ____________________________
Printed Name: _________________________
Date: _________________________________
Address: ______________________________
         ______________________________
```

- Separate signature pages if multiple guarantors executing remotely
- Include notary acknowledgment block if required/advisable under governing law
- Include **CONSENT OF SPOUSE** section in community property states with spouse signature block

## Guidelines

- **Scope maximization**: Draft for broadest enforceable scope; enumerate obligation categories explicitly rather than relying solely on catch-all language
- **Community property states**: Always include spousal consent section for guarantors domiciled in AZ, CA, ID, LA, NV, NM, TX, WA, WI
- **Jury waiver conspicuousness**: Must be in ALL CAPS or bold per most jurisdictions' requirements; verify enforceability in governing state
- **Bankruptcy reinstatement**: The automatic reinstatement clause is critical for preference avoidance scenarios — do not omit
- **No guaranteed dollar cap**: Unless franchisor specifically requests a cap, draft as unlimited guaranty
- **Defined terms**: Use consistent defined terms throughout; "Franchise Agreement," "Franchisee," "Franchisor," "Guarantor," "Guaranty"
- **Bracketed placeholders**: Mark all variable information with [BRACKETS] and clear labels
- **FTC Franchise Rule**: Guaranty requirement must be disclosed in Item 22 of the FDD; confirm franchisor compliance is not this document's scope but flag if relevant
- **State-specific suretyship statutes**: Some states (e.g., CA Civil Code §§ 2787-2855, NY GOL § 13-101) [VERIFY] have specific requirements for waiver of suretyship defenses — research governing jurisdiction before finalizing waivers
