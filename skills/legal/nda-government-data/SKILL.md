# Non-Disclosure Agreement — Government Data

Drafts a federal-regulation-compliant NDA governing disclosure of sensitive government data to contractors or third parties.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party identification** — government entity (agency name, authority), receiving party (legal name, clearance status/eligibility)
2. **Data classification** — classified (TS/S/C), CUI, SBU, PII, law enforcement sensitive, or other category
3. **Underlying agreement** — contract, grant, or cooperative agreement number triggering the NDA
4. **Security framework** — applicable NIST SP 800-series, FISMA tier, or agency-specific protocols
5. **Authorized personnel list** — individuals with need-to-know and clearance levels
6. **User-uploaded documents** — search for agency names, project IDs, prior breach history, existing security protocols, notice clauses

## Output Structure

Draft the NDA with the following sections in order:

### 1. Parties & Recitals

- Full legal name and agency designation of disclosing government entity
- Receiving party with legal capacity and clearance status
- Purpose of disclosure, program/project reference
- Legal authority for sharing; reference underlying contract/grant number
- Recital establishing heightened sensitivity and regulatory framework

### 2. Definition of Confidential Information

Tailor to the applicable data category:

| Category | Marking Requirement | Handling Standard |
|---|---|---|
| Classified (TS/S/C) | Per EO 13526 markings | NISPOM procedures |
| CUI | CUI banner markings per 32 CFR 2002 | NIST SP 800-171 |
| SBU | Agency-specific marking | Agency security policy |
| PII | Privacy Act notice | 5 U.S.C. § 552a safeguards |
| Law enforcement sensitive | LES markings | Agency LE policy |

Include provisions for:
- [ ] Oral disclosures — must be confirmed in writing within 10 business days
- [ ] Unmarked information — treated as confidential if reasonable person would recognize sensitivity
- [ ] Derivative works and compilations

### 3. Receiving Party Obligations

- Limit access to authorized personnel with need-to-know and appropriate clearance
- Implement safeguards (administrative, technical, physical) per applicable NIST/FISMA/agency standards
- No unauthorized copying, reproduction, or removal from approved secure locations
- Use restricted to authorized government purpose only — no commercial advantage or competitive use
- Subcontractor/third-party access requires:
  - Prior written government approval
  - Flow-down of all confidentiality obligations
  - Equivalent security clearance and safeguard requirements

### 4. Exclusions & Mandatory Disclosures

**Standard exclusions:**
- Publicly available (not through receiving party's breach)
- Already known without confidentiality restriction
- Independently developed without reference to confidential information
- Received from third party without breach of any obligation

**Government-specific mandatory disclosure protocol:**
1. Receiving party must notify government entity **within [48/72] hours** of any compelled disclosure demand (FOIA request, congressional inquiry, subpoena, court order)
2. Government entity has right to seek protective orders or assert FOIA exemptions
3. Compliance with lawful disclosure demands ≠ breach
4. Receiving party cooperates with government's efforts to limit scope

### 5. Term & Survival

| Data Category | Confidentiality Duration |
|---|---|
| Classified | Until declassified by proper authority |
| CUI | Per CUI Registry disposition schedule |
| PII | Life of record + 3 years [VERIFY] |
| Other sensitive | 5 years from disclosure (default; adjust per program) |

- Survival clause: return/destruction obligations, use restrictions, and indemnification survive termination
- Address interaction with underlying contract term

### 6. Return & Destruction

- Upon termination or government request: return **all** materials including copies, notes, derivative works
- Classified materials: destroy per NISPOM-approved methods or return to government facility
- CUI/other: destroy per NIST SP 800-88 media sanitization guidelines [VERIFY]
- **Written certification** of compliance required within [30] days
- Government retains right to audit destruction compliance

### 7. Remedies & Enforcement

- Stipulation of irreparable harm; injunctive relief without bond
- Monetary damages: actual, consequential, and statutory where applicable
- Classified breaches: reference criminal penalties under 18 U.S.C. §§ 793–798
- Prevailing party entitled to attorneys' fees and costs
- Optional: liquidated damages for specified breach scenarios (must be reasonable, not punitive)
- Government reporting obligations: breaches involving classified data must be reported to cognizant security agency

### 8. Governing Law, Jurisdiction & Disputes

- Governed by **federal law**
- Venue: U.S. District Court for [district of government entity or breach location]
- Preserve sovereign immunity — nothing constitutes waiver of governmental immunities or defenses
- Address administrative exhaustion requirements if applicable (e.g., Contract Disputes Act for contractor disputes) [VERIFY]

### 9. General Provisions

- Severability, integration, amendment (written, signed by authorized reps)
- Waiver (non-enforcement of one breach ≠ waiver of future enforcement)
- Assignment prohibited without prior written consent
- Notices: formal written notice to designated addresses, specify delivery methods
- Representations: receiving party warrants authority, understanding, and security capability

### 10. Signature Blocks

- Government entity: authorized signatory with delegated authority, printed name, title, date, agency ID
- Receiving party: authorized representative, printed name, title, date, organization ID
- Include CAGE code or DUNS/UEI if applicable

### Exhibits (as needed)

- **Exhibit A** — Authorized Personnel List (name, clearance level, need-to-know justification)
- **Exhibit B** — Data Classification Guide
- **Exhibit C** — Security Requirements Matrix

## Guidelines

- Always confirm the signatory's delegated contracting authority for the government entity
- Do not assume classification level — require explicit identification from the user
- FOIA exemption assertions (especially Exemption 4 for commercial/financial info) must be supported by specific factual basis
- If the receiving party is a foreign entity, flag ITAR/EAR export control implications and stop for user guidance
- Distinguish between NDAs under FAR/DFARS procurement framework vs. non-procurement data sharing — the regulatory overlay differs significantly
- Mark any statutory citation not independently verified with `[VERIFY]`
- State-law confidentiality provisions are generally preempted; draft under federal law framework only
