# Notice of Privacy Practices (HIPAA)

Drafts a complete Notice of Privacy Practices satisfying 45 CFR § 164.520, ready for legal review and patient distribution.

## Prerequisites

1. **Covered entity details** — legal name, business address, effective date
2. **Privacy Officer contact** — name, title, phone, email, mailing address
3. **Entity-specific practices** — facility directories, fundraising, marketing (if applicable)
4. **Fee schedule** — charges for PHI copies, if any
5. **State law overlay** — any state privacy requirements more stringent than HIPAA

## Output Structure

### 1. Header & Introduction

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Entity name | Full legal name |
| Address | Primary business address |
| Effective date | Date notice takes effect |
| Purpose statement | How medical information may be used and disclosed |

### 2. Legal Duties (45 CFR § 164.520(b)(1)(v))

State that the entity is required by law to:
- Maintain privacy of PHI
- Provide this notice of legal duties and privacy practices
- Follow the terms of the notice currently in effect
- Notify affected individuals following a breach of unsecured PHI

### 3. Permitted Uses & Disclosures Without Authorization

Draft concrete, patient-readable examples for each category:

| Category | Scope | Examples |
|----------|-------|----------|
| **Treatment** | Care coordination, provider consultations | Sharing records with a specialist for referral |
| **Payment** | Billing, claims, utilization review | Submitting claims to insurance, pre-authorization |
| **Healthcare Operations** | Quality assessment, training, business planning | Internal audits, staff training on procedures |
| **Required by Law** | Court orders, public health, law enforcement | Reporting communicable diseases, responding to subpoenas |
| **Public Interest** | Abuse/neglect reporting, FDA, workers' comp | Reporting suspected child abuse to authorities |

### 4. Conditional/Optional Disclosures

Address each if applicable to the entity. For each, state the patient's opt-out right:

- [ ] Facility directory (name, location, general condition, religious affiliation)
- [ ] Family/friends involved in care (patient present and opportunity to object)
- [ ] Disaster relief (to authorized organizations)
- [ ] Marketing communications (authorization required unless face-to-face or promotional gifts of nominal value)
- [ ] Fundraising (opt-out required in each communication)
- [ ] Sale of PHI (authorization required; 45 CFR § 164.508(a)(4))

### 5. Individual Rights

Draft each right with the exercise procedure:

| Right | Regulatory Basis | Key Details |
|-------|-----------------|-------------|
| Access & copies | § 164.524 | 30-day response; fees limited to labor + supplies + postage; electronic copy if maintained electronically |
| Amendment | § 164.526 | 60-day response; may deny if accurate/complete; must append disagreement |
| Accounting of disclosures | § 164.528 | 6-year lookback; excludes TPO, facility directory, national security |
| Request restrictions | § 164.522(a) | Entity not required to agree EXCEPT must restrict disclosure to health plan if patient pays out-of-pocket in full |
| Confidential communications | § 164.522(b) | Alternative address/means; must accommodate reasonable requests |
| Paper copy of notice | § 164.520(c) | Available upon request even if prior electronic agreement |
| File complaint | § 164.530(g) | No retaliation; to entity Privacy Officer or HHS OCR |

### 6. Breach Notification

- Entity will notify affected individuals without unreasonable delay, no later than 60 days after discovery (45 CFR § 164.404)
- Notification includes: description of breach, types of PHI involved, steps to protect, entity's mitigation actions, contact procedures
- Breaches affecting 500+ individuals: media notice and HHS notification

### 7. Amendment & Revision Clause

State that the entity reserves the right to:
- Change notice terms at any time
- Make new provisions effective for all PHI maintained (including pre-existing)
- Post revised notice in facility, on website, and provide copies upon request

### 9. Acknowledgment of Receipt

- Include a detachable or separate acknowledgment form
- State clearly: treatment will NOT be conditioned on signing
- Document good-faith efforts to obtain acknowledgment if patient refuses (45 CFR § 164.520(c)(2)(ii))

## Guidelines

- **Plain language**: Write at or below 8th-grade reading level; avoid legal jargon where possible
- **State law**: Check for more protective state requirements (e.g., CA CMIA, TX medical privacy, NY mental health) and incorporate stricter standards
- **Substance use records**: 42 CFR Part 2 records require separate, more restrictive notice if entity handles SUD treatment records [VERIFY — Part 2 final rule amendments may have aligned with HIPAA]
- **Psychotherapy notes**: Clarify that authorization is required for most uses/disclosures (§ 164.508(a)(2))
- **Genetic information**: GINA protections prohibit health plans from using genetic information for underwriting; note if entity is a health plan
- **Minors**: Address state-specific minor consent and parental access rules where applicable
- **Format**: Must be available in paper form; electronic notice permitted with individual's agreement (§ 164.520(c)(3))
- **Posting**: Health plans with websites must prominently post; providers with physical service sites must post in a clear and prominent location
- **All citations are to 45 CFR unless otherwise noted** — verify all section references against current CFR before finalizing
