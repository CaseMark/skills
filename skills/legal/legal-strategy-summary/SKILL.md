# Legal Strategy Summary

Produces a strategic roadmap document that aligns the legal team and stakeholders on case direction, tactical approach, and risk posture.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case materials** — complaints, answers, correspondence, prior orders, key exhibits
2. **Client objectives** — desired outcome, risk tolerance, budget constraints
3. **Jurisdictional context** — forum, governing law, any pending procedural deadlines
4. **Party roles** — plaintiff/defendant/third-party posture and relationships

## Output Structure

### 1. Executive Overview

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Case nature | Type of dispute, parties, forum |
| Client position | Plaintiff/defendant, posture summary |
| Strategic goal | Primary objective in one sentence |
| Key risk flag | Single biggest threat to success |

Keep accessible to non-lawyer stakeholders. No legal jargon without parenthetical explanation.

### 2. Factual Background

- Organize chronologically or thematically based on whichever better supports the strategy
- Cite to specific documents/exhibits for each material fact
- Flag disputed vs. undisputed facts explicitly
- Omit background facts that don't drive legal analysis

### 3. Legal Framework

For each cause of action or defense:

| Component | Detail |
|---|---|
| Claim/defense | Name and statutory/common law basis |
| Elements | Numbered list of required elements |
| Burden | Who bears it and standard (preponderance, clear & convincing, etc.) |
| Controlling authority | Key statutes, lead cases in jurisdiction |
| Strengths | Facts and law favoring this position |
| Weaknesses | Gaps, adverse facts, unfavorable precedent |
| Counterarguments | Anticipated opposition arguments and rebuttals |

### 4. Procedural & Motion Strategy

For each anticipated motion:

```
Motion: [Type]
Legal basis: [Rule/statute]
Strategic purpose: [Why file — dispositive, narrow issues, force disclosure, etc.]
Timing: [When to file relative to case schedule]
Success likelihood: [High/Medium/Low with one-line rationale]
```

### 5. Discovery Strategy

| Category | Plan |
|---|---|
| **Must obtain** | Key documents/testimony needed and from whom |
| **Must protect** | Privilege concerns, work product, trade secrets |
| **Offensive use** | How discovery strengthens affirmative case |
| **Defensive use** | How to limit opponent's discovery leverage |
| **ESI considerations** | Preservation obligations, custodians, date ranges |

### 6. Settlement & ADR Assessment

- Current settlement posture and recommended range (if assessable)
- Optimal timing for settlement discussions
- ADR suitability (mediation, arbitration, early neutral evaluation)
- BATNA analysis — best/worst/likely trial outcomes vs. settlement value

### 7. Risk Assessment

| Risk Factor | Rating | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Liability exposure | H/M/L | $ range or outcome | Strategy to reduce |
| Adverse ruling on key motion | H/M/L | Effect on case | Fallback approach |
| Witness credibility | H/M/L | Which claims affected | Corroboration plan |
| Litigation cost trajectory | H/M/L | Estimated burn rate | Cost controls |

### 8. Next Steps & Action Items

Numbered list with:
- **Action** — specific task
- **Owner** — responsible attorney/team member (if identifiable)
- **Deadline** — date or trigger event
- **Priority** — Critical / High / Standard

## Guidelines

- Assess strengths and weaknesses candidly — this is an internal strategic document, not advocacy
- Cite specific evidence and legal authorities for every strategic recommendation; do not make unsupported assertions
- Flag critical deadlines (statutes of limitation, response deadlines, scheduling order dates) prominently
- If governing law is uncertain or jurisdiction-specific rules are unverified, mark with `[VERIFY]`
- Adapt section depth to case complexity — early-stage summaries may have lighter discovery/motion sections
- Update recommendations based on matter phase (pre-filing through settlement)
- Do not include privileged communications verbatim; reference by date and subject only
