# Franchise Transfer Agreement

Drafts a tripartite franchise transfer agreement allocating rights, liabilities, and risks among franchisor, transferor, and transferee in compliance with the original franchise agreement and applicable franchise law.

## Prerequisites

1. **Original franchise agreement** with all amendments, amendments, and exhibits
2. **Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD)** — current and version in effect at original grant
3. **Transfer correspondence** — franchisor's preliminary approval or qualification requirements letter
4. **Financial statements** — transferor (3–5 years operating history) and transferee (qualification evidence)
5. **Premises lease** and any landlord consent or SNDA requirements
6. **Asset inventory** — equipment lists, vehicle titles, IP assignments, POS/tech systems
7. **Compliance history** — transferor's royalty payment records, default/cure history, outstanding disputes

## Output Structure

### 1. Recitals
- Identify original franchise agreement by exact execution date and parties
- State transferor's compliance standing (or disclose defaults to be cured pre-closing)
- Describe transferee's qualifications against franchisor's published criteria
- Articulate legitimate business purpose for transfer

### 2. Assets Transferred

| Category | Inclusions | Common Exclusions |
|---|---|---|
| Tangible | Equipment, fixtures, furniture, inventory, vehicles, POS/IT systems, signage | Transferor's personal property |
| Intangible | Franchise license, phone numbers, domains, social media, customer lists, supplier relationships, goodwill | Corporate records unrelated to franchise ops |
| Excluded | List specifically in schedule | Assets subject to separate disposition |

### 3. Purchase Price & Financial Terms
- Total consideration with allocation by asset category (tangible personal property, covenant not to compete, goodwill, franchise rights)
- Payment schedule: deposit at execution → balance at closing → seller-financing/earnout if applicable
- **Franchisor transfer fee**: specify exact amount or calculation method per original franchise agreement; allocate between parties; state timing
- Working capital: minimum cash requirement post-closing, A/R and A/P treatment, inventory adjustment based on pre-closing physical count

### 4. Liability Allocation

| Party | Assumes |
|---|---|
| Transferee | All franchise agreement obligations from closing date forward (royalties, ad fund, standards compliance, lease from closing) |
| Transferor | Pre-closing vendor invoices, tax liabilities, employee claims, warranty claims, pending litigation/regulatory matters |
| Straddling | Claims for pre-closing incidents reported post-closing stay with transferor; partially performed services prorated at closing |

### 5. Conditions Precedent
- **Franchisor written approval** — transferee submits complete application (financials, background, experience, conflict check); franchisor evaluates per its reasonable business judgment or franchise agreement standard
- **Training completion** — transferee completes franchisor's training program (typically 1–6 weeks depending on system); may include testing or supervised operation requirement
- **New franchise agreement execution** — transferee signs franchisor's then-current form (may differ from transferor's: higher royalties, modified terms, shorter renewal)
- **Lease assignment or new lease** — landlord consent; clarify whether transferor remains as guarantor or is released
- **Default cure** — transferor cures all existing franchise agreement defaults; brings location into current brand standards compliance (renovations, equipment upgrades at transferor's cost)
- **Lender consent** — if assets encumbered by security interests
- **Transferee due diligence** — access to location, financial records, employee interviews, system inspection

### 6. Representations & Warranties

**Transferor reps:**
- Financial statements accurate, prepared on consistent basis, no undisclosed liabilities
- Compliance with all franchise agreement obligations (royalties, ad fund, standards, insurance, reporting)
- Tangible assets in good operating condition, maintained per manufacturer and system specs
- Clear title to all assets, free of liens except as scheduled
- No material adverse change since most recent financials
- No pending/threatened litigation, governmental investigations, or regulatory proceedings

**Transferee reps:**
- Financial capacity to close and fund operations
- Receipt, review, and understanding of FDD, new franchise agreement, and assumed contracts
- Opportunity to consult legal and financial advisors
- Business experience and qualifications; full-time devotion commitment if required
- Acknowledgment that franchisor makes no financial performance guarantees

### 7. Restrictive Covenants

| Covenant | Scope | Duration | Geography |
|---|---|---|---|
| Non-compete | No direct/indirect ownership, operation, or employment in competing business | 2–3 years post-closing | Franchise location + protected territory + reasonable buffer |
| Non-solicitation (employees) | No soliciting, hiring, or engaging franchise employees | 1–2 years post-closing | System-wide |
| Non-solicitation (customers/vendors) | No soliciting customers or interfering with vendor/business relationships | 1–2 years post-closing | Location-specific |
| Confidentiality | Franchisor's proprietary info, ops manuals, supplier arrangements, trade secrets | Indefinite or maximum permitted by law | N/A |

- Extend restrictions to transferor's affiliates, family members, and controlled entities
- Carve out: passive ownership of publicly traded securities below specified threshold
- Require return/destruction of all confidential materials at closing

### 8. Closing Mechanics

**Transferor deliverables:**
- Bills of sale (tangible personal property)
- Assignment and assumption agreements (contracts, leases)
- IP assignment (domains, social media, phone numbers)
- Vehicle certificates of title
- Equity instruments if structured as equity sale
- Tax clearance certificates; UCC-3 termination statements; lien releases
- Closing certificate (reps and warranties true as of closing date)

**Transferee deliverables:**
- Purchase price via wire transfer or certified funds
- Executed assumption agreements
- Executed new franchise agreement
- Evidence of required insurance (naming franchisor and transferor as additional insureds where required)
- Personal guarantees or security agreements as required

**Franchisor deliverables:**
- Written consent to transfer
- Acknowledgment of transferee as new franchisee
- Confirmation transferor's obligations satisfied or addressed

**Adjustments at closing:**
- Prorate: rent, utilities, property taxes, insurance, prepaid ad contributions
- Physical inventory count immediately pre-closing; purchase price adjusted to cost basis
- Risk of loss: transferor maintains insurance and bears casualty risk through closing; transferee may terminate if material damage occurs

**Transition assistance (30–90 days post-closing):**
- Transferor trains transferee on operational procedures not covered by franchisor training
- Introduction to key customers, suppliers, employees
- Cooperation in transfer of licenses, permits, utility accounts
- Specify whether compensated separately or included in purchase price

### 9. Indemnification

| Indemnitor | Indemnifies Against |
|---|---|
| Transferor | Pre-closing liabilities; rep/warranty breaches; excluded liabilities; third-party claims from pre-closing operations |
| Transferee | Post-closing liabilities; assumed obligations; rep/warranty breaches; third-party claims from post-closing operations |

- **Notice**: Prompt written notice with description and estimated amount; supporting documentation required
- **Defense control**: Indemnifying party controls defense with chosen counsel; indemnified party consent required for non-monetary settlements or liability admissions
- **Survival periods**: General reps — 1 year; tax matters and title — 3 years
- **Basket**: Claims must exceed 1–3% of purchase price before obligations triggered (specify deductible vs. threshold)
- **Cap**: General indemnification capped at 25–50% of purchase price; cap does not apply to fraud, willful misconduct, or fundamental reps (title, authority)

### 10. Dispute Resolution & Governing Law
- Governing law: align with original franchise agreement jurisdiction or franchisor's home state; note applicable franchise relationship law overrides
- Tiered dispute resolution: (1) senior executive good faith negotiation → (2) mediation (30–60 days, shared costs) → (3) binding arbitration or exclusive venue litigation
- Arbitration: single arbitrator or three-panel, recognized arbitration organization, specified location and rules
- Prevailing party attorneys' fees provision

### 11. General Provisions
- Integration clause (supersedes all prior negotiations and agreements)
- Amendment: written instrument signed by all parties only
- Assignment: prohibited without prior written consent; franchisor may assign in connection with system sale
- Notices: personal delivery, overnight courier, certified mail, email with confirmation; specify addresses for all three parties
- Severability; waiver in writing only; counterparts; electronic signatures authorized
- Signature blocks for all three parties with authority documentation (corporate resolutions, certificates of authority, operating agreement excerpts if entity signatories)

## Guidelines

- **Franchise relationship laws**: Several states (CA, IL, MD, MI, MN, NJ, NY, WA, WI, and others) impose transfer approval standards, notice requirements, and good cause restrictions that override contractual provisions — verify applicability [VERIFY state-specific statutes]
- **FTC Franchise Rule**: Confirm FDD disclosure obligations are satisfied for transferee; re-disclosure may be required depending on timing and material changes [VERIFY]
- **Lease assignment**: Confirm whether original lease permits assignment or requires new lease; address personal guarantee release explicitly
- **Tax**: Allocation among asset categories has significant tax consequences for both parties — flag for accountant review; purchase price allocation must be consistent across IRS Form 8594 filings
- **Non-compete enforceability**: Varies significantly by state; California and others void post-sale non-competes — tailor duration, scope, and geography to applicable law
- **Equity vs. asset sale**: Confirm transaction structure early — equity transfers of franchise-owning entity may require different consent provisions and may not trigger all transfer fee obligations under the franchise agreement
- **Do not** warrant that franchisor will approve the transfer — approval is franchisor's sole discretion per franchise agreement terms
