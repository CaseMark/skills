# Office Lease Agreement

Drafts a complete, execution-ready commercial office lease agreement balancing landlord and tenant interests.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party information** — full legal names, entity types, and addresses for landlord and tenant
2. **Premises description** — building address, suite number, rentable square footage, legal description
3. **Term** — commencement date, expiration date, renewal option details (if any)
4. **Rent structure** — lease type (NNN / full-service gross / modified gross), base rent schedule with escalations
5. **Operating expenses** — proportionate share methodology, inclusions/exclusions, caps or stops, CAM charges
6. **Security deposit** — amount, interest-bearing status
7. **Tenant improvement scope** — work letter details, construction allowance, delivery conditions
8. **Insurance minimums** — CGL limits, property coverage requirements
9. **Guaranty requirements** — personal or entity guaranty if applicable

## Output Structure

Draft the lease using the following article framework:

### Article Outline

| Article | Subject | Key Contents |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Preamble & Definitions | Parties, effective date, recitals, defined terms |
| 2 | Premises & Term | Space description, common area rights, commencement/expiration, delivery conditions, rent abatement during buildout |
| 3 | Base Rent | Rent schedule table (year / monthly / annual), escalation methodology, payment terms |
| 4 | Additional Rent & Expenses | Pro-rata share calculation, operating expense pass-throughs, tax escalations, CAM, audit rights |
| 5 | Security Deposit | Amount, holding requirements, return conditions |
| 6 | Permitted Use | Specific use grant, compliance with laws, prohibited uses, exclusive-use carve-outs |
| 7 | Maintenance & Repairs | Landlord vs. tenant responsibility split (see table below) |
| 8 | Alterations & Improvements | Approval process, plan submission, code compliance, ownership at termination, restoration obligations |
| 9 | Services | HVAC (specify business hours), janitorial, electric, water/sewer, elevator, after-hours charges |
| 10 | Insurance & Indemnity | Tenant CGL, property, workers' comp; mutual waiver of subrogation; indemnification |
| 11 | Assignment & Subletting | Consent standard (not unreasonably withheld), recapture right, profit sharing, affiliate/merger carve-outs |
| 12 | Default & Remedies | Tenant defaults, cure periods, landlord remedies, tenant remedies, self-help provisions |
| 13 | Subordination & Estoppel | SNDA, estoppel certificates |
| 14 | Casualty & Condemnation | Rent abatement, restoration obligations, termination triggers |
| 15 | Environmental | Hazardous materials prohibition (office supplies excepted), tenant indemnity |
| 16 | Force Majeure | Performance excuse for events beyond reasonable control |
| 17 | General Provisions | Notices, governing law, entire agreement, severability, quiet enjoyment, holdover |
| 18 | Execution | Signature blocks, authority representations, notary blocks if recordable |

## Guidelines

- **Lease type drives structure**: NNN leases pass through all operating expenses, taxes, and insurance; gross leases bundle them into rent; modified gross splits specific categories. Confirm type before drafting expense provisions.
- **Insurance minimums**: Default to $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate CGL; adjust based on premises size and use. Always include landlord as additional insured.
- **Indemnification limits**: Do not draft provisions requiring a party to indemnify against its own negligence — unenforceable in many U.S. jurisdictions.
- **Consent standard**: "Not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed" is the market standard for assignment/subletting consent.
- **Cure periods**: Provide reasonable notice-and-cure periods for non-monetary defaults (typically 30 days with extension for diligent cure). Monetary defaults: 5-10 days after written notice.
- **Jurisdiction-sensitive provisions**: Unlawful detainer procedures, self-help remedies, recording requirements, and notary requirements vary by state. Flag with `[CONFIRM JURISDICTION]` where state-specific rules apply.
- **Table of contents**: Include if the lease exceeds 10 pages.
- **Defined terms**: Capitalize consistently throughout; collect all definitions in Article 1 or define inline at first use.
- **Balanced drafting**: Maintain neutral tone protecting both parties unless instructed to draft landlord-favorable or tenant-favorable.
