# EPA Consent Decree

Drafts an enforceable federal court consent decree resolving EPA civil enforcement actions, structured to satisfy DOJ lodging requirements, 30-day public comment, and judicial entry standards.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case materials** — complaint or draft complaint, NOVs, administrative orders, inspection reports, sampling data
2. **Defendant corporate information** — exact legal name, state of incorporation, principal place of business, parent/subsidiary relationships
3. **Applicable statutes** — identify which statute(s) are at issue (CWA, CAA, RCRA, CERCLA, or other)
4. **Facility details** — address, operational history, regulated activities, permit history
5. **Penalty calculation data** — economic benefit analysis, violation history, ability-to-pay documentation
6. **Technical attachments** — Statement of Work, Remedial Action Plan, or Compliance Implementation Plan (draft or final)

## Output Structure / Process

### 1. Case Caption & Party Identification

**Caption format:**
```
United States of America, on behalf of the
United States Environmental Protection Agency,
  Plaintiff,
v.
[Full Legal Name of Defendant],
  Defendant.
Civil Action No. ___
```

**Party identification checklist:**
- [ ] Verify defendant's exact legal name against corporate registration documents
- [ ] Identify EPA Regional Office and designated Project Coordinator
- [ ] Document parent/subsidiary/successor relationships affecting enforcement
- [ ] Confirm state of incorporation and principal place of business
- [ ] Address joint and several liability if multiple defendants

### 2. Jurisdictional Recitals

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Subject matter jurisdiction | Specific statute + enforcement/citizen suit provision |
| Facility description | Address, operations, regulated activities, permit requirements |
| Violation allegations | Reference NOVs, inspections, sampling data — framed as allegations (no admissions) |
| Public interest showing | Environmental benefits, compliance achievement, deterrence under statute's objectives |

**Primary statute reference:**

| Statute | Citation | Enforcement Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Clean Water Act | 33 U.S.C. § 1251 et seq. | § 1319 |
| Clean Air Act | 42 U.S.C. § 7401 et seq. | § 7413 |
| RCRA | 42 U.S.C. § 6901 et seq. | § 6928 |
| CERCLA | 42 U.S.C. § 9601 et seq. | §§ 9606–9607 |

### 3. Compliance & Remedial Obligations

**Corrective action provisions must specify:**
- Equipment installation, process modifications, operational parameters to be maintained
- Monitoring: sampling frequencies, analytical methods, detection limits, reporting formats
- Permit/regulatory approval requirements with proof-of-compliance delivery to EPA
- EPA review/approval triggers: work plan submissions, design documents, completion reports
- BMPs, employee training schedules, preventive maintenance, and record-keeping systems

**Cleanup standard basis — select applicable:**
- [ ] ARARs (applicable or relevant and appropriate requirements)
- [ ] Risk-based site-specific criteria from approved risk assessment
- [ ] Regulatory standards (effluent limits, emission standards, concentration limits)
- [ ] Institutional controls where active remediation is technically infeasible

### 4. Compliance Schedule

Structure each milestone as:
- Calendar date **or** number of days after triggering event (decree entry, EPA approval of prior deliverable, task completion)
- Sequential with explicit dependency mapping (design → EPA approval → construction → startup certification)
- Interim reporting intervals for multi-year projects
- Annual progress reports and reassessment points for long-duration remediation
- Clear distinction between document-submission deadlines (triggers EPA review clock) and physical performance deadlines

### 5. Financial Provisions

**Civil penalty — required elements:**
- Total amount in U.S. dollars with itemization if multiple components (civil penalty / NRD / past response costs / future oversight costs)
- Reflect statutory penalty factors: seriousness, economic benefit of noncompliance, ability to pay, prior history, culpability
- Note: civil penalties are not deductible as ordinary business expenses under 26 U.S.C. § 162(f) [VERIFY current applicability]

**Payment logistics:**

| Item | Specification |
|---|---|
| Payee | U.S. Treasury via DOJ Consolidated Debt Collection System |
| Timing | Lump sum within ___ days of entry OR installment schedule |
| Form | Wire transfer or certified check |
| Proof of payment | Delivered to EPA and DOJ within 5–10 business days of payment |
| Default (installments) | Acceleration of remaining balance + interest at federal judgment rate |

**Financial assurance (required for significant remedial work):**
- Acceptable instruments: surety bond, letter of credit, trust fund, corporate guarantee
- Amount: EPA-approved cost estimate plus contingency factor
- Periodic adjustment as work progresses or cost estimates are revised
- Conditions for reduction/release tied to milestone completion

### 6. Stipulated Penalties

**Tiered daily rates by violation category:**

| Category | Days 1–30 | Days 31–60 | Day 61+ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Major (cleanup standards, critical milestones, permit limits) | $[X]/day | $[2X]/day | $[3X]/day |
| Minor (routine reports, documentation deficiencies) | $[Y]/day | $[2Y]/day | $[3Y]/day |

**Procedural mechanics:**
- EPA issues written demand specifying: violation, applicable penalty, and basis
- Defendant has 15 days to invoke dispute resolution; otherwise payment due within 30 days of demand
- Stipulated penalties are in addition to — not in lieu of — all other available remedies
- Penalties do not accrue during valid force majeure periods; resume immediately upon FM cessation

### 7. Dispute Resolution (Multi-Tier)

| Tier | Participants | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Informal negotiation | Designated party representatives | 20–30 days from written notice |
| 2. Senior-level escalation | Senior EPA and defendant officials | Additional 15–20 days |
| 3. Court resolution | Motion to district court | After exhaustion of Tiers 1–2 |

- Defendant must comply with EPA's position during pendency unless compliance would cause irreparable harm
- Demands for stipulated penalties or material breach allegations may proceed directly to court
- Joint statement required upon court filing, identifying areas of agreement and respective positions

### 8. Force Majeure

**Qualifying events:** Acts of God (floods, earthquakes, storms); wars, riots, civil unrest; unanticipated government action (statutory changes, permit denial despite good-faith efforts)

**Excluded events:** Economic conditions, financial inability to perform, defendant's own labor disputes, contractor/subcontractor failures (unless themselves caused by a qualifying FM event)

**Invocation requirements (written notice to EPA within ___ days):**
- Description of event and how it prevents specific performance obligations
- Anticipated duration of delay and affected milestones
- Mitigation measures taken or planned
- Defendant must exercise best efforts to minimize delay and resume performance as soon as practicable

### 9. Covenant Not to Sue & Reservation of Rights

**Covenant scope — define with precision:**
- *Temporal:* violations occurring up to date of decree entry
- *Geographic:* named facility/property with legal description or attached map
- *Substantive:* named statutes and regulatory provisions only

**Standard carve-outs — United States retains right to pursue:**
- Criminal liability (non-waivable by civil settlement)
- Violations occurring after decree effective date
- Newly discovered conditions constituting imminent and substantial endangerment
- Environmental statutes not referenced in the decree
- Natural resource damages not specifically addressed in the settlement

**United States' reservation of rights includes:**
- Enforcement of all decree terms (specific performance, contempt, stipulated penalties, additional civil penalties)
- New violations and newly discovered conditions
- Authority to impose additional requirements upon changes in law, regulation, or site conditions
- Cost recovery: attorney's fees, expert witness fees, EPA oversight and response costs
- All claims against third parties (other PRPs, contractors, successors in interest)

### 10. Public Participation & Judicial Approval

**Required sequence:**
1. Lodge proposed decree with court simultaneously with or after filing complaint
2. Publish notice in Federal Register → minimum **30-day** public comment period (specific statute may require longer)
3. Publish in local newspapers and/or hold public meeting if significant local interest warrants
4. United States prepares responsiveness summary addressing significant comments filed with court
5. Court enters decree only after comment period closes and responsiveness summary is filed
6. United States retains right to withdraw if comments reveal decree is inappropriate, improper, or inadequate

**Withdrawal clause:** If United States withdraws, decree is null and void; defendant may not use settlement statements or positions in subsequent litigation.

### 11. Modification & Termination

- **Modification:** Written agreement of all parties + court approval through formal modification proceeding
- **Termination:** Defendant completes all obligations to United States' satisfaction → United States files termination motion → court enters termination order
- **Effective date:** Date of judicial entry; all deadlines and obligations run from this date unless explicitly stated otherwise

### 12. Execution

**Signature block requirements:**

| Signatory | Authorization Required |
|---|---|
| Defendant (corporate) | Officer with actual authority + corporate resolution or secretary's certificate |
| United States | U.S. Attorney for filing district + DOJ ENRD trial attorney(s) |
| EPA | Regional Counsel + designated Project Coordinator |
| Court | U.S. District Judge + entry date line |

- Counterparts clause: each counterpart constitutes an original; fax/electronic signatures have full legal effect
- One fully executed original filed with court; copies to all parties
- Specify service addresses, contact information, and acceptable service methods for all decree-related notices

## Guidelines

- **No admissions:** All violation references must include "neither admits nor denies" language; avoid factual recitals that could be used in collateral litigation or contribution actions
- **DOJ concurrence:** All EPA consent decrees require DOJ approval before lodging; coordinate with assigned ENRD trial attorney throughout drafting
- **Penalty maximums:** Per-day statutory maximums are subject to periodic inflation adjustment — verify current amounts via EPA's civil penalty inflation adjustment tables before drafting [VERIFY current per-violation-per-day caps for CWA § 1319(d), CAA § 7413(b), RCRA § 6928, CERCLA § 9609]
- **CERCLA-specific:** Include § 122 settlement provisions, contribution protection under § 113(f)(2), and covenant not to sue per § 122(f); address consistency with the National Contingency Plan
- **RCRA financial assurance:** Verify applicable requirements under 40 C.F.R. Parts 264/265 for TSD facilities [VERIFY current CFR provisions]
- **Multi-statute cases:** Address separate liability frameworks, penalty authorities, and penalty factors for each violated statute independently
- **Exhibit consistency:** Verify all SOWs, RAPs, and compliance plans are consistent with decree body; cross-reference by exhibit number throughout
- **State law:** Confirm whether state environmental agency must be a party or signatory, and whether state-specific public notice requirements apply in addition to federal requirements
