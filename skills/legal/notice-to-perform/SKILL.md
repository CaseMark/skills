# Notice to Perform (Residential Real Estate)

Create a clear, legally compliant cure demand that doubles as an evidentiary record.

## Prerequisites

1. Executed agreement and all incorporated documents (addenda, schedules, disclosures).
2. Party names, roles, and service addresses exactly as in the agreement.
3. Specific breaches with dates, amounts, and documentary support.
4. Governing law, venue, and property location.
5. Contract notice clause requirements (method, address, timing, cure period).
6. Statutory notice requirements for the jurisdiction and contract type.

## Output Structure / Process

Required Inputs Table

| Field | Required | Source |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sender legal name and role | Yes | Agreement |
| Recipient legal name and role | Yes | Agreement |
| Property address and APN | Yes | Agreement/records |
| Agreement title and execution date | Yes | Agreement |
| Notice clause section | Yes | Agreement |
| Breach description | Yes | Records |
| Cure action required | Yes | Agreement |
| Cure deadline | Yes | Contract/statute |
| Service method | Yes | Contract/statute |

Timeline Table

| Date | Event | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| YYYY-MM-DD | Obligation due | Invoice/email/section |
| YYYY-MM-DD | Nonperformance occurred | Record |
| YYYY-MM-DD | Prior notice/communication | Email/letter |

Cure Period Decision Table

| Basis | Rule | Deadline Calculation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Contract | Use stated cure period and method | Date of service + contract days |
| Statute | Minimum notice period applies | Date of service + statutory days |
| Reasonableness | Only if no contract/statute | Fact-based, documented |

Drafting Checklist

- [ ] Identify parties, roles, and agreement details.
- [ ] Quote the exact breached provisions.
- [ ] State factual breach narrative with dates and amounts.
- [ ] Specify cure actions in measurable terms.
- [ ] Set deadline with date, time, and time zone.
- [ ] State consequences tied to contract and law.
- [ ] Include reservation of rights.
- [ ] Include signature block and authority.
- [ ] Add certificate of service with method details.

Template

```text
[DATE]

VIA [SERVICE METHOD]
[SENDER NAME]
[SENDER ADDRESS]
[SENDER PHONE] | [SENDER EMAIL]

[RECIPIENT NAME]
[RECIPIENT ADDRESS]

Re: Notice to Perform / Cure
Property: [ADDRESS]
Agreement: [TITLE], dated [DATE]

Dear [RECIPIENT]:

This Notice to Perform is issued under Section [NOTICE CLAUSE] of the Agreement.

1. Parties and Roles
Sender: [SENDER NAME], [ROLE]
Recipient: [RECIPIENT NAME], [ROLE]

2. Contractual Obligations
Relevant provisions: [SECTION NUMBERS AND SHORT QUOTES]

3. Default
On [DATE], [OBLIGATION] was due. Recipient failed to [PERFORMANCE] as required.
Details: [FACTS WITH AMOUNTS, DATES, REFERENCES]

4. Demand to Cure
You must cure by:
- [SPECIFIC ACTION 1]
- [SPECIFIC ACTION 2]
Total amount due: $[AMOUNT] (principal $[X], fees $[Y], interest $[Z] per [SECTION])

5. Cure Deadline
Cure must be completed by [TIME] [TIME ZONE] on [DATE], calculated under [CONTRACT/STATUTE].

6. Consequences of Non-Cure
If not cured, Sender may pursue remedies under the Agreement and applicable law, including [TERMINATION/ACCELERATION/DAMAGES/EVICITION/OTHER], consistent with Sections [X, Y] and [STATUTE IF APPLICABLE] [VERIFY].

7. Reservation of Rights
Sender reserves all rights and remedies, at law and in equity. No waiver is intended.

Sincerely,

[SIGNATURE]
[NAME]
[TITLE, IF APPLICABLE]

Certificate of Service
I certify that on [DATE], I served this Notice by [METHOD] to [ADDRESS/EMAIL], in accordance with the Agreement and applicable law.

[NAME]
```

## Guidelines

- Use only verified facts from the record; flag gaps before drafting.
- Quote only the necessary contract language and cite section numbers.
- Match service method and address to the notice clause unless statute requires more.
- If statutory requirements are uncertain, mark with `[VERIFY]` and request confirmation.
- Do not threaten remedies not authorized by contract or law.
- Avoid contact with represented parties; route through counsel if indicated.
- Keep tone professional and factual; no accusations or moral judgments.
