# Confidentiality and Severance Agreement

Drafts a litigation-resistant employment separation agreement that secures a comprehensive release of claims, protects proprietary information, and enforces post-employment obligations while satisfying OWBPA and state-law enforceability requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Employee details** — full legal name (as on tax docs), position, hire date, termination date, age (determines OWBPA requirements), state of work and residence
2. **Compensation data** — salary, accrued PTO balance, bonus structure/proration, equity (vested/unvested options, RSUs, exercise periods)
3. **Existing agreements** — offer letter, prior confidentiality/NDA/PIIA, non-compete, indemnification agreements
4. **Termination context** — individual vs. group RIF (affects OWBPA 21- vs. 45-day period), reason for separation, any documented performance/misconduct record
5. **Severance package terms** — payment amount/structure, COBRA subsidy duration, outplacement services, benefits continuation
6. **Jurisdiction** — state where employee worked and will reside post-employment (critical for non-compete enforceability)
7. **Employer structure** — full legal name, parent/affiliates to be covered by release and covenants

## Output Structure

### 1. Parties & Dates

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Employer legal name | As registered with Secretary of State; define "Company Affiliates" if releasing subsidiaries |
| Employee full name | Match tax documents exactly |
| Termination date | Last day of active work |
| Effective date | Day after 7-day OWBPA revocation period expires (age 40+) or date of signing (under 40) |

### 2. Severance Package

- **Cash payment**: amount, lump sum vs. payroll-schedule installments, first payment trigger (effective date)
- **Accrued PTO**: state-law mandatory payout rules (CA, CO, IL — not discretionary)
- **Bonus proration**: specify whether contingent on performance metrics; confirm eligibility cutoff
- **Equity**: treatment of unvested awards per plan docs; any acceleration or exercise extension offered
- **COBRA subsidy**: duration (typically 3–12 months), company vs. employee premium split
- **Outplacement**: vendor, duration, dollar cap
- **Property return**: deadline, specific items (laptop, badges, cards, mobile); right to remote-wipe personal devices
- **Payment conditioned on**: execution + expiration of revocation period without revocation + ongoing compliance
- **Clawback**: company may cease installments and seek return of paid amounts upon material breach (subject to state wage payment laws)

### 3. Release of Claims

**Covered parties**: employer + parent, subsidiaries, affiliates, predecessors, successors, and all officers, directors, employees, agents, attorneys

**Federal statutes to enumerate**:
- Title VII, ADA, ADEA, OWBPA, FMLA, FLSA, § 1981, ERISA (non-vested claims only)

**State law claims to enumerate**:
- Wrongful discharge, breach of contract, implied covenant, fraud, defamation, invasion of privacy, IIED/NIED, state wage/hour and discrimination statutes, whistleblower statutes

**Unknown claims waiver** (if applicable):
- CA: include Cal. Civil Code § 1542 verbatim waiver
- Other states with similar savings statutes: adapt accordingly

**Mandatory carve-outs** (non-waivable):
- Workers' compensation and unemployment benefits
- ERISA-vested plan benefits
- Corporate indemnification rights
- Claims arising after execution date
- Right to file EEOC/NLRB/SEC charges (waive monetary recovery only, except where prohibited)

#### OWBPA Compliance Checklist (age 40+)

- [ ] Specific reference to ADEA/OWBPA rights
- [ ] Advisement to consult an attorney
- [ ] 21-day consideration period (individual) or 45-day period (group RIF/exit incentive program)
- [ ] 7-day revocation right after signing; agreement not effective until expiration
- [ ] Revocation procedure: written notice, designated recipient, delivery method, deadline
- [ ] Separate acknowledgment block immediately above signature line

### 4. Confidentiality Obligations

**Definition of Confidential Information** (enumerate categories):
Trade secrets, customer/client lists, pricing, financials, marketing/sales strategies, business plans, product development, source code, employee data, supplier terms

**Duration**:
- Trade secrets: indefinite (per applicable UTSA/DTSA)
- Other confidential information: 5 years (or as appropriate)

**Return/destruction obligations**:
- Return all physical and electronic materials by specified deadline
- Delete from personal devices, personal email, cloud storage
- Written certification of return/destruction

**Exceptions** (standard enforceable carve-outs):
- Publicly available (not through employee's breach)
- Pre-employment possession (no restrictions)
- Independent post-employment development
- Legally compelled disclosure (with advance written notice to employer to seek protective order)

**DTSA Immunity Notice** (mandatory — include verbatim):
> "An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any Federal or State trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is made in confidence to a Federal, State, or local government official or to an attorney solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law… See 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)."

### 5. Mutual Non-Disparagement

| Party | Scope | Carve-outs |
|---|---|---|
| Employee | No negative/disparaging statements re: company, affiliates, officers, directors, products, services — includes social media (LinkedIn, Glassdoor, Indeed, etc.) | Truthful testimony; legally compelled statements; NLRA-protected concerted activity; EEOC/agency participation |
| Employer | Named individuals only (HR, direct supervisors, officers) — no negative statements to third parties re: employee | Same carve-outs as above |

> **NLRA Note**: Non-disparagement clause must not restrict statements about wages, hours, or working conditions among employees. Avoid overbroad language that could be read to chill Section 7 rights.

### 6. Restrictive Covenants

**Jurisdiction check first** — assess enforceability before drafting:

| State | Non-Compete Status |
|---|---|
| California | Void (Bus. & Prof. Code § 16600) — do not include |
| Colorado | Restricted to senior executives earning above statutory threshold (C.R.S. § 8-2-113) [VERIFY current threshold] |
| Massachusetts | Enforceable with limits; garden leave or equivalent consideration required |
| Minnesota | Banned for agreements after Jan. 1, 2023 [VERIFY] |
| All others | Enforce if reasonable in scope, duration, geography |

**Non-Compete** (where enforceable):
- Duration: 12–24 months max (role/industry-dependent)
- Geography: limited to areas employee actually served
- Activities: narrowly tailored to employer's actual business (not entire industry)
- Separate consideration: check if required beyond severance (varies by state)

**Customer Non-Solicitation**:
- Duration: 12–24 months
- Covered customers: only those with whom employee had material contact or about whom employee received confidential information in final 12–24 months

**Employee Non-Solicitation**:
- Duration: 12–24 months
- Covered employees: those employed during employee's final 12 months; limit to direct reports or working team if jurisdiction requires

**Blue-pencil/reformation clause**: authorize court to reduce unenforceable restrictions to maximum enforceable scope rather than void entirely

### 7. Dispute Resolution

- **Governing law**: state where employee worked (or employer HQ — verify substantial relationship)
- **Arbitration**: AAA or JAMS Employment Rules; employer bears arbitration fees
- **Class/collective waiver**: include if jurisdiction permits; note NLRA enforceability risk for covered employees
- **Injunctive relief carve-out**: either party may seek TRO/preliminary/permanent injunction in designated court for breach of confidentiality, non-disparagement, or restrictive covenants (irreparable harm presumption)
- **Designated courts**: state and federal courts in county of employer HQ or employee's work location; mutual consent to personal jurisdiction and venue

### 8. Cooperation & Transition

- Employee cooperates in pending/future litigation, investigations, regulatory proceedings involving matters within employee's knowledge
- Company reimburses reasonable out-of-pocket expenses; compensates at reasonable hourly rate for post-termination cooperation (unless subpoenaed)
- Transition assistance during notice period: scope, compensation, deadlines

### 9. Representations & Acknowledgments

Employee represents and warrants:
- [ ] Read and understands agreement; had opportunity to consult counsel
- [ ] Signing voluntarily, without duress
- [ ] All company property returned (or will be by deadline)
- [ ] No retained copies of confidential information
- [ ] No pending undisclosed claims, charges, or complaints
- [ ] Received all earned wages, commissions, and bonuses through termination date
- [ ] Understands severance exceeds any amounts owed for work performed
- [ ] Understands tax withholding applies; advised to consult tax advisor

### 10. Standard Boilerplate

- **Integration**: supersedes all prior negotiations/agreements on subject matter; does not supersede PIIA, equity plan docs, or indemnification agreements (list carve-outs)
- **Amendment**: written, signed by both parties only
- **Severability**: invalid provisions modified to minimum extent necessary or severed; remainder survives
- **Assignment**: employee may not assign; company may assign to successor/affiliate
- **No-waiver**: failure to enforce ≠ waiver
- **Non-admission**: agreement is not an admission of liability or wrongdoing by either party
- **Notices**: specify addresses, permitted delivery methods (certified mail, overnight courier, hand delivery)

## Guidelines

- **Never include a non-compete for California employees** — even with choice-of-law selecting another state, courts routinely refuse to enforce against CA residents
- **Verify current state thresholds** for non-compete eligibility (CO, WA, IL have income thresholds that change annually) [VERIFY]
- **OWBPA is strict liability** — miscalculating consideration or revocation periods voids the ADEA release; when in doubt, extend the period
- **DTSA immunity notice is mandatory** in any agreement limiting use of trade secrets — omission bars punitive damages and attorneys' fees in trade secret litigation
- **Clawback provisions** must comply with state wage payment laws (e.g., CA prohibits most deductions from wages; structure as separate contractual repayment obligation)
- **NLRB scrutiny**: post-*McLaren Macomb* (2023), overbroad non-disparagement and confidentiality provisions in severance agreements may be unlawful under NLRA — include explicit Section 7 carve-outs [VERIFY current NLRB enforcement posture]
- **Group RIF**: if 2+ employees are terminated as part of a program, attach OWBPA-required disclosure of decisional unit, eligibility criteria, and ages/job titles of all considered employees
- **California § 1542**: if employee has any California nexus, include verbatim waiver even if primary jurisdiction is another state
