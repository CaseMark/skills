# Non-Competition & Non-Solicitation Agreement (Seller)

Drafts a standalone restrictive covenant agreement for a seller principal ancillary to an asset purchase transaction, enforceability-optimized with blue-pencil provisions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Executed or near-final Asset Purchase Agreement** — need party names, business description, closing date, purchase price structure
2. **Identity of Covenantor** — individual seller principal or key person whose competition poses greatest risk
3. **Business description** — specific products, services, markets of the acquired business
4. **Geographic scope** — where the business operates, customer locations, market areas
5. **Desired restriction duration** — typically 2–5 years (3 years standard for middle-market)
6. **Applicable state law** — enforceability standards vary significantly by jurisdiction

## Output Structure

### Document Framework

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| **Title** | "NON-COMPETITION AND NON-SOLICITATION AGREEMENT" |
| **Preamble** | Effective date, "Covenantor" (seller principal), "Company" (buyer) — names must match APA exactly |
| **Recitals** | (A) Reference APA, identify acquired business with specificity; (B) Covenantor's execution is **material inducement and condition precedent** to closing and payment of purchase price |
| **Definitions** | Restricted Period, Restricted Territory, Competitive Business, Company Business |
| **Art. 1: Non-Competition** | Core covenant + carve-outs |
| **Art. 2: Non-Solicitation of Customers** | Customer + prospective customer restrictions |
| **Art. 3: Non-Solicitation of Employees** | Employee/contractor recruitment restrictions |
| **Art. 4: Acknowledgments** | Reasonableness, counsel opportunity, voluntary execution |
| **Art. 5: Remedies** | Injunctive relief, damages, tolling |
| **Art. 6: Severability & Reformation** | Blue-pencil/reformation clause |
| **Art. 7: General Provisions** | Governing law, venue, attorneys' fees, assignment, amendments, entire agreement, counterparts |
| **Signature Block** | Covenantor name, signature, date; notarization if required |

### Non-Competition Covenant — Required Elements

Prohibit Covenantor from directly or indirectly:
- Owning, managing, operating, controlling any Competitive Business
- Joining, participating in, consulting with, rendering services to any Competitive Business
- Being connected with any entity engaged in or **planning to engage in** Competitive Business

**"Competitive Business" definition**: Tie to specific products/services/activities of the acquired business — not generic industry terms.

**Scope**: Restrict competition with the business **as conducted by Company post-closing** (not limited to pre-closing operations).

**Standard carve-out**: Passive ownership of <5% of publicly traded company stock.

### Non-Solicitation of Customers — Required Elements

- Cover customers **and** prospective customers (active discussions within [12–24] months pre-closing)
- Prohibit direct and indirect solicitation (including through intermediaries)
- Purpose limitation: solicitation for providing competitive products/services
- Define "solicit" to include diverting, enticing, or attempting to divert

### Non-Solicitation of Employees — Required Elements

- Cover employees who transitioned from seller + employees hired by buyer post-closing
- Prohibit soliciting, recruiting, hiring, or encouraging termination
- Define "solicit" broadly: includes making known that Covenantor is hiring
- **Optional carve-out**: General public job postings not specifically targeted at Company employees (evaluate case-by-case)

### Restricted Period Definition

- Specify exact duration from closing date
- Typical ranges:

| Deal Size | Non-Compete | Non-Solicit (Customers) | Non-Solicit (Employees) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small (<$5M) | 2–3 years | 2–3 years | 2–3 years |
| Middle-market | 3 years | 3–4 years | 3 years |
| Large (>$50M) | 3–5 years | 4–5 years | 3–5 years |

- Different durations per covenant type are permissible and may improve enforceability

### Restricted Territory Definition

- Use clear geographic parameters: radius from locations, county/state boundaries, zip codes, or defined market areas
- Tie to **actual market area** of acquired business
- Fixed as of closing date (courts favor fixed territories over expanding ones)
- If multi-location business, define separate territories per location

### Remedies & Enforcement Provisions

```
INJUNCTIVE RELIEF TEMPLATE:

Covenantor acknowledges that any breach or threatened breach would cause
immediate and irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be
inadequate. Company shall be entitled to temporary, preliminary, and
permanent injunctive relief and specific performance, without:
  (a) posting bond or other security; and
  (b) proving actual damages.

Such equitable remedies are IN ADDITION TO all other remedies at law
or in equity, including recovery of monetary damages.
```

### Blue-Pencil / Reformation Clause

```
REFORMATION TEMPLATE:

If any court of competent jurisdiction determines that any covenant is
unenforceable as written, the court is hereby authorized and directed to
reform such covenant to the maximum extent enforceable under applicable
law, rather than declaring such covenant void or unenforceable in its
entirety.
```

### Additional Provisions Checklist

- [ ] **Tolling**: Restricted Period extends by duration of any breach
- [ ] **New employer notice**: Covenantor must disclose restrictions to future employers/associates
- [ ] **Non-disparagement**: Mutual or one-way, as negotiated
- [ ] **Governing law**: Choose state favorable to enforcement; match APA if possible
- [ ] **Exclusive venue**: Specify court and jurisdiction
- [ ] **Prevailing party attorneys' fees**: Deters frivolous defenses
- [ ] **Assignment**: Agreement binds and inures to successors and assigns of Company
- [ ] **Amendments**: Written, signed by both parties only
- [ ] **Entire agreement**: With respect to subject matter; does not supersede APA
- [ ] **Counterparts / electronic signatures**

## Guidelines

1. **Consideration**: The recitals MUST establish that this agreement is ancillary to the APA and supported by the purchase price as consideration — this is the foundation for enforceability in sale-of-business context
2. **State-specific enforceability**: Non-compete enforceability varies dramatically by state. California (void except narrow exceptions per Bus. & Prof. Code § 16601 [VERIFY] — sale-of-business exception applies), Oklahoma, North Dakota are highly restrictive. Draft to the governing law.
3. **Sale-of-business exception**: Most states enforce broader restrictions in sale-of-business contexts than employment contexts — leverage this
4. **Standalone document**: Agreement must be understandable and enforceable without constant reference to the APA, while cross-referencing it for context and consideration
5. **Internal consistency**: Defined terms, party names, and cross-references must be uniform throughout
6. **Do not over-restrict**: Overbroad restrictions risk judicial invalidation even with reformation clauses — tailor scope to actual business interests
7. **Match APA party names exactly** — any discrepancy creates ambiguity and enforcement risk
