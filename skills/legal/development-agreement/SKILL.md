# Development Agreement

Produces a comprehensive owner-developer development agreement for a real estate project with clear scope, timeline, and risk allocation.

## Prerequisites

1. Parties and authority: legal names, entity types, signatory authority, addresses.
2. Property details: legal description, parcel numbers, zoning, current condition.
3. Project scope: project type, size, quality standards, exclusions.
4. Delivery model: design-bid-build, design-build, developer-as-manager, etc.
5. Pricing model: fixed, GMP, cost-plus, fee structure, contingency.
6. Schedule: target dates, milestone list, critical path assumptions.
7. Permits and entitlements: responsibility split, known approvals needed.
8. Insurance and risk allocation: required coverages and limits.
9. Exhibit list: plans, specs, budget, schedule, forms, notices.

### Change Order Procedure

1. Written change request with scope, cost, and schedule impact.
2. Owner approval deadline and deemed response rule.
3. No work on change without signed change order except emergency.
4. Approved change adjusts price and schedule.

## Guidelines

- Use the property’s state for governing law and venue unless negotiated.
- If entitlements are required, allocate who leads zoning or variance efforts.
- Tie schedule remedies to excusable vs. inexcusable delays with notice rules.
- Liquidated damages must be reasonable estimates of actual loss.
- Retainage and lien waivers often have statutory form or timing rules [VERIFY].
- Make confidentiality survival explicit (3-5 years or trade secret standard).
- Ensure indemnity does not violate anti-indemnity statutes [VERIFY].
- If recording is contemplated, confirm local recording format and notarization rules [VERIFY].
