# Ex Parte Seizure Order

Drafts a court-ready ex parte seizure order authorizing immediate property seizure in IP or asset-based litigation, with findings, scope, bond, and due process provisions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case caption** — court, division, case number, all parties with procedural designations
2. **Factual record** — declarations, investigative reports, financial records, or correspondence establishing urgency (dissipation risk, destruction of evidence, irreparable harm)
3. **Property description** — physical descriptions, serial numbers, locations, valuations, or technical identifiers for digital/IP assets
4. **Legal basis** — governing statute (e.g., Lanham Act § 34(d), 15 U.S.C. § 1116(d) for trademark counterfeiting; FRCP 65 for TRO-based seizure; state analog if applicable)
5. **Proposed executing party** — U.S. Marshals, local law enforcement, court-appointed receiver, or private investigator with law enforcement escort
6. **Bond amount** — proposed security covering potential value of seized property plus business interruption damages

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption & Title
```
[COURT NAME], [DISTRICT/DIVISION]

[PLAINTIFF], Plaintiff,          Case No. [NUMBER]
v.
[DEFENDANT], Defendant.

EX PARTE SEIZURE ORDER
```

### 2. Recitals
- Date and time of application; parties present (none on behalf of defendant)
- Documents reviewed (motion, memorandum, declarations, exhibits)
- Finding that notice would defeat the purpose of relief (specify: risk of destruction/concealment)

### 3. Findings of Fact — Four-Factor Analysis

| Factor | Required Finding | Cite to Record |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| Likelihood of success on merits | Plaintiff has established prima facie [claim] | Decl. ¶ ___, Ex. ___ |
| Immediate irreparable injury | Delay will result in [destruction/dissipation/ongoing IP harm] | Decl. ¶ ___ |
| Inadequacy of other remedies | Monetary damages insufficient; ordinary process inadequate | Decl. ¶ ___ |
| Balance of hardships | Harm to plaintiff from denial exceeds harm to defendant from grant | Decl. ¶ ___ |

> For **Lanham Act counterfeiting** seizures, also find: (a) defendant would destroy/hide goods if notified; (b) plaintiff has not publicized the application; (c) matter is within court's subject-matter jurisdiction. [VERIFY: 15 U.S.C. § 1116(d)(4)(B)]

### 4. Seizure Provisions

**Property to be seized** (list with particularity):
- Category, physical description, serial/model numbers
- Location(s): address, unit, hours of access
- Digital assets: specify preservation method (forensic image), access limitations

**Executing parties authorized:**
- [ ] U.S. Marshal Service
- [ ] [Local law enforcement agency]
- [ ] Court-appointed receiver: [Name], [Contact]
- [ ] Plaintiff's counsel/agent (with law enforcement escort required: Y/N)

**Execution parameters:**
- Permissible hours: [e.g., 6:00 a.m. – 10:00 p.m.]
- Entry: Peaceable; forced entry only if [condition]
- Inventory: Itemized list; photographic documentation required
- Business disruption: Minimize interference with non-seized operations

**Effective:** [Date and time] through [Expiration date/event]

### 5. Chain of Custody & Storage
- Seized items delivered to: [Custodian/facility]
- Preservation conditions for perishable or digital assets: [specify]
- Access: Restricted to [parties] pending further order

### 6. Bond & Security
- Plaintiff shall post bond of $[AMOUNT] with the Clerk by [DATE/TIME] as condition of order taking effect
- Failure to post bond: Order automatically vacated

### 7. Post-Seizure Notice & Hearing
- Service on defendant: Within [24/48] hours of execution — copy of order, motion, all supporting papers
- Adversarial hearing: Scheduled for [DATE], no later than 14 days post-seizure
- Defendant may move to dissolve or modify upon showing of changed circumstances or wrongful seizure
- Counter-bond option: Defendant may post $[AMOUNT] to regain possession pending final resolution

### 8. Prohibitions & Safeguards
- No unnecessary damage to property during seizure
- No seizure of property outside the described categories
- Plaintiff/agents shall not review seized materials until further order

### 9. Signature Block
```
SO ORDERED this ___ day of __________, 20__.

_________________________________
Hon. [NAME]
United States District Judge / [State Court Judge]
[District/Court]
```

## Guidelines

- **Specificity is jurisdictional**: Vague property descriptions are grounds for quashing; err toward more detail
- **Lanham Act seizures**: Distinct statutory framework from FRCP 65 TROs — confirm which applies; Lanham Act orders require the additional findings at § 1116(d)(4)(B) [VERIFY]
- **Bond amount**: Should reflect realistic value of all seized property plus reasonably anticipated business interruption; underestimating exposes client to wrongful seizure liability
- **14-day hearing**: Due process floor — do not draft without scheduling or noting the requirement
- **State court**: Verify analogous state seizure statute and local rules; many states have divergent bond and notice requirements
- **No ex parte contact**: Order application must not be disclosed to opposing party or counsel prior to entry
- **Digital assets**: Include specific forensic preservation protocol; generic "seize computers" language is routinely challenged
- **Complexity**: Senior review required before filing; constitutional due process errors can result in sanctions or fee-shifting
