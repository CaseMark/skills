# Employee Separation and Release Agreement

Draft a severance-backed agreement that resolves employment-related claims and sets post-employment obligations.

## Prerequisites

1. Employer legal entity, address, and authorized signatory.
2. Employee name, address, title, start date, and separation date.
3. Separation reason and any disputed issues or pending claims.
4. Final pay details: wages through separation date, PTO/vacation payout policy, bonuses/commissions, expense reimbursements.
5. Severance consideration: amount, schedule, method, and any conditions.
6. Benefits plan details: COBRA notice timing and any employer subsidy.
7. Equity treatment: options/RSUs/units and governing plan documents.
8. Existing agreements: confidentiality/IP, noncompete, non-solicit, arbitration, handbooks.
9. Governing law, forum, and dispute resolution preference.
10. Employee age and whether part of a group termination (OWBPA data if applicable).
11. Company property inventory and return process.

## Output Structure / Process

1. Build a fact sheet from the prerequisites and confirm missing items.
2. Assemble the agreement in the section order below.
3. Insert optional clauses based on the triggers list.
4. Run the compliance checklist and correct any gaps.

**Section Order**

| Section | Required Content | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Opening & Recitals | Parties, effective date, separation context, supersedes prior discussions | Keep factual and non-admission |
| Separation Terms | Last day, final wages, PTO payout, commissions/bonuses, expense reimbursements | Tie to company policy and state law |
| Return of Property | Items, deadline, and certification of return | Include access revocation |
| Consideration | Severance amount/schedule, benefits subsidy, outplacement, equity treatment | Must exceed existing entitlements |
| Release of Claims | General release covering known/unknown claims | Include federal, state, local employment claims |
| Carve-outs | Non-waivable claims, agency charges, post-signing claims, unemployment, workers’ comp, indemnification | Preserve right to file charges |
| OWBPA (if age 40+) | ADEA-specific release with statutory timing | 21/45-day review + 7-day revocation |
| Confidentiality | Company confidential info + agreement terms | Add DTSA immunity notice if trade secrets |
| Non-Disparagement | Mutual, with protected activity carve-outs | Avoid restricting truthful testimony |
| Restrictive Covenants | Reaffirm prior agreements; add new if enforceable | Omit if state law prohibits |
| Cooperation | Reasonable cooperation with cost reimbursement | Define compensation rate if post-separation |
| Non-Admission | No liability admission | Standard |
| Dispute Resolution | Governing law, forum, arbitration if chosen | Preserve agency charge rights |
| General Provisions | Severability, entire agreement, amendments, waiver, notices, counterparts | Standard |
| Execution & Acknowledgments | Signatures, voluntary assent, counsel advisement | Include OWBPA acknowledgments |

**Optional Clauses (Insert Only If Triggered)**

- `Noncompete` if enforceable under governing law and role justifies it.
- `No rehire` if requested by employer policy.
- `Nondisclosure of terms` if standard in the jurisdiction.
- `409A compliance` if severance extends beyond short-term deferral.

**Template Blocks**

```text
OWBPA Notice (if age 40+):
Employee is advised to consult with an attorney before signing. Employee has [21/45] days to consider this Agreement and 7 days after signing to revoke by written notice to [contact]. This Agreement is effective only after the revocation period expires.

Release Carve-out:
This release does not waive: (a) rights to unemployment or workers’ compensation benefits, (b) claims arising after execution, (c) the right to file a charge or participate in an investigation with any government agency, although Employee waives personal recovery to the extent permitted by law, and (d) rights to indemnification or advancement under applicable bylaws or law.
```

## Guidelines

- Ensure severance is clearly identified as consideration beyond what is already owed.
- Confirm final pay timing and PTO payout rules under the governing state’s law.
- If using arbitration, preserve the right to file agency charges and avoid waiving statutory remedies that are non-waivable.
- Do not include noncompete or non-solicit clauses where state law prohibits or severely restricts them.
- Use plain language for consideration, timelines, and revocation instructions.
- Include DTSA whistleblower immunity notice when trade secrets are covered.
- For group terminations, attach OWBPA disclosure of decisional unit, job titles, and ages of eligible/ineligible employees.
- Avoid confidentiality or non-disparagement terms that restrict truthful testimony or protected activity.
- If releasing wage claims, confirm enforceability requirements in the governing jurisdiction.
