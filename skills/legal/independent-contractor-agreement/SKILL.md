# Independent Contractor Agreement

Drafts a results-oriented ICA that survives IRS and state misclassification scrutiny while protecting both parties on IP, confidentiality, and liability.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party details** — full legal names, entity type/state of formation, principal addresses, authorized signatories
2. **Scope of services** — specific deliverables, milestones, acceptance criteria, and start/end dates
3. **Compensation structure** — fixed fee, hourly, retainer, or milestone-based; reimbursable expenses
4. **Governing state** — determines which ABC test or economic-realities test applies
5. **IP sensitivity** — whether contractor will use pre-existing tools/IP or create novel work product
6. **Insurance requirements** — coverage types and minimums appropriate to engagement risk

## Output Structure

### 1. Parties & Recitals
- Full legal names, entity type, state of formation, principal address
- Confirm signatory authority (board resolution, operating agreement, etc. if entity)
- State engagement purpose in one sentence

### 2. Scope of Services
- Enumerate specific deliverables with measurable acceptance criteria
- Set milestones and hard deadlines
- **Explicitly state:** Contractor controls manner, means, method, and schedule of performance
- List exclusions to prevent scope creep

### 3. Compensation & Payment

| Element | Required Language |
|---|---|
| Rate/fee | Exact dollar amount or formula |
| Invoice cycle | Contractor submits invoices; due date (net 15/30) |
| Expenses | Itemize reimbursables; require receipts |
| Tax allocation | Contractor solely responsible for all federal/state/local taxes, including self-employment tax |
| No withholding | Hiring party will NOT withhold income tax, FICA, FUTA, or state UI |
| 1099-NEC | Hiring party will issue Form 1099-NEC; no W-2 |
| Late payment | Specify interest rate or penalty if applicable |

### 4. Independent Contractor Classification

Include affirmative statements that contractor:
- [ ] Retains full control over manner and means of performance
- [ ] Provides own tools, equipment, and workspace (note any exceptions)
- [ ] May work for other clients concurrently without restriction
- [ ] Bears risk of profit or loss on the engagement
- [ ] Is NOT entitled to employee benefits (health, retirement, PTO, workers' comp)
- [ ] Is NOT supervised or subject to set hours

> **Jurisdiction note:** Confirm applicable misclassification test before finalizing:
> - **Federal/IRS:** Common law control test (behavioral, financial, type-of-relationship factors)
> - **FLSA:** Economic realities test
> - **California, NJ, MA (and others):** ABC test — Part B (scope outside usual course of business) is highest risk; flag if contractor's work is core to hiring party's business

### 5. Term & Termination

| Trigger | Notice Required |
|---|---|
| Without cause | 15–30 days written notice |
| Material breach (uncured) | Written notice + 10-day cure period; immediate if uncurable |
| Insolvency/bankruptcy | Immediate |

Upon termination: contractor delivers all work in progress, returns property, submits final invoice; hiring party pays for work accepted through termination date.

### 6. Confidentiality

- **Definition:** Trade secrets, business strategies, customer/prospect lists, financial data, proprietary processes, and anything marked confidential or reasonably understood as such
- **Duration:** 2–5 years post-termination for general confidential information; indefinitely for trade secrets
- **Standard carve-outs:** publicly available, independently developed, rightfully received from third party, legally compelled disclosure (with prompt notice)
- **Return/destroy:** all confidential materials upon termination

### 7. Intellectual Property

```
Work Made for Hire + Assignment (Belt-and-Suspenders)
1. All deliverables are works made for hire under 17 U.S.C. § 101 [VERIFY scope].
2. To the extent any work does not qualify as WMFH, contractor hereby assigns all right,
   title, and interest (including all IP rights) worldwide and in perpetuity to hiring party.
3. Contractor will execute additional documents to perfect ownership.
4. Pre-existing contractor IP: listed on Exhibit A; contractor grants hiring party a
   non-exclusive license to use as embedded in deliverables.
5. Moral rights waiver for copyrightable creative works.
```

### 8. Representations & Warranties

**Contractor warrants:**
- Authority to enter agreement; no conflicting obligations
- Possesses required skills, licenses, and permits
- Work will meet professional/workmanlike standards
- Deliverables will not infringe third-party IP rights
- Compliance with all applicable laws

**Hiring party warrants:**
- Authority to enter agreement
- Will provide reasonable cooperation and timely information

### 9. Indemnification & Liability

| Party | Indemnifies Against |
|---|---|
| Contractor | Breach, negligence/willful misconduct, IP infringement by deliverables, bodily injury/property damage |
| Hiring party | Breach, negligence/willful misconduct |

- Cap liability at total fees paid under agreement (carve out: confidentiality/IP breaches)
- Exclude consequential, incidental, punitive damages (same carve-outs)

**Insurance (contractor must maintain):**

| Type | Minimum (adjust to engagement) |
|---|---|
| Commercial general liability | $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate |
| Professional liability (E&O) | $1M per claim |
| Workers' comp (if contractor has employees) | Statutory limits |

Require certificates of insurance; hiring party named additional insured on GL policy.

### 10. Dispute Resolution

- Good-faith negotiation → mediation (30 days) → binding arbitration
- Rules: AAA Commercial Arbitration Rules
- Seat: [Governing State]
- Arbitrator fees: split equally; each party bears own attorneys' fees unless prevailing-party clause is included
- Governing law: [State]; exclusive jurisdiction/venue for non-arbitrable claims: [State courts]

### 11. General Provisions

- [ ] Entire agreement / merger clause
- [ ] Amendments in writing, signed by both parties
- [ ] No assignment by contractor without consent; hiring party may assign to successors/affiliates
- [ ] Severability
- [ ] No waiver by failure to enforce
- [ ] Notices (email + overnight courier; specify addresses)
- [ ] Force majeure (exclude payment obligations)
- [ ] Counterparts; electronic signatures valid under ESIGN Act / UETA

### Signature Block
```
HIRING PARTY                        INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR

By: ___________________________     By: ___________________________
Name:                               Name:
Title:                              Title (if entity):
Date:                               Date:
```

## Guidelines

- **Never use supervision language** — avoid "direct," "supervise," "report to," "schedule set by" — flags misclassification
- **Non-competes:** Enforceable in limited states (TX, FL); void/restricted in CA, MN, ND, OK — assess before including
- **California:** ABC test Part B (core business work) is near-impossible to satisfy; recommend separate California counsel review
- **Gig/platform engagements:** Consider NLRB independent contractor rules [VERIFY current standard post-2024]
- **Multi-state work:** Identify all states where services performed; each may apply its own classification test
- **Do not guarantee classification:** Include disclaimer that agreement does not guarantee IC status; recommend client consult tax counsel
