# Insurance Defense Pre-Trial Report

Drafts a comprehensive, objective pre-trial report to an insurance carrier that provides honest risk assessment and actionable settlement/trial recommendations.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case file** — pleadings, discovery, depositions, prior status reports
2. **Trial date** — confirmed date, judge assignment, courtroom
3. **Medical records** — plaintiff's treatment history, IME results, pre-existing conditions
4. **Expert reports** — all retained experts (both sides), opinions, and qualifications
5. **Billing records** — fees/costs incurred to date, projected trial costs
6. **Settlement history** — demand/offer chronology, mediation results if any
7. **Surveillance/impeachment materials** — if obtained

## Output Structure

### Header Block

```
PRE-TRIAL REPORT

TO:      [Claims Manager Name and Title]
         [Insurance Company]
FROM:    [Attorney Name], [Firm Name]
DATE:    [Current Date]
RE:      [Insured] v. [Plaintiff]
         [Court and Case Number]
         Policy No: [Number]
         Claim No: [Number]
         Date of Loss: [Date]
         Trial Date: [Date] - [Time]
```

### Report Sections

Draft each section below. Be direct and objective throughout — acknowledge weaknesses, quantify risks, and avoid over-promising.

| # | Section | Key Content |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | **Introduction** | Purpose, timeline (~60 days out), developments since last report |
| 2 | **Trial Logistics & Deadlines** | Trial date, duration estimate, judge, remaining deadlines (experts, discovery, settlement conference) |
| 3 | **Factual Summary** | Chronological, evidence-supported facts; flag disputed facts explicitly |
| 4 | **Venue & Jury Analysis** | Historical verdict data, jury pool demographics, plaintiff-friendly vs. defense-friendly reputation, comparable recent verdicts |
| 5 | **Judge & Opposing Counsel** | Judge's tendencies, evidentiary ruling patterns, prior relevant rulings; opposing counsel's trial record, strengths, settlement tendencies |
| 6 | **Costs & Fees** | Fees/costs incurred to date; projected trial costs (attorney time, experts, exhibits, post-trial); provide totals so carrier has no billing surprises |
| 7 | **Motions Practice** | Completed motions and their impact; pending motions (especially MSJ — likelihood of success, appeal implications); planned MILs with strategic purpose |
| 8 | **Liability Analysis** | Elements plaintiff must prove with evidence strength for each; comparative negligence evidence and likely reduction percentage; affirmative defenses and likelihood of success |
| 9 | **Damages Analysis** | Organize per table below |
| 10 | **Expert Testimony** | Plaintiff's and defense experts — qualifications, expected testimony, credibility, impeachment opportunities |
| 11 | **Party Presentation** | Plaintiff credibility/likability, deposition performance, impeachment opportunities; defendant/insured witness quality and jury impression |
| 12 | **Surveillance & Impeachment** | Surveillance footage quality and inconsistencies with claims; social media evidence; medical record contradictions; witness statement conflicts |
| 13 | **Trial Outcome Predictions** | Defense verdict likelihood (percentage with reasoning); verdict range if plaintiff prevails (low/middle/high with factors) |
| 14 | **Settlement Recommendations** | Current posture (demand, last offer, negotiation trajectory); recommended settlement range with reasoning; timing and alternative approaches |
| 15 | **Final Recommendations** | Overall assessment, primary risks/opportunities, cost-benefit analysis, clear trial-vs-settle recommendation with next steps and decision timeline |

## Guidelines

- **Objectivity is paramount** — this report may be reviewed by multiple levels of claims management and could be discoverable in bad faith litigation. Every statement must be accurate and defensible.
- **Do not sugar-coat risks** — acknowledge defense weaknesses honestly. Carriers need accurate information for business decisions.
- **Do not push settlement unnecessarily** — if the case is defensible and the risk is worth it, say so. If it is not, say that clearly too.
- **Be consistent with prior reports** — if the recommendation has changed, explain what new facts drove the change. Carriers distrust counsel who call a case defensible for months then reverse course at trial.
- **Quantify where possible** — percentages for liability outcomes, dollar ranges for verdicts, cost projections for trial.
- **Flag coverage issues** — note anything that could create coverage disputes or bad faith exposure.
- **Mark jurisdictional specifics** — note comparative negligence thresholds, damage caps, or procedural rules specific to the forum state.

### Final Review Checklist

- [ ] All case identifying information (policy, claim, case numbers) included
- [ ] Trial logistics and remaining deadlines covered
- [ ] Factual summary is objective and evidence-supported
- [ ] Venue/jury analysis provided with comparable verdict data
- [ ] Judge and opposing counsel assessed
- [ ] Complete cost analysis (incurred + projected)
- [ ] Liability analysis addresses each element and defense
- [ ] Damages fully analyzed with ranges
- [ ] Expert testimony assessed for both sides
- [ ] Surveillance/impeachment evidence catalogued
- [ ] Outcome predictions are realistic with supporting reasoning
- [ ] Settlement recommendations are specific with dollar ranges
- [ ] Weaknesses in defense case are acknowledged
- [ ] Recommendations are actionable with decision timeline
