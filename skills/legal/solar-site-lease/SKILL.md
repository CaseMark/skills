# Solar Site Lease Agreement

Draft a long-term ground lease for siting, constructing, operating, and decommissioning a solar energy facility.

## Prerequisites

1. Parties and authority: legal names, entity types, signatories, notice addresses.
2. Property data: legal description, parcel IDs, survey, acreage, title report, existing easements.
3. Project scope: capacity (MW), site plan, storage/BESS, interconnection point, access roads.
4. Business terms: option period, lease term, renewals, rent model, escalation, bonus or revenue share.
5. Jurisdiction inputs: zoning and setback rules, environmental and stormwater requirements, decommissioning rules.
6. Financing constraints: lender collateral assignment, step-in rights, SNDA requirements.
7. Insurance and tax positions: target limits, property tax treatment, REC ownership position.

Draft the agreement in numbered articles using this order.

1. Parties, recitals, and defined terms.
2. Premises and exhibits; legal description and survey.
3. Option and site investigation rights.
4. Lease commencement and conditions precedent.
5. Lease term and renewal mechanics.
6. Rent, escalation, and payment procedures.
7. Use rights and permitted improvements.
8. Access, easements, and utility interconnection.
9. Construction standards and site controls.
10. Operations, maintenance, and vegetation management.
11. Compliance with laws, permits, and environmental duties.
12. Ownership of improvements and personal property status.
13. Insurance, indemnity, and limitation of liability.
14. Taxes, assessments, incentives, and RECs.
15. Assignment, sublease, and lender protections.
16. Casualty, condemnation, and restoration.
17. Defaults, notices, and cure periods.
18. Termination rights and decommissioning.
19. Recording; memorandum of lease form.
20. Miscellaneous: confidentiality, notices, amendments, counterparts.

Use this decommissioning and security template and fill all placeholders.

```text
Decommissioning. Within {12} months after termination or expiration, Lessee shall
remove all solar equipment, foundations, cables, and improvements to a depth of
{X} feet, restore grade, and reseed disturbed areas, subject to applicable law.

Security. Beginning {Year/Trigger}, Lessee shall maintain a bond or letter of credit
in the amount of {$$} (adjusted every {N} years) to secure decommissioning, payable
to Lessor, with {Lessor approval} of issuer and form.
```

Include exhibits as separate attachments.

- Exhibit A: Legal description and survey.
- Exhibit B: Site plan and easement map.
- Exhibit C: Form of memorandum of lease.
- Exhibit D: Insurance requirements and certificates.
- Exhibit E: Decommissioning plan (if required by jurisdiction).

Before finalizing, confirm the checklist.

- [ ] Legal description matches the survey and title report.
- [ ] Option period and drop-dead COD date are defined.
- [ ] Rent schedule and escalation are unambiguous.
- [ ] Decommissioning security meets local ordinance or county guidelines.
- [ ] REC ownership and tax responsibility are explicitly allocated.
- [ ] Assignment and lender step-in rights are included.
- [ ] Recording requirements and notary blocks are correct.

## Guidelines

- Use the governing law of the state where the property is located.
- Tie commencement and extensions to objective milestones and permits.
- Avoid guaranteeing tax credits or incentives; state no assurance.
- Allocate property tax and PILOT obligations with clear reimbursement mechanics.
- Provide Lessor access and inspection rights with reasonable notice.
- Preserve Lessor compatible use rights if intended (e.g., grazing) with carve-outs.
- Use realistic cure periods (monetary and non-monetary) and define notice methods.
- Require compliance with zoning, setbacks, glare/noise, stormwater, and environmental permits.
- Record only a memorandum of lease to keep financial terms confidential when appropriate.
- Mark any jurisdiction-specific statutory citations with [VERIFY] if not confirmed.
