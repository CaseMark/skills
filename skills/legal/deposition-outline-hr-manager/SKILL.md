# Deposition Outline — HR Manager (Employment Litigation)

Creates a comprehensive deposition outline for examining the defendant employer's HR manager in contested employment matters.

## Prerequisites

1. **Operative pleadings** — claims, defenses, disputed issues
2. **Personnel file** — applications, evaluations, disciplinary records, termination documents
3. **HR policies** — employee handbook, anti-discrimination policy, complaint procedures, progressive discipline
4. **Communications** — emails, texts, investigation notes involving the HR manager
5. **Prior testimony** — any declarations, interrogatory responses, or prior deposition testimony
6. **Comparator information** — similarly situated employees, their treatment, and outcomes
7. **Investigation files** — internal complaint reports, investigation summaries

## Outline Structure

### I. Background and Foundation

- Full name, title, reporting structure, tenure
- HR education, training, certifications (PHR/SPHR)
- Role in hiring, discipline, and termination decisions
- Familiarity with company policies and procedures
- **Lock in**: "You're the person most knowledgeable about HR policies at [Company]?"

### II. Policies and Procedures

- Identify and authenticate the employee handbook version in effect
- Anti-discrimination and anti-harassment policies
- Complaint and investigation procedures
- Progressive discipline policy
- Termination approval process
- **Pin down deviations**: "Was this policy followed in [plaintiff]'s case?"

### III. Plaintiff's Employment History

- Hiring decision — who, why, qualifications assessed
- Job duties, responsibilities, performance expectations
- Performance evaluations — ratings, comments, trajectory
- Commendations, awards, raises, promotions
- Prior discipline (if any) — when, basis, who decided
- **Establish competence baseline** before adverse events

### IV. The Triggering Events

- When HR first became involved and how
- Protected activity timeline (complaint, leave request, whistleblowing)
- HR's response and actions taken
- Communications with decision-makers about plaintiff
- **Temporal proximity**: establish timeline between protected activity and adverse action

### V. Investigation (if applicable)

- Who investigated, methodology, timeline
- Witnesses interviewed
- Documents reviewed
- Findings and conclusions
- Credibility assessments — basis for believing/disbelieving witnesses
- **Probe thoroughness**: compare investigation of plaintiff vs. others

### VI. Termination Decision

- Who made the decision (was HR the decider or advisor?)
- Stated reasons — get them locked in precisely
- Documents supporting the decision
- When the decision was made vs. when communicated
- Were alternatives considered (PIP, transfer, demotion)?
- **Shifting explanations**: compare to interrogatory answers, position statements, prior statements

### VII. Comparative Treatment

- Identify similarly situated employees outside protected class
- Same supervisor, same position, same time period
- Comparable conduct or performance issues
- Different outcome — and who decided
- **Document comparators systematically**:

| Employee | Protected Class | Conduct | Outcome | Decision-Maker |
|----------|----------------|---------|---------|----------------|
| Plaintiff | [class] | [conduct] | Terminated | [name] |
| Comparator A | [class] | [comparable] | [outcome] | [name] |

### VIII. Pattern and Practice

- Prior complaints of discrimination/retaliation in the department
- HR's handling of prior complaints
- EEOC charges or lawsuits involving the same decision-makers
- Training on anti-discrimination (when, content, attendance)

### IX. Document Authentication

- Mark and authenticate key exhibits:
  - Personnel file documents
  - Investigation files
  - Email communications
  - Policy documents
  - Termination approval documentation

### X. Impeachment Preparation

- Compare testimony to:
  - Interrogatory responses
  - EEOC position statement
  - Documents in the personnel file
  - Plaintiff's version of events
- Note inconsistencies for potential impeachment at trial

## Examination Techniques

- **Open-ended first**: "Tell me everything you know about…" (lock in the narrative)
- **Then narrow**: specific questions with document references
- **Commit-credit-confront**: get commitment → show favorable document → confront with inconsistency
- **Exhaust "I don't recall"**: follow up with specific documents to refresh recollection
- **Silence**: let the witness fill uncomfortable pauses

## Guidelines

- Start with background to build rapport before adversarial topics
- Lock in the stated termination reason early — before showing contradictory evidence
- Track which documents were in the file at termination vs. added later
- Note any documents missing from the file that should be there
- Time allocation: ~60% on termination decision and comparators, ~20% on policies/investigation, ~20% on background/authentication
