# Expert Report on Damages

Produces a Daubert-compliant expert damages report for U.S. commercial litigation with defensible methodology, transparent calculations, and full FRCP 26(a)(2)(B) disclosure.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case materials** — pleadings defining damages scope, interrogatory responses with financial data, deposition transcripts
2. **Financial records** — historical income statements, balance sheets, tax returns, sales records, general ledger (3–5 years pre-wrongdoing minimum)
3. **Contracts/agreements** — governing relationship, any damages caps or limitations of liability
4. **Expert CV** — credentials, certifications, prior testimony list
5. **Industry/market data** — benchmarks, comparable company data, economic studies for baseline or valuation support
6. **Discovery status** — identify documentation gaps upfront; missing records require disclosed limitations or range opinions

## Output Structure

### 1. Title Page & Engagement Scope
- Retaining party, engagement date, specific questions presented
- Compensation statement: fees not contingent on outcome or amount
- Scope boundaries — what was and was not examined

### 2. Expert Qualifications (FRE 702 / Daubert)

| Element | Detail Required |
|---|---|
| Education | Degrees, institutions, graduation dates |
| Certifications | CPA, CFE, CFA, ABV, CVA (as applicable) |
| Experience | Years in damages/forensic/valuation work; types of matters |
| Prior testimony | Number of cases, deposition vs. trial, courts/jurisdictions |
| Publications/presentations | Peer-reviewed articles, conference papers, professional committee service |

### 3. Materials Reviewed
Itemized list of all documents, data, and testimony considered. Every item must be traceable to a source.

### 4. Assumptions & Limitations

| # | Assumption / Limitation | Basis for Reasonableness |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Accuracy of financial statements provided | Prepared by third-party accountants; management representations |
| 2 | Continuation of pre-breach business trends | Historical performance data |
| 3 | [Case-specific assumption] | [Support] |

Flag unavailable records, incomplete discovery, or destroyed documents. Explain how each gap affects certainty or requires a range opinion.

### 5. Factual Background
Objective chronological narrative from case documents:
- Parties, business relationship (contract terms, duration, economic significance to each side)
- Alleged wrongful conduct: start date, duration, specific acts/omissions
- Damages accrual period with key milestone dates
- Industry/market context: growth rates, competitive dynamics, regulatory changes, economic cycles
- Alternative causes considered: management decisions, market downturns, competitor actions, operational issues unrelated to defendant

### 6. Methodology

#### Damages Type Selection

| Type | When Applicable |
|---|---|
| Lost profits ("but for" analysis) | Lost business revenue/income caused by breach or tort |
| Diminution in value | Permanent impairment to asset or business value |
| Disgorgement / unjust enrichment | Defendant's profits derived from wrongful conduct |
| Reasonable royalty | IP infringement; licensing disputes |
| Cost to cure | Remediation cost for defective performance |

State which measure applies and why, with reference to the operative legal theory.

#### Daubert Reliability Factors (Address Each)

1. **Testability** — methodology can be tested or falsified
2. **Peer review** — published in recognized literature or professional standards
3. **Error rate** — known or potential error rate is acceptable and disclosed
4. **Standards** — governed by AICPA, NACVA, NACFE, or equivalent professional standards [VERIFY current editions]
5. **General acceptance** — accepted within forensic accounting/economics community

#### Lost Profits Framework
1. Establish pre-wrongdoing baseline: gross margin, operating margin, net margin over 3–5 historical periods
2. Project "but for" revenues/profits using trend analysis, regression, or market-based projections
3. Subtract actual results during the damages period
4. Apply adjustments: mitigation efforts, avoided costs, contingency discounts, tax effects

#### Diminution in Value Framework
Apply one or more valuation approaches consistently pre- and post-event:

| Approach | Common Techniques | Key Inputs |
|---|---|---|
| Market | Comparable company analysis, precedent transactions | Revenue/EBITDA multiples |
| Income | DCF, capitalization of earnings | Discount rate, growth rate, normalized earnings |
| Cost | Replacement/reproduction cost | Asset inventory, depreciation schedules |

Diminution = Pre-event value − Post-event value. Use same methodology for both dates.

### 7. Damages Calculations

Structure per category:

```
[Category Name]
  ├── Data sources
  ├── Step-by-step calculation with disclosed formulas
  ├── Adjustments and rationale
  └── Subtotal

Prejudgment Interest (if applicable)
  ├── Rate (statutory / contractual): ___
  ├── Interest period: ___ to ___
  └── Interest subtotal: $___

```

- All numbers must trace to source documents or disclosed assumptions
- Offset overlapping categories explicitly to avoid double recovery
- Include supporting schedules formatted for replication by another expert

### 8. Opinions & Conclusions

State each opinion to "a reasonable degree of [economic / financial] certainty" per jurisdiction convention. Format:

> **Opinion 1:** [Specific figure or range] for [category], based on [methodology].  
> **Opinion 2:** Under alternative scenario [X], damages are [Y].

Identify factors that could cause actual damages to differ. Confine opinions strictly to expertise area and available evidence.

### 9. Exhibits & Appendices Index

- [ ] Expert CV with full testimony history (FRCP 26(a)(2)(B) required)
- [ ] Financial statements analyzed (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow)
- [ ] Tax returns for relevant periods
- [ ] Calculation workpapers with formulas and cell-level traceability
- [ ] Industry benchmark and market data
- [ ] Deposition excerpts and witness statements relied upon
- [ ] Contracts and key business documents
- [ ] Authoritative literature and professional standards cited

## Guidelines

- **FRCP 26(a)(2)(B)**: Report must contain complete opinions, all bases and reasons, facts/data considered, exhibits, qualifications, prior testimony list (4 years), and compensation disclosure
- **Daubert / FRE 702**: Every methodological choice must satisfy reliability, sufficient facts, and appropriate application; anticipate Daubert motion on methodology and data sufficiency
- **Frye jurisdictions**: Confirm whether the applicable court uses Daubert or Frye "general acceptance" standard [VERIFY by jurisdiction before finalizing]
- **No advocacy**: Tone is objective and educational; acknowledge limitations, alternative scenarios, and opposing interpretations
- **Replicability**: All calculations must be independently reproducible from disclosed materials and assumptions
- **Tax treatment**: Address whether lost profits award is taxable and whether gross-up is appropriate [VERIFY jurisdiction-specific treatment]
- **Mitigation**: Confirm plaintiff's duty to mitigate; deduct actual mitigation from damages
- **No double recovery**: Explicitly document offsets where damages categories overlap
