# Environmental Impact Statement (EIS)

Drafts a NEPA-compliant EIS analyzing environmental consequences of a proposed federal action across all required resource categories and alternatives.

## Prerequisites

1. **Project identification** — official project title, lead agency, cooperating agencies, and NEPA coordinator contact
2. **Proposed action description** — location, timing, phases, scale, legal authority, required permits/approvals
3. **Purpose and need statement** — underlying problem, regulatory/policy mandates driving federal action
4. **Alternatives under consideration** — including any alternatives screened out and reasons for dismissal
5. **Baseline data** — environmental resource inventories for the affected area (physical, biological, cultural, socioeconomic)
6. **Consultation records** — scoping comments, agency/tribal consultation letters, Section 106 / Section 7 documentation
7. **Jurisdiction** — US federal; identify applicable agency NEPA implementing procedures

## Output Structure

### 1. Project Overview

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Project Title | Official name of proposed action |
| Lead Agency | Agency + office/division + NEPA coordinator |
| Cooperating Agencies | List with designated roles |
| Legal Authority | Statutory basis for agency action |
| Decision to be Made | Specific approval, permit, or authorization at issue |

### 2. Purpose and Need

- State the problem or opportunity necessitating federal action
- Cite legal, administrative, or policy mandates
- Be specific enough to define the range of reasonable alternatives without predetermining outcome
- Address timing constraints if relevant

### 3. Alternatives Analysis

**Required alternatives:**

| Alternative | Description | Key Distinguishing Features |
|---|---|---|
| No Action | Future conditions under current management/trends (not just present state) | Baseline benchmark per 40 C.F.R. § 1502.14 |
| Proposed Action | Full description: location, phases, methods, ancillary facilities | Agency's preferred alternative |
| Alternative B–N | Different location/technology/scale/approach | Must represent genuine range, not variations on a single outcome |
| Alternatives Eliminated | Brief description + reason for dismissal | Infeasibility, outside agency jurisdiction, fails purpose and need |

### 4. Affected Environment

Organize by resource category. Include only detail necessary to understand conditions and trends:

- **Physical**: geology, topography, soils, minerals, paleontological resources
- **Water**: surface hydrology, groundwater, water quality, floodplains, wetlands, Wild & Scenic Rivers
- **Air**: attainment status for criteria pollutants, applicable SIP requirements
- **Biological**: vegetation communities, wildlife habitat, federally listed threatened/endangered species, critical habitat, essential fish habitat, migratory bird habitat, wildlife corridors
- **Cultural**: archaeological sites, historic structures, TCP, NRHP-listed/eligible properties, tribal significance areas; note subsurface discovery potential
- **Human environment**: land use, demographics, EJ communities, recreation, visual resources, noise, transportation, subsistence uses, treaty rights

### 5. Environmental Consequences

For each resource category × each alternative:

| Impact Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| Direct | Same time and place as the action |
| Indirect | Later in time or farther in distance, but reasonably foreseeable |
| Cumulative | Incremental contribution combined with past, present, and reasonably foreseeable future actions |

**Per resource, address:**
- [ ] Intensity, duration, geographic extent
- [ ] Short-term vs. long-term; temporary vs. permanent
- [ ] Beneficial and adverse effects
- [ ] Irreversible/irretrievable resource commitments
- [ ] Significance criteria/thresholds and rationale for significance determinations
- [ ] Scientific uncertainty or incomplete information; whether gap is essential to reasoned choice (40 C.F.R. § 1502.22)
- [ ] Conflicts with federal/state/local land use plans or applicable regulatory requirements

### 6. Cumulative Impacts Analysis

- Define geographic and temporal scope with rationale
- Identify other past, present, and reasonably foreseeable future actions contributing to cumulative effects
- Assess whether combined effects exceed resource capacity or cross significance thresholds
- Address resource condition trends and whether the action improves, maintains, or degrades them

### 7. Mitigation Measures

Organize by resource category; for each measure:

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Measure | Specific, enforceable description |
| Alternative(s) | Applicable to which alternatives |
| Type | Avoid / minimize / rectify / reduce / compensate |
| Implementation | Who, when, how |
| Monitoring | How effectiveness is verified; adaptive management triggers |
| Residual Impact | Remaining impact after mitigation and why further mitigation is infeasible |

Distinguish measures within agency authority from those requiring voluntary third-party action.

### 8. Consultation and Coordination

- **Public involvement**: scoping process, meetings, comment periods; how input shaped alternatives and analysis scope
- **Draft EIS comments**: identification of substantive comments and how Final EIS responds
- **Tribal consultation**: government-to-government process, traditional knowledge, treaty rights, culturally significant resources
- **Section 106 (NHPA)**: status and findings
- **Section 7 (ESA)**: biological assessment/opinion status
- **Other agency coordination**: state/local agencies, other federal consultations

## Guidelines

- **Plain language required**: accessible to non-technical public while maintaining legal sufficiency; define technical terms; use active voice (40 C.F.R. § 1502.8)
- **Hard look standard**: analysis must reflect genuine, rigorous consideration of environmental effects; avoid conclusory statements unsupported by evidence
- **No-action must be forward-looking**: project future conditions, not just describe present state
- **Range of alternatives**: courts scrutinize whether alternatives represent a genuine range or are rigged toward a predetermined outcome
- **Significance determinations**: address both context and intensity factors per 40 C.F.R. § 1508.27 [VERIFY current CFR section after 2020 CEQ rule revisions]
- **CEQ 2020/2024 rule changes**: confirm applicable version of 40 C.F.R. Parts 1500–1508 — CEQ regulations were revised in 2020 and 2024; applicability depends on project initiation date [VERIFY]
- **EJ analysis**: required under E.O. 12898; identify disproportionate impacts on low-income and minority communities
- **Document all data sources**: cite scientific literature, regulatory standards, monitoring data; unsupported assertions are litigation vulnerability
- **Tables and figures**: use to present complex spatial, quantitative, or comparative information efficiently
