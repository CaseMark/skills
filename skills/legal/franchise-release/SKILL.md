# Release on Termination or Expiration of Franchise Agreement

Drafts an execution-ready General Release for use when a franchise relationship ends by expiration or termination, balancing broad franchisor protection with state-law enforceability.

## Prerequisites

1. **Franchise agreement** — full executed copy with notice clauses, governing law, and any required release language
2. **Termination/expiration details** — date, basis (natural expiration vs. early termination), and any cure history
3. **Party identification** — franchisee entity, all individual principals/guarantors, franchisor corporate family
4. **Dispute history** — any unresolved claims, pending amounts, or negotiated carve-outs
5. **Governing law jurisdiction** — for unknown-claims waiver selection and franchise-protective statute review

## Output Structure

### Document Sections

| # | Section | Key Requirements |
|---|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Title & Parties** | "GENERAL RELEASE" — identify Releasor (franchisee entity + all principals/guarantors) and Releasee (franchisor + parent, subs, affiliates, officers, directors, employees, agents, successors, assigns) |
| 2 | **Recitals** | (A) Reference franchise agreement by date/parties, state termination vs. expiration and date; (B) Acknowledge release required as condition of termination/expiration; (C) Parties wish to fully resolve all matters |
| 3 | **Operative Release** | Cover "any and all claims, demands, actions, causes of action, suits, debts, obligations, liabilities, damages, losses, costs, and expenses … whether known or unknown, suspected or unsuspected, fixed or contingent" arising from the franchise agreement, relationship, or operation of the business — under contract, tort, statute, common law, and equity; specifically reference franchise relationship laws, antitrust, consumer protection |
| 4 | **Unknown Claims Waiver** | Jurisdiction-tailored; if CA nexus, quote Cal. Civ. Code § 1542 in full and expressly waive; for other states, include equivalent provision or common-law waiver |
| 5 | **Covenant Not to Sue** | Independent promise not to commence, maintain, or participate in any action based on released claims |
| 6 | **Acknowledgments** | Right to counsel advised; voluntary execution; no duress; valuable consideration received; full understanding of legal effect |
| 7 | **Carve-Outs** | See table below |
| 8 | **Governing Law & Severability** | Match franchise agreement; reformation clause for unenforceable provisions |
| 9 | **Signature Blocks** | Franchisee entity (with title/authority), each individual principal/guarantor, optional franchisor acceptance; date lines; notarization space if required |

### Carve-Out Checklist

Narrow each exception to avoid undermining protective scope:

- [ ] Franchisor's post-termination purchase obligations (inventory/equipment)
- [ ] Franchisee indemnification rights for third-party claims
- [ ] Statutory rights that cannot be released by law in the governing jurisdiction
- [ ] Any specifically negotiated separate resolution items

### Post-Termination Obligations to Preserve

The release must explicitly state it does NOT release franchisee from:

- [ ] Non-competition covenants
- [ ] Confidentiality obligations
- [ ] De-identification of former franchise location
- [ ] Return of proprietary materials and confidential information
- [ ] Payment of amounts owed under the franchise agreement

### Cal. Civ. Code § 1542 Waiver Template

Use when California law governs or franchisee has CA nexus:

```
Releasor acknowledges that they have been advised of, and hereby
expressly waive and relinquish, all rights and benefits under
California Civil Code Section 1542, which provides:

  "A GENERAL RELEASE DOES NOT EXTEND TO CLAIMS THAT THE CREDITOR
  OR RELEASING PARTY DOES NOT KNOW OR SUSPECT TO EXIST IN HIS OR
  HER FAVOR AT THE TIME OF EXECUTING THE RELEASE AND THAT, IF
  KNOWN BY HIM OR HER, WOULD HAVE MATERIALLY AFFECTED HIS OR HER
  SETTLEMENT WITH THE DEBTOR OR RELEASED PARTY."

Releasor acknowledges they may later discover claims or facts
that would have materially affected this decision and nevertheless
intend this release to cover all claims, known or unknown.
```

## Guidelines

- **Unilateral vs. mutual**: Default to unilateral (franchisee → franchisor). If mutual (negotiated terminations), make the franchisor's release narrower and preserve enforcement of all post-termination covenants.
- **Consideration**: Identify specific consideration if beyond what the franchise agreement already requires; otherwise reference the contractual obligation itself as the basis.
- **Franchise-protective jurisdictions**: Review enforceability limits in CA, WI, MN, IL, WA, and other states with franchise relationship statutes — some void broad prospective waivers of statutory franchise rights. [VERIFY]
- **Target length**: 3–5 pages, execution-ready.
- **Placeholders**: Use `[BRACKETED TEXT]` for any missing party names, dates, or jurisdiction-specific provisions, with a note on what to insert.
- **Document extraction**: If user uploads franchise agreement or correspondence, extract concrete details (parties, dates, governing law, required release language) rather than using generic placeholders.
