# Mining and Land Use Regulatory Summary

Produces an actionable legal and regulatory summary for mining and land use matters, identifying permits, compliance obligations, property interests, and risks.

## Prerequisites

1. **Project documents** — permits, environmental impact assessments, regulatory correspondence, compliance reports
2. **Property records** — mineral rights documentation, land use agreements, easements, title records
3. **Applicable regulations** — federal, state, and local statutes governing the project jurisdiction
4. **Stakeholder information** — regulatory agencies involved, adjacent landowners, tribal governments if applicable

## Guidelines

- Cite all statutes and regulations in Bluebook format; mark unverified citations with `[VERIFY]`
- When regulatory requirements are ambiguous or discretionary, flag the uncertainty and recommend clarification steps (pre-application meetings, advisory opinions, regulatory guidance requests)
- Clearly label information gaps with `[NEEDS INVESTIGATION]` and specify what is needed
- Use plain language alongside legal terminology — the audience includes non-lawyer stakeholders
- Jurisdiction matters: do not assume federal law preempts without analysis; state and local requirements frequently impose additional obligations
- Reclamation bonding amounts and forms vary significantly by jurisdiction — always verify current requirements
- Tribal consultation is mandatory under federal law for projects with federal nexus; some states impose independent consultation obligations
