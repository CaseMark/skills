# Fiscal Sponsorship Agreement

Drafts a legally sound Fiscal Sponsorship Agreement that preserves the sponsor's 501(c)(3) status and donor deductibility while giving the sponsored project operational clarity.

## Prerequisites

1. **Fiscal sponsor details** — full legal name, state of incorporation, EIN, 501(c)(3) determination letter date
2. **Project details** — project name, mission statement, planned activities, authorized representative(s)
3. **Financial terms** — administrative fee percentage, disbursement thresholds requiring pre-approval
4. **Term preferences** — fixed term or at-will; notice period for voluntary termination (30–90 days)
5. **IP allocation decision** — sponsor-owned, project-retained, or shared with license-back
6. **Fund disposition preference** — named successor 501(c)(3) or sponsor-selected on termination

## Output Structure

Draft each section in formal contract prose, numbered for cross-reference. Use the structure below.

---

### 1. Agreement Header & Parties

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Title | FISCAL SPONSORSHIP AGREEMENT |
| Effective Date | [Date] |
| Fiscal Sponsor | [Full legal name], a [State] nonprofit corporation, tax-exempt under IRC § 501(c)(3) |
| Project | [Project name], represented by [Authorized individual(s)] |

---

### 2. Recitals

- Confirm sponsor's § 501(c)(3) status and public charity classification
- Describe project mission and nexus to sponsor's exempt purposes
- State parties' intent to enter fiscal sponsorship under a comprehensive model (Model A) or pre-approved grant model (Model C) — **confirm model with user**
- Affirm all project activities serve charitable, educational, or scientific purposes under IRC § 501(c)(3)

---

### 3. Fiscal Sponsor Responsibilities

**Must include:**
- [ ] Receive and deposit all project-designated contributions into a named restricted fund
- [ ] Maintain separate accounting records (GAAP-compliant)
- [ ] Issue timely donor acknowledgment letters per IRC § 170(f)(8) for gifts ≥ $250
- [ ] Review and approve project expenditures; retain ultimate disbursement discretion
- [ ] File all required IRS and state informational returns covering project activity
- [ ] Carry general liability and D&O insurance; specify coverage minimums

**Retained authority (non-waivable):**
- Final approval over all budgets and expenditures
- Right to suspend or terminate project activities jeopardizing exempt status
- Variance power to redirect funds if project purpose becomes impractical or impossible

---

### 4. Project Responsibilities

**Operational obligations:**
- [ ] Conduct all activities consistent with sponsor's exempt purposes
- [ ] Submit expense requests with invoices/receipts and charitable-purpose explanation
- [ ] Obtain written sponsor approval before executing contracts, hiring, or committing funds above $[threshold]
- [ ] Submit **quarterly** financial reports and **annual** narrative/outcomes report
- [ ] Notify sponsor immediately of legal inquiries, conflicts of interest, or status-threatening events

**Prohibited activities:**
- Political campaign intervention (IRC § 501(c)(3) absolute prohibition)
- Lobbying exceeding substantial part test or § 501(h) expenditure limits
- Private benefit or inurement transactions
- Fundraising without sponsor's prior written approval of materials

---

### 5. Financial Terms & Fund Management

| Item | Provision |
|---|---|
| Administrative fee | [X]% of gross contributions received for the project |
| Fee covers | Accounting, tax compliance, legal oversight, HR support, insurance allocation |
| Fund ownership | All contributions become sponsor's property, held in restricted fund |
| Disbursement process | Project submits written request → sponsor reviews → disbursement within [X] business days |
| Variance power | Sponsor may redirect funds to another § 501(c)(3) with similar purpose if project terminates or purpose fails |
| Earned income | Characterized and reported consistent with sponsor's exempt status; unrelated business income analyzed under IRC § 512 |

---

### 6. Term, Renewal & Termination

- **Initial term:** [One year / Indefinite] commencing on effective date
- **Renewal:** Automatic annual renewal unless either party provides [30/60/90]-day written notice of non-renewal
- **Voluntary termination:** [30/90]-day written notice by either party
- **Termination for cause:** Immediate upon material breach, policy violation, or activity threatening exempt status; [X]-day cure period for curable breaches

**Fund disposition on termination:**

> Remaining project funds shall be retained by the fiscal sponsor and granted to a § 501(c)(3) organization with a substantially similar charitable purpose. The project may recommend a recipient, subject to sponsor's approval. This provision preserves the deductibility of all prior contributions under IRC § 170.

- Sponsor retains copies of all financial records per applicable retention requirements
- Project returns or transfers sponsor-owned assets and IP within [X] days of termination

---

### 7. Legal Relationship & Liability

- Project personnel are **not** employees or agents of sponsor unless separately and formally engaged
- Sponsor is not liable for project's contracts, torts, or obligations unless expressly approved in writing
- **Indemnification:** Project indemnifies sponsor against claims arising from project activities, including attorney's fees; sponsor indemnifies project for sponsor's own negligence or willful misconduct
- **Insurance:** Project must obtain [general liability / professional liability / other] with limits of $[X], naming sponsor as additional insured

---

### 8. Intellectual Property

| Scenario | Default Provision |
|---|---|
| IP created solely by project personnel | Owned by [sponsor / project — specify] |
| License-back to project post-termination | [Grant perpetual, royalty-free license / No license] |
| Use of sponsor's name, logo, § 501(c)(3) status | Prior written approval required for each use |
| Publications and public communications | Must acknowledge fiscal sponsorship relationship in form approved by sponsor |

---

### 9. Compliance & Record-Keeping

- Project maintains activity and expenditure records; provides to sponsor on request
- Sponsor may conduct audits or financial reviews of project operations at any time with [X]-day notice
- Project complies with all applicable laws (data privacy, employment, licensing, human subjects protocols as applicable)
- Project notifies sponsor immediately of any governmental inquiry, subpoena, or proceeding

---

### 10. Miscellaneous

- **Entire agreement:** Supersedes all prior written or oral understandings
- **Amendments:** Must be in writing, signed by both parties
- **Governing law:** State of [Sponsor's state of incorporation]
- **Dispute resolution:** [Negotiation → mediation → litigation / arbitration in [venue]]
- **Severability:** Invalid provisions severed; remainder enforceable
- **Assignment:** Neither party may assign without prior written consent
- **Notices:** Delivered by [certified mail / email with confirmation] to addresses specified

## Guidelines

- **IRS control requirement:** Every financial provision must reinforce sponsor's ultimate discretion — this is the linchpin of donor deductibility and sponsor's exempt status
- **Model A vs. Model C:** Model A (comprehensive) transfers project assets to sponsor; Model C (pre-approved grant) does not — confirm which model applies before drafting, as it materially affects ownership and liability provisions
- **Variance power:** Must be explicit and unconditional; omitting it risks IRS challenge to contribution deductibility [VERIFY current IRS guidance]
- **State law:** Some states (CA, NY, others) impose additional nonprofit oversight requirements — flag for local counsel review
- **Lobbying:** If project anticipates any advocacy, add a § 501(h) election analysis note and expenditure tracking obligation
- **Unrelated business income:** Earned income activities must be analyzed under IRC §§ 511–514; flag for sponsor's tax counsel if substantial
