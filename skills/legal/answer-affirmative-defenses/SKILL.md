# Answer and Affirmative Defenses

Builds a complete, rule-compliant Answer with strategic admissions/denials and preserved affirmative defenses.

## Prerequisites

1. **Complaint** — final filed version with numbered paragraphs and captions
2. **Service data** — service date, method, proof of service
3. **Client facts** — timeline, key documents, communications, witnesses
4. **Governing instruments** — contracts, policies, statutes, notices
5. **Jurisdiction rules** — local rules on format, verification, and deadlines

## Output Structure / Process

### 1) Caption + Appearance

- Match court, parties, case number, and title exactly.
- Note whether appearance is general or special (if contesting jurisdiction/venue).

### 2) Response Matrix

Create a response table first, then draft the pleading from it.

| Complaint ¶ | Allegation Summary | Response Type | Qualified Admission | Notes / Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Party identity | Admit | Admit name/status only | Client docs |
| 2 | Jurisdiction facts | Deny | — | Investigate |
| 3 | Contract existence | Admit/Deny | Admit execution; deny breach | Contract |

Response types:
- Admit
- Deny
- Lack knowledge → deny on that basis
- Qualified admit/deny

### 3) Paragraph-by-Paragraph Responses

Use consistent, numbered responses aligned to complaint numbering.

Templates:
- Admit: `Defendant admits the allegations in Paragraph __ of the Complaint.`
- Deny: `Defendant denies the allegations in Paragraph __ of the Complaint.`
- Lack knowledge: `Defendant lacks knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief as to the truth of the allegations in Paragraph __ and on that basis denies them.`
- Qualified: `Defendant admits [specific fact] as alleged in Paragraph __, but denies the remaining allegations in that paragraph.`

Rules:
- Address every paragraph.
- Separate factual admissions from legal conclusions.
- Avoid overusing lack-knowledge where defendant should know.

### 4) Affirmative Defenses

List as separately numbered defenses. Include brief factual grounding without overcommitting.

Common procedural defenses:
- Lack of subject matter jurisdiction
- Lack of personal jurisdiction
- Improper venue
- Insufficient service of process
- Failure to join indispensable party
- Lack of standing

Common substantive defenses:
- Failure to state a claim
- Statute of limitations
- Statute of frauds
- Waiver
- Estoppel
- Laches
- Release
- Accord and satisfaction
- Payment
- Arbitration/award
- Unclean hands
- Illegality
- Comparative or contributory negligence (where applicable)
- Failure to mitigate damages

Defense drafting template:
```
[Defense Name]. Plaintiff’s claims are barred because [short factual basis]. The allegations show [timing/conduct/contract term], and therefore the claim fails as a matter of law.
```

Reservation clause:
- `Defendant reserves the right to assert additional defenses as they become known through discovery or further investigation.`

### 5) Counterclaim / Cross-Claim Triage

Checklist:
- [ ] Any compulsory counterclaim arising from same transaction?
- [ ] Any permissive counterclaim worth asserting now?
- [ ] Any cross-claim vs co-defendant (indemnity/contribution)?
- [ ] Any third-party claim needed to shift liability?

If counterclaims included, structure like a complaint:
- Parties and jurisdiction
- Factual allegations (numbered)
- Cause of action with elements
- Damages and relief

### 6) Prayer for Relief

Minimum requests:
- Dismissal with prejudice
- Judgment for defendant
- Costs of suit
- Fees if contract/statute allows
- Pre- and post-judgment interest
- Other relief as just and proper

Add specific relief tied to defenses/counterclaims.

### 7) Signature, Verification, Service

- Attorney signature block with bar number and contact info.
- Verification if required by jurisdiction or specific claim type.
- Certificate of service with method and date.

### 8) Quality Control

Checklist:
- [ ] Every complaint paragraph addressed
- [ ] No accidental admissions by omission
- [ ] Defenses tailored to facts and jurisdiction
- [ ] Counterclaims labeled compulsory/permissive if applicable
- [ ] Caption matches complaint exactly
- [ ] Deadline confirmed and calendared

## Guidelines

- Do not assert defenses without a plausible factual basis.
- Use qualified admissions to avoid conceding legal conclusions.
- Keep defenses concise but informative; avoid one-word defenses.
- If jurisdiction requires verified answers, include defendant verification.
- Flag jurisdiction-specific pleading standards (notice vs fact pleading).
- If unsure about timing defenses or limitation periods, mark for verification before filing.
