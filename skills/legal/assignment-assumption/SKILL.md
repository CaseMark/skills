# Assignment and Assumption Agreement

Drafts a complete Assignment and Assumption Agreement transferring specified contracts from assignor to assignee in connection with an asset purchase transaction.

## Prerequisites

1. **Underlying Asset Purchase Agreement** — with section references for assignment/assumption obligations
2. **Party details** — full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions of organization for both assignor and assignee
3. **Contract inventory** — complete list of contracts to be assigned (for Exhibit A)
4. **Consent requirements** — identification of which contracts require third-party consent
5. **Effective date and governing law** — closing date, chosen state law, forum selection

## Output Structure

### Document Sections

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Header | Title (caps), effective date, party identification with entity type/jurisdiction, defined terms |
| Recitals | WHEREAS clauses: (1) reference Purchase Agreement and business/assets, (2) assignment/assumption obligation per specific section, (3) third-party consent acknowledgment if applicable, (4) NOW THEREFORE with consideration language |
| §1 Assignment | Transfer of all right/title/interest in Assigned Contracts (Exhibit A); includes payment rights, enforcement rights, remedies accruing on/after Effective Date; further assurances covenant; power of attorney for enforcement |
| §2 Assumption | Assumption of obligations arising on/after Effective Date only; explicit carve-out of Retained Liabilities: (i) pre-Effective Date obligations, (ii) pre-closing breaches, (iii) Purchase Agreement exclusions, (iv) non-assumed obligations |
| §3 Consents | Commercially reasonable efforts to obtain; no assignment until consent received; alternative arrangements if consent withheld (subcontracting, sublicensing, enforcement for benefit); payment pass-through mechanics |
| §4 Reps & Warranties | **Assignor**: authority, enforceability, completeness of Exhibit A, true copies provided, no known material breach. **Assignee**: authority, enforceability, financial/operational capacity |
| §5 Indemnification | Reciprocal indemnity covering breach, respective liabilities (Assumed vs. Retained), post/pre-Effective Date contract performance; subject to Purchase Agreement procedures and limitations |
| §6 General Provisions | Purchase Agreement controls on conflict; governing law (no conflict-of-law); exclusive jurisdiction; written amendment/waiver only; binding on successors; no assignment without consent (affiliate exception); severability; counterparts (including electronic); entire agreement |
| Signature Block | IN WITNESS WHEREOF; blocks for both parties with By/Name/Title/Date lines |
| Exhibit A | Table: contract title, parties, effective date, expiration date, consent required (Y/N), contract number |

### Exhibit A Template

```
EXHIBIT A — ASSIGNED CONTRACTS

| # | Contract Title/Description | Counterparty | Effective Date | Expiration Date | Consent Required | Contract No. |
|---|---------------------------|--------------|----------------|-----------------|------------------|--------------|
| 1 | [Description]             | [Party]      | [Date]         | [Date/N/A]      | [Yes/No]         | [Ref]        |
```

### Key Defined Terms

- **Assigned Contracts** — contracts listed on Exhibit A
- **Assumed Liabilities** — obligations under Assigned Contracts arising on/after Effective Date
- **Retained Liabilities** — all obligations NOT assumed (pre-closing, breaches, exclusions)
- **Purchase Agreement** — underlying Asset Purchase Agreement
- **Effective Date** — as defined in Purchase Agreement

## Guidelines

- Match party names exactly to formation documents and Purchase Agreement
- Cross-reference correct section numbers from the Purchase Agreement
- The temporal dividing line (Effective Date) must be consistent throughout — all liability allocation turns on this date
- For real property leases, IP licenses, or government contracts, flag need for specialized assignment provisions
- Check anti-assignment clauses in each contract being assigned; note any that prohibit or restrict assignment
- Do NOT assume consent is unnecessary — default to flagging consent requirement unless confirmed otherwise
- Indemnification procedures and caps should incorporate Purchase Agreement limitations by reference rather than restating them
- If jurisdiction-specific assignment statutes apply (e.g., UCC Article 9 for certain receivables), note and conform
- Counterparts clause should expressly cover electronic/PDF execution
