# Distribution of Assets Plan

Drafts a legally compliant corporate Distribution of Assets Plan allocating entity assets among designated beneficiaries or stakeholders upon dissolution, restructuring, or other triggering events.

## Prerequisites

1. **Entity identification** — legal name, jurisdiction of formation, EIN
2. **Triggering event** — dissolution, restructuring, death/incapacity of principal, merger
3. **Asset inventory source documents** — balance sheets, deeds, account statements, appraisals
4. **Beneficiary information** — legal names, relationships, contact info, ownership percentages
5. **Governing documents** — articles of incorporation, bylaws, operating agreement, shareholder agreement
6. **Jurisdiction** — state of formation and any states where assets are located

## Output Structure

### 1. Preamble
| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Entity name & type | Full legal name, state, entity type |
| Effective date | Date of plan execution or triggering event |
| Triggering circumstances | Dissolution vote, court order, death, restructuring resolution |
| Legal authority | Cite governing statute (e.g., state Business Corporation Act, LLC Act) [VERIFY jurisdiction] |
| Plan purpose | One-sentence statement of intent |

### 2. Asset Inventory

For each asset, capture:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Asset ID | Sequential reference number |
| Category | Real property / Financial account / Business interest / IP / Equipment / Other |
| Description | Legal description (real property), institution + last-4 (accounts), registration # (IP) |
| Current FMV | Appraised or book value; date of valuation |
| Encumbrances | Liens, mortgages, pledges, restrictions on transfer |
| Disposition method | Liquidate / Transfer in kind / Retain for wind-down |

**Asset categories to address:**
- [ ] Real property (with legal descriptions and recording references)
- [ ] Bank and investment accounts
- [ ] Accounts receivable and notes receivable
- [ ] Business interests / subsidiary equity
- [ ] Intellectual property (patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets)
- [ ] Contracts and licenses (assignable vs. non-assignable)
- [ ] Equipment and personal property above threshold value
- [ ] Tax attributes (NOLs, credits) — note transferability limits [VERIFY]
- [ ] Pending litigation claims or recoveries

- Creditor priority → note any statutory distribution waterfall [VERIFY state law]
- Beneficiaries with special needs → flag for supplemental needs trust review

### 4. Distribution Methodology

**Specify method for each asset or asset class:**

| Method | Use When |
|---|---|
| Percentage allocation of liquidation proceeds | Assets to be sold; simple pro-rata distribution |
| Specific bequest of identified asset | Named asset to named beneficiary in kind |
| Staged distribution schedule | Ongoing wind-down, contingent assets, disputed claims |
| Liquidating trust | Complex assets requiring post-dissolution management |

**Tax and equalization provisions:**
- Identify assets with built-in gain; note IRC §§ 331, 336 implications for C-corps [VERIFY]
- Include equalization language if in-kind assets of unequal value are allocated
- Address transfer taxes, recording fees, and who bears costs of transfer

### 5. Distribution Timeline

```
Day 0:    Plan adopted / triggering event occurs
Day 1–30: Asset appraisals finalized; creditor notice period (per state law) [VERIFY]
Day 30–X: Liquidation of assets designated for sale
Day X:    Initial distribution to creditors (priority per state dissolution statute)
Day X+Y:  Distribution to equity holders / residual beneficiaries
Final:    Certificate of dissolution filed; plan administrator discharged
```

### 6. Contingency Provisions

| Scenario | Default Rule | Plan Provision |
|---|---|---|
| Beneficiary predeceases distribution | Per stirpes / lapse | Specify alternate or redistribution among survivors |
| Beneficiary disclaims interest | Pass to contingent | Specify timeframe for disclaimer (≤9 months for tax purposes [VERIFY]) |
| Beneficiary cannot be located | Escrow / state unclaimed property | Specify holdback period before escheat |
| Asset value materially changes | Administrator discretion | Specify rebalancing or pro-rata adjustment |
| Disputed asset | Hold pending resolution | Identify escrow agent and dispute resolution mechanism |

### 7. Administrative Powers

The plan administrator / liquidating trustee / designated officer shall have authority to:
- [ ] Sell, transfer, or convey assets
- [ ] Execute documents on behalf of the entity
- [ ] Retain professionals (counsel, accountants, brokers)
- [ ] Pay wind-down expenses from entity assets
- [ ] File tax returns and resolve tax liabilities
- [ ] Settle claims against the entity
- [ ] Open and close bank accounts
- [ ] Make partial distributions pending final asset resolution

**Dispute resolution:** Specify mediation → arbitration → [jurisdiction] court.

### 8. Execution Block

Per jurisdiction requirements [VERIFY state-specific formalities]:

## Guidelines

- **Creditor priority**: Distributions to equity holders cannot precede satisfaction of creditor claims; confirm state dissolution waterfall before drafting distribution schedule
- **Tax clearance**: Many states require tax clearance certificate before dissolution is final [VERIFY by state]
- **Non-assignable contracts**: Flag contracts requiring third-party consent to assign; do not include in distribution until consent obtained
- **Securities law**: Transfers of business interests may trigger state or federal securities law compliance obligations [VERIFY]
