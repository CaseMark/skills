# Complaint for Breach of Purchase Agreement

Drafts a filing-ready complaint alleging breach of a real property purchase agreement, with jurisdiction-specific formatting and element-driven pleading.

## Prerequisites

1. **Executed purchase agreement** — including all addenda, amendments, and exhibits
2. **Party information** — full legal names, entity types, states of formation, registered agents, addresses
3. **Breach documentation** — correspondence, notices, cure demands, timeline of failed performance
4. **Damages evidence** — closing costs, market value data, mitigation expenses, consequential losses
5. **Court/jurisdiction details** — target court, local rules, formatting requirements, filing fee schedule

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption & Court Information

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Court | Full legal name, division, department |
| Case number | Leave blank or insert assigned number |
| Parties | Full legal names with entity designations |
| Document title | "COMPLAINT FOR BREACH OF PURCHASE AGREEMENT" |
| Format | Per local rules (font, spacing, margins, line numbering) |

### 2. Jurisdiction & Venue

- State subject-matter jurisdiction basis (amount in controversy, statutory grant)
- Check purchase agreement for **forum-selection** and **choice-of-law** clauses — address directly
- Venue: tie to contract execution location, property location, performance location, or defendant's residence/principal place of business
- Cite jurisdictional statute (e.g., state long-arm statute if applicable)

### 3. Parties

For each party, allege:

| Field | Plaintiff | Defendant |
|---|---|---|
| Full legal name | ✓ | ✓ |
| Address | ✓ | ✓ |
| Entity type & state of formation | If applicable | If applicable |
| Registered agent | — | If entity |
| Role in agreement | Buyer/Seller/Assignee | Buyer/Seller/Assignee |
| Trade names / DBAs | If any | If any |

### 4. Factual Allegations (Chronological)

Structure as numbered paragraphs covering:

1. **Formation** — date of execution, subject property (address, APN, legal description), purchase price, payment terms
2. **Material terms** — contingencies, closing date, inspection periods, financing conditions, special provisions
3. **Plaintiff's performance** — obligations fulfilled, payments made, conditions satisfied (with dates and amounts)
4. **Defendant's breach** — specific obligation(s) not performed, date of breach, notice given, cure opportunity provided
5. **Post-breach** — communications, failed cure attempts, mitigation efforts

> Quote specific contract language for breached provisions. Use exact dates, dollar amounts, and document references.

### 5. Cause of Action: Breach of Contract

Plead each element with supporting factual paragraph references:

| Element | What to Allege |
|---|---|
| Valid contract | Mutual assent, consideration, capacity, lawful object |
| Plaintiff's performance | All conditions precedent performed or excused |
| Defendant's breach | Specific provisions violated, manner of breach |
| Causation | Breach directly and proximately caused damages |
| Damages | Categories and amounts (reference damages section) |

Cite governing state's elements standard (e.g., Restatement (Second) of Contracts or state pattern jury instructions) [VERIFY specific citation for target jurisdiction].

### 6. Damages

| Category | Description | Calculation Method |
|---|---|---|
| Direct / expectation | Benefit of the bargain | Contract price vs. market value differential |
| Consequential | Foreseeable losses from breach | Must show foreseeability at time of contracting |
| Incidental | Costs responding to breach | Inspection fees, appraisals, interim housing, storage |
| Mitigation costs | Expenses of reasonable mitigation efforts | Actual costs incurred |

- Check agreement for **liquidated damages** clauses or **damage caps**
- If earnest money deposit at issue, specify amount and current holder
- Provide specific dollar amounts or allege "according to proof at trial" per local practice

### 7. Prayer for Relief

Request each applicable remedy:

- [ ] Compensatory damages in the amount of $_____ (or according to proof)
- [ ] Specific performance (if property is unique and monetary damages inadequate)
- [ ] Pre-judgment interest at statutory rate from date of breach
- [ ] Post-judgment interest at statutory rate
- [ ] Attorney's fees — cite contract fee-shifting provision by section number, or applicable statute
- [ ] Costs of suit
- [ ] Such other relief as the court deems just and proper

> Some jurisdictions require ad damnum amounts; others prohibit specifying amounts in certain cases. Conform to local rules.

### 8. Verification & Signature Block

**Verification:**
- Plaintiff (or authorized representative with personal knowledge) verifies under penalty of perjury
- Check jurisdiction: some require notarization, others accept unsworn declaration under 28 U.S.C. § 1746 or state equivalent [VERIFY for target state]

**Signature block must include:**
- Attorney name and bar number
- Firm name
- Address, phone, email
- Party represented designation

## Guidelines

- **Numbered paragraphs** — every factual allegation gets its own numbered paragraph
- **Plead specifically** — avoid conclusions; state facts that establish each element
- **Exhibits** — attach the purchase agreement and key correspondence as exhibits; reference by exhibit letter
- **Entity defendants** — verify active status with Secretary of State; name the correct entity
- **Doe defendants** — include if jurisdiction permits and additional responsible parties are suspected
- **Demand for jury trial** — include if desired and permitted; some jurisdictions require it in the complaint
- **Certificate of service** — append if required by local rules at filing
- **E-filing** — confirm court's e-filing system requirements (file format, size limits, naming conventions)
- Do NOT allege fraud elements unless specifically instructed — maintain clean contract theory
- Do NOT include settlement communications or FRE 408 / state-equivalent protected material in factual allegations
