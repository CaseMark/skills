# Letter of Intent (LOI)

Drafts a professional LOI that formalizes preliminary deal terms between parties contemplating a corporate transaction, balancing binding and non-binding provisions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party information** — full legal names, entity types, authorized signatories
2. **Transaction type** — acquisition, merger, asset purchase, investment, partnership
3. **Economic terms** — purchase price/investment amount, payment structure, timing
4. **Timeline** — due diligence period, target closing date, exclusivity duration
5. **Key contingencies** — financing conditions, regulatory approvals, third-party consents
6. **Prior documents** (if any) — term sheets, correspondence, meeting notes, existing NDAs

If documents lack sufficient detail, gather missing items through focused questions before drafting.

## Output Structure

### Document Format

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| **Header** | Date, recipient name/address, subject line: "Letter of Intent – Proposed [Transaction Type]" |
| **Opening** | Party identification (full legal names), transaction purpose, concise deal description |
| **Transaction Overview** | Assets/equity/business units involved, deal structure, attachments reference |
| **Principal Terms** | See term checklist below |
| **Binding/Non-Binding Designation** | Explicit statement of LOI's non-binding nature with carved-out binding provisions |
| **Confidentiality** | Standalone provision or NDA cross-reference |
| **Exclusivity (if applicable)** | No-shop period, scope, duration, breach consequences |
| **Governing Provisions** | Governing law, good-faith negotiation obligation, termination/expiration |
| **Signature Blocks** | Authorized representatives, titles, counterpart/e-signature authorization |

### Principal Terms Checklist

Draft each term with enough specificity to demonstrate serious intent while noting that definitive agreements will elaborate:

- [ ] Purchase price / investment amount
- [ ] Payment structure and timing (cash, stock, seller note, earnout)
- [ ] Proposed closing date and flexibility conditions
- [ ] Transaction cost allocation
- [ ] Key representations and warranties expected in definitive agreements
- [ ] Material conditions precedent to closing
- [ ] Post-closing obligations (earnouts, employment/non-compete agreements, indemnification baskets)
- [ ] Break-up or termination fees (if any)

### Binding vs. Non-Binding Provisions

| Typically Binding | Typically Non-Binding |
|---|---|
| Confidentiality obligations | Purchase price and payment terms |
| Exclusivity / no-shop period | Representations and warranties |
| Cost/expense allocation | Closing conditions |
| Governing law / dispute resolution | Post-closing covenants |
| Non-binding designation clause itself | Timeline targets |

### Confidentiality Provision Template

If no separate NDA exists, include a binding confidentiality section covering:

```
- Scope of confidential information
- Permitted uses (solely for evaluating the transaction)
- Permitted disclosures (advisors, lenders — bound by same obligations)
- Duration (typically 18–24 months post-termination)
- Return/destruction of materials if transaction fails
```

If a separate NDA exists, cross-reference it and clarify it survives the LOI.

### Exclusivity Provision

```
- Duration: typically 30–90 days (specify exact period)
- Scope: no solicitation, encouragement, or engagement with competing buyers/investors
- Exceptions: unsolicited inbound inquiries (if negotiated), fiduciary-out (if applicable)
- Consequence of breach: termination right + expense reimbursement (typical)
```

## Guidelines

- **Binding language precision** — Every provision must be clearly designated as binding or non-binding. Ambiguity here creates unintended obligations.
- **Good-faith obligation** — Include a mutual covenant to negotiate definitive agreements in good faith with a target execution date. State explicitly that failure to reach definitive terms creates no liability beyond breach of binding provisions.
- **Governing law** — Default to the state where the target business is organized or where the transaction will primarily occur. Flag choice-of-law to the user for confirmation.
- **Counterparts / e-signature** — Include standard counterpart and electronic signature authorization language.
- **Tone** — Professional and direct. Accessible to both legal and business readers. Avoid unnecessarily adversarial framing in a document meant to advance a deal.
- **No definitive agreement terms** — Do not over-detail provisions (e.g., full indemnification mechanics, detailed rep schedules) that belong in definitive documents. The LOI should frame expectations, not replace the purchase agreement.
- **Jurisdiction note** — This skill targets U.S. transactions. Flag cross-border elements for additional counsel review.
