# Motion for Attorneys' Fees

Drafts a post-judgment motion for attorneys' fees establishing prevailing party status, applying the lodestar method, and presenting a fully documented fee petition that withstands opposition.

## Prerequisites

1. **Final judgment or order** — establishing prevailing party status with specific holdings and relief granted
2. **Fee-shifting authority** — statute (e.g., 42 U.S.C. § 1988), contract provision, or common law basis
3. **Contemporaneous time records** — detailed billing entries for all timekeepers
4. **Timekeeper qualifications** — resumes/CVs, years of practice, specializations, recognitions
5. **Rate evidence** — recent fee awards in jurisdiction, bar association surveys, market data
6. **Case docket/procedural history** — filing dates, motions, discovery, depositions, trial dates
7. **Local rules** — page limits, formatting, filing deadlines for fee motions, required documentation

## Output Structure

### I. Caption & Compliance

- Full court name, division, case number, party designations
- Title: "Motion for Award of Attorneys' Fees [and Costs]"
- Table of contents and table of authorities if required by local rules
- Verify: font, margins, spacing, page limits, footer/header requirements

### II. Introduction

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Moving party & status | Name + prevailing party identification |
| Fee authority | Exact statutory citation or contract clause (verbatim) |
| Judgment reference | Date, holdings, relief granted |
| Total fees sought | Dollar amount, broken down if helpful |
| Roadmap | Preview: authority → rates → hours → results |

### III. Factual Background & Litigation Narrative

Build chronological narrative from case documents emphasizing fee-reasonableness factors:

- **Nature of dispute** — claims, defenses, core issues, stakes
- **Procedural milestones** — with dates: pleadings, discovery, depositions, motions (with outcomes), settlement efforts, trial, judgment
- **Complexity indicators** — novel legal issues, document volume, expert witnesses, number of parties
- **Opposing conduct increasing work** — frivolous motions, discovery abuse, refusal to stipulate, unreasonable settlement positions (cite specific orders/correspondence)
- **Quality of representation** — favorable motion outcomes, judicial commendations, results achieved

### IV. Legal Standard

Present controlling framework in this order:

1. **Fee-shifting authority** — quote statutory/contractual language verbatim
2. **Prevailing party standard** — jurisdiction-specific test:
   - Judgment on merits / catalyst theory / substantial relief test
   - *Buckhannon* applicability for settlement-based recovery
3. **Lodestar method** — reasonable hours × reasonable rate (*Hensley v. Eckerhart*, 461 U.S. 424 (1983))
4. **Johnson factors** (where adopted) — 12 factors from *Johnson v. Georgia Highway Express*, 488 F.2d 714 (5th Cir. 1974) [VERIFY]
5. **Lodestar enhancements** — *Perdue v. Kenny A.*, 559 U.S. 542 (2010); jurisdiction-specific standards
6. **Fee allocation** — *Hensley* common-core-of-facts doctrine for partial success
7. **Fees-on-fees** — authority for recovering time spent on fee motion itself

### V. Prevailing Party Analysis

- Quote judgment/order establishing favorable outcome
- Apply jurisdiction's prevailing party test to specific findings
- Address degree of success → relationship to fees sought
- If settlement: address *Buckhannon* / catalyst theory / consent decree
- Preempt opposition arguments on limited success or partial recovery

### VI. Lodestar Calculation

#### A. Rate Justification

For each timekeeper, document:

| Timekeeper | Role | Years | Rate | Market Evidence |
|------------|------|-------|------|-----------------|
| [Name] | Partner | [X] | $[X]/hr | [Recent awards, surveys] |
| [Name] | Associate | [X] | $[X]/hr | [Recent awards, surveys] |
| [Name] | Paralegal | [X] | $[X]/hr | [Recent awards, surveys] |

Support with: recent fee awards approving comparable rates, bar association survey data, attorney qualifications (specialization, recognitions, publications).

#### B. Hours Analysis by Litigation Phase

| Phase | Hours | Justification |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| Case assessment & pleadings | | Complexity of claims, investigation scope |
| Written discovery | | Volume propounded/received, disputes |
| Document review & production | | Document count, privilege review |
| Depositions | | Number of witnesses, preparation |
| Motion practice | | Per-motion breakdown with outcomes |
| Expert work | | Retention, reports, preparation |
| Settlement/mediation | | Sessions attended, preparation |
| Trial preparation | | Witnesses, exhibits, research |
| Trial/hearing | | Days in court |
| Post-judgment | | Fee motion, enforcement |
| **Total** | | |

#### C. Billing Judgment

Document excluded time to establish credibility:

- Clerical tasks billed at professional rates
- Excessive/duplicative research
- Learning curve time
- Error correction
- Quantify total hours excluded per category

#### D. Staffing Appropriateness

- Routine tasks → associates/paralegals
- Complex strategy/argument → senior attorneys
- Justify any senior attorney time on routine tasks

#### E. Lodestar Enhancement (if sought)

- Proposed multiplier and resulting total
- Basis: exceptional results, contingent risk, complexity, quality
- Cite jurisdiction-specific authority permitting enhancement
- Show lodestar alone is inadequate to attract competent counsel (if required)

### VII. Supporting Declaration

Numbered-paragraph declaration under penalty of perjury covering:

```
¶¶ 1-3:   Declarant qualifications, role, personal knowledge basis
¶¶ 4-6:   Time record authentication — contemporaneous, accurate, complete
¶¶ 7-9:   Billing judgment exercised — categories excluded, amounts
¶¶ 10-12:  Rate reasonableness — customary rates, market consistency
¶¶ 13-15:  Hours reasonableness — necessity, complexity, staffing
¶¶ 16+:    Additional timekeepers (qualifications, rate justification)

Execution block: venue, date, signature, notarization if required
```

Consider additional declarations: market rate affidavits from peer practitioners, client declaration on value of representation.

### VIII. Exhibits

| Exhibit | Contents |
|---------|----------|
| A | Detailed time records (date, timekeeper, hours, description) |
| B | Judgment/order establishing prevailing party status |
| C | Fee-shifting statute or contract provision |
| D | Timekeeper resumes/CVs |
| E | Market rate evidence (fee awards, surveys) |
| F | Recent comparable fee award orders |
| G | Key court orders showing complexity/opposing conduct |

Redact work product from time entries; explain redactions in declaration.

### IX. Conclusion & Prayer for Relief

Specify exact relief:

- Attorneys' fees: $[amount] (lodestar) [+ $[amount] enhancement = $[total]]
- Taxable costs per statute/rule
- Expert witness fees (if recoverable under fee-shifting provision)
- Pre-judgment interest (if authorized; specify calculation date and rate)
- Post-judgment interest at statutory rate
- Fees-on-fees for time on fee motion (supplemental application reserved)
- Such other relief as the court deems just

## Guidelines

- **Cite precisely** — Bluebook format; verify all citations are current and not overruled
- **Quote the record** — every factual assertion must cite a specific document, order, or transcript
- **No inflation** — time entries must be truthful; never exaggerate hours or mischaracterize work
- **Candor to tribunal** — disclose adverse authority; accurately represent holdings of cited cases
- **Protect privilege** — redact work product from time entries; generalize strategy-revealing descriptions
- **Anticipate opposition** — preemptively address: excessive rates, excessive hours, limited success, staffing objections, block billing concerns
- **Deadline compliance** — calculate filing deadline from judgment date per local rules (commonly 14 days under FRCP 54(d)(2))
- **Jurisdiction-specific** — verify whether catalyst theory is recognized; check for state-specific lodestar variations; confirm local formatting rules
- **Mark uncertain citations** with [VERIFY] — especially for older or circuit-specific authority
