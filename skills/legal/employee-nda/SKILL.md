# Employee Non-Disclosure Agreement

Draft an enforceable employee confidentiality agreement that protects proprietary information without operating as a noncompete.

## Prerequisites

1. Employer legal name, entity type, state of formation, principal address.
2. Employee legal name and address.
3. Position, start date, and access level to sensitive systems/data.
4. Governing law and forum or arbitration preferences.
5. Confidential information inventory and industry context.
6. Third-party confidentiality obligations that bind the employer.
7. Duration targets for trade secrets and non-trade-secret information.
8. Notice addresses and execution method (wet or electronic).
9. Related policies or agreements to reference or incorporate.

## Output Structure / Process

Use this section map and fill each element.

| Section | Required Content |
| --- | --- |
| Title + Parties | Identify document as Employee Non-Disclosure Agreement; name parties; define Effective Date. |
| Recitals | State role and access to confidential info; employer investment and legitimate interest; consideration; employee acknowledgement of value and harm. |
| Definitions | Define Confidential Information; include form of disclosure; include information developed during employment; include third-party info; add exclusions and skills carve-out. |
| Employee Obligations | Non-disclosure; non-use beyond duties; reasonable care standard; restrict access to need-to-know; security practices; prompt breach reporting. |
| Compelled Disclosure | Allow disclosure when required by law; require prompt notice and cooperation for protective order unless prohibited. |
| Return/Destruction | Require return or certified destruction of all materials on separation; confirm no copies retained. |
| Term/Survival | State effective date; survival after termination; separate duration for trade secrets vs other info. |
| Statutory Notices | Include DTSA immunity notice for U.S. agreements executed or updated after 2016-05-11; add state-specific notices if required [VERIFY]. |
| Remedies | Acknowledge irreparable harm; permit injunctive relief; cumulative remedies; attorneys' fees allocation per jurisdiction. |
| Governing Law + Venue | Choose state law and venue with nexus to employment; consider public policy limits. |
| Dispute Resolution | If arbitration, specify rules, seat, arbitrator selection, costs; carve out injunctive relief as needed. |
| Boilerplate | Entire agreement; amendment in writing; severability/blue-pencil; waiver; assignment; notices; counterparts; e-sign. |
| Signatures | Employer authorized signatory with title; employee signature; optional employee acknowledgement paragraph. |

Define Confidential Information using this checklist.

- [ ] Scope statement: non-public information about the business in any form.
- [ ] Categories: trade secrets; customer/client data; pricing and financials; marketing/sales strategies; product plans/roadmaps; technical specs; source code/algorithms; R&D; business plans; vendor/supplier terms; HR/compensation data; security measures; information labeled confidential.
- [ ] Coverage: information disclosed, observed, accessed, or developed during employment; derivatives and notes; third-party confidential info.
- [ ] Exclusions: prior knowledge with evidence; public domain without breach; rightfully received without duty; independently developed without use.
- [ ] Skills carve-out: exclude general skills, knowledge, and experience acquired through employment.

Use this duration matrix unless instructed otherwise.

| Category | Duration |
| --- | --- |
| Trade secrets | For so long as information remains a trade secret under applicable law. |
| Other Confidential Information | Fixed term reasonable for the industry and jurisdiction, commonly 2-5 years [VERIFY]. |

Insert DTSA notice in a standalone section.

```text
[VERIFY] Defend Trade Secrets Act Immunity Notice (18 U.S.C. 1833(b)):
An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any Federal or State trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that (A) is made (i) in confidence to a Federal, State, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney; and (ii) solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (B) is made in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.
```

If arbitration is included, add an injunctive relief carve-out.

```text
Either party may seek temporary, preliminary, or permanent injunctive relief in a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent or remedy a breach or threatened breach of this Agreement.
```

## Guidelines

- Keep scope tied to legitimate business interests; avoid restrictions that function as noncompetes.
- Use precise, role-specific categories; avoid catch-all definitions without exclusions.
- Include compelled-disclosure notice and cooperation language.
- Ensure consideration is stated clearly and matches the timing of execution.
- Use venue and choice-of-law with a real nexus to the employment relationship.
- Make attorneys' fees provisions compliant with local law and bargaining norms [VERIFY].
- Keep formatting consistent, defined terms capitalized, and signature authority verified.
