# Master Service Agreement — Oilfield Services

Drafts a litigation-tested MSA governing the operator-contractor relationship for upstream oil and gas services, with knock-for-knock indemnification compliant with state anti-indemnity statutes.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party details** — legal names, entity types, states of organization, principal offices
2. **Service scope** — drilling, completion, workover, maintenance, or other oilfield services
3. **Operational jurisdictions** — states where services will be performed (controls anti-indemnity compliance)
4. **Insurance specs** — any existing coverage requirements or broker constraints
5. **Existing agreements** — prior MSAs, rate schedules, HSE policies between the parties
6. **Special terms** — exclusivity, minimum volume commitments, subcontracting restrictions

## Output Structure

Draft a complete MSA with the following articles. Adapt detail level to the engagement scope.

### Article Outline

| # | Article | Key Contents |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Parties & Recitals | Full legal names, entity types, business context, acknowledgment of hazardous operations |
| 2 | Definitions | See defined terms table below |
| 3 | Scope & Work Orders | MSA as framework (no volume commitment); WO incorporation; MSA controls unless WO explicitly modifies |
| 4 | Performance Standards | Good-and-workmanlike standard; qualified personnel; fit-for-purpose equipment; Contractor holds all permits |
| 5 | Term & Termination | Initial term + renewal mechanism; termination for convenience (30-90 days notice); termination for cause (HSE violations, uninsured, insolvency, uncured breach at 30 days); WO survival on MSA termination; wind-down obligations |
| 6 | Compensation & Payment | Rate structures per WO (day rate / unit / lump sum / cost-plus); monthly invoicing + documentation; Net-30 payment; dispute holdback process; late interest (1.5%/mo or legal max); annual escalation mechanism; audit rights |
| 7 | Insurance | See insurance minimums table below |
| 8 | Indemnification | See indemnification section below |
| 9 | HSE | Compliance with OSHA (29 CFR), EPA (40 CFR), DOT (49 CFR), BSEE (offshore), state O&G commissions; written HSE program required; pre-job safety meetings; PPE; personnel qualifications (HAZWOPER, H2S, well control); Operator stop-work authority; incident reporting (phone within 4 hrs, written within 24 hrs); environmental permits and spill response; more-stringent-controls-apply rule |
| 10 | Confidentiality | Broad definition; reasonable-care standard; need-to-know restriction; standard exceptions (public, prior possession, independent development, third-party receipt, legal compulsion with notice); return/destroy on termination; 3-5 year survival; all well/reservoir data is Operator property |
| 11 | Representations & Warranties | Mutual: due organization, authority, enforceability, no conflicts. Contractor additional: licensed, experienced, personnel qualified, equipment maintained, insurance current, law-compliant. Disclaimer of implied warranties (merchantability, fitness, non-infringement) |
| 12 | Intellectual Property | Pre-existing IP retained by owner; work product created for Operator under Operator direction → Operator owns (assignment); Contractor innovations → Operator gets perpetual royalty-free license; all well/operational data → Operator property; no trademark use without consent |
| 13 | Governing Law & Disputes | Governing law (typically TX, LA, OK, CO, or ND — match jurisdiction); choice of litigation or arbitration (AAA Commercial Rules); jury waiver if litigation; equitable relief carve-out; executive negotiation prerequisite (15-day meet, 30-day resolution window) |
| 14 | General Provisions | Force majeure (with mitigation duty, termination right at 60-180 days); assignment restrictions (affiliate and M&A exceptions); written notices (personal delivery, overnight, certified mail, email with confirmation); severability; entire agreement / no oral modification; no waiver by inaction; independent contractor status; FCPA / export controls / OFAC compliance; survival clause; no press releases without consent |

| Term | Scope |
|------|-------|
| Services | All oilfield activities: drilling, completion, stimulation, workover, intervention, equipment rental, maintenance, support |

| Oilfield Operations | All exploration, development, drilling, completion, production, workover, maintenance, abandonment activities |
| Indemnified Parties | Party + parent/subs/affiliates + their officers, directors, managers, members, employees, agents, contractors, subcontractors |
| Personnel | All employees, agents, contractors, subcontractors of a party |

| Hazardous Materials | Substances regulated under environmental laws including petroleum, chemicals, waste |
| Force Majeure Event | Events beyond reasonable control (natural disaster, war, terrorism, pandemic, government action, third-party failure) |
| Confidential Information | All non-public information disclosed by either party |
| Applicable Laws | All federal, state, local, foreign laws, regulations, orders, permits |

### Insurance Minimums

| Coverage | Minimum Limit |
|----------|--------------|
| Commercial General Liability | $5M per occurrence / $10M aggregate |
| Automobile Liability (owned/non-owned/hired) | $1M per accident |
| Workers' Compensation | Statutory (per state) |
| Employer's Liability | $1M per accident / $1M per employee disease / $1M policy limit disease |
| Pollution Liability | $5M per occurrence and aggregate |
| Excess/Umbrella Liability | $10M over CGL, Auto, Employer's |

**Policy requirements:** Operator + affiliates as additional insured (all except WC); primary and non-contributory; waiver of subrogation; 30-day cancellation notice; occurrence basis; A.M. Best A- VII minimum.

### Indemnification — Knock-for-Knock Structure

Use the industry-standard mutual indemnity model:

| Risk | Borne By | Scope |
|------|----------|-------|
| Contractor Personnel injury/death | Contractor | Regardless of fault, except Operator's **sole negligence or willful misconduct** |
| Contractor Equipment/property damage | Contractor | Regardless of fault, except Operator's **sole negligence or willful misconduct** |
| Operator Personnel injury/death | Operator | Regardless of fault, except Contractor's **sole negligence or willful misconduct** |
| Operator property damage | Operator | Regardless of fault, except Contractor's **sole negligence or willful misconduct** |
| Third-party claims | Comparative fault | Each party indemnifies for its proportionate fault |
| Contractor breach / law violations | Contractor | Full indemnity |

**Anti-Indemnity Statute Compliance — CRITICAL:**

Include a savings clause: indemnities are enforceable to the fullest extent permitted by law; any prohibited provision is deemed modified to the minimum extent necessary for compliance.

| Statute | Citation | Key Restriction |
|---------|----------|----------------|
| Louisiana Oilfield Indemnity Act | La. R.S. 9:2780 [VERIFY] | Voids indemnity for indemnitee's negligence causing death/bodily injury |
| Texas Oilfield Anti-Indemnity Act | Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code Ch. 127 [VERIFY] | Voids indemnity for indemnitee's negligence; mutual indemnity with insurance OK |
| Wyoming | Wyo. Stat. § 30-1-131 [VERIFY] | Voids broad-form indemnity in oilfield contracts |
| New Mexico | N.M. Stat. Ann. § 56-7-2 [VERIFY] | Voids indemnity for indemnitee's negligence in certain contracts |

**Indemnification procedures:** Prompt written notice (failure doesn't relieve unless material prejudice); indemnifying party controls defense with acceptable counsel; no settlement admitting fault without consent; indemnified party cooperates.

## Guidelines

- **Jurisdiction-first drafting**: Identify all states where services will be performed before drafting indemnification — anti-indemnity statutes vary dramatically and can void entire provisions
- **Never use broad-form indemnity** in states with anti-indemnity statutes; always carve out sole negligence/willful misconduct at minimum
- **Insurance must support indemnities**: Ensure additional-insured and waiver-of-subrogation endorsements align with the knock-for-knock allocation
- **HSE provisions are non-negotiable**: Operator stop-work authority and incident reporting requirements should not be weakened
- **Mark all statutory citations [VERIFY]** — state O&G statutes are frequently amended
- **Conspicuous provisions**: Jury waivers, liability limitations, and warranty disclaimers should be presented in bold or caps per state enforceability requirements
- **Data ownership**: All well, reservoir, geological, and production data defaults to Operator; Contractor gets use-license only during engagement
- **Survival**: Indemnification, confidentiality, IP, payment obligations, dispute resolution, and audit rights must expressly survive termination
