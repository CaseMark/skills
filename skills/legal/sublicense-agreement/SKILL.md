# Sublicense Agreement

Drafts a sublicense agreement that conveys derivative IP rights from an existing licensee to a third party while maintaining strict compliance with the Master License Agreement.

## Prerequisites

1. **Master License Agreement** — full executed copy identifying: original licensor, execution date, licensed IP (patents/trademarks/copyrights/trade secrets/know-how), scope of rights, territorial and field-of-use restrictions, royalty obligations, and sublicensing provisions
2. **Sublicensing authorization** — confirmation that the Master License expressly permits sublicensing; if consent is required, evidence of licensor approval
3. **Party details** — legal names, jurisdictions of organization, principal places of business, authorized signatories for both Sublicensor and Sublicensee
4. **Business terms** — exclusive vs. non-exclusive, territory, field-of-use, term, royalty rate, payment structure, minimum royalties or milestones

## Output Structure

Draft a formal agreement with the following sections:

### 1. Preamble & Recitals

| Recital | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Master License | Identify original licensor, execution date, licensed IP, cite specific sublicensing provision |
| Sublicensing Right | Affirm Master License grants sublicensing rights; reference section number |
| Purpose | Sublicensee's intended business use |

### 2. Definitions

Define at minimum:
- **Licensed IP** — specific rights being sublicensed
- **Net Sales** — gross revenue minus: (a) returns/allowances, (b) trade/quantity discounts, (c) sales/use/VAT taxes, (d) freight/insurance, (e) customs duties
- **Territory** — geographic scope (must not exceed Master License)
- **Field of Use** — permitted applications/industries/markets
- **Royalty Period** — calendar quarter unless otherwise specified

### 3. Grant of Rights

- [ ] Exclusive or non-exclusive designation
- [ ] Specific rights granted (make, use, sell, offer for sale, import, distribute)
- [ ] "Subject to all terms and conditions of the Master License"
- [ ] "Sublicensee receives no greater rights than Sublicensor holds under the Master License"
- [ ] Territory and field-of-use limitations stated explicitly
- [ ] Prohibition on further sublicensing (unless expressly permitted)

### 4. Scope & Limitations

Explicitly state that territory, field-of-use, and duration shall not exceed Master License grants. Flow down all Master License restrictions:
- Prohibited uses
- Quality control requirements
- Marking/notice obligations
- Export control compliance

### 5. Flow-Down & Incorporation

- Sublicensee bound by all applicable Master License terms
- Key flow-down obligations: confidentiality, quality standards, indemnification, insurance, reporting/record-keeping
- Sublicensor represents it has provided complete copy of Master License to Sublicensee

### 6. Financial Terms

```
Royalty Rate:        [__]% of Net Sales
Payment Schedule:    Within [30] days after each calendar quarter
Reporting:           Detailed royalty report with supporting documentation
Upfront Payment:     $[__] due on execution (if applicable)
Minimum Royalty:     $[__] per [year/quarter] (if applicable)
Milestone Payments:  [describe triggers] (if applicable)
Audit Rights:        Upon [30] days written notice; if underpayment
                     exceeds [5]%, Sublicensee bears audit costs
```

Ensure sublicense royalties are structured so Sublicensor can satisfy upstream royalty obligations under the Master License.

### 7. Relationship to Master License

- Master License controls in event of conflict
- Sublicensee must comply with all obligations Sublicensor owes to original licensor
- Sublicensor covenants to maintain Master License in good standing and not cause its termination

### 8. Representations & Warranties

| Party | Representations |
|-------|----------------|
| **Sublicensor** | Valid license under Master License; sublicensing permitted; required consents obtained; Master License in full force, no breaches |
| **Sublicensee** | Authority to enter agreement; no conflicts; capable of performance; acknowledges sublicense is derivative of and subordinate to Master License |
| **Mutual** | Authority to execute; compliance with applicable laws; no litigation threatening rights |

### 9. Termination

| Trigger | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| Master License terminates/expires | **Automatic** termination — Sublicensee acknowledges and accepts this risk |
| Material breach | Written notice + [30]-day cure period |
| Insolvency/bankruptcy | Termination at non-breaching party's option |

**Post-termination obligations:**
- Immediate cessation of all sublicensed rights
- Return or destruction of confidential information (with certification)
- Payment of all accrued royalties
- Wind-down period of [__] days for existing inventory (if applicable)
- Survival of: payment obligations, confidentiality, indemnification, audit rights

### 10. Indemnification & Liability

- Sublicensee indemnifies Sublicensor against claims arising from: Sublicensee's use of licensed IP, breach of agreement, violation of laws
- Address interaction with Master License indemnification provisions
- Limitation of liability: cap at [amount/royalties paid], excluding indemnification obligations and breaches of confidentiality

### 11. General Provisions

Governing law, jurisdiction (consistent with Master License if required), assignment restrictions (Sublicensee may not assign without consent), written amendments only, notices with full contact details, severability, integration clause referencing both this Agreement and the Master License.

### 12. Signature Block

Sublicensor and Sublicensee — name, title, date, with confirmation of authority to bind.

## Guidelines

- **Master License primacy**: Every grant, territory, field-of-use, and term provision must be cross-referenced against the Master License — never draft broader rights than the Sublicensor holds
- **Consent verification**: If Master License requires licensor consent for sublicensing, document consent in recitals and attach as exhibit
- **Automatic termination risk**: Always include explicit Sublicensee acknowledgment that sublicense terminates if Master License terminates
- **Royalty stacking**: Structure sublicense royalties to ensure Sublicensor can meet upstream royalty obligations while maintaining margin
- **Use bracketed placeholders** for all deal-specific terms: `[Date]`, `[Percentage]`, `[Territory]`, etc.
- **Jurisdiction**: Default U.S. law; flag state-specific IP licensing statutes if applicable
- **Defined terms**: Capitalize consistently throughout; cross-reference definitions section on first use
