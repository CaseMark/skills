# Interrogatories to Defendant — Personal Injury

Drafts plaintiff's interrogatories to defendant that pin down factual contentions, identify witnesses and evidence, and lay the foundation for depositions and trial.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case caption details** — court name, case number, full party names
2. **Operative pleadings** — complaint (claims alleged) and answer (affirmative defenses asserted)
3. **Jurisdiction** — federal vs. state; state-specific interrogatory rules if applicable
4. **Numerical limit** — confirm limit (FRCP 33: 25 including discrete subparts; state rules vary)
5. **Sequential designation** — first, second, etc. set
6. **Attorney information** — name, bar number, firm, address, phone, email

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption

Standard litigation caption per local formatting rules. Title: "Plaintiff's [First/Second/etc.] Set of Interrogatories to Defendant."

### 2. Introduction

- Formally propound under the governing rule (e.g., FRCP 33 or state equivalent)
- State response deadline (30 days unless local rule differs)
- State answers must be separate, full, in writing, and under oath

### 3. Definitions

Define at minimum:

| Term | Scope |
|------|-------|
| IDENTIFY (person) | Full name, address, phone, employer, relationship to parties |
| IDENTIFY (document) | Author, date, type, custodian, location, Bates number if produced |
| IDENTIFY (communication) | Date, participants, medium, substance |
| PERSON | Natural persons and all entities |
| DOCUMENT | All tangible and ESI formats |
| COMMUNICATION | Every form of information exchange |
| INCIDENT | The specific event(s) giving rise to this lawsuit |
| YOU/YOUR | Defendant including agents, employees, representatives |

Draft definitions broadly to prevent evasion but keep them defensible.

### 4. Instructions

Include all of the following:

- [ ] Duty to supplement under FRCP 26(e) or state equivalent
- [ ] Answer each interrogatory separately and fully
- [ ] Privilege claims must be accompanied by a privilege log
- [ ] Lack of personal knowledge does not excuse duty to investigate
- [ ] If interrogatory contains subparts, answer each separately
- [ ] Option to produce business records under FRCP 33(d) with specification of records

### 5. Substantive Interrogatories

Organize questions into these categories, tailoring to the specific facts of the case:

**A. Foundational / Witness Identification**
- All persons with knowledge of relevant facts (name, address, phone, substance of knowledge)
- All persons defendant may call at trial and subject of expected testimony
- All experts retained or consulted (identity, opinions, basis)

**B. Defendant's Version of Events**
- Detailed factual account of how the incident occurred
- Every act or omission by plaintiff that defendant contends contributed to incident or damages
- Basis for any comparative/contributory fault defense

**C. Evidence Identification**
- All documents, photos, recordings, tangible evidence relating to the incident
- All statements obtained from any person regarding the incident (written, recorded, or oral summaries)
- Surveillance or investigation conducted regarding plaintiff or incident

**D. Personal Injury–Specific**
- Defendant's knowledge of plaintiff's injuries
- All insurance agreements potentially providing coverage (carrier, policy number, limits)
- Prior similar incidents or claims involving defendant (same condition, product, premises, etc.)
- Damages defendant contends plaintiff failed to mitigate, with factual basis

**E. Contention Interrogatories**
For each affirmative defense in the answer:
- State every fact supporting the defense
- Identify every document supporting the defense
- Identify every person with knowledge of facts supporting the defense

### 6. Signature Block & Certificate of Service

- Date, attorney signature line, name, bar number, firm, address, phone, email
- Certificate of service: method (e-filing/mail/email), date, recipient counsel name and address

## Guidelines

- **Count subparts rigorously** — discrete subparts count toward the numerical limit; structure compound questions carefully to stay within the cap
- **Tailor to the case** — never submit generic form interrogatories without customizing to the specific facts, claims, and defenses
- **Proportionality** — each interrogatory must be relevant and proportional to the needs of the case (FRCP 26(b)(1))
- **Avoid objection bait** — do not draft interrogatories that are overbroad, unduly burdensome, or invade privilege
- **Strategic sequencing** — front-load questions that yield the most valuable information for depositions and settlement evaluation
- **Check local rules** — some jurisdictions require pattern/form interrogatories (e.g., California DISC-001) or impose different limits and procedures
- **Contention interrogatories timing** — some courts defer contention interrogatories until late discovery; flag for attorney review if served early
