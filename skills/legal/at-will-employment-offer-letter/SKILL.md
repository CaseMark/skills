# At-Will Employment Offer Letter

Drafts a formal U.S. offer letter that communicates all material employment terms while preserving at-will status and avoiding implied-contract liability.

## Prerequisites

1. **Employer information** — full legal entity name, address, authorized signatory name and title
2. **Candidate information** — full legal name, mailing address
3. **Position details** — job title, department, direct supervisor, start date, work location, remote/travel requirements
4. **Compensation** — base salary or hourly rate, pay frequency, FLSA classification (exempt/non-exempt)
5. **Variable pay** — bonus, commission, or incentive plan details (if any)
6. **Benefits summary** — health, dental, vision, 401(k), PTO, sick leave, eligibility waiting period
7. **Conditions precedent** — background check, drug screen, reference check, I-9, required separate agreements (NDA, arbitration, non-compete)
8. **Acceptance deadline** — typically 5–10 business days from letter date

## Output Structure

### 1. Header
- Company letterhead (name, address, contact)
- Date
- Candidate name and address (formal business letter format)

### 2. Opening
- Express genuine enthusiasm; state at-will status immediately:
  > *"We are pleased to offer you **at-will employment** as [Job Title]..."*
- No language suggesting guaranteed duration, job security, or promises of continued employment

### 3. Position Details

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Job Title | Exact title as it will appear in company records |
| Department | Department/division name |
| Reports To | Supervisor name and title |
| Start Date | Specific date (or "contingent on completion of pre-employment requirements") |
| Work Location | Office address; remote/hybrid terms if applicable |
| Travel/Relocation | State explicitly if required |
| Duties | Brief summary with reservation clause: *"Duties may be modified at the Company's discretion as business needs evolve."* |

### 4. Compensation

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Base Salary | Annual figure + per-period breakdown (e.g., "$85,000/yr, paid bi-weekly at $3,269.23") |
| Hourly Rate | If non-exempt under FLSA, state rate and standard schedule |
| Bonus/Commission | State that it is **discretionary**, subject to company policy, and contingent on continued employment and performance |
| Benefits | List coverages; specify eligibility start date; include: *"Benefits are governed by official plan documents, which control in case of any discrepancy and are subject to modification."* |

### 5. At-Will Clause *(legally critical — do not soften)*

> *"Your employment with [Company Name] is **at-will**, meaning that either you or the Company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, for any reason or no reason, with or without cause, and with or without notice. No one other than the [CEO/President] has authority to alter this at-will relationship or enter into any agreement for employment for a specified period, and any such modification must be in writing and signed by the [CEO/President]."*

- If an introductory/probationary period applies: avoid any language suggesting employment becomes "permanent" or changes status after that period.

### 6. Conditions Precedent

List all that apply:
- [ ] Satisfactory background check
- [ ] Drug screening
- [ ] Reference verification
- [ ] Proof of eligibility to work in the U.S. (Form I-9)
- [ ] Execution of [NDA / Arbitration Agreement / PIIA / Non-Compete]

> Reference separate agreements by name but **do not incorporate their terms** into the offer letter.

Include integration clause: *"This letter, together with any agreements referenced herein, constitutes the entire agreement regarding employment terms and supersedes all prior discussions or representations."*

### 7. Acceptance Instructions

- State specific deadline: *"This offer expires at [time] on [date] if not accepted."*
- Instruct candidate to sign, date, and return via [email PDF / physical mail]
- Note that offer may be withdrawn if conditions precedent are not met

### 8. Closing & Signature Blocks

**Company block:**
```
[Authorized Signatory Name]
[Title — CEO, President, or HR Director]
[Company Name]
```

**Candidate acceptance block:**
```
I accept the offer of at-will employment as described above and acknowledge
that I have read and understood all terms of this offer.

Signature: _______________________ Date: ___________
Printed Name: _______________________
```

## Guidelines

- **Never** use language implying job security, guaranteed tenure, or "permanent" status
- **Never** allow anyone other than the designated officer to be named as having authority to modify at-will status
- For **non-exempt** employees: confirm hourly rate and overtime eligibility comply with FLSA and applicable state wage laws
- **State-specific compliance**: verify requirements for wage statement disclosures, at-will disclaimers, and non-compete enforceability (California, Minnesota, North Dakota, and Oklahoma have significant restrictions)
- Keep benefits descriptions high-level; defer detail to plan documents and orientation
- Do not incorporate the terms of any separate agreement (NDA, arbitration clause) into the body of the offer letter
- Letter should be 2–3 pages; professional tone, plain language, no legalese beyond the required at-will clause
