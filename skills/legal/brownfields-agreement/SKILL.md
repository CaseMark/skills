# Brownfields Agreement

Drafts a transaction-ready Brownfields Agreement covering the full project lifecycle — due diligence through long-term stewardship — with CERCLA liability protections, remediation obligations, and institutional controls.

## Prerequisites

1. **Phase I/II ESA reports** — contaminant data, sampling results, remedial alternatives analysis, cost estimates
2. **Party identification** — full legal names, addresses, organizational details for all signatories (owner, purchaser, lenders, governmental entities)
3. **Property records** — legal description, site maps, historical use records, prior regulatory correspondence
4. **Regulatory program** — applicable state VCP or brownfields initiative; EPA grant award details if applicable
5. **Cleanup standards** — ARARs, state risk-based standards, intended future use (residential / commercial / industrial)
6. **Financial terms** — funding sources (EPA grants, TIF, private capital), cost-sharing arrangements, financial assurance requirements

## Agreement Structure

| Article | Key Content |
|---|---|
| Preamble & Recitals | Property location/legal description, contamination history, brownfields eligibility under CERCLA §§ 101(39)–(40), AAI completion per ASTM E1527-21 or 40 CFR Part 312, EPA grant references |
| Definitions | Source from CERCLA statutory language, 40 CFR Parts 300–312, state VCP rules, ASTM standards; define: Hazardous Substance, Release, Remediation, Institutional Controls, Engineering Controls, AAI, BFPP, ARARs |
| Environmental Liability | BFPP continuing obligations (CERCLA § 101(40)), innocent landowner / contiguous property owner defenses, mutual indemnification, third-party claim procedures |
| Remediation Scope | Investigation, cleanup, and monitoring activities; ARARs-based performance standards; contingency protocol for unexpected conditions |
| Institutional & Engineering Controls | Environmental covenants, deed restrictions, groundwater use prohibitions, cap maintenance obligations, long-term stewardship funding |
| Regulatory Compliance | State VCP reporting obligations, EPA grant conditions (Davis-Bacon, competitive procurement, NEPA/NHPA reviews), agency access and inspection rights |
| Financial Terms | Cost allocation by party, milestone-based payment schedule, overrun/contingency procedures, PRP cost recovery rights, escrow/LC/bond requirements |
| Representations & Warranties | Owner: knowledge of conditions, compliance during ownership, no pending enforcement. Purchaser: BFPP qualifications, no affiliation with prior responsible parties, financial capacity |
| Covenants | Maintain BFPP status, IC/EC compliance, site access for monitoring, insurance maintenance, new contamination notification protocol |
| Dispute Resolution | Negotiation → mediation (environmental law specialist) → arbitration/litigation; emergency injunctive relief carve-out |
| Execution & Recordation | Signature blocks with authority, conditions precedent to effectiveness, land records recordation, amendment/assignment procedures |

## BFPP Continuing Obligations Checklist

CERCLA § 101(40) requires the following — draft as affirmative covenants with cure periods:

- [ ] Exercise appropriate care with respect to hazardous substances at the facility
- [ ] Comply with all land use restrictions and institutional controls
- [ ] Not impede the effectiveness or integrity of any engineering control
- [ ] Provide full cooperation, assistance, and access for response actions
- [ ] Comply with information requests and subpoenas from regulatory agencies
- [ ] Provide legally required notices upon discovery of any release

> **Critical**: Loss of any continuing obligation eliminates BFPP protection retroactively. Consider step-in rights for governmental counterparty as a cure mechanism.

## Institutional Controls Framework

| Control Type | Mechanism | Recording Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Land use restriction | Deed restriction / environmental covenant | County land records |
| Groundwater prohibition | Restrictive covenant, well permit condition | Record + notify water authority |
| Cap/barrier maintenance | Engineering control covenant | Record + dedicated maintenance fund |
| Vapor intrusion | Sub-slab depressurization covenant | Record + inspection schedule |
| Chain-of-title notice | Environmental notice per state statute | Record with title documents |

## Financial Provisions Checklist

- [ ] EPA brownfields grant compliance — § 104(k) [VERIFY], Davis-Bacon Act, competitive procurement
- [ ] Uniform Guidance (2 CFR Part 200) compliance if federal grant funds involved
- [ ] Cost-sharing schedule tied to defined project milestones
- [ ] Contingency reserve (typically 10–20% of remediation estimate)
- [ ] Cost recovery rights against PRPs; specify allocation of any recovered funds
- [ ] Financial assurance for closure/stewardship (escrow, letter of credit, or performance bond)
- [ ] Environmental insurance requirements (coverage amount, policy terms, premium allocation)

## Guidelines

- **AAI standard**: Confirm completion per 40 CFR Part 312; ASTM E1527-21 is current [VERIFY supersession of E1527-13]; document in recitals
- **Affiliate screen**: Purchaser must have no familial or contractual affiliation with any prior owner/operator per CERCLA § 101(40)(H) — represent and verify
- **CERCLA pre-emption**: State indemnification provisions cannot override federal CERCLA liability — structure indemnities to complement statutory defenses, not displace them; avoid inadvertently creating arranger or transporter liability
- **State VCP variation**: Cleanup standards, IC mechanisms, and regulatory oversight requirements vary significantly by state — confirm applicable program before drafting cleanup standard provisions
- **Unknown contamination**: Include discovery protocol with notification timelines (to all parties and regulators), investigation cost allocation, and remediation responsibility triggers
- **Recordation timing**: Environmental covenants should be recorded promptly upon closing; confirm state-specific recording, notarization, and indexing requirements
- **Assignment**: Ensure any assignment provision preserves BFPP/innocent landowner status and does not create successor CERCLA liability — require regulatory notice on assignment
