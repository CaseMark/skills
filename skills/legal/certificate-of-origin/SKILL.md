# USMCA/NAFTA Certificate of Origin

Prepares a legally compliant Certificate of Origin certifying goods qualify for preferential tariff treatment under USMCA (effective July 1, 2020) or legacy NAFTA.

## Prerequisites

1. **Commercial invoices and bills of materials** — for goods being certified
2. **Production records** — material sourcing, manufacturing location, supplier documentation
3. **Party information** — legal names, addresses, tax IDs for exporter, producer, and importer
4. **HS tariff classification** — at minimum 6-digit; 8- or 10-digit if required by importing country
5. **Prior origin determinations** — any existing tariff rulings or certificates for same goods

## Process

### 1. Determine Applicable Agreement

| Shipment Date | Agreement | Key Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Before July 1, 2020 | NAFTA (Ch. 4-5) | Legacy criteria apply |
| On or after July 1, 2020 | USMCA (Ch. 4-5) | Current framework |

Confirm whether certificate is **single shipment** or **blanket** (max 12 months from signature per USMCA Art. 5.2).

### 2. Document Party Information

For each party (exporter, producer, importer), collect:

- [ ] Full legal business name (as registered with government authorities)
- [ ] Complete physical address (street, city, state/province, postal code, country)
- [ ] Tax ID / business registration number / customs-assigned codes

**Special cases:**
- Exporter = Producer → state "Same" in producer field
- Producer confidential → "Available to Customs upon request" (USMCA Art. 5.2); triggers verification risk
- Importer unknown → "Unknown" / "Various" / "To Whom It May Concern"; may need updating before preference claim

### 3. Describe Goods

Each good must include:
- Commercial name and technical specs (model/part numbers)
- Material composition
- Dimensions/capacity (if relevant)
- Intended use
- HS classification at 6+ digits matching what will appear on entry docs

**Do not** use vague descriptions ("parts," "merchandise").

### 4. Determine Origin Criterion

#### USMCA Criteria

| Criterion | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **A** | Wholly obtained/produced entirely in US/MX/CA — zero third-country content |
| **B** | Produced entirely in US/MX/CA using only originating materials |
| **C** | Produced in US/MX/CA using non-originating materials that satisfy product-specific rules (tariff shift, RVC, etc.) |
| **D** | Automotive goods meeting enhanced USMCA auto rules |

#### Regional Value Content (if required)

Calculate using either:
- **Transaction value method**: RVC = ((TV − VNM) / TV) × 100
- **Net cost method**: RVC = ((NC − VNM) / NC) × 100

Maintain calculation worksheets as supporting documentation.

#### Tariff Shift Analysis

Confirm non-originating materials classify in a different heading/subheading than the finished good per product-specific rules. Flag sector-specific rules for **automotive**, **textiles/apparel**, and **agricultural** goods.

### 5. Draft Certification Statement

The certification must include:

- [ ] Affirmation that goods qualify as originating under USMCA Ch. 4-5 (or NAFTA Ch. 4-5)
- [ ] Basis of certifier's knowledge (direct examination, production records, role in company)
- [ ] Acknowledgment of verification rights (written requests, questionnaires, facility visits)
- [ ] Record retention commitment: **5 years** from date of signature (USMCA) or importation (NAFTA)
- [ ] 30-day response obligation for verification requests
- [ ] Awareness of penalties for false certification:
  - Civil penalties up to domestic value per violation
  - Criminal prosecution (18 U.S.C. § 1001 [VERIFY] or equivalent MX/CA provisions)
  - Duty liability at MFN rate plus interest
  - Suspension/revocation of customs privileges

### 6. Signature Block

| Field | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Signatory name | As in company records |
| Title/position | Must have authority to bind certifying party |
| Company name | Full legal name |
| Date | DD/MM/YYYY or unambiguous format |
| Phone | With country + area code |
| Email | For customs correspondence |

If exporter certifies based on producer-supplied information, obtain and retain written origin statement from producer.

## Output Structure

Generate the certificate with these sections in order:

1. **Header** — Agreement identifier (USMCA or NAFTA), blanket vs. single shipment, coverage period
2. **Exporter block** — Name, address, tax ID
3. **Producer block** — Name, address, tax ID (or confidentiality notation)
4. **Importer block** — Name, address, tax ID (or "Unknown")
5. **Goods table** — Description, HS number, origin criterion, country of origin per line item
6. **Certification statement** — Full legal declaration
7. **Signature block**

Append a **compliance memo** noting:
- Basis for origin determination
- Supporting docs to maintain on file
- Risk flags (marginal RVC, ambiguous tariff shifts, sourcing changes)
- Recommendations for ongoing compliance monitoring

## Guidelines

- Always verify agreement applicability by shipment date — do not mix NAFTA/USMCA frameworks
- Flag RVC calculations within 5% of minimum threshold as high-risk
- Mark any uncertain HS classifications for client review before submission
- Blanket certificates must not exceed 12-month coverage
- All fields must be completed; use "N/A" or "Unknown" only where legitimately unavailable
- Review for consistency across certificate, commercial invoice, and entry documentation
- Do not provide final legal sign-off — flag for attorney review where origin qualification is marginal or rules interpretation is unclear
