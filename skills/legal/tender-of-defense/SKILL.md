# Contractual Tender of Defense Letter

Drafts a formal demand letter requiring a contracting party to assume defense and indemnification obligations under a commercial agreement in pending litigation.

## Prerequisites

1. **Underlying contract** — with indemnification clause, insurance requirements, notice provisions, scope of work, and choice of law
2. **Complaint/petition** — filed in the underlying litigation with case caption, court, case number, and specific allegations
3. **Factual background** — incident reports, correspondence, photographs, witness statements related to the claim
4. **Defense cost records** (if applicable) — billing summaries, fee statements for costs already incurred
5. **Contract notice provisions** — designated recipient, method of delivery, and timing requirements

## Document Analysis Checklist

Before drafting, extract and analyze from uploaded documents:

| Element | What to Find |
|---|---|
| **Indemnification clause** | Broad form ("arising out of") vs. limited form ("caused by negligence"); duty to defend trigger (immediate vs. post-determination) |
| **Insurance requirements** | Coverage types, minimum limits, additional insured obligations, primary & non-contributory language |
| **Notice provisions** | Designated recipient, delivery method, timing requirements |
| **Scope of work** | Services/obligations recipient assumed under contract |
| **Carve-outs/exclusions** | Mutual indemnification, comparative fault allocation, caps |
| **Choice of law** | Governing jurisdiction for enforceability |
| **Complaint allegations** | Specific paragraphs implicating recipient's scope of work |
| **Procedural deadlines** | Responsive pleading dates, discovery schedule, trial date |

## Output Structure

### Format Requirements
- Professional legal letter for law firm letterhead
- Sent via certified mail, return receipt requested
- Address to contractual notice designee; if none, to registered agent AND general counsel
- Reference line: `RE: Tender of Defense and Indemnification — [Plaintiff] v. [Client] — [Court], Case No. [Number] — Pursuant to [Contract Type] dated [Date]`

### Required Sections

**1. Opening Demand**
- Identify representation, pending litigation (full case caption), specific contract (date, parties, section numbers)
- Make explicit, unambiguous demand for assumption of defense AND indemnification
- Cite specific contractual sections

**2. Contractual Provisions**
- Quote indemnification clause verbatim with section/page references
- Quote insurance requirements verbatim
- Brief interpretive analysis connecting language to current allegations
- If "arising out of" language: note broad causal-connection trigger
- If "regardless of negligence" language: note elimination of comparative fault defense

**3. Factual Nexus**
- Quote specific complaint paragraphs implicating recipient's work
- Quote contract scope-of-work provisions
- Explicitly connect allegations → contractual obligations
- Emphasize duty to defend is broader than duty to indemnify (triggered by potential coverage)

**4. Insurance Coverage Demand**
- Reference exact insurance requirements from contract
- Demand immediate tender to recipient's CGL carrier
- Demand within 10 days:
  - Proof of tender to carrier
  - Current certificate of insurance showing client as additional insured
  - Certified copy of actual policy endorsement
  - Written acknowledgment of primary & non-contributory status

**5. Specific Demands with Deadlines**

| Demand | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Written acknowledgment of tender | 10 days |
| Retain qualified defense counsel acceptable to client | 15 days |
| Assume all defense costs from inception; reimburse $[amount] already incurred | 15 days |
| Tender claim to CGL carrier + provide proof | 10 days |
| Provide certificates of insurance + certified endorsements | 15 days |
| Written confirmation of full indemnification obligations | 10 days |

Adjust deadlines based on litigation timeline; flag imminent procedural deadlines (responsive pleadings, MSJ, CMC).

**6. Consequences of Non-Compliance**
State that failure constitutes material breach, preserving rights to:
- Breach of contract claim for all defense costs + attorney's fees (underlying + enforcement)
- Breach of covenant of good faith and fair dealing
- Recovery of damages, settlements, or judgments
- Consequential damages
- Note: delayed tender to carrier may prejudice recipient's own coverage — that risk falls on recipient

**7. Litigation Timeline**
- Current procedural posture, filing date, responsive pleading deadline
- Discovery schedule, deposition dates, CMC date, trial date
- Emphasize urgency to avoid default, waiver of defenses, duplicative work

**8. Attachments List**
- Exhibit A: Complaint
- Exhibit B: Contract (full or relevant excerpts)
- Exhibit C: Defense cost summary (if applicable)
- Exhibit D: Incident reports, correspondence, photos (as applicable)

**9. Reservation of Rights**
- Tender is without waiver of any rights, defenses, or claims
- Client reserves right to supplement tender with discovery information
- Communication subject to attorney-client privilege and work product protection
- No admission of fact or liability
- Acceptance of tender does not waive client's independent claims against recipient

## Guidelines

- **Tone**: Authoritative and firm but professional; recipients may be business executives encountering this for the first time, not claims adjusters
- **Educational framing**: Where appropriate, explain the tender process and recommend recipient forward to broker/carrier immediately; frame as contractual partnership, not adversarial attack
- **Business relationship**: Acknowledge ongoing relationship if one exists; frame as fulfillment of bargained-for obligations
- **Every assertion must cite** specific contract section/page numbers or complaint paragraph numbers
- **Broad vs. narrow indemnity**: Tailor analysis to the actual clause language; do not assume broad-form if the clause is limited
- **Preempt coverage defenses**: If recipient might argue claims fall outside scope, address directly with applicable reasoning
- **Length**: 4–6 pages depending on complexity
- **Active voice, direct sentences, no ambiguity** in demands or deadlines
- **Mark uncertain citations with [VERIFY]**
