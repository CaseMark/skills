# Legal Risk Assessment Report

Evaluates legal risks in business scenarios, transactions, or operational decisions and delivers an executive-ready report with categorized risks, quantified exposure, and mitigation strategies.

## Prerequisites

1. **Scenario description** — transaction summary, business venture details, or operational decision under review
2. **Source documents** — contracts, term sheets, regulatory filings, correspondence, corporate records
3. **Organizational context** — industry, jurisdiction(s), risk tolerance, strategic objectives
4. **Known constraints** — timeline, budget, regulatory environment, stakeholder concerns

## Process

### Phase 1: Document Review & Fact Extraction

Extract from all provided materials:

| Element | Look For |
|---|---|
| Obligations & duties | Performance requirements, deadlines, conditions precedent |
| Representations & warranties | Scope, survival periods, indemnification triggers |
| Financial exposure | Stated amounts, penalty provisions, liquidated damages |
| Jurisdictional issues | Choice of law, forum selection, regulatory bodies |
| Notice & cure provisions | Timelines, methods, consequences of non-compliance |
| Ambiguous or silent terms | Missing definitions, unclear allocation of risk, gaps |

### Phase 2: Risk Identification

Categorize all material risks using this framework:

| Category | Key Inquiry |
|---|---|
| **Regulatory compliance** | Licensing, permits, reporting obligations, pending rule changes |
| **Contractual liability** | Breach exposure, indemnification chains, limitation of liability gaps |
| **Litigation probability** | Dispute history, enforceability of provisions, venue risk |
| **IP vulnerability** | Ownership disputes, licensing gaps, infringement exposure |
| **Employment & labor** | Classification, non-competes, WARN Act, benefits obligations |
| **Tax implications** | Structure-dependent tax exposure, transfer pricing, nexus issues |
| **Reputational & governance** | Board fiduciary duties, disclosure obligations, ESG considerations |

### Phase 3: Risk Scoring

Rate each identified risk on a 5×5 matrix:

**Likelihood scale:**
1. Remote — no precedent, strong protections in place
2. Unlikely — weak precedent, some protections
3. Possible — mixed precedent, standard exposure
4. Likely — strong precedent or active enforcement trend
5. Near certain — clear violation or pending action

**Impact scale:**
1. Negligible — <$50K, no operational effect
2. Minor — $50K–$500K, limited disruption
3. Moderate — $500K–$5M, significant operational impact
4. Major — $5M–$50M, strategic consequences
5. Severe — >$50M, existential threat or regulatory shutdown

**Risk rating = Likelihood × Impact** → Low (1–6), Medium (7–12), High (13–19), Critical (20–25)

### Phase 4: Mitigation Strategies

For each Medium, High, or Critical risk, propose mitigations:

| Strategy Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Eliminate** | Restructure transaction, remove problematic terms |
| **Transfer** | Insurance, indemnification, contractual risk allocation |
| **Reduce** | Compliance protocols, enhanced due diligence, representations & warranties |
| **Accept** | Document rationale, establish reserves, monitor triggers |

Assess each mitigation for feasibility, cost, and residual risk after implementation.

### Phase 5: Information Gap Analysis

Flag explicitly:
- Missing documents or facts that affect risk precision
- Unsettled legal standards or pending regulatory changes
- Recommended due diligence steps or expert consultations to close gaps

## Output Structure

```
## Executive Summary
- 3–5 sentence overview of risk posture
- Top 3 risks with ratings
- Go / No-Go / Conditional recommendation

## Risk Matrix
[Table: Risk | Category | Likelihood | Impact | Rating | Key Driver]

## Detailed Risk Analysis
### [Risk Category]
#### [Specific Risk]
- **Exposure**: factual basis with document references
- **Legal basis**: applicable authority, statutory or case law
- **Likelihood rationale**: enforcement trends, precedent strength
- **Impact quantification**: dollar range, operational consequences
- **Mitigation options**: ranked by effectiveness

## Information Gaps & Limitations
[Bulleted list with recommended next steps]

## Recommendations
- Numbered, actionable items tied to specific risks
- Prioritized by risk rating and feasibility
- Clear decision framework: proceed / modify / decline
```

## Guidelines

- Reference specific contract sections, statutes, and factual circumstances — never offer generic risk language
- Quantify financial exposure with ranges; acknowledge uncertainty explicitly
- Calibrate recommendations to the organization's stated risk tolerance
- Distinguish between risks that are deal-breakers vs. commercially acceptable
- Mark any cited authority you cannot verify with `[VERIFY]`
- Do not minimize risks to favor a preferred outcome — present the full picture
- Note jurisdiction-specific variations where U.S. state law divergence is material
- Keep the executive summary accessible to non-lawyers; use plain language for ratings and recommendations
