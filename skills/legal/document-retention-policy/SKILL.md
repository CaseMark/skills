# Document Retention Policy (Nonprofit)

Drafts a board-adoptable document retention policy for nonprofit organizations with legally grounded retention schedules, destruction protocols, and litigation hold procedures.

## Prerequisites

1. **Organization details** — name, state of incorporation, operating states, EIN, tax-exempt status (501(c)(3), etc.)
2. **Existing policies** — any current records management or retention documents
3. **Record inventory** — types and volume of records maintained (paper and electronic)
4. **Industry context** — mission area (healthcare, education, social services) for sector-specific regulations
5. **Federal grant status** — whether org receives federal funds (triggers 2 CFR Part 200)

## Output Structure

### 1. Header Block

Include: policy title, effective date, version number, supersedes line, board resolution reference, signature block for board chair.

### 2. Introduction & Scope

State that the policy:
- Applies to all employees, officers, directors, volunteers, and contractors
- Governs all records in any format (paper, electronic, cloud)
- Was adopted by board resolution on [DATE]

### 3. Record Categories & Retention Schedule

Draft a table using these minimum periods. Adjust upward for state-specific requirements.

| Category | Examples | Minimum Retention | Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tax Returns & 990s** | Form 990, 990-T, schedules | **Permanent** | IRS Rev. Proc. 98-25 |
| **Tax Supporting Docs** | GL, receipts, donor records | **7 years** | IRC § 6501(e) (6-yr audit window + 1) |
| **Governance** | Articles, bylaws, minutes, resolutions, conflict-of-interest disclosures | **Permanent** | State nonprofit corp. statutes |
| **Audit Reports** | External audit, management letters | **Permanent** | Best practice; grantor requirements |
| **Bank & Financial** | Statements, reconciliations, AP/AR | **7 years** | IRC § 6501; UCC § 2-725 |
| **Grant Records** | Applications, reports, budgets, correspondence | **Per grant terms or 7 years after closeout** | 2 CFR § 200.334 (federal); funder terms |
| **Employment Tax** | W-2s, W-4s, 941s | **4 years after tax due/paid** | IRC § 6501(a); Treas. Reg. § 31.6001-1 |
| **Personnel Files** | Applications, evaluations, termination docs | **7 years after separation** | Title VII (300 days + litigation buffer); ADA; ADEA |
| **I-9 Forms** | Employment verification | **3 years from hire or 1 year after separation (whichever later)** | 8 CFR § 274a.2(b)(2)(i) |
| **Payroll Records** | Timesheets, pay stubs, deductions | **7 years** | FLSA § 11(c) (3 yr); state wage laws (longer) |
| **Benefits/ERISA** | Plan documents, SPDs, 5500s | **6 years after filing** | ERISA § 107; 29 CFR § 2520.107-1 |
| **Contracts & Leases** | Vendor agreements, MOUs, leases | **10 years after expiration** | Statute of limitations + buffer |
| **Insurance Policies** | Policies, certificates, claims | **Permanent** (occurrence-based) | Claims may surface decades later |
| **Program/Client Records** | Service delivery, outcomes, case files | **7 years or per funder/licensing** | Varies by sector regulation |
| **Correspondence** | Letters, emails (substantive) | **3 years** (unless tied to above categories) | Operational |
| **Electronic Backups** | System backups, databases | **Per underlying record category** | Mirror retention of source records |

> **Note:** These are *minimum* periods. Always check the org's state nonprofit corporation act and any sector-specific licensing (e.g., HIPAA for health nonprofits = 6 years for PHI records [VERIFY]).

### 4. Storage & Maintenance

Specify for both physical and electronic records:

**Physical:**
- Secure, climate-controlled storage with restricted access
- Labeling system tied to retention schedule categories
- Annual inventory review

**Electronic:**
- Encrypted storage with access controls and audit logging
- Regular automated backups (on-site + off-site/cloud)
- Email retention integrated with organizational retention schedule
- Version control for governance and policy documents

### 5. Destruction Procedures

```
DESTRUCTION PROTOCOL
1. Records Manager confirms retention period expired
2. Cross-check against active litigation holds
3. Department head reviews and approves destruction list
4. Execute destruction:
   - Paper: cross-cut shredding (NIST SP 800-88 equivalent)
   - Electronic: secure deletion or degaussing per NIST SP 800-88
5. Log entry: document type, date range, destruction date,
   method, authorized by, executed by
6. Retain destruction logs permanently
```

### 6. Litigation Hold Procedures

**Triggers:** Reasonably anticipated litigation, subpoena, government investigation, audit, or regulatory inquiry.

**Protocol:**
1. Executive Director or legal counsel issues written hold notice
2. Notice identifies: matter description, categories of records affected, preservation obligations
3. Distribute to all staff and departments with potentially relevant records
4. **Suspend all destruction** of identified record categories immediately
5. Confirm receipt and compliance from each custodian in writing
6. Maintain hold until written release by counsel

**Consequences of non-compliance:**
- Spoliation sanctions, adverse inference instructions
- 18 U.S.C. § 1519 (Sarbanes-Oxley § 802): criminal penalties for knowingly destroying documents to obstruct federal investigation — applies to nonprofits [VERIFY applicability to specific org type]

### 7. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| **Board of Directors** | Adopt policy; review biennially; approve substantive amendments |
| **Executive Director** | Overall compliance; authorize litigation holds; approve destruction |
| **Records Manager / CFO** | Maintain retention schedule; coordinate annual review; manage destruction logs |
| **Department Heads** | Identify records in their area; ensure proper storage; flag expiring records |
| **All Staff & Volunteers** | Comply with policy; report retention questions; preserve records under hold |

### 8. Review & Amendment

- Management review: **annually**
- Board review: **every two years** or immediately upon material legal changes, restructuring, or system migration
- Amendments require board approval; track with version number and effective date
- Staff training required at adoption and upon substantive amendment


## Guidelines

- **State variations matter** — retention periods differ significantly by state; always verify the org's incorporation state and each operating state
- **Federal grantees** — if the org receives federal funds, 2 CFR Part 200 Subpart D governs; retention runs from submission of the final expenditure report, and extends if audit findings are unresolved
- **Donor records** — substantiation requirements under IRC § 170 may require permanent retention of gift instruments, pledges, and quid pro quo disclosures
- **Do not include** specific legal advice about the org's existing compliance gaps — flag issues for counsel review
- **Plain language** — the policy must be implementable by non-lawyers; avoid unnecessary legal jargon
- **Mark all uncertain citations** with [VERIFY], especially state-specific statutes and sector regulations
- **HIPAA, FERPA, or other sector overlays** — if the nonprofit operates in healthcare, education, or child services, note that sector-specific retention requirements may exceed general minimums and require separate analysis
