# Forbearance Agreement (Loan)

Drafts a forbearance agreement that temporarily suspends lender enforcement rights while preserving all remedies, creating a clear roadmap for the forbearance period.

## Prerequisites

1. **Underlying loan documents** — loan agreement, promissory note, security agreements, guaranties, prior modifications/amendments
2. **Loan details** — outstanding principal balance, current interest rate, payment history, maturity date
3. **Default information** — nature and extent of defaults, missed payment dates/amounts, covenant violations
4. **Collateral description** — type, location, current valuation
5. **Party identification** — full legal names, entity types, states of formation, guarantors
6. **Forbearance terms** — desired duration, proposed payment structure, lender-imposed conditions
7. **Amendment requirements** — original loan document provisions governing modifications, required formalities

## Output Structure

### Document Architecture

| Section | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Title & Date | "Forbearance Agreement and Temporary Modification of Loan Payment Terms" |
| Parties | Full legal ID, entity type, state of formation, role designation |
| Recitals | Factual/legal foundation (see below) |
| Forbearance Terms | Period definition, payment modifications |
| Conditions & Covenants | Precedent conditions, ongoing borrower obligations |
| Lender Reservation of Rights | Narrow commitments, broad reservations |
| Representations & Warranties | Mutual reps, borrower acknowledgments |
| Default & Termination | Trigger events, consequences |
| Release & Waiver | Borrower release of claims, reaffirmation |
| General Provisions | Governing law, integration, notices, execution |
| Exhibits | Note copy, payment schedule, payoff statement |

### Recitals Checklist

- [ ] Original loan transaction reference (date, principal, balance, rate, maturity)
- [ ] Security/collateral description establishing lender's secured position
- [ ] Specific default description (dates, amounts, violations) — professional tone
- [ ] Statement: borrower requested forbearance; lender agrees in sole discretion without obligation
- [ ] **Borrower acknowledgment**: validity of debt, accuracy of balance, waiver of defenses/setoffs/counterclaims
- [ ] Recitals are contractual and form part of operative agreement

### Forbearance Period Terms

Define with precision:

- **Commencement and termination dates** (or specific duration with date-certain auto-termination)
- **Which obligations** are suspended, reduced, or modified
- **Payment during forbearance**: exact dollar amount, due dates; if suspended, whether interest-only or reduced P&I applies
- **Treatment of deferred amounts post-forbearance**:
  - Added to principal and re-amortized, OR
  - Balloon payment on specified date, OR
  - Maturity extension, OR
  - Other specified arrangement
- **Interest accrual**: whether interest continues on principal and deferred amounts; applicable rate; treatment of accrued unpaid interest (capitalized, deferred, or paid currently)
- **Successful completion**: consequences (return to original schedule, permanent modification, or other)
- **Explicit statement**: forbearance is temporary, not a permanent modification unless expressly stated

### Conditions Precedent & Ongoing Covenants

**Initial conditions:**
- Execution and delivery of agreement
- Payment of required fees/costs
- Current financial statements delivered
- Evidence of insurance on all collateral
- Any additional lender-required documentation

**Ongoing obligations:**
- Timely payment per forbearance schedule — **no grace period, no cure right**
- Maintain adequate insurance (lender as loss payee/additional insured)
- Pay property taxes/assessments on real property collateral
- Comply with all applicable laws/regulations
- Preserve collateral in good condition
- Periodic financial reporting (specify frequency, format, deadline)
- Maintain legal existence and good standing
- Operate business in ordinary course
- No asset dispositions or additional indebtedness without consent
- Good faith negotiation toward permanent workout (if applicable)

**Breach consequence**: Any breach = immediate default, terminates forbearance, restores all enforcement rights. Specify whether notice/cure applies or automatic termination for certain breaches.

### Lender Reservation of Rights

The lender's commitment is **narrow**: refrain from exercising specified remedies (acceleration, foreclosure, collection suit) only while borrower is in strict compliance.

Preserve explicitly:
- Forbearance covers only **pre-existing identified defaults** — new defaults enforceable immediately
- One-time accommodation; no obligation to grant future forbearance/modification
- No waiver of any rights except as expressly stated
- Right to accrue interest at contract/default rate
- Right to assess late fees/default charges post-forbearance
- Right to report to credit agencies per applicable law
- Right to preserve/protect collateral (appraisals, inspections, insurance)
- No representations regarding borrower's ability to cure or future willingness to modify
- Absolute discretion to refuse future accommodations
- Full enforcement available immediately upon expiration/termination

### Events of Default & Termination

**Termination triggers:**
- Failure to make any forbearance payment (no grace period from original docs)
- Breach of any covenant/condition in forbearance agreement
- New default under original loan documents
- Material misrepresentation
- Bankruptcy, insolvency, or receivership filing (voluntary or involuntary)
- Assignment for benefit of creditors
- Attachment or levy against collateral
- Material adverse change in financial condition/operations
- Unauthorized sale/transfer of collateral or ownership interests

**Consequences of termination:**
- Entire balance immediately due and payable without further notice/demand
- All loan document and legal remedies available (foreclosure, collection, receiver)
- Borrower liable for all enforcement costs including reasonable attorney's fees
- Forbearance-period payments applied per original loan document terms
- No credit or benefit for forbearance participation

### Release, Waiver & Reaffirmation

**Borrower release**: Release lender and affiliates from all claims (known/unknown) arising from loan, loan documents, collateral, or lender conduct. Knowing and voluntary with counsel-consultation acknowledgment.

**Waivers:**
- Defenses to enforcement (usury, TILA, UDAP, breach of duty, statutory/common law)
- Right to assert counterclaims/setoffs in enforcement actions
- Jury trial waiver (consistent with original loan documents)

**Reaffirmation**: All obligations under loan documents and all security interests/liens remain in full force. Forbearance is not a novation or release — merely temporary payment modification.

### General Provisions

| Provision | Key Points |
|---|---|
| Governing law | Same jurisdiction as original loan docs; without conflicts-of-law principles |
| Jurisdiction/venue | Exclusive forum designated; borrower consents to personal jurisdiction; lender retains right to enforce where collateral located |
| Arbitration | Address whether original arb clause applies or litigation is exclusive |
| Attorney's fees | Prevailing party entitled to reasonable fees and costs |
| Integration | Agreement + loan documents = entire agreement; supersedes prior negotiations |
| Amendment | Written instrument signed by both parties only; no oral modifications |
| Conflict | Forbearance agreement controls over loan documents where conflicting |
| Severability | Invalid provisions severable; good faith replacement negotiation |
| Notices | Addresses, delivery methods (personal, certified mail, overnight courier, email w/ confirmation), deemed-given timing |
| Counterparts | Multiple counterparts; electronic/PDF signatures effective |
| No continuing waiver | Written waivers only; no waiver of other breaches |
| Assignment | Binding on successors/assigns; borrower may not assign without consent |
| Time is of the essence | Strict and timely performance is material |
| Further assurances | Parties to execute additional documents as reasonably necessary |

### Execution Requirements

- Signature blocks: signature, printed name, title, date; entity language ("Duly Authorized")
- Check original loan docs for modification formalities (corporate resolutions, board approvals, third-party consents)
- Notarization if agreement affects real property or recording is needed
- **Guarantor consent**: If guarantors exist, include consent/acknowledgment and reaffirmation of guaranty with separate signature blocks

## Guidelines

- Review original loan document amendment/modification provisions before drafting — they dictate required formalities
- Forbearance scope must be precisely limited to identified pre-existing defaults only
- Never imply permanent modification unless parties expressly intend it
- All borrower acknowledgments (debt validity, balance accuracy, waiver of defenses) must be explicit and unambiguous
- Draft for litigation scrutiny — assume the forbearance will fail and a court will review every provision
- Include table of contents if document exceeds 10 pages
- Attach exhibits: note copy, forbearance payment schedule, current payoff statement
- Target length: 8–15 pages depending on transaction complexity
- Jurisdiction: drafted for U.S. commercial lending; verify state-specific requirements for enforceability of waivers, releases, and jury trial waivers
