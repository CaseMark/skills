# Certificate of Withdrawal — Foreign Corporation

Drafts the statutory filing by which a foreign corporation surrenders its authority to transact business in a US state jurisdiction.

## Prerequisites

1. **Corporation details** — exact legal name (as it appears on the certificate of authority), home state/country of incorporation, date of incorporation
2. **Certificate of authority** — original authorization date, certificate number if applicable, any DBAs or alternate names used in the withdrawal state
3. **Registered agent** — current agent name and address in the withdrawal state
4. **Tax clearance status** — confirmation of whether the state requires a tax clearance certificate or final returns before withdrawal
5. **Authorizing resolution** — corporate resolution or board consent authorizing the withdrawal
6. **Jurisdiction** — the specific withdrawal state (requirements vary significantly)

## Output Structure

### 1. Header
- Document title: "Certificate of Withdrawal of [Corporation Name]"
- Filing jurisdiction and home jurisdiction clearly identified

### 2. Corporate Identity Block

| Field | Source |
|---|---|
| Full legal name | Exactly as on certificate of authority |
| Home jurisdiction | State/country of incorporation |
| Authorization date | Date originally granted authority in withdrawal state |
| Certificate of authority no. | If required by state |
| Principal office address | Home jurisdiction address |
| Registered agent (withdrawal state) | Name and street address |

### 3. Withdrawal Declarations

Include all of the following affirmative statements (adapt to state-specific statutory language):

- [ ] Corporation hereby surrenders its certificate of authority to transact business in [State]
- [ ] Corporation has ceased transacting business in [State]
- [ ] All state taxes and fees have been paid or adequately provided for
- [ ] All known debts and obligations incurred in [State] have been satisfied or provision made for payment
- [ ] Corporation consents to service of process in any proceeding arising from business conducted while authorized, and designates [Secretary of State / specific agent] for such service

> **Note:** Some states require verbatim statutory language for the service of process consent — research the specific state's withdrawal statute before finalizing.

### 4. Effective Date

Specify whether withdrawal is effective:
- Upon filing (default in most states), or
- On a future date (only if permitted by state law — confirm before specifying)

### 5. Required Attachments Checklist

| Attachment | Required In |
|---|---|
| Tax clearance certificate | Many states (e.g., CA, NJ, PA) |
| Certificate of good standing from home state | Some states |
| Evidence of publication | States requiring newspaper notice (rare) |
| Final tax returns or annual reports | Varies |

Reference each attachment by label in the certificate body.

### 6. Execution Block

- Signature line for authorized officer (president, VP, or as permitted by state law and governing documents)
- Typed name and title beneath signature
- Date line
- **Notarization block** — include if required by the withdrawal state
- **Corporate seal / secretary attestation** — include if mandated

## Guidelines

- **Verify state requirements** before drafting — Secretary of State websites and administrative codes vary substantially; do not assume uniformity
- **Name must match exactly** — use the corporate name precisely as it appears on the original certificate of authority throughout
- **Withdrawal does not extinguish liability** — note in any cover memo that existing obligations and liabilities survive withdrawal
- **Tax clearance lead time** — advise client early; some states (e.g., California) have lengthy clearance timelines that delay filing
- **Cite statutes** — reference the applicable withdrawal statute in the body where required by state form (e.g., "Pursuant to [State] Corp. Code § [VERIFY]")
- Use `[VERIFY]` for any statutory citations not confirmed against current state law
- Format with standard filing margins and spacing suitable for direct submission to the Secretary of State
