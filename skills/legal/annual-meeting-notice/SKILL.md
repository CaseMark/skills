# Notice of Annual Shareholders Meeting

Drafts a formal notice of annual shareholders meeting that satisfies state corporate code requirements and, for public companies, SEC proxy rules.

## Prerequisites

1. **Corporate documents** — articles of incorporation, current bylaws, any prior meeting notices
2. **State of incorporation** — to determine applicable business corporation act and notice periods
3. **Public vs. closely held status** — determines SEC disclosure obligations
4. **Record date** — board-approved date fixing shareholder eligibility for notice and voting
5. **Meeting logistics** — date, time, location, format (in-person / virtual / hybrid), platform details
6. **Agenda items** — director nominees, auditor ratification, proposed amendments, other business
7. **Stock classes and voting rights** — shares outstanding, votes per share, class voting requirements

## Output Structure

### Document Header

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Title | "NOTICE OF ANNUAL MEETING OF SHAREHOLDERS" — prominent |
| Company name | Full legal name per articles of incorporation |
| Notice date | Must fall within statutory notice period |

### Opening Statement

- Formal notice that annual meeting will be held
- Identify record date shareholders entitled to notice and vote
- Confirm notice date falls within required statutory window

### Meeting Logistics

| Detail | In-Person | Virtual | Hybrid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Address | Full street address, building, floor, room | N/A | Full address |
| Platform | N/A | URL, meeting ID, password, tech requirements | URL + tech details |
| Pre-registration | Security/access procedures | Registration deadline and process | Both |
| Contingency | Board authority to change format/location; notification method for changes |||

### Agenda

Draft each item with this structure:

1. **Election of Directors** — number of seats, term length, individual vs. slate election
2. **Financial Statements** — approval of audited financials; reference to where full reports are available
3. **Auditor Ratification** — firm name, tenure, fees
4. **Proposed Amendments** (if any) — summary of changes, impact on shareholder rights, reference to full text in proxy materials
5. **Other Business** — standard catch-all provision
6. For each item: note approval threshold (plurality, majority, supermajority) and board recommendation

### Voting and Proxy Information

Include all of the following:

- [ ] Record date and eligibility explanation (ownership as of close of business on record date)
- [ ] Votes per share for each stock class
- [ ] Quorum requirement (percentage/number of shares present in person or by proxy)
- [ ] Vote threshold for each proposal type
- [ ] Proxy submission methods (mail, telephone, internet) and deadline
- [ ] Right to revoke proxy: later-dated proxy, written revocation to secretary, or in-person vote
- [ ] For public companies: compliance with Securities Exchange Act § 14(a) and Regulation 14A; reference proxy statement availability

### Shareholder Resources

- Corporate secretary / investor relations contact: phone, email, mailing address
- How to access annual report, proxy statement, and meeting materials (website, EDGAR for public companies, paper copy request)
- Notice and access procedures (if applicable): how to request paper copies
- Accessibility accommodations statement and contact

### Signature Block

```
By Order of the Board of Directors,

_________________________
[Name]
[Title — Corporate Secretary or Assistant Secretary]
[Date of execution]
```

## Guidelines

- **Notice period**: Verify state-specific statutory window (typically 10–60 days before meeting). Bylaws may impose stricter requirements — use the stricter standard.
- **Record date**: Must comply with state law on how far in advance of the meeting it must be set. If not provided, flag for client.
- **SEC compliance**: For public companies, ensure notice references proxy statement availability per Regulation 14A and notice-and-access rules. For closely held companies, omit SEC-specific provisions.
- **Multi-class stock**: If multiple classes exist, detail voting power per class and any class-specific approval requirements.
- **Distribution method**: Note applicable delivery method (mail, e-delivery with consent, notice and access posting) and advise client to maintain distribution records.
- **Tone**: Formal corporate register. Clear and precise — avoid ambiguity in dates, times, locations, and procedures.
- **Do not** fabricate specific statutory citations without verification. Mark uncertain references with [VERIFY].
