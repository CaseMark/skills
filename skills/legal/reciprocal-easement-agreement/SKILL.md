# Reciprocal Easement Agreement

Drafts a recordable REA creating perpetual cross-easements and operating covenants between adjacent commercial parcels developed as an integrated project.

## Prerequisites

1. **Legal descriptions** — complete metes-and-bounds or lot/block for each parcel (Exhibits A & B)
2. **Site plan** — showing parcel boundary, access points, drive aisles, parking layout, common areas (Exhibit C)
3. **Utility plan** — existing and proposed utility corridors, connection points (Exhibit D)
4. **Party information** — full legal names, entity type, jurisdiction of formation, principal addresses
5. **Development details** — use type (retail, office, mixed-use), GLA per parcel, parking ratios, anchor tenants, phasing
6. **Existing encumbrances** — title reports, existing easements, CC&Rs, zoning conditions, planning approvals
7. **Business terms** — cost-sharing formula, maintenance standards, use restrictions, insurance minimums

## Drafting Specifications

### §4 — Access Easements
- Non-exclusive, perpetual, 24/7 for vehicular and pedestrian use
- Beneficiaries: owners, tenants, subtenants, employees, customers, invitees, licensees
- Covers: driveways, drive aisles, fire lanes, sidewalks, crosswalks, turning radii, queuing areas
- **Relocation right**: 60 days' written notice; substantially equivalent access; must meet building/fire/ADA codes; new route operational before old route closed
- Emergency vehicle access must be maintained at all times per fire department requirements

### §5 — Parking Easements
- Non-exclusive shared parking across both parcels (exclude designated reserved spaces)
- Minimum ratios per parcel tied to GLA and use type — align with local zoning ordinance
- Neither party may reduce spaces below minimum without other's written consent (sole discretion standard)
- Reserved space designations allowed if shared-use minimums maintained
- Each owner responsible for enforcement/towing on own parcel
- Expansion trigger: additional parking required if GLA increases beyond original plan

### §6 — Utility Easements
- Perpetual, non-exclusive for: water, sanitary sewer, storm drainage, gas, electric, telecom, cable
- Standard corridor widths: 15 ft underground lines; 20 ft where structures/equipment present
- **Work protocol**: 10 business days' advance written notice (emergency exception); good and workmanlike manner; prompt restoration of surfaces/landscaping
- Cost allocation for shared infrastructure by proportionate benefit or metered usage
- Obligation to extend services for future development on either parcel

### §7 — Maintenance Covenants
- Each owner maintains easement areas on own parcel to first-class commercial standard
- **Paving**: no significant cracks/potholes; periodic seal coat, restripe, resurface
- **Landscaping**: regular mow/edge/prune/fertilize/irrigate; replace dead material
- **Lighting**: prompt bulb replacement; adequate illumination for safety
- Shared facilities (monument signs, entry features, water features) — cost split by GLA ratio
- **Cure procedure**: written notice → 30-day cure (immediate for safety hazards) → self-help with cost recovery including attorney fees

### §8 — Cost Sharing
- Default allocation: GLA ratio (Parcel GLA ÷ total Project GLA)
- Alternative bases where appropriate: metered usage (utilities), space count (parking maintenance)
- Invoice within 30 days of payment with documentation; payment due within 30 days of invoice
- Late interest: lesser of 1.5%/month or statutory maximum
- **Major expenditures** (>$50,000): advance written notice with scope, estimate, and allocation basis; other party may review and suggest alternatives; initiating party retains right to proceed
- Consider reserve accounts for anticipated capital replacements (resurfacing, roof, major utilities)

### §9 — Use Restrictions
- No obstruction of easement areas; no permanent structures in easement corridors
- No storage of materials/equipment in access or parking areas
- No hazardous materials beyond normal commercial operations
- No nuisance (excessive noise, odors, vibrations)
- No manufacturing/industrial inconsistent with development character
- **Design controls**: signage size/location/design subject to approval; new construction/renovation must be architecturally compatible (materials, colors, quality)
- **Drainage covenant**: no alteration of grading/drainage patterns that adversely affects adjacent parcel; maintain stormwater compliance

### §10 — Insurance & Indemnification

| Coverage | Minimum |
|----------|---------|
| CGL per occurrence | $2,000,000 |
| CGL aggregate | $4,000,000 |
| Additional insured | Other party named for operations on insured's property |

- Mutual indemnification for claims arising from indemnitor's use/maintenance/operation of own property or breach of REA
- Carve-out: no indemnity to extent caused by indemnitee's negligence or willful misconduct
- Obligations survive termination; extend to officers, directors, members, managers, employees, agents

### §11 — Term & Recording
- Perpetual duration; covenants expressly run with the land
- Binding on all successors: purchasers, heirs, lenders (including through foreclosure/deed-in-lieu)
- Record immediately upon execution in county official records
- All future deeds, mortgages, leases must reference recording information
- REA controls over subsequently recorded CC&Rs absent written amendment by both parties
- Require lender SNDA recognizing easement rights survive foreclosure

### §12 — Enforcement & Remedies
- Available remedies: specific performance, injunctive relief, declaratory relief, monetary damages
- Include finding of unique nature / irreparable harm (no bond requirement for injunction)
- **Pre-suit notice**: 30 days for non-emergency; 10 days for safety/material interference; extension if cure diligently pursued
- **Mandatory mediation** before litigation (except emergency injunctive relief / TRO)
- Prevailing party recovers attorney fees, expert fees, court costs, deposition expenses
- "Prevailing party" = party substantially obtaining relief sought (judgment, settlement, or post-filing voluntary compliance)

### §13 — Amendment & Termination
- Amendment: written instrument by all current owners of both parcels; must be recorded
- No oral modification; no waiver by course of dealing
- Termination only by recorded written instrument signed by all owners of both parcels
- **Consolidation**: easements may terminate upon single ownership; use/maintenance/operational covenants survive
- **Subdivision**: REA binds all resulting parcels; easement rights extend to all subdivided portions
- Unauthorized recording of termination/release is void

### §14 — General Provisions
Include: governing law (situs state, no conflict-of-law), severability with reformation, notice requirements (personal delivery / overnight courier / certified mail with addresses and deemed-received rules), construction (no contra proferentem), headings non-substantive, entire agreement / merger, counterparts, lender subordination/recognition.

## Execution Blocks

Draft signature blocks matching each party's entity type:

| Entity Type | Block Includes |
|-------------|---------------|
| Individual | Signature, printed name, date |
| Corporation | Officer signature, name, title, date; secretary attestation if required by state |
| LLC | Member/manager signature, name, title, date |

Notarial acknowledgment must comply with recording-state statutory form (venue, personal appearance, identification, acknowledgment statement, notary signature/seal/commission expiration).

## Guidelines

- Extract all property details, party information, and development specifics from provided documents before drafting
- Flag missing critical information with `[INSERT]` placeholders and clear completion instructions
- Verify legal descriptions match deeds and title policies
- Confirm parking ratios comply with applicable municipal zoning code
- Adjust insurance minimums to match project scale (stated minimums are baseline for substantial commercial projects)
- All exhibits must be cross-referenced with "attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference"
- For multi-state parties, confirm authority to transact business in the property's state
- If state has adopted statutory short-form acknowledgment, use that form
- Mark any cited statute or ordinance with `[VERIFY]` unless confirmed from provided documents
