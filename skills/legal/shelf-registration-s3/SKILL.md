# Shelf Registration Statement (Form S-3)

Drafts a complete Form S-3 shelf registration statement enabling eligible public companies to register securities for delayed or continuous offerings over a three-year effectiveness period.

## Prerequisites

1. **Corporate documents** — charter, bylaws, board resolutions authorizing the filing
2. **Recent SEC filings** — most recent 10-K, subsequent 10-Qs and 8-Ks, prior registration statements
3. **Capital structure details** — outstanding shares by class, debt instruments, credit agreements, warrant/option schedules
4. **Public float calculation** — aggregate market value of voting and non-voting common equity held by non-affiliates (within 60 days of filing)
5. **SEC identifiers** — CIK code, SEC file number, reporting history
6. **Offering parameters** — securities types to register, maximum aggregate offering amount, strategic capital raising objectives

## Eligibility Verification

Confirm one of these pathways before drafting:

| Pathway | Requirements |
|---|---|
| **I.B.1 — Public Float** | Organized under US/Canadian law + Section 12(b)/12(g) registered + 12 months reporting + all reports current + public float ≥ $75M |
| **I.B.2 — Investment Grade** | Same reporting requirements + non-convertible securities rated investment grade |
| **WKSI** | All I.B.1 requirements + either (a) worldwide public float ≥ $700M, or (b) ≥ $1B non-convertible securities issued in registered offerings in prior 3 years |

WKSI status enables automatic effectiveness upon filing under Rule 462(e).

## Output Structure

### 1. Cover Page

Include all of the following with exact precision:

- [ ] Registrant's exact legal name, jurisdiction of incorporation, IRS EIN
- [ ] Principal executive offices address and telephone
- [ ] Agent for service of process with full contact details
- [ ] Each security class with precise title (e.g., "Common Stock, par value $0.01 per share")
- [ ] Maximum aggregate offering price (specific amount or "to be determined from time to time")
- [ ] Registration fee calculation using current SEC fee rate per Rule 457(o)
- [ ] Eligibility checkbox referencing applicable General Instruction provision
- [ ] WKSI checkbox if applicable
- [ ] Incorporation by reference list with exact titles, filing dates, SEC file numbers

### 2. Prospectus Summary

Structure in this order:
1. **Business description** — what the company does, markets served, differentiation (sourced from 10-K Business section)
2. **Shelf mechanism explanation** — types of securities covered, aggregate amount, offering-by-offering via prospectus supplements
3. **Use of proceeds overview** — general capital allocation framework
4. **Key financial metrics** — revenue, net income/loss, total assets, stockholders' equity, significant trends
5. **Risk factors roadmap** — preview of major risk categories

### 3. Risk Factors (Item 503(c) of Reg S-K)

Organize by category, descending materiality within each:

| Category | Required Coverage |
|---|---|
| Business/Industry | Competition, customer/supplier concentration, technology obsolescence, key personnel |
| Financial | Cash flow adequacy, covenant compliance, interest rate/FX exposure, loss history |
| Legal/Regulatory | Pending litigation, regulatory changes, compliance costs |
| Securities Ownership | Market volatility, limited trading volume, analyst coverage |
| Shelf-Specific | Dilution from future equity issuances, timing/pricing uncertainty, management discretion over proceeds, debt subordination risks |

Each risk factor: descriptive heading → circumstances → potential consequences → mitigating factors (if any). Tailor to registrant's specific facts — no generic boilerplate.

### 4. Use of Proceeds

- General framework of anticipated uses (working capital, capex, R&D, acquisitions, debt repayment, general corporate purposes)
- Specific identified projects with estimated cost ranges and timelines where available
- For debt repayment: identify obligations (principal, rate, maturity, prepayment terms)
- Priority of allocation or statement of management discretion
- Pending-use investment policy (short-term, investment-grade instruments)
- Note: specific allocation per offering described in applicable prospectus supplement

### 5. Description of Securities

Draft framework descriptions for each registered class:

**Common Stock:**
- Voting rights (cumulative voting, class voting requirements)
- Dividend rights and restrictions (including debt covenant limitations)
- Liquidation rights and priority
- Conversion, preemptive, anti-dilution features
- Incorporate by reference Section 12 registration statement description

**Preferred Stock:**
- Board authority to issue series and fix terms without shareholder approval
- Framework for: dividend rates, liquidation preferences, voting rights, conversion/exchange, redemption, sinking fund
- Specific terms deferred to prospectus supplements

**Debt Securities:**
- Base indenture framework (file as exhibit or incorporate by reference)
- Term-setting framework: principal, maturity, interest rate/calculation, payment dates, ranking, redemption, covenants, events of default, modification/waiver
- Trustee identification and role
- Senior vs. subordinated, secured vs. unsecured, convertible vs. non-convertible — to be specified per offering

**Warrants / Purchase Contracts / Units:**
- Exercise terms framework, anti-dilution adjustments
- Specific terms established in warrant agreements filed with prospectus supplements

### 6. Incorporation by Reference

Specifically incorporate:
- [ ] Most recent Form 10-K (Sections 13(a) or 15(d))
- [ ] All subsequent Form 10-Qs
- [ ] All subsequent Form 8-Ks (exclude Items 2.02 and 7.01 furnished information)
- [ ] Section 12 registration statement description of common stock

Include forward-incorporation language: all future filings under Sections 13(a), 13(c), 14, or 15(d) of the Exchange Act are automatically incorporated from their respective filing dates. Later statements modify/supersede earlier ones.

Provide instructions for obtaining copies (investor relations contact, SEC EDGAR URL, company website with non-incorporation disclaimer).

### 7. Undertakings (Item 512 of Reg S-K)

Include verbatim regulatory language for each required undertaking:

| Item | Substance |
|---|---|
| 512(a)(1) | Post-effective amendments for Section 10(a)(3) compliance, fundamental changes, material distribution plan changes |
| 512(a)(2) | Each post-effective amendment deemed new registration statement; offering deemed initial bona fide offering |
| 512(a)(3) | Removal of unsold securities at offering termination |
| 512(a)(5) | (WKSI only) Rule 424(b) prospectus filing and incorporation |
| 512(a)(6) | SEC indemnification policy disclosure — indemnification against Securities Act liability is unenforceable per SEC opinion |
| 512(h) | Response to incorporation-by-reference information requests within one business day |

### 8. Signature Page

- [ ] Registrant signature by duly authorized officer
- [ ] Principal executive officer
- [ ] Principal financial officer
- [ ] Controller / principal accounting officer
- [ ] Majority of the board of directors
- [ ] Power of attorney (if used) — file as Exhibit 24, reference on signature page

For WKSI automatic shelf: signature date = filing date (effective immediately).

### 9. Exhibits Index (Item 601 of Reg S-K)

| Exhibit | Description | Status |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Form of underwriting agreement | Filed or to be filed via 8-K per takedown |
| 3.1 | Certificate of incorporation | Filed or incorporated by reference (cite source) |
| 3.2 | Bylaws | Filed or incorporated by reference (cite source) |
| 4.x | Instruments defining security holder rights (indenture, warrant agreements, forms of securities) | Filed herewith or by amendment |
| 5 | Opinion of counsel — legality of securities | Filed herewith |
| 23.1 | Consent of independent registered public accounting firm | Filed herewith |
| 23.x | Consents of other experts | As applicable |
| 24 | Powers of attorney | Filed herewith (if applicable) |
| 25 | Form T-1 — trustee eligibility (debt securities) | Filed herewith (if applicable) |
| 107 | Filing fee table | Filed herewith |

For each: indicate filed herewith, incorporated by reference (with SEC file number, filing type, date, exhibit number), or to be filed by amendment.

## Guidelines

- **Internal consistency**: cross-check all financial data against incorporated filings; verify cross-references point to correct sections
- **Forward-looking statements**: accompany with PSLRA safe harbor cautionary language
- **EDGAR compliance**: proper HTML tagging, acceptable file formats, size limits per EDGAR Filer Manual
- **Fee calculation**: verify current SEC fee rate at time of filing; show computation clearly
- **Risk factor specificity**: SEC routinely comments on generic risk factors — every risk must tie to registrant-specific facts
- **Use of proceeds / MD&A alignment**: ensure consistency between stated capital allocation and strategic priorities in incorporated filings
- **Hyperlinks**: verify all exhibits index links are functional and point to correct documents
- **Three-year shelf life**: structure maximum aggregate amount and security types to accommodate full range of anticipated capital needs
- **Prospectus supplement coordination**: base prospectus should establish frameworks; avoid over-specifying terms that belong in supplements
