# Transitional Services Agreement

Drafts a litigation-ready TSA that bridges closing to operational independence while protecting both parties through balanced risk allocation.

## Prerequisites

1. **Underlying transaction agreement** — purchase agreement or merger agreement (full text); note any TSA mandates, service pricing parameters, or term limits embedded therein
2. **Service inventory** — list of systems, functions, and support the acquired business currently relies on from seller (IT, HR/payroll, finance, facilities, procurement, etc.)
3. **Closing date** — confirmed or estimated; TSA effective date ties to closing
4. **Transition timeline** — buyer's integration plan and anticipated self-sufficiency milestones per service category
5. **Data map** — categories of personal data processed through shared systems (for GDPR/CCPA DPA exhibit)

## Output Structure

### 1. Preamble & Recitals
- Full legal names, states of organization; seller = "Service Provider," buyer = "Service Recipient"
- Reference transaction agreement by full title, date, and parties
- Effective date = closing date
- Recitals: describe acquired business's reliance on seller systems; TSA as temporary accommodation, not ongoing commercial relationship

### 2. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Services** | As specified in Exhibit A (Services Schedule); no implied expansion |
| **Transition Period** | Closing date through initial termination date; per-service end dates permitted |
| **Service Levels** | Standards in Exhibit A; absent specification — "substantially the same manner, quality, timeliness, and resources as the 6 months preceding closing" |
| **Service Fees** | Per Exhibit B; includes reimbursable out-of-pocket unless context indicates otherwise |
| **Confidential Information** | All non-public information disclosed in connection with this agreement |

Incorporate undefined capitalized terms by reference to the transaction agreement.

### 3. Services

**Organization:** Group by function — IT, HR/Payroll, Finance/Accounting, Facilities, Procurement/Supply Chain.

**Scope formula:** "Access to and support for [category] systems and applications used by the acquired business as of closing, including but not limited to [examples], at substantially the same level as provided pre-closing."

**Performance standard:** Good faith; same manner/quality/resources as pre-closing 6-month period. Does not require specific personnel retention (replacements must have substantially similar qualifications).

**Exclusions (state explicitly):**
- New capability development
- System upgrades/enhancements beyond routine maintenance
- Capacity expansion beyond historical levels
- Services to locations/functions outside the acquired business

**Operational mechanics:**
- Named contacts + escalation path for each party
- Service request mechanism (portal, email, etc.) + response SLAs
- Access rights, security protocols, on-site vs. remote delivery

### 4. Compensation

**Pricing methodology:** Cost reimbursement (no markup) is standard for TSAs.

**Reimbursable costs:**

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Direct labor | Actual hours × fully-burdened rate (base + benefits + payroll taxes + overhead) |
| Third-party costs | Vendor/contractor invoices supporting service delivery |
| Shared resource allocation | Pro-rata by usage, headcount, or other reasonable metric |
| Out-of-pocket expenses | Travel, shipping, telecom — with receipts |

**Invoicing:** Monthly, within [X] days after month-end; itemized by service category, personnel, hours, rates, third-party costs, expenses.

**Payment:** Undisputed amounts within 30 days; disputed amounts — written notice within 15 days of invoice receipt; senior finance officers to confer within 10 days of dispute notice.

**Taxes:** Fees exclude applicable sales/use/VAT/GST; Service Recipient bears all transaction taxes (excluding Service Provider net income tax).

**Extensions:** Fee escalation (10–25%) for extension periods incentivizes timely transition.

### 5. Term & Termination

| Right | Notice | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Initial term | — | Closing date + [6–18] months; per-service end dates in Exhibit A |
| Extension (Service Recipient) | 60–90 days before expiry | [1–2] extensions × [3–6] months; escalated fees |
| Termination for convenience (Service Recipient) | 30–90 days | Per service or entire agreement; no penalty |
| Termination for material breach (either) | 15–30 days cure period after written notice | Define materiality: sustained non-performance, repeated failures, confidentiality breach, non-payment 30+ days |
| Termination for insolvency/change of control | Immediate | Upon bankruptcy, receivership, or acquisition by competitor |

**Post-termination:** Service Provider cooperates in transition to replacement providers; Service Recipient pays all fees through termination date; mutual return/destruction of Confidential Information with written certification; survival of confidentiality (multi-year), indemnification, payment obligations, limitation of liability, dispute resolution.

### 6. Representations & Disclaimers

**Service Provider reps:** Corporate authority; no conflict; enforceability; good-faith performance; legal compliance.

**Disclaimer (Service Provider):** NO implied warranties of merchantability, fitness for purpose, or adequacy. No obligation to modify, enhance, or improve beyond closing-date baseline. No liability for third-party system degradation outside Service Provider's control.

**Service Recipient acknowledgment:** Accepts services as-is subject only to the "same manner" standard; bears risk of transition planning adequacy.

### 7. Confidentiality & Data Protection

**Standard carve-outs:** Public information (no breach); prior possession; independent development; third-party disclosure without restriction.

**Obligations:** Same care as own confidential information (no less than reasonable); limit disclosure to need-to-know personnel under equivalent obligations; use solely for TSA purposes.

**Data Protection (if personal data involved):**
- Service Recipient = data controller; Service Provider = data processor
- Service Provider obligations: process only per documented instructions; implement appropriate technical/organizational safeguards; ensure personnel confidentiality obligations; assist with DSARs and breach response
- Breach notification: written notice within 24–48 hours of discovery; describe nature, scope, affected data, potential consequences, remediation steps
- Attach GDPR Art. 28-compliant DPA as Exhibit D if required [VERIFY applicable threshold]

### 8. Indemnification

| Indemnifying Party | Covered Claims |
|-------------------|---------------|
| **Service Provider** | Gross negligence/willful misconduct in service delivery; material confidentiality breach; violation of applicable law (including data protection, employment); IP infringement not caused by Service Recipient specs |
| **Service Recipient** | Use of services / operation of acquired business; acquired business employee/customer/supplier claims; Service Recipient legal violations; all other acquired business liabilities |

**Procedure:** Prompt written notice (late notice excuses only if materially prejudicial); indemnifying party controls defense and settlement (no settlement imposing obligations on indemnified party without consent); indemnified party cooperates (costs reimbursed); participation by indemnified party at own expense.

**Relationship to transaction agreement:** TSA governs service-related claims; transaction agreement governs pre-closing and deal-related claims.

### 9. Limitation of Liability

**Aggregate cap:** Total fees paid/payable in the 12 months preceding the liability-triggering event (or full term if < 12 months elapsed).

**Excluded damages (mutual):** Lost profits, lost revenue, lost opportunities, anticipated savings, business interruption, data loss (except where backups are a specified service), reputational harm, and all consequential/indirect/incidental/special/exemplary/punitive damages — regardless of whether advised of possibility.

**Carve-outs (not subject to cap/exclusions):**
- Third-party indemnification obligations
- Confidentiality breaches
- Fraud, willful misconduct, gross negligence
- Payment obligations

**Specific performance:** Available without bond for confidentiality breaches or failure to provide critical services; irreparable harm acknowledged.

### 10. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

- **Governing law:** Same state as transaction agreement (typically Delaware or New York); conflicts-of-law principles excluded
- **Dispute escalation:** Designated senior executives meet within 10–15 days of notice; if unresolved after additional 15–30 days → formal proceedings
- **Forum:** Exclusive jurisdiction in state/federal courts of [governing state]; mutual consent to personal jurisdiction; venue objection waived
- **Jury trial waiver:** Include if parties agree; must be conspicuous
- **Equitable relief:** Preserved for irreparable harm (confidentiality, critical service failure)
- **Fees:** Each party bears own attorneys' fees unless parties agree otherwise

### 11. General Provisions (Checklist)

- [ ] Assignment — prohibited without consent; permitted to affiliates or acquirors of substantially all assets without consent
- [ ] Notices — written; effective on personal delivery, email with confirmation, next business day (overnight courier), or 3 days (certified mail)
- [ ] Entire agreement — TSA controls for service matters; transaction agreement controls for deal matters; specify conflict resolution hierarchy
- [ ] Amendment — written, signed by authorized representatives only; no course-of-dealing modifications
- [ ] Severability — minimum modification to preserve intent; remaining provisions unaffected
- [ ] Waiver — written only; no implied waivers from delay or partial exercise
- [ ] Independent contractor — no employment, agency, partnership, or joint venture
- [ ] Counterparts/e-signatures — original equivalents
- [ ] Force majeure — excludes payment obligations; 60–90 day continuation triggers termination right
- [ ] Publicity — mutual prior written consent; carve-out for legally required disclosure with advance notice

## Exhibits

| Exhibit | Contents |
|---------|----------|
| **A — Services Schedule** | Per-service: description, service levels, term, key personnel/systems, dependencies, third-party vendors |
| **B — Fee Schedule** | Per-service pricing, rates, volume tiers, setup charges, invoicing frequency |
| **C — Service Level Agreement** | Measurable metrics (uptime %, response times), measurement methodology, reporting cadence, remedies (credits, fee reductions, termination triggers) |
| **D — Data Processing Agreement** | GDPR Art. 28 / CCPA compliant; processing subject matter, duration, nature, purpose; data types; sub-processor list; security measures; deletion/return on termination [VERIFY jurisdiction-specific requirements] |
| **E — Transition Plan** | Per-service milestone timeline, knowledge transfer plan, governance (status meetings, reporting), party responsibilities |

## Guidelines

- **"Same manner" is the floor and ceiling** — Service Provider neither degrades nor upgrades services without agreement; document baseline pre-closing for each service
- **Align with transaction agreement** — check for mandated services, pricing caps, or approval rights; resolve any conflicts in the TSA's favor for service matters
- **Incentivize transition** — escalating fees for extensions and Service Recipient termination-for-convenience rights together discourage dependency
- **No windfall liability** — liability cap tied to fees received reflects accommodative (not commercial) nature of arrangement
- **Data protection is non-negotiable** — if any personal data flows, a compliant DPA is required regardless of deal size
- **Third-party consents** — flag any vendor contracts requiring consent to share access with Service Recipient; Service Provider's obligation limited to commercially reasonable efforts to obtain
- **Jurisdiction:** US-focused; adapt data protection provisions for cross-border services involving EU/UK data subjects
