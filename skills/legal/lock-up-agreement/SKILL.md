# Lock-Up Agreement

Drafts a market-standard lock-up agreement that protects post-offering price stability and aligns with the underwriting agreement.

## Prerequisites

1. **Underwriting agreement** — required lock-up period, form, release mechanics, representative name.
2. **Securityholder details** — name, capacity, entity type, address, beneficial ownership scope.
3. **Issuer details** — legal name, jurisdiction, offering type (IPO vs secondary), security class.
4. **Equity awards inventory** — options, RSUs, warrants, convertibles, ESPP, other awards.
5. **Existing transfer restrictions** — stockholders’ agreement, registration rights, ROFR/repurchase.

## Output Structure / Process

**Document Outline (mandatory sections)**
1. Title, date, addressee (representative of underwriters), parties.
2. Recitals: offering context, underwriting agreement reference, consideration.
3. Definitions: “Lock-Up Period,” “Common Stock,” “Beneficial Ownership” (Rule 13d-3).
4. Lock-Up Covenant: transfer and hedging restrictions.
5. Lock-Up Period: start/end formula and early release language.
6. Extension: earnings/material news blackout extension [VERIFY].
7. Permitted Transfers: categories + conditions.
8. Representations and Authority.
9. Enforcement: stop-transfer, legends, refusal to register.
10. Third-Party Beneficiary (underwriters).
11. Waiver/Release mechanics.
12. Governing Law, Venue, Injunctive Relief.
13. Miscellaneous: amendments, waivers, counterparts, e-signatures, severability.
14. Signature blocks (individual/entity).

**Core Covenant Checklist**
- Prohibit direct or indirect offer, sell, pledge, lend, hypothecate, gift, assign, transfer.
- Prohibit derivatives/hedges that transfer economic exposure (cash or physical settlement).
- Cover beneficially owned securities under Rule 13d-3.
- Cover securities issuable on conversion/exercise/vesting.
- Apply to any shares acquired during the Lock-Up Period.

**Lock-Up Period Template**
```text
The “Lock-Up Period” begins on the date of this agreement and ends on the date that is [180] days after the date of the final prospectus for the Offering, unless earlier released in writing by the Representative.
```

**Extension Clause (include if required)**
```text
If the Company issues an earnings release or announces material news or a material event during the period beginning [17] days before and ending [17] days after the Lock-Up Period end date, then the Lock-Up Period shall be extended until the expiration of [18] days after such release or announcement, unless waived in writing by the Representative. [VERIFY]
```

**Permitted Transfers Table (include conditions below)**
| Category | Permitted Transfer |
| --- | --- |
| Gifts | Bona fide gifts to family members or 501(c)(3) charities. |
| Estate Planning | Transfers to trusts for benefit of holder or immediate family; transfers by will or intestacy; QDRO/divorce transfers. |
| Entity Restructuring | Transfers to affiliates or entities controlled by the holder or same manager/GP/advisor. |
| Company Repurchase | Transfers to issuer under repurchase/ROFR agreements. |
| Equity Awards | Exercise/vesting/settlement of awards (no sale; shares remain locked). |
| Rule 10b5-1 | Establishment of plan with no sales during Lock-Up Period and no public announcement. |

**Conditions for All Permitted Transfers**
1. Transferee executes a joinder lock-up agreement in substantially the same form.
2. Section 16(a) report (if required) states transfer was permitted under the lock-up.
3. No voluntary public disclosure or filing about the transfer during the Lock-Up Period, except as required by law/regulation.

**Representations (minimum set)**
- Authority and enforceability (subject to bankruptcy/creditor rights and equity).
- No conflicting agreements; no inconsistent proxies or voting arrangements.
- Holder acknowledges reliance by issuer and underwriters.

**Enforcement Provisions**
- Stop-transfer instructions to transfer agent.
- Issuer may refuse to register noncompliant transfers.
- Restrictive legends on certificates or book-entry positions.

**Third-Party Beneficiary**
- Underwriters are intended third-party beneficiaries with direct enforcement rights.

**Waiver/Release**
- Representative may release all or part; specify pro rata release if required by underwriting agreement or stock exchange rules.

**Signature Blocks**
- Individual: name, signature, date, capacity if applicable.
- Entity: entity name, authorized signatory, title, date.

## Guidelines

- Align lock-up period, release mechanics, and exceptions with the underwriting agreement first.
- Use “final prospectus date” as the anchor unless the underwriting agreement dictates otherwise.
- Include Rule 13d-3 beneficial ownership scope and derivative/hedge prohibitions.
- Ensure permitted transfers keep restrictions attached to shares via joinder.
- Flag any conflict with existing transfer rights or registration rights for resolution.
- Use [VERIFY] for any exchange or regulatory timing rule not specified by the underwriting agreement.
- Keep governed law/venue consistent with underwriting agreement unless directed otherwise.
