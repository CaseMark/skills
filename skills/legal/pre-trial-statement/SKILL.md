# Pre-Trial Statement

Draft a court-ready pre-trial statement that narrows issues, fixes trial assumptions, and prevents evidentiary surprises.

## Prerequisites

1. Confirm applicable jurisdiction and court-specific filing rules, including local rule templates, exhibit-labeling conventions, page/style limits, and electronic filing requirements.
2. Gather core case file: complaint, answer/counterclaim, motions/orders, case management orders, discovery responses, deposition excerpts, correspondence, stipulations, admissions, and prior pretrial conferences.
3. Identify and verify:
   - Party identities and roles
   - Claims, defenses, and relief requested
   - Key deadlines (discovery cutoff, expert disclosure cutoff, trial date, conference dates)
4. Confirm protected information and sealing requirements before describing addresses, medical records, trade secrets, or sensitive personal data.
5. Confirm witness availability and whether testimony is live, by stipulation, or by deposition designations.
6. Confirm exhibit status: produced, pre-marked, contested as to authenticity/admissibility, and any redactions.
7. Confirm whether settlement/ADR obligations have been satisfied by case plan or court order.

## Output Structure / Process

### 1) Source matrix (required before drafting)
| Item | Required inputs | Output in statement |
|---|---|---|
| Caption | Court info, case number, parties, judge | Exact caption line |
| Procedural history | Filings and orders log | Timeline summary |
| Undisputed facts | Stipulations, admissions, agreed items | Numbered facts section |
| Contested issues | Pleadings/deposition conflicts | Fact/law dispute sections |
| Witness roster | Interview notes, deposition designations, party witness lists | Party-organized witness table |
| Exhibits | Document lists and custodians | Exhibit index + objections notes |
| Trial posture | Motions, scheduling orders, trial settings | Trial requirements section |

### 2) Build statement in this order
1. Draft complete caption and trial-ready cover block.
2. Insert procedural posture and case history in chronological, neutral style.
3. Construct **Undisputed Facts** as numbered declarative paragraphs only (no mixed legal conclusions).
4. Construct **Contested Facts of Fact** with issue title + why material.
5. Construct **Contested Legal Issues** with each side position and basis for disagreement.
6. Add **Witness Summaries** (party-organized):
   - Full name
   - Protected? (yes/no)
   - Testimony topic
   - Party to call
   - Live or designation
   - Expected objection hotspots
7. Add **Exhibits** as indexed list.
8. Add **Trial Logistics** and **Ancillary Matters** (motions in limine, evidentiary fights, expert challenges).
9. Add settlement/ADR status statement without privileged detail.
10. Run final quality pass against jurisdictional formatting and filing instructions, then paginate/sign.

## Guidelines

- Keep separate sections mandatory: caption, procedural history, undisputed facts, disputed facts/law, witnesses, exhibits, trial logistics, ADR posture.
- Each disputed matter must be grounded in source citations from the file; never restate hearsay as fact.
- Every paragraph in undisputed facts must be unequivocally non-controversial.
- Use neutral tone; avoid argumentative rhetoric.
- Preserve litigation positions, but do not reveal settlement demands, mediation positions, or privileged strategy.
- If local rules conflict with this template, local rules govern.
- For admissibility references, cite only authority and rule text you can verify in the case materials and governing law [VERIFY].
