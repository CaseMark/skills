# Intercreditor Agreement (Lien Priority)

Drafts a first lien/second lien intercreditor agreement governing creditor priority, enforcement rights, and payment waterfalls over shared collateral.

## Prerequisites

1. **First lien credit agreement** — including loan amounts, maturity, interest rates, collateral descriptions
2. **Second lien credit agreement** — same details for junior facility
3. **Security agreements** — perfection status, filing details, collateral schedules
4. **Borrower organizational documents** — entity type, jurisdiction of formation
5. **Existing intercreditor or subordination arrangements** (if any)

## Output Structure

### Article I — Recitals & Definitions

Establish commercial context referencing both credit facilities and shared collateral. Define at minimum:

| Defined Term | Scope |
|---|---|
| First Lien Obligations | Principal, interest (including post-petition), fees, expenses, indemnities under first lien docs |
| Second Lien Obligations | Same scope for second lien docs |
| Collateral | All shared collateral securing both sets of obligations |
| Lien Priority | Absolute first lien superiority regardless of time/order/method of attachment or perfection |
| Standstill Period | Duration (typically 180 days) triggered by payment default or EOD under first lien docs |
| Discharge of First Lien Obligations | Full and final payment; specify cash pay, L/C termination, hedge unwind conditions |
| Insolvency Proceeding | Bankruptcy Code proceedings, receivership, assignment for benefit of creditors, foreign equivalents |

Extract specific obligation amounts, maturity dates, and collateral descriptions from uploaded transaction documents.

### Article II — Lien Priority & Subordination

- [ ] Absolute priority of first lien regardless of attachment/perfection order
- [ ] Subordination covers principal, pre- and post-petition interest, fees, expenses, all other amounts
- [ ] Priority applies across all scenarios: voluntary payments, enforcement, bankruptcy, collateral dispositions
- [ ] Continuing agreement effective until Discharge of First Lien Obligations
- [ ] Second lien holder may not contest priority or seek equitable subordination of first lien

### Article III — Enforcement Rights & Standstill

**First lien holders may** (without second lien consent or notice):
- Enforce all remedies against collateral
- Accept/reject DIP financing proposals
- Vote on reorganization plans
- Exercise all secured creditor rights

**Second lien holders may NOT during Standstill Period:**
- Accelerate obligations
- Exercise remedies against collateral
- Object to first lien enforcement actions
- Take any action interfering with first lien rights
- Seek adequate protection for second lien interests

**Post-standstill:** Specify limited actions available to second lien holders if first lien holders have not completed enforcement after standstill expiration.

### Article IV — Payment Waterfall & Turnover

**Distribution priority (all collateral proceeds):**

```
1. First Lien Obligations → pay in full
2. Second Lien Obligations → remainder
```

**Turnover obligations:**
- Second lien holders must immediately remit any payments received in violation of priority
- Applies regardless of knowledge of priority violation
- Turned-over amounts treated as first lien collateral proceeds
- Covers direct borrower payments, collateral proceeds, bankruptcy distributions, and all other sources

**Specific scenarios to address:**
- Cash collateral use in bankruptcy
- Adequate protection payments
- Distributions under confirmed reorganization plans

### Article V — Representations & Warranties

Mutual representations from both lien holders:

- [ ] Due organization and authority
- [ ] No conflicts with organizational docs or other agreements
- [ ] Valid and perfected security interests
- [ ] All actions taken to establish and maintain lien priority
- [ ] No side agreements contradicting the priority scheme
- [ ] Representations re: specific transaction structure (verify against uploaded credit/security docs)

### Article VI — Covenants

**Notice requirements:**
- Prompt notice of amendments to respective credit agreements affecting collateral, obligations, or relative rights

**Second lien amendment restrictions — may not:**
- Increase principal amount
- Accelerate maturity
- Increase interest rates beyond specified thresholds
- Impose restrictions on borrower's ability to satisfy first lien obligations

**Bankruptcy coordination:**
- Filing proofs of claim
- Voting on reorganization plans
- Consent to DIP financing or asset sales

**Ongoing obligations:**
- Maintain perfection of security interests
- Cooperate on additional documentation to effectuate priority arrangement
- Precise notice mechanics (addresses, methods, timing)

### Article VII — Bankruptcy & Insolvency

- Agreement constitutes a "subordination agreement" under Section 510(a) of the Bankruptcy Code [VERIFY]
- Enforceable before, during, and after any bankruptcy case
- Second lien holders will not oppose first lien motions for:
  - Relief from automatic stay
  - Adequate protection
  - Use of cash collateral
- First lien holders have exclusive right to propose/vote/consent to DIP financing and cash collateral use
- Second lien holders may not seek adequate protection unless first lien holders are also receiving it
- Address post-petition interest treatment and Section 510(c) "equities of the case" exception [VERIFY]
- Enforceable against bankruptcy trustee or debtor-in-possession

### Article VIII — Miscellaneous

| Provision | Standard |
|---|---|
| Governing law | Specify jurisdiction (typically NY) |
| Amendments | Written consent of all parties |
| Severability | Invalid provisions severable |
| Integration | Supersedes all prior understandings |
| Counterparts | Electronic delivery permitted |
| Successors & assigns | Binding on successors; assignment mechanics |
| Third-party beneficiaries | None (or specify borrower rights if applicable) |
| Jury trial waiver | Mutual waiver |
| Jurisdiction & service | Submission to specified courts; service of process mechanics |
| Borrower consent | Specify whether amendments require borrower consent |
| Termination | Conditions for agreement termination |

## Guidelines

- Follow LSTA Model First Lien/Second Lien Intercreditor Agreement conventions
- Cross-check all defined terms: every defined term must appear in operative provisions and vice versa
- Verify internal cross-references are accurate
- Maintain consistent capitalization of defined terms throughout
- Use professional article/section numbering
- Flag any jurisdiction-specific UCC or Bankruptcy Code provisions that may vary by state
- Do not opine on enforceability — present provisions as drafted terms for counsel review
- If collateral includes real property, address mortgage subordination and recording requirements separately
- Mark any statutory citations not independently verified with [VERIFY]
