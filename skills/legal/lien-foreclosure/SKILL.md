# Mechanic's Lien Foreclosure Complaint

Drafts a court-ready complaint to foreclose a mechanic's lien, seeking judicial sale of real property to satisfy unpaid construction debts.

## Prerequisites

1. **Parties identified** — full legal names, entity types, and addresses for plaintiff (contractor/supplier), property owner, and all junior lienholders or encumbrancers
2. **Contract or purchase order** — original agreement with payment terms
3. **Payment records** — showing amounts invoiced, partial payments received, and outstanding balance
4. **Recorded mechanic's lien** — with recording date, instrument number, county, and exact legal description of property
5. **Timeline documentation** — work commencement, material delivery dates, completion/cessation date, demand dates
6. **Statutory compliance proof** — preliminary notices (if required), lien recording within statutory deadline, service on owner
7. **Applicable state lien statute** identified (varies significantly by jurisdiction)

## Complaint Structure

### Caption & Preliminary Matters

- Court: Superior/circuit court for the county where property is located
- Identify plaintiff with full entity type
- Defendants: property owner + all parties with recorded interests affected by foreclosure
- Note: this is an in rem proceeding tied to the property's situs

### Jurisdictional Allegations

- Subject matter jurisdiction: action to foreclose mechanic's lien under [cite state statute]
- Amount in controversy meets jurisdictional threshold
- Venue: property located in this county; proper venue for in rem proceedings

### Factual Allegations (Chronological)

1. Contractual relationship — parties, project description, scope of work/materials, price, payment terms
2. Plaintiff's performance — specific dates of labor/delivery, reasonable value of work and materials
3. Defendant's breach — failure to pay despite demand; exact amount owed, when due, partial payments received
4. Statutory compliance:
   - Preliminary notice served (if required) — date and method
   - Lien recorded within statutory period after completion/cessation — date, instrument no., county
   - Lien served on property owner — date and method

### Count I — Foreclosure of Mechanic's Lien

- Incorporate preceding allegations
- Allege valid, perfected lien in stated amount
- Request:
  - Determination of full amount due (principal + interest + costs)
  - Adjudication of lien validity and priority
  - Order of foreclosure
  - Judicial sale with proceeds applied to satisfy judgment

### Count II — Breach of Contract

- Contract formation and terms
- Plaintiff's full performance
- Defendant's material breach (nonpayment)
- Damages = unpaid balance + consequential damages if permitted

### Count III — Unjust Enrichment (Alternative)

- Defendant received benefit of plaintiff's labor/materials
- Defendant has knowledge of benefit
- Retention without payment is inequitable
- *Include only in jurisdictions permitting alternative pleading alongside express contract claims*

### Prayer for Relief

- [ ] Judgment foreclosing mechanic's lien
- [ ] Determination of full amount due with pre-judgment interest (statutory or contractual rate)
- [ ] Order directing judicial sale; proceeds applied: (1) costs of sale, (2) plaintiff's lien/judgment
- [ ] Deficiency judgment for any unpaid remainder (if state law permits)
- [ ] Breach of contract damages
- [ ] Attorney's fees and costs (if authorized by contract or statute)
- [ ] Such other relief as the court deems just

## Attachments

Attach as exhibits:
1. Recorded mechanic's lien (certified copy)
2. Underlying contract or purchase order
3. Statutory notices served (with proof of service)
4. Include verification/affidavit if required by state law

## Guidelines

- **Jurisdiction-specific**: Mechanic's lien statutes vary dramatically by state — preliminary notice requirements, recording deadlines, foreclosure time limits, and deficiency judgment availability all differ. Always confirm applicable statute and deadlines.
- **Strict compliance required**: Most states treat lien statutes as strictly construed; any procedural defect (late recording, deficient notice) can invalidate the lien entirely.
- **Legal descriptions**: Copy property legal descriptions exactly from the recorded lien or title documents — do not paraphrase.
- **Monetary precision**: State all amounts with exact figures; avoid rounding or estimates.
- **One allegation per paragraph**: Number paragraphs consecutively; each contains a single factual allegation or legal assertion.
- **Format per local rules**: Margins, line spacing, page numbering, and caption format must comply with the filing court's requirements.
- **Foreclosure deadline**: Most states impose a deadline to file suit after lien recording (commonly 6–12 months) — verify the lien has not expired.
- **Mark uncertain citations**: Use [VERIFY] for any statutory citation not confirmed against current state code.
