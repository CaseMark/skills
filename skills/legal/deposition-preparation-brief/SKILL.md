# Deposition Preparation Brief

Produces a structured preparation package for taking or defending depositions, organized by topic with supporting document references.

## Prerequisites

- Case file with pleadings and discovery responses
- Witness identification (name, role, relationship to case)
- Produced documents relevant to the witness
- Prior testimony or statements by the witness (if available)

## Workflow

### 1. Research Witness Background

Compile witness profile: employment history, role in disputed events, prior testimony in related matters, published statements, social media presence relevant to the case, and any known biases or credibility issues.

### 2. Map Documents to Witness

Identify every produced document the witness authored, received, or is referenced in. Organize by chronology and topic. Flag documents that contradict known positions.

### 3. Develop Topic Outlines

Structure examination by topic area rather than chronology. For each topic: state the factual proposition to establish, list supporting documents, draft open-ended questions progressing from general to specific, and note anticipated objections.

### 4. Prepare Impeachment Material

For each key assertion the witness is expected to make, identify contradictory evidence: prior inconsistent statements, documents that conflict with expected testimony, and testimony from other witnesses that diverges.

### 5. Create Quick-Reference Index

Produce a tabbed reference document: witness summary (1 page), topic outlines with document citations, impeachment materials organized by topic, and exhibit list in planned order of use.

## Pitfalls

- Over-relying on chronological questioning instead of topic-based examination
- Failing to prepare for the witness's likely narrative and redirect opportunities
- Not indexing exhibits for rapid retrieval during the deposition itself
