# Pipeline Easement Agreement

Drafts a pipeline easement agreement that grants infrastructure rights across private property while protecting landowner interests and ensuring regulatory compliance.

## Prerequisites

1. **Property deed and legal description** — metes and bounds or lot/block, confirming Grantor's ownership type (individual, joint tenants, trust, entity)
2. **Survey or plat** — licensed surveyor's depiction of easement route, width, and temporary workspace
3. **Pipeline specifications** — diameter, materials transported, operating pressure, depth of cover
4. **Grantee corporate details** — state of incorporation, registered agent, board resolution or officer certification of authority
5. **Governing contract terms** — negotiated compensation, term (perpetual vs. fixed), and any pre-existing encumbrances
6. **Applicable state recording requirements** — witness, notarization, and acknowledgment forms

## Output Structure

### Recitals
- Project nature and regulatory context
- Parties' intent to formalize easement rights
- Property and project background

### Party Identification

| Element | Grantor | Grantee |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Full legal name | As on deed | Corporate name |
| Address | Current mailing | Principal place of business |
| Entity type | Individual/trust/entity | State of incorporation |
| Authority | Ownership confirmation | Board resolution/officer cert |

### Easement Grant

Specify each right explicitly:

| Right | Scope |
|-------|-------|
| Estate type | Perpetual easement in gross / term easement / fee simple determinable |
| Authorized activities | Survey, construct, install, operate, maintain, inspect, repair, replace, remove |
| Ingress/egress | Personnel, vehicles, equipment |
| Vegetation clearing | Corridor maintenance rights |
| Ancillary facilities | Valves, markers, monitoring equipment |
| Material limitations | Specify permitted pipeline contents |
| Emergency access | Immediate access with post-hoc notice |

### Property Description & Easement Boundaries
- Full legal description (metes and bounds or lot/block per state law)
- Exhibit: survey/plat showing route, width (typically 25–100 ft), and corridor
- Temporary workspace: staging areas, access roads, spoil storage
- Must be unambiguous for future enforcement

### Grantee Obligations

- [ ] Restore surface to substantially pre-construction condition
- [ ] Minimize interference with Grantor's property use
- [ ] Comply with all federal, state, and local pipeline safety regulations
- [ ] Implement erosion control and environmental protections
- [ ] Promptly repair damage to fences, drainage, improvements
- [ ] Coordinate construction with Grantor's agricultural/commercial activities
- [ ] Adhere to specified construction timelines and noise restrictions

### Grantor Reserved Rights & Restrictions

**Permitted uses** (if non-interfering): farming, grazing, shallow utilities at approved depths

**Prohibited activities within easement:**
- Permanent structures or buildings
- Excavation, drilling, or blasting without written consent
- Deep-rooted trees or woody vegetation
- Hazardous material storage
- Heavy equipment exceeding weight limits
- Any activity impeding maintenance or emergency access

Include Grantor notification protocol for subsurface activities.

### Compensation

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Base payment | Lump sum, per-rod, or per-acre — specify method |
| Payment schedule | Upfront / milestone / completion payments |
| Temporary workspace damages | Separate from permanent easement compensation |
| Crop loss / business interruption | Construction-period compensation |
| Timber / improvement damage | Appraised value |
| Ongoing payments | Annual payments or royalties if applicable |
| Adjustments | Delays, scope changes, additional impacts |
| Payment method & timeline | Wire / check; remittance deadline |

### Indemnification
Grantee indemnifies, defends, and holds harmless Grantor from claims arising from:
- Construction, operation, or maintenance
- Environmental contamination or releases
- Personal injury or property damage
- Regulatory violations by Grantee

Include Grantee obligation for prompt environmental remediation to regulatory standards.

### Insurance Requirements

| Coverage | Minimum |
|----------|---------|
| Commercial general liability | $5–10M per occurrence (scale to risk) |
| Pollution liability | Required |
| Property damage | Required |

**Additional terms:**
- Grantor named as additional insured
- 30-day advance cancellation notice to Grantor
- Insurer rated A- or better (A.M. Best)
- Waiver of subrogation in Grantor's favor
- Primary and non-contributory designation
- Consider bonding for reclamation obligations

### Termination & Abandonment

- **Perpetual easements**: continue until formal abandonment + completion of restoration
- **Abandonment trigger**: cessation of use for defined period (e.g., 2 consecutive years)

**Grantee obligations on abandonment:**
- [ ] Remove all above-ground facilities
- [ ] Remove or abandon-in-place underground segments (specify election mechanism)
- [ ] Restore easement area to safe, usable condition
- [ ] Record release of easement

### Assignment
- Transfers to affiliates or successors: typically permitted without consent
- Transfers to unrelated third parties: require Grantor approval (or specify alternative)
- Address continuing liability of original Grantee post-assignment

### Governing Law & Dispute Resolution
- Governing law: state where property is located
- Venue: courts of the county where property is situated
- Escalation: negotiation → mediation → litigation (or binding arbitration)
- If arbitration: specify rules (e.g., AAA Commercial), arbitrator count, cost allocation
- Prevailing party attorney's fees (if permitted under state law)

### Execution & Recording

- [ ] Signature blocks with printed names, titles, dates
- [ ] Comply with state witness and notarization requirements
- [ ] Notary acknowledgment forms per jurisdiction
- [ ] Counterparts clause
- [ ] Electronic/facsimile signature clause (if permitted)
- [ ] Grantee records at own expense; provides recorded copy to Grantor within specified timeframe

## Guidelines

- Verify state-specific recording and acknowledgment requirements before finalizing
- Easement width should match pipeline diameter and regulatory setback requirements
- All statutory citations should be confirmed for the specific jurisdiction — mark uncertain references with [VERIFY]
- Environmental provisions must align with applicable PHMSA pipeline safety regulations and state environmental laws
- Compensation structures vary significantly by region — confirm market rates
- For FERC-regulated pipelines, ensure consistency with certificate conditions
- Do not include eminent domain language unless specifically instructed
- Flag any pre-existing encumbrances or title defects for attorney review
