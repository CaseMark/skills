# Site Selection Addendum

Drafts an addendum to an existing franchise real estate agreement establishing objective site selection criteria, a sequential approval process, and binding conditions precedent.

## Prerequisites

1. **Underlying agreement** — full executed copy with defined terms, party designations, execution date, and any prior amendments
2. **Party information** — exact legal names, entity types, states of formation, addresses as they appear in the base contract
3. **Business requirements** — intended use, operational needs, expansion plans, customer demographics, competitive positioning
4. **Site preferences** — geographic targets, square footage range, physical specifications, infrastructure needs
5. **Transaction context** — prior correspondence, discussed criteria, relevant deadlines, financial parameters

## Output Structure

### 1. Preamble & Parties

- Match party names, entity types, and addresses **exactly** as in underlying agreement
- Include guarantors, property managers, or affiliates if relevant
- Verify signatory authority for entity parties

### 2. Recitals

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Agreement reference | Full title, exact execution date, all parties as designated |
| Purpose | Specific site selection matters not adequately addressed in base contract |
| Business context | Operational needs, expansion plans, market factors justifying criteria |
| Prior amendments | Reference any prior amendments in sequence |

### 3. Site Selection Criteria

Draft objective, measurable standards across these categories:

| Category | Specifications |
|---|---|
| **Geography** | Cities, neighborhoods, submarkets, corridors; proximity to highways, transit, airports, customer concentrations, distribution centers |
| **Size** | Min/max square footage with variance flexibility provision |
| **Physical** | Ceiling height, column spacing, floor load capacity, loading dock config (dock-high/grade-level), truck court dimensions, parking ratios |
| **Infrastructure** | Power capacity, HVAC specs, telecom capabilities |
| **Zoning** | Permitted use alignment; as-of-right vs. CUP/variance willingness |
| **Visibility/Access** | ADA compliance, signage opportunities, thoroughfare visibility, co-tenancy considerations |

### 4. Selection Process & Timeline

Draft sequential process with concrete deadlines:

1. **Site identification** — specify who may propose sites (landlord/seller, tenant/buyer, or both)
2. **Proposal requirements** — legal description, survey, preliminary title, rent/price terms, square footage, criteria compliance confirmation
3. **Submission deadline** — [30/60] days from addendum effective date
4. **Evaluation period** — [X] business days to approve, reject, or request information
5. **Approval standard** — sole discretion / reasonableness / articulated grounds
6. **Final selection deadline** — state whether time is of the essence
7. **Failure consequences** — termination rights, extension options, liquidated damages

Include site inspection provisions: access rights, scope, notice requirements, owner presence, feasibility study allocation (obligation + cost).

### 5. Conditions Precedent

For each condition, specify: responsible party, expense allocation, deadline, and failure consequences.

| Condition | Key Terms |
|---|---|
| **Environmental** | Phase I ESA timeline, acceptability standards, Phase II trigger, remediation cost allocation |
| **Property condition** | Building systems, structural, roofing, parking inspection; acceptable condition standard or repair/credit negotiation |
| **Survey** | ALTA survey requirement, required items, encroachment/boundary dispute resolution |
| **Title** | Commitment delivery timeline, acceptable title standard, defect cure process, permitted exceptions |
| **Financing** (if applicable) | Loan amount, rate parameters, commitment deadline, appraisal requirements, failure consequences |
| **Governmental approvals** | Zoning verification, special use permits, CO, building permits, use-specific regulatory approvals |

### 6. Representations & Warranties

**Property owner represents:**
- Good and marketable title (subject to permitted exceptions)
- No undisclosed material physical defects
- No undisclosed code/zoning violations
- No undisclosed hazardous materials or environmental violations
- Current use legally conforming; intended use permitted under zoning

**Tenant/buyer represents:**
- Authority to execute; necessary organizational approvals obtained
- Financial capacity to perform

Specify: survival period, breach remedies (indemnification, termination, damages).

### 7. Economic Terms

- Rent/price adjustments based on selected site characteristics
- TI allowance or construction obligation variations by location
- Formula/methodology for final pricing if base agreement contemplated a range
- Security deposit / LOC / earnest money: amount, delivery timing, draw/forfeiture conditions, return circumstances

### 8. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

- Governing law: align with underlying agreement unless site location dictates otherwise
- Dispute resolution mechanism: negotiation → mediation → arbitration/litigation
- If arbitration: administering org (AAA/JAMS), applicable rules, number of arbitrators, location, authority limits
- Prevailing party fee-shifting
- Consent to jurisdiction, venue designation
- Consider jury trial waiver

### 9. Boilerplate & Signature

- **Modification scope** — identify specific base agreement provisions modified/supplemented/superseded
- **Ratification** — all unmodified terms remain in full force
- **Integration** — base agreement + all amendments = entire agreement
- **Counterparts** — electronic/facsimile signatures valid
- **Amendment sequencing** — number appropriately if multiple amendments exist

**Signature blocks:**
- Individuals: signature, printed name, date
- Entities: entity name, "By:" line, printed name, title, "duly authorized" language, date
- Witness lines if required by jurisdiction
- Notary acknowledgment if underlying agreement was notarized or recording is anticipated

## Guidelines

- Mirror all defined terms, formatting conventions, and structural approaches from the underlying agreement
- Verify all cross-references, consistent defined term usage, and logical numbering before finalizing
- Do not introduce new defined terms that conflict with the base agreement
- If jurisdiction-specific requirements apply (e.g., notarization, recording), confirm compliance
- Flag any provisions where the parties' instructions conflict with the base agreement terms
- Environmental representations should be carefully scoped — avoid unqualified "clean site" warranties without factual basis
- Approval standard (sole discretion vs. reasonableness) is a key negotiation point — draft per client instruction or flag for decision
