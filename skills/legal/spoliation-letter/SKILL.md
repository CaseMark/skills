# Letter of Spoliation (Evidence Preservation Demand)

Drafts a litigation-ready spoliation letter that establishes the opposing party's duty to preserve evidence and creates a record supporting future sanctions motions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Client and adverse party identities** — full legal names, addresses, counsel if known
2. **Incident details** — date, location, nature of incident and injuries
3. **Known evidence** — any specific items already identified (surveillance cameras, vehicles, products, records)
4. **Case type** — premises liability, motor vehicle, product liability, medical malpractice, or other PI subcategory
5. **Jurisdiction** — for jurisdiction-specific spoliation standards

## Output Structure

### Letter Format

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Delivery method** | "Via Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested" (and email if available) |
| **Reference line** | Client name, adverse party name, date of incident |
| **Addressee** | Person/entity with evidence custody (property owner, employer, insurer, opposing party) |

### Required Sections

**1. Opening — Representation & Notice**
- Identify attorney/firm and client represented
- State date, location, and general nature of incident
- Do NOT disclose work product or litigation strategy

**2. Legal Duty to Preserve**
- Demand preservation of all evidence relevant to anticipated litigation
- State that duty attaches once litigation is reasonably anticipated
- Require immediate suspension of routine document destruction/retention policies
- Require implementation of a litigation hold across all custodians

**3. Evidence Itemization — Case-Type Specific**

Select and tailor from applicable categories:

| Case Type | Key Evidence Categories |
|---|---|
| **Premises liability** | Surveillance footage (specify camera locations + time window), incident/accident reports, maintenance/inspection logs, prior complaints, repair records, photographs, safety policies |
| **Motor vehicle** | EDR/black box data, dashcam/body cam footage, vehicle maintenance records, driver logs/hours-of-service, employment/training records, cell phone records, GPS data |
| **Product liability** | Subject product and exemplars, design specs, testing/QA records, prior complaints/recalls, modification/repair history, marketing materials, regulatory submissions |
| **All cases** | Emails, text messages, instant messages, social media posts, internal communications, photographs/video, insurance files, personnel/training files, policies and procedures, financial records |

Always include a catch-all: *"any other documents, data, or tangible items potentially relevant to [Client]'s claims."*

**4. Electronic Evidence Specifics**
- Demand preservation of metadata, backup tapes, cloud storage, archived data
- Name specific systems/platforms if known
- Require preservation in native format

**5. Consequences of Non-Compliance**

State that spoliation may result in:
- Adverse inference jury instructions
- Issue/fact preclusion
- Monetary sanctions
- Dismissal of defenses or default judgment
- Independent tort liability for spoliation
- Punitive damages exposure

**6. Compliance Demand**
- Written confirmation within **10 business days** of receipt
- Confirmation must identify: (a) preservation steps taken, (b) custodians responsible, (c) scope of litigation hold implemented

**7. Closing & Signature Block**
- Attorney name, firm, address, phone, email, bar number

## Template Shell

```
[DATE]

Via Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested
[Also via email: ___]

[RECIPIENT NAME]
[TITLE]
[COMPANY/ENTITY]
[ADDRESS]

Re: Preservation of Evidence — [CLIENT NAME] v. [ADVERSE PARTY]
    Date of Incident: [DATE]
    Location: [LOCATION]

Dear [RECIPIENT]:

[OPENING — representation, incident identification]

[LEGAL DUTY — preservation obligation, litigation hold demand]

[EVIDENCE LIST — case-specific itemization]

[ELECTRONIC EVIDENCE — ESI-specific demands]

[CONSEQUENCES — sanctions warning]

[COMPLIANCE DEADLINE — 10 business days, written confirmation]

[CLOSING]

Respectfully,

[ATTORNEY NAME]
[FIRM NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[PHONE] | [EMAIL]
[BAR NUMBER]
```

## Guidelines

- **Tone**: Professional, firm, unambiguous — not adversarial or threatening
- **Specificity**: The more specific the evidence list, the stronger the sanctions foundation; always supplement generic categories with case-specific items drawn from uploaded documents
- **Breadth**: List must be specific enough for clear notice but broad enough to capture all relevant materials
- **No work product disclosure**: Do not reveal legal theories, damage calculations, or litigation strategy
- **Jurisdictional variance**: Spoliation standards differ by state — some recognize an independent tort, others rely solely on sanctions; flag jurisdiction-specific requirements where known [VERIFY specific state standards when drafting]
- **Proof of service**: Always use certified mail with return receipt; consider concurrent email delivery
- **Timing**: Send as early as possible — delay weakens sanctions arguments
- **Multiple custodians**: If evidence is held by multiple entities (e.g., property owner + management company + insurer), send separate letters to each
