# Plan of Liquidation and Dissolution

Creates a litigation-grade dissolution plan that supports lawful wind-up, fiduciary defense posture, and execution tracking from resolution to filing completion.

## Prerequisites

1. Confirm state(s) of incorporation and governing entity documents: charter, bylaws, shareholder agreement, operating constraints.
2. Confirm dissolved entity path: voluntary dissolution under applicable state corporation statute.
3. Confirm approved corporate actions:
   - Board resolution with date, quorum, vote counts, and authorization language.
   - Shareholder vote/sufficient written consents with class-vote handling if relevant.
4. Collect governance and transaction data:
   - Stock ledger (class/rank), ownership percentages, transfer restrictions.
   - Current financials, schedules of assets/liabilities, pending litigation list, tax status.
5. Confirm notice and filing requirements with counsel:
   - State secretary of state requirements (forms, fees, waiting periods).
   - Federal tax milestones (including Form 966 and final returns).
6. Confirm stakeholder communications plan and publication channel for unknown creditors.

## Output Structure / Process

### A. Build Entity & Jurisdiction Matrix

| Item | Required Input | Draft Control |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Entity identity | Legal name, formation state, tax ID, principal office | Use exact legal names/codes; mirror formation records |
| Closing timeline | Target adoption, creditor response, expected filing dates | Ensure state/publication intervals are state-compliant [VERIFY] |
| Approvals | Board + shareholder actions, class vote mechanics | Include vote tallies and voting rights analysis |
| Creditor administration | Known/unknown creditor list, claim deadlines, reserve policy | Align notice language with statute and case posture |
| Distribution policy | Liabilities stack, reserves, preference classes | Explicitly document liquidation preference and conversion formula |
| Tax package | EIN, filing history, state tax IDs/licences | Integrate Form 966 and final tax obligations |

### B. Required Sections (Generate in Fixed Order)

| Section | Minimum Required Elements | Output Artifact |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Preamble & Recitals | Corporate identity, statutory basis, date, reasoned basis | Introductory recital clause |
| Board/Shareholder Authorization | Meeting/consent details, resolutions, voting mechanics, class vote results | Authorization section + exhibit references |
| Notice & Claims Process | Methods of delivery, deadlines, publication terms, response handling | Claims procedure clauses and claim form language |
| Winding Up Operations | Contract closure, litigation handling, asset conversion strategy | Operational winding-up matrix |
| Priority Payments | Liquidation expenses, wages/benefits, taxes, secured and unsecured claims, contingencies | Payment waterfall and reservation schedule |
| Shareholder Distribution | Class-based preference, pro rata mechanics, in-kind vs cash process | Distribution clause + math worksheet |
| Dissolution Filings | Filing form list, signature authority, recordkeeping and proof | Filing checklist and filing signature block |
| Tax & Compliance | Final return duties, IRS/Form 966, clearances, record retention | Compliance roadmap |
| Execution & Effectiveness | Effective date, cure period notes, officer certifications | Signature blocks + execution statements |

### C. Drafting Template

```text
PLAN OF LIQUIDATION AND DISSOLUTION
of [Entity Name], a [State] corporation

1. Preamble
   - Entity identifiers
   - Authority to dissolve
   - Purpose of plan
2. Authorization
   - Board proceedings (date, quorum, votes)
   - Shareholder actions (votes by class)
3. Notices
   - Known creditors: method + deadline
   - Unknown creditors: publication + period [VERIFY]
4. Claims and Winding Up
   - Claim submission forms/timing
   - Disputed claims reserves
5. Payment Priority
   - Expense waterfall
   - Reserve methodology and release triggers
6. Distributions
   - Class ladder
   - Per-share calculation
7. Tax & Compliance
   - Form 966 and final return filing timeline
   - State/federal tax certificates
8. Filing & Termination
   - Articles/Certificate of Dissolution package
9. Execution
   - Effective date
   - Officer certification
```

### D. Mandatory Deliverables

1. `[ ]` Clean, date-stamped preamble and recitals.
2. `[ ]` Governance evidence paragraph for every required threshold.
3. `[ ]` Creditor notice log template and publication proof list.
4. `[ ]` Reserve memo: disputed, environmental/contingent, tax exposure.
5. `[ ]` Distribution worksheet by share class.
6. `[ ]` State filing exhibit index with filing copies.
7. `[ ]` Post-filing action list with retention and audit handling.

## Guidelines

- Use precise defined terms; do not mix “dissolved,” “wound up,” and “terminated” without defining each term first.
- Preserve board/shareholder decision chronology as enforceable fact paragraphs.
- Never state creditors are “waived” without documented statutory or consent basis.
- For unknown creditors, use published notice period exactly as permitted by state law [VERIFY].
- Use separate class distribution schedule whenever preferred rights exist, even if zero-value in final distribution.
- Include statutory references only after jurisdiction verification to avoid portability errors [VERIFY].
- Nonprofit wind-down: route residual assets to qualified tax-exempt recipients per governing tax law [VERIFY].
- Post-dissolution records: retain closing papers and tax support for statutory period (often 3–7 years, confirm exact duration).
