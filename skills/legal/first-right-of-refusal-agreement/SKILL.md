# First Right of Refusal Agreement (Franchise)

Drafts a U.S. franchise right-of-first-refusal agreement that secures franchisor approval rights while preserving a lawful transfer path for the franchisee.

## Prerequisites

1. Signed Franchise Agreement with key transfer/consent clauses and termination rights.
2. Verified party details: legal names, entity type, jurisdiction of formation/organization, authorized signers.
3. State law preferences for enforceability (governing law, venue, equitable remedies).
4. Transfer framework: whether covered entity owns one or multiple units and what constitutes transfer of control.
5. Negotiation parameters: notice/election days, completion windows, carve-out policy, valuation approach, remedy priorities.

## Output Structure / Process

1) Build an input table before drafting.

| Input | Capture |
|---|---|
| Parties | Franchisor / franchisee identities and addresses |
| Governed contract | Franchise Agreement date and cross-reference language |
| Covered transfer scope | Asset sale, stock/membership transfer, merger, reorg, pledge, etc. |
| Trigger windows | Notice period, exercise period, closing period |
| Non-compliant behavior | Default remedies and cure standards |
| Jurisdiction | Governing law, forum, service addresses |

2) Draft core sections in this exact order:

- Heading and recitals
- Definitions
- ROFR grant and covered transaction scope
- Notice package requirements
- Franchisor election process
- Non-exercise/approval-to-close rules
- Value and economics matching
- Closing conditions and costs
- Reinstatement and relationship-to-franchise agreement
- Warranties, breaches, and remedies
- Miscellaneous (assignment, survival, amendment, integration, notices, signatures)

3) Use clause-level output blocks only, each with placeholders.

```text
[FRANCHISOR NAME]
[FRANCHISEE NAME]
[FRANCHISE EFFECTIVE DATE]
[GOVERNING LAW]
[NOTICE ADDRESS]
```

4) Enforceable trigger logic template:

| Trigger condition | Required franchisee action | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Third-party bona fide offer received | Deliver complete notice package within agreed days | Franchisor gets matching right |
| Incomplete info | Cure request + fixed cure period | No exercise period starts until cured |
| Franchisor elects to purchase | Written election within exercise period | Binding matching purchase terms |
| Franchisor declines/non-response | Franchisee may close only on materially identical terms | Material changes restart process |
| Sham or structured avoidance | Immediate breach workflow | Equitable relief + damages |

5) Required notice package (insert as checklist):

- Signed offer documentation (including LOI / term sheet / purchase agreement)
- Purchase price and all payment components
- Financing terms and contingencies
- Identity/affiliates of proposed purchaser
- Escrow/security assumptions, liabilities, excluded assets
- Proposed closing timeline and conditions
- Certifications of non-circumvention and no bad-faith structuring

6) Include numeric timeline blocks, not narrative-only terms:

```text
Notice deadline: ____ business days after offer
Franchisor exercise window: ____ days after complete notice
Closing target: ____ days after election
Third-party completion window: ____ days
Materiality trigger: price change >____% OR >$____
```

7) Required matrix for transfer scope.

| Transaction type | Apply ROFR? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Direct sale of franchised business | Yes/No | Scope-defined |
| Transfer of controlling ownership interest | Yes | Include mergers/reorgs |
| Transfer of minority non-controlling interests | Yes/No | Clarify by threshold |
| Estate or family transfer exception | Carve-out/conditional | Preserve franchisor rights |
| Pledge or financing collateralization | Optional carve-out | If default on collateral event occurs, define restart |

8) Add an anti-circumvention section:
- no step transaction to evade ROFR
- no pre-closing assignment/flip structures without disclosure
- all related-party or changed-party deals require refresh of notice and election period

## Guidelines

- Do lock legal definitions first; every operative clause should reference defined terms.
- Do harmonize with the Franchise Agreement transfer framework and state which document controls on conflict.
- Do separate scope exceptions (family, estate, affiliate) from approval rights to reduce interpretation disputes.
- Do preserve franchisor remedy stack: injunctive relief, specific performance, attorneys’ fees/costs, and any termination rights expressly allowed by the Franchise Agreement.
- Do add compliance guardrails for reasonableness of time limits and geographic/control restrictions by state law.
- Don’t use vague “reasonable” terms where fixed deadlines are intended.
- Don’t mix form and content clauses; keep drafting logic, mechanics, and boilerplate isolated.
- Don’t leave references to undefined attachments or exhibits.
- If enforceability is jurisdiction-sensitive, add explicit legal review flags for reasonableness, restraints on alienation, and remedy limits. [VERIFY]
