# Private Placement Memorandum (PPM)

Drafts a legally compliant PPM for Regulation D private offerings that provides full and fair disclosure while shielding the issuer from securities liability.

## Prerequisites

1. **Offering terms** — security type, total amount, price/valuation, min/max amounts, minimum investment, Rule 506(b) vs. 506(c) election
2. **Corporate documents** — articles of incorporation, cap table, stockholder agreements, option/warrant schedules, prior financing docs
3. **Financial statements** — balance sheet, income statement, cash flow (audited/reviewed/compiled status noted)
4. **Management bios** — names, titles, education, professional history for officers, directors, and key advisors
5. **Business materials** — pitch deck, business plan, material contracts, IP portfolio, customer/supplier agreements
6. **Transaction documents** — subscription agreement, investor questionnaire, escrow arrangements

## Output Structure

### Document Sections

| # | Section | Key Contents |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | **Cover Page** | Company name, offering description (amount, security type, series), date, confidentiality legend, securities law legend, transfer restriction warnings |
| 2 | **Executive Summary** | Offering terms table, company overview, 3-5 principal risk factors, intended use of proceeds summary |
| 3 | **Risk Factors** | Organized by category (see below) |
| 4 | **Use of Proceeds** | Gross proceeds → expenses → net proceeds; specific allocations with strategic rationale; min/max scenarios |
| 5 | **Business Description** | History, products/services, market analysis, competitive landscape, sales/marketing, operations, regulatory environment, IP portfolio |
| 6 | **Management & Board** | Bios in reverse-chronological format; board composition and independence; advisory board |
| 7 | **Capitalization** | Pre-offering and post-offering cap tables (min and max scenarios); fully diluted ownership; option/warrant/convertible schedules |
| 8 | **Securities Terms** | Full term-sheet-level detail on all rights (see below) |
| 9 | **Plan of Distribution** | Offering mechanics, timeline, subscription procedures, escrow, accredited investor verification |
| 10 | **Legal Matters** | Counsel identification, auditor identification, forward-looking statement disclaimers |
| 11 | **Investor Qualifications** | Accredited investor definitions, suitability requirements, verification procedures |

### Risk Factors Categories

Draft each risk as a **titled subsection** with description, materiality explanation, and potential consequences.

| Category | Required Topics |
|----------|----------------|
| **Financial** | Operating losses/burn rate, additional capital needs, dilution, debt obligations, limited resources |
| **Business/Operational** | Competition, market acceptance, technology/product development, regulatory compliance, key personnel, IP risks, customer concentration, supply chain, scaling |
| **Investment-Specific** | No public market/illiquidity, transfer restrictions, speculative nature, voting limitations, management discretion over proceeds, subordination, no dividends |

### Securities Terms Checklist

Address each applicable term at term-sheet precision:

- [ ] Dividends — rate, cumulative vs. non-cumulative, participation rights
- [ ] Liquidation preference — amount, participating vs. non-participating, deemed liquidation events
- [ ] Conversion — ratio/price, adjustments (splits, anti-dilution), optional vs. mandatory vs. auto-convert triggers
- [ ] Voting — votes per share, as-converted basis, protective provisions (enumerate veto rights)
- [ ] Redemption — company vs. investor rights, triggers, pricing, subordination to debt
- [ ] Transfer restrictions — lock-ups, ROFR, co-sale/tag-along, drag-along, permitted transfers
- [ ] Registration rights — demand, piggyback, S-3, expense allocation, cutback provisions
- [ ] Anti-dilution — weighted average vs. full ratchet; preemptive rights and exceptions
- [ ] Information rights — financial statement frequency, budget access, inspection rights
- [ ] Board rights — designation rights, observer rights, committee assignments

### Cover Page Legends

Include three mandatory legends:

1. **Confidentiality** — proprietary info, no reproduction/distribution, return/destroy obligation
2. **Securities law** — not registered under Securities Act of 1933 or state laws; no SEC/state review or approval; criminal offense to represent otherwise
3. **Transfer restrictions** — significant resale limitations; no public market expected; acquire for investment only; prepare to hold indefinitely

### Subscription Procedure Template

```
SUBSCRIPTION PROCESS
1. Review entire PPM including all exhibits; focus on Risk Factors
2. Complete and execute Subscription Agreement
3. Complete Investor Questionnaire (KYC/AML compliance)
4. Provide accredited investor verification documentation:
   - Individuals (income): Tax returns + W-2s (2 most recent years)
   - Individuals (net worth): Brokerage/bank statements + credit report
   - Individuals (certification): Letter from CPA, attorney, or RIA
   - Entities (assets): Financial statements showing >[threshold]
   - Entities (ownership): Accredited status of all equity owners
5. Submit payment via [wire transfer/check]
   Wire instructions: [Bank, Account, Routing, Reference]
6. Await acceptance confirmation (company reserves right to reject)
```

## Guidelines

- **Disclosure standard**: Material = any fact a reasonable investor would consider important. Disclose both positive developments and risks/challenges.
- **Dual function**: The PPM is simultaneously a disclosure document, liability shield, marketing document, and compliance tool. Balance accordingly.
- **Anti-fraud compliance**: Every statement must satisfy Section 10(b) / Rule 10b-5 — no material misstatements or omissions.
- **506(b) vs. 506(c)**: If 506(b), ensure no general solicitation language appears. If 506(c), document reasonable verification steps for accredited investors.
- **State blue sky**: Flag state filing requirements; note that Rule 506 preempts state registration but not notice filing or fee requirements.
- **Forward-looking statements**: Mark all projections with cautionary language; list specific factors that could cause actual results to differ.
- **Financial statements**: Clearly label as audited, reviewed, or compiled. If unaudited, add prominent disclaimer.
- **Information gaps**: When source documents lack required information, flag with `[COMPANY TO PROVIDE: specific item needed]` and explain the disclosure obligation.
- **Accredited investor thresholds**: Reference current Rule 501 definitions; include professional certification category added in 2020 amendments. [VERIFY current thresholds]
- **Form D filing**: Note requirement to file Form D with SEC within 15 days of first sale.
- **No promotional overreach**: Maintain factual, balanced tone throughout. Business description may be compelling but never unsubstantiated.
- **Consistency check**: Cross-reference all figures (cap table, use of proceeds, offering terms) across sections for internal consistency.
- **Date sensitivity**: Note that PPM is accurate only as of its date; flag obligation to supplement if material changes occur during offering period.
