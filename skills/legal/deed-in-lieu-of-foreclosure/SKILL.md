# Deed in Lieu of Foreclosure Agreement

Drafts a recordable, enforceable Deed in Lieu of Foreclosure Agreement transferring property from borrower to lender in full satisfaction of a defaulted secured debt.

## Prerequisites

1. **Original loan documents** — promissory note, mortgage or deed of trust (with recording date, instrument number, county recorder's office)
2. **Current payoff figures** — outstanding principal, accrued interest through a specific date, fees and costs
3. **Complete legal description** — exact as recorded in the mortgage/deed of trust
4. **Party identification** — full legal names as they appear on recorded instruments; entity parties need jurisdiction of organization, authorized signatory title, and corporate resolutions
5. **Negotiated deal terms** — deficiency waiver or survival, any cash consideration/relocation assistance, credit reporting treatment
6. **Junior lienholders** — identify any subordinate liens, judgments, or encumbrances requiring subordination or payoff
7. **State of situs** — all recording requirements, notary form, witness rules, and anti-deficiency statutes are jurisdiction-specific

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption & Parties

| Field | Borrower (Grantor) | Lender (Grantee) |
|---|---|---|
| Full legal name | As on title/note | As on note/mortgage |
| Address | Current (note if differs from property) | Principal place of business |
| Entity details | Authority to convey | Jurisdiction, trustee capacity if applicable |

### 2. Recitals

- Original loan: date, principal amount, note reference
- Mortgage/DOT: recording date, instrument number, recorder's office
- Nature and duration of default
- Outstanding debt as of specified date (principal + interest + fees)
- Lender's right to foreclose; parties' mutual preference for negotiated resolution
- Voluntary nature of conveyance; arms-length, good-faith negotiation

### 3. Property Conveyance

- **Granting clause**: Borrower grants, conveys, and transfers all right, title, and interest
- **Legal description**: Reproduce verbatim from recorded instrument (lot/block, metes and bounds, or other per state standard)
- **Deed type**: Warranty deed (most states) or grant deed (CA and others) — confirm per jurisdiction
- **Fixtures/personal property**: List any items transferring with real property
- **Delivery condition**: Vacant, broom-clean, free of personal belongings by closing date; surrender all keys, access codes, remotes

### 4. Consideration & Debt Resolution

- Lender accepts conveyance in **full satisfaction and discharge** of the note and mortgage
- **Deficiency**: State explicitly whether waived in full or surviving
  - Research state anti-deficiency statutes [VERIFY for applicable state] — many states (AZ, CA, MN, NC, ND) restrict or prohibit deficiency judgments on purchase-money or residential loans
  - Include express waiver language if negotiated; preserve lender rights if not
- **Additional consideration**: Cash payment, relocation assistance, credit reporting accommodation — specify dollar amount, payment timing, and conditions precedent

### 5. Mutual Releases

**Lender releases borrower from:**
- All liability on the promissory note and related loan documents
- Effective upon acceptance of deed and recording of mortgage satisfaction

**Borrower releases lender from:**
- Claims arising from loan origination, servicing, default handling, and deed-in-lieu process
- Carve out claims unrelated to the loan transaction

**Indemnification (Borrower → Lender):**
- Undisclosed liens, encumbrances, or claims against title
- Environmental contamination, code violations, or property defects existing pre-conveyance
- Survival period: 1–3 years (negotiated); specify in agreement

### 6. Representations & Warranties

**Borrower warrants:**
- [ ] Marketable title, free of undisclosed liens and encumbrances
- [ ] No pending or threatened litigation affecting title or conveyance
- [ ] Property complies with zoning, building codes; all permits obtained
- [ ] All known material physical defects disclosed (structural, water, mold, pests, mechanical)
- [ ] No environmental violations or hazardous materials to borrower's actual knowledge
- [ ] Full authority and capacity to execute; no conflicting orders or agreements

**Lender warrants:**
- [ ] Authority to accept conveyance, release debt, and satisfy mortgage
- [ ] Necessary corporate/board authorizations obtained

> Use "to the best of Borrower's actual knowledge, without duty of inquiry" qualifiers where appropriate.

### 7. Closing Mechanics

**Closing date, time, and location** — specify with reasonable notice period.

| Borrower Delivers | Lender Delivers |
|---|---|
| Executed, notarized deed in recordable form | Executed satisfaction of mortgage/DOT release |
| Entity authority docs (resolutions, cert. of good standing) | Cash consideration / relocation payment |
| All keys, access codes, alarm codes, remotes | Corporate authorization / officer's certificate |
| Title policies, surveys, inspection reports, warranties on hand | Estoppel letter or credit reporting accommodation |
| Gov't approvals (CO, final inspection) if required | |

**Cost allocation** (customize per negotiation):
- Recording fees: Lender
- Transfer/excise taxes: Lender or split (per local custom)
- Title insurance: Lender
- Each party's attorney fees: own account

**Recording**: Lender records deed and mortgage satisfaction within [X] business days post-closing; provides borrower certified copies within [X] days of recording.

### 8. Tax Implications

- **1099-C**: Lender must issue IRS Form 1099-C for any forgiven deficiency; borrower advised to consult tax counsel regarding potential COD income and available exclusions (insolvency exception under IRC § 108; qualified principal residence indebtedness exclusion [VERIFY current availability])
- **Capital gains**: If principal residence, borrower should evaluate IRC § 121 exclusion applicability
- Each party bears its own tax liability; mutual indemnification for own tax obligations
- Agreement does not constitute tax advice; parties directed to independent counsel

### 9. General Provisions

| Provision | Summary |
|---|---|
| Governing law | State where property is located (law of situs) |
| Entire agreement | Supersedes all prior negotiations and oral agreements |
| Amendment | Must be in writing, signed by both parties |
| Severability | Invalid provisions severed; parties negotiate replacement |
| Notices | Writing required; personal delivery, overnight courier, or certified mail (3-day deemed receipt) |
| Waiver | No failure to exercise constitutes waiver of future rights |

### 10. Execution & Recording Requirements

- Signature blocks: full name, title (if entity signatory), date
- **Notarial acknowledgment**: Use exact statutory form for state of situs — individual vs. entity acknowledgments differ; confirm with state statutes [VERIFY per state]
- **Witness requirement**: Some states (FL, GA, SC, CT) require 1–2 witnesses in addition to notary [VERIFY per state]
- **Required recording elements** (confirm per county):
  - Preparer's name and address
  - Return-to address
  - Grantor/grantee names
  - Legal description
  - Granting clause
  - Transfer tax statement or preliminary change of ownership report (CA and others)
  - Notarial acknowledgment

## Guidelines

- **Junior liens**: Agreement does not eliminate subordinate liens; arrange payoffs or subordination agreements before closing or lender accepts subject to them
- **Deed type**: Confirm warranty deed vs. grant deed vs. special warranty deed per jurisdiction — do not default to quitclaim without explicit lender agreement
- **Entity borrowers**: LLC/corp borrowers must provide operating agreement/bylaws authorization; trusts need trustee certification
- **Involuntariness risk**: Document the voluntary, arms-length nature throughout; inadequate consideration or duress can void the conveyance
- **Title search**: Recommend lender obtain a current title search before closing to identify undisclosed liens
- **Environmental**: For commercial property, consider Phase I ESA requirement before acceptance
- **Recording**: Record promptly to perfect lender's title and cut off borrower's redemption rights where applicable
- **No tax/financial advice**: Refer parties to independent counsel for tax and credit implications
