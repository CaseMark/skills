# Easement Dispute Complaint

Drafts a plaintiff-side complaint for easement interference, trespass, declaratory judgment, and/or injunctive relief in U.S. real property litigation.

## Prerequisites

1. **Easement instrument** — recorded grant, deed reservation, or basis for implied/prescriptive easement with recording info (book/page or document number)
2. **Legal descriptions** — dominant estate and servient estate, including any surveys or plats
3. **Interference evidence** — dated photos, correspondence, or descriptions of obstructions, barriers, or encroachments
4. **Party information** — full legal names, addresses, and ownership/interest roles for all plaintiffs and defendants
5. **Jurisdiction/venue** — county and court where property is located; local rules for formatting (line numbering, font, spacing, caption format)
6. **Damages information** — alternative access costs, property value impact, repair/removal expenses

## Output Structure

### Caption & Introduction
- Court name with county/district/division
- Complete legal names for all parties (include entity type for non-individuals)
- Document title (e.g., *Complaint for Interference with Easement Rights, Trespass, and Injunctive Relief*)
- One-paragraph roadmap: easement type, defendant's interference, relief sought

### Party Allegations
| Party | Required Allegations |
|---|---|
| Plaintiff | Full name, address, nature of interest in dominant estate, standing basis |
| Defendant | Full name, address, relationship to servient estate (owner/tenant/other), role in interference |

### Jurisdiction & Venue
- **State court**: Claim affecting title/possession of real property; amount in controversy if threshold applies
- **Federal (diversity)**: Citizenship of each party, complete diversity, AIC > $75,000
- **Venue**: Property location statute; cite applicable venue code; personal jurisdiction allegations if out-of-state defendant

### Factual Narrative (Chronological)

**Easement Creation — allege by type:**

| Type | Required Facts |
|---|---|
| Express grant | Granting instrument date, parties, recording info; quote scope/location language; chain of title |
| Reservation | Conveyance history, language of reservation, recording info |
| Implication | Apparent + continuous use at severance; reasonable necessity; intent to continue |
| Necessity | Common ownership, severance date, landlocked/no public access at severance |
| Prescriptive | Open & notorious; continuous & uninterrupted; adverse; claim of right; statutory period with specific use examples and dates |

**Physical Description of Easement:**
- Dimensions, boundaries, location referenced to survey/plat
- Improvements (road, driveway, gate, utilities, drainage) and who constructed/maintains them
- Historical use pattern: plaintiff and predecessors exercised rights peaceably for [X] years

**Defendant's Interference — allege with specificity:**
- Each act with date, time, location, and description (barrier erected, vehicles parked, debris placed, verbal threats)
- Location and dimensions of any physical obstruction relative to easement boundary
- Communications: demand letters sent, defendant's responses/refusals, failed negotiations
- Defendant's stated position (abandonment, scope dispute, location dispute) — address preemptively

**Damages:**
- Cost of alternative access routes
- Expenses incurred removing/responding to obstructions
- Diminution in dominant estate market value
- Lost use and enjoyment

### Causes of Action

**Count I — Interference with Easement Rights**
1. Plaintiff holds valid easement [creation facts incorporated]
2. Defendant has interfered through specific acts of obstruction/blockage
3. Interference is substantial and wrongful
4. No legal justification or excuse
5. Plaintiff has suffered and continues to suffer harm
6. Cite applicable state property law

**Count II — Trespass** *(if applicable)*
1. Plaintiff holds possessory interest in easement area
2. Defendant intentionally entered or caused objects/structures to be placed in easement area without permission
3. Entry/placement constitutes unlawful interference with possessory rights
4. Allege continuing vs. temporary trespass as facts support

**Count III — Declaratory Judgment**
1. Actual, justiciable controversy exists
2. Specify questions requiring determination: easement location, scope of permitted uses, maintenance obligations, validity
3. Uncertainty causes substantial harm OR defendant has taken adverse position
4. Cite state declaratory judgment act [VERIFY applicable statute]

**Count IV — Injunctive Relief**
1. No adequate remedy at law (loss of access not fully compensable by damages; interference ongoing)
2. Irreparable harm — describe specifically
3. Balance of hardships favors plaintiff (defendant need only cease interference)
4. Public interest: enforcing property rights; ensuring access to dominant estate
5. Seek temporary, preliminary, and permanent relief as warranted

**Additional Counts to Consider:**
- **Nuisance** — interference with use/enjoyment of dominant estate beyond easement area
- **Breach of covenant** — if easement created by agreement with express promises
- **Slander of title** — if defendant made false statements challenging easement validity

---

### Prayer for Relief

1. **Declaratory judgment** — existence, validity, location, scope of easement; plaintiff's rights; defendant's obligation to refrain from interference
2. **Preliminary and permanent injunction** — cease all interference; remove obstructions/barriers/encroachments; specify any affirmative conduct required; court retains jurisdiction to enforce
3. **Compensatory damages** — itemized: alternative access costs, removal expenses, property value diminution, other economic losses
4. **Consequential damages** — foreseeable harm per applicable state law
5. **Attorney's fees** — if available by statute, contractual provision, or equitable doctrine [VERIFY fee-shifting authority]
6. **Pre-judgment interest** from date of each damage item at statutory rate
7. **Post-judgment interest**
8. **Costs of suit**
9. **Such other and further relief as the court deems just and proper**

## Guidelines

- **Pleading specificity**: Each interference act must be alleged with date, description, and location — conclusory allegations of "interference" alone are insufficient
- **Exhibits**: Attach recorded easement instrument, relevant chain-of-title excerpts, survey/plat, photographs, and demand correspondence; reference each exhibit in the body
- **Verification**: Include if required by local rules or if seeking TRO/preliminary injunction
- **Paragraph structure**: One allegation per numbered paragraph; number consecutively throughout
- **Anticipate defenses**: Preemptively allege facts rebutting abandonment, scope excess, and location disputes in the factual narrative
- **Jurisdiction-specific**: Prescriptive easement statutory periods vary by state [VERIFY]; injunction standards (likelihood of success vs. probability) vary by circuit/state [VERIFY]
- **Citation format**: Use Bluebook or local citation rules; verify all statutory citations before filing
- **Internal consistency**: Confirm every cause of action is supported by factual allegations and every item in the prayer corresponds to an asserted claim
