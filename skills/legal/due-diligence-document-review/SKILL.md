# Due Diligence Document Review

Reviews large document sets to identify risks, obligations, and material issues for transactions or investigations.

## Prerequisites

1. **Document set** — data room contents, corporate records, or production documents uploaded to vault
2. **Review protocol** — what to look for (material contracts, litigation, IP, regulatory issues, change of control)
3. **Priority matrix** — which document types and issues matter most for this deal

## Workflow

### 1. Inventory and Categorize

- Catalog all documents by type: contracts, filings, correspondence, financials, IP registrations
- OCR any scanned documents
- Flag incomplete or missing categories against the standard due diligence checklist

### 2. Systematic Review

For each document category, extract:
- Key obligations and deadlines
- Change of control or assignment restrictions
- Unusual or non-standard terms
- Pending or threatened litigation
- Regulatory compliance status

### 3. Risk Flagging

| Severity | Criteria |
|---|---|
| Critical | Deal-breaker issues: undisclosed litigation, material breaches, regulatory violations |
| High | Significant risk requiring negotiation: unfavorable contracts, IP gaps |
| Medium | Notable items for awareness: approaching deadlines, unusual terms |
| Low | Minor items: administrative gaps, formatting issues |

### 4. Produce Report

- Executive summary with top findings
- Detailed findings organized by category
- Risk matrix with severity ratings
- List of missing or incomplete documents
- Recommended follow-up questions for the target

## Pitfalls

- **Don't skip boilerplate** — material risks hide in standard-looking clauses
- **Cross-reference** — a contract may reference side letters or amendments not in the data room
- **Track what's missing** — absence of expected documents is itself a finding
- **Date sensitivity** — check whether documents are current or superseded
