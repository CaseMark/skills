# Tenant Improvement Work Letter

Drafts a binding Work Letter exhibit to a commercial lease governing the design, approval, construction, and payment of tenant improvements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Executed or draft lease** — with identified parties, premises, permitted use, and rent commencement mechanics
2. **TI allowance terms** — total amount and per-RSF calculation, eligible cost categories
3. **Base building scope** — what Landlord delivers vs. what constitutes TI Work
4. **Building rules / construction standards** — Landlord's contractor requirements, insurance minimums, working-hour restrictions
5. **Target completion date** — and relationship to rent commencement

## Output Structure

### 1. Header & Recitals

| Element | Instruction |
|---|---|
| Title | "WORK LETTER" or "TENANT IMPROVEMENT WORK LETTER" — label as Exhibit __ to Lease |
| Lease reference | Date, parties (full legal names), premises description |
| Integration | State Work Letter is incorporated into Lease; specify which controls on conflict |
| Effective date | Align with lease execution date |

### 2. Definitions

Define at minimum:

| Term | Content |
|---|---|
| **Tenant Improvements / TI Work** | All improvements per Approved Plans; distinguish from Landlord's base building work |
| **TI Allowance** | Total $, per-RSF calculation, one-time vs. replenishable |
| **Approved Plans** | Final space plan + construction drawings + specs approved in writing by Landlord |
| **Substantial Completion** | Sufficiently complete for occupancy per intended use; only punch list items remain; reference applicable building codes |
| **Hard Costs** | Direct construction — labor, materials |
| **Soft Costs** | Architectural/engineering fees, permits, project management |
| **Over-Allowance Amount** | Costs exceeding TI Allowance |

### 3. Design & Approval Process

```
Phase 1: Preliminary space plan → Landlord review (10-15 business days)
Phase 2: Construction documents (arch/MEP) → Landlord review (10-15 business days)
Phase 3: Revisions if needed → Shortened review period per resubmission
Phase 4: Written approval of final Approved Plans → Construction may commence
```

- Landlord approval not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed
- Permitted bases for withholding: adverse impact on building systems, code violations, value reduction, conflict with permitted use
- **Change orders** during construction require prior written Landlord approval
- Address whether approval delays extend completion deadline or rent commencement

### 4. Permitting & Compliance

- [ ] Tenant responsible for all permits, licenses, certificates of occupancy at Tenant's cost
- [ ] Copies of permits to Landlord before construction starts
- [ ] Final certificates of occupancy to Landlord upon completion
- [ ] Compliance required: building codes, ADA, environmental regulations, all applicable federal/state/local law
- [ ] Contractor insurance minimums: CGL, workers' comp, builder's risk — Landlord as additional insured
- [ ] Certificates of insurance delivered before work commences

### 5. Construction Provisions

**Contractor selection** — specify model:
- Tenant as GC, Landlord-managed construction, or Landlord-approved contractor list
- Pre-qualification: experience, financial stability, licensing, insurance

**Standards:**
- Good and workmanlike manner; new materials ≥ building standard
- Minimize interference with other tenants and Landlord operations
- Restrictions on hours, noise, common area use, material storage, debris disposal

**Inspection & Punch List:**
- Landlord right to inspect in progress; non-conforming work must be corrected
- Punch list process triggered at Substantial Completion; 30-60 day completion window
- Tenant delivers as-built drawings, warranties, and O&M manuals

### 6. TI Allowance Structure

| Item | Provision |
|---|---|
| Amount | $__ total ($__ per RSF × __ RSF) |
| Eligible costs | Hard costs, A&E fees, permit fees, project management |
| Excluded costs | FF&E, telecom/data cabling, security systems, Tenant-requested change order increases |
| Unused allowance | Specify: reverts to Landlord / rent credit / cash payment (with conditions and timing) |
| Subsequent use | Available only for initial improvements unless otherwise stated |

### 7. Disbursement Procedures

**Required documentation per draw request:**
- [ ] Paid invoices
- [ ] Conditional and unconditional lien waivers (all tiers)
- [ ] Evidence of payment
- [ ] Certification of compliance with Approved Plans

**Mechanics:**
- Monthly progress draws or lump sum at Substantial Completion (specify)
- 10% retainage until: final completion + final unconditional lien waivers + lien-filing period expiration
- Landlord processing period: 30 days after complete documentation
- Payment method: direct to Tenant or to Tenant's GC
- Tenant indemnifies Landlord against mechanics' liens; must bond or remove liens promptly

### 8. Cost Overruns

- Tenant solely responsible for Over-Allowance Amount
- Tenant pays excess directly to contractors (or deposits with Landlord if Landlord-managed)
- Before construction: Tenant demonstrates ability to fund overruns (LOC, cash deposit, or other acceptable security)
- Cost savings treatment: specify reversion to Landlord, credit, or additional improvement use
- Change order cost increases approved and paid by Tenant

### 9. Timeline & Completion

| Milestone | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Plan submission | __ days after lease execution |
| Plan approval | __ business days after submission |
| Permit issuance | __ days after plan approval |
| Construction start | __ days after permits |
| Substantial Completion | __ days after construction start |

**Delay classification:**

| Type | Examples | Effect on Rent Commencement |
|---|---|---|
| Excusable | Force majeure, Landlord-caused, governmental | Extends rent commencement day-for-day |
| Non-excusable | Tenant-caused, contractor performance | No extension |

- Tenant provides written notice of claimed Substantial Completion → triggers Landlord inspection
- Rent commencement not delayed by Tenant's non-excusable failure to complete on time

### 10. Default & Remedies

**Tenant defaults:** failure to complete by deadline, failure to pay overruns, non-conforming construction

**Landlord remedies:**
- Complete work and charge Tenant
- Require removal of non-conforming work + restoration
- Cross-default under Lease; Lease termination rights

**Early termination obligations:**
- Remove partially completed work; restore premises
- Reimburse Landlord for disbursed TI Allowance funds
- TI ownership: improvements become Landlord's property; Tenant maintains during Lease term

### 11. Signature Blocks

Include for both parties: full legal name, authorized representative signature line, printed name/title, date. Add entity authority representations. Include notarization blocks if required by jurisdiction or recording.

## Guidelines

- Cross-reference Lease defined terms consistently; use same capitalization conventions
- All dollar amounts, RSF figures, and timeframes must be internally consistent
- Specify governing law matching the Lease
- Include amendment procedure (written, signed by both parties)
- Active voice, present tense, numbered sections/subsections for cross-referencing
- Verify local lien-filing periods and mechanic's lien statutes for the applicable jurisdiction
- If premises are in a multi-tenant building, address coordination with Landlord's base building contractor
- Do not include FF&E procurement terms — those belong in a separate exhibit if needed
