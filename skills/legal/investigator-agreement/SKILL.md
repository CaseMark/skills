# Investigator Agreement (Clinical Trial)

Drafts a legally enforceable agreement governing the sponsor–principal investigator relationship for FDA-regulated clinical research.

## Prerequisites

1. **Clinical trial protocol** — protocol number, version, phase, design, investigational product, indication, enrollment target, endpoints, special populations
2. **Party identification** — sponsor (pharma/biotech/device/academic/government), any CRO intermediary, PI credentials, institution legal name and type (academic/community/private), governing state
3. **Contracting structure** — PI individually, institution as primary with PI signatory, or joint
4. **Site personnel** — sub-investigators, study coordinators, specialists (pharmacist, lab director)
5. **Prior agreements or institutional templates** — mandatory provisions, indemnification policies, payment processing requirements
6. **Budget and compensation terms** — per-subject, per-procedure, or hybrid; fair market value basis
7. **Single-site vs. multi-center** — affects publication rights, data ownership, oversight role

## Output Structure

Draft the agreement with the following sections, adapting to the institutional type and study specifics.

### 1. Recitals & Definitions

- Identify all parties with full legal names and roles
- Define: Protocol, Investigational Product, Study Site, Confidential Information, Study Data, Serious Adverse Event, Effective Date, Study Completion

### 2. Investigator Obligations

| Obligation | Regulatory Basis | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| Protocol compliance | 21 CFR 312.60 / 812.110 | Absolute; deviations only to eliminate immediate hazard; report within 24 hrs |
| Delegation of authority | ICH E6(R2) §4.2 | Maintain signed delegation log; document qualifications/training; PI retains ultimate responsibility |
| IP accountability | 21 CFR 312.62 / 812.140 | Receipt, storage, dispensing, return/destruction records; use only per protocol |
| Informed consent | 21 CFR 50; ICH E6(R2) §4.8 | Written consent before any study procedure; current IRB-approved form only; private setting; document all required elements |
| Vulnerable populations | 45 CFR 46 Subparts B/C/D | Additional protections per population; immediate notification if subject becomes pregnant or incarcerated |
| HIPAA compliance | 45 CFR 164 | Authorization for PHI; coded identifiers on CRFs; confidential linking log at site; breach reporting |

### 3. IRB Governance

- **Initial submission**: Complete package (protocol, ICF, IB, recruitment materials, PI CV, training evidence) before any subject contact
- **Continuing review**: Per IRB schedule; include enrollment counts, AE summary, deviations, new risk information
- **Amendments**: IRB + sponsor approval before implementation (exception: immediate hazard — document, notify IRB/sponsor within 24 hrs, submit formal amendment)
- **Reporting to IRB**: SAEs (unexpected + possibly related), unanticipated problems, deviations increasing risk, subject complaints, suspension/termination
- **Lapse provision**: Cease all activities if approval lapses; exception: monitoring for enrolled subject safety
- **Document flow**: Copies of all IRB submissions/correspondence to sponsor within 5 business days

### 4. Data Integrity & Documentation

- **ALCOA+ principles**: Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate + Available, Consistent, Enduring, Traceable
- **Source document verification**: All CRF entries verifiable against source
- **Corrections**: Single line-through (paper) or audit trail (EDC); no correction fluid/erasures; date, initial, reason
- **Essential documents file**: Per ICH E6(R2) §8 — FDA 1572, financial disclosures, IRB correspondence, signed ICFs, delegation log, IP accountability records, all protocol versions, lab certifications, monitoring logs
- **Retention**: Whichever is longest — 2 years post-marketing approval (or 2 years post-discontinuation + FDA notification), institutional policy, or state law; notify sponsor before destruction; transfer if PI unable to maintain

### 5. Adverse Event Reporting

| Category | Timeline | Recipient |
|---|---|---|
| All AEs | Document regardless of severity/causality | Study file |
| SAEs (death, life-threatening, hospitalization, disability, congenital anomaly, important medical event) | Within 24 hours of awareness | Sponsor |
| Unanticipated problems involving risk | Per IRB policy | IRB |
| Sponsor safety reports | Review + submit per IRB policy | IRB |

### 6. Monitoring & Inspection

- **Sponsor monitoring**: On-site during business hours; ≥1 week advance notice (except urgent); direct access to source documents; HIPAA training for monitors; PI to respond promptly to findings and implement corrective actions
- **Regulatory inspections**: Notify sponsor within 24 hours of contact from FDA/other authority; cooperate fully; may have institutional counsel present (must not delay inspection); provide copies of FDA 483, EIR, warning letters within 5 business days
- **Corrective actions**: Collaborative development with sponsor; document implementation

### 7. Confidentiality & IP

- **Scope**: Protocol, IB, CRFs, study data, product formulation/manufacturing, regulatory strategy, all non-public sponsor information
- **Duration**: Study term + [3–5] years post-termination or until publicly available (no breach)
- **Permitted disclosures**: IRB, regulators, study personnel (bound by equivalent obligations), legal counsel
- **Return/destruction**: Upon request or termination; retain one regulatory copy; certify deletion from electronic media
- **Data ownership**: Sponsor owns all data, results, discoveries, inventions; PI cooperates on patent applications; obligation survives termination
- **Biological samples**: Sponsor-owned; subject to institutional policies on genetic testing/destruction; specify collection, processing, storage, shipment requirements

### 8. Publication Rights

- **Eligibility**: After study completion + database lock + primary analyses
- **Review period**: Submit to sponsor [45–60] days before journal submission or conference presentation
- **Sponsor rights**: Request removal of confidential information; delay [60–90] days for patent filing; request accuracy modifications; request consistency with multi-center publications
- **No suppression**: Sponsor may not suppress legitimate scientific findings; independent expert dispute resolution
- **Authorship**: Per ICMJE guidelines; multi-center — site PI may lead site-specific publications; single-center — PI leads primary publication

### 9. Financial Terms

- **Compensation structure**: Per-subject/per-procedure/hybrid; attach itemized budget as exhibit (screening, enrollment, each visit, procedures, AE management, query resolution, closeout)
- **Fair market value**: Based on time, effort, complexity, qualifications, prevailing local rates; not intended to induce prescribing/purchasing (Anti-Kickback Statute compliance)
- **Invoicing**: [Monthly/quarterly]; include visit logs, completed CRFs, query resolution evidence; payment within [30–45] days of compliant invoice
- **Adjustments**: Screen failures — [specify rate]; early withdrawals — pro rata for completed visits; subjects discontinued for PI non-compliance — no payment for unusable data
- **Reimbursable costs**: Protocol-required labs/imaging, pharmacy fees, IP storage, subject stipends/travel, recruitment ads; require documentation; pre-approval for costs above [threshold]
- **Financial disclosure**: Per 21 CFR 54 — equity >$50K, prior-year compensation >$25K (excluding study payments), proprietary interests; update during study; conflict management plan

### 10. Indemnification & Insurance

**Sponsor indemnifies PI/institution for claims arising from:**
- Protocol design
- IP formulation, manufacture, labeling, defects
- Product-related injuries when used per protocol

**Exceptions (no sponsor indemnity):**
- PI negligence, willful misconduct
- Failure to obtain/follow informed consent procedures
- Unauthorized protocol deviation
- Agreement breach
- Regulatory non-compliance

**Insurance minimums:**

| Party | Per Occurrence | Aggregate |
|---|---|---|
| Sponsor (clinical trial liability) | $[1–5]M | $[2–10]M |
| PI/Institution (professional liability) | $[1–3]M | $[3–5]M |

- Certificates of insurance required; notice of cancellation/material reduction
- Claims procedure: 10-day notice; indemnifying party controls defense; no settlement admitting fault without consent
- Obligations survive termination through applicable statute of limitations

> **Note**: Many academic/public institutions cannot contractually indemnify — rely on professional liability insurance with specified minimums instead.

### 11. Term & Termination

| Trigger | Notice | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Study completion | N/A | Agreement ends after all closeout complete |
| Sponsor — convenience | [30–60] days written | Payment through termination + reasonable wind-down costs; not a breach |
| Sponsor — for cause (protocol non-compliance, fraud, loss of IRB approval/license, material breach uncured after notice) | Immediate | May seek damages; may report to FDA/ORI |
| PI — sponsor material breach | 30-day cure period | After cure period expires |
| PI — sponsor insurance lapse or insolvency | Written notice | Effective upon notice |

**Wind-down obligations:**
- PI: Cease enrollment immediately; notify subjects; complete procedures for enrolled subjects where feasible; complete CRFs through termination; cooperate with final monitoring; return/destroy IP and confidential information; submit final IRB report
- Sponsor: Pay for completed services; reimburse reasonable wind-down costs; provide IP or alternative treatment for benefiting subjects where appropriate

### 12. Governing Law & Disputes

- Governing law: [Sponsor's state] for contractual interpretation; site jurisdiction for research conduct standards, informed consent, malpractice
- Venue: [Specify; negotiate per institutional requirements]
- Dispute resolution: (1) PI ↔ sponsor project manager → (2) institutional officials ↔ sponsor executives → (3) mediation (costs shared equally) → (4) litigation
- Consider arbitration clause based on party preferences

### 13. Representations & Warranties

**Sponsor represents:** Authority to contract; effective IND/IDE; IB contains all known safety information; will maintain insurance; IP manufactured per applicable regulations

**PI represents:** Authority to contract; qualified and licensed; disclosed all financial interests/conflicts; adequate facilities and personnel; will comply with all applicable laws; not debarred or disqualified from clinical research

**Disclaimer:** No implied warranties; investigational product is experimental; PI will not make representations beyond IRB-approved ICF

### 14. Compliance Provisions

- **Anti-corruption**: FCPA/UK Bribery Act compliance; if PI is government official, payments reviewed by institutional compliance; accurate books and records; immediate notification of improper payment requests; sponsor may terminate immediately for violation
- **Export controls/sanctions**: PI not subject to OFAC sanctions; not on SDN list; will not export IP or technical data to restricted parties/destinations
- **Healthcare compliance**: Anti-Kickback Statute, False Claims Act, Sunshine Act reporting; compensation = FMV for services rendered; cooperate with government investigations

### 15. Execution & Exhibits

- **Signature blocks**: Authorized institutional official (not just PI) for institutional agreements; PI signature acknowledging personal obligations; authority representations
- **Startup timeline**: Allow [6–12] weeks for institutional review (sponsored research office, legal, IRB, COI committee, export control)
- **Exhibits** (incorporated by reference):
  - A: Clinical Trial Protocol (with amendments)
  - B: Detailed Budget
  - C: Sub-investigator/Personnel List
  - D: PI Curriculum Vitae
  - E: Financial Disclosure Forms
  - F: Institutional Policies (if applicable)
- **Amendment process**: Material changes to protocol/budget require written amendment signed by both parties; administrative personnel updates via written notice

## Guidelines

- Adapt contracting structure and indemnification to institutional type (academic centers typically cannot indemnify sponsors)
- All bracketed values `[X]` require client-specific input — do not leave placeholders in final draft
- Verify all regulatory citations against current CFR; FDA regulations are amended periodically [VERIFY current versions]
- For device studies, substitute 21 CFR 812 for Part 312 references throughout
- Publication restrictions must not constitute suppression of legitimate findings — ensure ICMJE-compliant authorship and anti-suppression language
- Financial terms must withstand fair market value scrutiny under Anti-Kickback Statute
- Multi-center trials require coordination provisions for publication timing and data consistency
- State-specific requirements (e.g., informed consent, medical records retention) may impose additional obligations beyond federal minimums
