# Confidentiality and Invention Assignment Agreement (CIAA/PIIA)

Drafts an execution-ready CIAA that secures company IP and trade secrets while complying with state-specific invention assignment statutes and federal DTSA requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party details** — company full legal name (as formed), state of formation, principal address; employee/contractor full legal name, address, title
2. **Governing jurisdiction** — determines invention carve-outs, non-compete enforceability, and required exhibits
3. **Relationship type** — employee vs. independent contractor (affects work-made-for-hire eligibility and enforceability standards)
4. **Prior inventions** — any pre-employment IP employee wants carved out (Exhibit A)
5. **Restrictive covenants desired** — non-solicitation of employees/customers, non-compete (if any)
6. **Existing agreements** — prior CIAAs, equity award agreements, offer letters to reconcile or supersede

## Output Structure

### 1. Parties & Recitals
- Full legal entity name, jurisdiction, principal address
- Employee full name, address, role
- Recitals: access to CI, IP creation during employment, legitimate business interest in protection

### 2. Confidential Information

**Definition — include all of:**
| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Technical | Source code, algorithms, formulas, processes, designs, R&D data |
| Business | Strategic plans, pricing, financials, customer/supplier lists, business opportunities |
| Personnel | Compensation, performance reviews, org structure |
| Third-party | Customer, partner, and supplier information received under NDA |

**Standard exceptions (all four required):**
1. Publicly available through no breach by employee
2. Rightfully in employee's possession pre-disclosure (written records required)
3. Received from third party without confidentiality obligation
4. Independently developed without use of company CI (written records required)

**Obligations:**
- Hold in strict confidence; use only for company purposes
- No less than reasonable care; same standard as employee's own confidential information
- No copying, removal, or transmission beyond job requirements
- Return all materials + written certification upon termination or demand (including deletion from personal devices and cloud accounts)

**Duration:**
- Trade secrets: indefinitely (as long as secret status maintained — do not cap with a fixed term)
- Other CI: [3–5 years] post-termination

**DTSA Immunity Notice (mandatory for all agreements executed or updated after May 11, 2016):**
> An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or state trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that: (i) is made in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official or to an attorney solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (ii) is made in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit if such filing is made under seal.

Failure to include this notice bars the employer from recovering exemplary damages and attorney's fees in a DTSA action.

### 3. Invention Assignment

**"Inventions" defined:** ideas, discoveries, improvements, original works of authorship, software, algorithms, processes, formulas, know-how, data, whether or not patentable or reduced to practice, made alone or jointly during employment.

**Assignment trigger (any one of):**
1. Relates to company's current or demonstrably anticipated business or R&D
2. Uses company equipment, supplies, facilities, time, materials, or trade secret information
3. Results from tasks assigned by company

**Assignment language:** Use present-tense "hereby assigns" to create immediate vesting of future rights. Include fallback "agrees to assign" for jurisdictions not recognizing present assignments of future rights.

**Statutory carve-outs — include for employees in applicable states:**

| State | Statute | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| California | Labor Code § 2870–2872 | Must attach full § 2870 text as exhibit (required by § 2872) [VERIFY] |
| Delaware | 19 Del. C. § 805 [VERIFY] | Similar to CA scope |
| Illinois | 765 ILCS 1060/2 [VERIFY] | Similar to CA scope |
| Kansas | K.S.A. § 44-130 [VERIFY] | Similar to CA scope |
| Minnesota | Minn. Stat. § 181.78 [VERIFY] | Similar to CA scope |
| North Carolina | N.C. Gen. Stat. § 66-57.1 [VERIFY] | Similar to CA scope |
| Utah | Utah Code § 34-39-3 [VERIFY] | Similar to CA scope |
| Washington | RCW § 49.44.140 [VERIFY] | Similar to CA scope |

**Prior Inventions (Exhibit A):** Employee lists all pre-employment IP relating to company business excluded from assignment. If none, employee represents no such IP exists. Checkbox option: ☐ No prior inventions / ☐ See attached list.

**Invention Disclosure:** Employee must promptly disclose all Inventions in writing; company reviews and confirms in writing whether it claims ownership.

**Cooperation / Power of Attorney:**
- Employee cooperates post-termination at company expense plus reasonable compensation for time spent
- Irrevocable POA coupled with an interest, surviving death or incapacity, limited to executing IP prosecution documents for already-assigned Inventions

**Copyright / Works Made for Hire:** Works within scope of employment are WMFH under 17 U.S.C. § 101; include backup assignment for any works that do not qualify.

**Moral rights:** To the extent permitted by law, employee waives moral rights in assigned works. Note: moral rights are not waivable in certain non-US jurisdictions.

### 4. Restrictive Covenants

**Non-solicitation of employees** (generally enforceable nationwide):
- Prohibit direct/indirect solicitation, recruitment, or encouragement to leave
- Limit to employees with whom departing employee directly worked, supervised, or about whom employee obtained CI
- Duration: 12 months post-termination

**Non-solicitation of customers** (generally enforceable):
- Prohibit solicitation for competitive purposes only (not all contact)
- Define "customers" as those with material contact during final [12–24] months of employment
- Duration: 12–24 months post-termination

**Non-competition — jurisdiction status before including:**

| Jurisdiction | Status |
|---|---|
| California | Generally void (Bus. & Prof. Code § 16600; 2023 amendments reinforce prohibition) [VERIFY] |
| North Dakota | Generally prohibited |
| Oklahoma | Generally prohibited |
| Minnesota | Prohibited for agreements executed on/after Jan. 1, 2023 [VERIFY] |
| All others | Permissible if reasonable in scope, duration, and geography |

If including: duration 6–12 months; geographic scope limited to actual operating territory; activity scope tied to company's actual products/services. Include blue-pencil/reformation clause. Research whether jurisdiction requires independent consideration for post-hire covenants.

### 5. General Provisions

| Provision | Key points |
|---|---|
| Governing law | Specify state; some states void choice-of-law clauses that strip employees of home-state protections |
| Forum selection | State/federal courts in [county/state] or AAA Employment Arbitration Rules |
| Entire agreement | Supersedes prior CIAAs; carve out offer letter, equity agreements, employee handbook |
| Severability | Modify invalid provisions to minimum necessary; sever if court declines modification |
| Amendment | Written, signed by both parties |
| Assignment | Company may assign to successors; employee assignment requires written consent |
| Tolling | Covenant periods tolled during breach (verify enforceability in governing jurisdiction) |
| Survival | CI obligations, IP assignment, covenants, and cooperation survive termination |
| Counterparts | Electronic signatures and delivery acceptable |

### 6. Acknowledgments & Execution

Employee acknowledges:
- [ ] Read and fully understands the agreement
- [ ] Opportunity to consult independent counsel
- [ ] Voluntarily executed, without duress or coercion
- [ ] Restrictions are reasonable and will not prevent earning a living
- [ ] Received DTSA immunity notice
- [ ] (CA / applicable states) Received copy of Labor Code § 2870

Signature blocks: employee (signature, printed name, date) + company authorized officer (signature, printed name, title, date).

### 7. Exhibits

- **Exhibit A** — Prior Inventions Disclosure form
- **Exhibit B** — Cal. Labor Code § 2870 full text (CA agreements only, required by § 2872 [VERIFY])

## Guidelines

- **Present-tense assignment**: "Hereby assigns" vests ownership on creation; "agrees to assign" alone may require a second act to transfer title
- **DTSA notice**: Mandatory post-May 11, 2016; omission bars exemplary damages and attorney's fees under 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)
- **CA non-competes**: Void regardless of choice-of-law clause selecting another state if employee is CA-based; recent amendments (SB 699, AB 2288) reinforce and expand prohibition [VERIFY effective dates]
- **Multi-state employees**: Include carve-outs for all states where employees work; consider jurisdiction-specific addenda
- **Trade secret duration**: Statutory trade secret protection is indefinite — never cap trade secret CI obligations at a fixed post-termination term
- **WMFH scope**: Independent contractors generally cannot create WMFH except for enumerated categories under 17 U.S.C. § 101; always include backup assignment for contractor agreements
- **Consideration for existing employees**: States including Illinois, Washington, and Minnesota require independent consideration (e.g., raise, bonus, promotion) for restrictive covenants presented after hire [VERIFY current statutes]
