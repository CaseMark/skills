# Settlement Distribution Statement

Produces a final financial accounting of personal injury settlement proceeds — from gross recovery through all deductions to net client payout — suitable for client delivery, trust accounting compliance, and regulatory review.

## Prerequisites

1. **Executed Settlement Agreement** — fully signed, with gross amount and any allocation terms
2. **Fee Agreement** — contingency percentage, sliding scales, fee caps, cost-advancement provisions
3. **Litigation Cost Ledger** — every expense with date, vendor, description, amount
4. **Lien Resolution Summary** — each lienholder's original claim, negotiated amount, payment instructions
5. **Trust account confirmation** — settlement funds cleared and available
6. **Client payment instructions** — delivery method, split directions if any

## Output Structure

### Document Format

Formal letter on firm letterhead addressed to client (full legal name, current address).

### Required Sections

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| **Opening** | Acknowledge resolution, transition to financial accounting (2-3 sentences max) |
| **Distribution Table** | See template below |
| **Payment Details** | Check number/wire confirmation, delivery method, expected date |
| **Attachments** | Cost breakdown, lien satisfaction letters, fee agreement excerpts |

## Process

1. **Extract** gross amount, fee terms, all costs, and all liens from source documents
2. **Calculate** attorney fees per fee agreement (verify percentage, base amount, caps/sliding scales)
3. **Sum** all deductions; compute net to client
4. **Reconcile** — Gross = Total Deductions + Net to Client (must balance to the penny)
5. **Draft** statement using template above
6. **Attach** cost breakdown, lien satisfaction letters/releases, fee agreement excerpts
7. **Include** payment delivery details (check #, wire confirmation, dates)

## Guidelines

- **Reconciliation is mandatory** — flag any discrepancy before finalizing; every dollar must be accounted for
- **Lien completeness** — cross-check every lien from the resolution summary appears in the statement; missing a lien exposes the client to future liability
- **Fee agreement fidelity** — apply the exact percentage/rate contracted; honor all caps, sliding scales, and special provisions
- **Plain language** — avoid legal/accounting jargon the client won't understand; add brief context for each line item
- **No placeholders in final output** — all bracketed fields must be populated or flagged
- **Ethical compliance** — statement must satisfy applicable Rules of Professional Conduct re: client property (Model Rule 1.15), trust accounting, and fee transparency
- **Tax awareness** — note that the statement may be used for tax reporting; do not provide tax advice but flag any structured or allocated amounts the client should discuss with a tax professional
- **Confidentiality** — if delivered electronically, note that the document should be secured/encrypted with password communicated separately
- **Retention** — flag that this document should be archived per firm policy for the applicable statute of limitations plus any additional retention period required by professional responsibility rules
