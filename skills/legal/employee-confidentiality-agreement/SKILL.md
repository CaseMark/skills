# Employee Confidentiality and Security Agreement

Drafts an enforceable confidentiality and security agreement that protects company trade secrets and digital assets while satisfying applicable employment, trade secret, and data protection law.

## Prerequisites

1. **Governing jurisdiction** — state law drives enforceability, cure periods, and blue-pencil doctrine
2. **Employee role and access level** — determines scope of CI categories and post-employment restrictions
3. **Existing policies** — employee handbooks, IT security policies, prior agreements to incorporate or supersede
4. **Regulated industry flags** — HIPAA, GLBA, ITAR, or other sector-specific overlays if applicable
5. **Consideration context** — new hire (employment = consideration) or existing employee (identify additional consideration)

## Output Structure

### 1. Definitions

**Confidential Information** — broad foundational definition covering all non-public information in any medium, whether marked confidential or not, including:

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Technical/IP | Trade secrets, source code, algorithms, R&D, engineering designs, manufacturing processes |
| Business strategy | Business plans, pricing, margins, financial projections, M&A targets, competitive intel |
| Customer/relationship | Customer lists, preferences, contract terms, supplier/distributor relationships |
| Financial/operational | Budgets, revenue, compensation structures, performance metrics |
| Derivative works | Analyses, compilations, summaries prepared by employee using CI |

**Exceptions** (employee bears burden of proof by clear and convincing evidence):
- Already public domain through no breach by employee
- Rightfully in employee's possession pre-disclosure (written evidence required)
- Received from third party without restriction
- Independently developed without reference to CI (contemporaneous written documentation required)

> Clarify: exceptions apply to specific qualifying information only — not combinations or compilations incorporating that information with other CI.

---

### 2. Confidentiality Obligations

- **Non-disclosure**: No disclosure without written authorization from an authorized officer; applies during employment and post-termination
- **Survival**: Trade secrets → indefinite (per applicable UTSA/DTSA); other CI → [3–5 years] post-termination (calibrate to jurisdiction)
- **Limited use**: CI used solely to perform assigned duties; no personal benefit or third-party benefit
- **Standard of care**: Same care as employee's own confidential information; never less than reasonable standard
- **Need-to-know**: Limit access to employees bound by equivalent obligations
- **Secure storage**: Encryption for electronic CI; locked storage for physical; secure disposal
- **Prompt notification**: Report unauthorized disclosure, loss, or suspected compromise immediately to [Security Officer / Legal]

**Compelled disclosure protocol**:
- Immediately notify Legal upon receipt of subpoena or court order
- Cooperate with protective order efforts before disclosing
- Disclose only what counsel advises is legally required after company has opportunity to seek protection

**DTSA Whistleblower Notice** *(required by 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b))*:
> An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any Federal or State trade secret law for disclosure of a trade secret made in confidence to a government official or attorney solely for reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law, or in a court filing under seal.

**NLRA Carveout**: Nothing in this agreement prohibits employee from discussing wages, hours, or working conditions with other employees or engaging in other NLRA-protected concerted activity.

---

### 3. Security Responsibilities

**Password & Access Controls**
- Unique, strong passwords for each company system (minimum 12 characters; upper/lower/numbers/special chars)
- No sharing, reuse across systems, or storage in unencrypted files
- Enable MFA on all systems where available
- Lock/log out of unattended workstations
- Change passwords immediately upon suspected compromise
- Report suspicious access attempts promptly

**Acceptable Use**
- Company systems = business purposes; limited incidental personal use permitted if it does not interfere with work, consume significant resources, or create legal/security risk
- **Prohibited**: Unauthorized software installation; circumventing security controls; connecting unauthorized devices; storing/transmitting malicious code; pirated content; operating outside business; discriminatory/harassing communications
- **Social media**: Personal accounts identifying company affiliation require disclaimer that views are personal; no CI disclosure
- **BYOD/Remote**: Company-approved MDM required; remote wipe consent for company data; approved VPN only; no public network access without VPN

**Monitoring Acknowledgment**: Employee has no expectation of privacy in any information stored on or transmitted through company systems; company may monitor without prior notice.

**Security Incident Reporting**

*Reportable events include*: Data breach, unauthorized access, malware, phishing, lost/stolen devices, inadvertent CI disclosure, unusual system behavior, physical security breaches.

*Obligations upon discovery*:
1. Report to IT Security and direct supervisor within [2–4] hours
2. Preserve all evidence; take no action that could compromise investigation
3. Provide complete information; cooperate fully with response team
4. Maintain confidentiality of incident and investigation

*Non-retaliation*: Company will not retaliate for good-faith incident reporting, even if incident resulted from employee error; prompt reporting is a mitigating factor.

---

### 4. Termination Obligations

**Return of Property** (upon termination or earlier request):
- All company-issued equipment (devices, tokens, keys, access cards)
- All physical documents, notes, files containing CI
- All electronic copies from personal devices, cloud storage, personal email
- Written certification confirming return/deletion before final compensation release

**Company Rights**: Remote wipe of MDM-enrolled personal devices; right to inspect company-issued devices; failure to return = conversion, subject to injunctive relief and damages.

**Post-Employment Restrictions**:
- Ongoing confidentiality obligations as stated in §2
- **Employee non-solicitation**: [12–24 months] — no direct/indirect recruitment of company employees
- **Customer non-solicitation**: [12–24 months] — no solicitation of customers with whom employee had material contact or about whom employee obtained CI during final [12–24 months] of employment
- Restrictions are limited to active solicitation; do not prohibit employment with competitor or response to unsolicited inquiries

**New Employer Notice**: Employee must inform prospective employer of existence of confidentiality obligations and provide written notice to company of new employment (employer name + general duties).

### 5. Legal Framework

| Provision | Standard Language |
|---|---|
| Governing law | Laws of [State]; exclusive jurisdiction in [County, State] courts |
| Equitable relief | Breach causes irreparable harm not compensable by damages; company entitled to injunction without bond |
| Attorneys' fees | Prevailing party recovers fees, costs, and investigation expenses |
| Severability | Invalid provisions reformed to minimum extent necessary; blue-pencil authorized; remainder survives |
| Integration | Supersedes prior oral/written agreements on subject matter; amendments require written officer signature |
| Assignment | Company may assign to successor; employee may not assign |
| Counterparts/E-signatures | Electronic signatures have same force as originals |

---

### 6. Employee Acknowledgments

Employee must expressly acknowledge:
- [ ] Read and understood entire agreement; had opportunity to consult counsel
- [ ] Voluntary execution; no duress or coercion
- [ ] Will access valuable CI that company could not share without these protections
- [ ] Restrictions are reasonable in scope, duration, and geography
- [ ] Violations may result in termination, civil liability, injunctive relief, and potentially criminal prosecution
- [ ] DTSA whistleblower rights and NLRA protections are not waived
- [ ] Adequate consideration received (specify if post-hire)
- [ ] Received copy of executed agreement

## Guidelines

- **Jurisdiction-first**: Verify state enforceability of non-solicitation provisions; some states (CA, ND, MN) broadly restrict; others apply reasonableness tests
- **Consideration**: For existing employees, identify specific additional consideration beyond continued employment in states requiring it
- **Trade secret vs. CI**: Draft duration tiers explicitly — indefinite for statutory trade secrets; fixed term for other CI
- **DTSA notice**: Required for enforcement of exemplary damages and attorney fee provisions (18 U.S.C. § 1836) [VERIFY current citation]
- **NLRA compliance**: Wage/working condition discussions must be explicitly carved out; overly broad CI definitions can violate NLRA
- **HIPAA/GLBA/ITAR**: Add sector-specific exhibits or cross-references if employee has access to regulated data
- **FTC Non-Compete Rule**: Monitor enforceability developments affecting related restrictive covenants [VERIFY current status as of drafting date]
- **Regulated industries**: Healthcare, finance, and defense employees may require additional compliance-specific provisions
