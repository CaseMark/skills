# Post-Judgment Asset & Enforcement Report

Produces a strategic enforcement roadmap for collecting on a court-ordered judgment by synthesizing asset investigations, exemption analysis, and collection results into prioritized recommendations.

## Prerequisites

1. **Judgment documents** — full text of judgment order, amount (principal, pre/post-judgment interest, costs, fees), case caption, court, entry date
2. **Post-judgment discovery** — debtor examination transcripts, interrogatory responses, document production, third-party subpoena returns
3. **Public records results** — property records, business entity filings, UCC searches, vehicle registrations, professional licenses
4. **Enforcement history** — writs of execution, garnishment orders, levy results, lien recordings, contempt proceedings
5. **Correspondence** — demand letters, settlement communications, debtor/counsel correspondence

### 5. Fraudulent Transfer Assessment

Analyze under UVTA (Uniform Voidable Transactions Act):

**Checklist — Badges of Fraud**
- [ ] Transfer to insider/family member
- [ ] Debtor retained possession/control after transfer
- [ ] Transfer concealed
- [ ] Transfer after suit filed or judgment entered
- [ ] Substantially all assets transferred
- [ ] Less than reasonably equivalent value received
- [ ] Debtor insolvent at time of transfer or rendered insolvent
- [ ] Transfer of business assets followed by new entity doing same business

**Limitations**: Actual fraud — typically 4 years; constructive fraud — typically 1 year under UVTA [VERIFY state-specific periods]

**Cost-benefit**: Value of transferred assets vs. litigation cost, strength of evidence, transferee solvency.

### 6. Jurisdictional Issues

If debtor relocated or holds assets in multiple states:
- Domestication requirements under Uniform Enforcement of Foreign Judgments Act (filing, notice, challenge period)
- Compare exemption laws between rendering state and enforcement state
- Flag strategic relocation to exploit more protective exemptions

|---|---|---|
| **Solvent** | Substantial non-exempt assets identified | Aggressive multi-front enforcement |
| **Marginally collectible** | Limited accessible assets | Selective cost-effective remedies |
| **Judgment-proof** | No recoverable assets beyond exemptions | Settlement, payment plan, or judgment sale |

- Continuing wage garnishment
- Judgment lien recording in all relevant counties
- Fraudulent transfer complaint (if within limitations)
- Turnover order for specific non-exempt assets

## Guidelines

- Calculate post-judgment interest precisely — state the rate, start date, and methodology
- Cite specific transcript pages (page:line) for debtor admissions about assets
- Redact confidential information per court protective orders and Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act requirements
- Note all investigative sources consulted (databases, subpoena targets, service dates, social media)
- Provide FMV support for significant assets (comparables, appraisals, tax assessments, valuation multiples)
- Flag any pending appeals, stays of execution, or bankruptcy filings that affect enforcement
- Be candid about collectibility — do not overstate recovery prospects
- All statutory citations must reference the specific state; use [VERIFY] for uncertain citations
- This skill assumes U.S. jurisdiction; state-specific rules vary significantly for exemptions, garnishment limits, and fraudulent transfer periods
