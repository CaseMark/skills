# Regulatory Change Impact Analysis

Evaluates how a new or amended regulation affects existing agreements, internal policies, and business operations, then produces a prioritized remediation plan.

## Prerequisites

- Full text of the new or amended regulation
- Inventory of affected contracts, policies, and procedures
- Current compliance posture documentation
- Effective date and transition period details

## Workflow

### 1. Parse Regulatory Requirements

Break the regulation into discrete requirements. For each: identify who it applies to, what conduct it mandates or prohibits, the effective date, and penalties for non-compliance.

### 2. Map to Existing Obligations

Cross-reference each requirement against current contracts, policies, and procedures. Identify: already compliant (no action), partially compliant (amendment needed), and non-compliant (new obligation).

### 3. Assess Business Impact

For each gap, evaluate: number of affected contracts, operational changes required, cost of remediation, and risk of non-compliance (financial penalties, enforcement likelihood, reputational exposure).

### 4. Build Remediation Timeline

Sequence required changes against the regulatory effective date. Account for: contract amendment negotiation time, internal policy approval processes, system changes, and training requirements.

### 5. Draft Impact Memo

Produce an executive memo with: regulation summary, total affected agreements and policies, gap analysis matrix, prioritized remediation plan with owners and deadlines, and budget estimate.

## Pitfalls

- Analyzing only new contracts while overlooking existing agreements that need amendment
- Underestimating the time required for counterparty consent to contract amendments
- Treating the effective date as the action deadline instead of the completion deadline
