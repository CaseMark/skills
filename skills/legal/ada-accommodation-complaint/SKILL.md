# ADA Failure to Accommodate Complaint

Drafts a litigation-ready complaint alleging ADA failure to accommodate under Title I (employment) or Title III (public accommodations) for federal or state court filing.

## Prerequisites

1. **Plaintiff info** — full legal name, state of residence
2. **Disability documentation** — diagnosis, functional limitations, treating provider name
3. **Accommodation request record** — exact date, form (oral/written), recipient name and title, specific accommodation sought, supporting medical documentation submitted to defendant
4. **Defendant response** — denial letter, non-response timeline, or interactive process failure evidence
5. **Employment records** (Title I) — job description, hire date, performance reviews, adverse action date and stated reason
6. **EEOC charge** (Title I) — charge number, filing date, Right to Sue notice date, and plaintiff's receipt date
7. **Damages evidence** — pay stubs, W-2s, benefits records, therapy/treatment records for emotional distress

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption
- Court name and division
- Plaintiff identified as "an individual with a disability"; Defendant by full corporate/legal name
- Title: `COMPLAINT FOR FAILURE TO PROVIDE REASONABLE ACCOMMODATION UNDER THE AMERICANS WITH DISABILITIES ACT`
- Case number placeholder

### 2. Nature of Action (2–4 sentences)
State claim under Title I **or** Title III, then summarize: plaintiff's disability and functional limitation → specific accommodation requested → defendant's refusal or interactive process failure → resulting harm.

### 3. Parties

| Party | Required Allegations |
|-------|----------------------|
| Plaintiff | Name; state of residence; disability status under 42 U.S.C. § 12102 (impairment / record of / regarded as); employment or access relationship with defendant |
| Defendant (Title I) | Full legal name; business form; principal place of business; **15+ employees for 20+ weeks** in current or preceding calendar year (coverage threshold) |
| Defendant (Title III) | Full legal name; owns/leases/operates a place of public accommodation; category under 42 U.S.C. § 12181(7) |

### 4. Jurisdiction and Venue

| Issue | Citation |
|-------|----------|
| Federal question | 28 U.S.C. § 1331 |
| Title I (employment) | 42 U.S.C. § 12117(a) (incorporates Title VII procedures) |
| Title III (public accommodations) | 42 U.S.C. § 12188 |
| Supplemental state claims | 28 U.S.C. § 1367(a) |
| Venue | 28 U.S.C. § 1391(b) — substantial events occurred in this district |

**EEOC Exhaustion (Title I — mandatory):**
- Charge timely filed within **300 days** (deferral state) or **180 days** (non-deferral state)
- Right to Sue issued on [date]; complaint filed within **90 days** of receipt per 42 U.S.C. § 2000e-5(f)(1)
- Attach Right to Sue letter as Exhibit A

### 5. Factual Allegations (Numbered Paragraphs, Chronological)

1. **Disability** — impairment type; how it substantially limits a major life activity (walking, lifting, concentrating, working, caring for oneself, etc. per ADAAA); medical documentation provided to defendant
2. **Qualification** — hire date, position title, essential functions per job description, performance history establishing "otherwise qualified" status
3. **Accommodation request** — exact date, form, recipient, verbatim or paraphrased content; medical documentation submitted
4. **Interactive process failure** — allege with specificity: no meeting initiated, no questions asked about limitations, no alternatives proposed, no good-faith engagement
5. **Denial and rebuttal** — defendant's stated reason(s) and factual rebuttal for each:
   - *Undue hardship* → low cost of accommodation vs. defendant's resources; tax credits available; minimal operational impact (factors: 42 U.S.C. § 12111(10)(B))
   - *Not qualified* → plaintiff's performance record; prior successful performance with informal accommodation
   - *Fundamental alteration* → nature of requested modification and why it does not alter core service/function
6. **Adverse action** — date; type (termination, demotion, denial of access, constructive discharge); stated reason
7. **Damages** — lost wages from adverse action date to present; lost benefits (health insurance, retirement); out-of-pocket costs; emotional distress with treatment records; reputational/career harm
8. **Malice/reckless indifference** (if punitive damages sought) — pattern of disability discrimination; ignored HR or legal counsel advice; statements evidencing discriminatory animus

### 6. Causes of Action

**Count I — Failure to Accommodate (Title I), 42 U.S.C. § 12112(a), (b)(5)(A)**

- [ ] Plaintiff is an individual with a disability (§ 12102)
- [ ] Plaintiff is otherwise qualified with or without reasonable accommodation
- [ ] Defendant had actual notice of disability and accommodation need
- [ ] Plaintiff requested a specific reasonable accommodation
- [ ] Defendant failed to provide accommodation or engage in good-faith interactive process
- [ ] Plaintiff suffered damages as a proximate result

**Count I (alt) — Denial of Equal Enjoyment (Title III), 42 U.S.C. § 12182(a), (b)(2)(A)(ii)**

- [ ] Plaintiff is an individual with a disability
- [ ] Defendant owns/leases/operates a place of public accommodation
- [ ] Plaintiff requested reasonable modification to defendant's policies, practices, or procedures
- [ ] Modification would not fundamentally alter defendant's goods or services
- [ ] Defendant refused modification, denying plaintiff full and equal access

**Count II — Retaliation (if applicable), 42 U.S.C. § 12203(a)**

- [ ] Protected activity (accommodation request or opposition to discrimination)
- [ ] Adverse action by defendant
- [ ] Causal nexus between protected activity and adverse action

**Count III — State disability discrimination** (supplemental; cite applicable state statute)

### 7. Prayer for Relief

**Title I:**
- Reinstatement with full seniority and benefits, or front pay in lieu
- Back pay from adverse action date through judgment with prejudgment interest
- Compensatory damages (emotional distress, mental anguish, loss of enjoyment of life)
- Punitive damages if malice or reckless indifference to federally protected rights shown
- Attorney's fees, litigation expenses, and costs (42 U.S.C. § 12205)
- Pre- and post-judgment interest; such other relief as the Court deems just

**Title III** (injunctive-focused; compensatory damages limited under federal law):
- Permanent injunction requiring reasonable modification and ongoing ADA policy compliance
- Declaratory judgment that defendant's policies violate the ADA
- Attorney's fees and costs (42 U.S.C. § 12205)

> Per FRCP 8(a)(3), omit specific dollar amounts in federal court filings.

### 8. Jury Demand
> "Plaintiff demands a trial by jury on all issues so triable." (FRCP 38(b))
> *Title III claims are equitable — no jury right; limit or omit jury demand accordingly.*

### 9. Signature Block
Per FRCP 11: date, attorney signature, printed name, bar number, state of admission, firm name, address, phone, email — designated "Attorney for Plaintiff." Include client verification under penalty of perjury if required by local rule.

## Guidelines

- **ADAAA (2008)**: Courts must construe "substantially limits" broadly; do not concede a narrow reading of the disability definition
- **Interactive process**: Defendant's failure to engage — even if plaintiff's preferred accommodation is unreasonable — creates independent liability; plead this separately [VERIFY: circuit split on whether failure alone is actionable]
- **Damages cap (Title I)**: Compensatory + punitive capped at $50K–$300K by employer size per 42 U.S.C. § 1981a(b)(3); cap does not apply to back pay
- **State law**: FEHA, NYSHRL, and many state analogs provide broader coverage and uncapped damages — always plead supplemental state claims
- **Twombly/Iqbal**: Each element must be supported by specific factual allegations raising plausibility above speculation; avoid conclusory recitations
- **Medical privacy**: Include only the detail necessary to establish substantial limitation; avoid gratuitous disclosure of medical history
- **Title I only**: Verify EEOC charge was dual-filed with applicable state agency in deferral states to preserve state law claims
