# Complaint for Breach of Contract

Drafts a plaintiff-side breach of contract complaint structured around the four elements of the claim with jurisdiction-appropriate procedural compliance, ready for supervising attorney review before filing.

## Prerequisites

1. **Governing contract(s)** — executed agreement with all amendments, exhibits, and attachments
2. **Correspondence** — emails, letters, and cure/notice communications in chronological order
3. **Performance evidence** — invoices, delivery records, payment confirmations, or work product showing plaintiff's performance
4. **Breach evidence** — documentation of defendant's failure, any cure notices sent, and defendant's response or non-response
5. **Damage calculation** — itemized losses with supporting documentation (lost profits, cover costs, consequential harm)
6. **Filing court** — identified jurisdiction and division to apply correct caption format and local rules

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption
- Full court name including division or department
- Pre-assigned case number (if any)
- Complete legal names and capacities of all parties (plaintiff(s) v. defendant(s))
- Follow jurisdiction-specific formatting — federal district courts and state courts have distinct conventions; verify local rules

### 2. Introduction, Jurisdiction & Venue

| Element | Federal Court | State Court |
|---|---|---|
| Subject matter | Diversity: 28 U.S.C. § 1332 (complete diversity + AIC > $75,000); or federal question: 28 U.S.C. § 1331 | State statutory or constitutional general jurisdiction grant |
| Venue | 28 U.S.C. § 1391: defendant's residence, where contract to be performed, or where breach occurred | Defendant's residence, place of business, or contract performance/breach location |

State the amount in controversy when jurisdictionally required.

### 3. Party Identification

**Individuals**: Full legal name, address, contractual role (buyer/seller/service provider/borrower)

**Entities**: Exact legal form (Corp./LLC/LP/GP), state of organization, principal place of business, registered agent — capture all details needed for diversity jurisdiction analysis

**Representative parties**: State capacity (executor/trustee/guardian/receiver) and source of authority

### 4. Factual Background

Draft a chronological, fact-based narrative covering:
- **Contract formation**: date, place of execution, consideration exchanged, essential terms agreed upon
- **Plaintiff's performance**: specific dates, dollar amounts, deliverables, services rendered
- **Defendant's breach**: which obligation(s) were not performed, date of breach, whether notice and opportunity to cure were provided
- **Defendant's response**: acknowledgment, dispute, or failure to respond
- **Resulting harm**: causal link from breach to damages

Use concrete facts drawn from uploaded documents — dates, amounts, quoted contract language, and specific communications.

### 5. Cause of Action — Breach of Contract

Allege each element with dedicated factual paragraphs:

1. **Valid and enforceable contract** — identify parties, subject matter, consideration, and mutual assent; address any statute of frauds compliance if applicable
2. **Plaintiff's performance** — all conditions precedent satisfied, or excused/waived by defendant's conduct
3. **Defendant's material breach** — specify which contract provisions were violated, the manner of breach, and the date
4. **Resulting damages** — direct causal link between breach and quantified harm

Incorporate contract-specific provisions: notice requirements, cure periods, liquidated damages clauses, and attorney's fees provisions. [VERIFY jurisdiction-specific element variations — some states impose additional pleading requirements beyond the standard four elements]

### 6. Prayer for Relief

- [ ] Compensatory damages (itemized where calculable)
- [ ] Consequential/special damages (allege foreseeability at time of contracting)
- [ ] Specific performance or injunctive relief (where applicable and legally available)
- [ ] Pre-judgment interest (statutory or contractual rate — cite applicable statute)
- [ ] Post-judgment interest
- [ ] Costs of suit
- [ ] Attorney's fees (if authorized by contract or applicable statute)
- [ ] Punitive damages (rare in contract claims; include only if jurisdiction permits and facts support fraud or bad faith)
- [ ] General relief: "such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper"

### 7. Procedural Requirements

- **Jury demand**: Include in complaint body if jurisdiction requires it; some courts permit a separate filing — verify local rule deadline
- **Attorney certification**: Rule 11 (federal) or state equivalent — good faith factual and legal basis required for all allegations
- **Plaintiff verification**: Some state courts require a sworn verification — check local rules before filing
- **Signature block**: Attorney name, bar number, firm name, address, telephone, and email

## Guidelines

- Number all paragraphs consecutively for easy cross-reference in motions and briefs
- Maintain factual, objective tone — no inflammatory language or excessive rhetoric
- All factual allegations must be supported by documents in counsel's possession at time of filing
- Anticipate likely affirmative defenses (statute of frauds, waiver, impossibility, failure of consideration, accord and satisfaction) and ensure factual narrative is sufficient to rebut them
- Do not include legal argument in the factual background section — reserve for briefs
- Verify page limits, margin, font, and spacing requirements under applicable local rules before finalizing
