# Lease Guaranty

Drafts an unconditional guaranty of payment and performance for commercial lease obligations, maximizing landlord protection while ensuring enforceability.

## Prerequisites

1. **Underlying lease agreement** — execution date, premises description, rent amounts, payment terms, duration, renewal options, special provisions (percentage rent, CAM, TI allowances)
2. **Party identification** — full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions of formation, principal addresses for landlord, tenant, and guarantor
3. **Guarantor financial information** — financial statements, credit information, net worth data (if available)
4. **Jurisdiction requirements** — notarization rules, anti-deficiency statutes, one-action rules, community property considerations
5. **Scope instructions** — full term vs. initial term only, cap on liability (if any), multiple guarantors (joint and several)

## Output Structure

### Document Framework

| Section | Key Content |
|---|---|
| Title | "Unconditional Guaranty of Lease Obligations" or "Continuing Guaranty of Payment and Performance" |
| Preamble | All three parties with exact legal names matching lease; guarantor identifiers for enforcement |
| Recitals | Lease reference (date, premises, term, rent); landlord's rationale; guarantor's relationship to tenant; inducement language |
| Core Guaranty | Unconditional, irrevocable guarantee of all payment and performance obligations |
| Absolute Nature | Survives modifications, bankruptcy, forbearance, collateral changes, assignments |
| Waivers | Suretyship defenses, subrogation, marshaling, notice requirements |
| Representations | Capacity, authority, financial adequacy, independent investigation |
| Duration & Survival | Continuing guaranty; treatment of renewals/extensions; post-termination obligations |
| Remedies | Default triggers, enforcement rights, interest rate, application of payments |
| Governing Law | Jurisdiction, venue consent, service of process, jury trial waiver |
| Execution | Signature blocks, entity authority, notarization, spousal consent if needed |
| Boilerplate | Severability, integration, amendment, counterparts, e-signatures |

### Core Guaranty Obligations Checklist

Draft must explicitly guarantee each of the following:

- [ ] Base rent, additional rent, percentage rent
- [ ] Tax payments, insurance premiums, CAM charges, utilities
- [ ] All other monetary obligations under the lease
- [ ] Maintenance and repair duties
- [ ] Compliance with laws and regulations
- [ ] Insurance procurement and maintenance
- [ ] Indemnification obligations
- [ ] Restoration requirements upon termination
- [ ] Enforcement costs: attorneys' fees, court costs, collection fees, expert witnesses

### Critical Operative Language

**Payment vs. Collection distinction** — State explicitly:
> "This is an absolute and unconditional guaranty of payment and performance and not of collection. Landlord shall not be required to proceed first against Tenant, exhaust any security deposit, pursue any other remedies, or take any action whatsoever against Tenant before enforcing this Guaranty against Guarantor."

**Primary liability** — Guarantor's liability is primary, direct, and immediate. Landlord may proceed against guarantor and tenant simultaneously, separately, or in any order.

**Multiple guarantors** — If applicable, include joint and several liability language.

### Waiver Provisions (enumerate each separately)

1. All suretyship defenses (common law and statutory)
2. Right to require landlord to proceed against tenant first
3. Right to require landlord to mitigate damages
4. Presentment, demand, protest, notice of dishonor
5. Notice of acceptance of guaranty
6. Notice of default under the lease
7. Subrogation, reimbursement, indemnification, and contribution rights (postponed until all obligations indefeasibly satisfied)
8. Right to participate in landlord's security
9. Right to require marshaling of assets

### Duration Options

| Scenario | Language Approach |
|---|---|
| Full term + renewals | Guaranty automatically continues through extensions, renewals, holdovers without further consent |
| Initial term only | Terminates at initial term expiration; guarantor remains liable for accrued obligations and surviving duties |
| Burn-off / declining | Specify reduction schedule tied to tenant performance milestones |

### Survival Provisions

Guarantor remains liable post-termination for:
- All obligations accrued prior to termination
- Indemnification obligations
- Environmental remediation costs
- Restoration and repair obligations
- Damages from tenant's breach

### Default and Remedies

| Default Trigger | Cure Period |
|---|---|
| Failure to pay after written demand | 5 days |
| Failure to perform non-monetary obligation | 10 days after notice |
| Material misrepresentation | None |
| Bankruptcy/insolvency of guarantor | None |

**Interest on past-due amounts**: Lesser of 18% per annum or maximum rate permitted by applicable law.

**Remedies are cumulative** — exercise of one does not preclude others. Landlord has sole discretion over application of payments.

### Execution Formalities

- [ ] Signature blocks with printed name, signature, title, date
- [ ] Entity authority representation if guarantor is not an individual
- [ ] Notarization/acknowledgment per jurisdiction requirements (many states require for real estate guaranties)
- [ ] Spousal consent/joinder in community property states (AZ, CA, ID, LA, NV, NM, TX, WA, WI) if guarantor is married individual
- [ ] Jury trial waiver in ALL CAPS or bold per jurisdictional conspicuousness requirements
- [ ] Counterparts and e-signature provisions per applicable law

## Guidelines

- **Guaranty of payment, not collection** — This distinction is critical and must be stated explicitly
- **Match all names exactly** to the underlying lease; inconsistencies create enforcement risk
- **Confession of judgment** clauses are prohibited or unenforceable in many states — only include after confirming jurisdiction permits them [VERIFY specific state]
- **Anti-deficiency statutes** in some states (e.g., CA CCP §580d) may limit guarantor liability on certain obligations [VERIFY applicability]
- **One-action rules** (e.g., CA CCP §726) may affect enforcement sequence [VERIFY applicability]
- **Bankruptcy waiver** of tenant's discharge is enforceable against guarantor — guarantor's own bankruptcy discharge of the guaranty obligation is governed by 11 U.S.C. §523/§524
- All monetary amounts should appear both numerically and in words where appropriate
- Defined terms must be used consistently throughout; cross-references must be verified
- If lease contains arbitration clause, explicitly address whether guaranty disputes are subject to arbitration
- Financial disclosure covenants: require statements within 30 days of execution and annually thereafter if landlord needs ongoing monitoring
