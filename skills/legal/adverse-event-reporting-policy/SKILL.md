# Adverse Event Reporting Policy

Drafts a legally binding AE reporting policy meeting FDA requirements and ICH standards for pharma, biotech, CROs, and healthcare organizations conducting or sponsoring clinical research.

## Prerequisites

1. **Organization type** — pharmaceutical sponsor, CRO, healthcare system, academic medical center, or combination
2. **Product portfolio** — IND products (Phase I–IV), approved drugs, biologics, devices, combination products, REMS-covered products
3. **Geographic footprint** — domestic only vs. multi-jurisdictional (EU, Japan, Canada, etc.)
4. **Therapeutic areas** — flag specialized populations: oncology, vaccines, biologics, pediatrics
5. **Existing SOPs** — any current pharmacovigilance SOPs, IRB agreements, DSMB charters to cross-reference

## Output Structure

### 1. Introduction & Compliance Statement

- Cite controlling regulations:
  - **21 CFR 312.32** — IND safety reporting
  - **21 CFR 314.80** — Postmarketing adverse drug experience
  - **ICH E2A** — Clinical safety data management
  - **21 CFR 803** — Medical device reporting (if applicable)
  - Applicable state and international requirements
- Effective date, review cycle (annual minimum), approval authority
- Binding compliance statement: policy adherence is a condition of employment; violations may constitute federal law violations

---

### 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Adverse Event (AE)** | Any untoward medical occurrence in a patient/subject administered a product; causal relationship need not be established |
| **Serious Adverse Event (SAE)** | Event meeting ≥1 of 6 FDA/ICH criteria (see below) |
| **Unexpected AE** | Not identified in nature, severity, or frequency in current IB, package insert, or ICH; assessed against reference safety information current at time of event |
| **Suspected Adverse Reaction** | Reasonable possibility of causal relationship between product and event |
| **Causality Assessment** | Systematic evaluation using validated algorithm (Naranjo, WHO-UMC) |
| **Sponsor Awareness** | When *any* sponsor employee first receives AE information — starts all reporting clocks |
| **Expedited Report** | 7-day (fatal/life-threatening) or 15-day (other serious) IND safety report |

**SAE Seriousness Criteria (6 FDA/ICH):**
1. Death
2. Life-threatening (immediate risk of death at time of event)
3. Inpatient hospitalization or prolongation
4. Persistent/significant disability or incapacity
5. Congenital anomaly/birth defect
6. Important medical event requiring intervention to prevent serious outcome

### 3. Scope

**Covered activities:**
- Phase I–IV clinical trials; post-marketing surveillance; expanded access/compassionate use; investigator-initiated studies

**Covered products:**
- IND products, approved drugs, biologics, vaccines, gene therapies, medical devices (IDE), combination products, REMS-covered products

**Geographic scope:**
- Specify domestic-only vs. global; address how international events feed FDA reporting; handle countries where product is unapproved

**Temporal boundaries:**
- Begins: informed consent signature or first dose
- Ends: per protocol follow-up period (specify days post-treatment; address long half-life/delayed-effect products)

**Exclusions (with redirect):**

| Excluded item | Redirect to |
|--------------|-------------|
| Product quality complaints (no patient impact) | Quality Assurance SOP |
| Occupational exposures without health effects | Occupational Health |
| Near-miss medication errors | Medication Safety Program |
| Competitor product AEs in comparator arms | Protocol-specific requirements |

### 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Key Obligations | Timeline |
|------|----------------|----------|
| **Safety Officer / PV Director** | Final reportability, seriousness, causality, expectedness determinations; FDA liaison | Review within 4 hrs of receipt; reportability determination within 8 hrs |
| **Principal Investigator** | Evaluate each AE within 24 hrs; causality/seriousness determination; IRB notification | Report to Safety Officer within 24 hrs of awareness |
| **Clinical Research Coordinator** | Active surveillance (structured interviews, lab review, vital signs); source documentation; escalate SAEs immediately | Escalate immediately; do not wait for scheduled visits |
| **Clinical Monitor/CRA** | Verify source docs vs. CRF; confirm timeline compliance; escalate systemic deficiencies | Document in monitoring reports; verify CAPAs at next visit |
| **Senior Management** | Resource adequacy; aggregate safety review; risk-benefit decisions | Quarterly review minimum |
| **QA** | Independent audits; CAPA oversight; report to senior management | Annual minimum audit; ad hoc for signals |

### 5. AE Identification & Assessment

**Active surveillance methods:**
- Structured patient interviews (standardized questionnaires at each contact)
- Laboratory values reviewed against protocol normal ranges and clinically significant change thresholds
- Physical examination with baseline comparison
- Concomitant medication review (may represent treatment for unreported AE)

**Passive surveillance:**
- Dedicated patient reporting line/email/portal
- External provider reporting pathway
- EHR alert integration (hospitalizations, ED visits, critical labs) where feasible

**Causality Assessment — Required Documentation:**

| Factor | What to document |
|--------|-----------------|
| Temporal relationship | Time from last dose to onset |
| Biological plausibility | Known pharmacology/class effects |
| Dechallenge | Symptom change upon discontinuation |
| Rechallenge | Symptom recurrence upon restart |
| Alternative explanations | Disease progression, comedications, other factors |
| Prior literature/experience | Published reports, IB data |

Use validated tool (Naranjo Scale or WHO-UMC categories). Document specific algorithm applied and narrative rationale — not just final conclusion.

**Severity grading:** CTCAE (or protocol-specified scale); document grade and supporting clinical findings.

**Special populations requiring enhanced monitoring:**
- Pediatric subjects (developmental considerations)
- Pregnant women (maternal and fetal endpoints)
- Elderly with polypharmacy (attribution complexity)
- Immunocompromised (atypical presentations)

### 6. Reporting Timelines & Submission

**Expedited IND Safety Reports (21 CFR 312.32):**

| Event type | FDA deadline | Internal trigger |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Fatal or life-threatening SUSAR | 7 calendar days from sponsor awareness | Safety Officer notified within 4 hrs |
| Other serious SUSAR | 15 calendar days from sponsor awareness | Safety Officer notified within 4 hrs |
| Follow-up to 7-day report | 8 additional calendar days (day 15 total) | Initiate at day 7 submission |

**Annual IND Safety Reports:** Within 60 days of IND anniversary — tabular AE summaries, narrative SAE descriptions, signal analysis, updated risk-benefit assessment.

**IRB/IEC Reporting:** Same timeline as FDA or 24 hours per IRB requirements, whichever is more stringent. All SAEs regardless of causality.

**DSMB/IDMC:** Per charter; unblinded data; expedited notification for predefined stopping rules.

**Postmarketing (21 CFR 314.80):**
- 15-day alert reports for serious unexpected AEs
- Periodic Adverse Drug Experience Reports (PADERs) per approved schedule

**Submission mechanics:**
- FDA: Electronic Submission Gateway; FDA Form 3500A; ICH E2B(R3) format
- Backup: telephone for urgent situations; paper with proof of delivery
- Retain: submission confirmations, FDA acknowledgment receipts, all subsequent correspondence

**Multi-jurisdictional overlay:**

| Agency | Key differences to address |
|--------|--------------------------|
| EMA | EudraVigilance submission; potential differences in seriousness/expectedness definitions |
| PMDA (Japan) | Local reporting timelines; Japanese labeling as reference document |
| Health Canada | MedEffect reporting; [VERIFY current timelines] |

### 7. Documentation Standards

**Source document requirements:**
- Created in real-time or within 24 hours
- Corrections: single strikethrough (original legible), correct entry, initials, date — no deletions or obliteration
- Required elements per AE record:

```
- Date/time of onset (with maximum available precision)
- Clinical description: all signs/symptoms, severity (CTCAE grade), frequency, duration, anatomical location
- CTCAE grade with supporting findings
- Causality assessment: algorithm used, each factor considered, narrative rationale, final determination
- Seriousness determination: specific criterion/criteria met
- Expectedness determination: IB/labeling section consulted
- Actions: dose modifications (specify doses), discontinuation date, concomitant treatments, procedures
- Hospitalizations: dates, facility
- Outcome: recovered/resolved | recovering/resolving | not recovered | recovered with sequelae | fatal
- Regulatory submission: date, submission number, FDA acknowledgment
```

**Record Retention:**

| Record type | Retention period |
|-------------|----------------|
| Clinical trial AE records | 2 years post-NDA/BLA approval; or 2 years after IND discontinuation notified to FDA |
| Postmarketing AE reports | 10 years from creation; or 2 years after product no longer marketed — whichever is longer |
| Training records | Duration of employment + 3 years |

**Storage:** Access-controlled; audit trail with user ID and timestamps; geographically separate backups; validated electronic systems (21 CFR Part 11 where applicable).

### 8. Training & Competency

**Initial training (required before assuming AE responsibilities):**
- Regulatory framework (21 CFR 312.32, 314.80, ICH E2A)
- Organizational policy and reporting workflows
- Event identification (active and passive surveillance)
- Causality assessment with case scenario exercises
- Documentation standards
- Reporting timelines and consequences of missed deadlines

**Annual refresher:** Regulatory updates, audit lessons learned (anonymized), process revisions.

**Role-specific advanced training:**

| Role | Advanced content |
|------|----------------|
| Medical monitors/safety physicians | Advanced causality in polypharmacy/comorbidity scenarios; dechallenge/rechallenge interpretation |
| Regulatory/safety coordinators | E2B(R3) submission mechanics; FDA gateway; Form 3500A completion |
| Regulatory writers | FDA narrative standards; MedDRA coding; QC before submission |

**Competency assessment:** Written exam (minimum passing score required); practical case scenario evaluation; supervised performance period before independent authorization.

**Annual certification:** Written attestation of policy awareness, training completion, and compliance commitment. Failure = immediate suspension of research privileges.

---

### 9. Quality Assurance & Continuous Improvement

**Audit program (minimum annually; risk-based frequency):**

Audit scope:
- Timeline compliance: site awareness → Safety Officer → FDA submission
- Documentation completeness and causality rationale adequacy
- Causality consistency (independent medical review of sample)
- Submission accuracy vs. source documents

**KPIs (reviewed quarterly by senior management):**

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| 7-day reports submitted on time | 100% |
| 15-day reports submitted on time | 100% |
| Time from site awareness to Safety Officer notification | < 4 business hours |
| Documentation deficiency rate | Track trend; target < [org-defined threshold] |
| CAPA completion on schedule | ≥ 95% |

**Root cause analysis:** Required for all timeline failures, missed reports, and quality deficiencies. Must address systemic causes (training gaps, resource constraints, process design), not just immediate cause. CAPA with assigned owner and target date.

**Quarterly safety review meetings:** Aggregate signal detection using disproportionality analysis (PRR, BCPNN [VERIFY methodology applicability]); clinical review of event clusters; regulatory affairs assessment of notification obligations.

**Protocol amendments / informed consent updates:** Required when safety data identifies new risks material to participant decision-making; re-consent active participants; amend forms for future enrollment.

---

### 10. Enforcement & Non-Retaliation

- Violations subject to progressive discipline up to termination; knowing/willful failures may constitute federal law violations (false statements, obstruction of oversight)
- Organization may be required to identify individuals in regulatory reports to FDA
- **Non-retaliation:** No adverse action for good-faith AE reporting or safety concern escalation; over-reporting is preferred to under-reporting
- Reporting pathways for compliance concerns: compliance officer, hotline (anonymous option required), legal counsel
- Manager accountability: performance evaluations include AE compliance metrics for direct reports

---

## Guidelines

- **Sponsor awareness starts the clock** — train all staff, not just safety personnel; any employee awareness triggers timelines
- **Reference safety information is event-specific** — expectedness assessed against IB/labeling current at time of event; document specific version consulted
- **Causality is medical judgment** — document factors, not just conclusion; reasonable disagreement is acceptable, undocumented determination is not
- **Over-report, then correct** — missed 7/15-day deadline is a regulatory violation; a submitted report later determined non-reportable is not
- **Multi-jurisdictional conflicts** — where international requirements exceed FDA, apply the higher standard; document conflict resolution rationale
- **REMS products** — assess whether AE data triggers REMS assessment report obligations under 21 CFR 314.520 [VERIFY current cite]
- **Investigator-initiated trials** — establish contractual AE reporting obligations and communication protocols with external PIs before trial start
- **Combination products** — coordinate drug and device reporting pathways; 21 CFR 803 obligations may run concurrently
