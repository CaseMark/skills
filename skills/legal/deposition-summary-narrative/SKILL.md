# Deposition Summary — Narrative

Synthesizes a deposition transcript into a strategic narrative memorandum with citations, flagged admissions, and credibility analysis.

## Prerequisites

1. **Deposition transcript** — full text, paginated with line numbers
2. **Case caption and docket number** — to populate the header
3. **Witness role** — plaintiff, defendant, fact witness, expert, or corporate representative (30(b)(6))
4. **Attendee list** — attorneys present and represented parties (preferred)

## Output Structure

### 1. Header Block

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Case caption | Full style of cause, court, docket number |
| Deponent | Full name, role in litigation |
| Deposition date & location | As stated in transcript |
| Attorneys present | Name, firm, represented party |
| Privilege notice | "Prepared at the direction of counsel. Attorney Work Product — Privileged and Confidential." |

### 2. Executive Overview (1–2 paragraphs)
- Key topics covered
- Most significant admissions or testimony
- Major credibility concerns or impeachment opportunities
- Preliminary impact on case theory

### 3. Narrative Body (thematic sections)

Organize by topic — not by Q&A order. Typical sections:

- Background & Qualifications
- Events Leading to Incident
- The Incident
- Damages & Injuries
- Medical Treatment & Recovery
- Employment & Financial Impact
- *(adjust sections to fit case type)*

**Within each section:**
- Write flowing prose; do not reproduce Q&A format
- Embed citations immediately after each factual assertion: `(78:3–15)`
- Flag admissions: *"Significantly, the witness acknowledged..."*
- Flag contradictions: *"This testimony conflicts with his affidavit stating..."*
- Flag convenient memory gaps: *"The witness claimed no recollection of X despite testifying moments earlier with specificity about Y from the same period (167:12–168:3)."*
- Note demeanor shifts, hesitation, or evasiveness inline

### 4. Exhibit Log

| Exhibit No. | Description | Witness Testimony |
|---|---|---|
| Ex. 1 | [Document type / date] | Authenticated / Denied / No recognition / Contradicted contents |

### 5. Privilege & Objection Notes

- Instructions not to answer: question posed, objecting counsel, stated basis
- Privilege assertions: attorney-client, work product, FRE 408 settlement communications
- Material objections on form, foundation, or scope

## Guidelines

- **Citation format**: `(page:line–line)` after every material assertion; never paraphrase a critical admission without a cite
- **Length target**: ~25–33% of transcript length (e.g., 300-page transcript → 75–100 pages); completeness over brevity
- **Accuracy**: do not characterize testimony beyond what the transcript supports; summary must be defensible to opposing counsel
- **Work product header**: repeat on every page alongside case name and deponent name
- **Follow-up queries**: when asked about specific testimony post-delivery, cite both the summary location and the underlying transcript page/line
- **Jurisdiction**: US federal and state civil litigation conventions
