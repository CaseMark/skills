# GLBA Privacy Notice

Drafts a 16 CFR Part 313-compliant consumer privacy notice using the model form safe harbor (Appendix A), covering all mandatory disclosures and opt-out rights.

## Prerequisites

1. **Institution identity** — legal name, DBA names, charter type (bank, credit union, securities firm, insurer, etc.), federal regulator
2. **Affiliate structure** — full corporate family, types of affiliates (banking, insurance, securities, lending)
3. **Data collection inventory** — categories of nonpublic personal information (NPI) collected and sources
4. **Sharing arrangements** — all affiliate and nonaffiliate sharing agreements, joint marketing agreements, service provider contracts
5. **Opt-out infrastructure** — toll-free number, online URL, mailing address; hours of operation; processing timeline
6. **Security program summary** — physical, electronic, and procedural safeguards in place
7. **Operating jurisdictions** — states where institution operates or serves customers (for state law overlay)

## Output Structure

### 1. FACTS Table Header (Mandatory Model Form Format)

```
FACTS: What Does [Institution Name] Do With Your Personal Information?

| Why? | [One-sentence explanation of why notice is provided] |
| What? | [Categories of NPI collected — summary] |
| How? | [Whether and how consumers can limit sharing] |
```

Opening statement (verbatim or substantially similar):
> "Federal law requires us to tell you how we collect, share, and protect your personal information. Federal law also gives you the right to limit some but not all sharing. Please read this notice carefully to understand what we do."

Include: institution legal name, effective date, DBA names consumers recognize.

---

### 2. Information Collection Disclosure

Group NPI by source:

| Source | Examples |
|---|---|
| Consumer-provided | SSN, driver's license, income, assets/liabilities, account preferences, contact info |
| Account-generated | Balances, payment history, transaction records, check images, wire details, card activity |
| Consumer reporting agencies | Credit reports/scores from Equifax, Experian, TransUnion |
| Other third parties | Identity verification, fraud databases, public records, marketing lists, other FIs (transfers) |

---

### 3. Sharing Matrix and Opt-Out Rights

| Sharing Purpose | Can You Limit? | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Everyday business (process transactions, legal compliance, credit bureaus, fraud prevention) | **No** | 16 CFR § 313.14–.15 exceptions |
| Affiliates — transaction & experience info | **No** | Permitted unless institution opts into restriction |
| Affiliates — creditworthiness info for their marketing | **Yes** | FCRA § 603(d)(2)(A)(iii); 30-day notice before sharing begins |
| Nonaffiliates — joint marketing (formal agreement, financial products only) | **No** | 16 CFR § 313.13 exception |
| Nonaffiliates — for their own marketing purposes | **Yes** | Full GLBA opt-out |

**Affiliate marketing timing language (required):**
> "If you are a new customer, we can begin sharing your information with our affiliates for their marketing purposes 30 days from the date we sent this notice. When you are no longer our customer, we continue to share your information as described in this notice. However, you can contact us at any time to limit this sharing."

---

### 4. Opt-Out Instructions

Provide all three channels:
- **Phone:** Toll-free number, hours, whether live agent available
- **Online:** Full URL, login requirement, accessibility statement (ADA-compliant, assistive-technology compatible)
- **Mail:** Complete address; specify required fields (name, address, account number, signature, election)

Include:
- Processing timeline (e.g., "within 30 days of receipt")
- Joint account treatment (one holder opts out for all, or each separately — specify which)
- Duration of opt-out election (indefinite unless revoked)
- How to revoke opt-out

---

### 5. Security Safeguards

Organize by safeguard type:

| Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| Physical | Restricted facility access, badge controls, secure document disposal/shredding |
| Electronic | Encryption (in transit and at rest), firewalls, MFA, tokenization, security patch management |
| Procedural | Employee training, need-to-know access controls, background checks, vendor management, incident response |

Required statement (verbatim or substantially similar):
> "To protect your personal information from unauthorized access and use, we use security measures that comply with federal law. These measures include computer safeguards and secured files and buildings."

---

### 6. State-Specific Overlays

Apply where institution operates or has customers:

| Jurisdiction | Additional Requirement |
|---|---|
| California | CCPA/CPRA rights (access, deletion, opt-out of sale); California Financial Information Privacy Act (SB 1) — opt-in for certain nonaffiliate sharing [VERIFY current SB 1 applicability] |
| Vermont | Affirmative consent (opt-in) required before sharing NPI with nonaffiliated third parties for marketing [VERIFY current VT Dept. of Financial Regulation rules] |
| Nevada | Opt-out right for sale of covered information; NRS Chapter 603A [VERIFY] |
| Massachusetts | 201 CMR 17.00 data security standards cross-reference [VERIFY] |
| Insurance states (NAIC) | NAIC Insurance Information and Privacy Protection Model Act disclosures for underwriting/claims data |

Highlight state-specific rights in callout boxes or clearly labeled separate sections.

---

### 7. Contact Information Block

- Privacy office: direct phone, email, mailing address
- Chief Privacy Officer title (if disclosed)
- Website URL for current notice
- Regulatory body / supervisory authority (e.g., OCC, FDIC, NCUA, SEC, state insurance dept.)

### 8. Mandatory Elements Checklist (16 CFR § 313.6)

Before finalizing, confirm all elements present:

- [ ] Categories of NPI collected
- [ ] Categories of affiliates/nonaffiliates to whom NPI is disclosed
- [ ] Categories of NPI disclosed about former customers
- [ ] Opt-out rights and exercise methods
- [ ] FCRA § 603(d)(2)(A)(iii) exception disclosures
- [ ] Security policies and practices statement
- [ ] Effective date
- [ ] State-law-required additions (where applicable)

## Guidelines

- **Model form = safe harbor**: Use 16 CFR Part 313, Appendix A format; deviations require independent compliance analysis
- **Plain language**: Active voice, short sentences, descriptive headings ("What information do we collect?"), no regulatory cross-references visible to consumer
- **Delivery compliance**: Paper, electronic (E-SIGN Act consent required), or web posting — ensure format is clear and conspicuous in the delivery medium
- **Annual notice**: Required for ongoing customer relationships; update whenever material changes occur in information practices
- **No over-promising**: Every security and practice statement must reflect current actual operations — do not include aspirational language
- **FCRA / GLBA interplay**: Affiliate marketing opt-out (FCRA) and sharing opt-out (GLBA) are distinct rights — disclose separately
- **Former customers**: Sharing practices for former customers must be disclosed; opt-out rights may not extend post-relationship depending on sharing type
- **Joint marketing**: Only qualifies for § 313.13 exception if parties are financial institutions and have a formal written agreement limiting use
