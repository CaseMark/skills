# Aviation Law Summary

Deliver a structured, citation-ready summary of aviation law authorities and operational impacts for the specified topic.

## Prerequisites

1. **Topic scope** — precise issue area (e.g., accident liability, slot allocation, UAS operations, air service agreements).
2. **Jurisdictional frame** — U.S. federal, international, or mixed; list relevant countries.
3. **Source set** — uploaded materials, known cases, regulations, treaties, agency actions.
4. **Time horizon** — confirm whether current updates or pending rules must be covered.
5. **Citation standard** — Bluebook required; mark uncertain citations with `[VERIFY]`.

## Output Structure / Process

**Section order and required contents**

| Section | Required Contents |
| --- | --- |
| Executive Overview | Core issue, governing regimes, and 3–5 key takeaways. |
| Regulatory Framework | Applicable statutes/regulations, agency authority, jurisdictional reach, conflicts/overlaps. |
| Treaty & International Regime | Conventions, bilateral agreements, preemption/interaction with domestic law. |
| Case Law Analysis | Landmark and recent cases, holdings, reasoning, splits, open questions. |
| Liability & Remedies | Liability caps, defenses, notice requirements, damages, preemption. |
| Enforcement & Procedure | Agency enforcement tools, penalty ranges, certificate actions, appeals. |
| Operational Implications | Compliance duties, documentation, reporting, and risk controls. |
| Emerging/Unsettled Issues | Novel tech, pending rules, legislative initiatives, uncertainties. |
| Practical Recommendations | Action items, monitoring items, counsel-review flags. |
| Table of Authorities | All citations with proper Bluebook format. |

**Regulatory analysis checklist**

- [ ] Identify governing agencies and delegated authority.
- [ ] Distinguish domestic vs. international applicability.
- [ ] Note preemption or conflict points.
- [ ] Identify operational requirements (licensing, certification, maintenance, safety).
- [ ] Flag reporting or recordkeeping obligations.
- [ ] Include recent amendments or proposed rules if within scope.

**Case analysis template**

**Liability regime coverage**

- [ ] Warsaw Convention applicability and limits.
- [ ] Montreal Convention applicability and limits.
- [ ] Domestic statutory regimes affecting liability (e.g., GARA) and scope.
- [ ] Circumstances that negate liability caps.
- [ ] Interaction between regulatory compliance and tort standards.

**Table of Authorities format**

```text
Cases:
- Case Name, Volume Reporter Page (Court Year) (pinpoint)

Statutes:
- Title U.S.C. § Section (Year)

Regulations:
- Title C.F.R. § Section (Year) (Federal Register cite for recent amendments)

Treaties/Agreements:
- Treaty Name, citation, entry into force date, relevant articles
```

**Process**

1. Extract and summarize authorities from provided materials first.
2. Supplement with external sources only when required for currentness or gaps.
3. Verify each authority for jurisdiction, holding, and citation accuracy.
4. Populate sections using the template order above.
5. Produce a complete Table of Authorities.

## Guidelines

- Use precise Bluebook citations; mark any uncertain citation with `[VERIFY]`.
- Clearly separate international and domestic regimes and explain preemption effects.
- Do not assume uniform application across circuits; state splits explicitly.
- Avoid conclusory legal advice; present risks and options with jurisdictional caveats.
- Highlight compliance steps and documentation that reduce regulatory exposure.
- Flag unresolved questions and pending rulemakings that affect risk.
