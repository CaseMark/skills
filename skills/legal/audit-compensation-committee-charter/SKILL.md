# Audit and Compensation Committee Charter

Produces a single charter establishing Audit and Compensation Committees with compliant authority, composition, and duties.

## Prerequisites

1. Company status (public, private, or preparing to list) and exchange or market (NYSE, NASDAQ, OTC, none).
2. State of incorporation and any governance statute constraints (e.g., DGCL 141(c) [VERIFY]).
3. Current charter and bylaws, plus any existing committee charters or board resolutions.
4. Board size, desired committee size, and independence standard to apply.
5. Auditor relationship details, internal audit function status, and financial reporting timeline.
6. Executive compensation structure, equity plans, and existing employment or severance agreements.
7. Industry-specific regulatory overlays (e.g., financial services, healthcare, energy).
8. Whistleblower and compliance reporting channels and owners.
9. Desired meeting cadence and reporting expectations to the full board.

## Output Structure / Process

Use the following outline and fill placeholders with company-specific facts.

| Section | Required Content |
| --- | --- |
| Adoption and Authority | Board adoption statement, effective date, delegated authority, board retains ultimate fiduciary authority. |
| Committee Structure | Separate Audit and Compensation Committees; each is a standing committee. |
| Membership and Independence | Minimum members, independence tests, appointment process, chair designation, term or rotation. |
| Authority and Resources | Sole authority to retain advisors, funding, access to records and personnel, investigation authority. |
| Meetings and Minutes | Quorum, voting, written consent limits, minutes requirements, executive sessions. |
| Responsibilities | Detailed lists for Audit and Compensation Committees (below). |
| Reporting | Regular board reporting, escalation triggers, required public reports. |
| Evaluation and Charter Review | Annual self-assessment, annual charter review, amendment process. |

Audit Committee core responsibilities checklist:

- Oversee integrity of financial statements and disclosures (annual and quarterly).
- Review critical accounting policies, estimates, judgments, and alternative treatments.
- Appoint, compensate, oversee, and terminate the independent auditor; auditor reports to the committee.
- Pre-approve audit and permissible non-audit services; document any delegation to chair.
- Receive required auditor communications under PCAOB standards.
- Evaluate auditor independence and quality controls annually.
- Oversee internal audit charter, plan, budget, staffing, and head of internal audit.
- Oversee internal controls over financial reporting and remediation of deficiencies.
- Establish and oversee whistleblower procedures for accounting and auditing concerns (SOX 301).
- Review related-party transactions and disclosure process.
- Oversee compliance and legal risks that could materially affect financial statements.
- Review enterprise risk management for financial and reporting risks.
- Hold private sessions with the independent auditor and internal audit at least annually.

Compensation Committee core responsibilities checklist:

- Establish compensation philosophy and review annually.
- Set CEO goals, evaluate performance, and determine CEO compensation in executive session.
- Approve or ratify compensation for other executive officers.
- Approve employment agreements, severance, CIC provisions, and special compensation.
- Administer equity incentive plans and approve grants; set grant timing and MNPI safeguards.
- Assess compensation-related risk and mitigation tools (caps, clawbacks, holding periods).
- Oversee executive succession planning and leadership development.
- Evaluate and retain independent compensation advisors; assess independence factors.
- Meet with the independent compensation consultant at least annually without management present.

Public company add-on requirements table:

| Topic | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Audit Committee independence | SEC Rule 10A-3; no compensatory fees; no affiliates. |
| Financial expert | At least one audit committee financial expert (Reg S-K) and disclosure. |
| SOX authority | Sections 201, 206, 301, 806 (non-audit services, auditor rotation, whistleblower). |
| Compensation committee independence | NYSE or NASDAQ standards; consultant independence factors. |
| Equity awards | Rule 16b-3 non-employee director approvals. |
| Tax deductibility | IRC 162(m) outside director concepts only if still applicable [VERIFY]. |
| Proxy disclosures | Audit Committee Report; Compensation Committee Report; CD&A review. |
| Charter availability | Post on website and provide in filings as required by exchange or SEC rules. |

Template (edit for company specifics):

```text

I. Authority
The Board adopts this Charter pursuant to the [Bylaws/State Law]. The Audit Committee and Compensation Committee are standing committees of the Board. The Board retains ultimate fiduciary authority.

II. Membership
A. Audit Committee: [#] independent directors meeting [NYSE/NASDAQ/10A-3] standards; at least one financial expert.
B. Compensation Committee: [#] independent directors meeting [NYSE/NASDAQ] standards; Rule 16b-3 compliance.

III. Meetings and Procedures
Quorum: majority. Actions: majority vote of those present or unanimous written consent. Executive sessions required as specified below. Minutes maintained and approved.

IV. Authority and Resources
Each committee may retain independent advisors; the Company will provide funding. Full access to records, personnel, and facilities.

V. Audit Committee Responsibilities
[List from checklist tailored to company.]

VI. Compensation Committee Responsibilities
[List from checklist tailored to company.]

VII. Reporting
Committee chairs report to the Board after each meeting and escalate material issues promptly.

VIII. Evaluation and Charter Review
Annual self-assessment and annual Charter review; amendments require Board approval.
```

## Guidelines

- Tailor independence definitions to the applicable exchange rules; default to the strictest standard if multiple apply.
- Avoid promising activities that management or committees cannot deliver; keep obligations realistic and auditable.
- For private companies, remove public-company disclosures and replace with best-practice equivalents.
- Keep CEO absent from deliberations on their compensation; document executive sessions.
- Confirm 409A compliance for severance and deferred compensation provisions.
- Use consistent defined terms and cross-references; align with bylaws and board committee authority limits.
- Mark uncertain legal citations with `[VERIFY]` and resolve before final delivery.
