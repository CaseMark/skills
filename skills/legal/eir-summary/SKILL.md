# Environmental Impact Report Summary

Condense EIR/EIS technical findings into a legally actionable summary with citations to the record.

## Prerequisites

1. Full EIR/EIS (including appendices, technical studies, and modeling outputs).
2. Project description materials (maps, site plans, phasing, schedule).
3. Lead agency, jurisdiction, and governing statutes/regulations.
4. Public notice and comment record, including responses.
5. Thresholds of significance or regulatory standards used.
6. Mitigation Monitoring and Reporting Program (if separate).
7. List of required permits/approvals (if available).

## Output Structure / Process

1. Identify document type (CEQA EIR, NEPA EIS, or other) and review level (programmatic, tiered, supplemental, addendum).
2. Extract project snapshot, lead agency role, and decision timeline.
3. Build the Environmental Impacts Matrix from each resource area.
4. Build the Alternatives Analysis table and identify the environmentally superior alternative.
5. Isolate Significant and Unavoidable Impacts with required findings.
6. Summarize mitigation measures and enforceability via MMRP.
7. Extract public comments, agency responses, and controversy/discord.
8. Complete the Compliance and Vulnerability Checklist.
9. Provide a source map for all key findings.

**Compliance and Vulnerability Checklist**

- [ ] Baseline data adequate and supported by substantial evidence.
- [ ] Alternatives range is reasonable and feasible; no improper elimination.
- [ ] Cumulative impacts analyzed with defined geographic/temporal scope.
- [ ] Significance thresholds disclosed and applied consistently.
- [ ] Mitigation is specific, enforceable, and not improperly deferred.
- [ ] Growth-inducing/indirect impacts addressed.
- [ ] Segmentation/piecemealing avoided or justified.
- [ ] Responses to comments are substantive and address expert critiques.
- [ ] Required consultations/permits identified (e.g., CWA, CAA, ESA, NHPA) [VERIFY].
- [ ] Statement of overriding considerations (if needed) supported by record.
- [ ] Recirculation triggers evaluated (new significant impacts or information).

## Guidelines

- Use the document’s exact significance labels and thresholds; do not normalize or reword.
- Cite every material finding to section and page/exhibit.
- Do not fill gaps; write “Not found in EIR/EIS” when missing.
- Separate fact from inference; label inference explicitly.
- If jurisdiction or governing statute is unclear, flag and request clarification.
- Mark uncertain legal citations or regulatory triggers with `[VERIFY]`.
- Maintain neutral, analytical tone; no advocacy or conclusions of law.
