# Legislative Bill Summary

Deliver a neutral, section-pinned summary of a U.S. legislative bill with effects, implementation, and controversy captured in plain language.

## Prerequisites

1. **Bill text** — full text or latest engrossed version
2. **Bill metadata** — bill number, title, sponsor(s), chamber, introduction date, current status, amendments
3. **Supporting materials** — fiscal notes, committee reports, legislative analyses, staff briefs
4. **Current law references** — cited statutes/regulations being amended or affected
5. **Jurisdiction** — federal or specific state/territory; session/legislature

## Output Structure / Process

**Research checklist**
- Confirm the latest bill version and amendment history
- Capture official status “as of” date
- Identify related statutes and current-law baseline
- Extract fiscal/appropriation figures and agency impacts
- Note opposition/support positions if documented

**Section map (use all, in order)**

| Section | Required contents |
| --- | --- |
| Identifiers | Bill number, title, jurisdiction, session, chamber, sponsor(s), co-sponsors, introduction date, status with “as of” date, last action, effective date(s) |
| Intent / Problem | Problem statement, policy objectives, triggering events or case law (if any) |
| Major Provisions | For each provision: current law → change → operational effect; affected parties; enforcement or penalties; admin authority |
| Fiscal / Budget | One-time costs, ongoing costs, revenue impacts, funding sources, agency burden |
| Stakeholder Impact | Individuals, businesses, nonprofits, agencies; rights expanded/restricted; compliance obligations |
| Implementation | Rulemaking authority, phase-in, reporting, evaluation, sunset clauses |
| Controversies / Risks | Opposition points, constitutional or federal preemption issues, conflicts with state law |
| Comparative / Trend | Alignment or divergence from other jurisdictions or recent legislative trends |
| Open Questions | Missing data, unclear definitions, unresolved operational details |

**Provision formatting (repeat per provision)**
- Provision label: Section or line cite
- Current law: One sentence
- Change: One sentence
- Effect: 1–3 sentences
- Affected: Groups or agencies
- Enforcement: Entity and mechanism

## Guidelines

- Use precise section pinpoints (e.g., “Sec. 4(b)(2)”) for every major provision.
- Keep tone neutral; do not advocate or predict outcomes.
- If fiscal data is missing, state “No fiscal note located.”
- Flag potential constitutional, preemption, or conflict issues; add `[VERIFY]` if uncertain.
- Translate terms into plain language, but preserve defined terms of art.
- State uncertainty explicitly when sources disagree.
- Use “as of” dates for status and version control.
- Do not infer intent beyond documented statements or committee materials.
