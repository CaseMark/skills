# IP Litigation Deposition Supplement

Practice-area supplement adding IP-specific examination frameworks to general deposition preparation. Use with @deposition-preparation as the primary skill.

## Prerequisites

1. Relevant patents, registrations, or trade secret identification statements
2. Prosecution history (patent cases)
3. Claim construction positions and disputed terms (patent cases)
4. Prior art references at issue
5. Accused product/process technical documentation
6. Licensing history and comparable licenses
7. Expert reports (if experts already designated)
8. Confidentiality agreements (trade secret cases)

## Output Structure / Process

### IP Case Type Reference

| Case Type | Key Legal Issues | Primary Witnesses |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Patent** | Claim construction, infringement (literal/DOE), validity (anticipation, obviousness, enablement), reasonable royalty, lost profits, willfulness | Inventor, prosecuting attorney (limited), R&D/engineering, licensing/business, technical expert, damages expert |
| **Trademark** | Validity/distinctiveness, priority of use, likelihood of confusion, willfulness, damages | Mark owner/creator, marketing witnesses, consumer survey expert, damages expert |
| **Copyright** | Ownership/authorship, originality, access and copying, substantial similarity, fair use, damages | Author/creator, access witnesses, similarity expert, damages expert |
| **Trade Secret** | Trade secret existence, reasonable secrecy measures, misappropriation (acquisition/disclosure/use), damages | Trade secret developer, accused misappropriator, security witnesses, damages expert |

### Patent: Inventor Deposition

**Conception & Reduction to Practice**
- When did you first conceive of this invention? Describe it in detail.
- What problem were you solving? What records exist from that time?
- Who did you discuss it with? When was it first reduced to practice?
- What testing or prototyping was done?

**Prior Art Knowledge**
- What prior art were you aware of when inventing?
- Did you search for prior art? How is your invention different from [prior art]?
- Did you know about [specific reference]?

**Claims & Prosecution**
- What does claim 1 cover in your understanding? What does [claim term] refer to?
- Were you involved in prosecution? Did you review office actions or provide input on amendments?
- Why were certain claims amended?

---

### Patent: Accused Infringer Technical Witness

**Product/Process**
- Describe how [accused product] works; walk through the relevant process.
- When was it developed and by whom?

**Design Choices**
- What alternatives were considered? Why was this approach chosen?
- Were you aware of the patent during design? Did you design around it?

**Claim Element Mapping**
- Does the accused product have [claim element]? How does it perform [claim function]?

**Non-Infringement**
- What claim limitation is not met? How does the product differ from the claims?

---

### Patent: Licensing / Damages Witness

**Licensing History**
- Has this patent been licensed? To whom, on what terms, at what royalty rate?
- What was the negotiation process? Are there comparable licenses?

**Hypothetical Negotiation (Georgia-Pacific)**
- Which Georgia-Pacific factors were most significant?
- What royalty rate and royalty base would have been agreed?
- Are there acceptable non-infringing alternatives? What is their market availability?

**Lost Profits (Panduit)**
- Does patentee have manufacturing capacity?
- What market share would patentee have captured but for infringement?
- Is the patented feature a driver of consumer demand? How did you apportion value?

### Patent: Expert Depositions

Apply @deposition-expert-witness framework plus:

**Technical Expert**
- How do you construe [disputed claim term]? What prosecution history supports that?
- Walk through infringement analysis element by element. Did you physically examine the product? Review source code?
- Does [prior art reference] disclose [claim element]? Would a PHOSITA have been motivated to combine references? What is the teaching/suggestion/motivation?

**Damages Expert**
- What reasonable royalty rate do you calculate? What methodology?
- Which of the 15 Georgia-Pacific factors did you apply and how? [VERIFY factor count]
- What comparable licenses did you analyze? What is the royalty base?
- For lost profits: what is the "but for" world? Are non-infringing alternatives available?
- How did you apportion value to the patented feature specifically?

---

### Trademark: Mark Owner / Creator

- When was the mark created? By whom? Why chosen?
- First use in commerce date — what documentation exists?
- Is the mark inherently distinctive or has it acquired secondary meaning? What advertising supports recognition?
- When did you become aware of defendant's mark? What confusion incidents have occurred?
- What harm (lost sales, goodwill damage, costs) has defendant's use caused?

### Trademark: Accused Infringer

- When did you adopt this mark? Who decided? Was a trademark search conducted?
- Were you aware of plaintiff's mark? Did you receive legal advice?
- Did you intend to trade on plaintiff's goodwill or cause confusion?
- Are you aware of any actual confusion incidents — misdirected orders, customer inquiries?

---

### Trade Secret: Owner / Developer

- Describe the trade secret with specificity. What makes it secret?
- Physical security: locks, access control? Electronic: passwords, encryption?
- Contractual protections: NDAs, employment agreements? Need-to-know limitations?
- What is the trade secret's value? Investment to develop? Cost to independently replicate?
- How do you contend defendant acquired and used it? When did misappropriation occur?

### Trade Secret: Accused Misappropriator

- What was your relationship with plaintiff? What confidentiality agreements did you sign?
- Did you have access to the claimed information? How did you obtain what you used?
- Did you develop it independently? What evidence supports independent development?
- Did you know the information was confidential or a trade secret?
- Did you disclose it to anyone? How does your product/process relate to plaintiff's alleged secret?

## Deposition Checklist

**For Every IP Deposition**
- [ ] Review all relevant patents, registrations, or trade secret identification statements
- [ ] Review prosecution history and claim construction positions (patent)
- [ ] Build claim element mapping chart (patent)
- [ ] Identify and analyze all prior art at issue (patent/copyright)
- [ ] Understand technical operation of accused product/process
- [ ] Review all licensing agreements and identify comparable licenses
- [ ] Prepare likelihood of confusion factor analysis (trademark)
- [ ] Review all confidentiality agreements and security measures (trade secret)
- [ ] Review expert reports if available
- [ ] Prepare damages theory framework matched to case type

## Guidelines

- **Claim construction first**: In patent cases, establish the witness's understanding of disputed claim terms before asking about infringement or validity — inconsistencies become impeachment.
- **Pin inventors on prior art knowledge**: Failure to disclose known prior art bears on inequitable conduct; document what the inventor knew and when.
- **Prosecution history estoppel**: Explore amendments and arguments that may limit claim scope under DOE.
- **Trade secret specificity**: Plaintiff must identify trade secrets with reasonable particularity before discovery; use deposition to test adequacy of identification.
- **Willfulness**: Establish knowledge of the patent/mark and steps (or lack thereof) taken after learning of IP rights.
- **Apportionment (patent damages)**: Required where patented feature is not the entire basis for consumer demand — press experts on methodology.
