# Initial Board Resolutions

Drafts comprehensive organizational meeting minutes and initial board resolutions for a newly formed U.S. corporation.

## Prerequisites

1. **Articles of incorporation** — exact legal name, state, date of incorporation, authorized capital structure (classes, numbers of shares)
2. **Bylaws** — adopted or draft form, noting key provisions (indemnification, exculpation, supermajority requirements)
3. **Directors & officers** — full legal names, addresses, titles, and compensation (or deferral mechanism)
4. **Initial shareholders** — name, address, share count, class, and consideration (cash amount, property description + valuation, or services description + FMV)
5. **Pre-incorporation agreements** — any contracts, leases, or commitments signed by founders before filing
6. **IP inventory** — prior-developed IP being contributed; existing PIIA agreements or gaps
7. **Tax elections intended** — S-corp, Section 83(b) for vesting shares, Section 1244, Section 1202 QSBS
8. **Banking institution** — name, signatory designees, and signature threshold preferences

## Output Structure

### 1. Preamble & Meeting Authentication

```
MINUTES OF THE ORGANIZATIONAL MEETING OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS
OF [CORPORATION LEGAL NAME]

Date/Time/Location: _______________
Format: [ ] In-person  [ ] Telephonic  [ ] Video  [ ] Unanimous Written Consent
Directors Present: _______________ (constituting a quorum — majority of [N] total directors)
Chairperson: _______________   Secretary: _______________
Notice: [ ] Proper notice given  [ ] All directors waived notice in writing
```

### 2. Resolution Categories

| # | Resolution | Key Drafting Points |
|---|-----------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Bylaw Adoption** | Reference bylaws by date; direct Secretary to execute and maintain original; acknowledge material provisions (indemnification, exculpation, conflict-of-interest procedures) |
| 2 | **Officer Elections** | State each title, full legal name, effective date; enumerate authority or cross-reference bylaws sections; address compensation or defer to future resolution |
| 3 | **Banking Authorization** | Name institution(s); designate signatories; set dual-signature thresholds (e.g., $10K); authorize electronic banking, wire transfers, ACH, remote deposit, corporate cards |
| 4 | **Stock Issuance** | Per issuance: shares, class, recipient, consideration (cash/property/services with FMV); board finding of adequate consideration; identify securities exemption (§4(a)(2), Reg D); identify state exemption; direct stock ledger entry and certificate issuance |
| 5 | **Pre-Incorporation Ratification** | List each agreement by title/date/counterparty; recite material terms; board finding that ratification is in corporation's best interest; authorize officers to execute confirmations |
| 6 | **IP Assignment** | Direct all founders/employees/consultants to execute PIIA agreements; specifically schedule any pre-incorporation IP being contributed; confirm corporation owns all business-essential IP free of encumbrances |
| 7 | **Tax & Fiscal Year** | Adopt fiscal year (calendar or specify end date); authorize EIN application; authorize state/local tax registrations; authorize S-corp election (Form 2553) if applicable; authorize §83(b) elections for vesting shares if applicable |
| 8 | **Operational Authorizations** | Authorize officers to execute ordinary-course contracts up to $[X]; approve principal office; authorize foreign qualification filings; authorize business licenses and permits |
| 9 | **Professional Advisors** | Ratify engagement of legal counsel; approve accountant/CPA; authorize payroll service; confirm registered agent |
| 10 | **D&O Insurance & Indemnification** | Confirm indemnification to fullest extent of state law and bylaws; authorize indemnification agreements with each director/officer; authorize D&O, EPLI, and fiduciary liability insurance procurement |
| 11 | **Record-Keeping** | Direct Secretary to maintain minute book (articles, bylaws, minutes, consents, stock ledger, certificates); establish procedures for future meeting minutes and written consents |
| 12 | **Adjournment & Certification** | Time of adjournment; Secretary certification of quorum, due adoption, and accuracy of minutes; signature lines for Secretary and Chairperson |

### 3. Resolution Formatting Template

```
RESOLVED, THAT [corporation name] hereby [action], effective [date]; and

FURTHER RESOLVED, THAT the officers of the Corporation are hereby authorized
and directed to take all actions necessary or appropriate to carry out the
purposes and intent of the foregoing resolution.
```

### 4. Securities Law Exemption Reference

| Situation | Federal Exemption | Common State Analog |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| Founder shares, accredited investors | §4(a)(2) Securities Act | Founder/limited offering exemption |
| Broader private offering | Reg D (Rule 504/506) | Reg D notice filing |
| Ordinary loss treatment on loss | §1244 (≤$1M aggregate) | N/A — federal only |
| QSBS capital gains exclusion | §1202 (C-corp, ≤$50M assets at issuance) [VERIFY current asset threshold] | N/A — federal only |

### 5. S-Corp Election Checklist (if applicable)

- [ ] File Form 2553 within **2 months + 15 days** of tax year start (or any time in preceding year)
- [ ] All shareholders must consent on Form 2553
- [ ] ≤100 shareholders; one class of stock; no corporate/partnership shareholders
- [ ] Resolution authorizes officers to execute and file; attach shareholder consents as exhibit

## Guidelines

- Use exact legal name from articles of incorporation throughout — no abbreviations unless defined
- Confirm quorum before proceeding (typically majority of authorized directors under state law)
- Each stock issuance requires an independent board finding of adequate consideration to avoid watered-stock claims
- §83(b) elections must be filed within **30 days** of grant — flag urgency if vesting shares are issued
- State corporate law varies significantly (Delaware vs. California vs. others) — confirm officer requirements, notice periods, and consent procedures for the state of incorporation
- Do not include securities exemption reliance language without confirming purchaser qualification
- For regulated industries (fintech, healthcare, cannabis), flag that additional industry-specific resolutions and licensing authorizations are required
- Attach executed PIIA agreements, stock purchase agreements, and investment representation letters as exhibits
