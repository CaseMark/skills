# Complaint for Negligence

Drafts a litigation-ready negligence complaint that survives a motion to dismiss, establishes all elements with factual specificity, and positions the plaintiff for discovery leverage.

## Prerequisites

1. **Incident facts** — date, time, location, parties present, sequence of events
2. **Defendant identification** — full legal name, entity type, state of incorporation/domicile, registered agent
3. **Medical records and billing** — diagnoses, treatment timeline, itemized expenses
4. **Economic loss documentation** — wage records, property damage estimates, lost earnings data
5. **Jurisdiction/venue determination** — court, applicable pleading standard, local rules
6. **Governing authority** — applicable statutes, traffic codes, or professional standards for negligence per se

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption
- Full court name with division/district/county
- All party names (complete legal names; verify corporate names via secretary of state)
- Case No. field; document title: `COMPLAINT FOR NEGLIGENCE`

### 2. Jurisdiction & Venue

| Basis | Federal | State |
|---|---|---|
| Subject matter | Diversity: 28 U.S.C. § 1332 (complete diversity + >$75K); individuals = domicile; corps = state of incorporation **and** principal place of business | Cite state general jurisdiction statute for tort claims |
| Venue | FRCP 1391: where defendant resides (if all in same state), where substantial events occurred, or where any defendant subject to personal jurisdiction | State analog: where defendant resides, cause arose, or substantial business conducted |

Include specific factual allegations supporting venue (address, county, district).

### 3. Parties

**Each plaintiff:** Full name, residential address (city, county, state), legal status. For minors/incapacitated persons: name guardian ad litem and legal authority.

**Each defendant:** Full name, address, entity type. For employed defendants: `"Defendant [Name] was employed by Defendant [Employer] and acting within the course and scope of employment at all relevant times."` → foundation for respondeat superior.

### 4. Factual Allegations

Draft chronological numbered paragraphs, each containing discrete, observable facts. No legal conclusions — describe what happened.

**Structure:**
1. **Background** — relationship between parties, defendant's role/control, plaintiff's status (invitee, patient, motorist)
2. **The incident** — specific date/time, precise location, sequence of events with measurements, speeds, signals, observable conditions
3. **Injuries** — medical precision: fracture type/location, diagnoses, loss of consciousness, surgical interventions
4. **Treatment timeline** — emergency transport → hospitalization → surgery → rehab → ongoing/future needs
5. **Economic losses** — itemized past medical expenses (amounts if available), lost wages (calculated from earnings history), property damage, future costs
6. **Non-economic losses** — pain and suffering, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment (specific activities lost), disfigurement, permanent disability, consortium impact

> **Drafting standard:** Replace `"Defendant was negligent"` with `"Defendant drove through a steady red light at 45 mph in a 25 mph zone without slowing."` Concrete facts allow the court to draw inferences.

### 5. Count I — Negligence

Reallegation paragraph incorporating all prior facts, then plead each element in separate numbered paragraphs:

| Element | Allegation Form |
|---|---|
| **Duty** | State the specific standard: reasonable care on roadway / professional standard of care in locality |
| **Breach** | Specific acts/omissions violating the standard |
| **Causation** | Actual (but-for) + proximate (foreseeable consequence); substantial factor where multiple causes exist |
| **Damages** | Physical injuries, medical expenses, lost wages, earning capacity, pain and suffering, other harms described in facts |

**Negligence per se (if applicable):** Identify violated statute → class of protected persons → type of harm → `"Defendant's violation of [Vehicle Code § X] constitutes negligence as a matter of law."`

**Additional counts** (if applicable): Count II — Respondeat Superior (employer); Count III — Gross Negligence/Recklessness (if facts support heightened culpability).

### 6. Prayer for Relief

- Compensatory damages (general + special) — amount per local rule (specific or "to be proven at trial")
- Past and future medical expenses
- Past and future lost wages / loss of earning capacity
- Property damage (specific amount if repair estimate available)
- Pain and suffering, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment of life, disfigurement
- Pre-judgment interest from date of injury (cite jurisdiction's authorizing statute; not universally available)
- Post-judgment interest; costs of suit; attorney's fees (only if fee-shifting statute or contract applies)
- Catchall: `"Such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper."`
- **Jury demand:** `"Plaintiff demands trial by jury on all issues so triable."` (FRCP 38: must be served within 14 days of last pleading directed to the issue; file with complaint to be safe)

### 7. Signature Block & Verification

- Attorney name (as registered), bar number, firm, address, phone, email
- FRCP 11 / state equivalent certification implicit in signature
- **Verification:** Required in some states (not federal, unless specific statute). If required: sworn statement that facts are true and correct to plaintiff's personal knowledge; jurisdiction may require notarization.

## Guidelines

- **Federal pleading standard:** Satisfy *Twombly*/*Iqbal* — factual allegations must raise relief above speculative level and state a plausible claim on its face
- **State pleading:** Determine whether jurisdiction follows notice, fact, or code pleading before calibrating specificity
- **No legal conclusions in facts section** — save duty/breach/causation/damages labels for the Cause of Action count
- **Verify all entity names** via secretary of state registry before filing
- **Statutory citations:** Mark any citation you cannot independently verify as `[VERIFY]`
- **Local rules:** Confirm margin (typically 1"), font (typically 12pt Times New Roman or court-specified), spacing, page limits, and e-filing requirements for the specific court/judge
- **Consistency check:** Party names, dates, and factual details must be identical throughout all sections
- **Do not plead damage amounts** in jurisdictions that prohibit specific figures in complaints (research before including)
