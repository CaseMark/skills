# Trademark License Agreement

Drafts a litigation-defensible Trademark License Agreement that protects the licensor's mark ownership and goodwill while establishing enforceable commercial terms for licensee use.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party details** — full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions of organization, principal places of business
2. **Licensed marks** — mark names, registration numbers, jurisdictions (registered or common law), visual representations for Exhibit A
3. **License scope** — exclusive or non-exclusive; field of use; licensed products/services (specific categories, not broad descriptions)
4. **Territory** — countries, states, or distribution channels; treatment of online/passive internet sales
5. **Financial terms** — upfront fees, royalty rate (% of Net Sales or per-unit), minimum guarantees, payment frequency
6. **Term** — initial duration, auto-renewal mechanics, notice period for non-renewal
7. **Quality control requirements** — approval process, sample submission timelines, applicable standards

## Output Structure

### 1. Header & Recitals
- Title: **TRADEMARK LICENSE AGREEMENT**
- Effective date, full party identification, defined terms ("Licensor," "Licensee")
- Recitals: licensor's ownership of valuable marks; licensee's desire to obtain authorized use

### 2. Grant of License

| Element | Drafting Requirement |
|---|---|
| Exclusivity | State exclusive or non-exclusive; exclusive = licensor cannot grant others in territory |
| Transferability | Non-transferable and non-sublicensable unless expressly permitted |
| Field of use | Tied directly to licensed products/services; no open-ended descriptions |
| Permitted uses | Advertising, packaging, digital media — enumerate specifically |
| Rights reserved | All rights not expressly granted reserved to licensor |

### 3. Licensed Marks (Exhibit A)
- Reference Exhibit A for visual representations, registration numbers, and jurisdictions
- Specify required trademark symbols (®, ™, or ℠) per mark
- Address combination use with licensee's or third-party marks; state prominence requirements

### 4. Licensed Products & Services
- Specific product categories or service descriptions; avoid broad class-level language
- Approval process for expanding scope during term
- Pre-launch sample/prototype submission requirement

### 5. Territory
- Define by country, state, province, or distribution channel
- For international: address online sales and passive internet reach beyond territory

### 6. Quality Control
- All goods/services bearing marks must meet licensor-specified standards
- **Approval workflow**: licensee submits samples of products, packaging, and marketing materials → licensor has **15 business days** to approve/reject → silence = deemed approved
- Licensor right to inspect facilities and operations on reasonable notice
- Immediate discontinuation right upon quality failure
- Short cure period or immediate termination right for QC violations (distinguish from standard 30-day cure)

### 7. Ownership & Goodwill
- Licensee acknowledges licensor's sole ownership of all right, title, and interest, including goodwill
- All use inures to licensor's benefit
- Licensee prohibited from challenging mark validity or ownership during and after term
- Required trademark notices and source indicators

### 8. Royalties & Payment

| Term | Specification |
|---|---|
| Net Sales definition | Gross revenue minus returns, allowances, taxes, and shipping |
| Royalty rate | [% of Net Sales] or [fixed amount per unit] |
| Payment schedule | Quarterly, due within **30 days** after quarter end |
| Minimum guarantee | State whether failure to meet minimums = material breach |
| Late payment | Specify interest rate on overdue amounts |
| Currency | Specify payment currency; allocate FX risk for international deals |

### 9. Reporting & Audit Rights
- **Quarterly royalty reports**: units sold, gross sales, deductions, net sales, royalties due
- Licensor audit right on **30 days' notice**, during business hours, **once per year**
- Exception: unlimited audits if prior audit revealed underpayment > **5%**
- Licensee bears audit cost if underpayment exceeds threshold
- Record retention: **3–5 years** per transaction

### 10. Term & Renewal
- State initial term (e.g., 3 or 5 years)
- Auto-renewal for successive periods unless either party gives notice (e.g., 90 days pre-expiration)
- Option: adjusted royalty rates or expanded territory on renewal

### 11. Termination

**Grounds:**

| Trigger | Cure Period |
|---|---|
| Material breach (general) | 30 days written notice |
| Quality control violation | Shorter period or immediate at licensor's election |
| Mark challenge by licensee | Immediate |
| Bankruptcy/insolvency | Immediate |
| Failure to meet minimum thresholds | Per agreement |
| Convenience termination | 90–180 days notice |

**Post-termination obligations:**
- Immediate cessation of all mark use (products, packaging, web, social media)
- **Sell-off period**: 60–90 days for existing inventory; royalties continue
- Written certification of destruction of remaining materials
- Transfer of domain names and social media accounts incorporating the marks
- Survival: accrued royalties, confidentiality, post-term use restrictions

### 12. Standard Provisions
- **Indemnification**: licensee indemnifies licensor for claims arising from licensee's use or licensed products
- **Representations**: both parties — authority to contract; licensor — mark ownership; licensee — quality compliance
- **Limitation of liability**: mutual cap appropriate to transaction value
- **Dispute resolution**: governing law (specify state), jurisdiction, litigation or ADR election
- **Assignment**: licensee may not assign without licensor consent; licensor assignment permitted
- **Notices**: method, addresses, deemed receipt timing
- **Boilerplate**: entire agreement, amendment (written only), severability, waiver, force majeure, counterparts, electronic signatures

### 13. Exhibits
- **Exhibit A**: Licensed marks with visual representations, registration numbers, jurisdictions
- **Exhibit B** (if applicable): Quality control standards and guidelines

### 14. Signature Block
- Signature lines: authorized representative name, title, date
- Entity-appropriate authority (officer for corp, manager/member for LLC, general partner for LP)
- Counterparts and electronic signature clause

## Guidelines

- **Quality control is validity-preserving**: naked licenses (no QC) can result in abandonment of the mark — ensure QC provisions are substantive, not pro forma
- **Exclusivity language must be precise**: distinguish exclusive license (no other licensees in territory/field) from sole license (licensor retains own use rights)
- **Field of use and territory** must be defined narrowly enough to avoid scope disputes
- **Goodwill inurement clause** is mandatory for licensor protection — all use accrues to licensor
- **Minimum royalties** should be tied to breach mechanics if commercially significant
- **International deals**: confirm mark registrations in each territory; unregistered marks may lack protection
- For **registered marks**, verify current USPTO (or foreign registry) status before drafting — cite registration numbers only if confirmed [VERIFY registrations with client]
- Anti-challenge clause (licensee cannot challenge validity) should be reviewed for enforceability in applicable jurisdiction [VERIFY]
