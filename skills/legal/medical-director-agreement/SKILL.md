# Medical Director Agreement

Drafts a regulatory-compliant agreement establishing the relationship between a healthcare organization and a medical director, with AKS/Stark safe harbor protections and state-law adaptations.

## Prerequisites

1. **Organization details** — legal name, entity type, state of formation, facility type (hospital, ASC, SNF, home health, etc.)
2. **Medical director candidate** — full name, license number/state, DEA registration, board certifications, whether contracting individually or through a professional entity
3. **Scope parameters** — exclusive vs. non-exclusive role, expected hours/week, reporting structure, departments/service lines covered
4. **Compensation data** — proposed amount, basis (annual/monthly/hourly), FMV support (survey data, independent valuation, or comparable arrangements)
5. **Governing contract documents** — bylaws, existing templates, board resolutions, compliance program policies
6. **Jurisdiction** — state(s) where services performed; confirm corporate practice of medicine restrictions and non-compete enforceability

## Output Structure

Draft a 10–15 page agreement with the following sections:

### 1. Preamble & Recitals

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Parties | Full legal names, entity types, formation state, principal addresses; MD license #, DEA, board certs |
| Recitals | Organization's operational need; regulatory/accreditation requirement for medical director; candidate's qualifications; mutual intent for compliant arrangement with preserved independent medical judgment |

### 2. Appointment & Duties

- Exclusive or non-exclusive designation
- Time commitment (hours/week or month), effective date, reporting line (CEO / Board / Medical Staff President)
- **Core duties checklist:**
  - [ ] Clinical oversight and leadership for designated services/departments
  - [ ] Develop, review, update clinical policies, protocols, procedures
  - [ ] Quality assurance and performance improvement participation
  - [ ] Medical staff–administration liaison
  - [ ] Credentialing and privileging process participation
  - [ ] Clinical operations consultation
  - [ ] Regulatory agency and accreditor representation
  - [ ] Compliance with federal/state healthcare laws
- Decision-making authority matrix: unilateral clinical decisions vs. administration consultation vs. board approval required
- Emergency decision-making authority and on-call expectations
- Align duties to MD's specialty/scope of practice; flag corporate practice of medicine restrictions

### 3. Compensation & Benefits

**Regulatory compliance language (mandatory):**
> Compensation is for legitimate medical director services actually rendered, consistent with fair market value, and not determined in any manner that takes into account the volume or value of referrals or other business generated between the parties.

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Base compensation | Amount, frequency (annual/monthly/hourly), payment method |
| Supplemental compensation | Extra committee work, special projects, expanded on-call — tied to identifiable services |
| Expense reimbursement | Travel, CME, license/DEA fees, professional dues; receipts required above threshold |
| Professional liability insurance | Occurrence vs. claims-made; limits (typically $1–3M per occurrence / $3–5M aggregate); tail coverage obligations |
| Other benefits | Health insurance, retirement plan, PTO (if applicable) |
| FMV documentation | Methodology used: published surveys, independent valuation, regional comparables, time-based calculation |
| Periodic reassessment | Annual review against compensation surveys or periodic independent valuation |

### 4. Term & Termination

- **Initial term:** 1–3 years with precise start/end dates
- **Renewal:** Auto-renewal for successive periods with 60–90 day non-renewal notice, OR affirmative renewal requirement

**Termination for cause (immediate):**
- Loss of medical license or DEA registration
- Exclusion from Medicare/Medicaid/federal healthcare programs
- Felony conviction or healthcare-related misdemeanor
- Material uncured breach (15–30 day cure period after written notice)
- Professional misconduct or ethical violation
- Failure to maintain required credentials/insurance
- Conduct posing immediate patient safety or organizational risk

**Termination without cause:** 90–120 day written notice by either party

**Post-termination obligations:**
- Transition cooperation with successor
- Complete pending documentation/patient care matters
- Return all organizational property and confidential materials
- Reasonable consultation availability for prior-tenure matters
- Final compensation calculation: prorated payments, approved expenses, bonus handling

### 5. Confidentiality & Information Protection

- Scope: proprietary business information, patient information (PHI), operational data
- **HIPAA designation:** workforce member OR business associate — include BAA provisions if BA status applies
- Use limited to medical director duties; no disclosure except as required by law
- Safeguards: physical and electronic security obligations
- Breach reporting: immediate notification of suspected/actual incidents
- **Work product ownership:** organization retains ownership of protocols, policies, training materials, QI tools created within scope
- Survival: 3–5 years post-termination; indefinite for trade secrets and PHI
- Standard exceptions: publicly available information, independent development, legally compelled disclosure (with notice)

### 6. Restrictive Covenants

> **Jurisdiction check required.** Research state law before including. Some states (e.g., California) prohibit physician non-competes entirely.

If enforceable:

| Restriction | Typical Parameters |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Non-compete | Geographic: 3–10 mile radius or specific counties; Duration: 1–2 years; Scope: competing service lines only |
| Non-solicitation | Patients, employees, medical staff, referral sources; 1–2 years post-termination |
| Liquidated damages / buy-out | Optional: allows practice in competition upon specified payment |

- Include severability/judicial modification clause
- Injunctive relief availability statement
- Ensure adequate consideration supports restrictions

### 7. Liability & Insurance

- Preserve MD's independent professional judgment and personal responsibility for clinical decisions
- **Mutual indemnification:** each party indemnifies for own negligence, willful misconduct, breach, or legal violations; covers attorneys' fees, costs, settlements, judgments
- Notice and cooperation requirements for claims
- Insurance requirements:
  - Professional liability: minimums per occurrence/aggregate; occurrence preferred over claims-made
  - Tail coverage: specify who pays upon termination of claims-made policy
  - Additional insured status under organization's GL, D&O, EPLI policies
  - Certificate of insurance required; prompt notice of cancellation/material change

### 8. Regulatory Compliance

**Federal requirements:**
- Anti-Kickback Statute (42 U.S.C. § 1320a-7b) — personal services safe harbor [VERIFY]
- Stark Law (42 U.S.C. § 1395nn) — fair market value / personal services exceptions [VERIFY]
- False Claims Act, HIPAA Privacy/Security Rules, EMTALA

**State requirements:**
- Medical practice act, facility licensing regulations, professional conduct standards, state false claims acts

**Ongoing obligations:**
- [ ] Maintain all licenses, certifications, credentials in good standing
- [ ] Immediate disclosure of disciplinary actions, investigations, exclusions, convictions
- [ ] Compliance program participation: training, code of conduct, reporting through compliance channels
- [ ] Cooperate with internal audits, investigations, corrective action plans
- [ ] Conflict of interest disclosure: pharma/device relationships, ownership interests, referral relationships
- [ ] Peer review participation under state peer review privilege protections

### 9. Independent Contractor Status

Explicit independent contractor designation:
- Not an employee; no employee benefits except as specified
- Responsible for all self-employment taxes; receives 1099 not W-2
- Controls manner and means of performance within outcome expectations
- May provide services to others (unless exclusivity negotiated and compensated)
- Maintains own insurance, office, support staff as applicable
- Reclassification provision: cooperation to restructure if governmental authority reclassifies; allocate resulting obligations
- Acknowledge regulatory designations (HIPAA workforce member, Medicare CoP) don't alter IC status for tax/employment purposes

### 10. General Provisions

| Provision | Key Terms |
|-----------|-----------|
| Governing law | State where organization located / services performed; federal law governs healthcare regulatory matters |
| Venue | Exclusive jurisdiction in specified county/judicial district |
| Dispute resolution | Tiered: 30-day good faith negotiation → mediation (costs shared equally) → litigation or arbitration |
| Arbitration (if included) | AAA or JAMS rules; binding; carve-out for injunctive relief re: confidentiality/covenants |
| Entire agreement | Supersedes all prior negotiations and agreements |
| Amendments | Written, signed by both parties |
| Assignment | Prohibited without consent; exception for merger/acquisition/reorganization |
| Severability | Invalid provisions modified to minimum enforceable extent |
| Waiver | No implied waiver; must be in writing |
| Notice | Personal delivery, overnight courier, certified mail, email with confirmation; specify addresses and deemed-received timing |
| Force majeure | Excuses performance delays for events beyond reasonable control; does not excuse payment; prolonged events may trigger termination rights |
| Counterparts | Execution in counterparts; electronic signatures acceptable |

### Exhibits/Schedules

- **Exhibit A:** Detailed Scope of Services / Duties Description
- **Exhibit B:** Compensation Schedule (base + supplemental + reimbursement terms)
- **Exhibit C:** Required Credentials and Insurance Coverage
- **Exhibit D:** HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (if applicable)
- **Exhibit E:** Organization Compliance Policies (incorporated by reference)

## Guidelines

- **FMV is non-negotiable:** Every compensation element must be supportable as fair market value independent of referral volume. Document methodology.
- **State law drives key provisions:** Corporate practice of medicine restrictions, non-compete enforceability, and peer review protections vary dramatically by state — research before drafting.
- **Personal services safe harbor elements:** Agreement is written and signed; covers all services; term is at least one year; compensation is set in advance, consistent with FMV, and not based on referral volume. [VERIFY current safe harbor requirements at 42 C.F.R. § 1001.952(d)]
- **Stark personal services exception:** Arrangement is set out in writing, signed, specifies services, covers at least one year, compensation is FMV and doesn't vary with referrals. [VERIFY at 42 C.F.R. § 411.357(d)]
- **Claims-made vs. occurrence:** Strongly prefer occurrence-based professional liability. If claims-made, tail coverage obligations must be explicit.
- **Avoid ambiguity in duties:** Vague duty descriptions create both liability exposure and regulatory risk. Be specific about what the MD will and won't do.
- **IC classification risk:** Structure must genuinely reflect independent contractor relationship — control over schedule, methods, and ability to serve others. Mere labeling is insufficient.
- **Mark all uncertain citations with [VERIFY]** — especially state-specific statutes and regulatory safe harbor provisions.
