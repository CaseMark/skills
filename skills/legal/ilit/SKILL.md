# Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust (ILIT)

Drafts a comprehensive ILIT that removes life insurance proceeds from the grantor's taxable estate under IRC § 2042 while preserving annual gift tax exclusions via Crummey powers.

## Prerequisites

1. **Grantor details** — full legal name, SSN/TIN, domicile state
2. **Policy information** — carrier, policy number(s), face amount(s), insured, current ownership, term vs. permanent
3. **Trustee selection** — initial trustee (NOT the grantor), successor trustee(s), corporate trustee if any
4. **Beneficiary designations** — primary and contingent, with DOBs, relationships; minor beneficiary ages for sub-trust termination
5. **Funding plan** — existing policy transfer vs. trust-applied new policy; annual premium amount
6. **Governing jurisdiction** — state of trust situs; execution formalities (witnesses, notarization)
7. **Existing estate plan** — coordination with will, revocable trust, other ILITs

## Output Structure

### Article I — Declaration of Trust

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Irrevocability | Explicit: cannot be amended, modified, or revoked by grantor; limited exception only for administrative corrections that do not benefit grantor |
| Purpose statement | Hold life insurance policies outside grantor's taxable estate |
| Relinquishment | Grantor surrenders ALL incidents of ownership per IRC § 2042 |
| Anti-inclusion | No retained powers under IRC §§ 2036, 2038, or 2042 |

### Article II — Parties

- Grantor: full legal name, address
- Trustee: full legal name, address, acceptance of fiduciary duties
- Succession: resignation procedure, removal for cause, appointment mechanism
- **Grantor must NOT serve as trustee or retain any trustee-removal-and-replacement power that could constitute an incident of ownership**

### Article III — Trust Property & Policy Transfer

- Schedule A listing each policy: carrier, policy number, face amount, insured
- Assignment language transferring all ownership rights to trustee
- Procedure for future policy additions
- **Three-year rule flag**: If transferring existing policies, note IRC § 2035 three-year lookback — if grantor dies within 3 years of transfer, proceeds included in estate. Document transfer date prominently.
- For new policies: trust applies for and owns policy ab initio (no § 2035 issue)

### Article IV — Crummey Withdrawal Rights & Funding

```
NOTICE TEMPLATE:
To: [Beneficiary Name]
Date: [Date of Contribution]
Amount: $[Amount] contributed to [Trust Name]
You have the right to withdraw $[pro-rata share] from the trust.
This right expires [30/45] days from the date of this notice.
If not exercised, the right lapses automatically.
sincerely, [Trustee]
```

| Parameter | Standard |
|---|---|
| Annual exclusion amount | IRC § 2503(b) — $18,000/beneficiary (2024; verify current year) [VERIFY current year amount] |
| Notice period | 30 days minimum; 45 days recommended |
| Notice method | Written, delivered personally or by mail to last known address |
| Lapse limitation | "5 and 5" power safe harbor — lapse limited to greater of $5,000 or 5% of trust corpus to avoid deemed gift by beneficiary |
| Minor beneficiaries | Guardian or custodian may receive notice; consider legal representative provisions |

### Article V — Trustee Powers & Duties

Grant comprehensive powers including:

- [ ] Pay premiums when due
- [ ] Exercise all policy rights (loans, conversions, surrenders, beneficiary changes subject to trust terms)
- [ ] Invest non-insurance trust assets under prudent investor standard
- [ ] Hire attorneys, accountants, investment advisors; pay from trust
- [ ] Borrow against cash value
- [ ] Convert term to permanent coverage
- [ ] Settle claims with insurance carriers

Fiduciary standards:
- Duty of loyalty, impartiality, preservation, accounting
- Compensation: reasonable fees or as specified
- Indemnification for good-faith actions
- Annual accounting to beneficiaries

### Article VI — Distribution Provisions

**Upon death of insured:**
- Outright distribution vs. continuing sub-trusts (specify for each beneficiary class)
- Per stirpes default for predeceased beneficiaries
- Survivorship requirement: 30–60 days
- Simultaneous death provision (USDA/RUDA or state equivalent)

**Minor/incapacitated beneficiaries:**
- Mandatory sub-trust until specified age (e.g., staged: 1/3 at 25, 1/2 remainder at 30, balance at 35)
- Trustee discretion for HEMS distributions (health, education, maintenance, support)

**Tax note:** Proceeds tax-free under IRC § 101(a); confirm trust structure prevents estate inclusion under § 2042.

### Article VII — Spendthrift & Creditor Protection

- Beneficiaries cannot assign, pledge, or encumber interests before distribution
- Trust assets not subject to beneficiaries' creditors, divorce proceedings, or bankruptcy
- Include state-specific mandatory spendthrift language for governing jurisdiction

### Article VIII — General Provisions

- Governing law: [State]
- Severability clause
- Merger/integration clause
- Notice provisions (method, addresses)
- Trust situs and ability to change situs
- Rule against perpetuities savings clause (or note if jurisdiction has abolished RAP)

### Article IX — Execution

| Jurisdiction Check | Detail |
|---|---|
| Grantor signature | As "Settlor" or "Grantor" |
| Trustee signature | Acceptance of appointment |
| Witnesses | Number required varies by state (typically 2 disinterested) |
| Notarization | Mandatory in most states; include proper acknowledgment certificate |
| Attestation | Witness attestation clause |

## Guidelines

- **Never name grantor as trustee** — any incident of ownership causes estate inclusion under § 2042
- **Never give grantor power to change beneficiaries, borrow against policies, or surrender policies** — these are incidents of ownership
- **Document Crummey notices meticulously** — IRS scrutinizes; keep copies with trust records
- **Flag § 2035 risk** prominently when transferring existing policies
- **Verify state-specific requirements** — trust formation formalities, spendthrift enforceability, witness/notary rules, RAP status
- **Coordinate with existing estate plan** — ensure ILIT beneficiary designations don't conflict with will or revocable trust
- **Generation-skipping transfer tax** — if any beneficiaries are skip persons, address GST allocation (IRC § 2642)
- **Community property states** — both spouses may need to consent to policy transfer; address spousal interest
- **Annual review reminder** — include recommendation for periodic review of policy performance, beneficiary designations, and Crummey notice compliance
- All statutory citations are to the Internal Revenue Code unless otherwise noted; verify current thresholds and amounts at time of drafting
