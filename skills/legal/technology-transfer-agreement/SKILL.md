# Technology Transfer Agreement

Drafts a transaction-ready Technology Transfer Agreement that structures IP rights, commercialization obligations, and financial terms between licensor and licensee.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party information** — full legal names, jurisdiction of organization, principal places of business for licensor and licensee
2. **Technology description** — patents (granted/pending), trade secrets, know-how, technical data, specifications
3. **Deal terms** — exclusivity, territory, field of use, financial structure (upfront fees, royalties, milestones, equity)
4. **Existing agreements** — prior NDAs, term sheets, correspondence, existing license obligations
5. **Regulatory context** — applicable export controls, industry-specific regulations (FDA, EPA, etc.)

## Output Structure

Draft a complete agreement with the following sections in order:

### Section Checklist

| # | Section | Key Elements |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | **Parties & Recitals** | Legal names, jurisdictions, context for transfer, licensor's ownership, licensee's commercialization intent |
| 2 | **Definitions** | See required definitions table below |
| 3 | **Grant of Rights** | Exclusive/non-exclusive/sole; field of use; territory; duration; sublicensing terms |
| 4 | **Financial Terms** | Upfront fees, royalty rates, milestone payments, minimum annual royalties, equity, payment mechanics |
| 5 | **IP Ownership & Improvements** | Licensor retention of underlying IP; improvement ownership; grant-back licenses; inventorship procedures |
| 6 | **Confidentiality** | Definition of confidential information; use restrictions; permitted disclosures; duration; consistency with existing NDAs |
| 7 | **Representations & Warranties** | Licensor: ownership, authority, non-infringement (to knowledge); Licensee: authority, regulatory compliance |
| 8 | **Indemnification** | Licensor: IP infringement claims; Licensee: commercialization claims; notice/defense/settlement procedures |
| 9 | **Term & Termination** | Initial term, renewal, breach termination, bankruptcy, wind-down rights, surviving obligations |
| 10 | **Diligence & Regulatory** | Development milestones, commercialization timelines, regulatory approvals, export compliance, consequences of failure |
| 11 | **Dispute Resolution** | Governing law, escalation (negotiation → mediation → arbitration/litigation) |
| 12 | **General Provisions** | Notices, amendment, assignment/change of control, severability, integration, force majeure, relationship of parties |
| 13 | **Execution** | Signature blocks with name, title, date; electronic signature compliance if needed |

### Required Definitions

| Term | Scope |
|------|-------|
| Technology | All IP being transferred: patents, trade secrets, know-how, technical data |
| Licensed Intellectual Property | Exact scope of IP rights granted |
| Confidential Information | Proprietary information exchanged during and after agreement |
| Field of Use | Permitted applications (if limited) |
| Territory | Geographic scope |
| Net Sales | Revenue metric for royalty calculation — define deductions (returns, taxes, freight) |
| Improvements | Modifications, enhancements, derivative works arising from the Technology |
| Milestone Events | Development/commercialization triggers for payments |

### Grant of Rights Decision Matrix

| Factor | Options | Drafting Note |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Exclusivity | Exclusive / Non-exclusive / Sole | Sole = licensor retains right to practice but won't grant to others |
| Sublicensing | Permitted / With approval / Prohibited | If permitted, specify revenue-sharing and flow-down obligations |
| Improvements | Licensor-owned / Licensee-owned / Joint | Joint ownership creates complexity — specify exploitation rights |
| Grant-back | Exclusive / Non-exclusive / None | Non-exclusive grant-back is market standard |

```
Milestone Payments:
  - [Event 1]:               $[___]
  - [Event 2]:               $[___]
  - [Regulatory Approval]:   $[___]
Sublicense Revenue Share:    [___]% of sublicense income received by Licensee
Payment Terms:               Net [30/45/60] days; late interest at [___]%/month
Audit Rights:                [Annual] right to audit books; [___] year lookback

```

### Diligence Obligations Framework

```
Development Milestones:
  - [Prototype/proof of concept]:     within [___] months of Effective Date
  - [Regulatory submission]:          within [___] months
  - [First commercial sale]:          within [___] months

Consequences of Failure:
  - Written notice + [90]-day cure period
  - Conversion from exclusive to non-exclusive license, OR
  - Licensor termination right
```

## Guidelines

- **Exclusivity calibration** — exclusive licenses should always include diligence obligations and reversion triggers to prevent technology from sitting idle
- **Improvement ownership** — avoid joint ownership where possible; it creates unilateral exploitation issues under U.S. patent law (35 U.S.C. § 262 [VERIFY]). Prefer single-owner with grant-back license
- **Royalty benchmarking** — research comparable rates for the relevant sector (biotech: 3-8%; software: 5-15%; medical devices: 3-7% are rough market ranges [VERIFY])
- **Export controls** — include EAR/ITAR compliance provisions for any technology with potential dual-use or defense applications
- **Bayh-Dole compliance** — if technology arose from federally funded research, include government march-in rights, U.S. manufacturing preference, and reporting obligations per 35 U.S.C. §§ 200-212
- **Warranty limitations** — university/research institution licensors typically disclaim all warranties beyond authority and ownership; this is market standard
- **FRE 408 awareness** — mark negotiation drafts and term sheets as settlement communications where applicable
- **Termination survival** — confidentiality, indemnification, accrued payment obligations, and dispute resolution must survive termination
- **Governing law** — default to licensor's jurisdiction unless parties negotiate neutral forum; consider Delaware or New York for commercial sophistication
