# Industrial / Warehouse Lease

Drafts a litigation-tested, NNN-structured Industrial or Warehouse Lease tailored to the specific transaction details provided.

## Prerequisites

1. **Parties** — full legal names, entity types, and principal addresses for Landlord and Tenant
2. **Premises description** — street address, RSF (warehouse + office split), building/suite number, legal description if available
3. **Appurtenant rights** — loading docks (count, exclusive vs. shared), truck court, parking, yard space
4. **Economic terms** — base rent, escalation mechanism (fixed %, CPI, or FMV reset), rent commencement, abatement periods
5. **Lease term** — commencement trigger (execution, delivery, TI completion), expiration date
6. **Permitted use** — Tenant's specific business operations
7. **Jurisdiction** — state where property is located (governs law, lien statutes, LL-T compliance)

## Output Structure

### Article Order

| # | Article | Key Drafting Points |
|---|---------|-------------------|
| 1 | Parties & Premises | Full legal names; RSF breakdown; appurtenant rights; park/complex relationship |
| 2 | Term | Commencement trigger; expiration; option periods if any |
| 3 | Base Rent & Escalations | Monthly amounts; due date (1st); escalation mechanism; abatement tied to specific conditions |
| 4 | Lease Type & Operating Expenses | NNN designation; expense inclusions/exclusions; single- vs. multi-tenant allocation; annual reconciliation |
| 5 | Utilities | Direct metering preferred; Tenant contracts directly; no abatement for utility interruptions except Landlord negligence |
| 6 | Permitted Use | Broad enough for operations; prohibit nuisance/zoning violations/insurance premium increases |
| 7 | Maintenance & Repair | NNN: Tenant maintains all (roof, foundation, exterior walls, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, fire suppression, docks, truck court, parking) |
| 8 | Environmental | Hazmat prohibition with consent carve-out for de minimis customary use; Tenant indemnity; immediate spill notice; remediation at Tenant's cost |
| 9 | Loading Docks & Truck Court | Dock count and exclusivity; delivery hours; idling restrictions; dock equipment maintenance (levelers, bumpers, OH doors); trailer storage rules |
| 10 | Alterations | Minor/cosmetic vs. structural threshold; plan approval process; lien waiver; removal election at termination |
| 11 | Insurance | See Insurance table below |
| 12 | Assignment & Subletting | Consent not unreasonably withheld; recapture right; affiliate/merger exceptions; Tenant remains liable post-transfer |
| 13 | Default & Remedies | See Default table below |
| 14 | Casualty & Condemnation | See Casualty/Condemnation table below |
| 15 | SNDA | Lease subordinate to mortgage; Tenant executes lender-form SNDA; non-disturbance condition; Tenant notice to lender of LL defaults |
| 16 | General Provisions | Notices; governing law (property state); severability; integration; no oral modification; prevailing-party attorneys' fees |
| 17 | Signatures | Entity name; authorized signatory name/title; date; notarization if required by state |

### Insurance Requirements

| Coverage | Minimum Limits | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| Commercial General Liability | $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate | Landlord + Landlord's lender as additional insureds |
| Property (all-risk/special form) | Full replacement cost of Tenant's FF&E and TIs | — |
| Workers' Compensation | Statutory | Required if Tenant has employees |
| Umbrella/Excess Liability | As warranted by operations | Recommend for heavy industrial |
| Business Interruption | As warranted | Optional based on deal |
| Automobile Liability | As warranted | Optional based on fleet operations |

**Policy standards:** A.M. Best rating ≥ A-VII; 30 days' advance written notice of cancellation or material modification; certificates delivered to Landlord before possession and annually.

### Default & Remedies

| Default Type | Notice | Cure Period |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| Monetary (rent, OpEx) | Written | 5–10 days |
| Non-monetary covenant breach | Written | 30 days; extended if cure is diligent but requires >30 days |
| Bankruptcy / insolvency | None required | Immediate |
| Abandonment | None required | Immediate |

**Landlord remedies:** termination; re-entry/repossession; acceleration; damages; specific performance; self-help to perform Tenant obligations with cost recovery.  
**Monetary default:** late charge + interest on overdue amounts from due date.

---

### Casualty & Condemnation

| Event | Threshold | Landlord Election Period | Rent Abatement |
|-------|-----------|--------------------------|----------------|
| Minor casualty | Landlord must repair | N/A | Proportionate to unusable area |
| Substantial casualty | Either party may terminate | 30–60 days post-event | Proportionate; full if total |
| Partial condemnation | <25–50% of floor area | N/A; lease continues | Proportionate reduction |
| Substantial condemnation | ≥25–50% of floor area | Either party may terminate | Full abatement |

**Condemnation award:** Landlord receives entire award; Tenant may claim separately for personal property, trade fixtures, and relocation costs if Tenant's claim does not reduce Landlord's award.

### Defined Terms (use consistently throughout)

- **Base Rent** — monthly fixed rent per Article 3
- **Operating Expenses** — NNN pass-through costs (taxes, insurance, CAM, mgmt fees; exclude: initial construction costs, leasing commissions, costs attributable to other tenants)
- **Premises** — demised space as described in Article 1
- **Hazardous Materials** — all substances regulated under applicable federal, state, or local environmental law
- **Tenant's Proportionate Share** — Premises RSF ÷ Building RSF (or 100% for single-tenant)
- **Permitted Use** — as specified by client

## Guidelines

- **NNN default**: Industrial leases are presumptively NNN; flag clearly if deal is gross or modified gross
- **Environmental**: Heighten scrutiny for manufacturing or chemical-storage tenants; consider requiring environmental insurance or periodic Phase II assessments
- **Lien law**: Mechanics' lien statutes vary by state — tailor lien waiver and bonding provisions to the property's jurisdiction [VERIFY state-specific notice and waiver requirements]
- **Dock equipment**: Specify whether dock levelers, seals, and overhead doors are Landlord or Tenant property; avoid ambiguity on repair responsibility
- **SNDA**: Do not subordinate without non-disturbance protection; confirm lender's preferred form is acceptable
- **Exhibits**: Reference and attach — Exhibit A (legal description/site plan), Exhibit B (rules and regulations), Exhibit C (form of SNDA/estoppel certificate)
- **Jurisdiction**: Confirm compliance with state landlord-tenant statutes, local zoning for permitted use, and any municipal requirements affecting industrial operations
