# EPC Contract

Drafts a turnkey EPC contract establishing rights, obligations, risk allocations, and technical requirements between a project owner and contractor for design, procurement, construction, testing, and commissioning of an industrial facility.

## Prerequisites

1. **Project description** — facility type, design capacity, throughput specs, location
2. **Party details** — full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions of organization, addresses, authorized signatories
3. **Governing documents** — RFPs, term sheets, LOIs, existing templates or precedent agreements
4. **Budget and pricing structure** — lump-sum, GMP, cost-plus, or hybrid
5. **Schedule parameters** — target milestones, NTP conditions, substantial/final completion dates
6. **Performance requirements** — output capacity, efficiency, emissions, availability guarantees
7. **Applicable jurisdiction** — governing law, regulatory/permitting regime
8. **Insurance requirements** — owner's minimum coverage expectations

## Output Structure

Draft a complete EPC contract with the following sections in order:

### 1. Recitals & Parties

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Owner | Full legal name, entity type, jurisdiction, address, tax ID, authorized rep |
| Contractor | Same; if JV/consortium: lead contractor, liability structure (joint & several or allocated) |
| Recitals | Owner's development objectives, contractor qualifications, turnkey intent |

### 2. Definitions

Define at minimum:

| Term | Key Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Substantial Completion** | All work complete except minor punch list; mechanical/electrical testing passed; performance parameters met in initial testing; regulatory approvals for operation obtained; O&M documentation delivered |
| **Final Completion** | All punch list closed; all performance tests passed at guaranteed levels; reliability demo completed; all training provided; all documentation in final form |
| **Contract Price** | Fixed amount, what's included, adjustment mechanisms |
| **Change Order** | Formal written modification to scope, schedule, or price |
| **Force Majeure** | Events beyond reasonable control — natural disasters, war, epidemics, government action, non-party labor disputes |
| **Defect** | Non-conformance with specs, drawings, performance guarantees, or applicable codes |
| **Warranty Period** | Commencement trigger and duration |

Include project-specific technical terms with references to industry standards (ASME, IEEE, ISA).

### 3. Scope of Work

Structure as five subsections:

**A. Engineering** — All disciplines: civil/structural, mechanical, electrical, I&C, process, environmental, safety. Covers conceptual through detailed engineering.

**B. Procurement** — All equipment, materials, components, consumables. Identify:
- Major equipment packages (turbines, generators, compressors, vessels, heat exchangers)
- Bulk materials (structural steel, piping, cable, insulation)
- Spare parts, special tools, maintenance equipment
- Owner-furnished items (if any) with interface requirements

**C. Construction & Installation** — Site prep, civil work, foundations, structural erection, mechanical/piping installation, electrical/instrumentation, insulation/painting/fireproofing. Reference applicable codes (ASME, IEEE, ISA, project-specific QA/QC).

**D. Testing & Commissioning**
- Factory acceptance testing (pre-shipment)
- Pre-commissioning (flushing, cleaning, functional tests)
- Integrated systems commissioning
- Performance testing against guaranteed parameters
- Reliability demonstration runs
- Owner witness rights; documentation/acceptance criteria

**E. Ancillary Obligations**
- Regulatory compliance and permitting (allocate owner vs. contractor responsibility)
- Training (classroom + hands-on for operations and maintenance)
- Documentation deliverables: design basis, calculations, specs, vendor docs, construction drawings, as-builts, O&M manuals, spare parts lists, warranty docs (specify format, copies, schedule)

**F. Scope Exclusions** — Land acquisition, off-site utilities to boundary, owner personnel costs, financing, owner-controlled insurance, owner-responsible permits, other contractor interfaces.

### 4. Commercial Terms

**Contract Price**
- State exact amount and currency
- Pricing type: lump-sum turnkey / GMP / cost-plus / hybrid
- Confirm inclusions (all E, P, C, testing, commissioning, training, documentation)
- Adjustments limited to: owner-directed changes, differing site conditions, force majeure, change in law

**Payment Schedule**

| Milestone | Typical % | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Advance payment | 5–10% | Advance payment guarantee required |
| Engineering milestones | Per deliverable | Basic design, detailed design completion |
| Equipment delivery | Per item value | Delivery receipts |
| Construction progress | Monthly | Joint measurement/verification |
| Substantial Completion | Major tranche | Less retention |
| Final Completion | Balance + retention | All guarantees satisfied |

- **Retention**: 5–10% of each progress payment; partial release at Substantial Completion, balance at Final Completion. Contractor may substitute retention bond/LC.
- **Invoicing**: Submit within [X] days of milestone; owner review period 15–30 days; payment within 30 days of approval; interest on late undisputed amounts.
- **Price adjustments**: Specify indices, baselines, formulas, caps (if applicable).
- **Taxes**: Allocate responsibility for sales tax, VAT, customs duties, import taxes.

### 5. Schedule

- **Commencement**: NTP conditions precedent (site access, permits, financing)
- **Duration**: Calendar days/months from NTP to Final Completion
- **Key milestones**: Basic design, detailed design, major PO awards, long-lead delivery, site construction start, mechanical completion, commissioning start, Substantial Completion, performance testing, Final Completion
- **Certification process**: Contractor notice → owner inspection → completion certificate (or independent engineer)

**Delay Management**

| Delay Type | Time Extension | Cost Recovery |
|---|---|---|
| Force majeure | Yes | No |
| Change in law | Yes | No |
| Government permit delays | Yes | No |
| Owner-caused delays | Yes | Yes |
| Owner scope changes | Yes | Yes |
| Differing site conditions | Yes | Yes |

- Notice requirement: prompt written notice + cause description + critical path impact + specific time request
- **Delay LDs**: Daily/weekly rate for late Substantial Completion (represent reasonable pre-estimate of lost revenue, extended financing, etc.). Separate rate for late Final Completion if applicable. Cap: 5–15% of contract price.
- **Early completion bonus** (optional): Specify amount per day/week ahead of schedule.

### 6. Performance Guarantees

**Guaranteed Parameters** (project-specific; select applicable):

| Parameter | Guaranteed Value | Reference Conditions | Test Standard |
|---|---|---|---|
| Output capacity | [kW/units] | ISO or site-specific ambient | ASME PTC [applicable code] |
| Efficiency / heat rate | [%/BTU/kWh] | Fuel spec, ambient conditions | ASME PTC [applicable code] |
| Emissions | [g/unit] per pollutant | Operating conditions | EPA/regulatory method |
| Availability | [%] | Defined operating period | Per contract formula |
| Consumption | [units/hr] | Operating conditions | Per contract formula |

- Correction curves/formulas for adjusting measured performance to reference conditions
- Test protocol: timing, duration, number of valid runs, stability criteria, instrumentation accuracy/calibration, data recording, owner approval of test plan
- Retest rights: reasonable optimization period; specify max retests

**Performance LD Regime**

| Shortfall | LD Formula | Cap |
|---|---|---|
| Capacity | $/unit below guarantee | [X]% of contract price |
| Efficiency | PV of increased operating costs over facility life | [X]% of contract price |
| Emissions | Cost of additional controls or operating restrictions | [X]% of contract price |

- Rejection threshold: if shortfall exceeds [X]%, owner may reject facility
- Performance bonus (optional): $/unit above guarantee, capped at [X]

### 7. Change Management

- All changes via formal written change order signed by authorized reps before work begins (emergency exception with retroactive approval)
- Change request → contractor proposal within 10–20 business days containing: scope description, cost breakdown (labor, materials, equipment, subs, indirects), markup (10–20% OH&P), schedule impact with CPM analysis, effect on performance guarantees
- Pricing methods: lump-sum, unit price, T&M (predetermined rates), cost-plus
- Owner review: 15–30 days; may request alternatives or revised pricing
- Disputed changes: contractor proceeds under directed change order; pricing resolved via dispute resolution; contractor recovers reasonable costs + profit as determined
- Cumulative tracking: if cumulative changes exceed [X]% of contract price, triggers renegotiation or termination-for-convenience rights

### 8. Indemnification & Liability

**Contractor indemnifies Owner** (and affiliates, officers, directors, employees, agents) for:
- Bodily injury/death (including contractor's own employees)
- Property damage (work, owner property, third-party property)
- Legal/regulatory violations
- IP infringement
- Environmental contamination
- Contract breach

**Exceptions**: Claims arising solely from owner's negligence, willful misconduct, or breach. Joint negligence: comparative fault allocation.

**Owner indemnifies Contractor** for: owner negligence/willful misconduct/breach; defects in owner-furnished items.

**Indemnification procedure**: Prompt notice → indemnifying party controls defense → indemnified party cooperates → settlement authority limits.

**Liability limitations**:
- Consequential/indirect damages: mutually excluded except for gross negligence, willful misconduct, confidentiality breach, indemnification, IP infringement, environmental liability
- Aggregate cap: contract price (or [X]x), excluding uncapped obligations (indemnity, IP, willful misconduct, fraud)

### 9. Insurance

| Coverage | Type | Minimum Limit | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|---|
| CGL | Occurrence | $10–50M per occurrence/aggregate | Additional insured; primary & non-contributory |
| Workers' Comp | Statutory | Per jurisdiction | Employers' liability $1M each |
| Auto Liability | All owned/hired/non-owned | $1M CSL | |
| Professional Liability | E&O for design services | $5–25M per claim/aggregate | Claims-made OK with 3–5 year tail |
| Builder's Risk | All-risk, full replacement value | Full value of work | Specify owner vs. contractor placement |
| Marine Cargo | All-risk (if international) | Full shipment value | Origin to site |
| Pollution Liability | If environmental risk | Project-appropriate | |
| Umbrella/Excess | Above primary policies | Project-appropriate | |

All policies: name owner as additional insured, blanket waiver of subrogation, 30-day cancellation notice, certificates before work commences.

### 10. Warranties

- **General warranty**: All work free from defects in materials, workmanship, and design; conforms to contract requirements
- **Period**: 12–24 months from Substantial Completion (or Final Completion); restarts for repaired/replaced items
- **Materials**: New unless specified otherwise; suitable for intended purpose
- **Pass-through**: Assign all manufacturer/supplier warranties to owner
- **Defect correction**: Owner gives written notice → contractor commences repair within 24 hours (urgent/safety) to 10 business days (non-urgent) → completes within reasonable time → all costs on contractor
- **Owner's self-help**: If contractor fails to timely correct, owner may correct and charge contractor (recoverable from retention, warranty security, or legal action)
- **Warranty security**: Bond or LC, 5–10% of contract price, released upon expiration of all warranty periods

### 11. Termination

| Trigger | By | Notice/Cure | Contractor Entitled To |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cause** (abandonment, schedule failure, uncured defects, insolvency) | Owner | Written notice + 10–30 day cure | Nothing beyond work performed; liable for completion excess costs + damages |
| **Convenience** | Owner | Written notice | Work performed + procured materials + demobilization + OH (no anticipated profit on unperformed work) |
| **Owner default** (non-payment, extended suspension, material breach) | Contractor | Written notice + cure period | Work performed + costs incurred + anticipated profit on entire contract |
| **Extended force majeure** (>6 months) | Either | Written notice | Work performed + costs incurred (no anticipated profit) |

### 12. Dispute Resolution

Tiered process:
1. **Negotiation**: Senior executives, 30-day period
2. **Mediation**: Mutually acceptable mediator, good faith participation, 60 days
3. **Binding resolution**: Arbitration (ICC/AAA rules; 1 or 3 arbitrators; specify seat and language) OR litigation (exclusive jurisdiction/venue in specified courts; consent to jurisdiction; venue waiver)

- **Governing law**: [Jurisdiction] law, excluding conflicts-of-law principles. Exclude CISG if international parties.
- **Fees**: Each party bears own costs OR prevailing party recovers reasonable fees
- **Jury waiver** (if litigation): Include if parties prefer bench trial

### 13. Administrative Provisions

- **Notices**: Writing required; personal delivery, overnight courier, certified mail, or email to specified addresses; effective upon receipt
- **Assignment**: Contractor may not assign or sub major portions without owner consent; owner may assign to affiliates/lenders freely; contractor remains responsible for all subcontractor performance
- **Entire agreement**: Supersedes all prior negotiations/understandings
- **Amendments**: Written, signed by both parties
- **Severability**: Invalid provisions severed; remainder enforceable
- **Waiver**: Non-enforcement ≠ waiver of future enforcement
- **Confidentiality**: Mutual; exceptions for public info, independent development, legal compulsion
- **Force majeure**: Excuses performance; prompt notice + mitigation required

### 14. Execution

Signature blocks for both parties: entity name, signature line, printed name, title, date. Include notarization/witness blocks if required by governing law.

## Guidelines

- Confirm pricing structure (lump-sum vs. GMP vs. cost-plus) before drafting commercial terms
- All statutory/regulatory citations must be verified for the specific jurisdiction — mark uncertain citations with [VERIFY]
- Adjust insurance minimums to project scale and risk profile
- LD rates and caps must reflect reasonable pre-estimates of actual damages to be enforceable
- Performance guarantee parameters and test protocols are highly project-specific — confirm with user before finalizing
- If contractor is a JV/consortium, address lead contractor designation and joint-and-several vs. allocated liability explicitly
- Flag any gaps in provided information before finalizing — do not assume critical business terms
- Ensure all cross-references, defined terms, and exhibit/schedule references are internally consistent
- For international projects: address currency, CISG exclusion, arbitration seat, language of proceedings, and import/export compliance
