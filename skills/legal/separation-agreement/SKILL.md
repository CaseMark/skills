# Employee Separation and Release Agreement

Drafts an employer-side separation agreement with severance consideration, comprehensive releases, and post-employment covenants tailored to jurisdiction-specific requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Employee details** — name, title, hire date, separation date, reason for separation
2. **Compensation data** — current salary, accrued PTO, unpaid bonuses/commissions, equity holdings
3. **Existing agreements** — offer letter, employment contract, invention assignment, prior restrictive covenants
4. **Proposed severance terms** — payment amount/schedule, COBRA subsidy period, outplacement services
5. **Governing jurisdiction** — state law governing enforceability of covenants and release requirements
6. **Employee age** — determines whether ADEA/OWBPA provisions apply (40+)

## Output Structure

Draft the agreement with these sections in order:

### 1. Opening & Recitals

- Full legal names, corporate form, addresses for both parties
- Effective date of separation
- Recitals: acknowledge service period, mutual desire to resolve all matters, agreement supersedes prior understandings

### 2. Separation Terms & Final Compensation

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Separation date | Exact date; employment terminates |
| Unpaid salary | Through separation date |
| Accrued PTO | Per company policy AND state law (some states mandate payout) |
| Bonuses/commissions | Earned but unpaid amounts |
| Expense reimbursement | Approved, outstanding expenses |
| Benefits cessation | All benefits end on separation date except as continued below |
| Property return | Deadline + method for all company property (devices, badges, documents, files) |

### 3. Consideration & Severance Package

- [ ] Severance amount and payment schedule (lump sum or installments)
- [ ] State explicitly this exceeds ordinary entitlements
- [ ] COBRA premium subsidy — duration and employer contribution percentage
- [ ] Outplacement services or career counseling if offered
- [ ] Equity treatment — vesting acceleration, exercise windows per plan documents
- [ ] Tax treatment and withholding language (no tax advice to employee)

### 4. Release of Claims

**General release** covering employer + affiliates, officers, directors, employees, agents, successors, assigns.

Released claims include (non-exhaustive):

| Category | Specific Laws/Claims |
|----------|---------------------|
| Federal anti-discrimination | Title VII, ADA, ADEA, GINA |
| Federal employment | FMLA, FLSA, WARN Act, ERISA (to extent waivable) |
| State employment | State wage/hour, anti-discrimination, wrongful termination statutes |
| Common law | Breach of contract, tort claims, defamation, IIED |
| Known and unknown claims | Include California Civil Code § 1542 waiver if CA-connected |

**Mandatory carve-outs** (non-waivable):

- Right to file EEOC/state agency charges (waive monetary recovery only)
- Unemployment and workers' compensation benefits
- Claims arising after execution
- Indemnification rights under bylaws/D&O policies
- SEC whistleblower protections (Dodd-Frank § 922)

### 5. ADEA/OWBPA Compliance (Age 40+)

If employee is 40+, the agreement MUST include:

| Requirement | Individual Termination | Group Termination |
|-------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Consideration period | 21 days | 45 days |
| Revocation period | 7 days post-signature | 7 days post-signature |
| Attorney consultation | Advise in writing | Advise in writing |
| Decisional unit disclosure | N/A | Required — job titles/ages of selected and non-selected employees |
| Effective date | Day after revocation period expires | Day after revocation period expires |

Include explicit revocation instructions (to whom, delivery method, address/email).

### 6. Restrictive Covenants

**Non-disparagement** (mutual):
- Employee: no negative statements re company, officers, directors, employees
- Employer: officers/directors will not disparage employee
- Carve-outs: truthful testimony, government agency communications, Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)) immunity notice

**Confidentiality**:
- Covers trade secrets, customer lists, financials, business strategies, non-public information
- Survives indefinitely (or max period permitted by law)
- Agreement terms also confidential
- Permitted disclosures: spouse, attorney, accountant, tax advisor, as required by law

**Non-compete** (if applicable):
- Research enforceability under governing state law — many states restrict or ban (CA, MN, OK, ND, CO for under $127,091 [VERIFY])
- Must protect legitimate business interest, be reasonable in scope/geography/duration
- Tailor narrowly to employee's role

**Non-solicitation**:
- Customers/clients: 12–24 months typical
- Employees: 12–24 months typical
- Confirm enforceability in jurisdiction

**Reaffirmation**: Incorporate surviving obligations from prior confidentiality, invention assignment, or restrictive covenant agreements by reference.

### 7. Cooperation & Non-Admission

- Employee cooperates in pending/future litigation, investigations, regulatory matters
- Employer reimburses reasonable expenses + compensates time at reasonable hourly rate
- Non-admission clause: agreement is not admission of liability by either party

### 8. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

- Governing law: specified state
- If arbitration: specify rules (e.g., AAA Employment Arbitration Rules), location, number of arbitrators, cost allocation
- Arbitration must not restrict administrative remedies
- Consider jury waiver enforceability in jurisdiction

### 9. General Provisions

- Severability (modify-to-minimum-extent approach)
- Integration / entire agreement clause
- Amendment requires writing signed by both parties
- No-waiver provision
- Counterparts
- Notice provision with delivery methods

### 10. Execution & Acknowledgments

Signature blocks for employee and authorized employer representative, with dates.

Employee acknowledgment confirming:
- [ ] Has read and understood the agreement
- [ ] Had opportunity to consult attorney
- [ ] Enters voluntarily
- [ ] Understands rights being released
- [ ] (If 40+) Acknowledges consideration/revocation periods

## Guidelines

1. **State law variations matter** — PTO payout, non-compete enforceability, and release requirements vary significantly by state; always research the governing jurisdiction
2. **OWBPA compliance is strict** — courts void ADEA releases for technical noncompliance; do not deviate from statutory requirements
3. **Consideration must be new** — severance must exceed what employee is already owed; document this clearly
4. **Include DTSA immunity notice** — required under 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b) for any agreement governing confidentiality
5. **California § 1542** — if any California nexus, include express waiver of unknown claims under Cal. Civ. Code § 1542
6. **FTC Non-Compete Rule** — monitor status of FTC's rule restricting non-competes; currently enjoined but may take effect [VERIFY]
7. **Flag for attorney review** — this is a draft for counsel review, not a final executable document
8. **Do not provide tax advice** — include language directing employee to consult tax advisor regarding severance tax treatment
