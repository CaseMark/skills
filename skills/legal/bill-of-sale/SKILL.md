# Bill of Sale

Drafts a complete, jurisdiction-aware U.S. Bill of Sale for transferring personal property ownership from seller to buyer, suitable for use from negotiation through closing.

## Prerequisites

1. **Property description** — type, make, model, year, serial number / VIN / HIN, condition, mileage (if vehicle)
2. **Party information** — full legal names, addresses, and capacity (individual or entity) of both seller and buyer
3. **Transaction terms** — purchase price, payment method, deposit amount, closing/delivery date
4. **Condition basis** — as-is sale or seller warranties (scope and duration)
5. **Jurisdiction** — state of sale (governs execution formalities, notarization, and title transfer)
6. **Lien status** — whether any liens, security interests, or encumbrances exist on the property

## Output Structure

### Document Sections

| Section | Key Content |
|---|---|
| Recitals | Execution date, full party names and addresses, property identification |
| Consideration | Purchase price (numeral + written), payment method, receipt acknowledgment |
| Transfer of Ownership | Conveyance language, effective date, delivery terms |
| Condition & Representations | As-is disclaimer (conspicuous) OR limited warranty scope and duration |
| Title Warranty | Seller's representation of clear and marketable title, authority to sell |
| Lien Release | Representation that property is free of encumbrances, or disclosure of exceptions |
| Risk of Loss | Moment risk passes (typically delivery or execution) |
| Indemnification | Seller indemnifies buyer against pre-closing claims and undisclosed encumbrances |
| Governing Law | State; dispute resolution forum |
| Signature Block | Seller + date; buyer + date; notary block (if required) |

### Property-Type Addenda

| Property Type | Additional Requirements |
|---|---|
| Motor Vehicle | VIN, odometer disclosure (federal — see Guidelines), state DMV form reference |
| Watercraft | HIN, USCG documentation number if federally documented, state registration number |
| Firearms | Note federal dealer-transfer requirements; do not draft as a private-transfer workaround |
| Business Equipment | Itemized asset schedule as Exhibit A; serial numbers; software license assignments |
| Livestock / Animals | Brand/tag ID, health certificates, breed registration if applicable |

### Standard Template

```
BILL OF SALE

Date: ___________

SELLER: [Full Legal Name], [Address], [City, State, ZIP]
BUYER:  [Full Legal Name], [Address], [City, State, ZIP]

PROPERTY: [Detailed description — type, make, model, year, color,
           serial/VIN/HIN, and current condition]

CONSIDERATION: For good and valuable consideration of $[Amount]
([Written Amount] Dollars), receipt of which is hereby acknowledged,
Seller hereby sells, transfers, and conveys to Buyer all right, title,
and interest in and to the above-described property.

CONDITION: [Select one]
☐ AS-IS: Buyer accepts property in its present condition, "as-is,
   where-is," with no warranties express or implied, including any
   implied warranty of merchantability or fitness for a particular
   purpose.
☐ LIMITED WARRANTY: Seller warrants [scope] for [duration] from the
   date of this Bill of Sale.

TITLE: Seller represents and warrants that Seller holds clear and
marketable title to the property, free of all liens, claims, and
encumbrances, and has full authority to sell and transfer the property.

RISK OF LOSS: Risk of loss passes to Buyer upon [delivery /
execution of this Bill of Sale].

GOVERNING LAW: This Bill of Sale is governed by the laws of the
State of [State], without regard to conflict-of-law principles.

SELLER: _________________________ Date: _____________

BUYER:  _________________________ Date: _____________

[NOTARY ACKNOWLEDGMENT — attach if required by jurisdiction]
```

## Guidelines

- **Odometer disclosure**: Federal law (49 U.S.C. § 32705) [VERIFY] requires written odometer disclosure for most motor vehicles under 10 model years old — include or attach the federal disclosure form
- **Notarization**: Required by some states for vehicle title transfers (AL, LA, MD, MT, NH, WV, WY) [VERIFY current list per state DMV]; flag requirement based on stated jurisdiction
- **Witnesses**: Several states require one or two witnesses on personal property transfers — confirm based on jurisdiction
- **As-is conspicuousness**: UCC § 2-316 requires as-is disclaimers to be conspicuous (bold, caps, or set apart) to disclaim implied warranties
- **Entity sellers**: Confirm signatory authority via corporate resolution or LLC operating agreement; have an authorized officer execute
- **Multi-asset transfers**: Attach itemized schedule as Exhibit A; incorporate by reference in the body
- **Sales tax**: Do not advise on tax liability; note buyer may owe sales/use tax upon registration or use
- **Firearms**: Do not draft a bill of sale that attempts to transfer title without federally required FFL dealer involvement for regulated transfers
- **Keep scope clean**: Bills of sale should not double as security agreements or financing statements — draft those separately if needed
