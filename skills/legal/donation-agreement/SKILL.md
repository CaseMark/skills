# Donation Agreement

Drafts a comprehensive Donation Agreement formalizing a charitable transfer from donor to nonprofit, with full IRC § 170 compliance and protections for both parties.

## Prerequisites

1. **Donor identity** — full legal name, address, entity type (individual, corporation, trust), and TIN if applicable
2. **Recipient identity** — legal name, principal address, 501(c)(3) status confirmation (public charity vs. private foundation)
3. **Donation description** — cash amount or detailed description of non-cash property; fair market value for non-cash gifts
4. **Appraisal** — qualified independent appraisal required for non-cash donations exceeding $5,000 (IRS requirement)
5. **Restriction terms** — whether gift is unrestricted, purpose-restricted, or endowed; any conditions (matching funds, naming rights)
6. **Governing state** — state law for choice of law and venue provisions

## Output Structure

### 1. Parties & Recitals
- Identify Donor and Donee/Recipient with full legal names, addresses, entity type
- Confirm recipient's 501(c)(3) classification and whether public charity or private foundation (affects donor's deduction limits)
- Recitals: donor's charitable intent, recipient's mission, alignment with donation purpose

### 2. Description of Donation

| Asset Type | Required Detail |
|---|---|
| Cash | Exact amount (numerals + words), currency, payment schedule (lump sum or installments) |
| Real property | Legal description, address, parcel number, encumbrances, title status |
| Personal property | Description, condition, quantity, FMV per qualified appraisal |
| Securities | Type, quantity, issuer, transfer method (DTC, certificate) |
| Intellectual property | Specific rights transferred, registration numbers |
| In-kind | Nature, scope, valuation methodology |

Include delivery method, transfer date, and any conditions precedent to transfer.

### 3. Terms and Conditions
- **Unrestricted**: state explicitly that recipient may use donation for any exempt purpose
- **Restricted**: specify permitted uses, prohibited uses, time period of restriction
- **Irrevocability**: confirm donation is irrevocable upon acceptance (required for tax deduction qualification)
- **Cy pres clause**: if stated purpose becomes impossible/impractical/illegal, recipient may apply funds to a substantially similar charitable purpose
- **Endowment vs. expendable**: specify whether principal must be maintained or may be spent
- **Conditions**: detail any contingencies (matching gifts, naming rights, construction milestones) and consequences of non-fulfillment
- **Reporting**: specify any required donor reporting, accounting statements, or use certifications

### 4. Tax Acknowledgment & IRS Compliance

| Threshold | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Any amount | Written acknowledgment: recipient's 501(c)(3) status, date, amount/description, statement of goods/services provided or "no goods or services" |
| > $250 | Contemporaneous written acknowledgment required before donor files return — IRC § 170(f)(8) |
| Non-cash > $500 | Donor must file Form 8283; recipient completes Section B if donation > $5,000 |
| Non-cash > $5,000 | Qualified appraisal required; appraisal summary attached to Form 8283 |
| Quid pro quo > $75 | Recipient must provide good-faith FMV estimate of benefits received |

Include disclaimer: neither party provides tax advice; donor should consult their own tax counsel.

### 5. Representations & Warranties

**Donor warrants:**
- Full legal title and authority to transfer the donated property
- Property free of undisclosed liens, encumbrances, or defects
- Donation does not breach any other agreement or obligation
- All information provided is accurate and complete
- (Non-cash) Discloses all known defects, environmental issues, or legal problems

**Recipient warrants:**
- Duly organized, validly existing, and in good standing
- Holds current 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status (attach IRS determination letter or reference EIN)
- Has authority under governing documents to accept the donation
- Acceptance does not violate bylaws, board resolutions, or applicable law
- Will use donation in accordance with stated restrictions

### 6. Standard Provisions
- **Governing law**: [State], without regard to conflict of law principles
- **Venue**: [County/District courts of State]
- **Dispute resolution**: Optional mediation/arbitration before litigation
- **Attorneys' fees**: Specify whether prevailing party recovers fees
- **Severability**: Invalid provisions severed; remainder continues in effect
- **Entire agreement**: Supersedes all prior oral or written understandings
- **Amendment**: Written instrument signed by both parties

### 7. Execution
- Signature blocks: printed name, title, date for each party
- Corporate/organizational donors: include signatory's authority (title, board authorization if large gift)
- Recipient: authorized officer per bylaws (Executive Director, President, or Board Chair)
- Notarization: required for real property transfers; recommended for gifts above materiality threshold
- Witnesses: per state law requirements
- Exhibits: attach property descriptions, appraisal reports, restriction schedules, IRS determination letter

## Guidelines

- **501(c)(3) verification**: Always confirm recipient's exempt status via IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search (apps.irs.gov) before closing
- **Private foundation donors**: Excess business holdings, self-dealing, and expenditure responsibility rules may apply — flag for tax counsel
- **State charitable solicitation laws**: Many states require recipient registration; note but do not opine
- **Anti-money laundering**: For large or foreign-sourced donations, include AML/OFAC representations
- **Pledge vs. gift**: A pledge (promise to donate) is generally unenforceable absent consideration or reliance; this agreement should document a completed or contemporaneous transfer, not a future pledge, unless expressly structured as an enforceable pledge under state law
- **Naming rights**: If naming rights are granted, address FMV of the benefit and impact on donor's deduction (quid pro quo rules)
- **Document length**: Target 5–10 pages; complex endowment or real property gifts may run longer
