# Immigration Support Letter

Drafts a first-person support letter from a named writer (employer, family member, or community supporter) to serve as testimonial evidence in a U.S. immigration matter.

## Prerequisites

1. **Letter writer identity** — full legal name, title/relationship to applicant, address, phone, email
2. **Immigration benefit type** — family-based petition, employment-based, asylum, hardship waiver, removal defense, naturalization, etc.
3. **Applicant details** — full name, A-Number if known, country of origin
4. **Key facts to establish** — dates, locations, specific observations, employment details, or hardship circumstances
5. **Recipient** — USCIS office, immigration court, consular post, or "To Whom It May Concern"

## Output Structure

### Header / Letterhead
- Writer's full name, title, organization (if applicable), address, phone, email
- Date
- Addressee (specific officer or "To Whom It May Concern")
- Re: [Applicant Full Name] / [Petition or Application Type] / [A-Number if known]

### Opening Paragraph
- Identify writer, their relationship to applicant, and letter's purpose in one paragraph
- State basis of knowledge (years known, capacity in which writer knows applicant)

### Body — Content by Letter Type

| Letter Type | Required Specifics |
|---|---|
| **Employment verification** | Job title, start/end dates, job duties, salary/wage, performance observations, future employment intent |
| **Family relationship** | How writer knows both parties, duration, shared experiences, specific observations demonstrating genuine relationship, personal details only a genuine witness would know |
| **Character reference** | Concrete anecdotes of good moral character, community ties, family involvement, positive contributions — no vague generalities |
| **Extreme hardship** | Specific hardships resulting from denial or removal: medical, financial, educational, country-condition ties; tie each hardship to the applicant or qualifying relative |
| **Asylum corroboration** | Dates, locations, incidents witnessed or known; basis for fear; country conditions known to writer |

> **Specificity rule**: Adjudicators weigh specific dates, locations, and named incidents heavily over general character praise. Every factual claim must be grounded in the writer's personal knowledge.

### Closing
- Clear statement of support for the application
- Offer to provide additional information or appear if needed
- Perjury declaration (required):
  > *"I declare under penalty of perjury under the laws of the United States of America that the foregoing is true and correct to the best of my knowledge and belief."*
- Signature block: printed name, title, date, space for wet signature

## Guidelines

- **All statements must reflect the writer's personal knowledge** — flag any claim the writer cannot personally attest to
- **Do not exaggerate or generalize** — false statements in support letters constitute immigration fraud with criminal penalties (18 U.S.C. § 1001, INA § 274C)
- **Notarization**: not universally required but recommended for employment letters and character references; flag the option for the user
- **Letterhead**: use for employer or organizational letters; individual writers use standard block format
- **Length**: 1–3 pages; enough detail to be meaningful, short enough to hold attention
- **Jurisdiction**: U.S. federal (USCIS, EOIR, DOS); tailor salutation and citations to the specific adjudicating body
- If key facts are missing (dates, relationship duration, specific incidents), ask before drafting
