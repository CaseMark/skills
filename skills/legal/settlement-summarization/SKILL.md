# Settlement Summarization

Produces structured summaries of settlement negotiations and agreements, from simple two-party resolutions to complex multi-party commercial settlements.

## Output Structure

### Executive Summary
- Parties and their roles
- Settlement amount / payment structure
- Key conditions and contingencies
- Effective dates and deadlines

### Settlement Terms

**Financial Terms**:
- Lump sum vs. structured payments (amounts, schedule, triggers)
- Allocation across claims or parties
- Tax treatment provisions
- Attorney's fees and costs

**Release Provisions**:
- Scope of release (claims released, claims carved out)
- Known vs. unknown claims (Civ. Code § 1542 waivers if applicable)
- Mutual vs. one-way release

**Future Conduct Provisions**:
- Non-disparagement
- Confidentiality
- Cooperation obligations
- Non-compete / non-solicitation

**Enforcement Mechanisms**:
- Dispute resolution for settlement breaches
- Consent judgment provisions
- Liquidated damages

### Compliance Obligations

- Court approval requirements (class action, minor settlements)
- Regulatory filings or disclosures
- Reporting obligations
- Deadlines and notification requirements

### Risk Assessment

- Ambiguous or missing provisions
- Potential enforcement challenges
- Provisions that may require negotiation clarification
- Comparison to pre-settlement valuation (if available)

## For Complex Multi-Party Settlements

Additional sections:
- **Party-by-Party Breakdown**: Each party's obligations, rights, and payment responsibilities
- **Cross-claims and Contribution**: How cross-claims and indemnity obligations are resolved
- **Allocation Matrix**: Payment flows between parties in tabular form
- **Sequencing**: Order of performance, conditions precedent between parties

## Guidelines

- Extract exact dollar amounts, dates, and party names — never approximate
- Quote key contractual language verbatim for ambiguous or critical provisions
- Note any provisions that deviate from standard settlement terms
- Flag missing provisions that are typically expected (e.g., no confidentiality clause, no integration clause)
- Maintain neutral, analytical tone
