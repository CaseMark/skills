# Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Compliance Program

Drafts a comprehensive, board-ready AML compliance program tailored to a financial institution's risk profile, satisfying BSA, FinCEN, and federal/state AML requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Existing AML policies** — current program, risk assessments, exam reports, regulatory correspondence
2. **Institutional profile** — org chart, business lines, product offerings, customer demographics, geographic footprint
3. **Risk data** — prior risk assessments, audit findings, enforcement actions, consent orders
4. **Governing regulations** — confirm current CFR citations, FinCEN guidance, OCC bulletins applicable to institution type

## Output Structure

Draft a formal policy document with the following sections. Use numbered sections/subsections for navigation.

### 1. Program Foundation & Institutional Commitment

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Board endorsement | Explicit board/senior management approval and oversight |
| Scope | All business lines, customer relationships, geographies, transaction types |
| Risk-based approach | Controls calibrated to risk assessment findings |
| Resource commitment | Adequate personnel, technology, budget; compliance integrated into strategic decisions |

### 2. AML Compliance Officer

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Qualifications | Relevant education, CAMS or equivalent, demonstrated BSA/AML expertise |
| Reporting line | Direct to senior management; regular board/committee access |
| Independence | Performance evaluation tied to compliance effectiveness, not production |
| Authority | Unrestricted access to all records, systems, personnel |

**Core responsibilities checklist:**
- [ ] Primary regulatory contact (FinCEN, banking regulators, law enforcement)
- [ ] SAR, CTR, and BSA filing oversight
- [ ] Risk assessment coordination
- [ ] Training program management
- [ ] Independent testing oversight and remediation tracking
- [ ] Program design, updates, and effectiveness assessment

### 3. Customer Identification Program (CIP)

Per 31 CFR § 1020.220:

| Data Point | Individual | Legal Entity |
|---|---|---|
| Full legal name | Required | Required |
| Date of birth | Required | N/A |
| Address | Residential/business street (no PO Box) | Principal place of business |
| ID number | SSN/TIN or passport + country | EIN or equivalent |

**Verification methods:**
- **Documentary** — government-issued photo ID (individuals); articles of incorporation, business license (entities)
- **Non-documentary** — consumer reporting agencies, public databases, other reliable sources
- **Non-face-to-face** — additional verification measures for online/mail/phone channels

**Recordkeeping:** All CIP records retained 5 years after account closure.

### 4. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

Per 31 CFR § 1010.230 (FinCEN CDD Rule):

- [ ] Identify beneficial owners: each individual ≥25% equity + one individual with significant management control
- [ ] Collect via certification form; verify identity per CIP standards
- [ ] Update beneficial ownership on risk-based schedule and upon known changes
- [ ] Document nature/purpose of relationship, business activities, anticipated activity, source of funds
- [ ] Build expected transaction profiles (customer type, industry, geography, historical patterns)
- [ ] Ongoing monitoring: automated systems, periodic reviews, exception reporting

### 5. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

**Mandatory EDD categories:**

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| PEPs | Per FinCEN guidance |
| High-risk geographies | FATF high-risk/monitored jurisdictions |
| Complex ownership | Opaque structures obscuring beneficial ownership |
| High-risk businesses | MSBs, virtual currency exchanges, cash-intensive businesses |
| Elevated risk rating | Multiple risk factors per internal methodology |

**EDD requirements:**
- Background investigation (public records, media, commercial databases)
- Senior management or committee approval for new and reclassified relationships
- Enhanced monitoring: lower alert thresholds, more frequent periodic reviews
- Risk rating methodology: customer type × geography × product × activity → drives monitoring intensity and review frequency

### 6. Training Program

| Audience | Timing | Content |
|---|---|---|
| All employees/officers/directors | Annual minimum | Core curriculum (below) |
| New hires | Within 30 days or before customer-facing duties | Core curriculum |
| High-risk positions (account opening, SAR investigators, OFAC, wire) | Role-specific schedule | Specialized deep-dive + case studies |

**Core curriculum:**
- Institution's AML policies mapped to employee responsibilities
- BSA, USA PATRIOT Act, FinCEN regulations, OFAC programs
- ML/TF typologies relevant to institution's business
- Red flags and suspicious activity indicators
- CIP/CDD procedures
- Reporting/escalation obligations

**Documentation:** Attendance records, completion certificates, comprehension assessments, effectiveness metrics.

### 7. Independent Testing

| Element | Standard |
|---|---|
| Independence | Qualified personnel independent of AML function (internal audit, external firms) |
| Frequency | Every 12–18 months minimum; higher-risk = more frequent |
| Reporting | Findings to Compliance Officer, senior management, and board/committee |

**Scope must cover:**
- [ ] Regulatory compliance across all BSA/AML requirements
- [ ] Policy/procedure adequacy relative to identified risks
- [ ] Risk assessment process and methodology
- [ ] Transaction monitoring system effectiveness
- [ ] Training program adequacy
- [ ] SAR/CTR filing timeliness and accuracy
- [ ] CIP/CDD compliance
- [ ] OFAC screening procedures

**Remediation:** Management response required for all findings; action plans with timelines; follow-up testing to verify corrective actions.

### 8. Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

Per 31 CFR § 1020.320:

**Filing thresholds:**
- Transactions ≥ $5,000 where institution knows/suspects/has reason to suspect illegal activity, BSA evasion, no business purpose, or facilitation of criminal conduct

**Filing deadlines:**

| Scenario | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Suspect identified | 30 calendar days from initial detection |
| No suspect identified | 60 calendar days from initial detection |

**Key SAR indicators to document:**
- Structuring to evade CTR thresholds
- Activity inconsistent with customer profile
- Unusually large currency/monetary instrument transactions
- Wire transfers lacking economic rationale or involving high-risk jurisdictions
- Attempts to avoid recordkeeping/CIP requirements
- Shell company transactions with no apparent legitimate purpose

**Confidentiality:** Federal law prohibits SAR disclosure to subjects or unauthorized persons; civil and criminal penalties for unauthorized disclosure. SAR records retained 5 years from filing; access restricted to need-to-know personnel.

**Escalation:** All employees must report suspicious activity immediately to Compliance Officer; good-faith reporters protected from adverse consequences.

### 9. Currency Transaction Reporting (CTR)

Per 31 CFR §§ 1010.310, 1020.310:

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Threshold | Currency transactions > $10,000 per person per business day |
| Aggregation | Multiple transactions by/on behalf of same person in one business day |
| Filing deadline | 15 calendar days following transaction date via BSA E-Filing |
| Currency definition | Coin and paper money (legal tender); excludes cashier's checks, money orders, traveler's checks |

**Exemptions (31 CFR § 1020.315):**
- Banks, government entities, listed public companies, qualifying non-listed businesses/payroll customers
- Require documentation, approval, biennial renewal, annual eligibility review
- Discretionary — institution may file CTRs for exempt customers when warranted

### 10. OFAC Compliance

**Screening requirements:**

| Trigger | Timing |
|---|---|
| Account opening | Before relationship established |
| Existing customers | Periodically (minimum annually; risk-based frequency) |
| Transactions (wires, ACH, monetary instruments) | Real-time or near real-time |

**Lists:** SDN List, Consolidated Sanctions List, country-based programs.

**Match investigation:** Compare all identifying data points; resolve same business day for transactions where possible.

**Blocking/rejection:**
- **Blocking** — mandatory for property of sanctioned persons; place in interest-bearing account; file blocking report with OFAC within 10 business days
- **Rejection** — for prohibited transactions not involving blocking; notify originator; document decision

**Recordkeeping:** All screening records, investigations, blocking/rejection actions retained ≥ 5 years.

### 11. Risk Assessment

| Dimension | Factors |
|---|---|
| Products/services | Transaction velocity, geographic reach, anonymity, abuse susceptibility |
| Customers | Type, occupation, geography, relationship characteristics |
| Entities | Ownership structure, business purpose, formation jurisdiction |
| Geography | Physical presence, customer concentrations, high-risk jurisdiction flows (FATF, State Dept.) |

- Assess both **inherent risk** (pre-controls) and **residual risk** (post-controls)
- Conduct at least annually or upon significant operational/regulatory changes
- Document methodology and results; present to senior management and board
- Findings drive CDD intensity, monitoring sensitivity, review frequency, resource allocation

### 12. Governance & Continuous Improvement

**Board responsibilities:**
- [ ] Approve AML program and material updates
- [ ] Review risk assessment findings
- [ ] Receive quarterly Compliance Officer reports
- [ ] Review independent testing results and remediation status
- [ ] Allocate adequate resources

**Quarterly reporting metrics:** SAR/CTR filing activity, OFAC screening/blocking, CDD/EDD activities, training completion, testing findings, regulatory developments, significant issues.

**Change management:** Proposed updates documented with rationale → Compliance Officer + legal counsel review → senior management/board approval → communicate to affected personnel → maintain version history.

### 13. Recordkeeping

| Record Type | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| SARs + supporting docs | 5 years from filing |
| CTRs + supporting docs | 5 years from filing |
| CIP/CDD records | 5 years after account closure |
| Beneficial ownership | 5 years after account closure |
| OFAC screening/blocking | 5 years minimum |
| Risk assessments, testing reports, training records | 5 years minimum |

**Standards:** Records in any retrievable format; organized for prompt retrieval (typically within days of regulatory request); security controls and audit trails for sensitive records (especially SAR-related).

## Guidelines

- Include disclaimer: program is a framework requiring review by qualified legal counsel and tailoring to institution's specific risk profile, size, complexity, and operations
- Verify all CFR citations are current at drafting date — mark uncertain citations with [VERIFY]
- Effective AML compliance requires adequate resources, qualified personnel, robust systems, and a culture of compliance at all organizational levels
- Program must be reviewed and updated regularly for regulatory changes, emerging risks, and implementation lessons
- Consult legal counsel for interpretation questions
- OFAC obligations are strict liability — err on the side of caution
- SAR confidentiality violations carry serious penalties — build this into all procedures and training
