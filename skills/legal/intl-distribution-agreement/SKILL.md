# International Distribution Agreement

Drafts a litigation-tested international distribution agreement balancing legal enforceability across jurisdictions with commercial practicality.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party details** — full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions of incorporation, registered addresses, tax IDs
2. **Product information** — specifications, regulatory classifications, export control classification numbers (ECCN/EU dual-use), existing IP registrations
3. **Territory definition** — countries, sub-regions, customs territories, or trade zones
4. **Commercial terms** — exclusivity preference, pricing structure, payment method, Incoterms selection
5. **Existing documents** — prior distribution agreements, correspondence, regulatory approvals, compliance certifications
6. **Governing law / arbitration preferences** — seat, institution, language

## Document Structure

| Article | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Recitals & Definitions | Parties, defined terms, purpose |
| Appointment & Territory | Exclusive/non-exclusive, territory, carve-outs, performance thresholds |
| Products & Orders | Order lifecycle, acceptance/rejection, modification, cancellation |
| Pricing & Payment | Price structure, adjustment triggers, currency, payment method, credit support |
| Delivery & Risk | Incoterms (current ICC edition), packaging, inspection, claims |
| Regulatory Compliance | Export controls, sanctions, anti-corruption, product regulations, data protection |
| Intellectual Property | Ownership, trademark license, brand guidelines, confidentiality, infringement |
| Term & Termination | Duration, renewal, termination for cause/convenience, post-termination |
| Dispute Resolution | Governing law, arbitration clause, interim relief |
| General Provisions | Independent contractor, assignment, force majeure, liability cap, notices, severability, entire agreement |

## Drafting Instructions

### 1. Parties & Appointment

- Identify each entity with: legal name, entity type, jurisdiction, registered address, registration/tax numbers
- If part of corporate group, clarify contracting entity and whether parent guarantee is needed
- **Exclusivity**: If granted, define scope precisely:
  - Supplier direct-sale carve-outs (government, military, MNCs, e-commerce)
  - Commission on Supplier direct sales in Territory
  - Minimum purchase commitments: amount, measurement period, calculation method, consequences of shortfall
- **Territory**: Define by political subdivisions, customs territories, or regulatory jurisdictions — not just country names
  - Address: geopolitical changes, sanctions-driven territory changes
  - Cross-border scenarios: customers in-Territory reselling out; orders from outside delivered within
  - Explicit carve-outs for restricted customers/industries/end-uses

### 2. Commercial Terms

**Order lifecycle:**
- Required PO contents → Supplier acceptance/rejection window → order acknowledgment → modification/cancellation terms

**Pricing:**
- Fixed-period or adjustable; if adjustable, define triggers:
  - Raw material cost threshold (specify %)
  - Currency fluctuation band (specify %)
  - Transport cost increases
- Notice period for price changes; Distributor cancellation right on increase
- Volume tiers, promotional pricing, price protection on purchased inventory
- Allocate: VAT, customs duties, freight, insurance, transaction costs

**Payment:**
- Method: letter of credit (irrevocable/confirmed, sight/deferred, required documents) or wire transfer (advance, against documents, net-X)
- Invoice and payment currency; FX risk allocation
- Late payment: interest rate, shipment suspension right, termination trigger
- Credit support if warranted: parent guarantee, standby LC, bank guarantee

**Delivery (Incoterms 2020):**

| Term | Risk Transfer | Export Clearance | Import Clearance |
|------|--------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| EXW | Supplier's facility | Distributor | Distributor |
| FCA | Named place/carrier | Supplier | Distributor |
| FOB | On board vessel | Supplier | Distributor |
| CIF | On board vessel | Supplier | Distributor |
| DDP | Destination | Supplier | Supplier |

- Supplement with: packaging standards, labeling, insurance, inspection window, defect claims procedure, remedy (repair/replace/refund)

### 3. Regulatory Compliance

**Export controls:**
- Supplier: comply with home jurisdiction export laws; obtain required licenses
- Distributor: comply with Territory import laws; obtain licenses/permits
- Specify product ECCN or EU dual-use classification; end-use/end-user restrictions

**Sanctions:**
- Both parties: represent not owned/controlled by sanctioned persons
- Screen against: OFAC SDN List, EU Consolidated List, UN Sanctions List
- Ongoing screening of counterparties, sub-distributors, agents
- Immediate notification + suspension/termination right if party becomes sanctioned

**Anti-corruption:**
- Comply with FCPA, UK Bribery Act 2010, local anti-bribery laws
- Prohibit payments to government officials (define broadly: SOE employees, international org officials)
- Require: accurate books/records, internal controls, audit rights
- Breach = immediate termination + indemnification

**Product regulations:**
- Allocate responsibility for: safety certifications, conformity assessment, labeling (language, warnings, origin), product registrations (e.g., medical devices, chemicals)
- Cost allocation for obtaining/maintaining approvals

**Data protection:**
- If personal data transfers involved: comply with GDPR (if applicable), Territory data laws
- Implement transfer mechanisms (SCCs if EU data)
- Data breach notification obligations; liability allocation

### 4. Intellectual Property

- **Ownership**: All IP remains with Supplier — explicit statement, no transfer
- **License**: Limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, royalty-free; Territory only; term only; per brand guidelines
- **Restrictions**:
  - No modification/combination of marks
  - Prior written approval for all marketing materials
  - No registration of Supplier marks by Distributor
  - All goodwill inures to Supplier
- **Confidential information**: Broad definition; reasonable care standard; use limited to agreement purposes; survives termination 3–5 years (indefinite for trade secrets)
- **Infringement**: Distributor notifies promptly; Supplier controls enforcement; Distributor cooperates; recovery allocation
- **Post-termination**: Cease all IP use immediately; return/destroy branded materials (with certification); sell-off period for branded inventory (30–90 days, specify conditions)

### 5. Term & Termination

- **Initial term**: Specify duration (typically 3–5 years) and commencement trigger
- **Renewal**: Auto-renewal for successive periods unless non-renewal notice given (specify notice period, e.g., 90–180 days)
- **Termination for cause**:
  - Material breach + cure period (typically 30 days written notice)
  - Specifically material: payment default, IP violation, confidentiality breach, compliance violation
  - Incurable (immediate termination): insolvency, sanctions violation, FCPA breach
- **Insolvency termination**: Bankruptcy, receivership, assignment for creditors, cessation of business
- **Post-termination checklist**:
  - [ ] Pending orders: Supplier election to fulfill or cancel
  - [ ] Confidential information: return or destroy
  - [ ] IP materials: return or destroy with certification
  - [ ] Inventory sell-off: 30–90 day period at regular prices
  - [ ] Cease holding out as authorized distributor
  - [ ] Customer obligations: allocate warranty/service responsibility
  - [ ] Return demonstration units, samples, Supplier property

### 6. Dispute Resolution

**Governing law options** (choose one):

| Jurisdiction | Strength |
|-------------|----------|
| England & Wales | Developed commercial law, international enforceability |
| New York | Sophisticated commercial law, familiar globally |
| Switzerland | Neutral, strong international commerce tradition |
| Singapore | Common law, pro-business, strong arbitration infrastructure |

- Exclude conflict-of-laws principles
- **CISG**: Explicitly include or exclude; if included, supplement gaps (liability limits, dispute resolution)

**Arbitration clause essentials:**
- Institution: ICC, LCIA, SIAC, or HKIAC
- Seat: pro-arbitration jurisdiction, New York Convention signatory (London, Paris, Geneva, Singapore, Hong Kong)
- Language: specify (typically English)
- Arbitrators: 3 for significant disputes; 1 for smaller claims; appointment procedure
- Scope: all disputes arising out of or relating to the agreement
- Optional multi-tier: negotiation (X days) → mediation (X days) → arbitration
- Emergency/interim relief: via institution's emergency arbitrator or court applications
- Costs: each bears own, or prevailing party recovers reasonable costs
- Exclude punitive/exemplary damages if desired
- Performance continues during dispute (except disputed payment obligations)

### 7. General Provisions

- **Independent contractor** — no agency, partnership, or employment
- **Assignment** — Supplier may assign to affiliates or in M&A; Distributor requires consent; define change-of-control trigger
- **Force majeure** — Acts of God, war, terrorism, government action, epidemics, strikes, natural disasters; notice requirements; mitigation duty; termination right if >90 days
- **Limitation of liability** — Cap on damages; exclude consequential/incidental/punitive; carve-outs for indemnification, confidentiality breach, IP violation
- **Notices** — Method (courier/registered mail; email if permitted), addresses, deemed-receipt rules
- **Severability** — Invalid provisions modified to minimum extent
- **Amendments/waivers** — Written and signed; waiver of breach ≠ waiver of subsequent breach
- **Entire agreement** — Supersedes all prior negotiations/agreements
- **Counterparts/e-signatures** — Multiple counterparts; electronic signatures valid; accommodate jurisdictions requiring seals/notarization
