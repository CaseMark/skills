# Multi-Year Tax Return Income Analysis

Produces a litigation-ready financial assessment from one or more years of tax returns, organized as a professional memorandum.

## Prerequisites

1. **Tax return(s)** — Form 1040 with all schedules for each year under review
2. **Legal context** — proceeding type (bankruptcy, family law, personal injury, other) and which party the taxpayer is
3. **Jurisdiction** — state and court system for relevant thresholds (e.g., median income for means testing)
4. **Optional** — bank statements, Schedule K-1s, business records, prior analyses for cross-reference

## Output Structure

Produce a professional memorandum with these sections in order:

### 1. Executive Overview (2-3 paragraphs)

| Element | Include |
|---|---|
| Taxpayer profile | Individual vs. couple, primary occupation(s) |
| Income trajectory | Growing, declining, volatile — characterize the trend |
| Life events | Marriage, divorce, job changes, business ventures reflected in returns |
| Bottom-line framing | Immediate context for the legal professional |

### 2. Multi-Year Comparison Table

| Tax Year | Filing Status | Total Income | AGI | Tax Liability | Refund/(Owed) | Key Observations |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| *per year* | *status* | *amount* | *amount* | *amount* | *amount* | *notable changes* |

Follow with narrative interpreting trends across years.

### 3. Taxpayer Identification & Status

- Full names, last four SSN digits for all filers
- Filing status per year; flag changes and legal significance (e.g., MFJ→MFS may signal marital discord)
- Dependents: name (last four SSN), age, relationship, status (minor, student, elderly parent)
- Note relevance to support obligations, means testing, or custody

### 4. Income Analysis

Organize by category with context, not just amounts:

| Category | What to Address |
|---|---|
| W-2 wages | Employer if identifiable, stability, year-over-year changes |
| Schedule C | Business nature, gross receipts, net profit/loss, expense ratio |
| Investment income | Interest, dividends, capital gains — implications for asset base |
| Retirement distributions | Regular vs. premature; financial distress indicators |
| Social Security | **Flag: excluded from bankruptcy means test calculations** |
| Other (K-1, rental, alimony) | Source, consistency, legal significance |

Conclude with AGI and comparison to applicable median income standards.

### 5. Deductions, Credits & Tax Position

- **Standard vs. itemized** — what the choice reveals
- **Itemized breakdown**: mortgage interest (homeownership/debt level), SALT, charitable contributions (flag large pre-filing donations as potential trustee concern)
- **Schedule C deductions** — reasonableness relative to income; flag disproportionate vehicle, travel, or home office claims
- **Credits** — child tax credit, EITC, education credits and their significance
- **Refund/balance due** — large refunds suggest overwithholding; amounts owed suggest cash flow or estimated tax issues

### 6. Red Flags & Areas of Concern

Flag and explain legal significance of each:

| Category | Watch For |
|---|---|
| Income discrepancies | Reported income vs. known lifestyle; unreported sources |
| Deduction anomalies | Excessive/undocumented business deductions; luxury items as business expenses |
| Timing patterns | Sudden income drops or deduction spikes near filing/litigation dates |
| Bankruptcy-specific | Large charitable gifts pre-petition; business losses inconsistent with continued operations |
| Family law-specific | Cash business underreporting; inflated expenses; hidden assets/income diversion |
| Fraud indicators | Inconsistent schedules; missing forms; round-number patterns |

### 7. Trustee & Legal Considerations

Narrative addressing proceeding-specific implications:

- **Bankruptcy**: means test positioning, above/below median income, disposable income calculation impact, carryover losses affecting future tax positions
- **Family law**: income available for support beyond AGI, imputed income arguments, lifestyle inconsistencies
- **Personal injury**: pre-injury vs. post-injury earning capacity, lost income quantification
- Explain *why* each issue matters legally, not just what it is

### 8. Information Gaps & Recommendations

For each gap, state:
1. What is missing
2. What question it leaves unanswered
3. What legal significance the missing information holds
4. What specific document to request (bank statements, business records, K-1 detail, etc.)

### 9. Conclusion

- Synthesize key findings and legal implications (1-2 paragraphs)
- Assess reliability of returns as presented
- Prioritize concerns requiring immediate attention
- Recommend next steps for due diligence

## Guidelines

- Maintain objective, analytical tone throughout — this is a professional memorandum
- Use tables only for comparative multi-year data and structured checklists; otherwise use narrative prose
- **Social Security income**: always flag exclusion from bankruptcy means test
- **Charitable contributions**: scrutinize gifts in the 1-2 years pre-petition as potential preferential transfers
- **Schedule C**: apply heightened scrutiny to expense-to-income ratios above 70%
- Do not draw legal conclusions — identify issues and explain significance for the attorney to evaluate
- Mark any citation or threshold figure you cannot verify with `[VERIFY]`
- If fewer than 2 years of returns are provided, note limitations on trend analysis and recommend obtaining additional years
