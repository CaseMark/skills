# Purchase and Sale Agreement — Commercial Real Estate

Drafts a litigation-ready PSA for commercial property transfers, protecting client interests while ensuring enforceability under applicable state law.

## Prerequisites

1. **Parties' legal names** — exact entity names, entity types, states of formation, addresses
2. **Property information** — legal description, address, tax parcel ID, improvement details
3. **Deal terms** — purchase price, earnest money, closing timeline, financing structure
4. **Title commitment** and existing survey (if available)
5. **Lease schedule** — current rent roll, all leases/amendments (income-producing properties)
6. **Environmental reports** — Phase I ESA or known conditions
7. **Service contracts** — management, maintenance, vendor agreements
8. **Client role** — buyer or seller; risk tolerance and priority items

## Document Review

Before drafting, extract from uploaded materials:

| Data Point | Source |
|---|---|
| Party names & entity structure | Org docs, term sheet |
| Legal description & address | Deed, title commitment, survey |
| Purchase price & earnest money | Term sheet, LOI |
| Closing timeline | LOI, correspondence |
| Financing type & amount | Term sheet, lender correspondence |
| Lease terms & rent roll | Lease files |
| Environmental conditions | Phase I/II reports |
| Special conditions/contingencies | Correspondence, LOI |

## Output Structure

### 1. Header & Parties

- Title: "PURCHASE AND SALE AGREEMENT" (add property type subtitle if appropriate)
- Effective date: date of last signature unless parties specify otherwise
- Full party identification: legal name, entity type, state of formation, principal address, notice address
- If SPE or multi-tiered entity, identify parent/guarantor relationships

### 2. Property Description & Purchase Price

**Property description must include:**
- Street address and common name
- Legal description (reference Exhibit A)
- County, state, tax parcel ID, approximate acreage/SF
- Improvement summary: building count, SF, construction type, year built, current use
- Appurtenant rights (access easements, utility easements, parking rights, development rights)
- Personal property schedule (reference Exhibit B) — HVAC, mechanical, electrical, fire suppression, signage, security systems, building automation
- Personal property conveys "as-is, where-is" without warranty

**Purchase price structure:**

| Component | Details |
|---|---|
| Total price | Numerical + written form |
| Allocation | Real property vs. personal property (if applicable; must withstand IRS scrutiny) |
| Earnest money | 1-5% typical; wire transfer within 3-5 business days to escrow agent |
| Additional deposits | Amount, trigger event, refundability |
| Cash due at closing | Price minus deposits minus financing proceeds |
| Financing | Loan amount, type (conventional/SBA/CMBS/mezzanine), lender if known |

All closing payments by wire transfer of immediately available federal funds.

### 3. Title Review & Survey

**Title process:**
- Seller delivers title commitment within 10-15 days of effective date, plus copies of all Schedule B exception documents
- Buyer review period: 10-15 days after receipt
- Distinguish mandatory cure defects (monetary liens, title defects preventing marketable fee simple) from voluntary defects (easements, CCRs not impairing use)
- Seller response: 10-15 days to elect cure/not cure voluntary defects
- Buyer election: 5-10 days to accept or terminate if Seller declines to cure
- Mandatory defect cure obligation survives closing date

**Survey requirements:**
- ALTA/NSPS Land Title Survey; specify required Table A items:
  - Improvements, zoning/setbacks, parking count, utilities, flood zone
- Certified to Buyer, lender, and title company; certification date no earlier than effective date
- Buyer review period: 10-15 days after receipt
- Seller must cure encroachments, boundary disputes, zoning violations

### 4. Due Diligence

**Period:** 30 days (single-tenant) to 60-90 days (complex/multi-tenant/environmental concerns). Time is of the essence on expiration.

**Access rights:**
- Buyer and representatives may enter on 24-48 hours' notice during business hours
- For occupied properties, coordinate tenant access through Seller/property management

**Scope of investigations:**

| Category | Items |
|---|---|
| Physical | Buildings, structures, HVAC, electrical, plumbing, roofing, structural, parking, drainage, utilities |
| Environmental | Phase I (ASTM E1527-13 [VERIFY current edition]); Phase II if RECs identified |
| Financial (income properties) | Leases, tenant files, rent rolls, service contracts, operating statements, budgets, capex records |
| Regulatory | Zoning, building code, ADA, certificates of occupancy, permits |

**Buyer obligations during DD:**
- Maintain CGL insurance ≥ $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate; name Seller as additional insured
- No unreasonable interference with operations or tenant rights
- Repair damage, restore property, keep free of mechanics' liens
- Buyer bears all investigation costs

**Seller cooperation:**
- Provide all property documents: title, leases, rent rolls, financials, environmental reports, plans, permits, governmental correspondence
- May require confidentiality agreement; may redact tenant confidential business info (not lease terms)

**Termination right:**
- Buyer may terminate for any/no reason by written notice by 5:00 PM (property time zone) on last day of DD period
- On termination: earnest money refunded within 3-5 business days; Buyer delivers copies of third-party reports to Seller (retains internal analyses and attorney work product)
- If Buyer does NOT terminate: earnest money becomes non-refundable (except for Seller default or express contingency failure)

### 5. Representations & Warranties

**Seller reps (true as of effective date and closing):**

| Topic | Substance |
|---|---|
| Authority | Duly organized, validly existing, full power/authority, all consents obtained |
| Title | Good and marketable fee simple, free of undisclosed liens; no other sale/encumbrance agreements |
| Litigation | No pending/threatened suits, claims, arbitrations, condemnation, special assessments |
| Environmental | To knowledge: no hazardous substances (except ordinary operations in compliance); no violation notices; no prior contaminating uses |
| Compliance | To knowledge: property complies with zoning, building codes, land use regs; all COs/permits in force |
| Leases (income properties) | Rent roll true/complete/accurate; all leases in force; no landlord defaults; no tenant termination notices; security deposits disclosed and held per law |
| Financial info | Income/expense statements true/accurate, prepared per normal accounting practices |
| Contracts | All service contracts/warranties/guaranties disclosed; no undisclosed options or RoFRs |

**Knowledge qualifiers:** "To Seller's actual knowledge without independent investigation" for environmental and compliance reps.

**Buyer reps:**
- Authority, organization, capacity
- As-is acknowledgment: acquiring based on own investigation, not relying on Seller statements except express written reps
- As-is does NOT limit remedies for breach of express reps or fraud

**Survival & caps:**
- Reps survive closing for 12-18 months
- Claim threshold: $10K-$25K (de minimis)
- Aggregate liability cap: 5-10% of purchase price
- Fraud/intentional misrepresentation: unlimited survival, no caps

### 6. Closing

**Date:** Specify date certain or formula (e.g., 30 days post-DD expiration). Time is of the essence; allow 15-30 day extension for title cure/contingency satisfaction.

**Seller closing deliveries:**

| Document | Notes |
|---|---|
| Special warranty deed | Executed, acknowledged, legal description from survey/commitment |
| Bill of sale | Personal property per Exhibit B, as-is |
| FIRPTA certificate | Non-foreign status; if foreign seller, 15% withholding |
| Owner's affidavit | No unrecorded liens, no parties in possession (except tenants), no mechanic's lien exposure |
| Assignment of leases | With Buyer assumption of post-closing landlord obligations |
| Tenant estoppel certificates | All tenants (or major tenants per agreement) |
| Assignment of contracts/warranties | Service contracts Buyer assumes; building warranties |
| Keys, codes, access items | All keys, access codes, security codes |
| Updated rent roll | Certified accurate as of closing |
| Licenses, permits, COs | All governmental approvals in Seller's possession |
| Settlement statement | Title company prepared |
| Entity authority documents | Resolutions, consents authorizing transaction |
| Transfer tax declarations | Per state/local requirements |

**Buyer closing deliveries:**
- Purchase price (wire transfer), less deposits and financing proceeds, plus/minus prorations
- Entity authority documents
- Assumption agreements (leases, contracts)
- Lender-required documents (if applicable)

### 7. Prorations & Adjustments

Prorate as of 11:59 PM day before closing:

| Item | Method |
|---|---|
| Real estate taxes | Based on most recent ascertainable bill; post-closing true-up within 30 days of actual bill |
| Rent (income properties) | Based on collected (not accrued) rents; post-closing collections applied first to current, then delinquent |
| Security deposits | Transfer to Buyer or credit at closing; Buyer assumes return obligations |
| Operating expenses | Utilities, insurance, CAM, management fees — prorate prepaid/accrued |
| TI allowances & leasing commissions | Seller responsible for all obligations under pre-closing leases; holdback/escrow if incomplete |
| Special assessments | Allocate per agreement (levied pre-closing but payable post-closing) |

Post-closing adjustment period: 90-120 days.

### 8. Default & Remedies

**Seller default — Buyer may elect:**
1. **Terminate** — full earnest money refund + reimbursement of reasonable out-of-pocket expenses (survey, environmental, engineering, appraisal, legal fees); consider cap of $25K-$50K; OR
2. **Specific performance** — compel conveyance; may record lis pendens

**Buyer default — Seller's sole remedy:**
- Terminate and retain earnest money as **liquidated damages**
- Include enforceability language: parties agree amount is reasonable pre-estimate of damages, not a penalty
- Escrow agent release procedures: notice to Buyer, cure period, interpleader if disputed

**General provisions:**
- Notice and cure: written notice specifying default; 5-10 business day cure period
- No cure period for: failure to close when conditions satisfied, repudiation, fraud
- Prevailing party attorneys' fees (optional — note to client that this cuts both ways)

### 9. General Provisions

**Notices:** Written; delivered by personal delivery, overnight courier, email (with confirmation), or certified mail return receipt requested. Deemed delivered: personal delivery same day (if before 5 PM local); overnight courier next business day; email same day (if before 5 PM local); certified mail 3 business days.

**Assignment:** Buyer may assign to affiliated entity (controlling/controlled/common control) without Seller consent if Buyer remains liable; unaffiliated assignment requires Seller consent (not unreasonably withheld). Either party may assign to facilitate §1031 exchange.

**Governing law:** State where property is located, without conflicts of law principles.

**Dispute resolution:** Mandatory mediation before litigation; costs split equally; 30-60 day completion window. Exclusive jurisdiction: state and federal courts in county where property is located.

**Jury trial waiver:** Mutual, knowing, voluntary waiver (separately initialed if required by jurisdiction).

**Standard provisions:** Entire agreement/integration; written amendments only; severability; no oral waivers; counterpart execution; electronic signatures valid.

**Signature blocks:** Full legal name, entity type, signatory name/title/date for each party. Include guarantor blocks if applicable.

## Required Exhibits

- **Exhibit A** — Legal description
- **Exhibit B** — Personal property schedule
- **Exhibit C** — Form of deed (optional)
- **Exhibit D** — Form of bill of sale (optional)
- **Exhibit E** — Form of assignment and assumption of leases (income properties)
- **Exhibit F** — Form of tenant estoppel certificate (income properties)

## Guidelines

- **Jurisdiction check:** Verify state-specific requirements — transfer tax rates, mandatory disclosures (lead-based paint if pre-1978 residential component), recording requirements, mandatory contract provisions
- **Property type adjustments:**
  - Office: parking ratios, building services/operating hours, TI standards
  - Retail: exclusive use provisions, operating covenants, percentage rent
  - Industrial: environmental compliance emphasis, specialized equipment, industrial zoning
  - Multifamily: rent control/stabilization, tenant notification requirements, security deposit regulations
- **FIRPTA:** 15% withholding for foreign sellers unless exemption applies [VERIFY current rate under IRC §1445]
- **§1031 exchanges:** Include cooperation clause for either party's exchange; cooperating party bears no additional cost or liability
- **Entity verification:** Confirm exact legal names against secretary of state records before finalizing
- **Timeline validation:** Calculate all deadlines from effective date through closing; flag conflicts or unrealistic periods
- **Cross-reference check:** Verify all exhibit references, defined terms, section cross-references, and dollar amounts for internal consistency
- Do NOT draft if insufficient deal terms provided — request missing information first
- Mark any uncertain statutory citations with [VERIFY]
