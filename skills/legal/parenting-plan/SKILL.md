# Parenting Plan

Drafts a jurisdiction-compliant parenting plan tailored to the parties' circumstances, incorporating custody frameworks, schedules, and enforceable provisions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Jurisdiction** — state where the plan will be filed (terminology and statutory requirements vary)
2. **Party information** — full legal names, addresses, children's names and DOBs
3. **Case information** — court, case number, related proceedings
4. **Existing orders** — prior custody orders, temporary orders, mediation recommendations
5. **Party preferences** — desired custody arrangement, schedule constraints, work schedules, geographic factors
6. **Financial information** — income data for support calculations, insurance coverage, existing support orders

## Output Structure

### 1. Parties and Case Information

| Element | Details |
|---|---|
| Parents | Full legal names, addresses, relationship to children |
| Children | Full names, DOBs, ages |
| Court | Name, county, case number, related proceedings |
| Jurisdiction-specific IDs | Check whether state requires DL numbers, partial SSNs, or other identifiers |

### 2. Custody Framework

Draft **legal custody** and **physical custody** separately:

- **Legal custody** — joint or sole; if joint, specify:
  - Categories requiring mutual agreement vs. independent decision
  - Tie-breaking mechanisms
- **Physical custody** — primary/joint; designate primary residential parent if applicable
- Research state presumptions (many states presume joint legal custody)
- Use jurisdiction's preferred terminology ("parenting time," "time-sharing," "custody," "residential schedule")

### 3. Parenting Time Schedule

Address each category with **specific days, times, and locations**:

| Category | Specify |
|---|---|
| Regular weekly schedule | Days/overnights with each parent |
| Weekends | Alternating pattern, Friday pickup to Sunday return times |
| Midweek visits | Day, pickup/dropoff times |
| Summer vacation | Division (e.g., alternating weeks, block time), notice requirements |
| School breaks | Fall, winter, spring — alternating by year |
| Holidays | Two-year rotation table (see template below) |
| Birthdays | Child's, each parent's, Mother's/Father's Day |
| Special days | Cultural/religious observances specific to the family |

**Holiday Rotation Template:**

| Holiday | Even Years | Odd Years |
|---|---|---|
| New Year's | Parent A | Parent B |
| MLK Day | Parent B | Parent A |
| Presidents' Day | Parent A | Parent B |
| Spring Break | Parent B | Parent A |
| Memorial Day | Parent A | Parent B |
| July 4th | Parent B | Parent A |
| Labor Day | Parent A | Parent B |
| Halloween | Parent B | Parent A |
| Thanksgiving | Parent A | Parent B |
| Winter Break (1st half) | Parent B | Parent A |
| Winter Break (2nd half) | Parent A | Parent B |

**Exchange logistics:**
- Location (school, parent's residence, neutral site)
- Transportation responsibility for each exchange
- Late pickup/no-show protocol

### 4. Decision-Making Allocation

| Category | Subcategories | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Education | School selection, special ed, tutoring, extracurriculars | Joint / Sole |
| Healthcare | Routine, emergency, mental health, dental, vision, alternative | Joint / Sole |
| Religious | Upbringing, participation, ceremonies | Joint / Sole |
| Extracurriculars | Sports, arts, camps | Joint / Sole |

Include:
- Consultation timeline for major decisions (e.g., 30 days' written notice)
- Definition of emergency decisions permitting unilateral action
- Escalation path before court intervention

### 5. Communication Protocols

- **Parent-child contact** — phone/video call schedule when child is with other parent; age-appropriate technology guidelines
- **Record access** — both parents' right to school records, medical records, extracurricular information (cite FERPA for education records)
- **Co-parent communication** — preferred method (email, co-parenting app, text), response timeframes (routine: 48 hrs; urgent: 24 hrs; emergency: immediate)

### 6. Financial Provisions

- **Child support** — reference state guidelines calculator; specify payor, amount (if determined), frequency, payment method
- **Shared expenses** — allocation table:

| Expense | Split | Reimbursement Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Uncovered medical/dental | 50/50 or pro rata | 30 days from invoice |
| Health insurance premiums | Per guidelines | Monthly |
| Childcare | 50/50 or pro rata | 30 days |
| School expenses | 50/50 or pro rata | 30 days |
| Extracurricular fees | Mutual agreement | 30 days |

- Documentation requirements for reimbursement requests

### 7. Relocation Provisions

- **Notice requirement** — research state statute for mandatory notice period (typically 30–90 days) and distance threshold
- **Notice contents** — new address, move date, proposed revised schedule, reason for move
- **Objection procedure** — timeline and method for non-moving parent to object
- **Burden of proof** — note applicable standard (varies by state)

### 8. Travel

- Advance notice period for out-of-state travel (typically 14–30 days)
- Itinerary and contact information sharing
- Passport possession and international travel consent requirements
- If abduction risk: include UCCJEA references [VERIFY], ne exeat provisions, or passport surrender requirements

### 9. Dispute Resolution

Mandatory escalation sequence:
1. Good-faith negotiation (14 days)
2. Mediation — selection process, cost split, timeline
3. Parenting coordinator (if jurisdiction recognizes; check state statute)
4. Court petition — reserve for emergencies or exhaustion of ADR

### 10. Modification and Review

- Standard: substantial change in circumstances affecting child's best interests
- Optional: scheduled review at developmental milestones (e.g., school entry, adolescence)
- All modifications must comply with state filing requirements

### 11. Execution and Filing

- Signature blocks for both parents with dates
- Check jurisdiction for: notarization, witness requirements, attorney certification
- Effective date clause
- "Subject to court approval" language if required
- Attach any mandatory state cover sheets, certifications, or declarations

## Guidelines

- **Jurisdiction-first**: Always research the filing state's specific statutes, terminology, and court forms before drafting. Many states publish model parenting plans — align structure with local expectations.
- **Facts over templates**: Incorporate details from the parties' actual documents (work schedules, geography, prior arrangements) rather than generic boilerplate.
- **Best interests standard**: Frame all provisions around the child's best interests — this is the universal judicial standard.
- **Enforceability**: Use specific dates, times, and deadlines. Vague language ("reasonable time") invites disputes.
- **Neutral tone**: Draft as a bilateral agreement, not an advocacy document. Avoid characterizations of either parent.
- **Age-appropriate**: Adjust schedules and communication provisions for children's developmental stages. Infant/toddler schedules differ significantly from adolescent schedules.
- **FRE 408 awareness**: If plan is part of settlement negotiations, include appropriate confidentiality language.
- **Mark uncertain citations**: Use `[VERIFY]` for any statutory citation not confirmed through research.
