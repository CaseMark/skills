# Historic Preservation Law Summary

Delivers a thematic, jurisdiction-aware legal summary of historic preservation statutes, cases, and regulatory frameworks.

## Prerequisites

1. **Jurisdiction scope** — federal only, specific state(s), and/or specific locality(ies)
2. **Audience and purpose** — developer, agency, advocate, counsel, court submission, or internal memo
3. **Time horizon** — include recent developments or fixed cutoff date
4. **Source set** — uploaded materials, known ordinances, prior memos, or request to locate authorities
5. **Focus topics** — designation, takings, enforcement, incentives, zoning/land use, environmental review

## Output Structure / Process

1. **Scope Header**
   - Jurisdictions covered
   - Coverage period
   - Source list (uploaded + publicly available)

2. **Executive Overview**
   - 4–8 bullets summarizing core principles and practical effects

   - Provide short, authority-backed synthesis for each topic

| Topic | Rule/Standard | Leading Authority | Practical Implication |

6. **Procedure Checklist**
   - For the relevant jurisdiction, list the procedural steps
   - Use short, directive items

Checklist:
- Identify applicable designation criteria
- Confirm notice and hearing requirements
- Document administrative record standards
- Map appeal routes and timelines
- Verify permit sequencing with zoning/building

7. **Evidentiary Standards**
   - Summarize how agencies/courts assess:
   - Historical significance
   - Architectural integrity
   - Economic hardship
   - Alternatives analysis

8. **Jurisdiction Variations**
   - Compare state/local differences
   - Note preemption, delegation, or home-rule impacts

9. **Open Questions / Splits**
   - Highlight unresolved issues or regional splits
   - Mark items that require updated research

10. **Forward-Looking Trends**
   - Emerging categories (e.g., mid-century, cultural sites)
   - Climate/adaptation pressures
   - Pending legislation or notable docket items

## Guidelines

- Use Bluebook citations for every legal assertion.
- Mark uncertain authority with `[VERIFY]`.
- Separate federal, state, and local rules; do not conflate.
- Summarize holdings neutrally; avoid advocacy tone.
- Flag jurisdiction-specific procedural traps (notice, appeals, record).
- Note constitutional constraints on preservation restrictions.
- Avoid legal advice; provide informational analysis only.
- If local ordinance text is missing, state the gap explicitly and request it.
