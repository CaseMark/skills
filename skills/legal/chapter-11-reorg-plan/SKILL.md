# Chapter 11 Plan of Reorganization

Drafts a statutory-compliant Plan of Reorganization governing debtor restructuring under Chapter 11, with precise claim classifications, treatment provisions, and implementation mechanics.

## Prerequisites

1. **Debtor identification** — full legal name, DBA names, jurisdiction of organization, principal place of business
2. **Case information** — case number, court venue, petition date, filing circumstances
3. **Financial data** — asset valuations, liability amounts, creditor classifications, existing financing
4. **Claims register** — all filed claims with disputed amounts, secured positions, priority designations
5. **Executory contracts/leases** — complete list with cure amounts
6. **Funding sources** — exit financing term sheets, projected cash flow, asset sale proceeds, capital contributions
7. **Schedules/disclosures** — schedules of assets and liabilities, SOFAs, monthly operating reports, disclosure statement

## Output Structure

Draft the plan using the following article structure. Each article must satisfy the referenced statutory provisions.

### Article I — Preamble & Introduction

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Debtor identification | Legal name, DBAs, state of org, principal place of business |
| Statutory authority | § 1121(a) (exclusive period) or § 1121(c) (non-debtor proponent) |
| Case narrative | Concise summary of distress, post-petition performance, reorganization strategy |

### Article II — Definitions

Include definitions for all of the following categories:

**Statutory terms** (cite specific Code sections):
- Bankruptcy Code, Bankruptcy Court, Bankruptcy Rules
- Administrative Claim (§ 503(b)), Allowed Claim (§ 502), Secured Claim (§ 506(a))
- Priority Tax Claim (§§ 507(a)(8), 1129(a)(9)(C)), Priority Claim (§ 507(a))
- Impaired/Unimpaired (§ 1124), Estate (§ 541), Creditor (§ 101(10))

**Temporal terms** (use actual dates where known):
- Petition Date, Confirmation Date, Effective Date, Plan Supplement Filing Date

**Party terms:**
- Debtor, Reorganized Debtor (note any name changes), Holder, Disbursing Agent

**Operational terms:**
- Distribution Date, Professional Fee Claim, Convenience Class, Disputed Claim
- Plan-specific: Exit Facility, New Common Stock, Litigation Trust (as applicable)

### Article III — Classification (§ 1122)

**Unclassified claims** (paid in full on Effective Date per § 1129(a)(9)):
- Administrative expenses (§ 503(b))
- Priority tax claims (§§ 507(a)(8), 1129(a)(9)(C))

**Classified claims** — create separate classes using this framework:

| Class | Description | Impairment | Notes |
|-------|-------------|------------|-------|
| 1 | Secured Claims (per collateral pool) | Varies | Separate class per distinct collateral; note § 506(a) allowed amount vs. deficiency |
| 2 | Priority Unsecured Claims (§ 507(a)(4)-(5)) | Typically unimpaired | Wage, employee benefit claims |
| 3 | General Unsecured Claims | Typically impaired | Largest class by creditor count |
| 3A | Convenience Class (optional) | Unimpaired | Small claims below threshold; streamlines administration |
| 4 | Subordinated Claims | Impaired | Agreement, statutory, or equitable subordination |
| 5 | Equity Interests | Impaired | Common/preferred; separate classes if complex capital structure |

For each class, state: class designation, precise description, estimated aggregate amount, impairment status.

### Article IV — Treatment Provisions

For each class, specify:

**Unimpaired classes:**
- Payment in full in cash on Effective Date, or rights unaltered per § 1124
- Source of funds

**Impaired classes — required detail:**

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Recovery percentage | Calculated against allowed claim amount |
| Form of distribution | Cash, property, new securities, or combination |
| Payment timing | Lump sum vs. installment schedule |
| Interest rate | For deferred payments (market rate for cramdown) |
| Funding source | Operations, exit facility, asset sales, contributions |
| Present value | Discount rate and PV calculation for installment payments |

**Secured claim treatments** (select one per class):
- Cure and reinstatement (§ 1124(2)) — cure all defaults, restore maturity
- Modification (§ 1129(b)(2)(A)) — new terms, market interest, lien retention + deferred cash ≥ allowed amount
- Surrender of collateral
- Indubitable equivalent (§ 1129(b)(2)(A)(iii))

**Equity treatment:**
- State whether cancelled, diluted, or preserved
- If cancelled with impaired dissenting senior class: cite absolute priority rule (§ 1129(b)(2)(B))
- If preserved despite impaired classes: address new value contribution per circuit law

**Confirmation tests** — demonstrate compliance for each class:
- Best interests test (§ 1129(a)(7)): recovery ≥ Chapter 7 liquidation value
- Reference liquidation analysis from disclosure statement

### Article V — Implementation Mechanics

**Funding sources** — quantify each:

| Source | Amount | Status |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Cash on hand (projected Effective Date) | $ | |
| Operating cash flow | $ | Reference projections |
| Asset sales (§ 363 or post-confirmation) | $ | Timeline, process |
| Exit financing | $ | Terms, lender, commitment status |
| Capital contributions | $ | Source, conditions |

**Post-confirmation operations:**
- Business changes, cost reductions, strategic initiatives
- Management team (continuing or new) with qualifications
- Corporate governance: board composition, investor rights, org doc modifications

**Securities compliance:**
- New securities exempt under § 1145 (exchange for claims) or § 4(a)(2) (sophisticated investors)
- Transfer restrictions and registration rights

### Article VI — Distribution Procedures

- **Disbursing Agent**: identity and duties (calculations, records, tax forms, disputes)
- **Initial Distribution Date**: later of Effective Date or 10 business days after disputed claim becomes allowed
- **Method**: check (default) or wire transfer above threshold
- **Disputed Claims Reserve**: reserve distributable amount pending resolution; distribute upon allowance as if allowed on initial date
- **Unclaimed distributions**: revert to Reorganized Debtor after 120 days–1 year; claim discharged and barred
- **Tax compliance**: withholding per applicable law; require W-9/W-8 before distribution
- **Priority waterfall**: Administrative → Priority → Secured → General Unsecured → Equity (no junior distribution until senior paid in full unless cramdown satisfied)

### Article VII — Executory Contracts & Leases (§ 365)

- **Assumed**: list by counterparty, description, cure amount; cure paid in full on Effective Date per § 365(b)(1)
- **Cure disputes**: resolved by bankruptcy court; contract assumed upon court-determined cure payment
- **Rejected**: list specifically, or provide that unlisted contracts deemed rejected on Effective Date
- **Rejection claims**: treated as general unsecured; bar date = 30 days post-Effective Date
- **Special contracts**:
  - IP licenses (§ 365(n)): licensee election to retain rights
  - Real property leases (§ 365(d)): timely assumption/rejection
  - CBAs (§ 1113): confirm compliance if modified/rejected

### Article VIII — Conditions Precedent

**To Confirmation:**
- Entry of confirmation order
- Court findings under § 1129(a) or cramdown under § 1129(b)
- Disclosure statement approved (§ 1125)
- At least one impaired class accepted (§ 1129(a)(10))

**To Effective Date:**
- Confirmation order is final and not stayed
- Regulatory approvals and third-party consents obtained
- Exit financing closed on acceptable terms
- Capital contributions received; asset sales completed

**Waiver/deadline:**
- Debtor may waive Effective Date conditions (except non-waivable as matter of law) without notice or court approval
- Deadline: 60–90 days post-confirmation; if not met, confirmation order vacated, plan void
- Debtor may extend by filing notice (no hearing required)

### Article IX — Confirmation Effects (§ 1141)

- **Binding effect**: plan binds debtor, Reorganized Debtor, all holders regardless of vote (§ 1141(a))
- **Discharge** (§ 1141(d)): all pre-confirmation debt discharged on Effective Date
- **Injunction**: permanent injunction against actions on discharged claims (commencing actions, enforcing judgments, creating liens, asserting setoff)
- **Vesting** (§ 1141(b)-(c)): estate property vests in Reorganized Debtor free and clear on Effective Date
- **Releases**: consensual debtor releases from accepting/receiving creditors; third-party releases (officers, directors, professionals, committee members) limited per circuit law, supported by consideration, prominently disclosed
- **Exculpation**: fiduciary protection for good-faith actions; carve-out for gross negligence/willful misconduct

### Article X — Retained Jurisdiction (§§ 1142, 105)

Exclusive jurisdiction retained for:
- Plan interpretation and implementation disputes
- Claim allowance/disallowance and objections
- Professional fee applications
- Post-confirmation plan modifications (§ 1127)
- Executory contract disputes (cure amounts, adequate assurance)
- Tax liability determinations
- Estate asset recovery and avoidance actions (Chapter 5)
- Distribution disputes and improper distribution recovery
- Enforcement of discharge and plan injunctions
- Entry of final decree (Bankruptcy Rule 3022)

### Article XI — Miscellaneous

- **Severability**: invalid provisions severed; remainder enforceable
- **Amendments**: pre-confirmation per § 1127(a)/Rule 3019; post-confirmation per § 1127(b) (notice/hearing, resolicitation if materially adverse)
- **Governing law**: state law of debtor's organization (or court location) except where federal law applies; plan controls over disclosure statement
- **Notices**: written, to specified addresses; deemed given on delivery or 3 business days after mailing
- **Statutory fees**: 28 U.S.C. § 1930 fees and quarterly UST reports until case closed/converted/dismissed
- **Substantial consummation** (§ 1101(2)): distributions commenced + business assumed + restructuring transactions completed
- **Plan supplement**: filed ≥ 7 days before voting deadline; includes assumed/rejected contract schedules, org docs, officer/director identity and compensation, new security agreements

## Guidelines

- All cross-references must be verified for accuracy before finalizing
- Section numbering: Article I → Section 1.1 → (a) → (i)
- Include table of contents if plan exceeds 10 pages
- Coordinate with disclosure statement (§ 1125) — adequate information standard
- **Cramdown readiness** (§ 1129(b)): if any impaired class rejects, verify fair-and-equitable and no-unfair-discrimination for each class
- **Feasibility** (§ 1129(a)(11)): financial projections must demonstrate reasonable probability of success
- **Good faith** (§ 1129(a)(3)): plan proposed with honest and legitimate purposes
- Required exhibits: liquidation analysis, financial projections, executory contract schedules, new security forms, org documents
- Mark any uncertain statutory citations with [VERIFY]
- Adapt to local bankruptcy rules of the relevant court venue
