# Charitable Solicitation Registration

Prepares a complete, jurisdiction-specific registration package enabling a nonprofit to legally solicit charitable contributions while satisfying applicable state regulatory requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Organizational identity** — exact legal name (as on IRS determination letter), EIN, state/date of formation, legal structure (nonprofit corp, charitable trust, unincorporated association)
2. **IRS determination letter** — 501(c)(3) or other applicable exemption and effective date
3. **Formation documents** — articles of incorporation (state-certified within 6 months), current bylaws or trust instrument
4. **Financial records** — most recent Form 990 (all schedules), audited/reviewed/compiled financials for most recent fiscal year; pro forma projections with stated assumptions if newly formed
5. **Fundraising plan** — methods, geographic scope, projected revenue, and executed professional fundraiser contracts if applicable
6. **Governance roster** — all officers and directors with full contact info, titles, terms, and signatory authority over bank accounts
7. **Adverse history** — prior registration denials/revocations, litigation, regulatory actions, bankruptcy filings, and criminal convictions of officers (lookback period: 5–10 years depending on jurisdiction)
8. **Target jurisdictions** — all states where solicitation will occur, including states where the organization's website or online campaigns are accessible

## Output Structure

### 1. Jurisdictional Nexus Map

For each target state, determine:

| Factor | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| Physical presence | Offices, employees, mailing addresses |
| Digital nexus | Donation button, online campaigns, social media, peer-to-peer platforms |
| Revenue threshold | Does org exceed state's registration floor? |
| Registration required? | Yes / No / Exemption available |
| Filing deadline | Before solicitation begins vs. grace period |
| Renewal cycle | Annual / biennial |
| Bonding requirement | Amount; scales with projected revenue? |
| Filing method | Mail / state portal / URS |

> **Note**: 41 states + D.C. require registration. Many assert jurisdiction over out-of-state organizations based solely on website accessibility to state residents.

### 2. Exemption Analysis

Assess each target jurisdiction for available statutory exemptions:

- [ ] **Religious organizations** — soliciting only from own congregation or membership
- [ ] **Educational institutions** — regionally accredited
- [ ] **Hospitals / healthcare** — state-licensed facilities
- [ ] **Membership organizations** — soliciting exclusively from established members
- [ ] **Small org revenue threshold** — varies by state (commonly $25K–$50K) [VERIFY per jurisdiction]
- [ ] **Federated campaigns** — all contributions received via United Way or similar federated fund
- [ ] **Government grantees** — no independent public solicitation conducted

> Even when exempt from registration, many states require a filed **notice of exemption claim** and periodic reaffirmation that exemption criteria remain satisfied. Cite the specific statutory provision for each claimed exemption; burden of proof rests with the organization.

### 3. Registration Package Components

**Core forms:**
- [ ] State-specific registration form or Unified Registration Statement (URS) with any required state supplements
- [ ] Signed certification/perjury attestation — president/CEO, and CFO if required by jurisdiction
- [ ] Board resolution authorizing filing and designating signatories

**Required attachments (standard across most states):**
- [ ] IRS determination letter
- [ ] Certified articles of incorporation (within 6 months)
- [ ] Current bylaws or trust instrument
- [ ] Form 990 with all schedules (most recent fiscal year)
- [ ] Audited/reviewed/compiled financial statements (most recent fiscal year)
- [ ] Officer and director roster with addresses, titles, and terms
- [ ] Executed professional fundraiser or fundraising counsel contracts (if applicable)
- [ ] Surety bond (if required by jurisdiction)

|------|--------|

| Compensation structure | Flat fee / % gross receipts / hybrid |

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Method | Direct mail / online platform / telephone / door-to-door / events / crowdfunding / commercial co-venturer |

### 6. Mandatory Adverse Disclosures

For the organization and all officers within the applicable lookback period:

- [ ] Criminal convictions
- [ ] Prior registration denials, suspensions, revocations, or voluntary withdrawals in any jurisdiction
- [ ] Regulatory investigations or enforcement actions
- [ ] Bankruptcy filings
- [ ] Civil judgments involving charitable assets or fiduciary duty

For each disclosed item: describe the matter, current status or final resolution, and remedial measures implemented since.

### 7. Filing Checklist

- [ ] Verify current filing fee (varies by state; often revenue-tiered)
- [ ] Confirm acceptable payment methods (check, credit card, EFT — cash rarely accepted; some states require separate payments for initial vs. renewal)
- [ ] Determine filing method: mail to AG/Secretary of State / state portal / URS multi-state submission
- [ ] Confirm whether registration must precede all solicitation or grace period applies
- [ ] Note registration certificate issuance timeline and any required posting or notice obligations
- [ ] Identify required disclosure language for solicitation materials (registration number, percentage disclosures)
- [ ] Calendar all renewal deadlines by jurisdiction
- [ ] Identify jurisdictions offering expedited processing if immediate fundraising is needed

## Guidelines

- **Digital solicitation creates nexus**: A passive donation button accessible to state residents may trigger registration even without physical presence — analyze each state independently
- **URS accepted by ~40 states** but some require state-specific supplements or reject URS entirely; confirm current acceptance before filing [VERIFY current URS state acceptance list]
- **Exemptions construed narrowly**: Document every statutory element; confirm whether a notice of exemption filing is still required
- **Consistency is non-negotiable**: Reconcile all financial disclosures against Form 990 before submission
- **Professional fundraiser obligations are independent**: Most states require separate PFR registration and bonding — the organization's registration does not satisfy PFR requirements
- **Post-filing obligations**: Track certificate receipt, update solicitation materials with registration numbers, and file amended registrations for material changes (officer turnover, new fundraising methods, financial restatements)
- **Enforcement priority**: AG offices in CA, NY, IL, PA, and FL are most active; if phased filing is necessary due to budget constraints, prioritize these jurisdictions first
