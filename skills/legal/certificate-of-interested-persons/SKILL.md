# Certificate of Interested Persons

Drafts a FRAP 26.1-compliant certificate disclosing all persons and entities with a financial or legal interest in the outcome of a federal appeal.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case caption** — appellate case number, full names of all appellants/appellees as they appear on the notice of appeal
2. **Lower court filings** — complaints, answers, corporate disclosure statements, settlement agreements
3. **Corporate structure documents** — ownership charts, parent/subsidiary relationships, merger/acquisition records
4. **Representation details** — all law firms and attorneys of record for each party
5. **Target circuit** — specific U.S. Court of Appeals (disclosure requirements vary by circuit)

## Process

### Step 1: Identify Target Circuit Requirements

Research the destination circuit's local rules. Key variations:

| Requirement | Varies By Circuit |
|---|---|
| Document title | "Certificate of Interested Persons" vs. "Corporate Disclosure Statement" vs. "Disclosure of Corporate Affiliations and Financial Interests" |
| Listing order | Alphabetical vs. categorical by party affiliation |
| Amici disclosure | Some require anticipated amici; others only filed briefs |
| Negative statements | Some require explicit "none" for empty categories |
| Formatting | Single- vs. double-spaced lists; page limits |

### Step 2: Extract Interested Persons from Case Materials

Search all uploaded documents for every disclosable person/entity. Organize into these categories:

| Category | What to Disclose |
|---|---|
| **Named parties** | All parties including dismissed/settled parties; use complete legal names |
| **Counsel** | All law firms, office locations, individual attorneys of record |
| **Parent corporations** | Every layer up to ultimate parent; state if wholly-owned subsidiary |
| **Subsidiaries** | Those affected by litigation outcome |
| **10%+ stockholders** | Any publicly held company owning ≥10% of a party's stock; include percentage if known |
| **Insurers** | Companies providing coverage for claims at issue |
| **Litigation funders** | Third-party financiers with recovery interest |
| **Indemnitors/guarantors** | Entities obligated to satisfy judgment |
| **Bankruptcy parties** | Trustee, DIP, creditors' committee (if applicable) |
| **Amici curiae** | Trade associations, advocacy orgs that have filed or indicated intent to file |

### Step 3: Draft the Certificate

**Caption:**
- Full court name (e.g., "United States Court of Appeals for the Fifth Circuit")
- Appellate case number
- Party names exactly as on notice of appeal
- Document title per local rule

**Disclosure body:**
- List each person/entity with complete legal name (no abbreviations or trade names)
- Include relationship descriptor (e.g., "Parent corporation of Defendant XYZ Corp.")
- For corporate parties, trace full ownership chain

**Certification statement must:**
- Reference FRAP 26.1 and applicable local rule by number
- Attest completeness after reasonable inquiry
- Acknowledge continuing duty to supplement
- Comply with FRAP 32(g) certification requirements

**Signature block:**
- Attorney printed name and signature (compliant with CM/ECF e-filing requirements)
- Bar number and jurisdiction of admission
- Firm name, street address, phone, email
- Date of execution

### Step 4: Quality Control Checklist

- [ ] All names spelled correctly with proper capitalization
- [ ] Complete legal names used (not trade names or abbreviations)
- [ ] Every required category addressed (including negative statements where required)
- [ ] Corporate ownership chains fully traced
- [ ] Consistent with any prior trial-court disclosure statements
- [ ] Changes in corporate structure or representation since trial court identified
- [ ] Formatting meets circuit specifications (margins, typeface, spacing)
- [ ] Page limits respected
- [ ] Properly dated and signed

## Guidelines

- **Completeness over brevity** — omitting an interested person risks recusal motions and procedural delays; when in doubt, disclose
- **Verify corporate structures with the client** — public filings may be outdated; confirm current ownership before filing
- **Flag changes from trial court** — if corporate structure or representation changed during litigation, note the update explicitly
- **Never abbreviate entity names** — courts use these for automated conflict checks
- **Supplement promptly** — if new interested persons are discovered post-filing, file a supplemental certificate immediately
- **Circuit-specific rules control** — always check the local rules; FRAP 26.1 sets the floor, not the ceiling
