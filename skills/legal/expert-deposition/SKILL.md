# Expert Witness Deposition

Builds a motion-ready expert deposition record or a defense‑prep plan tailored to Daubert/Frye admissibility.

## Prerequisites

1. Governing rules and venue standard (Daubert, Frye, or state hybrid) and any local expert disclosure rules
2. Rule 26(a)(2) expert report(s) and disclosure materials
3. Expert CV, publications list, and prior testimony list/transcripts if available
4. Case facts and key documents the expert did and did not review
5. Known alternative theories, critiques, or rebuttal materials

## Output Structure / Process

### Intake Snapshot

| Field | Required Detail |
|---|---|
| Jurisdiction | Federal/State, Daubert/Frye, local expert rules |
| Expert | Name, field, retention side, role |
| Opinions | Enumerated list from report |
| Methodology | Stated methods, standards, tools, testing |
| Materials | Documents reviewed, missing, assumed facts |
| Bias | Compensation, retention history, firm relationship |

### Deliverable A: Taking Expert Deposition

**Outline Template**

```text
I. Qualifications and Boundaries
II. Engagement, Scope, and Compensation
III. Materials Reviewed and Assumptions
IV. Methodology and Reliability
V. Each Opinion (state, basis, certainty)
VI. Application to Case Facts
VII. Prior Testimony and Publications
VIII. Lock‑In and Completeness
```

**Question Bank (use for each topic)**

| Topic | Objective | Sample Prompts |
|---|---|---|
| Qualifications | Define limits | “Your expertise is X, not Y, correct?” “How recent is your hands‑on work in this area?” |
| Engagement/Bias | Show incentives | “Rate for review/testimony?” “Percent of income from litigation?” “Prior exclusions or criticism?” |
| Materials | Show gaps | “What did you review?” “Did you see [critical doc]?” “Any independent investigation?” |
| Methodology | Daubert record | “Tested? Peer‑reviewed? Error rate?” “Standards controlling use?” “Alternative methods?” |
| Opinions | Lock in | “State the opinion precisely.” “Basis and data?” “Degree of certainty?” |
| Application | Fit to facts | “Inputs used?” “Calculations?” “If X fact is wrong, opinion changes?” |
| Impeachment | Inconsistency | “In [case/publication], you said ___?” “What changed?” |

**Lock‑In Questions**

- “Are those all opinions you intend to offer?”
- “Any opinions not in your report?”
- “Any bases not stated in your report?”

**Daubert/Frye Record Checklist**

- [ ] Qualifications limited in relevant sub‑field
- [ ] Method not testable or not tested
- [ ] No peer review or publication
- [ ] Error rate unknown or unacceptable
- [ ] No governing standards or not followed
- [ ] Not generally accepted in the field (Frye)
- [ ] Unreliable application to case facts
- [ ] Insufficient facts/data or unverified assumptions
- [ ] Failure to consider contrary evidence
- [ ] Opinion not helpful to trier of fact (Rule 702)

### Deliverable B: Defending Expert Deposition

**Preparation Plan**

| Session | Goal | Materials |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Report Mastery | Clear explanation of each opinion | Final report, exhibits |
| 2. Methodology Defense | Explain reliability and fit | Standards, literature, calculations |
| 3. Mock Deposition | Practice hostile questions | Prior testimony, publications |

**Coaching Points**

- Explain, don’t advocate
- Stay within expertise boundaries
- Acknowledge limitations without overstating certainty
- Use plain language understandable to a jury
- Separate facts provided by counsel from independent analysis

**Work Product and Discovery Boundaries (FRCP 26)**

| Protected | Discoverable |
|---|---|
| Draft reports (26(b)(4)(B)) | Facts/data considered |
| Attorney‑expert communications about draft opinions (26(b)(4)(C)) | Assumptions provided by counsel |
| Attorney mental impressions | Compensation terms |

**Objection Guide**

- “Objection, work product” for draft report inquiries
- “Objection, attorney‑client privilege” when applicable
- Preserve objections but allow testimony on facts/data considered

## Guidelines

- Verify the governing admissibility standard and adapt the methodology attack accordingly (Daubert vs Frye).
- Tie every methodology question to Rule 702’s reliability and fit requirements and Rule 703 bases.
- Do not allow opinions outside the report unless strategically beneficial; force explicit lock‑in.
- Avoid coaching experts to be evasive; credibility hinges on clarity and candor.
- Flag jurisdictional variations in expert disclosure scope and deposition limits.
- Preserve privilege and work‑product objections while allowing discovery into facts/data and assumptions.
- Cite controlling authority in the final outline when helpful: FRCP 26(a)(2), 26(b)(4), FRE 702–703, Daubert, Joiner, Kumho, Frye.
