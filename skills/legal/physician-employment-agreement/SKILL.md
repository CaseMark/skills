# Physician Employment Agreement

Drafts a regulatory-compliant employment agreement between a healthcare employer and a licensed physician, balancing clinical practice requirements with federal and state healthcare law constraints.

## Prerequisites

1. **Employer details** — entity type (PC, LLC, hospital system, academic medical center), state of organization, principal place of business
2. **Physician details** — full name, license number, state(s) of licensure, board certification status, specialty/subspecialty, DEA registration
3. **Position terms** — FT/PT, clinical hours, practice location(s), call coverage expectations
4. **Compensation structure** — base salary, productivity metrics, bonus criteria, signing bonus, relocation assistance
5. **Benefits summary** — insurance, retirement plan type, CME allowance, PTO policy, tail coverage terms
6. **Restrictive covenant parameters** — geographic radius, duration, buyout option, applicable state law on physician non-competes
7. **Any predecessor agreement** being superseded

## Output Structure

Draft the agreement using the following sections in order:

### 1. Parties & Recitals

| Element | Details |
|---|---|
| Employer | Full legal name, entity type, state of organization, principal address |
| Physician | Full name, license #, state(s) of licensure, board cert status, residence |
| Recitals | Specialty retained for, physician qualifications, effective date, predecessor agreements superseded |

### 2. Position & Duties

- Title, specialty, primary practice location(s)
- FT/PT definition (clinical hours + administrative hours per week/month)
- Clinical scope: patient types, procedures, call coverage, hospital privileges required
- Administrative duties: QI committees, teaching/supervision, research, meetings
- Reporting structure and supervisory responsibilities
- Credential maintenance: license, board cert, DEA, privileges, CME
- Outside activities/moonlighting policy (permitted, prohibited, or conditional)

### 3. Compensation & Reimbursements

- **Base salary**: annual amount, pay schedule
- **Variable compensation**: productivity metrics (wRVUs, collections), quality incentives, bonus formulas
- **Stark/AKS compliance**: compensation must reflect fair market value; variable pay must not correlate to volume/value of referrals [VERIFY specific safe harbor relied upon]
- **Salary review**: timing, COLA, merit increase process
- **Reimbursable expenses**: malpractice tail, licensing fees, society dues, CME costs, journals
- **Sign-on/relocation/loan repayment**: amounts and repayment clawback schedule if early termination

### 4. Benefits

| Benefit | Key Terms to Specify |
|---|---|
| Health insurance | Medical/dental/vision, employer contribution %, dependent coverage, effective date |
| Retirement | 401(k)/403(b)/pension, employer match, vesting schedule |
| Malpractice insurance | Occurrence vs. claims-made, coverage limits, **tail coverage** on termination (critical) |
| PTO | Vacation, sick, personal, holidays — accrual rates, carryover, payout on termination |
| CME | Annual allowance amount, permitted uses, approval process |
| Other | Disability, life insurance, parking, professional development |

### 5. Term & Termination

- **Term**: definite (1-3 years) vs. at-will; auto-renewal terms; non-renewal notice period
- **Without cause**: notice period (typically 90-180 days); severance; benefit continuation
- **With cause** — enumerate grounds:
  - [ ] Loss of medical license or DEA registration
  - [ ] Exclusion from federal healthcare programs (OIG/GSA)
  - [ ] Felony conviction or crime of moral turpitude
  - [ ] Material breach (with cure period if applicable)
  - [ ] Gross negligence or willful misconduct
  - [ ] Violation of employer policies
  - [ ] Failure to maintain credentials/privileges
- **Death/disability**: define disability (inability to perform essential functions for __ consecutive days)
- **Post-termination obligations**: patient transition plan, records handoff, property return, cooperation

### 6. Restrictive Covenants

> **State-specific enforceability varies significantly.** Confirm applicable state law before drafting. Some states (e.g., CA, CO, OK) prohibit or severely limit physician non-competes. [VERIFY current status in target jurisdiction]

- **Non-compete**: geographic scope (radius or named counties), duration (1-2 years), restricted specialty
- **Non-solicitation**: patients, employees, referral sources — duration
- **Buyout option**: liquidated damages amount for practicing in restricted area
- **Confidentiality**: employer proprietary info (patient lists, referral sources, financials, business strategy) — survives termination indefinitely
- HIPAA obligations are baseline; confidentiality clause must extend beyond HIPAA to cover business information

### 7. Intellectual Property & Records

- **Work product ownership**: employer owns work created within scope of employment using employer resources
- **Physician retains**: scholarly publications, presentations (clarify boundary)
- **Revenue sharing**: process for commercialized inventions/discoveries if applicable
- **Medical records**: employer property; physician access post-termination for continuity of care and malpractice defense
- **Documentation obligations**: timely completion, compliance with HIPAA and state record-retention laws

### 8. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

- Governing state law (typically state of primary practice)
- Dispute resolution sequence:
  1. Good-faith negotiation
  2. Mediation
  3. Arbitration (AAA/JAMS — specify rules, location, arbitrator count, cost allocation) OR litigation
- Carve-out for injunctive relief (restrictive covenants, confidentiality) — court access preserved
- Attorney's fees: prevailing party or each party bears own

### 9. General Provisions

Include: severability (with judicial modification provision), entire agreement/merger, amendment (written, signed by both), waiver, assignment (physician non-assignable; employer may assign to successor), notices (addresses + delivery methods), counterparts, survival clause (identify surviving sections: confidentiality, non-compete, indemnification, dispute resolution).

### 10. Signature Blocks

- Employer: authorized signatory name, title, date; note if board approval required
- Physician: printed name, date

## Guidelines

- All compensation terms must satisfy **fair market value** under Stark Law (42 U.S.C. § 1395nn) and not vary with volume/value of referrals
- Structure productivity compensation to fit an **Anti-Kickback Statute safe harbor** (42 U.S.C. § 1320a-7b) — typically the employment safe harbor [VERIFY 42 C.F.R. § 1001.952(i)]
- Confirm non-compete enforceability in the target state before including; draft alternative non-solicitation if non-compete is unenforceable
- Tail coverage allocation is a frequent negotiation point — specify clearly who pays and under what termination scenarios
- Flag any provisions requiring separate compliance review (e.g., academic medical center conflict-of-interest policies, tax-exempt entity intermediate sanctions under IRC § 4958)
- Do not include provisions that could be construed as conditioning employment on referral volume
- Include HIPAA Business Associate provisions only if the relationship structure requires it (typically not for direct employment)
