# Case Valuation & Settlement Analysis

Produces data-driven case valuations and settlement range recommendations.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case facts** — liability assessment, comparative fault analysis
2. **Medical records** — completed summarization with severity assessment
3. **Damages calculation** — economic and non-economic damages computed
4. **Comparable data** — jury verdicts and settlements in the jurisdiction for similar injuries

## Workflow

### 1. Liability Assessment

- Strength of liability: clear (90%+), strong (70-90%), contested (50-70%), weak (<50%)
- Comparative fault exposure: percentage attributable to plaintiff
- Insurance coverage: policy limits, umbrella policies, assets

### 2. Injury Severity Scoring

- Injury type and permanence
- Treatment duration and intensity
- Impact on daily life and employment
- Future medical needs

### 3. Comparable Analysis

- Search verdict databases for same jurisdiction, similar injuries, similar liability
- Adjust for: venue differences, economic conditions, plaintiff demographics
- Identify range: 25th percentile (conservative) to 75th percentile (aggressive)

### 4. Produce Valuation

- Settlement range: low / midpoint / high
- Policy limits analysis: is the case worth more than available coverage?
- Risk-adjusted value: account for trial risk and litigation costs
- Recommendation: settle vs. litigate with reasoning

## Pitfalls

- **Policy limits ceiling** — a case worth $2M against a $100K policy has a practical cap
- **Venue matters enormously** — the same case can be worth 3x more in one county vs. another
- **Don't ignore litigation costs** — a $500K case that costs $200K to try nets less than a $350K settlement
- **Update valuations** — case value changes as discovery progresses; reassess at key milestones
