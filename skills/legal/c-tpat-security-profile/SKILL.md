# C-TPAT Security Profile

Produces a CBP-ready C-TPAT Security Profile grounded in documented, verifiable practices.

## Prerequisites

1. **Corporate identifiers** — legal name, EIN, DUNS, headquarters, facility list, C-TPAT account/tier if enrolled.
2. **Supply chain map** — roles (importer/broker/forwarder), trade lanes, origin countries, product categories, volume.
3. **Security governance** — org chart, C-TPAT coordinator, reporting lines, budgets, authority.
4. **Policies & SOPs** — physical, personnel, procedural, conveyance, IT security procedures.
5. **Risk assessment artifacts** — methodology, frequency, recent assessments, mitigation plans.
6. **Training records** — onboarding, refresher, role-based training, attendance logs.
7. **Business partner vetting** — questionnaires, audits, certifications, corrective actions.
8. **Incident logs** — security events, seal discrepancies, investigations, remediation.
9. **Records management** — retention schedule and retrieval procedures.
10. **Validation history** — prior CBP validations, findings, corrective actions.

## Guidelines

- Use only verifiable, current practices; avoid future-tense promises.
- Mirror CBP Minimum Security Criteria structure where possible.
- Ensure internal consistency across all sections and exhibits.
- Identify all facilities and trade lanes; avoid omissions.
- Do not claim certifications or tier status without documentation.
- If citing regulations or specific CBP guidance, confirm accuracy and mark uncertain citations with `[VERIFY]`.
- Keep confidentiality markings consistent with company policy and CBP submission expectations.
