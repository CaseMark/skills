# Expungement Petition

Drafts a petition for expungement or sealing of criminal records that satisfies jurisdiction-specific statutory requirements and presents a compelling case for relief.

## Prerequisites

1. **Jurisdiction identified** — state/county, applicable statute (expungement, sealing, set-aside, or certificate of rehabilitation), local court rules and formatting requirements
2. **Criminal record details** — for each incident: arrest date, arresting agency, charges (common name + statutory citation), case/docket number, court, disposition, sentence, completion date
3. **Petitioner identification** — full legal name (as it appeared in criminal proceedings), DOB, address, any name changes with documentation
4. **Eligibility confirmed** — waiting periods satisfied, offense category eligible, no statutory disqualifiers
5. **Rehabilitation evidence** — employment history, education, community involvement, treatment programs, character references

## Output Structure

### 1. Court Caption

| Element | Requirements |
|---------|-------------|
| Court name | Full legal name, county/district, state |
| Case number | Original criminal case number if related; "to be assigned" if new civil petition |
| Petitioner name | Exactly as in criminal proceedings; aliases noted parenthetically |
| Document title | Jurisdiction-appropriate: "Petition for Expungement," "Motion to Seal," "Petition for Certificate of Rehabilitation," etc. |
| Formatting | Per local rules: font, margins, line spacing, page numbering |

### 2. Petitioner Identification & Standing

- Full legal name, address, DOB, contact information
- Name change explanation if applicable (marriage, legal change)
- Jurisdictional connection (residency status, duration)
- Statement confirming identity as the individual in the criminal matter
- Privacy considerations — flag if identifying info should be filed under seal per local rules

### 3. Records Sought for Expungement

For each record, present in chronological order:

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Date of arrest | Exact date |
| Arresting agency | Full name |
| Charges filed | Common name + statutory citation |
| Court / Case no. | Court name + docket number |
| Disposition | Dismissed (with/without prejudice), plea, trial conviction |
| Sentence | If convicted: sentence imposed, completion date, probation/parole details |
| Post-conviction relief | Any modifications, early termination already granted |

Flag whether multiple cases require a single petition or separate filings per local rules.

### 4. Legal Grounds & Statutory Eligibility

Structure as a checklist against the controlling statute:

```
Controlling authority: [Statute citation + quoted operative language]

☐ Waiting period satisfied: [Period required] → [Date eligible] → [Current date]
☐ Offense category eligible: [Offense classification] → [Statutory category]
☐ Sentence fully completed: [Completion date including probation/parole]
☐ No disqualifying convictions: [Address criminal history requirements]
☐ Additional requirements: [Rehabilitation showing / interests of justice / other]
```

- Distinguish any facts that appear disqualifying but are not under careful statutory reading
- This section must demonstrate entitlement to relief as a matter of law

### 5. Rehabilitation & Equitable Considerations

Present as a structured narrative covering:

- **Employment** — history, promotions, professional licenses obtained
- **Education** — degrees, vocational training, certifications
- **Family** — dependents, caregiving responsibilities
- **Community** — volunteer work, civic engagement, charitable activities
- **Treatment** — substance abuse programs, counseling, anger management (with completion documentation)
- **Harm from record** — specific denials: employment, housing, licensing, education
- **Public interest** — how expungement serves rehabilitative policy goals

Reference attached exhibits (certificates, employment records, character letters) by exhibit label.

### 6. Prayer for Relief

Request all of the following as authorized by the controlling statute:

1. Order expunging/sealing all identified records (court records, law enforcement, prosecutor files)
2. Specify relief type: physical destruction, sealing from public access, or confidential treatment
3. Authorization for petitioner to lawfully deny arrest/conviction per statutory protections
4. Order directing all agencies to update databases and confirm compliance
5. Retention of jurisdiction to enforce the expungement order
6. "Any other relief the Court deems just and proper"

Do NOT request relief beyond what the statute authorizes.

### 7. Verification & Signature Block

- Verification under penalty of perjury (determine if notarization required or unsworn declaration sufficient)
- Petitioner signature line: printed name, date
- Attorney signature line (if applicable): name, bar number, firm, address, phone, email
- E-filing compliance if applicable

## Guidelines

- **Terminology matters** — use the jurisdiction's specific term (expungement, sealing, set-aside, certificate of rehabilitation) consistently throughout
- **Precision on records** — the court order is limited to specifically identified records; ambiguity results in incomplete relief
- **Attach required filings** — check for mandatory cover sheets, civil case information statements, proposed orders, certificates of service, notices of hearing
- **Memorandum of law** — recommend a supporting memo if the case presents novel issues or requires distinguishing unfavorable precedent
- **All statutory citations must be verified** — mark any uncertain citation with [VERIFY]
- **Copy/fee requirements** — note local rules on number of copies and filing fees
- **Privacy** — flag sensitive information that may require redaction in public filings
