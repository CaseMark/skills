# Nonprofit Corporation Bylaws

Create a board-adoptable bylaws document that is consistent with the articles and state nonprofit law while preserving 501(c)(3) eligibility.

## Prerequisites

1. State of incorporation and applicable nonprofit corporation act.
2. Final or near-final articles of incorporation.
3. Tax-exempt status target (e.g., 501(c)(3)) and purpose statement.
4. Governance design decisions: membership vs non-membership, board size/terms, officer roles, committee model.
5. Operational policies to embed by reference: conflicts, fiscal controls, records retention.

## Output Structure / Process

Use this structure and include all marked items.

| Article | Required Content |
| --- | --- |
| I. Name and Offices | Exact corporate name; principal office address; board authority to change office; registered agent if desired. |
| II. Purpose and 501(c)(3) Limits | Exclusive exempt purpose; private inurement prohibition; political campaign ban; lobbying limits; dissolution distribution clause to 501(c)(3) or government. |
| III. Members | Either "no members" clause or membership classes, qualifications, dues, rights, meetings, quorum, votes, termination. |
| IV. Board of Directors | Number or range; qualifications; election/appointment; term length and staggering; removal; vacancies; powers; compensation limits; reimbursement. |
| V. Board Meetings | Regular/annual meeting cadence; special meeting call authority; notice method and timing; quorum; voting thresholds; written consent; remote participation; waivers; minutes. |
| VI. Officers | Required officer titles; duties; election; term; removal; vacancies; dual office limits; compensation approval standards. |
| VII. Committees | Board vs advisory committees; appointment; authority limits; meeting rules; reporting to board. |
| VIII. Fiscal and Records | Fiscal year; budget approval; contract authority; checks/signatures; audits/reviews; indemnification; D&O insurance; conflicts policy; corporate records and inspection rights. |
| IX. Amendments | Who may amend; notice; vote threshold; member approval if applicable. |
| X. Dissolution | Board and member approvals; asset distribution for exempt purposes; compliance with state dissolution procedures [VERIFY]. |

Drafting steps:

1. Confirm no conflicts with articles; if conflicts exist, revise bylaws or flag for amendment.
2. Select membership model and encode all member powers and meeting rules.
3. Set board size, term structure, and voting thresholds that comply with state defaults.
4. Insert required 501(c)(3) clauses verbatim or near-verbatim.
5. Define fiscal controls and conflict procedures suited to the client’s risk profile.
6. Add severability and adoption clause.

### Clause Templates (fill in brackets)

```text
Purpose: The Corporation is organized exclusively for [charitable/educational/etc.] purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

Private Inurement: No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its directors, officers, members, or private persons, except that the Corporation shall be authorized to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered.

Political Activity: The Corporation shall not participate in, or intervene in, any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office, and no substantial part of its activities shall be the carrying on of propaganda or otherwise attempting to influence legislation.

Dissolution: Upon dissolution, assets shall be distributed for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3), or to the federal government, or to a state or local government, for a public purpose.
```

### Membership Decision Checklist

- [ ] Corporation has members.
- [ ] Corporation has no members; all member powers are vested in the board.
- [ ] Member classes defined (if applicable).
- [ ] Member admission, dues, and termination procedures defined.
- [ ] Member meeting notice, quorum, and voting thresholds set.

### Board Governance Checklist

- [ ] Board size or range set and compliant with state minimums.
- [ ] Terms and staggering described.
- [ ] Removal and vacancy procedures stated.
- [ ] Quorum and voting thresholds set for ordinary and extraordinary actions.
- [ ] Written consent and remote participation authorized (if allowed by state law).
- [ ] Executive session rules addressed.

### Fiscal and Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Fiscal year aligned to Form 990 reporting.
- [ ] Budget approval process defined.
- [ ] Contract and expenditure authority thresholds set.
- [ ] Indemnification and D&O insurance authorized.
- [ ] Conflict of interest policy required and enforced.
- [ ] Records and inspection rights addressed.

## Guidelines

- Ensure bylaws mirror the articles of incorporation; articles control if inconsistent.
- Keep 501(c)(3) clauses intact; do not soften prohibitions on inurement or political activity.
- State nonprofit law varies on notice, quorum, remote meetings, and director removal; verify state-specific defaults before finalizing.
- If members exist, confirm member approval requirements for amendments, mergers, and dissolution.
- Officer compensation must be reasonable and approved under a conflicts policy; include comparability review if needed.
- Dissolution may require attorney general notice and charitable asset restrictions; verify state requirements [VERIFY].
- Avoid delegating non-delegable board powers to committees; list prohibited committee actions explicitly.
- Use clear defined terms and consistent cross-references; include severability and adoption clause.
