# ADA Failure to Accommodate Complaint

Drafts a complete, court-ready plaintiff's complaint for ADA failure to accommodate claims under Title I (employment) or Title III (public accommodations).

## Prerequisites

1. **Client identity** — full legal name, address, state of residence
2. **Disability documentation** — diagnosis, functional limitations, treating provider info
3. **Accommodation request record** — date, form (oral/written), recipient name/title, exact accommodation sought, any supporting medical documentation submitted to defendant
4. **Defendant profile** — full legal name, business form, principal place of business, employee count (Title I coverage threshold)
5. **Adverse action details** — termination, demotion, denial of access, or constructive discharge with dates and stated reasons
6. **EEOC record** (Title I only) — charge number, filing date, Right to Sue letter with issue date
7. **Damages information** — lost wages (start date), lost benefits, out-of-pocket costs, emotional distress treatment records

## Output Structure

### Caption & Header
- Court name and division
- Full party names; plaintiff identified as "an individual with a disability"
- Document title: `COMPLAINT FOR FAILURE TO PROVIDE REASONABLE ACCOMMODATION UNDER THE AMERICANS WITH DISABILITIES ACT`
- Space for clerk-assigned case number

---

### I. Nature of the Action (2–4 sentences)
- Statute invoked (Title I or Title III, 42 U.S.C. § 12101 et seq.)
- Plaintiff's disability and functional limitation
- Specific accommodation requested and denied
- Harm suffered

---

### II. Parties

| Party | Required Allegations |
|-------|---------------------|
| Plaintiff | Name, state of residence, disability status under 42 U.S.C. § 12102 |
| Defendant (Title I) | Full legal name, business form, principal place of business; employs **≥ 15 employees** for ≥ 20 calendar weeks — 42 U.S.C. § 12111(5) |
| Defendant (Title III) | Owns/leases/operates a place of public accommodation; category under 42 U.S.C. § 12181(7) |

---

### III. Jurisdiction & Venue

| Basis | Citation |
|-------|----------|
| Federal question | 28 U.S.C. § 1331 |
| Title I enforcement | 42 U.S.C. § 12117(a) (incorporates Title VII remedial scheme) |
| Title III enforcement | 42 U.S.C. § 12188 |
| Supplemental state claims | 28 U.S.C. § 1367(a) |
| Venue | 28 U.S.C. § 1391(b) — substantial events occurred in this district |

**EEOC Exhaustion (Title I — plead all three):**
- Charge timely filed within **300 days** (deferral states) or **180 days** (non-deferral states)
- Right to Sue issued on [date]
- Complaint filed within **90 days** of receipt — 42 U.S.C. § 2000e-5(f)(1)
- Attach Right to Sue letter as Exhibit A

---

### IV. Factual Allegations (chronological narrative)

Draft numbered paragraphs covering each of the following:

1. **Disability** — impairment type; how it substantially limits one or more major life activities (walking, standing, concentrating, working, communicating, etc.) per 42 U.S.C. § 12102; reference medical documentation provided to defendant
2. **Qualified individual** — education, experience, performance history; essential job functions plaintiff performed with or without informal accommodation prior to formal request
3. **Accommodation request** — exact date; oral or written; recipient's name and title; precise accommodation sought; any medical documentation submitted
4. **Interactive process failure** — allege defendant: failed to respond timely; refused to meet; did not ask clarifying questions; proposed no alternatives; acted in bad faith
5. **Denial and adverse action** — quote denial language verbatim if available; date of termination/demotion/exclusion; stated reasons
6. **Rebuttal of undue hardship** — low cost of accommodation; defendant's financial resources and size; availability of tax credits; minimal operational impact; comparable accommodations provided to others
7. **Damages** — lost wages from [date] to present; lost benefits (health insurance, retirement); out-of-pocket costs; emotional distress with treatment history; reputational and career harm
8. **Punitive damages basis** *(if applicable)* — pattern of disability discrimination; deliberate indifference to ADA obligations; statements of discriminatory animus; disregard of HR or legal counsel advice

---

### V. Causes of Action

#### Count I — Title I Failure to Accommodate, 42 U.S.C. § 12112(a) & (b)(5)(A)

- [ ] Plaintiff is an individual with a disability — 42 U.S.C. § 12102
- [ ] Plaintiff is a qualified individual who can perform essential job functions with or without accommodation
- [ ] Defendant had notice of plaintiff's disability and need for accommodation
- [ ] Plaintiff requested a reasonable accommodation
- [ ] Defendant failed to provide the accommodation or meaningfully engage in the interactive process
- [ ] Plaintiff suffered damages as a direct and proximate result

#### Count II — Title III Failure to Make Reasonable Modification *(if applicable)*
42 U.S.C. § 12182(a) & (b)(2)(A)(ii)

- [ ] Plaintiff is an individual with a disability
- [ ] Defendant owns, leases, or operates a place of public accommodation
- [ ] Plaintiff sought to access defendant's goods, services, or facilities
- [ ] Plaintiff requested a reasonable modification necessary for equal access
- [ ] Modification would not fundamentally alter the nature of the goods or services
- [ ] Defendant refused, denying full and equal enjoyment

#### Count III — Retaliation *(if applicable)*, 42 U.S.C. § 12203(a)

- [ ] Protected activity — accommodation request or opposition to discriminatory practices
- [ ] Adverse action by defendant
- [ ] Causal connection between protected activity and adverse action

#### Count IV — State Disability Discrimination Law *(if applicable)*
Cite applicable state statute; allege same operative facts; assert supplemental jurisdiction under 28 U.S.C. § 1367(a)

---

### VI. Prayer for Relief

**Title I claims:**
- Reinstatement with full seniority and benefits, or front pay in lieu thereof
- Back pay from date of adverse action through judgment, with prejudgment interest
- Compensatory damages — emotional distress, mental anguish, humiliation
- Punitive damages — malice or reckless indifference to federally protected rights
- Attorney's fees and costs — 42 U.S.C. § 12205
- Pre- and post-judgment interest
- Such other relief as the Court deems just and proper

**Title III claims:**
- Permanent injunction requiring reasonable modification and policy reform
- Declaratory judgment that defendant's practices violate the ADA
- Compensatory damages to the extent permitted; supplement with state law claims
- Attorney's fees and costs — 42 U.S.C. § 12205

> **FRCP 8(a)(3):** Do not plead specific dollar amounts in federal court complaints.

---

### VII. Jury Demand

> "Plaintiff demands a trial by jury on all issues so triable." — FRCP 38(b)

*(Omit for Title III-only complaints where injunctive relief is the primary remedy.)*

## Guidelines

- **Plausibility standard**: Allegations must satisfy *Twombly/Iqbal* — factual and specific, not conclusory; raise the right to relief above speculation
- **ADAAA (2008)**: Construe "disability" broadly; "substantially limits" is a lower threshold post-amendment — cite 29 C.F.R. Part 1630 [VERIFY current version]
- **Title I vs. Title III remedies**: Title I allows compensatory and punitive damages; Title III is primarily injunctive — do not conflate the remedial schemes
- **Interactive process**: Failure to engage is independently actionable in most circuits; plead it explicitly even if the accommodation itself may have been unreasonable
- **Undue hardship**: Defendant's affirmative burden to prove — plaintiff should preemptively plead facts that make hardship implausible (defendant's size, resources, low cost of accommodation)
- **State court filing**: Defendant retains removal rights under 28 U.S.C. § 1441; concurrent jurisdiction exists but federal filing is generally preferred
- **FRCP 11**: Verify all factual contentions have evidentiary support before filing; confirm EEOC charge dates and Right to Sue receipt date precisely
- **Exhibits**: Attach Right to Sue letter; consider attaching accommodation request and written denial where authenticity is not disputed
