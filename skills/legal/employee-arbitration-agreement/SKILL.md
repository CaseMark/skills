# Employee Arbitration Agreement

Drafts a mutual binding arbitration agreement for employment disputes that withstands unconscionability challenges and satisfies FAA and state-specific enforceability requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Employer information** — legal entity name, state of incorporation, principal place of business
2. **Employee information** — classification (exempt/non-exempt, at-will vs. contract), work location state
3. **Governing jurisdiction** — primary state(s) where employees work (drives research phase)
4. **Existing agreements** — prior arbitration clauses, employment agreements, or handbook policies to supersede
5. **Industry context** — any sector-specific regulatory overlay (financial services, healthcare, transportation) that may affect FAA coverage

## Output Structure

### Phase 1 — Jurisdictional Research (before drafting)

Run jurisdiction-specific analysis covering:

| Issue | Key Questions |
|---|---|
| Consideration | Is continued employment sufficient, or must independent consideration be provided? |
| Unconscionability | Does the jurisdiction apply heightened scrutiny? (CA, WA, NJ, IL) |
| Class waiver validity | Is PAGA waiver enforceable? (CA: Viking River Cruises analysis) |
| Cost allocation | Does state law require employer to pay all arbitration costs? (CA: Armendariz) |
| Opt-out requirement | Does the jurisdiction favor or require an opt-out period? |
| Sexual harassment carve-out | Does the Ending Forced Arbitration of Sexual Assault and Sexual Harassment Act (2022) apply to covered claims? |

**California-specific checklist (Armendariz factors):**
- [ ] Employer pays all costs beyond court filing fee
- [ ] Adequate discovery guaranteed
- [ ] Written arbitration award with findings of fact and conclusions of law
- [ ] Full statutory remedies available
- [ ] Neutral arbitrator selection
- [ ] PAGA representative claims proceed in court if waiver is void

### Phase 2 — Agreement Structure

**Title:** Mutual Agreement to Arbitrate Employment-Related Disputes

#### Section 1 — Consideration and Mutuality
- Recite dual consideration: offer/continued employment AND mutual promise to arbitrate
- Explicitly bind both employer and employee to covered claims
- List example employer claims (trade secret misappropriation, breach of NDA, non-compete, theft, fraud) to demonstrate genuine mutuality

#### Section 2 — Covered Claims

Include all employment-related claims:
- Wrongful termination, constructive discharge
- Discrimination and harassment (race, color, religion, sex, pregnancy, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, sexual orientation, gender identity)
- Retaliation
- Wage and hour violations
- Breach of express or implied contract; violation of public policy
- Defamation, invasion of privacy, IIED/NIED

Enumerate statutory bases:
- Title VII, ADA, ADEA, FMLA, FLSA, EPA, § 1981
- State equivalents in governing jurisdiction
- Claims arising during and after termination of employment

#### Section 3 — Exclusions

Mandatory exclusions:
- [ ] Workers' compensation claims
- [ ] Unemployment insurance claims
- [ ] ERISA benefit plan claims (to extent ERISA precludes arbitration)
- [ ] Claims under the Ending Forced Arbitration of Sexual Assault and Sexual Harassment Act (9 U.S.C. § 401 et seq.) [VERIFY current scope]
- [ ] Claims that cannot be arbitrated as a matter of law

Employer carve-out (court relief only for interim equitable relief; final merits through arbitration):
- Breach of confidentiality/NDA
- Trade secret misappropriation
- Non-compete/non-solicitation violations

#### Section 4 — Administration and Arbitrator Selection

```
Administering Body: AAA (Employment Arbitration Rules) or JAMS (Employment Arbitration Rules)
Arbitrator Qualifications: Licensed attorney with substantial employment law experience, OR retired judge
Selection Method: Provider's list-ranking/strike process
Hearing Location: Within [X] miles of employee's primary work location or residence, or in the federal judicial district where employee works
```

#### Section 5 — Cost Allocation

- Employer pays: arbitrator compensation, AAA/JAMS administrative fees, hearing room costs
- Employee pays: no more than the applicable court filing fee for the equivalent claim
- Attorney's fees: each party bears own unless statute, contract, or arbitrator's award provides otherwise; arbitrator may award fees where authorized by law

#### Section 6 — Discovery and Hearing Rights

- Document production, written interrogatories (limited), requests for admission, depositions of essential witnesses
- Scope determined by arbitrator based on claim complexity and amount in controversy
- Employee rights: hearing before arbitrator, counsel of choice (at own expense), presentation of evidence, witness examination, cross-examination
- Substantive law applied: same as applicable court (including statutes of limitations and burdens of proof)
- Evidence: Federal Rules of Evidence as guidelines; arbitrator may admit relevant evidence as appropriate

#### Section 7 — Arbitrator Authority

- Authority to resolve disputes over agreement's interpretation, applicability, enforceability, and formation (delegation clause; cite FAA § 2)
- Full remedial authority: compensatory damages, punitive damages (where statute authorizes), injunctive relief, declaratory relief, attorney's fees
- Required: written decision with findings of fact and conclusions of law sufficient for FAA § 10 judicial review

#### Section 8 — Class and Collective Action Waiver

Draft as separately initialed provision:

> Employee agrees to bring claims only in an individual capacity. Employee waives any right to participate as plaintiff, class member, or representative in any: (a) class action; (b) collective action under the FLSA or state wage laws; or (c) representative action under state PAGA or equivalent statutes.

**Severability options** (elect one based on jurisdiction):
- **Option A (majority rule):** If waiver is unenforceable as to a particular claim category, those class/representative claims proceed in court; individual claims remain in arbitration.
- **Option B (employer election):** If waiver is severed, employer may void the entire agreement and allow all claims to proceed in court.

#### Section 9 — Statutory and Administrative Rights Preserved

```
Nothing in this Agreement prevents Employee from:
- Filing charges or complaints with the EEOC, NLRB, DOL, OSHA, SEC, CFPB, or equivalent state agencies
- Participating in agency investigations or proceedings
- Exercising rights under NLRA § 7 (concerted activity, discussing wages/hours/conditions)
- Receiving whistleblower awards from government agencies

Post-agency judicial relief (damages, reinstatement, etc.) must be pursued through arbitration.
```

#### Section 10 — Governing Law

- FAA (9 U.S.C. § 1 et seq.) governs enforceability and interpretation of arbitration obligation
- Substantive law of [State] governs merits of underlying disputes
- FAA preempts conflicting state procedural arbitration law

#### Section 11 — Miscellaneous

- **Severability:** Unenforceable provisions severed; remainder survives
- **Entire Agreement:** Supersedes prior dispute resolution procedures (identify by name)
- **Amendment:** Only by mutual written agreement signed by both parties
- **Survival:** Continues after termination of employment

#### Section 12 — Execution

```
EMPLOYEE
Signature: _______________________    Date: ___________
Printed Name: ___________________    Employee ID: ______

CLASS/JURY WAIVER ACKNOWLEDGMENT (initial separately): _______

EMPLOYER
Signature: _______________________    Date: ___________
Printed Name: ___________________    Title: ____________
```

Include acknowledgment: Employee received a copy, had [5–7] business days to review, and had opportunity to consult counsel before signing.

**Opt-out clause** (include if jurisdiction favors or requires):
> Employee may opt out within [30] days of signing by submitting written notice to [HR contact/address]. Opting out will not result in adverse employment action. Opt-out voids only this Agreement; all other employment terms remain in effect.

## Guidelines

- **EFAA (2022):** Sexual assault and sexual harassment claims filed on or after March 3, 2022 cannot be compelled to arbitration pre-dispute — exclude these or confirm post-dispute agreement [VERIFY current circuit interpretations]
- **Transportation workers:** FAA Section 1 exempts certain interstate transportation workers; confirm coverage before relying on FAA
- **California:** Do not include PAGA full waiver — post-*Viking River Cruises* (2022), individual PAGA claims may be arbitrated but representative PAGA claims survive; monitor ongoing *Adolph v. Uber* developments [VERIFY current CA Supreme Court posture]
- **New York:** Heightened scrutiny of mandatory arbitration for discrimination claims; confirm current enforceability under CPLR § 7515 [VERIFY]
- **Plain language:** Define "arbitration," "discovery," "class action" on first use; use short sentences; avoid undefined terms of art
- **Not a substitute for counsel:** Flag jurisdiction-specific edge cases for attorney review before implementation
