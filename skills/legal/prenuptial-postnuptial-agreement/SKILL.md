# Prenuptial or Postnuptial Agreement

Drafts a jurisdiction-compliant marital agreement that is disclosure-complete and defensible against enforceability challenges.

## Prerequisites

1. Agreement type and governing jurisdiction(s): prenuptial or postnuptial, execution state, residence state, forum.
2. Party identities and counsel status: names, contact details, counsel retained or written waiver.
3. Full financial disclosures: assets, liabilities, income, expenses, valuations, supporting documents.
4. Intended outcomes: separate vs marital property treatment, business treatment, debt allocation, support position.
5. Estate planning intent: elective share waivers, beneficiary designations, children from prior relationships.
6. Execution logistics: timeline before wedding (if prenup), witnesses, notary, required certificates.

## Output Structure / Process

1. **Jurisdiction Gate**
   - Identify statutory framework: UPAA, UPMAA, or state-specific rules.
   - Confirm enforceability requirements for:
     - Disclosure standards
     - Independent counsel expectations
     - Timing before wedding (prenup)
     - Spousal support waiver limitations
     - Formalities: notarization, witnesses, certificates
   - Record any mandatory language or prohibited clauses.

2. **Core Document Sections**
   - Title: “Premarital Agreement” or “Postnuptial Agreement”
   - Recitals: marriage status, intent, voluntariness, disclosure acknowledgement
   - Definitions: separate property, marital property, commingling, appreciation
   - Financial Disclosure Schedules (Exhibits A/B)
   - Property Classification and Division
   - Business Interests
   - Debt Allocation and Indemnification
   - Spousal Support / Maintenance
   - Estate Rights and Death Provisions
   - Taxes and Filings
   - Insurance and Beneficiary Designations
   - Dispute Resolution and Venue
   - Modification / Sunset / Review
   - Notices, Confidentiality, Severability, Integration
   - Execution, Notary, Witnesses, Counsel Certificates

3. **Financial Disclosure Schedules (Required)**
```text
Exhibit A - Party A Financial Disclosure
- Assets
  - Real property: address, ownership %, value, encumbrances
  - Accounts: institution, account type, balance
  - Retirement: plan type, vested value
  - Business interests: entity, % ownership, valuation method
  - Vehicles: make/model/year, value, lien
  - IP/royalties: description, value estimate
- Liabilities
  - Secured: creditor, collateral, balance
  - Unsecured: creditor, balance
  - Tax obligations: year, amount
- Income
  - Employment: employer, base, bonus, equity
  - Business: entity, distributions
  - Other: rental, dividends, royalties
- Expenses
  - Monthly fixed and variable
- Certification
  - Full and fair disclosure; penalties for omission if required by law

Exhibit B - Party B Financial Disclosure
(Same structure as Exhibit A)
```

4. **Property Classification Matrix**
| Asset/Category | Separate (A) | Separate (B) | Marital | Special Treatment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Pre-marital assets | Yes | Yes | No | Identify by Exhibit |
| Gifts/Inheritances | As specified | As specified | No | Trace and title rules |
| Appreciation | As specified | As specified | As specified | Active vs passive |
| Marital residence | As specified | As specified | As specified | Buyout/refi formula |

5. **Business Interests Protocol**
- Ownership remains separate unless stated otherwise.
- Define compensation for marital efforts: formula or waiver.
- Restrict transfer of equity and address valuation on dissolution.

6. **Debt Allocation Checklist**
- Pre-marital debt: retained by originating party with indemnity.
- Marital debt: allocation rule (incurred by, benefit received, or proportional income).
- Joint debt: primary responsibility and refinancing obligations.
- Tax liabilities: joint return allocation and audit cooperation.

7. **Spousal Support Clause Options**
- Full waiver: specify scope, temporary vs permanent, non-modifiable if allowed.
- Limited support: duration, cap, triggers, termination.
- Enforceability rider: acknowledge state-specific constraints on waiver.

8. **Estate and Death Provisions**
- Elective share/community property waiver (if allowed).
- Retirement plan survivor benefits waiver (ERISA compliance).
- Life insurance obligations: amount, beneficiary, duration.
- Estate planning alignment: wills/trusts consistency and priority rules.

9. **Procedural Fairness Record**
- Counsel representation or informed written waiver.
- Voluntary execution, no duress, full review time.
- Receipt of disclosures and opportunity to verify.

10. **Execution Formalities**
- Signature blocks, dates, printed names.
- Notary acknowledgment (state-specific).
- Witness attestation if required.
- Counsel certificates if customary or required.

## Guidelines

- Do not include child custody or child support waivers; generally unenforceable.
- If postnuptial, confirm state requirements for consideration and heightened scrutiny.
- Ensure disclosure is specific and attached; vague disclosures are a common invalidation basis.
- If spousal support waiver is restricted or reviewable at enforcement, add a severability fallback.
- Use clear governing law and venue; avoid conflict-of-law ambiguity.
- Keep timing defensible: execute well before wedding and document review period.
- If any statutory citation is added, mark uncertain ones with `[VERIFY]`.
- If jurisdiction is unclear, include a placeholder “Jurisdiction Addendum” requiring completion before execution.
