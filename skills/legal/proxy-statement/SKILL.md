# Annual Meeting Proxy Statement

Drafts a compliant annual meeting proxy statement that enables informed voting and satisfies Regulation 14A disclosure requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. Meeting logistics and record date parameters (date, time, location or virtual platform, record date window, notice timing).
2. Charter, bylaws, committee charters, governance guidelines, board resolutions, and exchange listing standards (NYSE or Nasdaq).
3. Prior year proxy statement and current year board-approved proposals.
4. Director and officer questionnaires and independence determinations.
5. Executive compensation data, plans, grants, and committee materials.
6. Auditor engagement details and fee breakdowns for the last two fiscal years.
7. Related party transaction inventory and approval policies.
8. Shareholder proposals and nominations received, plus Rule 14a-8 correspondence.
9. Annual Report on Form 10-K and fiscal year end date.
10. Investor relations contact info and company principal executive offices address.

Notice of Annual Meeting checklist:
- Meeting date, time, and location or virtual platform.
- Record date and statutory/bylaw compliance.
- Matters to be voted on listed with specificity.
- Delivery timing window (statute and bylaws).
- Delaware reference if applicable: DGCL 222 notice and DGCL 213 record date.

Proxy Statement Cover and Introduction checklist:
- Board solicits proxies and pays solicitation costs.
- First mailing date (approximate).
- Voting rights by class and cumulative voting status.
- Voting methods and step-by-step instructions.
- Revocation rights and methods.
- Availability of proxy materials (company site, EDGAR, paper copies).

Matters to Be Voted On checklist:
- Each proposal listed with board recommendation.
- Required vote standard for approval.
- Effects of abstentions and broker non-votes.
- Director election standard and consequences (plurality or majority).
- Auditor ratification with fees and pre-approval policy.
- Say-on-pay and say-on-pay frequency (if applicable).
- Shareholder proposals presented as submitted.
- Rule 14a-8 eligibility statement and board response.

Board and Governance checklist:
- Director bios with last five years employment and other public boards.
- Qualifications and skills matrix narrative.
- Board size, meeting count, attendance, annual meeting attendance.
- Committee membership, chairs, meeting counts, charters.
- Independence determinations under listing standards.
- Board leadership structure and lead independent director role.
- Risk oversight framework.

Executive Compensation checklist:
- CD&A covering Item 402(b) elements.
- Summary Compensation Table for last three fiscal years.
- Grants of Plan-Based Awards table.
- Outstanding Equity Awards at Fiscal Year-End table.
- Option Exercises and Stock Vested table.
- Pension Benefits and NQDC tables as applicable.
- Pay vs Performance disclosure under Item 402(v).
- CEO pay ratio under Item 402(u).
- Compensation committee report and consultant disclosures.

Related Party Transactions checklist:
- Item 404(a) disclosures with amounts and interests.
- Approval policy and approving body.
- Identification process and monitoring.

Shareholder Proposals and Nominations checklist:
- Rule 14a-8 deadline date and address.
- Eligibility thresholds and proof of ownership requirements [VERIFY].
- 500-word limit and exclusion bases summary.
- Advance notice bylaw deadlines and required information.
- Proxy access procedures if in bylaws.

Voting Procedures checklist:
- Proxy card signature rules.
- Telephone and internet voting steps and deadlines.
- In-person or virtual voting steps and registration.
- Revocation mechanics.
- Discretionary authority for other matters.
- Broker voting rules and routine vs non-routine matters.
- Inspector of elections and Form 8-K results timing.

Other Business and Additional Information checklist:
- Annual Report on Form 10-K availability and non-incorporation statement.
- How to request copies and contact details.
- Availability of governance documents and code of ethics.
- Board communications process and screening.

Vote treatment matrix:
| Vote Standard | Abstentions | Broker Non-Votes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Plurality (directors) | No effect | No effect |
| Majority of votes cast | Not counted | Not counted |
| Majority of outstanding shares | Same as against | Same as against |

## Guidelines

- Use company-specific facts from uploaded records; avoid generic placeholders except `[TO FILL]`.
- Confirm compliance with Regulation 14A, Rule 14a-3, and Item 402 disclosure requirements.
- Apply state corporate law and bylaws for notice and record dates; use DGCL 222 and 213 if Delaware.
- Verify Rule 14a-8 eligibility thresholds and update if amended [VERIFY].
- Keep tone neutral, plain, and investor-facing; no advocacy or sales language.
- Cross-reference sections where needed (CD&A to say-on-pay, auditor fees to audit committee).
- Flag missing facts with a clear request list rather than guessing.
- Do not include legal advice; limit to disclosure and compliance drafting.
- Maintain consistency of dates, titles, and vote standards across all sections.
