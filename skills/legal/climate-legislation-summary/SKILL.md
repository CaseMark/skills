# Climate Change Legislation Summary

Creates a structured, citation-ready summary of climate change laws for compliance and policy analysis.

## Prerequisites

1. Final text of the statute/regulation/treaty or official versioned source
2. Jurisdiction and enacting authority
3. Effective date(s), amendments, and current status (enacted, proposed, in force)
4. Any implementing regulations or guidance, if applicable
5. Target industry or client context (optional but preferred)

## Output Structure / Process

### Required Sections (Table)

| Section | Required Content | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Citation & Status | Full legal citation; instrument type; enactment/entry-into-force date; amendments; current status | Mark proposed vs enacted |
| Scope & Authority | Jurisdiction, regulator, covered entities, geographic scope | Identify federal/state/international |
| Executive Overview | 3–4 sentences on purpose, mechanisms, intended outcomes | Plain language |
| Core Provisions | Key obligations, thresholds, timelines, exemptions | Cite sections |
| Compliance & Reporting | Monitoring, MRV, verification, registry, reporting cadence | Note deadlines |
| Enforcement | Oversight body, penalties, inspections, private rights | Include penalty ranges if specified |
| Business Impacts | Sectors most affected, costs, transition periods, incentives | Quantify if sources allow |
| Implementation Notes | Required permits, pending rulemaking, guidance gaps | Identify agency |
| Legal Considerations | Preemption, litigation, constitutional issues, sunset/review | Cite docket if known |
| Forward Look | Proposed amendments, expected rulemakings, policy fit | Distinguish speculation |
| Glossary | Define key technical terms used | 1–2 lines each |

### Core Provisions Checklist

- Covered entities (sector, size, emissions threshold)
- GHG scope coverage (Scope 1/2/3 if applicable)
- Baseline year and reduction targets
- Compliance deadlines and phase-in schedule
- Allowable compliance pathways (offsets, trading, credits)
- Exemptions and de minimis thresholds
- Data collection and reporting requirements

### Carbon Pricing Addendum (if applicable)

- Mechanism type (tax, cap-and-trade, hybrid)
- Rate or cap formula, escalation schedule
- Covered emissions sources and exemptions
- Revenue allocation and rebates
- Border adjustments or leakage provisions
- Registry or market infrastructure
- Price collar, floor, or ceiling rules

### International Agreement Addendum (if applicable)

- Binding vs voluntary commitments
- NDCs or equivalent commitments
- Finance and technology transfer
- Transparency framework and review cycle
- Compliance or dispute mechanisms

### Renewable/Adaptation Addendum (if applicable)

- Renewable portfolio standard or mandate details
- Procurement or credit trading rules
- Adaptation planning requirements
- Infrastructure resilience standards

## Guidelines

- Use exact section citations and regulatory references; mark any uncertain citation with [VERIFY].
- Distinguish enacted law, proposed legislation, and guidance; label each clearly.
- If a detail is missing, state “Not specified in source.”
- Keep executive overview to 3–4 sentences; use tables for dense material.
- Avoid policy advocacy; focus on legal obligations and operational impacts.
- If multiple jurisdictions are compared, normalize by mechanism type and stringency.
- Explain technical terms only once in the Glossary, then use consistently.
