# Director Indemnification Agreement

Drafts a binding indemnification agreement providing maximum permissible protection to a corporate director, including expense advancement, indemnification procedures, and D&O insurance coordination.

## Prerequisites

1. **Corporate documents** — certificate of incorporation, bylaws, any prior indemnification agreements, board resolutions authorizing indemnification
2. **D&O insurance policies** — current coverage terms, limits, and carriers
3. **Party information** — corporation legal name (as in articles), state of incorporation, director full name and title(s)
4. **Scope details** — whether director serves on subsidiaries/JVs/benefit plans at company's request; date of initial appointment; whether retroactive coverage is needed

## Information to Gather

| Item | Why It Matters |
|------|---------------|
| Corporation legal name + entity type | Must match articles exactly |
| State of incorporation | Governs scope/limits of permissible indemnification |
| Director name + all titles | Ensures comprehensive coverage across capacities |
| Related-entity service | Covers subsidiary/JV/benefit plan board seats |
| Effective date | May predate agreement for retroactive coverage |
| Board authorization | Required for enforceability; attach resolution as exhibit if needed |
| Existing indemnification provisions | Ensure consistency with bylaws/articles |

## Output Structure

### Document Skeleton

```
INDEMNIFICATION AGREEMENT

Parties: [Corporation] / [Director]
Effective Date: [Date]

RECITALS (WHEREAS clauses)
- Governing documents authorize indemnification
- Directors face substantial personal liability exposure
- Contractual assurance needed to attract/retain qualified directors
- Director willing to serve/continue in reliance on agreement
- Board has determined agreement serves valid corporate purpose

NOW, THEREFORE...

ARTICLE I — DEFINITIONS
ARTICLE II — INDEMNIFICATION
ARTICLE III — ADVANCEMENT OF EXPENSES
ARTICLE IV — PROCEDURES FOR DETERMINATION
ARTICLE V — NOTICE AND DEFENSE COORDINATION
ARTICLE VI — NON-EXCLUSIVITY AND VESTED RIGHTS
ARTICLE VII — D&O INSURANCE
ARTICLE VIII — CONTRIBUTION AND PARTIAL INDEMNIFICATION
ARTICLE IX — GENERAL PROVISIONS

SIGNATURE BLOCKS
```

### Key Definitions

| Term | Scope |
|------|-------|
| **Expenses** | Attorneys' fees, court costs, transcripts, expert/witness fees, travel, duplicating, postage, delivery — plus judgments, fines, penalties, ERISA excise taxes, settlement amounts |
| **Proceeding** | Any threatened/pending/completed action, suit, arbitration, ADR, investigation, inquiry, admin hearing — derivative, third-party, civil, criminal, administrative, investigative; includes informal regulatory inquiries and threatened-but-unfiled claims |
| **Official Capacity** | Director, officer, employee, agent of company; service at company's request for subsidiaries, JVs, trusts, benefit plans, other enterprises |

### Article II — Indemnification Coverage

- Indemnify to **maximum extent permitted** under state of incorporation's law
- Trigger: director made/threatened to be made party "by reason of" official capacity
- **Conduct standard**: good faith + reasonable belief action was in/not opposed to company's best interests; for criminal matters, no reasonable cause to believe conduct unlawful
- **Derivative suits**: expenses only (not settlement) unless court-approved

### Article III — Advancement of Expenses

- Mandatory advancement within **30 days** of written request + documentation
- Director executes **undertaking to repay** if ultimately found not entitled (unsecured, unlimited, no financial-ability requirement)
- Advancement is **independent** of ultimate indemnification determination
- Company may not deny advancement based on preliminary conduct determination

### Article IV — Determination Procedures

Determination methods (in priority order):

1. Majority vote of **disinterested directors** (even if less than quorum)
2. Committee of disinterested directors designated by majority vote
3. **Independent legal counsel** written opinion (no relationship with company/director within 5 years)
4. Shareholder vote (excluding shares held by party-directors)

Procedural safeguards:
- Determination within **60 days** of written request
- **Presumption**: director met standard unless rebutted by **clear and convincing evidence**
- Failure to determine within 60 days = **deemed approved**
- Director may seek **judicial determination**; company bears burden of proof (clear and convincing)
- Company advances expenses for enforcement proceedings

### Article V — Notice and Defense

- Director must notify company **promptly** upon service; failure does not relieve company unless **material adverse effect** on defense
- Company may assume defense with director-approved counsel (consent not unreasonably withheld)
- **Conflict of interest** → director retains separate counsel at company expense (company also a party, differing positions, dual-representation conflict)
- Director cooperates; no settlement without company consent unless unconditional release + no liability admission

### Article VI — Non-Exclusivity and Vested Rights

- Rights are **non-exclusive** — supplement bylaws, articles, other agreements, law
- **Survival**: continues after director ceases service; inures to heirs/executors/administrators
- **Anti-diminishment**: no amendment adversely affects rights for prior acts/omissions

### Article VII — D&O Insurance

- Agreement is **primary**; insurance is **excess**
- Rights not contingent on insurance availability
- Company must cover director under D&O policies to same extent as any other director/officer
- Company provides copies of policies; notifies director of material changes
- Director entitled to insurance proceeds **before** company

### Article VIII — Contribution

- If indemnification unavailable, company **contributes** in fair proportion considering relative benefit and fault
- **Priority**: expenses paid before judgments/fines/settlements
- If partial indemnification only, indemnify to **maximum extent permitted by law**

### Article IX — General Provisions

| Provision | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| Governing law | State of incorporation (no conflicts-of-law) |
| Jurisdiction | Exclusive — courts of state of incorporation or federal courts therein |
| Term | Later of **10 years** after director ceases service or final termination of all covered proceedings |
| Successor obligation | Survives merger/consolidation/asset transfer; successor must assume |
| Severability | Reform invalid provisions to minimum extent necessary |
| Fee-shifting | Director recovers expenses for successful enforcement of agreement |
| Amendment | Written instrument signed by both parties only |
| No waiver | No failure/delay operates as waiver |
| Notices | Written; effective on personal delivery, confirmed email/fax, overnight courier, or certified mail |
| Counterparts | Multiple counterparts; electronic signatures valid |

### Signature Block

- **Corporation**: authorized officer (CEO/President/Secretary) with printed name, title, date; representation of authority and board authorization
- **Director**: printed name, signature, date

## Guidelines

- **State law governs scope** — Delaware permits broadest indemnification; verify limitations for other states [VERIFY specific state statute before finalizing]
- Draft to **maximum permissible** scope; do not self-limit below statutory ceiling
- Ensure consistency with existing bylaws/articles indemnification provisions
- For **public companies**, include specific representations re: board authorization
- The **undertaking to repay** is a statutory requirement in most states — do not omit
- **Presumption in favor of indemnification** and **clear and convincing evidence** standard are director-protective and standard in sophisticated agreements
- Cross-reference related provisions throughout for navigability
- Use defined terms consistently (capitalized) after first definition
- Review any uploaded governance documents for company-specific drafting conventions
