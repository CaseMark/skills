# Confidentiality Agreement (NDA)

Drafts a professional-grade NDA for corporate transactions, M&A, partnerships, and sensitive business discussions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party information** — exact legal names, entity types, principal addresses for both parties
2. **Transaction context** — type (acquisition, JV, licensing, partnership), target entity/business unit
3. **Mutual vs. unilateral** — direction of information flow
4. **Sensitivity level** — determines care standard, duration, and whether standstill/non-solicit are needed
5. **Governing law preference** — jurisdiction for choice of law and venue

## Output Structure

### 1. Document Header & Parties

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Title | Reflect mutual vs. unilateral; reference specific transaction if applicable |
| Effective Date | Typically date of final execution; parties may specify alternative |
| Disclosing Party | Full legal name matching formation docs (Inc., LLC, Ltd.), principal address |
| Receiving Party | Same requirements; clarify subsidiary/parent relationships |

### 2. Purpose / Recitals

- State specific transaction type (asset purchase, stock purchase, merger, JV, licensing)
- Define permitted scope of use — narrow for sensitive deals, broader for exploratory
- Address ancillary uses (financing, internal approvals) if authorized
- Include: "No obligation to proceed with any transaction"

### 3. Definition of Confidential Information

**Included categories:**
- Financial (revenues, costs, projections, pricing)
- Business (strategic plans, customer/supplier lists, methods)
- Technical (designs, specifications, formulas, know-how)
- Personnel (employee lists, compensation, org structure)
- IP (patents, copyrights, trade secrets)
- **The existence and terms of discussions themselves**

**Standard exclusions:**
- [ ] Public domain at time of disclosure
- [ ] Becomes public through no breach
- [ ] Already in receiving party's possession (with written evidence)
- [ ] Independently developed (with contemporaneous written records)
- [ ] Received from non-obligated third party

**Marking protocol:** All information qualifies regardless of marking; best practice is to mark written materials and confirm oral disclosures in writing within a reasonable period.

### 4. Core Obligations

| Obligation | Specification |
|------------|---------------|
| Use restriction | Solely for evaluating the stated transaction; no competitive use, product development, or solicitation |
| Care standard | Same degree as own confidential info of similar importance, no less than reasonable care; consider "highest degree" for exceptionally sensitive info |
| Non-disclosure | No disclosure to any third party without prior written consent, except permitted representatives |

### 5. Permitted Disclosures

**Representatives** (need-to-know basis only):
- Officers, directors, employees directly involved in evaluation
- External advisors: attorneys, accountants, financial advisors, investment bankers, consultants

**Conditions for representative disclosure:**
1. Legitimate need to know the specific information
2. Informed of confidential nature and agreement obligations
3. Bound by obligations at least as restrictive (professional duty, employment agreement, or separate NDA)
4. **Receiving party liable for representative breaches**

**Legally compelled disclosure procedure:**
1. Prompt written notice to disclosing party (sufficient time to seek protective order)
2. Cooperate with efforts to limit disclosure
3. Disclose only the minimum legally required portion
4. Seek confidential treatment for disclosed information

### 6. Protective Provisions

**Non-solicitation** (include when appropriate):
- Scope: employees contacted or learned about during evaluation
- Duration: typically 1–3 years
- Covers direct and indirect solicitation and hiring

**Standstill** (for potential acquisitions, especially public companies):
- Prohibited: acquiring securities/assets, proposing mergers, proxy solicitation, board representation, forming shareholder groups, public announcements
- Duration: typically 6 months–2 years
- Exceptions: board consent, disclosing party's unsolicited proposal, third-party acquisition announcement

**No obligation to transact:**
- No duty to proceed, continue negotiations, provide information, or enter further agreements
- Binding commitments arise only from definitive written agreements explicitly stating intent to be bound
- LOIs and term sheets non-binding except provisions expressly designated as binding

### 7. Term & Return/Destruction

| Element | Standard Range |
|---------|---------------|
| Confidentiality duration | 2–5 years (3 typical); trade secrets indefinite |
| Trigger for return/destruction | Written request or termination of discussions |

**Return/destruction requirements:**
- All documents, copies, excerpts, summaries, analyses, derivatives — any form/medium
- Reasonable steps to delete electronic copies; backups remain subject to obligations
- **Written certification** by authorized officer confirming compliance

### 8. IP & Disclaimers

- No license, ownership, or rights granted by disclosure
- No right to use disclosing party's name, marks, or logos without written consent
- Disclosing party makes no representations regarding accuracy or completeness of information

### 9. Remedies

- Acknowledgment: information is valuable/unique; breach causes irreparable harm; monetary damages inadequate
- Equitable relief available (TRO, preliminary/permanent injunction) without bond or proof of actual damages
- Equitable relief is in addition to all other remedies (damages, specific performance)
- Consider prevailing-party attorneys' fees provision
- Consider liquidated damages for specific breach types

### 10. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

- Governing law: specified state, without conflicts-of-law principles
- Exclusive jurisdiction and venue: state and federal courts in chosen jurisdiction
- Consider: jury trial waiver, arbitration clause (specify rules, location, arbitrator selection)

### 11. Boilerplate

- Entire agreement / supersedes prior discussions
- Amendments only in writing signed by both parties
- No waiver by conduct; written waiver only
- Severability with reformation to maximum extent
- No assignment without written consent (consider merger/acquisition exception)

### 12. Execution

Signature blocks for each party: signature line, printed name, title, date. Verify signatory authority (officer, director, or authorized representative).

## Guidelines

- **Mutual vs. unilateral**: if mutual, ensure all obligations apply symmetrically to both parties as discloser and receiver
- **Public companies**: address Regulation FD, insider trading, and MNPI trading restrictions
- **Cross-border**: address data privacy (GDPR if EU parties), export controls, cross-border judgment enforcement
- **Electronic execution**: ensure compliance with E-SIGN Act / UETA [VERIFY] if using e-signatures
- **Securities compliance**: for M&A involving public targets, coordinate standstill with securities counsel
- **Calibrate duration and scope** to transaction type — exploratory partnerships need less than full M&A due diligence
- **Do not** include provisions that would override legal compulsion to disclose (unenforceable)
- **Do not** draft standstill provisions for private company transactions unless specifically requested
