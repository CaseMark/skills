# Objection to Proof of Claim

Drafts a litigation-ready objection challenging a creditor's proof of claim in U.S. bankruptcy court, targeting disallowance or reduction.

## Prerequisites

1. **Bankruptcy petition** — debtor's full legal name, case number, chapter, petition date
2. **Proof of claim** — claim number, filing date, creditor name (including DBAs), amount, stated basis, and all attachments
3. **Underlying documents** — contracts, invoices, account statements, correspondence, payment history between debtor and creditor
4. **Schedules and SOFA** — debtor's characterization of the debt and any discrepancies with the creditor's claim
5. **Local rules** — formatting, page limits, verification requirements, and procedural protocols for the specific bankruptcy court
6. **Bar date order** — claims filing deadline and whether the claim was timely filed

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption

Format per local court rules. Must include:

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Court | Full name of U.S. Bankruptcy Court with district |
| Case number | Court-required format |
| Debtor name | Exactly as on petition |
| Chapter | As filed |
| Title | "OBJECTION TO PROOF OF CLAIM NO. [X]" centered |

### 2. Introduction

- Identify objector's standing (debtor, trustee, or party in interest under 11 U.S.C. § 502(a))
- State claim number, creditor name (as on POC form), and total asserted amount
- Preview objection grounds in one to two sentences — do not argue here

### 3. Factual Background

Chronological narrative covering:

- [ ] Origin of alleged debt (contract, goods/services, date, terms)
- [ ] Pre-petition payment history and disputes
- [ ] Petition date, bar date, and POC filing date relative to bar date
- [ ] Discrepancies between debtor's schedules and creditor's claim
- [ ] Documentation creditor attached (or failed to attach) to POC

Present facts neutrally. Clearly distinguish disputed from undisputed facts. Every assertion must be supportable by admissible evidence.

### 4. Grounds for Objection

Identify all applicable grounds from this checklist:

| Ground | Key Statute | What to Show |
|---|---|---|
| Insufficient documentation | FRBP 3001(f); § 502(b) | Creditor failed to attach contracts, invoices, or account statements establishing validity and amount |
| Mathematical/calculation errors | § 502(b)(1) | Incorrect interest, unauthorized fees, principal errors |
| Statute of limitations | § 502(b)(1) + applicable state law | Cause of action accrued outside limitations period |
| Improper priority classification | § 503(b), § 507(a)(8), § 506 | Claim does not meet statutory requirements for asserted priority |
| Improper secured status | § 506(a) | No valid, perfected security interest documented |
| Lack of standing | § 502(a) | Filer is not real party in interest; deficient assignment chain (common with debt buyers) |
| Duplicative claim | § 502(b)(1) | Claim duplicates another filed claim |
| Post-petition claim improperly filed | § 502(b) | Debt arose post-petition without administrative expense basis |

For each ground asserted:
1. Cite the specific Bankruptcy Code section, FRBP rule, or state law
2. State the legal standard
3. Apply facts showing how the claim fails that standard

### 5. Legal Argument

Structure each argument as:

```
A. [Legal Principle]
   - Governing standard: [statute + controlling case law]
   - Burden framework: FRBP 3001(f) creates prima facie validity for
     properly filed POCs; once objection raises colorable challenge,
     burden shifts to creditor to prove by preponderance
   - Application: [connect specific facts to legal standard]
   - Anticipate and distinguish creditor counterarguments
```

Cite controlling authority from the relevant Circuit and BAP. Use Bluebook format with pinpoint cites. Mark any unverified citations with [VERIFY].

### 6. Prayer for Relief

Structure in order of preference:

1. **Primary**: "WHEREFORE, [Objector] respectfully requests that this Court enter an order disallowing Claim No. [X] filed by [Creditor] in its entirety."
2. **Alternative reduction**: "In the alternative, reduce Claim No. [X] to $[amount] representing [basis]."
3. **Alternative reclassification** (if applicable): "Reclassify Claim No. [X] from [current status] to [requested status]."
4. **Fees/costs** (if statutory or contractual basis exists): Cite specific authority.
5. **General**: "Such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper."

### 7. Signature Block

Per FRBP 9011:
- Printed name, bar number, firm name, address, phone, fax, email
- Include verification under penalty of perjury if required by local rules or nature of objection

## Guidelines

- **Tone**: Professional and measured — no inflammatory language or personal attacks on the creditor
- **Formatting**: 12pt serif font, double-spaced body, single-spaced caption/signature/block quotes, 1-inch margins, consecutive page numbers, footer with abbreviated case name and number
- **Length**: Typically 5–15 pages depending on complexity
- **Citations**: Bluebook format for all authorities; include pinpoint cites
- **Evidence**: Every factual assertion must be traceable to a source document producible at an evidentiary hearing
- **Fresh start doctrine**: For individual debtors, frame arguments within bankruptcy's rehabilitative purpose where applicable
- **Do not** assume secured status, priority, or standing — require the creditor to prove each element
- **Do not** concede facts favorable to the objector's position when characterizing disputed matters
