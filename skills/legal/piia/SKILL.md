# Proprietary Information and Inventions Agreement (PIIA)

Drafts an enforceable PIIA that protects company IP and confidential information while complying with state invention-assignment statutes.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party details** — company legal name, state of organization, principal office; individual's full name, address, role/title
2. **Effective date** and nature of relationship (employment vs. consulting)
3. **Governing law** jurisdiction and any multi-state considerations
4. **Prior inventions** — any pre-existing IP the individual intends to exclude
5. **Existing agreements** — offer letter, employment agreement, equity docs that may overlap

## Output Structure

### Section 1: Introduction & Consideration

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Parties | Full legal names, addresses, entity type/state of organization |
| Relationship | State PIIA is a material condition of employment/engagement |
| Effective date | Specify; reference role/title for scope context |
| Consideration | Access to proprietary information + compensation + employment/engagement itself |

### Section 2: Confidentiality

**Definition of Proprietary Information — include all of:**
- Technical data, trade secrets, know-how, R&D, product plans
- Business strategy, financials, pricing, customer/supplier lists, marketing plans
- Information in any form (written, oral, electronic, visual)
- Information about employees, contractors, business partners

**Standard carve-outs:**
- Publicly available (not through individual's breach)
- Independently developed without company resources
- Received from third party without restriction

**Obligations checklist:**
- [ ] No disclosure without prior written authorization
- [ ] Use only for assigned duties
- [ ] Care standard: at least same as own confidential info, no less than reasonable care
- [ ] No removal from premises without authorization
- [ ] No storage on personal devices/unapproved systems
- [ ] Obligations survive termination indefinitely (while info remains confidential)

**Return of Materials:**
- Immediate return of all documents, files, devices, credentials upon termination
- Written certification of return and deletion (including personal devices/cloud)
- Company right to remote wipe and inspect

### Section 3: Invention Assignment

**Definition of "Inventions":** All discoveries, developments, designs, ideas, improvements, processes, software, algorithms, works of authorship — patentable or not.

**Scope — inventions covered if any of:**
1. Relate to company's actual or anticipated business/R&D
2. Result from work performed for the company
3. Developed using company equipment, facilities, trade secrets, or time

**State Invention-Assignment Statutes (mandatory carve-outs):**

| State | Statute | Protection |
|---|---|---|
| California | Cal. Lab. Code § 2870 | Inventions on own time, without company resources, unrelated to company business |
| Delaware | 19 Del. C. § 805 | Same general framework |
| Illinois | 765 ILCS 1060/2 | Inventions on own time, without company resources |
| Kansas | K.S.A. § 44-130 | Similar employee protection |
| Minnesota | Minn. Stat. § 181.78 | Own time, no company resources, unrelated |
| North Carolina | N.C.G.S. § 66-57.1 | Similar framework |
| Utah | Utah Code § 34-39-3 | Own time, no company resources |
| Washington | RCW 49.44.140 | Own time, no company resources, unrelated |

> Include the applicable statutory notice verbatim when the individual works in a covered state.

**Assignment clause requirements:**
- Present assignment: "hereby assigns and agrees to assign"
- Worldwide, all IP rights (patent, copyright, trade secret, mask work, moral rights to extent assignable)
- Works of authorship: "work made for hire" + fallback assignment
- No additional compensation beyond regular pay

**Prior Inventions Exhibit:**
- Attach exhibit for individual to list excluded prior inventions (title, date, description)
- If blank/not attached → representation that none exist
- Improvements to prior inventions using company resources → assigned to company

**Cooperation & Further Assurances:**
- Prompt written disclosure of all inventions
- Execute all IP filing documents; provide testimony
- Survives termination; company reimburses expenses + reasonable rate for post-termination time
- Irrevocable power of attorney (coupled with interest) for document execution if individual unavailable

### Section 4: General Provisions

**DTSA Immunity Notice (18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)) — REQUIRED:**

```
NOTICE: An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any
federal or state trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is
made (i) in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official, either
directly or indirectly, or to an attorney, solely for the purpose of reporting
or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (ii) in a complaint or other
document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under
seal. An individual who files a lawsuit for retaliation by an employer for
reporting a suspected violation of law may disclose the trade secret to the
attorney of the individual and use the trade secret information in the court
proceeding, if the individual files any document containing the trade secret
under seal and does not disclose the trade secret, except pursuant to court
order.
```

**Remaining provisions:**
- [ ] Governing law & exclusive forum selection (must be reasonable nexus)
- [ ] Consent to personal jurisdiction; forum non conveniens waiver
- [ ] Entire agreement / integration clause
- [ ] Amendment only by signed writing
- [ ] Severability with judicial modification (blue-pencil) language
- [ ] Relationship to other agreements (offer letter, equity docs — specify if superseded or coexistent)

**Signature block:**
- Individual: name, signature, date + acknowledgment of reading, understanding, opportunity to consult counsel, voluntary execution
- Company: authorized officer name, title, signature, date
- Attach Prior Inventions Exhibit (even if blank)

## Guidelines

- **State law compliance is mandatory** — never draft an assignment clause that overrides statutory carve-outs; include verbatim statutory notice where required
- **Avoid overbreadth** — provisions deemed unreasonable restraints on trade risk unenforceability
- **DTSA notice must be included** in substantially statutory form per 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)
- **Post-termination cooperation** must not create uncompensated work violating wage/hour laws
- **Consulting vs. employment** — adjust work-for-hire language (copyright work-for-hire doctrine is narrower for independent contractors; verify the relationship qualifies)
- **Multi-state/international** — flag conflicts of law; mandatory local protections override choice-of-law clauses
- Use consistent defined terms throughout; cross-reference accurately
