# CCPA/CPRA Privacy Policy

Drafts a California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) compliant privacy policy with all required statutory disclosures.

## Prerequisites

1. **Applicability threshold** — which trigger applies: ≥$25M annual gross revenue; buys/sells/shares ≥100,000 consumers' or households' records/year; or derives ≥50% annual revenue from selling/sharing personal information
2. **Data inventory** — categories of personal information collected, sources (direct, automated, third-party), and retention periods
3. **Purpose map** — specific business and commercial purposes for each data category
4. **Third-party sharing list** — all service providers, contractors, vendors, ad networks, analytics providers, affiliates; distinguish business-purpose disclosure from sale/sharing
5. **Sensitive personal information (SPI)** — whether the business collects SPI as defined by Cal. Civil Code § 1798.121 and whether the right to limit use applies
6. **Children's data** — whether the business has actual knowledge it collects data from consumers under 16
7. **Request channels** — minimum two designated methods (toll-free number + website URL required); also email or portal if available
8. **Contact details** — legal name, mailing address, privacy contact, DPO/CPO title if appointed

## Output Structure

### 1. Introduction
- Effective date and scope (applies to California residents)
- Applicable CCPA threshold(s) triggering coverage
- Governing law: Cal. Civil Code §§ 1798.100–1798.199 (CCPA as amended by CPRA); CPPA implementing regulations

### 5. Your Privacy Rights

| Right | Statutory Basis | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Right to Know | § 1798.110 | Categories + specific pieces; 12-month lookback minimum |

### 6. How to Exercise Your Rights

**Submission methods** (at least two required):
- Toll-free number: [___]
- Web portal / online form: [URL]
- Email: [privacy@company.com] *(if offered)*

**Verification:** Describe matching consumer-provided information against existing records. Business may not require account creation to submit a request.

**Timelines:**
- Acknowledgment: within 10 business days of receipt
- Substantive response: within 45 calendar days (extendable by additional 45 days with prior notice)

**Authorized agents:** Accepted with signed written permission or power of attorney; business may verify authority directly with consumer.

**Frequency:** Two free Right-to-Know requests per 12-month period.

### 7. Children's Privacy

| Age Group | Consent Required |
|---|---|
| Under 13 | Affirmative opt-in from parent or guardian (§ 1798.120(d)) |
| 13–15 | Affirmative opt-in from the consumer |

If the business does not knowingly collect data from consumers under 16, state this explicitly and describe safeguards in place.

### 8. Policy Updates

- Identify circumstances triggering revisions (legal changes, new practices, new services/technologies)
- Specify notice method for material changes (email notification, homepage banner, in-app alert)
- State effective date of revised policy and whether affirmative consent is required for material revisions
- Commit to archiving prior versions for compliance reference

### 9. Contact Information

- Business legal name and mailing address
- Designated privacy contact email
- Toll-free number (if designated for CCPA requests)
- Data Protection Officer / Chief Privacy Officer (title and contact, if applicable)
- Online request portal URL

## Guidelines

- **Accuracy over aspiration**: Policy must reflect *actual* data practices — material discrepancies may constitute unlawful deceptive practices under Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 17200
- **Plain language**: Accessible to an average consumer; retain statutory terms of art (e.g., "service provider," "sell," "share") precisely as defined
- **CPRA alignment**: CPRA amendments effective Jan. 1, 2023 — ensure right to correct, right to limit SPI, and CPPA rulemaking compliance are incorporated [VERIFY current CPPA rulemaking for any subsequent amendments]
- **Conspicuous posting**: Must be accessible from the business's homepage; provide accessibility-compliant format
- **Coordination**: Align with cookie policy, employee privacy notice, vendor DPAs, and internal data governance documentation
- **Annual review**: Review at minimum annually and upon any material change to business practices or applicable law
- **Legal review**: Have qualified California privacy counsel review before publishing
