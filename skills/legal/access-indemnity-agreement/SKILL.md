# Access and Indemnity Agreement (Due Diligence)

## Why This Skill Exists

Pre-closing property inspections create a concentrated window of operational and legal risk. A buyer's contractor drills a soil boring that hits a utility line, contaminates groundwater, or triggers a mechanic's lien — and without a properly structured access agreement, the liability allocation is ambiguous, the insurance doesn't respond, and the deal collapses into litigation. The access agreement is also the document that controls what happens when due diligence *discovers* pre-existing contamination: without a clear discovery carve-out, the buyer's indemnity can be read to cover conditions the owner already created.

This skill produces a short-form, execution-ready right-of-entry contract that allocates operational control and risk during pre-closing inspections — with insurance tied to entry, indemnity scoped correctly, and anti-indemnity statutes addressed.

---

## Checkpoint A: Pre-Draft Intake (Mandatory)

Ask every time unless the user says "use defaults" or "just draft." Gather:

1. **Governing law and property location** — state-specific anti-indemnity, lien, and environmental rules
2. **Parties and roles** — owner, buyer/recipient, property manager, lender (if any), and who administers access
3. **Property details** — address/legal description, occupancy/tenants, restricted areas, hazards, union/site rules
4. **Deal posture** — PSA/LOI status, diligence period, exclusivity, termination triggers
5. **Scope of investigations** — Phase I only vs. Phase II, destructive testing, sampling, drones, roof access
6. **Insurance requirements** — limits, additional insureds, primary/noncontributory, waiver of subrogation, carrier ratings
7. **Confidentiality framework** — existing NDA, report delivery expectations, privacy/security limits

**If the user doesn't respond**, apply and clearly label these defaults: buyer-side access agreement; Phase I non-invasive only; $1M/$2M CGL; standard indemnity with discovery carve-out; 48-hour notice requirement; governing law per property state.

## Step 1: Establish Defined Terms and License Grant

### Defined Terms Checklist

- `Property`, `Access Period`, `Investigations`, `Invasive Activities`
- `Owner Indemnitees`, `Recipient Representatives`
- `Confidential Information`, `Work Plan`

### License Grant Template

```text
Owner grants Recipient a limited, revocable, non-exclusive license (not a lease
or easement) to enter the Property solely to perform the Investigations during
the Access Period, subject to Owner's reasonable rules and tenant rights.
Recipient has no possessory interest or exclusive use of the Property.
```

Always characterize access as a **license**, not a lease or easement.

---

## Step 2: Draft Access Procedures and Activity Gates

### Access Procedures Template

```text
Entries require at least [48] hours' prior written notice to [Owner Rep]
identifying each entrant, purpose, equipment, and confirmation of current
insurance. Owner may require escorts, reschedule for operational reasons, and
deny entry for non-compliance. No tenant contact without Owner's written consent.
```

### Permitted vs. Invasive Activities Template

```text
Permitted Investigations include visual inspections, measurements, surveys, and
Phase I ESA activities without sampling. Invasive Activities (soil borings,
sampling, cores, roof/slab penetrations, system shutdowns) require Owner's prior
written consent based on a Work Plan (scope, schedule, contractor licenses,
insurance, restoration plan).
```

Require **Work Plan approval** before any invasive testing.

---

## Step 3: Draft Insurance Requirements

Tie **entry** to receipt of acceptable **endorsements**, not just certificates.

```text
As a condition to entry, Recipient and each Recipient Representative shall
maintain CGL on an occurrence form with limits not less than [$1,000,000] per
occurrence and [$2,000,000] aggregate, workers' compensation as required by law,
and employer's liability of [$500,000]. If Invasive Activities are approved:
umbrella/excess of [$5,000,000] and Contractor's Pollution Liability of
[$1,000,000]. Owner, property manager, and [LENDER] are additional insureds on a
primary and noncontributory basis. Deliver certificates and endorsements (CG 20
10 and CG 20 37) [VERIFY] before entry.
```

---

## Step 4: Draft Indemnity, Restoration, and Confidentiality

### Indemnity + Discovery Carve-Out Template

```text
Recipient shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Owner Indemnitees from
claims, losses, liens, and costs (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising
from entry, Investigations, Invasive Activities, or Recipient's breach, except
for the mere discovery of pre-existing conditions not caused or exacerbated by
Recipient. Carve out Owner's [gross negligence/willful misconduct/sole
negligence] as required by governing law [VERIFY].
```

Indemnity must cover **defense costs, liens, tenant claims, and property manager/lender**.

### Restoration + Lien Control Template

```text
Recipient shall promptly repair damage and restore to substantially the same
condition. If Recipient fails to commence repairs within [5] business days
(immediately for safety hazards), Owner may self-help and invoice costs plus
[15]% admin fee. Recipient shall keep Property free of liens and discharge or
bond any lien within [10] days after notice.
```

### Confidentiality + Reports Template

```text
Confidential Information includes documents, observations, photos, and data
derived from access. Disclosure only to Representatives bound by confidentiality.
Compelled disclosure requires notice and cooperation. Report delivery: [full
reports / factual data only / no reports]; if Owner declines reports, state that
Owner is not receiving them to avoid "actual knowledge" consequences [VERIFY].
```

### Priority Clause (if PSA exists)

```text
If a PSA exists, its access provisions control only to the extent of an express
conflict; insurance, indemnity, restoration, and confidentiality obligations
herein survive unless expressly superseded.
```

---

## Step 5: Assemble Agreement in Section Order

1. Parties, Property, Recitals, and **License Grant** (revocable, non-exclusive, not a lease/easement)
2. Access Period and **Access Procedures** (notice, hours, escorts, safety, tenant non-interference)
3. **Permitted vs. Invasive Activities** with Work Plan approval gate
4. **Insurance Requirements** and endorsement delivery
5. **Indemnity, Defense, and Survival** with discovery carve-out
6. **Restoration, Lien Control, and Incident Response** with self-help
7. **Confidentiality and Report Handling** (NDA cross-reference, actual-knowledge trap)
8. **Disclaimers, Termination, Dispute Terms, Priority/Integration**
9. Signatures

## Checkpoint B: Post-Draft Alignment (Mandatory)

After delivering the initial draft, ask:

1. Does the scope correctly distinguish non-invasive from invasive activities for this deal?
2. Are the insurance limits and endorsement requirements acceptable to all parties?
3. Is there a lender who needs to be added as an additional insured or notice recipient?
4. Should report delivery be full reports, factual data only, or no reports (actual-knowledge implications)?

If the user doesn't answer, recommend confirming insurance limits and the report-delivery election (highest-risk decisions) and proceed if authorized.

## Quality Audit

Before finalizing, verify:

- [ ] Access characterized as license, not lease or easement
- [ ] Work Plan approval required before any invasive testing
- [ ] Insurance tied to endorsement delivery, not just certificates
- [ ] Indemnity covers defense costs, liens, tenant claims, and lender
- [ ] Discovery carve-out for pre-existing conditions included
- [ ] Owner negligence carve-out appropriate for governing law
- [ ] Restoration obligations and self-help remedy included
- [ ] Lien cure periods contractual (not claimed as statutory without verification)
- [ ] Confidentiality/NDA conflict resolved
- [ ] Anti-indemnity statute compliance verified for governing jurisdiction
- [ ] All bracketed business terms filled or flagged
- [ ] Environmental reporting obligations not stated as mandatory without verification

## Guidelines

- Always characterize access as a **license**, not a lease or easement
- Require **Work Plan approval** before any invasive testing
- Tie **entry** to receipt of acceptable **endorsements**, not just certificates
- Include a **discovery carve-out** for pre-existing conditions but keep liability for **exacerbation**
- Use **contractual lien cure periods**; do not claim statutory deadlines without verification
- If governing law is TX/NY/CA/FL, verify anti-indemnity requirements and formatting rules `[VERIFY]`
- Environmental reporting obligations are fact- and state-specific; do not state them as mandatory without verification `[VERIFY]`
- If an NDA exists, specify which document controls confidentiality to avoid conflicts
- Do not fabricate statutory citations, insurance form numbers, or anti-indemnity rules
- **All outputs require attorney review** in the property's jurisdiction
