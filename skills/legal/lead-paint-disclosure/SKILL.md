# Lead-Based Paint Disclosure

Drafts a federally compliant lead-based paint disclosure for sale or lease of pre-1978 residential property, ready for execution by all parties.

## Prerequisites

1. **Property details** — full address, legal description, confirmed construction date pre-1978
2. **Transaction type** — sale or lease
3. **Party information** — names and addresses of all sellers/landlords, buyers/tenants, and agents/brokers (with license numbers)
4. **Lead paint records** — any inspection reports, risk assessments, abatement certificates, clearance reports, or health department correspondence (or confirmation that none exist)
5. **Prior disclosures or remediation documentation**, if any

## Governing Law

| Authority | Citation |
|---|---|
| Federal statute | 42 U.S.C. § 4852d |
| Implementing regulation | 24 CFR Part 35, Subpart A & H |
| EPA disclosure rule | EPA Section 1018 (40 CFR Part 745, Subpart F) |
| Enabling act | Residential Lead-Based Paint Hazard Reduction Act of 1992 (Title X) |

### Penalty Exposure

- Civil: up to **$16,000/violation** (EPA enforcement) [VERIFY current adjusted amount]
- Private suit: **treble damages + attorney's fees**
- Criminal: knowing/willful violations

## Document Structure

Draft the disclosure with these sections in order:

### 1. Header & Party Identification

- Title: **"Lead-Based Paint Disclosure"** (bold, prominent)
- Property address + legal description
- Date of disclosure
- Full legal names and mailing addresses of all parties
- Transaction type: Sale ☐ / Lease ☐
- Agent/broker names, brokerage, license numbers (if applicable)

### 2. Seller/Landlord Disclosure Statement

Require the disclosing party to select **one** of three options:

| Option | Required Detail |
|---|---|
| **A — Known hazards exist** | Specific locations, condition/extent, date knowledge acquired; distinguish between lead-based paint and lead-based paint *hazards* (deteriorating paint, friction/impact surfaces, contaminated dust/soil) |
| **B — No known hazards** | Affirmative statement after reasonable inquiry |
| **C — Prior hazards remediated** | Description of remediation/abatement, professional credentials, attach documentation as exhibits |

### 3. Records & Reports Attachment Schedule

- List all attached documents by exhibit number:
  - Inspection reports (certified lead inspector)
  - Risk assessment reports
  - Lead-safe/lead-free certificates
  - Abatement/interim control documentation
  - Clearance reports
  - Health department correspondence
- If no records exist, include **affirmative statement** that none are available (do not leave blank)

### 4. Pamphlet & Inspection Opportunity

- Confirm delivery of EPA pamphlet *"Protect Your Family from Lead in Your Home"* (or EPA-approved state equivalent)
- **Sales only** — memorialize 10-day inspection right:
  - ☐ Buyer **waives** inspection opportunity (initial line)
  - ☐ Buyer **exercises** inspection right — agreed period: ___ days
  - Include: inspection contingency, cancellation right based on results, deadline provisions

### 5. Buyer/Tenant Acknowledgments

Individual initialed/signed acknowledgments for each:

- [ ] Receipt of seller/landlord disclosure statement
- [ ] Receipt of EPA lead hazard information pamphlet
- [ ] Receipt of all attached records and reports
- [ ] *(Sales only)* Acknowledgment of inspection opportunity — waived or exercised

Use plain English; each item gets its own initial/signature line.

### 6. Certifications & Signature Blocks

**Three certification categories**, each with signature block (printed name, signature, date):

| Party | Certifies |
|---|---|
| **Seller/Landlord** | Information is true, accurate, complete; all known hazards disclosed; all available records provided |
| **Buyer/Tenant** | Receipt of all required disclosures, pamphlet, and records; understanding of federal rights |
| **Agent/Broker** (if any) | Informed seller/landlord of disclosure obligations; ensured compliance; provided pamphlet to buyer/tenant |

Provide sufficient blocks for multiple co-sellers, co-buyers, co-tenants, or agents.

## Formatting Requirements

- Readable font (Times New Roman or Arial), minimum 10pt
- Full line break between major sections
- Bold/underlined section headings
- Footer on every page: property address + "Page X of Y"
- Target length: 2–4 pages

## Guidelines

- Disclosure **must** be provided during negotiation — before the buyer/tenant becomes obligated under any contract
- All parties retain signed copies for **minimum 3 years** from sale date or lease commencement
- For leases: disclosure obligation **renews** with each lease renewal or new tenant
- Distinguish *lead-based paint* from *lead-based paint hazards* — they are legally distinct concepts
- Check for state-specific supplemental requirements (e.g., state-approved pamphlet substitutes, additional disclosures)
- Never leave the records section blank — use affirmative "no records available" language
- If user-provided documents have gaps, identify and request missing information before drafting
