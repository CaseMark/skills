# Motion for Stay Pending Appeal

Drafts a court-ready motion to stay enforcement of a trial court judgment pending appellate review, structured around the four-factor balancing test with supersedeas bond analysis.

## Prerequisites

1. **Trial court judgment** — exact date of entry, specific relief ordered, monetary amounts
2. **Notice of appeal** — filing date, appellate case number, designated appellate court
3. **Enforcement status** — any threatened or initiated enforcement actions (liens, garnishment, foreclosure, compliance demands)
4. **Appellate grounds** — identified legal errors for appeal (misapplication of law, evidentiary errors, procedural defects)
5. **Harm evidence** — financial records, business impact data, declarations supporting irreparable harm
6. **Applicable rules** — FRCP 62, FRAP 8, or corresponding state rules; local filing requirements
7. **Bond capacity** — client's financial condition, available surety, alternative security options

## Output Structure

### Document Components

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Caption** | Trial court name, parties with appellate designations (Appellant/Appellee), case number, title: "Motion to Stay Enforcement of Judgment Pending Appeal" |
| **Introduction** | Three anchors: (1) judgment date, (2) notice of appeal date, (3) specific irreparable harm from enforcement |
| **Procedural Posture** | What was ordered, amounts awarded, enforcement mechanisms available, any enforcement steps already taken |
| **Legal Standard** | Governing rule + seminal cases; frame as balancing test, not rigid checklist |
| **Four-Factor Analysis** | See detailed breakdown below |
| **Bond/Security** | Amount calculation, alternative arrangements if needed |
| **Proposed Conditions** | Duration, reporting, asset preservation, partial compliance if appropriate |
| **Conclusion & Prayer** | Synthesize strongest arguments; specific numbered relief requests |

### Four-Factor Analysis

Draft each factor as a separate headed section:

**Factor 1 — Likelihood of Success on the Merits**
- Identify 2-3 strongest legal errors (do not reproduce the appellate brief)
- Cite controlling appellate authority from the reviewing court
- For unsettled questions or issues of first impression, argue uncertainty itself supports a stay
- Standard varies by jurisdiction: "substantial question" vs. "strong showing" vs. sliding scale

**Factor 2 — Irreparable Harm**
- Quantify harm with specifics: dollar amounts, job losses, asset liquidation values, license revocations
- Explain why monetary damages cannot compensate (unique property, business dissolution, insolvency)
- Document imminence: scheduled sales, pending garnishments, compliance deadlines
- Support with client declarations and financial exhibits

**Factor 3 — Harm to Appellee**
- If monetary judgment: argue supersedeas bond fully protects appellee's financial interest
- If injunctive relief: propose partial stay or modified compliance that balances interests
- Acknowledge legitimate appellee concerns directly — enhances credibility
- Distinguish genuine harm from mere delay in receiving a potentially reversible windfall

**Factor 4 — Public Interest**
- Address only if genuinely implicated (government action, constitutional rights, public safety, regulatory enforcement)
- For purely private disputes: state neutrality honestly rather than manufacturing arguments
- If judgment affects third parties (employees, customers, community), address those impacts

```
Anticipated costs:         $[X]
─────────────────────────────────
Total bond:                $[X]
```

- If seeking reduced bond: present financial evidence (hardship, impossibility, asset liquidation harm)
- Alternative security options: letter of credit, asset pledge, transfer restrictions, partial cash deposit
- If requesting waiver: cite specific authority (governmental entity, injunction stays, equitable grounds)

### Prayer for Relief

Request specifically that the Court:
1. Stay execution and enforcement of the judgment entered on [date] pending final disposition of the appeal
2. Set the supersedeas bond at $[amount] or such other amount as the Court deems appropriate
3. Impose such conditions on the stay as the Court finds necessary
4. Grant such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper

## Guidelines

- **Tone**: Respectful, measured — frame the stay as preserving meaningful appellate review, not as a delay tactic
- **Sliding scale**: Strength in one factor can compensate for weakness in another — lead with your strongest factor
- **Jurisdiction check**: Verify whether motion must be filed in trial court first, appellate court, or both; confirm deadline (often 10-14 days from judgment or notice of appeal)
- **Local rules compliance**: Formatting, page limits, certificate of service, proposed order if required
- **Evidence**: Every factual assertion must be supported by declarations, exhibits, or record citations
- **Specialized standards**: Check for modified tests applicable to injunction stays, administrative appeals, or constitutional challenges
- **Emergency procedures**: If enforcement is imminent, flag availability of expedited or emergency briefing schedules
- **No hyperbole**: Avoid inflammatory language about opposing counsel or the trial court; credibility is paramount
