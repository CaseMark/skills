# M&A Disclosure Schedules

Drafts seller disclosure schedules that carve out known exceptions from representations and warranties, defining the boundary between accepted risk and indemnifiable breach.

## Prerequisites

1. **Definitive agreement** — executed or near-final draft with numbered R&W sections to match schedule numbers
2. **Capitalization table and stock ledger** — for ownership schedule
3. **Material contracts** — customer, vendor, IP license, credit, employment agreements
4. **Corporate records** — minute books, organizational documents, equity plan documents
5. **Due diligence materials** — financial statements, tax returns, litigation files, IP registrations, real property leases, insurance policies, environmental reports, benefit plan documents
6. **Closing checklist** — identifies which schedules require pre-closing updates

## Output Structure

### Document Header

```
Disclosure Schedules
to the [Stock/Asset/Merger] Purchase Agreement
dated as of [Date]
by and among [Buyer full legal name], as Buyer,
[Seller full legal name], as Seller, and
[Company full legal name], as the Company
```

### General Interpretive Provisions (prepend to all schedules)

- **Cross-disclosure**: Items disclosed on any schedule are deemed disclosed on all other schedules to the extent relevance is reasonably apparent on its face
- **No materiality admission**: Inclusion of any item does not constitute admission that it is material, constitutes a MAE, or establishes a materiality standard beyond what the agreement expressly provides
- **Defined terms**: Capitalized terms have meanings in the agreement unless otherwise defined herein
- **Third-party use**: Disclosures are solely for purposes of the agreement and do not constitute admissions to third parties or acknowledgments of required disclosure under applicable law

### Per-Schedule Content Standards

**Capitalization Schedule**

| Holder | Class | Shares/Units | Cert. No. | Issue Date | Price Paid | Restrictions/Encumbrances |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

- Separate tables: issued equity; options/warrants (include vesting schedule, exercise price, expiration, governing plan); convertible instruments (conversion formula, triggers)
- Confirm totals reconcile to agreement cap table representation and stock ledger
- Attach: equity plan documents, all outstanding grant agreements

**Material Contracts Schedule**

For each contract include: title, parties (full legal names), execution date, term/expiration, renewal options + notice periods, termination provisions + penalties, key economic terms, change-of-control/assignment provisions, consent/notice required for transaction (Y/N), breach or default status.

Organize by category: customer; supplier/vendor; IP licenses (in/out); real property leases; equipment leases; credit/debt; guarantees; JV/partnership; employment/consulting; other.

**Litigation Schedule**

For each matter: full case caption, court/tribunal/jurisdiction, docket number, filing date, nature of claims and legal theories, relief sought ($ amount), procedural status, next hearing/trial date, company's liability assessment, estimated exposure range, insurance coverage + any reservation of rights, company counsel.

For threatened claims: identifying party, date/manner of threat, basis, likelihood assessment.

Include company-as-plaintiff matters and outstanding judgments/orders.

**Intellectual Property Schedule**

| Type | Mark/Title/No. | Filing Date | Issue/Reg. Date | Expiration | Jurisdiction | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Patent | | | | | | | |
| Trademark | | | | | | | |
| Copyright | | | | | | | |
| Domain | | | | | | | |

- Inbound licenses: licensor, licensed IP, scope (exclusive/non-exclusive, field of use, territory), term, royalties, performance minimums, CoC provisions
- Outbound licenses: same fields plus sublicense rights
- Open source: identify components, applicable license (GPL, LGPL, MIT, Apache, etc.), integration method (linked/embedded), any copyleft disclosure risk

**Real Property Schedule**

*Owned*: street address, legal description, recording reference, use, sq. ft., zoning, assessed value, encumbrances (mortgage lender, outstanding principal), easements/covenants/restrictions, condemnation proceedings, title policy reference + exceptions.

*Leased* (table):

| Landlord | Property/Suite | Term (Start–End) | Base Rent | Rent Escalations | Security Deposit | Renewal Options | CoC/Assignment Clause | Defaults |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Note any subleases (subtenant, space, term, rent received).

**Financial Statements Schedule**

- GAAP departures or auditor qualifications
- Changes in accounting methods during covered periods
- Off-balance sheet arrangements (describe parties, commitments, purpose, financial impact)
- Related-party transactions (identify party, relationship, terms, arm's-length comparison)
- Contingent liabilities not fully reflected on balance sheet
- Subsequent events post-balance sheet date

**Tax Schedule**

- Filing jurisdictions by tax type (federal, state, local, foreign)
- Open tax years by jurisdiction
- Active audits/examinations: jurisdiction, years, issues, proposed adjustments, estimated exposure
- Tax sharing/allocation agreements: parties, covered taxes, allocation method, payment obligations
- Material elections (S corp, §338(h)(10), cost segregation, etc.) and impact
- Uncertain tax positions: position, benefit claimed, basis, potential exposure [VERIFY applicable FIN 48/ASC 740 disclosure standard]
- Credit/deduction recapture risks

**Employee Benefits Schedule**

List all plans by type: 401(k)/defined benefit, health/welfare (insured vs. self-funded), equity compensation, deferred compensation, severance/CIC arrangements.

For each qualified plan: plan name, EIN/plan number, most recent IRS determination letter date, funded status (DB plans), required contributions, pending claims/investigations.

Equity grants table: recipient, grant date, type, shares, vesting schedule, vested/unvested split, exercise price, expiration, treatment on closing (accelerate/assume/cash-out).

CIC agreements: employee, position, trigger, payment amount/formula, benefits, restrictive covenants (non-compete, non-solicit terms and duration).

**Environmental Schedule**

- Permits: type, issuing agency, number, expiration, conditions, compliance status
- NOVs/enforcement actions: date, agency, alleged violation, penalty, status, corrective measures
- Phase I/II assessments: date, consultant, findings summary, recommendations
- Active/completed remediation: contamination type, remedial actions, estimated cost, regulatory oversight, cost-sharing arrangements
- Hazardous materials present: ACM, LBP, PCBs, USTs, other regulated substances; management/compliance measures
- Off-site disposal liabilities; liabilities from formerly owned/operated facilities

**Insurance Schedule**

| Coverage Type | Carrier | Policy No. | Period | Per-Occurrence Limit | Aggregate Limit | Deductible/SIR | Claims-Made/Occurrence | Premium |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

5-year claims history per policy type: date, incident description, amount claimed, insurer payment, company payment, status.

Note: denied claims and basis; coverage disputes; known circumstances that may generate future claims; cancellation/non-renewal notices; coverage gaps.

**Permits/Regulatory Schedule**

- All material governmental licenses and permits (business, professional, industry-specific, import/export)
- For each: type, issuing authority, number, expiration, conditions, compliance status, pending renewal
- Governmental inspections/investigations: agency, date, subject, findings, penalties, corrective actions, status
- Consent decrees, settlement agreements, compliance plans: obligations, term, monitoring/reporting requirements, compliance status

### Certification Block

```
CERTIFICATION

The undersigned officer of [Company], on behalf of [Company] and solely in
his/her/their capacity as an officer thereof, hereby certifies that the
information set forth in these Disclosure Schedules is true, correct, and
complete in all material respects as of the date of the Agreement [and as of
the Closing Date, as updated pursuant to Section [__] of the Agreement].

Signature: _______________________
Name:
Title:
Date:
```

Negotiate certification standard (knowledge after due inquiry vs. best of knowledge after reasonable investigation) to match agreement standard.

## Guidelines

- **Specificity over generality**: Never use placeholders ("certain consents may be required"). Always name parties, cite section numbers, state amounts, and describe current status
- **Cross-reference discipline**: Include explicit cross-references between schedules where the same matter qualifies multiple reps; rely on general cross-disclosure provision as backstop only
- **Consent column**: Flag every material contract with a change-of-control or assignment provision — critical to closing conditions analysis
- **No unattached exhibits**: Every exhibit referenced in the schedules must be attached and labeled before delivery
- **Schedule updates**: If the agreement permits pre-closing updates, track changes clearly (redline or dated addendum) and confirm whether updates cure breaches or only reduce indemnity exposure
- **Open source risk**: GPL/LGPL copyleft components embedded in proprietary products may require disclosure of source code — flag for IP counsel review [VERIFY applicable license terms]
- **Jurisdiction**: US-focused; for cross-border transactions, add foreign IP registrations, foreign regulatory permits, and applicable foreign tax jurisdictions as additional rows/sections
- **Confidentiality**: Confirm governing NDA or agreement confidentiality provisions protect competitively sensitive disclosures (customer names, pricing, trade secrets) before delivery to buyer
