# Audit and Compensation Committee Charter

Creates a board-adopted governance agreement for Audit and Compensation Committees with enforceable duties, delegated powers, and documentation standards.

## Prerequisites

1. **Corporate charter documents** — certificate of incorporation, bylaws, existing board resolutions.
2. **Company profile** — public vs private, state of incorporation, exchange listing, and jurisdiction(s).
3. **Industry context** — any sector-specific regulatory overlay (banking, healthcare, energy, etc.).
4. **Governance inputs** — current committee structure, independence policy, director roster, term rotation model.
5. **Financial/reporting context** — financial systems, internal audit function, audit firm history, material risk areas.
6. **Compensation context** — compensation philosophy, equity plans, officer agreements, severance framework.
7. **Disclosure context** (public companies) — proxy/SEC workflow, prior Audit & Comp Committee Reports, related filing practices.
8. **Authority chain confirmation** — whether committee authority can be adopted by board resolution under bylaws/state law.

## Output Structure / Process

1. Perform a regime map and fill the table:

| Input | Required determination |
| --- | --- |
| Company type | Public vs private; if public: exchange + SEC regime |
| Directors | Independent status baseline + required expertise |
| State law | Statutory authority for board committees and duties |
| Audit governance | Internal audit scope, auditor relationship, SOX/independence posture |
| Compensation governance | Equity + officer compensation exposure, clawbacks, 280G sensitivity |

2. Draft charter in this exact section order:

| Section | Required content |
| --- | --- |
| Preamble | Board adoption language, purpose, hierarchy, relationship to bylaws |
| Definitions | Terms used repeatedly (committee, Independent Director, Affiliate, Covered Person, etc.) |
| Formation | Committees created, terms, appointment, vacancies, removal, chair duties |
| Composition | Independence standards, financial expert standard, 16b-3-related committee function [VERIFY] |
| Powers and resources | Audit firm and advisor engagement authority, budget, information rights |
| Meetings | Calendar, quorum, notice, written consents, executive sessions, minutes |
| Audit duties | Financial reporting oversight, internal audit, auditor relations, complaints process |
| Compensation duties | CEO and officer pay process, equity administration, risk controls, succession |
| Reporting | Board reporting cadence, escalation triggers, disclosure coordination |
| Compliance | SOX, SEC, exchange requirements, industry overlays, record retention |
| Evaluation and amendment | Annual self-evaluation, charter review, training, amendment procedure |

3. Attach draft output template:

```markdown
BOARD RESOLUTION ADOPTING COMMITTEE CHARTERS
Adopted this ___ day of _________, 20__ by the Board of Directors.

ARTICLE I. ESTABLISHMENT AND AUTHORITY
- Establish Board Audit Committee
- Establish Board Compensation Committee
- Delegated authority and limits

ARTICLE II. PURPOSE
- Audit Committee purpose
- Compensation Committee purpose

ARTICLE III. COMPOSITION AND INDEPENDENCE
- Membership criteria
- Independence rules (NYSE/Nasdaq/private adaptations)
- Term and rotation

ARTICLE IV. POWERS AND DUTIES
- Audit Committee powers
- Compensation Committee powers

ARTICLE V. PROCEDURES
- Meetings
- Quorum and voting
- Information rights and officer attendance

ARTICLE VI. REPORTING
- Board report format and timing
- Public-company disclosures

ARTICLE VII. DOCUMENTATION AND REVIEW
- Minutes standards
- Annual evaluation and charter refresh
```

4. Append **compliance checklist** at end of every draft:

- [ ] Jurisdiction-specific law references verified.
- [ ] Exchange rule citation mapped to exact requirement.
- [ ] Independence and financial-expert criteria fit all directors.
- [ ] Auditor engagement and non-audit service controls covered.
- [ ] No management-voting or conflicted-attendance during sensitive deliberations.
- [ ] Compensation risk controls include caps/clawbacks/holding/risk metric checks.
- [ ] Public-company disclosure/report obligations included and routing defined.
- [ ] Executive session practices defined for both committees.

## Guidelines

### Do
- Use clear, directive language: “shall,” “must,” and “is prohibited.”
- Separate mandatory rules from best-practice enhancements with marked subsections.
- Keep private-company charter lean where SOX/SEC requirements do not apply.
- Tie every committee power to enforceable oversight outputs (reviews, reports, escalation).

### Don’t
- Don’t assume exchange rules apply without confirming listing status.
- Don’t mix charter authority with operational management tasks.
- Don’t require unanimous committee action where statute/precedent expects majority unless intentionally stricter by design.
- Don’t import boilerplate from other jurisdictions without state-law confirmation.

### Compliance / citation notes
- Public-company references must be updated against current text before final delivery.
- Core U.S. touchpoints: Sarbanes-Oxley (including Section 301, Section 201, whistleblower protections such as Section 806), SEC rules (notably Reg S-K/S-X and Rule 10A-3), exchange standards (NYSE/Nasdaq), and governance best practices.
- Independence standards and compensation-advice mechanics must be checked against evolving SEC/NYSE/Nasdaq updates and company-specific charter rules. [VERIFY]
- Cross-border groups should add host-country governance addenda; do not rely solely on U.S. statute language.
