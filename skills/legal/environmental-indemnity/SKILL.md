# Environmental Indemnity Agreement

Drafts a transactional environmental indemnity that protects lenders and secured parties from environmental liabilities on financed or acquired properties.

## Prerequisites

1. **Primary transaction documents** — loan agreement, purchase agreement, or mortgage with defined terms, party names, loan amounts, key dates
2. **Property information** — legal description, address, current/prior uses
3. **Environmental reports** — Phase I/II ESAs, remediation reports, regulatory correspondence
4. **Party details** — full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions of formation for indemnitor and indemnitee
5. **Governing jurisdiction** — state where property is located

## Output Structure

### 1. Recitals

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| Transaction type | Loan agreement / purchase agreement |
| Parties & roles | Indemnitor = borrower/owner/guarantor; Indemnitee = lender/secured party + successors/assigns |
| Property identification | Legal description from deed/title |
| Known environmental conditions | Phase I/II findings, disclosed contamination |
| Purpose statement | Risk allocation for environmental liabilities |

Align all defined terms with primary deal documents.

### 2. Definitions

Include at minimum:

- **Environmental Laws** — CERCLA, RCRA, CWA, CAA, TSCA, EPCRA, applicable state equivalents, and all regulations promulgated thereunder
- **Hazardous Substances** — broad definition cross-referencing Environmental Laws; include petroleum, asbestos, PCBs, lead-based paint, mold, PFAS
- **Environmental Claim** — any demand, action, investigation, or proceeding relating to Environmental Laws or Hazardous Substances
- **Remediation** — investigation, cleanup, removal, containment, monitoring, and any response action
- **Release** — as defined under CERCLA § 101(22) [VERIFY], including migration from adjacent properties

### 3. Indemnification Provisions

Scope must cover:

- [x] Pre-existing contamination (known and unknown)
- [x] Releases during indemnitor's ownership/operations
- [x] Environmental law violations
- [x] Remediation costs (voluntary and compelled)
- [x] Regulatory enforcement actions and penalties
- [x] Third-party toxic tort claims
- [x] Natural resource damages
- [x] Diminution in property value
- [x] Defense costs, attorneys' fees, consultant fees
- [x] Costs of environmental assessments and monitoring

Key drafting points:
- Indemnity covers conditions regardless of when discovered
- Survives regardless of indemnitee's knowledge or due diligence findings
- Absolute and unconditional — not contingent on loan default
- Includes right of indemnitee to undertake remediation and seek reimbursement
- Covers consequential damages including lost loan interest and opportunity costs

### 4. Representations and Warranties

| Category | Representation |
|---|---|
| Compliance | Property complies with all Environmental Laws |
| No violations | No pending or threatened enforcement actions, investigations, or proceedings |
| No undisclosed releases | No releases except as disclosed in identified reports |
| Full disclosure | All environmental reports, assessments, and correspondence provided |
| USTs | No underground storage tanks, or all USTs properly registered and in compliance |
| ACMs/LBP | Status of asbestos-containing materials and lead-based paint disclosed |
| Permits | All required environmental permits obtained and current |
| Hazardous materials | All storage, use, and disposal in compliance |
| Prior operations | No prior operations reasonably likely to have caused contamination beyond disclosed conditions |

Qualify representations only where Phase I/II findings require specific disclosures — do not use blanket knowledge qualifiers.

### 5. Covenants

- Maintain compliance with all Environmental Laws
- Provide immediate written notice of any:
  - Violations or alleged violations
  - Government communications, inspections, or orders
  - Releases or threatened releases
  - Third-party claims
- Maintain all required environmental permits
- No new Hazardous Substances storage/use without indemnitee consent
- Grant access for environmental assessments at indemnitee's discretion
- Cooperate with any investigation or remediation
- Not settle any Environmental Claim without indemnitee's prior written consent

### 6. Remediation and Enforcement Mechanisms

```
If contamination discovered:
  1. Indemnitor must promptly investigate and remediate to applicable standards
  2. Indemnitor provides remediation plan for indemnitee approval
  3. If indemnitor fails to act within [__] days → indemnitee may undertake remediation
  4. All costs reimbursable on demand with interest at default rate
  5. Indemnitee may require establishment of escrow/reserve
  6. Remediation must achieve regulatory closure or no-further-action determination
```

### 7. Survival and Duration

- Survives closing of underlying transaction
- Continues until the **latest** of:
  - Full repayment of all loan obligations
  - Transfer of property by indemnitee
  - Completion of all required remediation and receipt of regulatory closure
  - Expiration of all applicable statutes of limitation (note: CERCLA has no SOL for certain claims) [VERIFY]
- Survives bankruptcy, insolvency, foreclosure, deed-in-lieu
- Independent of and not merged into any judgment on the loan documents

### 8. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

- Governing law: state where property is located (environmental regulations are primarily state-specific)
- Exclusive jurisdiction and venue in courts of that state
- Waiver of jury trial
- Note: arbitration generally disfavored for environmental disputes due to potential regulatory involvement and need for injunctive relief
- Prevailing party entitled to attorneys' fees and costs

### 9. General Provisions

- Severability with reformation preference
- Amendments require written consent of all parties
- Notices: specify addresses, methods (personal delivery, overnight courier, certified mail), effective dates
- Assignment: indemnitee may freely assign to loan participants, successors, servicers; indemnitor may not assign without consent
- No waiver by conduct; waivers must be written
- Entire agreement (as to environmental indemnity matters; does not supersede loan documents)
- Counterpart execution; ESIGN/UETA compliance if electronic signatures used

### 10. Signature Blocks

- Entity signatories with name, title, authority recital
- Witness/notary lines per jurisdictional requirements
- Include guarantor signature blocks if indemnitor is SPE

## Guidelines

- **Align with deal docs** — every defined term, party name, and property description must match the primary transaction documents exactly
- **State-specific statutes** — research the property state's environmental cleanup standards, voluntary cleanup programs, and indemnity enforcement rules; CERCLA provides federal floor but state law often imposes additional requirements
- **No knowledge qualifiers on indemnity** — representations may be knowledge-qualified where appropriate, but the indemnification obligation itself must be absolute
- **Phase I/II integration** — if environmental reports reveal recognized environmental conditions (RECs), draft specific disclosure schedules rather than broad carve-outs
- **PFAS/emerging contaminants** — ensure Hazardous Substances definition is broad enough to capture emerging contaminants not yet listed under CERCLA/RCRA
- **Bankruptcy considerations** — environmental indemnity obligations may be treated as administrative claims; include language asserting non-dischargeable status to the extent permitted by law
- **Multiple indemnitors** — if guarantors or related entities are co-indemnitors, obligations should be joint and several
- **Mark all uncertain statutory citations with [VERIFY]**
