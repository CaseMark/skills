# Employment Arbitration Agreement

Drafts a comprehensive, enforceable mutual arbitration agreement for employment relationships, balancing employer protections with procedural fairness requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Jurisdiction(s)** — state(s) where employee will work (drives unconscionability standards, PAGA treatment, etc.)
2. **Employee type** — prospective vs. current (affects consideration analysis); executive vs. general workforce
3. **Existing employment documents** — offer letter, handbook, prior arbitration agreements, equity plans
4. **Arbitration administrator preference** — JAMS or AAA (determines applicable rule set)
5. **Company entity** — full legal name of employing entity

## Output Structure

Draft the agreement with these sections in order:

### 1. Recitals & Parties

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Parties | Full legal entity name + employee full legal name |
| Effective date | Specify; clarify if applies to current/prospective employment |
| Consideration | Prospective: employment itself. Current: must provide independent consideration (bonus, continued employment per state law, equity access) |
| Mutual obligation | Both parties bound to arbitrate — required for enforceability |

### 2. Covered Claims

Draft broad coverage with specific enumeration:

- **Statutory**: Title VII, ADEA, ADA, GINA, FLSA, state equivalents
- **Wage & hour**: overtime, meal/rest breaks, misclassification, commissions
- **Common law**: wrongful termination, breach of contract, breach of implied covenant
- **Retaliation**: under any statute or public policy
- **Post-employment**: trade secrets, non-competes, non-solicits
- **Temporal scope**: claims arising before, during, and after employment
- Include "illustrative, not exhaustive" language

### 3. Excluded Claims & Carve-Outs

| Exclusion | Reason |
|---|---|
| Workers' comp, UI claims | Statutory administrative schemes |
| ERISA benefit claims | Where arbitration conflicts with statutory procedures |
| Injunctive relief for trade secrets/non-competes | Employer needs immediate judicial remedy |
| NLRB charges | Protected concerted activity |
| Agency charges (EEOC, state equivalents) | Right to file preserved; individual damages claims arbitrated |
| PAGA claims (CA) | Carve out per *Viking River Cruises v. Moriana* [VERIFY]; individual PAGA to arbitration, representative PAGA per current law |
| Auto-exemption savings clause | Any claim a court determines non-arbitrable |

### 4. Arbitration Procedures

```
Administrator:    [JAMS / AAA]
Rules:            [JAMS Employment Arbitration Rules / AAA Employment Rules]
Arbitrator:       Single neutral; retired judge or 10+ yr employment attorney
Location:         Metro area where employee works/worked
Panel threshold:  Three arbitrators if claim > $250,000 (optional)
Fallback:         If administrator unavailable → mutual selection → court appointment
```

**Discovery rights** (critical for enforceability):
- Presumptive 5 depositions per side; additional on good cause
- Document requests and interrogatories permitted
- Expert discovery including reports and depositions
- Arbitrator resolves disputes; may sanction abuse
- Reasonable discovery period specified

**Arbitrator authority**:
- All remedies available in court (compensatory, punitive where authorized, injunctive, declaratory)
- Written decision with findings of fact and conclusions of law
- Must apply substantive law (not equity dispensation)
- May rule on dispositive motions under court standards
- May NOT consolidate with other arbitrations without written consent
- May NOT award relief to non-parties

### 5. Cost Allocation

| Cost | Allocation |
|---|---|
| Filing fee (employee) | Capped at local court filing fee ($200–$400) |
| Hardship | Company advances employee's filing fee |
| Administrator fees | Company pays |
| Arbitrator compensation | Company pays |
| Attorney's fees | Each party bears own; fee-shifting per statute if employee prevails |
| Company prevailing | No fee recovery unless claim frivolous/bad faith |
| Transcript | Ordering party pays; if arbitrator requires, Company pays |
| Savings clause | If allocation unenforceable → reform to minimum necessary; presume Company bears disputed costs |

### 6. Waivers

**Jury trial waiver**:
- ALL CAPS or bold formatting — must be conspicuous
- Mutual waiver (both parties)
- Separate signature/initial line
- Acknowledgment of understanding

**Class/collective action waiver**:
- Individual claims only; no class, collective, or representative proceedings
- Severability sub-clause: if waiver invalidated for specific claim type → that claim severed to court; remaining claims stay in arbitration
- Employee election option: if class waiver struck, employee may void entire agreement (prevents forced class arbitration)

### 7. Employee Acknowledgments

Include all of the following:
- [ ] Received complete copy of agreement
- [ ] Had adequate review period (5 business days prospective; 21 days current employee)
- [ ] Opportunity to consult attorney
- [ ] Understands jury trial waiver and class action waiver
- [ ] Not waiving substantive rights, only forum
- [ ] Agreement is condition of employment (if applicable — state clearly)
- [ ] Revocation period if required by state law (typically 7 days)
- [ ] E-SIGN compliance language if electronic execution

### 8. General Provisions

**Governing law**: FAA (9 U.S.C. § 1 et seq.) governs agreement enforceability; state substantive law governs underlying claims.

**Severability**: If any provision invalid → sever and enforce remainder. Include reformation authorization (modify to minimum enforceable extent rather than strike).

**Integration**: Entire agreement re: arbitration; supersedes prior arbitration terms and conflicting handbook provisions. Modifications require mutual written consent.

**Survival**: Survives termination of employment; binds successors, assigns, heirs, executors. Address corporate transactions (successor assumes obligations). Enforceable in bankruptcy to maximum extent permitted.

### 9. Signature Block

- Employee signature, printed name, date
- Company representative signature, printed name, title, date
- Separate initial lines for: jury trial waiver, class action waiver
- E-SIGN acknowledgment block if electronic
- Statement confirming employee received executed copy

## Guidelines

- **Armendariz requirements** (CA and states following similar standards): mutual obligation, adequate discovery, no limitation on statutory remedies, employer bears arbitration costs, written arbitrator decision — all five must be satisfied
- **NY sexual harassment**: post-2018 restrictions on mandatory arbitration of sexual harassment claims; confirm current status of Ending Forced Arbitration Act (9 U.S.C. § 401–402) which invalidates pre-dispute arbitration agreements for sexual assault/harassment claims [VERIFY current scope]
- **PAGA**: *Viking River Cruises v. Moriana* (2022) and *Adolph v. Uber Technologies* (2023) [VERIFY] — individual PAGA claims compellable to arbitration; standing for representative claims may survive
- **Consideration for current employees**: varies by state — some accept continued employment, others require independent consideration
- Do NOT include unreasonably short limitations periods, one-sided discovery, or prohibitive cost terms — unconscionability risk
- Do NOT draft confidentiality provisions restricting employee's right to discuss wages/working conditions (NLRA § 7)
- Research jurisdiction-specific enforceability standards before finalizing
- Plain language accessible to non-lawyers; defined terms used consistently
