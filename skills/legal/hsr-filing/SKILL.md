# Hart-Scott-Rodino Premerger Notification Filing

Prepares a complete HSR filing for FTC/DOJ submission, covering threshold analysis, NAICS revenue breakdowns, competitive overlap assessment, and Item 4(c)/(d) document collection.

## Prerequisites

1. **Transaction documents** — definitive agreement, all exhibits, schedules, and amendments
2. **Corporate structure charts** — ultimate parent entities for both acquiring and acquired persons
3. **Financial statements** — most recent fiscal year revenues by business segment/product line
4. **SEC filings** — 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, proxy statements for the past fiscal year (or audited financials if private)
5. **Board/management materials** — presentations, memos, studies used by officers/directors to evaluate the deal
6. **Prior acquisitions list** — all acquisitions by acquiring person in the past 10 years

## Output Structure

### 1. Threshold Analysis

| Test | Requirement | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Size-of-Transaction | Meets current annual threshold (adjusted for inflation) | Valuation per 16 C.F.R. § 801.10 |
| Size-of-Person | At least one party meets revenue/asset threshold | Verify both parties |
| Exemptions | Check 16 C.F.R. Part 802 | Common misapplications: ordinary course assets, real property, investment-only securities |

**Valuation components to document:**
- Cash consideration
- FMV of non-cash consideration (stock, etc.)
- Assumed liabilities includable under § 801.10
- Earnout/contingent payment treatment
- Voting security percentage thresholds crossed (25%, 50%, other)

---

### 2. Party Identification

**Acquiring Person:**
- Ultimate parent entity + full chain of control through intermediaries
- Which entities hold acquired assets/securities
- Which entities' revenues and prior acquisitions must be reported

**Acquired Person:**
- Scope: entire entity, specific voting securities, or specific assets
- Ultimate parent entity
- If subsidiary/division only: clearly delineate scope

---

### 3. NAICS Revenue Breakdown

For each party, report revenues ≥ $1M at the six-digit NAICS level for the most recent fiscal year.

| NAICS Code | Description | Acquiring Person Revenue | Acquired Person Revenue | Overlap? |
|------------|-------------|--------------------------|-------------------------|----------|
| XXXXXX | [Product/service] | $X | $X | Yes/No |

**Methodology notes:**
- Classify at the most specific six-digit level available
- Document allocation methodology for bundled/integrated products
- Note any variance between internal reporting and NAICS classifications

---

### 4. Competitive Overlap Analysis

For each six-digit NAICS overlap:

- Products/services competing; degree of customer-side substitutability
- Market share estimates (source: industry reports, SEC filings, internal data)
- Principal competitors and approximate shares
- HHI analysis: flag post-merger HHI > 2,500 with delta > 200 [VERIFY current Guidelines thresholds]
- Vertical relationships: supplier-customer links, foreclosure risk, coordination concerns
- Geographic scope: local / regional / national / international

---

### 5. Item 4(c) and 4(d) Documents

**Scope:** All studies, surveys, analyses, and reports prepared by or for any officer or director evaluating the acquisition with respect to:
- Market shares, competition, or competitors
- Potential for sales growth or geographic/product market expansion

**Search targets:**
- Board presentations and minutes
- Investment committee memoranda
- Confidential information memoranda (CIMs)
- Market research, competitive landscape analyses, synergy studies
- Financial advisor, consultant, and investment bank materials provided to officers/directors

**Output:** Descriptive index with document title, date, author, recipient, and relevance to 4(c)/4(d).

> ⚠️ These documents drive substantive agency review. Include anything that touches competitive factors, even tangentially. Omissions risk civil penalties.

---

### 6. Prior Acquisitions (10-Year Lookback)

For each acquisition by the acquiring person in overlapping NAICS codes within the past 10 years:

| Date | Entity/Assets Acquired | Transaction Value | NAICS Codes | Overlap with Current Deal |
|------|------------------------|-------------------|-------------|--------------------------|
| | | | | |

Assess whether a roll-up pattern exists that may trigger cumulative scrutiny.

---

### 7. Certification and Execution

- Certifying person: officer or director with supervisory responsibility for filing preparation
- Attestation: filing is true, correct, and complete; all required documents submitted
- Legal exposure: false statements subject to 18 U.S.C. § 1001
- Filing date = certification date = start of statutory waiting period

---

### 8. Filing Assembly Checklist

- [ ] Notification and Report Form — all items complete and internally consistent
- [ ] NAICS revenue tables — verified against financial records
- [ ] Item 4(c)/4(d) documents — exhaustive search completed; descriptive index attached
- [ ] SEC filings / audited financials — most recent fiscal year
- [ ] CIMs and offering memoranda — included if prepared for this transaction
- [ ] Filing fee — calculated on transaction value; payment arranged
- [ ] Electronic submission — FTC HSR e-filing system format/naming conventions met
- [ ] Dual filing coordination — both parties filing same day if required

---

### 9. Post-Filing Monitoring

| Milestone | Timing | Action |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Initial waiting period | 30 days from filing | Monitor for deficiency notices |
| Early termination | Agencies' discretion | Request if no competitive concerns apparent |
| Second Request | Issued within waiting period | Triggers full document production + interrogatories |
| Closing eligibility | After waiting period expires or early termination granted | Advise client |

## Guidelines

- **Jurisdiction**: U.S. federal only (15 U.S.C. § 18a); non-U.S. merger control filings require separate analysis
- **Thresholds**: Dollar thresholds adjust annually — always confirm current amounts from FTC published notice before filing
- **Item 4(c)/(d) over-inclusion**: When in doubt, include the document; exclusion disputes are more costly than over-production
- **No legal conclusions on market definition**: Present competitive data factually; leave ultimate market definition to the agencies
- **Confidentiality**: HSR filings are exempt from FOIA disclosure (15 U.S.C. § 18a(h)); advise client accordingly
- **Penalties**: Non-compliance carries civil penalties up to $51,744/day [VERIFY current penalty amount] per violation
