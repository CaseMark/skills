# Conservation Easement

Drafts a perpetual conservation easement that is recordable, IRC §170(h)-compliant, and enforceable against successors in interest.

## Prerequisites

1. **Property information** — legal description, county, state, tax parcel numbers, total acreage, survey/plat
2. **Party details** — grantor (fee owner) full legal name/capacity; grantee (qualified organization) name, 501(c)(3) status, state-law eligibility to hold easements
3. **Baseline documentation report** — current condition inventory of conservation values
4. **Conservation values identification** — ecological, scenic, agricultural, historical, or open space features
5. **Existing encumbrances** — mortgages (subordination needed), liens, existing easements, title exceptions
6. **Reserved rights scope** — what uses grantor intends to retain (residential, agricultural, recreational, limited development)
7. **Tax deduction intent** — whether easement is a qualified conservation contribution under IRC §170(h)
8. **State-specific requirements** — enabling statute, execution formalities, recording format rules

## Output Structure

Draft the easement with the following sections in order:

### 1. Preamble & Parties

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Grantor | Full legal name, address, capacity (individual/trust/entity) |
| Grantee | Name, address, 501(c)(3) status, state-law qualification |
| Property | Legal description, county, state, parcel numbers, acreage |
| Consideration | Monetary amount or nominal/$1 for donated easement |
| Recitals | Shared intent to preserve conservation values in perpetuity |
| Exhibits referenced | Survey, baseline documentation report, management plans |

### 2. Grant of Easement

- Use jurisdiction-appropriate conveyancing language
- State easement is granted **in perpetuity**, runs with the land, binds successors and assigns
- Identify conservation purposes under IRC §170(h)(4): habitat, open space, agriculture, scenic, historic
- Specify rights conveyed: development rights, subdivision rights, restricted use rights
- Confirm grantor retains fee simple subject to easement
- Address priority relative to existing mortgages (require subordination)
- If tax deduction intended: state easement qualifies as "qualified conservation contribution" under IRC §170(h)

### 3. Conservation Purposes & Values

- Describe specific conservation values present on the property:
  - Wildlife habitat and species
  - Water resources (streams, wetlands, aquifers)
  - Agricultural soils and productive capacity
  - Viewsheds and scenic resources
  - Natural communities and ecological connectivity
- Align each purpose with IRC §170(h)(4) categories
- Reference baseline documentation report (attached as exhibit)
- Articulate public benefit: habitat connectivity, watershed protection, agricultural preservation, scenic conservation
- State this section governs interpretation of all restrictions and reserved rights

### 4. Prohibited & Restricted Uses

Address each category with specific, enforceable standards:

| Category | Typical Restrictions |
|---|---|
| Subdivision & development | Prohibit or limit to defined building envelopes; density caps |
| Commercial/industrial/mining | Prohibit extraction of soil, minerals, resources beyond reserved rights |
| Alteration of natural features | No grading, filling, excavation, vegetation removal outside permitted management |
| Water resources | No diversion, impoundment, pollution of streams, wetlands, groundwater |
| Waste & hazardous materials | No disposal, storage, or release |
| Signage & utilities | Limit to property-serving infrastructure |

- Tailor restrictions to the specific property and conservation values — do not rely on generic boilerplate alone
- Where restrictions are conditional, specify approval process and standard (e.g., "grantee approval, which shall not be unreasonably withheld if consistent with conservation purposes")

### 5. Reserved Rights of Grantor

For each reserved right, specify conditions ensuring consistency with conservation purposes:

- **Residential** — maintain, repair, replace existing structures within defined parameters (footprint, location, height)
- **Agricultural/forestry** — per best management practices and any attached management plan
- **Recreational** — personal/guest use that does not impair conservation values
- **Access** — maintain existing roads, trails, utilities for reasonable property use
- **Limited development** — if any, strictly define by location (building envelope), size, and purpose

For tax-qualifying easements: ensure reserved rights comply with Treasury Regulation standards on inconsistent uses. Where grantee approval is required, state whether approval may be withheld only if activity would harm conservation values or at broader discretion.

### 6. Stewardship, Monitoring & Enforcement

- **Access rights** — grantee has perpetual right to enter for monitoring/inspection with reasonable notice (specify notice period, typically 48 hours except emergencies)
- **Annual monitoring** — at least one annual site visit
- **Grantor reporting** — annual written compliance confirmation; advance notice of proposed activities requiring approval
- **Enforcement sequence**:
  1. Written notice of violation
  2. Cure period (typically 30 days, shorter for ongoing harm)
  3. Injunctive relief, damages, restoration of conservation values
  4. Recovery of enforcement costs and attorney's fees
- **Non-waiver** — failure to enforce promptly does not waive future enforcement
- **Third-party defense** — grantee obligation to defend easement; grantor duty to cooperate
- **Stewardship funding** — endowment contribution or annual stewardship fee requirement

### 7. Assignment, Amendment & Extinguishment

- **Assignment** — only to another qualified conservation organization with capacity and commitment to enforce; notice to grantor required
- **Amendment** — permitted only if it does not diminish conservation purposes; must comply with Treasury Regulations for donated easements; requires written agreement of both parties; may require state AG or other regulatory approval
- **Extinguishment** — by judicial proceeding only; if conservation purposes become impossible, grantee entitled to proportionate share of proceeds (value of easement relative to unencumbered FMV at time of gift per Treasury Reg. §1.170A-14(g)(6))
- **Perpetuity savings clause** — express intent that easement continues forever despite changed circumstances

### 8. General Provisions

Include: governing law (situs state), venue, severability, notice requirements (addresses + delivery methods), successors and assigns, recording obligations (grantor records and provides proof), liberal construction in favor of conservation purposes, integration clause, counterparts, authority of signatories, state-specific statutory notices or disclosures.

### 9. Exhibits, Execution & Acknowledgment

| Exhibit | Contents |
|---|---|
| A | Survey / legal description map with property boundaries, building envelopes, restricted areas |
| B | Baseline documentation report |
| C | Management plan(s) (forest, agricultural) if applicable |
| D | Mortgagee subordination agreement (if encumbered) |

- Signature blocks: grantor and grantee with appropriate capacity designations
- Notarial acknowledgments compliant with situs state
- Witness lines if required by state law
- Verify state recording office formatting requirements (margins, font size, legibility)

## Guidelines

- **IRC §170(h) compliance is mandatory** when tax deduction is intended — every restriction and reserved right must be evaluated against qualified conservation contribution requirements
- **Treasury Reg. §1.170A-14** governs baseline documentation, extinguishment proceeds, and inconsistent-use analysis — cross-check all provisions [VERIFY current regulation version]
- **State enabling statute varies significantly** — confirm state-specific requirements for execution, recording, amendment, and enforcement before finalizing
- Use defined terms consistently throughout; define all key terms in a definitions section or on first use
- Resolve all ambiguities in favor of protecting conservation values (include express construction clause)
- If property crosses jurisdictions, address governing law for each parcel
- Do not draft representations about tax consequences — advise parties to consult tax counsel
- Subordination of existing mortgages is **required** for IRC §170(h) compliance — flag if missing
