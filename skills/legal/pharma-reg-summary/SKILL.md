# Pharmaceutical Regulation Compliance Summary

Produces an actionable regulatory compliance summary for pharmaceutical companies, structured around FDA frameworks, patent/IP strategy, and promotional compliance.

## Prerequisites

1. **Product portfolio** — marketed products, pipeline compounds, therapeutic areas
2. **Regulatory filings** — pending NDAs, ANDAs, BLAs, active INDs, post-approval commitments
3. **Commercial activities** — speaker programs, consulting arrangements, DTC campaigns, digital marketing
4. **Compliance history** — prior warning letters, consent decrees, audit findings

## Output Structure

### Section 1: Drug Approval & Post-Market Obligations

| Pathway | Key Requirements | Common Pitfalls |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| NDA (505(b)(1)) | Full clinical data package, CMC, labeling | Data integrity deficiencies, incomplete safety databases |
| ANDA (505(j)) | Bioequivalence, Para IV certifications | Improper BE study design, patent certification errors |
| BLA (351(a)) | Biosimilarity/interchangeability data | Analytical similarity gaps, immunogenicity concerns |
| Expedited (BT/FT/AA) | Meeting post-market confirmatory requirements | Missing confirmatory trial milestones under accelerated approval |

For each product, address:
- [ ] IND compliance: 21 CFR Part 312 — safety reporting (7/15-day), annual reports, protocol amendments
- [ ] Data integrity: 21 CFR Part 11 (electronic records), GCP/GLP/GMP compliance
- [ ] Post-market: FAERS reporting (15-day expedited, 90-day periodic), REMS if applicable
- [ ] PSURs/PBRERs for internationally marketed products
- [ ] Post-approval commitments (PMRs/PMCs) — track status and deadlines

### Section 2: Patent & IP Regulatory Intersections

| Framework | Statute/Reg | Key Compliance Points |
|-----------|------------|----------------------|
| Orange Book listings | 21 CFR 314.53 | List patents claiming drug/method of use; improper listings risk antitrust exposure |
| Purple Book (biologics) | 42 USC § 262 | Reference product exclusivity, biosimilar interchangeability status |
| Hatch-Waxman certifications | 21 USC § 355(j)(2)(A)(vii) | Para I–IV certs; 30-month stay triggers; 180-day FTF exclusivity |
| Patent term extension | 35 USC § 156 | One extension per product; file within 60 days of approval |
| Pediatric exclusivity | 21 USC § 355a | 6-month addition; requires written request compliance |
| NCE exclusivity | 21 USC § 355(c)(3)(E) | 5-year (NCE), 3-year (new clinical investigations) |

Flag:
- [ ] Alignment between patent strategy and regulatory submission timelines
- [ ] Accuracy of Orange Book use codes (risk of delisting petitions, FTC scrutiny)
- [ ] Upcoming exclusivity expirations and generic/biosimilar entry risk

### Section 3: Marketing & Promotional Compliance

**Off-Label Promotion**
- Prohibition: 21 USC § 331(d); FDA treats as misbranding
- Narrow exceptions: unsolicited requests (respond with on-label redirect + medical affairs), peer-reviewed reprints under 21 USC § 360aaa (must meet specific criteria)
- Enforcement trend: DOJ continues pursuing under FCA theories

**Promotional Materials Requirements**
- Fair balance: adequate risk disclosure proportional to benefit claims (21 CFR 202.1)
- Substantial evidence standard for efficacy claims
- Pre-dissemination review: Form FDA 2253 filing at time of first use

**Digital & Social Media**
- Character-limited platforms: FDA draft guidance on presenting risk info in space-constrained formats
- User-generated content: monitor and correct misinformation on owned channels
- Influencer/KOL arrangements: disclose per FTC + Sunshine Act

**Financial Compliance**

| Statute | Scope | Key Rules |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| Anti-Kickback (42 USC § 1320a-7b) | Remuneration to induce referrals | Safe harbors: FMV compensation, written agreements, legitimate services |
| Sunshine Act (42 USC § 1320a-7h) | Physician payment reporting | Report transfers of value >$10; track meals, consulting, speaking fees |
| FCA (31 USC § 3729) | False claims to federal programs | Off-label promotion as predicate for FCA liability |

**DTC Advertising**
- Adequate provision requirement for broadcast ads (major statement + toll-free number/website)
- Print: brief summary of prescribing information
- Pre-review: submit to OPDP; consider Advisory Comment process for novel claims

## Compliance Checklist

- [ ] SOPs for regulatory submissions, safety reporting, and labeling updates
- [ ] Commercial team training on promotional boundaries (annual + new hire)
- [ ] Internal MLR (medical-legal-regulatory) review of all promotional materials
- [ ] Audit program: labeling accuracy, advertising claims, speaker program FMV
- [ ] Post-approval commitment tracking system with escalation triggers
- [ ] Whistleblower/compliance hotline and investigation protocols
- [ ] International harmonization assessment (EMA, PMDA) if applicable

## Guidelines

- Cite specific CFR sections, USC provisions, and FDA guidance documents by title/date
- Distinguish requirements by product type (small molecule vs. biologic vs. generic)
- Note where state laws impose additional requirements (e.g., state Sunshine laws, marketing consent statutes)
- Flag any requirements that differ for controlled substances (DEA scheduling overlay)
- Mark uncertain or recently updated citations with [VERIFY]
- If international operations exist, briefly compare FDA vs. EMA requirements where material differences affect strategy
- Do not provide legal conclusions on enforcement risk — present facts and let counsel assess
