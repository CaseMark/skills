# Personal Injury Demand Summary

Synthesizes case materials into a persuasive demand summary for settlement negotiations or internal case evaluation.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case file documents** — police/incident reports, witness statements, photographs, correspondence
2. **Medical records** — ER records through current treatment, imaging, surgical notes, discharge summaries
3. **Billing records** — itemized medical bills from all providers
4. **Employment/wage records** — pay stubs, tax returns, employer verification letters
5. **Expert reports** (if available) — life care plans, vocational assessments, accident reconstruction
6. **Insurance information** — known policy limits, prior correspondence with adjusters

## Output Structure

### 1. Executive Overview

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Incident date & location | Specific date, city/county, state |
| Parties | Plaintiff(s) and defendant(s) with roles |
| Case type | MVA / premises liability / product defect / med mal / other |
| Theory of liability | One-sentence primary negligence theory |
| Demand amount | Total demand figure |

### 2. Liability Analysis

- [ ] Identify defendant's specific acts/omissions constituting breach of duty
- [ ] Cite supporting evidence: police reports, eyewitness accounts, expert opinions
- [ ] Reference applicable legal standards (traffic laws, building codes, industry standards, statutes)
- [ ] Address comparative fault arguments and rebut or minimize
- [ ] State conclusion on liability apportionment

### 3. Injury Documentation (Chronological)

Organize by treatment phase:

| Phase | Details to Include |
|---|---|
| **Emergency/acute** | Date, facility, presenting complaints, initial diagnoses, imaging results |
| **Surgical** | Procedures, dates, operative findings, hardware/implants |
| **Post-operative/rehab** | PT/OT frequency and duration, progress notes, functional benchmarks |
| **Ongoing/maintenance** | Current treatment, medications, remaining complaints |
| **Prognosis** | MMI status, permanent impairments, future treatment needs |

- Distinguish **objective findings** (imaging, lab results, surgical notes) from **subjective complaints**
- If pre-existing conditions exist: clearly delineate aggravation/exacerbation vs. new injury causation

### 4. Damages Itemization

#### A. Economic Damages

| Category | Source | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Past medical expenses | Itemize by provider, service date, amount | $ |
| Future medical expenses | Life care plan or treating physician projection | $ |
| Past lost wages | Employment records, pay period, rate | $ |
| Future lost earning capacity | Vocational expert or wage trajectory analysis | $ |
| Out-of-pocket expenses | Transportation, home mods, Rx, assistive devices | $ |
| **Economic subtotal** | | **$** |

#### B. Non-Economic Damages

For each category, support with specific evidence:

- **Pain and suffering** — treatment intensity, duration, medication regimen
- **Emotional distress** — mental health treatment, documented psychological impact
- **Loss of enjoyment of life** — specific activities/hobbies curtailed, before/after comparison
- **Permanent disfigurement/disability** — functional limitations, rating if available
- **Loss of consortium** (if applicable) — spousal testimony, relationship impact

#### C. Total Demand

| Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Economic damages | $ |
| Non-economic damages | $ |
| **Total demand** | **$** |

### 5. Settlement Positioning

- [ ] Comparable jury verdicts in the jurisdiction (cite specific verdicts if available)
- [ ] Policy limits considerations
- [ ] Litigation risk assessment (strengths and vulnerabilities)
- [ ] Causation strength evaluation
- [ ] Comparative fault exposure percentage estimate
- [ ] Response deadline
- [ ] Conditions (confidentiality, structured settlement, liens)

## Guidelines

- **Every factual assertion must trace to a specific document** in the case file — cite by document name/date
- **Flag documentation gaps** that need additional discovery or investigation
- Maintain persuasive but professional tone — specific human impact examples, not emotional appeals
- Present damages conservatively enough to be credible but with room to negotiate
- Do not fabricate or speculate on medical causation — rely on treating physician opinions
- If jurisdiction-specific damage caps apply (e.g., non-economic caps in some states), note them
- Mark any unverified legal citations with `[VERIFY]`
