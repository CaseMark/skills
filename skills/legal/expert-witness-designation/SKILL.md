# Expert Witness Designation

Drafts a comprehensive expert witness designation that satisfies procedural disclosure requirements while strategically positioning the expert's qualifications and opinions.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Case information** — caption, case number, court, judge, designation deadline from scheduling order
2. **Operative pleadings** — to identify disputed issues the expert addresses
3. **Expert information**:
   - Full legal name, business address, phone, email, current title
   - Current CV (most recent version)
   - Engagement letter / fee agreement
   - Preliminary reports, memoranda, or findings
4. **Prior testimony** — cases testified in during previous 4 years (FRCP 26(a)(2)(B)(v))
5. **Prior designations** — from other matters for consistency
6. **Compensation details** — hourly rates by activity type, total paid to date

## Output Structure

### Front Matter

- Case caption matching court records
- Title: "[Party]'s Expert Witness Designation Pursuant to [Rule]"
- Citation to governing rule (FRCP 26(a)(2), or state equivalent like CA CCP § 2034.260)
- Procedural context: timely designation, sequential/simultaneous exchange, rebuttal status

### Expert Identification

- Full name, business address, phone, email
- Precise subspecialty description connecting to case issues
  - ✓ "Board-certified forensic pathologist specializing in biomechanics of traumatic injury"
  - ✗ "Medical expert"
- Current professional role and primary activities

### Qualifications Narrative

Present credentials strategically under FRE 702 framework:

**Education**
- Degrees, institutions, years, honors
- Specialized fellowships, post-doctoral training, certifications relevant to case issues

**Professional Experience**
- Positions emphasizing relevance to disputed issues
- Leadership roles demonstrating peer standing
- Quantified experience ("performed over 500 of the specific procedure at issue")

**Credentials**
- Licenses (jurisdiction, number, status)
- Board certifications (body, area, date, recertification)
- Professional organization memberships, fellowships, leadership

**Publications and Presentations**
- Peer-reviewed articles (emphasize those on case-relevant topics)
- Textbooks, treatises, book chapters
- Conference presentations, keynote addresses
- Editorial board positions

**Prior Testimony (4-year disclosure)**
- Case name, court/forum, jurisdiction, issues addressed, depo/trial
- Balance of plaintiff vs. defendant testimony
- Address any prior Daubert exclusions proactively if likely discoverable

### Opinion Summary

For each discrete opinion:

1. **Conclusion** — concise, definitive statement addressing a claim element or disputed fact
2. **Methodology** — analytical framework, testing protocols, industry standards applied
3. **Data and materials** — specific documents, exhibits, depositions, test results reviewed (cite by exhibit number or description)
4. **Reasoning** — logical progression from data + methodology → conclusion
5. **Reliability basis** — Daubert/Frye compliance: peer review, error rate, general acceptance, standards
6. **Limitations addressed** — assumptions, rejected alternatives, qualifications

Frame in technically accurate but jury-accessible language.

### Expert Report Integration

- Attach or reference full report per FRCP 26(a)(2)(B)
- Confirm report contains all required elements:
  - Complete opinion statement with basis and reasons
  - All data/information considered
  - Exhibits to summarize/support opinions
  - Qualifications and CV
  - Publications (10-year list)
  - Prior testimony (4-year list)
  - Compensation statement

### Compensation Disclosure

- Hourly rates by activity (consultation, document review, report writing, deposition, trial)
- Total paid to date
- Estimated total through trial
- Non-standard arrangements (flat fees, retainers, expenses)

### Supplementation and Certification

- Acknowledge duty to supplement under FRCP 26(e)
- Certification under FRCP 26(g) that disclosure is complete and accurate
- Attorney signature block with bar number
- Certificate of service

## Guidelines

- Connect every qualification to the specific case issues — not a generic CV dump
- Each opinion must be traceable to specific case evidence, not generalized
- Anticipate Daubert challenges — build methodology defense into the designation
- Proactively address potential bias (repeat retention, publication positions)
- Verify all procedural requirements for the specific jurisdiction and judge
- Flag any non-retained expert exceptions under FRCP 26(a)(2)(C)
