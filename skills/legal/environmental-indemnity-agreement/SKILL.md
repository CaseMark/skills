# Environmental Indemnity Agreement

Allocate environmental risk and remediation liability in a transaction while aligning with core deal documents.

## Prerequisites

1. **Primary transaction documents** — loan/acquisition agreement, security instruments, guarantees, and any environmental riders
2. **Property details** — legal description, address, APN, current and historical uses, tenant/occupant list
3. **Environmental diligence** — Phase I/II ESA reports, remediation plans, agency correspondence, permits
4. **Risk allocation terms** — indemnity scope, caps or exclusions, escrows/holdbacks, insurance requirements
5. **Parties and authority** — full legal names, entity type, formation jurisdiction, signatory authority
6. **Governing law/venue** — usually the property state; confirm venue requirements and local practice

## Output Structure / Process

1. **Intake and alignment** — confirm deal structure, parties, property, governing law, and map defined terms to the primary deal documents.
2. **Assemble using the outline** — follow the section table below and keep defined terms consistent across documents.
3. **Populate core protections** — apply the indemnity/covenant checklist below to ensure full coverage.
4. **Add schedules/exhibits** — attach property description and environmental disclosure materials.
5. **QA pass** — confirm survival, assignment, enforcement, and consistency with the transaction documents.

**Document Outline**

| Section | Required Content |
| --- | --- |
| Parties and Recitals | Identify indemnitor/indemnitee, transaction background, property description, and intent to allocate environmental risk |
| Definitions | Environmental Laws, Hazardous Substances, Release, Losses, Remediation, Governmental Authority, Property |
| Indemnity | Scope covering pre-existing and ongoing contamination, regulatory actions, third-party claims, costs/fees, NRD, and property value impacts |
| Reps and Warranties | Compliance status, permits, notices/claims, known conditions, disclosure of reports and site conditions |
| Covenants | Ongoing compliance, notice obligations, cooperation/access, maintenance of permits, no new releases |
| Remediation Rights | Indemnitee rights to investigate/remediate and recover costs; escrow/holdback mechanics if applicable |
| Insurance/Financial Assurance | Coverage types, limits, additional insured/loss payee status if required |
| Survival and Enforcement | Survival period, independence from loan default, remedies and reimbursement rights |
| Assignment | Indemnitee assignment rights to successors, participants, and affiliates |
| Governing Law/Venue | Property-state law and exclusive venue, unless deal terms dictate otherwise |
| Miscellaneous | Notices, amendments, counterparts, severability, waiver, electronic signatures |

**Indemnity and Covenant Checklist**

- Cover losses arising from pre-closing, current, and future releases or violations connected to the property or operations.
- Include regulatory actions, third-party claims, penalties, investigation costs, response/remediation costs, and attorneys’ fees.
- State indemnity applies regardless of indemnitee knowledge or diligence results, unless the deal expressly carves out.
- Require prompt notice of violations, agency communications, and releases.
- Require cooperation and access for sampling, testing, and response actions.

**Schedules / Exhibits**

- Property legal description and map or survey reference.
- Environmental reports and assessments list.
- Known conditions and disclosure schedule.

**QA Checklist**

- Defined terms match the loan/acquisition documents.
- Survival language addresses long-tail liability and completion of remediation.
- Assignment clause aligns with lender participation or sale rights.
- Notice addresses and methods match the main deal documents.
- Remedies are cumulative and do not limit statutory rights.

## Guidelines

- Keep the indemnity scope as broad as the deal permits; narrow only by explicit, negotiated carveouts.
- Tie governing law and venue to the property state unless the deal mandates a different forum.
- Avoid contradicting representations or covenants in the primary transaction documents.
- If escrows or reserves are required, state funding triggers, release conditions, and control rights.
- Confirm the agreement survives closing, repayment, and transfer unless the transaction expressly limits survival.
- Do not include jurisdiction-specific statutory citations unless verified for the property state.
- Use disclosure schedules to capture known conditions without reducing indemnitor liability beyond negotiated terms.
