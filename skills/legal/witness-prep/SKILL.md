# Deposition Witness Preparation Session

Complete framework for conducting ethical, effective witness preparation sessions before depositions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Witness identity and type** — party, fact witness, corporate rep (30(b)(6)), or expert
2. **Deposition notice** with date, time, location, and noticed topics
3. **Key documents** — authored by witness, received by witness, mentioning witness, and likely exhibits
4. **Prior statements** — interrogatory answers, declarations, prior testimony
5. **Case context** — claims, defenses, and anticipated difficult areas

## Session Structure

| Model | When to Use | Sessions | Total Hours |
|-------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| **Two-Session** (recommended) | Most depositions | 2 sessions, 1-7 day gap | 4-7 hrs |
| **Single Extended** | Simple matters / scheduling constraints | 1 session | 4-6 hrs |
| **Multi-Session** | Complex cases or anxious witnesses | 3 sessions | 6-8 hrs |

For **30(b)(6) witnesses**: add topic-by-topic preparation time; may require additional sessions.

---

## Session 1: Orientation & Document Review (2-4 hrs)

### A. Opening (15-20 min)

Set expectations:
- Purpose is truthful, clear testimony — not scripting answers
- Explain deposition mechanics: oath, who's present, court reporter, transcript use
- Address common concerns:

| Concern | Response |
|---------|----------|
| "What if I don't know?" | Say "I don't know" |
| "What if I don't remember?" | Say "I don't recall" |
| "What if I make a mistake?" | We can correct via errata |
| "Will you help me?" | I can object, but you must answer |

### B. Ground Rules (20-30 min)

- **Listen fully** — wait for the complete question before answering
- **Clarify** — "I don't understand" is always acceptable
- **Answer only what's asked** — don't volunteer, explain, or justify
- **Tell the truth** — even if it hurts; evasion always makes it worse
- **"I don't know" vs. "I don't recall"** — distinguish never-knew from can't-remember-now
- **Take your time** — pause before answering
- **Flag problem questions** — compound, false premise, characterization, absolutes ("always"/"never")

### C. Document Review (60-90 min)

Review in this order:
1. Documents witness authored
2. Documents witness received
3. Documents mentioning witness
4. Key exhibits likely to be used
5. Witness's prior statements

For each document, build a familiarity table:

| Document | Witness Role | Key Points | Potential Issues |
|----------|-------------|------------|------------------|
| | Author / Recipient / Mentioned | | |

**Red-flag documents** (contradictions, bad phrasing, gaps in memory):
- Ensure witness understands the document
- Ask what they recall — do NOT suggest answers
- Let witness formulate their own honest explanation

### D. Substantive Topic Review (60-90 min)

For each anticipated topic:
1. Explain why it matters to the case
2. Ask what witness knows (capture genuine recollection)
3. Review relevant documents
4. Identify uncertainty or difficulty areas

**Vulnerable areas** — focus on:
- Distinguishing knowledge vs. assumption
- Preparing witness for extensive probing
- Ensuring document comprehension

### E. Session 1 Close

- **Homework**: list of documents to re-review, topics to reflect on
- **Confirm**: Session 2 date/time, deposition date/time/location
- **Reassure**: normal to feel uncertain; practice session is next

---

## Session 2: Practice & Refinement (2-3 hrs)

### A. Check-In (10-15 min)
- Address new concerns or memories since Session 1
- Confirm document review completed

### B. Rule Refresh (10 min)
- Quick review of ground rules from Session 1

### C. Practice Examination (60-90 min)

Play opposing counsel. Include these question types:
1. Background (warm-up)
2. Open-ended ("Tell me about...")
3. Document-based ("Showing you Exhibit X...")
4. Detail (dates, times, people)
5. Commitment ("Is that everything?")
6. Challenging (confrontational, compound, false premise)
7. Impeachment setup (locking in testimony)

**Coaching feedback table:**

| Behavior | Correction |
|----------|-----------|
| Answers before question finishes | "Wait for the full question" |
| Volunteers extra info | "Answer what's asked, then stop" |
| Guesses or speculates | "Say you don't know" |
| Gets defensive | "Stay calm, just answer" |
| Looks at attorney for help | "You need to answer — I can't help on substance" |
| Rambling answers | "Shorter. Answer, then stop." |
| Uses absolutes | "Are you sure 'never'?" |

Spend extra time on identified vulnerable topics with multiple phrasings.

### D. Objection Explanation (15-20 min)

Instruct witness:
1. Keep listening when you hear "objection"
2. Wait a beat for the objection to finish
3. Then answer — unless specifically instructed not to
4. Don't interpret the objection; just answer truthfully

Instruction not to answer is rare — limited to:
- Attorney-client privilege
- Work product (jurisdiction-dependent)
- Court order violation
- Genuine harassment

### E. Day-Of Logistics (15-20 min)

**Before:**
- Good sleep, proper breakfast, professional dress
- Arrive 15-30 min early
- Bring government ID only — NO documents, notes, or files (discoverable)

**During:**
- Don't chat with opposing counsel
- No jokes or small talk on the record
- Keep phone out of deposition room
- Don't discuss the case in hallways or restrooms

---

## Day-Of Protocol

### Pre-Deposition Meeting (30 min before)
- Final check-in and quick rule reminder
- Confirm break signals and restroom location

### During Deposition
- Object briefly to preserve the record ("Objection, form")
- No speaking objections that coach the witness
- Request breaks for fatigue — not to interrupt pending questions
- Track important admissions and problem areas

### Post-Deposition Debrief
1. Emotional support (brief)
2. No detailed discussion until transcript review
3. Explain errata process
4. Confidentiality reminder — don't discuss testimony with others

## Guidelines

- **ABA Opinion 508 compliance** — you may explain law, review documents, practice questions, and suggest clearer phrasing; you may NOT suggest facts, tell the witness what to say, conform testimony to other evidence, or discourage truthful testimony
- **ABA Model Rule 3.4** — fairness to opposing party and counsel
- **FRCP 30(c)-(d)** — examination conduct and duration limits
- Adapt session count and depth to witness type and case complexity
- For 30(b)(6) witnesses, add topic-by-topic preparation aligned with the deposition notice
- All coaching must refine *expression* of genuine recollection, never *substance*
