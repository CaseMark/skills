# M&A Closing Checklist

Generates a party-coded, status-tracked closing checklist serving as the central coordination and risk management instrument for a U.S. M&A transaction.

## Prerequisites

1. **Definitive agreement** — purchase agreement, merger agreement, or governing transaction document with all executed disclosure schedules
2. **Transaction structure confirmed** — stock purchase, asset purchase, merger, or other
3. **Party identification** — full legal names of buyer, seller, target (if distinct), escrow agent, and key counsel
4. **Closing logistics** — anticipated date, time (with time zone), location or virtual platform
5. **Material contracts inventory** — to identify change-of-control/consent triggers
6. **Regulatory profile** — jurisdictions of organization, HSR thresholds, industry-specific regulators

## Guidelines

- **Version control**: Label every distribution with version number and date; all parties must confirm they are working from the current version
- **Lead times**: Flag items requiring extended third-party response (landlord consents, regulatory approvals) well in advance; these cannot be left to closing week
- **Sequencing dependencies**: Funds flow memorandum cannot be finalized until all payoff letters received; closing certificates cannot be signed until pre-closing covenants performed
- **Wire fraud prevention**: Establish verbal confirmation protocol for all wire instructions; never accept last-minute wire instruction changes by email alone
- **Legal opinions**: Confirm opinion scope, qualifications, and reliance parties at the outset — opinion negotiation is a frequent source of closing delays
- **Good standing timing**: Certificates typically must be dated ≤30 days pre-closing; confirm standard with buyer's counsel and lender (if any)
- **FIRPTA**: For foreign sellers, buyer must withhold under IRC §1445 [VERIFY current withholding rate and thresholds]; Seller may apply to IRS for withholding certificate to reduce or eliminate withholding
- **Bulk sales**: Determine applicability under UCC Article 6 or state-specific bulk sale statutes for asset deals in relevant jurisdictions [VERIFY states where business operates]
- **Cross-reference**: Tie each checklist item to the specific purchase agreement section creating the obligation to facilitate quick resolution of disputes during closing
- **Jurisdiction-specific**: Adapt for state transfer taxes, recording fees, merger statute requirements, and any foreign jurisdiction filings if assets or parties are located outside the U.S.
