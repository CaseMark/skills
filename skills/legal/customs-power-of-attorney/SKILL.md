# Customs Power of Attorney

Drafts a CBP-ready Power of Attorney authorizing a licensed customs broker to act as agent for an importer or exporter in all U.S. customs matters under 19 CFR Part 141.

## Prerequisites

1. **Principal identity** — full legal name (as in formation docs), entity type, jurisdiction of formation, EIN or SSN, complete business address
2. **Broker identity** — legal business name, business address, CBP-issued customs broker license number (must be current under 19 CFR Part 111)
3. **Scope determination** — limited (specific transactions/shipments/period) or unlimited continuing authority
4. **Delegation policy** — whether sub-agent delegation is permitted and under what conditions
5. **Special authorities** — confirm whether protests, prior disclosures, binding ruling requests, drawback claims, and C-TPAT/ISA participation are included or excluded

## Output Structure

### 1. Title & Recitals
- Heading: **CUSTOMS POWER OF ATTORNEY**
- Effective date; whether continuing or limited (with expiration date/conditions)

### 2. Party Identification

| Field | Grantor (Principal) | Grantee (Broker) |
|---|---|---|
| Legal name | Exact corporate/LLC/individual name | Licensed broker entity name |
| Address | Full street, city, state, ZIP | Principal business address |
| ID number | EIN or SSN | CBP License No. (19 CFR Part 111) |
| Entity type | Corp / LLC / Partnership / Sole prop | — |

### 3. Grant of Authority

Appoint Grantee as "true and lawful agent and attorney-in-fact" to transact customs business with CBP on Grantor's behalf. Enumerate authority by category:

**Entry & Filing**
- Make, sign, declare, and file all CBP entry types: consumption, warehouse, TIB, FTZ admission, ABI electronic entries
- Execute declarations and certifications under penalty of perjury, including USMCA/certificates of origin and other preferential tariff program documentation

**Financial**
- Pay duties, taxes, fees, and CBP-assessed charges on Grantor's account
- Utilize Grantor's customs bond; authorize ACH debit/wire as applicable
- Specify whether broker may post additional bonds or security without prior approval

**CBP Communications**
- Receive and respond to all CBP correspondence: CF-28 (Request for Information), CF-29 (Notice of Action), exam notices, holds
- Represent principal in discussions with CBP officers on classification, valuation, admissibility, and compliance matters
- Handle FDA, USDA, and other partner government agency holds

**Extended Authorities** *(include or exclude explicitly)*

| Authority | Include / Exclude |
|---|---|
| Protests under 19 USC 1514 | ☐ Yes ☐ No — require separate written auth |
| Prior disclosure under 19 USC 1592 | ☐ Yes ☐ No — require separate written auth |
| Binding ruling requests | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| Duty drawback claims | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| C-TPAT / ISA program participation | ☐ Yes ☐ No |

### 4. Limitations & Conditions

- If limited POA: define scope precisely (entry numbers, HTS chapters, countries of origin, date range)
- Delegation to sub-agents: permitted/prohibited; if permitted, state broker remains fully responsible for sub-agent acts
- Principal's duty to provide timely, accurate commercial invoices, packing lists, BOLs, COOs, and agency-specific certifications
- Broker entitled to rely in good faith on principal-supplied information; principal bears accuracy liability
- Mandatory consultation triggers (e.g., before agreeing to intensive exam, before classification decision above $X duty exposure)

### 5. Revocation (19 CFR 141.46)

- Revocation requires **written notice** to both broker and CBP (filed at each port where POA is on record)
- Effective upon broker's **actual receipt** of written notice
- All actions taken before receipt of notice remain valid and binding
- Pending entries: specify whether broker's authority continues for completion, CF-28/29 responses, and protests on pre-revocation entries
- Principal remains liable for duties and broker fees incurred prior to revocation

### 6. Indemnification & Confidentiality

- Principal indemnifies broker for duties, penalties, and liquidated damages arising from principal's inaccurate information or regulatory non-compliance
- Broker maintains confidentiality of principal's pricing, trade secrets, and business information; disclosure only as required by law or CBP regulation

### 7. Recordkeeping

Both parties retain copies per 19 CFR Part 163 (5 years from entry date or final claim determination, whichever is later). Broker must produce copy to CBP upon request.

### 8. Execution Block

**Certification** (above signature): Signatory certifies under penalty of perjury that they hold actual authority to bind the principal and that all information is true and correct.

| Entity Type | Required Signatory |
|---|---|
| Corporation | President, VP, Secretary, or Treasurer; consider corporate resolution if authority may be questioned |
| LLC | Managing member or authorized manager per operating agreement |
| Partnership | General partner with binding authority |
| Sole proprietorship | Proprietor personally |

Signature block fields: Signature / Printed Name / Title / Date

> Note: CBP does not require notarization or corporate seal for standard customs POAs, but both may be included if broker requests or multi-jurisdiction use is anticipated.

## Guidelines

- **Mandatory elements per 19 CFR Part 141, Subpart B**: written form, identified parties, specified scope, execution by person with legal authority to bind principal
- Explicitly state whether authority is **continuing** or **limited** — ambiguity defaults against the principal in CBP disputes
- Explicitly **exclude** any authority not intended to be granted (protests, prior disclosures) rather than relying on silence
- Use the principal's exact legal name as registered with CBP/IRS — mismatches can cause entry rejection
- If broker holds multiple licenses (individual + company), specify which license governs
- Revocation notice to CBP must be filed at **each port** where the POA is on record — a single filing is insufficient if entries are made at multiple ports
- `[VERIFY]` current CBP form requirements and any port-specific filing procedures before finalizing
