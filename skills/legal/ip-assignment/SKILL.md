# Assignment of Intellectual Property

Drafts an agreement effectuating complete transfer of all right, title, and interest in specified intellectual property assets from assignor to assignee.

## Prerequisites

1. **IP inventory** — type (patent/trademark/copyright/trade secret), registration numbers, filing dates, jurisdictions, pending applications
2. **Chain of title** — prior assignments, employment/contractor agreements, co-ownership status
3. **Existing encumbrances** — active licenses (exclusive/non-exclusive), liens, security interests, surviving obligations
4. **Party details** — legal names, entity types, jurisdictions of organization, signatory authority (board resolutions/operating agreements)
5. **Deal terms** — consideration amount/structure, payment timing, license-back arrangements, retained rights (if any)
6. **Recording requirements** — target registries (USPTO, USCO, state offices, foreign offices), notarization needs

## Output Structure

### 1. Parties

| Element | Assignor | Assignee |
|---|---|---|
| Legal name & entity type | Required | Required |
| Jurisdiction of organization | Required | Required |
| Principal address | Required | Required |
| Signatory + title | Required | Required |
| Authority confirmation | Board resolution / operating agreement | Board resolution / operating agreement |

Flag: multiple assignors, joint ownership (requires all co-owner consent), or partial assignments.

### 2. Recitals

- Describe IP with registration-level specificity (reg. numbers, filing dates, titles)
- Reference exhibits/schedules containing full IP listings
- State business context (asset purchase, settlement, employment separation)
- Note chain-of-title history and relationship to parent transaction
- Do NOT embed operative warranties in recitals

### 3. Assignment Clause

Core conveyancing language must include:

```
Assignor hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to Assignee
all right, title, and interest in and to the Assigned IP, including:
```

**Rights checklist:**
- [ ] Use, reproduce, modify, distribute, display, perform, sublicense, enforce
- [ ] Associated goodwill (required for trademark validity)
- [ ] Right to sue for past, present, and future infringement
- [ ] All renewals, extensions, continuations, divisionals
- [ ] Ancillary materials (source code, design files, prototypes, documentation, domain names)

**Scope parameters:**
- Geographic: worldwide or territory-limited
- Temporal: full remaining term including renewals
- Retained rights: none, or specify license-back terms

**Registration-specific language:**

| IP Type | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Patents | USPTO recordation language; inventorship confirmation |
| Copyrights | Writing requirement per 17 U.S.C. § 204(a) [VERIFY]; specificity of works |
| Trademarks | Goodwill transfer (assignment without goodwill = invalid); USPTO recording for constructive notice |
| Trade secrets | Confidentiality maintenance obligations; employee/contractor NDA inventory |

### 4. Consideration

- State exact amount, payment method, timing
- Structured payments: schedule with dates, amounts, acceleration triggers, default remedies
- Non-monetary consideration (equity, services, liability assumption): describe with same precision
- Tax allocation among IP assets if bundled transfer
- Payment security: assignor's remedies for non-payment, whether assignment is voidable, retained security interests
- Specify which party bears recording fees

### 5. Representations & Warranties

**Assignor reps:**
- [ ] Sole/exclusive owner; full authority to transfer; free of liens/encumbrances
- [ ] No infringement of third-party rights; no claims asserted or threatened
- [ ] Registered IP is subsisting, valid, enforceable; maintenance fees current
- [ ] All existing licenses disclosed; no inconsistent grants
- [ ] Trade secret confidentiality maintained; appropriate NDAs in place
- [ ] Compliance with applicable laws (export controls, privacy, industry-specific)
- [ ] Patents: accurate inventorship, duty of candor compliance
- [ ] Copyrights: originality; contributor/co-author rights obtained

**Negotiate:** knowledge qualifiers, materiality thresholds, disclosure schedules, survival periods, liability caps.

### 6. Indemnification

| Obligation | Covers |
|---|---|
| Assignor → Assignee | Third-party infringement claims, breach of reps/warranties, pre-closing IP violations |
| Assignee → Assignor | Post-assignment use, modifications, combinations with other products |

Include: notice procedures, defense control rights, settlement approval, cost advancement vs. reimbursement, survival period, liability caps.

### 7. Post-Closing Obligations

**Assignor must:**
- [ ] Execute additional documents for registry recordings
- [ ] Deliver all tangible IP embodiments (originals, files, records) per specified timeline and format
- [ ] Provide testimony/declarations for prosecution, enforcement, or validity defense
- [ ] Cease all use; remove ownership indicia from materials, websites, products
- [ ] Transition relationships with IP counsel/service providers
- [ ] Execute powers of attorney for foreign recordings
- [ ] Cooperate until all recordings complete

### 8. Dispute Resolution & Governing Law

- Contract interpretation: chosen state law
- Note: patent law is exclusively federal; copyright is federal; trademark is federal + state
- Jurisdiction/venue clause (consider exclusive vs. concurrent)
- Equitable relief carve-out (monetary damages inadequate for IP breaches)
- Attorney's fees: prevailing party or each-bears-own
- If arbitration: evaluate suitability (IP validity may require judicial determination; international enforcement concerns)

### 9. General Provisions

Include: entire agreement, amendment (written only), assignment/succession, severability, notice procedures, counterparts.

### 10. Execution

- Signature blocks matching entity types (authorized rep, printed name, title, date)
- Notary acknowledgment if required for recording
- Multiple originals if recording in multiple jurisdictions
- Verify formatting compliance with target registry requirements

## Guidelines

- Trademark assignments **must** include goodwill transfer or assignment is void
- Copyright assignments **must** be in writing per 17 U.S.C. § 204(a) [VERIFY]
- Always confirm assignor's chain of title — employment agreements, prior assignments, and co-ownership can defeat transfer
- For patent assignments, include USPTO-compliant recordation language and confirm compliance with duty of candor
- If part of a larger asset purchase, ensure IP assignment is independently enforceable and cross-referenced
- Draft exhibits/schedules for IP listings rather than embedding lengthy descriptions in operative text
- Flag any jurisdiction-specific recording formalities (foreign filings may require legalization/apostille)
- Do not assume IP is unencumbered — always require disclosure of existing licenses and liens
