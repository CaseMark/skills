# Art Law Summaries

Produces structured art law research summaries with case citations, doctrinal analysis, and stakeholder-specific guidance across the major categories of art market legal disputes.

## Prerequisites

1. **Topic or scope** — specific area (e.g., VARA, Holocaust restitution, NAGPRA) or "comprehensive overview"
2. **Jurisdiction focus** — U.S. federal (default), specific circuit/state, or international framework
3. **Audience** — artist, gallery/dealer, collector, or legal professional (shapes depth and framing)
4. **Specific case or question** — optional; narrows output to targeted analysis

### Stakeholder Guidance Sections

**Artists**
- Copyright registration timing and deposit requirements
- VARA-covered works (visual art, limited editions ≤200); waiver must be written and signed
- Destruction/modification claims: *Carter v. Helmsley-Spear* and circuit treatment [VERIFY]
- Gallery representation contracts: exclusivity scope, consignment terms, resale royalty clauses

**Galleries & Dealers**
- Provenance due diligence checklist: Art Loss Register, INTERPOL Works of Art database, Getty Provenance Index, published restitution databases
- Consignment agreement essentials: title retention, insurance obligations, authority-to-sell limits, accounting frequency
- Disclosure obligations to buyers; warranty of title under UCC § 2-312 [VERIFY]
- AML compliance: when dealer reporting thresholds apply

**Collectors**
- Acquisition documentation: bill of sale, full provenance records, export/import certificates
- Authentication and warranty: recourse for forgery; contractual vs. statutory remedies
- Estate planning: qualified appraisal requirements (IRC § 170), charitable contribution rules, fractional interest issues

## Guidelines

- Use Bluebook citation format for all case references; mark unverified citations `[VERIFY]`
- Note circuit splits explicitly (e.g., VARA "recognized stature" standard varies by circuit [VERIFY])
- HEAR Act: flag statute-of-limitations revival provisions for Holocaust-era claims filed after 2016
- NAGPRA applies to federally funded institutions — note private museum carve-outs
- Distinguish federal VARA protections from broader state moral rights statutes (e.g., California Art Preservation Act, New York Artists' Authorship Rights Act)
- For international repatriation claims, distinguish UNESCO 1970 (public law framework) from UNIDROIT 1995 (private law claims)
- NFT and AI-art authority is rapidly evolving — always flag publication date of any cited source
