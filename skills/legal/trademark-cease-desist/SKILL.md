# Trademark Cease and Desist Letter

Formal pre-suit notice demanding cessation of alleged trademark infringement and preservation of evidence.

## Prerequisites

1. **Owner identity and authority** — client name, entity type, signatory, counsel contact info
2. **Mark details** — exact mark, registration number(s) if any, class(es), registration date, first use date
3. **Scope of use** — goods/services covered, channels of trade, geography
4. **Infringement evidence** — dated screenshots, URLs, product images, ads, packaging, listings
5. **Confusion evidence** — actual confusion, overlap in customers, similarity of goods/services
6. **Requested remedies** — stop use, remove content, destroy inventory, transfer domains/handles, accounting
7. **Deadline and service method** — response date, delivery method, proof of receipt plan

## Output Structure / Process

### Inputs Checklist

| Item | Required | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Owner legal name and address | Yes | Include counsel if represented |
| Mark type | Yes | Word, design, or composite |
| Registration status | No | If registered, provide USPTO details |
| First use date | No | Required for common-law rights |
| Goods/services description | Yes | Match to registered or actual use |
| Infringer legal name and address | Yes | Include DBA and online identifiers |
| Infringing uses | Yes | URLs, listings, products, social handles |
| Evidence list | Yes | Attach as exhibits |
| Demands and deadline | Yes | State exact response date |

### Likelihood of Confusion Factors (address only applicable)

| Factor | Evidence to include |
|---|---|
| Similarity of marks | Appearance, sound, meaning, commercial impression |
| Relatedness of goods/services | Overlap or complementarity |
| Strength of mark | Distinctiveness, duration, marketing spend |
| Channels of trade | Same platforms, retailers, or customer base |
| Actual confusion | Customer inquiries, misdirected emails |
| Intent | Copying, bad-faith adoption, knowledge of mark |
| Consumer sophistication | Purchase care level |

### Demands Matrix

| Demand | When to include | Proof requested |
|---|---|---|
| Immediate cessation | Always | Written confirmation |
| Removal from websites/marketplaces | If online use | Screenshots showing takedown |
| Destroy inventory/packaging | If physical goods | Certification of destruction |
| Transfer domains/handles | If used | Transfer confirmation |
| Notify distributors/retailers | If third parties involved | Copy of notice |
| Accounting of sales/profits | If damages likely | Sales report summary |

### Letter Template

```text
[Sender Letterhead]
[Date]

[Recipient Name]
[Recipient Entity / DBA]
[Address]

Re: Cease and Desist — Trademark Infringement of [MARK]

[1. Representation and Purpose]
We represent [OWNER]. This letter provides formal notice and demands immediate cessation of unauthorized use of [MARK].

[2. Rights in the Mark]
[Registered or common-law rights summary]
- If registered: USPTO Reg. No. [#], Reg. Date [date], Classes [#], Goods/Services [description], Lanham Act (15 U.S.C. § 1051 et seq.).
- If unregistered: first use [date], continuous use in [geography], scope of goods/services, evidence of distinctiveness.

[3. Infringing Use]
You are using [INFRINGING MARK] on/with [products/services/platforms], including:
- [URL / listing / social handle / product image]
- [Date observed]
Your use is likely to cause confusion as to source, sponsorship, or affiliation.

[4. Likelihood of Confusion Summary]
[Concise factor-based analysis tailored to evidence]

[5. Demands]
You must:
1. Cease all use of [INFRINGING MARK] and any confusingly similar mark.
2. Remove infringing content from [websites/platforms].
3. [Destroy/recall] infringing materials and certify completion.
4. Transfer [domain/handle] to [OWNER].
5. Provide an accounting of sales and profits from [date range].

[6. Deadline]
Provide written confirmation and evidence of compliance no later than [DATE].

[7. Remedies Notice]
If you do not comply, [OWNER] will pursue all available remedies under federal and state law, including injunctive relief, damages, and disgorgement of profits (15 U.S.C. § 1117(a)).

[8. Reservation of Rights]
All rights are reserved. No license or waiver is granted by this letter.

[9. Response Instructions]
Send your response to: [contact info]. Include evidence of compliance and the requested accounting.

Sincerely,
[Name]
[Title]
[Law Firm / Company]
[Contact Info]

Enclosures: [Exhibit list]
```

## Guidelines

- Use only verified facts; attach exhibits to support each allegation.
- Keep tone firm and professional; avoid threats unrelated to civil remedies.
- If proposing settlement, consider FRE 408 framing and keep offers separate.
- Tailor to jurisdictional law and state unfair competition statutes if applicable.
- Address possible defenses only when evidence supports rebuttal.
- Do not claim willfulness unless supported by facts (e.g., prior notice).
- Use exact dates and deadlines; avoid “within X days” without a calendar date.
