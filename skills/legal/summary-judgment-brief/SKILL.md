# Summary Judgment Motion Brief

Drafts a dispositive motion brief demonstrating entitlement to judgment as a matter of law under FRCP 56 or state equivalent.

## Prerequisites

1. **Completed discovery record** — depositions, interrogatory answers, RFAs, document productions
2. **Claims/defenses at issue** — which counts or affirmative defenses the motion targets
3. **Controlling authority** — binding precedent on substantive elements and SJ standard in jurisdiction
4. **Local rules** — formatting, page limits, separate statement requirements, combined vs. separate motion/brief
5. **Party role** — plaintiff (proving all elements undisputed) or defendant (negating element or establishing affirmative defense)

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Court | Full name with division/department |
| Case title | All parties as in original complaint |
| Case number | Civil action / docket number |
| Document title | "Motion for Summary Judgment" or per local practice |
| Formatting | Per local rule (font, margins, line spacing) |

Check whether jurisdiction requires separate notice of motion or combined motion/brief.

### 2. Introduction (2–3 paragraphs)

- Identify moving party and claims/defenses at issue
- Preview key undisputed facts material to disposition
- State why judgment as a matter of law follows
- Specify precise relief requested
- Frame: no reasonable jury could find for non-movant on this record

### 3. Statement of Undisputed Material Facts

```
1. [Fact statement — objective, no argument or legal conclusions].
   (Ex. A, Smith Dep. 45:12–46:3; Ex. B, Invoice dated 3/15/2024.)

2. [Next fact...]
   (Jones Decl. ¶ 7; RFA No. 12, admitted.)
```

**Rules:**
- [ ] Each fact in a separately numbered paragraph
- [ ] Every fact cited to admissible evidence (deposition page:line, declaration ¶, exhibit, RFA)
- [ ] Only material facts — no background filler
- [ ] Objective language — no argument or conclusions
- [ ] Organized chronologically or by topic/element
- [ ] Unsupported facts by opponent deemed admitted under most rules

### 4. Legal Standard

| Component | Content |
|-----------|---------|
| Rule | FRCP 56(a) or state equivalent with citation |
| Standard | No genuine dispute of material fact; movant entitled to judgment as a matter of law |
| Movant's burden | Demonstrate absence of genuine dispute (cite *Celotex Corp. v. Catrett*, 477 U.S. 317 (1986)) |
| Burden shift | Non-movant must set forth specific facts showing genuine issue (*Anderson v. Liberty Lobby*, 477 U.S. 242 (1986)) |
| Viewing standard | Evidence viewed in light most favorable to non-movant; all reasonable inferences drawn in their favor (*Matsushita Elec. v. Zenith Radio*, 475 U.S. 574 (1986)) |
| Non-movant's obligation | Cannot rest on mere allegations or denials; must produce actual evidence |

Add substantive legal standards for specific claims/defenses at issue.

### 5. Argument

Structure with headings per claim, defense, or element:

```
I. [CLAIM/DEFENSE] FAILS BECAUSE [ELEMENT] IS UNDISPUTED

   A. Legal Rule
      - Cite controlling statute/regulation
      - Cite binding case law on elements

   B. Application to Undisputed Facts
      - Map numbered facts to each element
      - If defendant: show plaintiff cannot establish element [X]
      - If plaintiff: show every element satisfied, no valid defense

   C. Preemptive Rebuttal
      - Anticipate opponent's arguments
      - Distinguish unfavorable cases (factually or legally)
```

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Each element addressed with rule → fact application → conclusion
- [ ] Binding precedent prioritized; persuasive authority supplemental
- [ ] Unfavorable cases distinguished, not ignored
- [ ] Tone: measured, confident — "reasonable minds could not differ"
- [ ] Cross-references to numbered undisputed facts

### 6. Conclusion (1–2 paragraphs)

Synthesize key points. No new arguments or facts. Restate why record and law compel relief.

### 7. Prayer for Relief

- [ ] Specify: all claims or only identified claims/counts
- [ ] Dismissal with prejudice where applicable
- [ ] Costs and attorney's fees if authorized by statute, contract, or rule
- [ ] "Such other and further relief as the Court deems just"

### 8. Signature Block

| Field | Required |
|-------|----------|
| Attorney name | Full name |
| Bar number | State bar ID |
| Firm | Name and full address |
| Contact | Phone + email |
| Designation | "Attorney for [Moving Party]" |
| Signature format | Per local rule (wet, electronic, /s/) |

### 9. Certificate of Service

- Method (ECF, email, mail, hand delivery)
- Date of service
- Names and addresses of all served parties/counsel
- Compliance with local timing and electronic service rules

## Guidelines

- **Every fact cited** — no unsupported factual assertions anywhere in the brief
- **Every legal proposition cited** — controlling authority for each rule statement
- **Citation format** — Bluebook unless local rules specify otherwise; verify court preference
- **Internal consistency** — facts section, argument, and prayer must align; no orphan facts
- **Strategic framing** — if defendant, focus on weakest plaintiff element; if plaintiff, lead with strongest undisputed evidence
- **Partial SJ** — if moving on fewer than all claims, clearly delineate scope throughout
- **Page limits** — confirm and comply with local rule page/word limits
- **Exhibits** — reference by exhibit letter/number consistently; confirm all cited evidence is in the record
