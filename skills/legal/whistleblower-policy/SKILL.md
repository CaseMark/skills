# Whistleblower Protection Policy

Drafts a board-adoptable whistleblower protection policy balancing reporting encouragement, retaliation prohibition, confidentiality, and investigation rigor.

## Prerequisites

1. **Organization details** — legal name, entity type (public company, private, non-profit), state of incorporation
2. **Governance structure** — titles of compliance officer, board committee assignments (Audit/Governance), reporting hierarchy
3. **Existing policies** — current code of conduct, ethics policy, grievance procedures, any prior whistleblower policy to supersede
4. **Regulatory profile** — whether SOX Section 806 applies (public company), Dodd-Frank bounty eligibility, state-specific statutes
5. **Reporting infrastructure** — hotline vendor, online portal URL, designated mailbox/email, or channels to be established

## Output Structure

Draft a complete policy with the following sections. Use `[Organization Name]`, `[Title]`, `[Date]`, and similar bracketed placeholders throughout.

### Section Map

| # | Section | Key Content |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Purpose & Scope | Org commitment; covered persons (directors, officers, employees, volunteers, contractors); relationship to broader ethics framework |
| 2 | Covered Concerns | What triggers this policy vs. routine HR grievances |
| 3 | Reporting Procedures | Multi-channel hierarchy with anonymous option |
| 4 | Investigation Process | Receipt → assessment → investigation → resolution → communication |
| 5 | Anti-Retaliation | Prohibition, definitions, consequences, remedies |
| 6 | Confidentiality | Protections and limitations |
| 7 | Good Faith & False Reports | Standard of reasonable belief; consequences for bad-faith abuse |
| 8 | Administration & Governance | Oversight, recordkeeping, training, annual review |
| 9 | Legal Compliance & External Rights | Federal/state law interaction; right to report externally |
| 10 | Adoption & Effective Date | Board resolution, signature blocks, supersession clause |

### Section 2: Covered Concerns Checklist

**In scope:**
- [ ] Violations of federal, state, or local law
- [ ] Financial fraud, accounting irregularities, asset misappropriation
- [ ] Conflicts of interest compromising organizational integrity
- [ ] Threats to public health, safety, or environment
- [ ] Gross mismanagement or waste of resources
- [ ] Violations of code of conduct or ethics policies

**Out of scope** (route to HR/standard grievance):
- Compensation disputes, performance reviews, interpersonal conflicts, general workplace complaints

### Section 3: Reporting Channels Template

```
REPORTING HIERARCHY

1. Immediate supervisor (unless implicated)
2. [Compliance Officer / Executive Director]
   Address: [____________]
   Email:   [____________]
   Phone:   [____________]
3. [Board Chair / Audit Committee Chair] (for concerns involving senior mgmt)
   Address: [____________]
   Email:   [____________]
4. Anonymous hotline/portal: [____________]
```

- Accept reports in writing, verbal, phone, or electronic form
- Reports should include: description of concern, individuals involved, timeframe, supporting evidence, contact info (if not anonymous)
- Anonymous reports accepted; note limitations on follow-up and investigation thoroughness

### Section 4: Investigation Process

| Phase | Timeframe | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Acknowledgment | 5-10 business days | Confirm receipt to reporter |
| Preliminary assessment | 10 business days | Determine severity; assign investigator(s) |
| Investigation | Varies by complexity | Document review, witness interviews, evidence collection |
| Findings | Upon completion | Determine whether allegations substantiated |
| Corrective action | Prompt | Discipline, controls, referral to law enforcement as warranted |
| Reporter notification | Upon conclusion | Inform of outcome to extent permitted by confidentiality/law |

- Investigators may include internal personnel, board committee, outside counsel, or forensic specialists
- Information shared on need-to-know basis only

### Section 5: Anti-Retaliation Provisions

**Prohibited conduct** — include all of the following in the definition:
- Termination, demotion, suspension, adverse employment action
- Threats, harassment, intimidation, coercion
- Unfavorable evaluations or assignments as reprisal
- Reduction in compensation or benefits
- Any action that would dissuade a reasonable person from reporting

**Key drafting points:**
- Protection applies regardless of investigation outcome, provided good-faith report
- Retaliation = independent policy violation subject to discipline up to termination regardless of seniority
- Suspected retaliation reported through same channels; investigated with equal rigor
- Note external legal remedies under SOX § 806, Dodd-Frank § 922, state statutes

### Section 6: Confidentiality Framework

**Protections:**
- Reporter identity disclosed only on need-to-know basis
- All recipients of report information instructed to maintain confidentiality

**Mandatory disclosure exceptions:**
- Necessary for adequate investigation
- Required by law, regulation, or legal process
- Needed for corrective action that inherently reveals information
- Defense in legal proceedings
- Reporting to law enforcement or regulators

### Section 7: Good Faith Standard

| Element | Standard |
|---------|----------|
| Good faith | Honest belief + reasonable grounds, even if ultimately unsubstantiated |
| Not required | Proof, personal investigation, certainty of violation |
| Bad faith | Knowingly false allegations, reckless disregard for truth, reports made to harass |
| Consequence of bad faith | Discipline up to termination; potential civil liability for defamation |

Emphasize: unfounded ≠ bad faith. No adverse consequences for good-faith reports that prove unsubstantiated.

### Section 8: Governance & Administration

- **Day-to-day administration:** [Compliance Officer / Executive Director]
- **Board oversight:** [Audit Committee / Governance Committee]
- **Recordkeeping:** Secure, confidential records of all reports, investigations, outcomes
- **Board reporting:** Aggregate summary (number, nature, outcomes, corrective actions) quarterly/annually — no individual identification
- **Training:** All personnel at onboarding + annual refresher; policy in employee handbook, volunteer materials, intranet
- **Review cycle:** Annual board review; amendments require board approval

### Section 9: Legal Compliance & External Rights

Draft must include:
- Policy supplements but does not replace legal protections under SOX, Dodd-Frank, False Claims Act, OSHA § 11(c), and state statutes
- Internal reporting is optional, not a prerequisite to external reporting
- Right to report directly to SEC, DOJ, OSHA, state AG, or other regulators preserved
- No retaliation for cooperating with government investigations
- Disclaimer: policy is not legal advice; consult attorney for individual rights

### Section 10: Adoption Block Template

```
ADOPTED by the Board of Directors of [Organization Name]
on [Date].

Effective Date: [Date]

_________________________    _________________________
[Name]                       [Name]
Board Chair                  [Executive Director/CEO]

In case of conflict with other organizational policies,
this policy controls within its scope.
```

## Guidelines

- **Length target:** 2,500–4,000 words in final policy prose
- **Tone:** Professional, reassuring to reporters, unequivocal on anti-retaliation; avoid excessive legalese
- **Format:** Primarily narrative prose; limit bullet lists to 3–5 items for examples/categories
- **SOX applicability:** For public companies, ensure SOX § 806 protections and audit committee reporting are explicitly addressed
- **Non-profit considerations:** Address volunteer coverage, donor-related concerns, and IRS Form 990 disclosure requirements for policies
- **Dodd-Frank bounty programs:** For public companies, acknowledge SEC whistleblower bounty rights without discouraging internal reporting
- **State law variation:** Flag that state whistleblower statutes vary significantly; recommend jurisdiction-specific legal review [VERIFY specific state statute citations before inclusion]
- **Do not** draft language that could be read as requiring internal reporting before external reporting — this conflicts with federal protections
- **Do not** include overly broad confidentiality/NDA language that could chill protected disclosures
- **Placeholders:** Use `[brackets]` consistently for all org-specific information; policy should be adoptable with placeholder completion only
