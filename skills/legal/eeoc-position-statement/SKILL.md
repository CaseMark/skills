# EEOC Position Statement

Drafts the employer's formal response to an EEOC charge of discrimination, shaping agency investigation findings while building a defensible record for potential litigation.

## Prerequisites

1. **EEOC Charge** — complete charge number, allegations, protected bases, dates of alleged conduct
2. **Charging party's personnel file** — hire date, position history, evaluations, disciplinary records, attendance, manager communications
3. **Workplace policies** — performance management, anti-discrimination/harassment, disciplinary procedures, employee handbook
4. **Comparator evidence** — performance records, disciplinary actions, and outcomes for similarly situated employees outside the protected class
5. **Corroborating records** — emails, meeting notes, witness statements, contemporaneous documentation
6. **Business justification documents** — RIF criteria, promotion requirements, selection processes, operational needs

## Document Review Checklist

Before drafting, extract and confirm:

| Item | Source | Verify |
|---|---|---|
| Charge number & filing date | EEOC charge | Exact match required |
| Protected basis/bases | EEOC charge | Title VII / ADA / ADEA / other |
| Specific allegations | EEOC charge | Map each to response section |
| Statute of limitations compliance | Filing date vs. alleged conduct dates | Flag untimely claims |
| Charging party employment timeline | Personnel file | Hire → separation, all key dates |
| Documented performance issues | Evaluations, PIPs, warnings | Contemporaneous records only |
| Policy communication evidence | Handbook acknowledgments, training records | Signed receipts preferred |
| Comparator data | HR records | Same supervisor, similar conduct, different protected class |
| Complaint/reporting history | HR files, hotline logs | Did CP use available channels? |
| Decision-maker knowledge | Emails, testimony | Awareness of protected activity (for retaliation) |

## Output Structure

### 1. Header Block

```
RESPONDENT'S POSITION STATEMENT
EEOC Charge No. [number]

Respondent:  [Full legal name]
Address:     [Complete business address]
Charging Party: [Name as listed on charge]
Date:        [Submission date]
```

### 2. Introduction (1–2 paragraphs)

- Deny allegations or specify contested vs. acknowledged aspects
- Roadmap the response structure
- Frame: legitimate business practices, consistent policy application, lawful decisions
- Tone: professional, assertive, non-dismissive

### 3. Factual Background (chronological narrative)

Structure as timeline with specific dates, names, titles:

| Date | Event | Documentation |
|---|---|---|
| [Hire date] | Initial hire — position, department, supervisor | Offer letter, onboarding docs |
| [Date] | Performance milestones / issues | Evaluations, PIPs |
| [Date] | Relevant incidents | Emails, witness statements |
| [Date] | Employment decision at issue | Decision memo, policy basis |

Include for each significant event:
- Date, location, individuals involved
- What occurred and what actions were taken
- Documentary corroboration
- Discrepancies with CP's account (if any), with explanation of why records should be credited

### 4. Systematic Response to Each Allegation

For **each** allegation in the charge:

1. **Direct factual response** — what actually occurred
2. **Documentary contradiction** — evidence disproving false/misleading assertions
3. **Legitimate non-discriminatory reason** — business justification
4. **Absence of discriminatory intent** — no nexus to protected characteristic

#### Claim-Specific Frameworks

**Disparate Treatment:**
- Comparator analysis: similarly situated employees outside protected class who received same/comparable treatment
- If no true comparators: articulate material differences in conduct, performance, or circumstances

**Harassment:**
- Anti-harassment policy details: communication, training, complaint procedures
- CP complaint history: when reported, investigation, findings, corrective action
- If unreported: document robust available reporting mechanisms

**Retaliation:**
- Timeline showing: (a) no protected activity, (b) employer unaware of protected activity, or (c) decision independent of protected activity
- Evidence of: decision-maker unawareness, pre-existing plans, temporal gaps, intervening legitimate reasons

### 5. Legal Analysis & Affirmative Defenses

Identify applicable statute(s): Title VII, ADA, ADEA, other.

| Defense Type | Application |
|---|---|
| Failure of prima facie case | Identify missing element(s) under McDonnell Douglas or applicable framework |
| Legitimate non-discriminatory reason | Articulate business necessity, consistent policy application |
| No severe/pervasive conduct | For harassment — below hostile work environment threshold |
| Prompt corrective action | Employer remediated upon notice |
| After-acquired evidence | Misconduct discovered post-decision (resume fraud, policy violations) |
| Statute of limitations | Untimely filing or stale allegations outside charging period |
| BFOQ | If applicable to position |
| Procedural defects | Failure to exhaust remedies, jurisdictional issues, wrong respondent |

Cite applicable EEOC regulations, compliance manual provisions, and controlling circuit/Supreme Court authority. [VERIFY] all citations.

### 6. Conclusion

- Reiterate charge lacks merit; request no-cause determination and dismissal
- State cooperation commitment and willingness to provide additional information
- Designate point of contact with full contact information
- Express openness to EEOC mediation (frame as good faith, not admission)

### 7. Exhibits

Compile numbered exhibit list:

```
Exhibit 1:  [Description — e.g., Anti-Discrimination Policy, rev. date]
Exhibit 2:  [Description — e.g., CP Performance Evaluations 2023–2025]
Exhibit 3:  [Description — e.g., Progressive Discipline Documentation]
...
```

Requirements:
- Label with exhibit number and descriptive title
- Redact irrelevant third-party personal information
- Order matches narrative reference sequence
- Include certification statement for authorized company representative

## Guidelines

- **Tone**: Professional, fact-driven. No personal attacks on CP, no dismissive language, no speculation about CP's motives.
- **Evidence standard**: Every factual assertion must be supported by documentary evidence or verifiable witness testimony. Do not make unsupported claims.
- **Gaps**: Acknowledge evidentiary gaps honestly rather than overreaching — credibility is paramount.
- **Privilege protection**: Exclude attorney-client privileged communications, work product, and litigation strategy. This document is discoverable if CP files suit.
- **Length**: 10–25 pages depending on complexity, plus exhibits.
- **Formatting**: Clear headings, consistent formatting, topic sentences, logical transitions.
- **Comparator data**: Use specific names, dates, and outcomes — not generalizations.
- **Filing deadlines**: Confirm response is within EEOC's deadline (typically 30 days from notice; extensions available).
- **Confidential information**: Follow EEOC procedures for designating confidential material that should not be shared with CP.
