# Rules and Regulations Exhibit

Drafts a comprehensive, enforceable Rules and Regulations Exhibit as an attachment to a primary transactional agreement (typically a commercial lease).

## Prerequisites

1. **Primary agreement** — executed or draft lease/contract to which this exhibit attaches (parties, property description, defined terms, notice clauses, use restrictions)
2. **Existing governing documents** — CC&Rs, condominium declarations, master association docs, prior lease exhibits, operational manuals
3. **Jurisdiction** — state/municipality for compliance with landlord-tenant law, fair housing, ADA, building codes, environmental regs
4. **Property specifics** — type (office, retail, industrial, mixed-use), common areas, parking, amenities, hours of operation

## Process

### Phase 1: Context Analysis

| Check | Action |
|-------|--------|
| Primary agreement review | Extract party names, property description, defined terms, use restrictions, cross-references to rules exhibit |
| Existing rules audit | Identify current rules/regulations to avoid conflicts; note gaps |
| Jurisdiction scan | Confirm applicable landlord-tenant statutes, fair housing, ADA, fire/building codes, environmental regs |
| Defined terms | Catalog terms from primary agreement; maintain consistent capitalization and meaning |

### Phase 2: Draft Structure

Use this section order. Adapt categories to the specific property/transaction.

```
RULES AND REGULATIONS EXHIBIT

Exhibit [__] to [Agreement Title]
Dated [__], by and between [Landlord] and [Tenant]

1. PREAMBLE
2. GENERAL RULES
3. SPECIFIC REGULATIONS
4. ENFORCEMENT
5. AMENDMENT PROCEDURES
6. ACKNOWLEDGMENT
```

### Phase 3: Section Drafting

#### 1. Preamble

- Reference primary agreement by exact title, date, and party names
- State incorporation: "This Exhibit is incorporated into and made part of the Agreement"
- Specify scope (property use, operations, safety, financial obligations)
- Establish hierarchy: primary agreement controls in event of conflict unless exhibit expressly states otherwise
- State binding effect on parties, successors, assigns, tenants, guests, invitees
- Preview amendment authority (unilateral by landlord with notice, mutual consent, or board approval)

#### 2. General Rules

Draft provisions for each applicable category:

| Category | Key Provisions |
|----------|---------------|
| **Permitted/prohibited uses** | Align with lease use clause + zoning; affirmative permissions then prohibited activities |
| **Access & hours** | Authorized persons, common area access, after-hours procedures, emergency/inspection access with statutory notice requirements |
| **Maintenance & care** | Responsibility matrix (structural vs. cosmetic vs. routine); condition standards; repair request procedures; timelines |
| **Conduct & noise** | Quiet hours; acceptable noise levels; guest/visitor policies (registration, duration, supervision) |
| **Pet policy** (if applicable) | Size/breed restrictions (check local prohibitions on breed bans); vaccination; leash; waste; noise |
| **Parking & vehicles** | Assigned/unassigned spaces; permits; vehicle type restrictions; guest parking; towing procedures |

#### 3. Specific Regulations

Organize by category. Include only categories relevant to the transaction:

| Category | Key Provisions |
|----------|---------------|
| **Safety & security** | Smoke detectors, fire extinguishers, egress, security systems, access codes, emergency contacts, evacuation procedures; meet/exceed building codes |
| **Compliance & reporting** | Record-keeping requirements; incident reporting (damage, legal proceedings, insurance claims); occupancy change notices; reporting format and deadlines |
| **Operations** | Business hours, signage, common area protocols, customer conduct, exterior modifications, landscaping, storage, holiday decorations |
| **Financial obligations** | Assessment procedures for shared costs; payment methods and due dates; late fees (comply with usury limits); special assessment notice and approval thresholds |
| **Environmental** | Waste management, recycling, hazardous materials handling, energy efficiency, water conservation |

#### 4. Enforcement Framework

Draft graduated enforcement:

```
Step 1: Written notice — describe violation, cite rule, state required corrective action
Step 2: Cure period — [X] days for curable violations (vary by severity)
Step 3: Written warning — for minor/first-time violations
Step 4: Monetary fines — specify amounts or calculation; escalation for repeat violations; cap to avoid unconscionability
Step 5: Privilege suspension — common area access, parking, amenities
Step 6: Self-help remedy — enforcing party corrects at violator's expense (emergency exception: no prior notice required)
Step 7: Legal action — injunctive relief, specific performance, damages
```

Include:
- Inspection rights with statutory notice (except emergencies)
- Right to respond, present evidence, and appeal
- Appeal decision-maker and standard of review
- Prevailing party attorney fee provision
- Statement that emergency action preserves right to contest charges afterward

#### 5. Amendment Procedures

- Amendment authority (who proposes, who approves)
- Approval threshold (unanimous, supermajority, simple majority, or unilateral with notice)
- Notice requirements: method, advance period, content
- Effective date with reasonable adjustment period
- Prospective application only (no retroactive amendments)
- Identify any provisions requiring unanimous consent to amend

#### 6. Acknowledgment & Execution

- Acknowledgment language: received, read, understood, agrees to comply, acknowledges penalties
- Representation re: opportunity to consult counsel
- Signature blocks: printed name, signature, date
- Consider notarization if recording is required or parties are unsophisticated

### Phase 4: Final Review Checklist

- [ ] Defined terms consistent with primary agreement
- [ ] Hierarchical numbering throughout; cross-references accurate
- [ ] Severability clause included
- [ ] Governing law matches primary agreement
- [ ] Non-waiver clause (failure to enforce ≠ waiver)
- [ ] Integration clause if superseding prior rules
- [ ] No provisions violating fair housing, ADA, statutory tenant protections, or public policy
- [ ] All rules rationally related to legitimate interests (safety, property protection, quiet enjoyment, operational efficiency)
- [ ] Enforcement penalties proportionate (not unconscionable)
- [ ] Tone matches audience (plain language for residential/consumer; technical for sophisticated commercial parties)

## Guidelines

- **Hierarchy**: Primary agreement always controls unless exhibit expressly overrides a specific provision
- **Fair housing compliance**: Never include rules that discriminate against protected classes; breed-specific pet bans may be prohibited in some jurisdictions
- **Statutory notice**: Inspection/access provisions must meet jurisdiction-specific landlord entry notice requirements (typically 24-48 hours)
- **Reasonableness**: Every rule must survive a reasonableness challenge — avoid arbitrary restrictions without legitimate justification
- **Fines**: Cap monetary penalties; excessive fines risk unenforceability as penalties rather than liquidated damages
- **ADA**: Accessibility requirements are non-negotiable; reasonable accommodation language should be included
- **Environmental**: Hazardous materials provisions must comply with federal (RCRA, CERCLA) and state environmental statutes [VERIFY]
- **Recording**: If the exhibit or acknowledgment will be recorded, ensure notarization and proper formatting for the county recorder
