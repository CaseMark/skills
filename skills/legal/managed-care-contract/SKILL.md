# Managed Care Contract

Drafts a managed care contract establishing the legal and operational relationship between a managed care organization (MCO) and a healthcare provider for delivery of services under managed care plans.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party information** — MCO: legal name, entity type, state of incorporation, insurance license number, accreditation status (NCQA/URAC/AAAHC). Provider: legal name, entity type, NPI, license numbers, DEA registration, board certifications, practice addresses
2. **Existing agreements** — prior contracts, fee schedules, credentialing materials, provider manual
3. **Payment terms** — fee-for-service rates (% of Medicare or proprietary schedule) or capitation PMPM rates with risk adjustment methodology
4. **Regulatory context** — applicable state managed care statutes, whether contract covers commercial, Medicare Advantage, and/or Medicaid managed care lines of business
5. **Network parameters** — provider participation category (PCP, specialist, hospital, ancillary), geographic service area, panel size limits

## Output Structure

### Article I: Parties & Recitals

| Element | MCO | Provider |
|---------|-----|----------|
| Legal name & DBA | Full name, DBA on member ID cards | Full name, group vs. individual |
| Entity type | State of incorporation | Professional corp, medical group, etc. |
| Identifiers | Tax ID, state insurance license # | Tax ID, NPI, state license #, DEA # |
| Accreditation | NCQA/URAC/AAAHC status | Board certifications, specialties |

- Effective date: specify execution date, calendar date, or regulatory approval date
- State whether new agreement or amendment/restatement of prior agreement (cite prior agreement)
- Recitals: MCO's authority under state insurance law, provider qualifications, mutual intent, regulatory framework

### Article II: Definitions

Define at minimum:

| Term | Key Elements |
|------|-------------|
| **Covered Services** | Enumerated service categories; reference to benefit plan documents; prior auth vs. non-auth services; conflict resolution hierarchy |
| **Capitation** | PMPM payment; scope (global vs. primary care only); risk-sharing model (full risk, shared savings/risk, stop-loss); panel calculation; mid-month enrollment adjustments |
| **Utilization Review** | Prospective, concurrent, retrospective review; evidence-based clinical criteria; qualified reviewer requirement; appeal rights |
| **Clean Claim** | All required data elements; correct form/format; valid codes; no inconsistencies; triggers prompt payment clock |
| **Member/Enrollee** | Subscriber + dependents; eligibility verification method |
| **Emergency Services** | Prudent layperson standard per federal law; no prior auth required |
| **Credentialing** | Initial verification + periodic recredentialing of licenses, certifications, training, standing |
| **Medical Director** | MCO physician overseeing UM, QA, clinical policy |
| **Provider Manual** | Incorporated by reference; may be updated with reasonable notice |
| **Experimental/Investigational** | Lacks safety/effectiveness evidence; not accepted practice; under clinical trial; excluded unless approved via technology assessment |

### Article III: Network Participation & Service Delivery

**Scope & Access Standards:**
- Provider participation category and specific services within scope
- Referral obligations for out-of-scope services to network providers

**Availability Requirements:**

| Appointment Type | Standard |
|-----------------|----------|
| Routine/preventive | Within 4 weeks |
| Urgent/symptomatic | 48–72 hours |
| Emergency | Immediate |
| After-hours | On-call coverage or answering service with triage |

- Geographic service area definition
- New patient acceptance / panel size limits
- Care coordination: communicate with other providers, facilitate referrals, coordinate transitions, participate in multidisciplinary teams

**Credentialing & Ongoing Obligations:**
- [ ] Active, unrestricted state license(s)
- [ ] Board certification (or eligibility with timeline to certify)
- [ ] DEA registration (if prescribing controlled substances)
- [ ] Professional liability insurance: $1M–$3M per occurrence / $3M–$5M aggregate (higher for hospitals/high-risk specialties); carrier rated A- or better (A.M. Best); tail coverage if claims-made
- [ ] Notify MCO within 10–30 days of: license restriction/loss, privilege restriction/loss, Medicare/Medicaid exclusion, felony conviction, licensing board sanctions, malpractice judgments above threshold, termination from other networks

**Clinical & Records Standards:**
- Comply with MCO medical policies and clinical practice guidelines (evidence-based, developed with practicing physicians) — guidelines inform but do not override independent medical judgment
- Medical records: accurate, complete, legible, timely; HIPAA Security Rule compliant; MCO access for UM/QA/audit; retain 6–10 years per state law (including post-termination)

### Article IV: Payment & Claims Administration

**Fee-for-Service:**
- Attach fee schedule as exhibit — specify basis (e.g., "120% of Medicare PFS" or proprietary)
- Annual update mechanism: Medicare rate changes, new CPT/HCPCS codes, renegotiated rates

**Capitation:**
- PMPM rates by age band, gender, geography, or HCC risk scores
- Panel assignment method: prospective (member selection) vs. retrospective (plurality of care)
- Mid-month enrollment/disenrollment: pro-rated or monthly reconciliation

**Claims Submission:**
- Forms: CMS-1500 / 837P (professional); UB-04 / 837I (institutional)
- Required data: demographics, member ID, dates of service, place of service, ICD codes, CPT/HCPCS, charges, rendering provider NPI/TIN
- Timely filing: 90–180 days from date of service (exceptions for retroactive eligibility, COB)

**Payment Timelines:**
- Clean claims paid within 30–45 days per state prompt payment law
- Late payment interest: 10%–18% APR per state statute
- Additional information requests: specify whether they suspend or do not suspend prompt payment clock

**Adjustments & COB:**
- Provider must bill primary payers first (Medicare for dual-eligibles, other commercial, auto/workers' comp)
- Recoupment procedures for overpayments, duplicates, ineligible members, non-covered services

**Payment Disputes:**
- Remittance advice with standard CARC/RARC codes
- Provider dispute window: 30–90 days from remittance
- Escalation: medical director review (clinical) → executive review (policy) → formal dispute resolution
- Retroactive eligibility termination: specify whether MCO pays for services rendered when member appeared eligible

**Balance Billing:**
- Prohibited for covered services (except copay/coinsurance/deductible)
- Permitted for non-covered services only with advance written member consent

### Article V: Quality Assurance & Utilization Management

**Quality Program:**
- HEDIS measure reporting (specify applicable measures by specialty)
- CAHPS survey participation: provide accurate member contacts, address deficiencies
- Clinical outcome tracking via registries, EHR interfaces, or claims data
- Peer review participation (conducted under state peer review protection statutes)

**Prior Authorization:**

| Service Category | Auth Required |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Elective inpatient admissions | Yes |
| Outpatient surgical procedures | Yes |
| Advanced imaging (MRI, CT) | Yes |
| Specialty medications | Yes |
| DME | Yes |
| Out-of-network referrals | Yes |

**Authorization Timelines:**

| Request Type | Decision Deadline |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Urgent | 24–72 hours |
| Non-urgent | 14 days (or per state regulation) |

- Decisions by qualified healthcare professionals; denials by physicians/licensed practitioners using MCG, InterQual, or MCO medical policies
- Appeal: 30–60 days to submit; expedited appeal within 72 hours for urgent cases; reviewer not involved in initial decision; external IRO review per ACA/state law

**Audits:**
- On-site audits with 10–30 days advance notice during business hours
- Provider must cooperate: records access, facility access, staff interviews
- Findings may trigger education, claims adjustments/recoupment, corrective action, or termination

**Case/Disease Management:**
- Cooperate with MCO case managers for complex/chronic conditions
- Case management recommendations are advisory; provider retains treatment authority
- Provider assumes financial responsibility for services rendered without required prior authorization

### Article VI: Term, Renewal & Termination

**Term:** Initial term of 1–3 years. Auto-renewal for successive 1-year terms unless 90–180 days written non-renewal notice.

**Termination Without Cause:** 90–180 days written notice; no reason required by either party; MCO must notify affected members.

**Termination For Cause (30 days or immediate):**
- [ ] Loss/suspension of required licenses
- [ ] Exclusion from Medicare/Medicaid (Section 1128 SSA)
- [ ] Uncured material breach after written notice and cure period
- [ ] Fraud or material misrepresentation (credentialing, claims)
- [ ] Failure to maintain required insurance
- [ ] Felony conviction or healthcare-related crime
- [ ] Conduct threatening member health/safety
- [ ] Professional ethics violations

**Automatic Termination (no notice required):**
- Provider death or permanent disability (individual)
- Dissolution or bankruptcy of either party
- MCO loss of state insurance license
- Mutual written agreement

**Post-Termination Obligations:**
- Transitional care: 90 days for active treatment; through delivery + postpartum for pregnant patients; extended for life-threatening/acute conditions
- Claims submission deadline: 60–90 days post-termination
- Return MCO property (member ID cards, manuals, confidential materials)
- Surviving obligations: payment for pre-termination services, record retention, PHI confidentiality

### Article VII: HIPAA & Data Protection

**Regulatory Framework:**
- HIPAA Privacy Rule & Security Rule
- HITECH Act breach notification
- State health information privacy laws (to extent more protective)
- State data breach notification laws

**PHI Exchange:**
- Permitted for treatment, payment, healthcare operations without patient authorization
- Minimum necessary standard applies (except treatment disclosures)
- Both parties must execute BAAs with subcontractors accessing PHI per HIPAA Omnibus Rule

**Breach Notification:**
- Notify other party within 24–72 hours of discovering breach of unsecured PHI
- Conduct risk assessment per HIPAA 4-factor test
- Breaching party: investigate scope, notify individuals, offer credit monitoring if applicable, implement corrective measures, cooperate with OCR/AG investigations
- Cost allocation: breaching party bears notification, mitigation, and penalty costs

**Security Safeguards:**
- Administrative: risk management, workforce security, access management, training, incident response
- Physical: facility access controls, workstation security, device/media controls
- Technical: access controls, audit controls, integrity controls, transmission security
- Encryption required at rest and in transit (or documented risk assessment with equivalent alternatives)

**Data Retention:** Medical records 6–10 years per state law. Return or destroy PHI upon termination (except designated record set and legally required retention).

### Article VIII: Indemnification & Insurance

**Mutual Indemnification:**
- Each party indemnifies the other for claims arising from its negligence, willful misconduct, breach, or legal violations
- Includes duty to defend with mutually acceptable counsel

**Provider Indemnification Scope:** Malpractice, negligent diagnosis/treatment, failure to obtain informed consent, improper PHI disclosure. Applies even if MCO UM decisions also alleged — provided provider's conduct was proximate cause. Does NOT cover claims arising solely from MCO UM/coverage decisions where provider followed standards and obtained authorizations.

**MCO Indemnification Scope:** UM decisions, coverage determinations, payment denials, credentialing decisions, network termination. Includes claims that auth denial/termination caused member harm where provider appropriately sought auth.

**Insurance Requirements:**

| Party | Coverage | Minimum Limits |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| Provider (physician) | Professional liability | $1M/$3M (higher for OB, neurosurgery, ortho) |
| Provider (hospital) | Professional + general liability | $10M–$25M+ |
| MCO | General liability, professional liability (UM/CM), E&O, cyber liability | Appropriate to size/scope |

- Carrier rated A- or better (A.M. Best)
- Claims-made policies require tail/ERP coverage
- MCO named as additional insured/certificate holder with 30-day cancellation notice
- Insurance minimums do not cap indemnification liability

### Article IX: Dispute Resolution

**Tiered Process:**

| Step | Timeframe | Process |
|------|-----------|---------|
| 1. Negotiation | Meet within 15–30 days of dispute notice | Designated reps with settlement authority |
| 2. Mediation | Within 30 days if negotiation fails; complete within 60 days | Neutral mediator (AAA/JAMS); confidential; costs shared equally |
| 3. Binding Arbitration | If mediation fails | Commercial arbitration rules (AAA/JAMS); single arbitrator (small disputes) or 3-member panel (complex); healthcare law expertise required |

**Arbitration Parameters:**
- Location: provider's practice city or MCO principal office
- Arbitrator authority on punitive damages and attorneys' fees: specify
- Costs: each party bears own attorneys' fees; arbitrator fees shared equally (unless bad faith finding)
- Decision final and binding; limited judicial review

**Exceptions to Arbitration:**
- Injunctive relief for irreparable harm (confidentiality/IP breaches)
- Medical necessity disputes affecting patient care (resolved via UM appeals or expedited judicial review)
- Small claims below specified dollar threshold

**Governing Law:** State where provider practices [or MCO domicile]. Venue in specified state/county; parties waive inconvenient forum objections.

**Member Rights Preserved:** Members retain all rights to external review, regulatory complaints, and court claims regardless of contract dispute resolution provisions.

### Article X: General Provisions

- **Amendments:** Written, signed by both authorized representatives
- **Assignment:** Prohibited without prior written consent (not unreasonably withheld); exception for mergers/acquisitions where successor assumes all obligations; unauthorized assignment is void and material breach
- **Notices:** Written; certified mail (return receipt), overnight courier, or personal delivery with acknowledgment; deemed received on delivery/refusal date; prompt address change notification required
- **Severability:** Invalid provisions severed; remainder continues; parties negotiate replacement achieving same objectives
- **Independent Contractor:** No employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship; neither party binds the other; each responsible for own employees, taxes, benefits
- **Entire Agreement:** Contract + exhibits + incorporated documents (provider manual, fee schedules) constitute complete agreement; supersedes prior negotiations/agreements; hierarchy: main contract > exhibits; specific provisions > general provisions; provider manual updates with reasonable notice; material term changes require amendment
- **Survival:** Indemnification survives for applicable statute of limitations (2–6 years); confidentiality survives 3–5 years (indefinite for trade secrets); payment obligations survive until resolved; dispute resolution provisions survive

**Regulatory Compliance:**
- Federal Anti-Kickback Statute (42 U.S.C. § 1320a-7b) [VERIFY]
- Stark Law (42 U.S.C. § 1395nn) [VERIFY]
- False Claims Act (31 U.S.C. §§ 3729–3733) [VERIFY]
- ACA prohibition on provider gag clauses
- State insurance laws (network adequacy, prompt payment, UM, provider termination)
- Neither party shall cause the other to violate applicable law
- Cooperate with regulatory audits/investigations; notify other party of subpoenas/CIDs

### Execution

- Signature blocks: printed name, title, date, authority representation
- Electronic signatures valid per E-SIGN Act and UETA
- May execute in counterparts; electronic delivery effective; exchange originals within 10–30 days
- Attach exhibits: fee schedule, covered services list, credentialing requirements, prior authorization forms

## Guidelines

1. **Jurisdiction-specific:** Verify state managed care statutes, prompt payment laws (timelines and interest rates vary by state), and insurance filing requirements before finalizing
2. **Line of business:** For Medicare Advantage contracts, incorporate CMS requirements (42 CFR Part 422) [VERIFY]; for Medicaid managed care, incorporate 42 CFR Part 438 [VERIFY] and state Medicaid agency requirements
3. **Accreditation alignment:** If MCO holds or seeks NCQA/URAC accreditation, ensure contract terms satisfy applicable accreditation standards
4. **Anti-Kickback safe harbors:** Structure payment terms to fit within personal services and management contracts safe harbor (42 CFR § 1001.952(d)) [VERIFY] — fair market value, commercially reasonable, written and signed, specifying services
5. **Do not** include specific payment rates without client instructions — use placeholders (e.g., "[X]% of Medicare PFS")
6. **Do not** draft as if representing both parties — maintain drafter's perspective while ensuring balanced terms
7. **Balance billing prohibition** must be explicit and unambiguous — required by most state laws and CMS for government program contracts
8. **Transitional care obligations** are often statutorily mandated — verify minimum periods under applicable state law
