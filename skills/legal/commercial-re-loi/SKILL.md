# Letter of Intent — Commercial Real Estate Purchase

Drafts a non-binding LOI for commercial real estate acquisition with enforceable confidentiality and exclusivity provisions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Buyer and seller** — full legal names, entity types, addresses
2. **Property identification** — address, APN, property type, included/excluded items
3. **Deal terms** — purchase price, deposit amount, financing structure, target closing date
4. **Timeline preferences** — exclusivity period, due diligence duration, acceptance deadline
5. **Brokerage information** — commission structure and responsible parties

## Output Structure

### Document Sections

| Section | Required Content |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Header** | Date, seller name/address, subject line with "Letter of Intent" + property address |
| **Introduction** | Identify LOI purpose, name both parties with legal designations, describe property type |
| **Buyer/Seller ID** | Full legal names, entity types; buyer assignment rights to affiliates/designees |
| **Property Description** | Street address, city, county, state, APN; land, improvements, fixtures, appurtenances; note exclusions |
| **Purchase Price** | Dollar amount; adjustment methodology (if any); form of consideration (cash/debt/seller financing) |
| **Earnest Money** | Amount, escrow holder, deposit deadline (business days post-PSA); refundability conditions; crediting at close |
| **Due Diligence** | Duration (days post-PSA); scope checklist; termination right with deposit refund; buyer's sole discretion standard |
| **Financing Contingency** | Loan type, acceptable terms/rates, securing timeframe; OR all-cash with proof-of-funds commitment |
| **Closing Date** | Target date or formula; extension conditions; consequences of delay |
| **Closing Costs** | Allocation table for: title insurance, escrow fees, transfer taxes, recording fees, survey, inspections, prorations, commissions, attorney fees |
| **Non-Binding Clause** | Expressly non-binding except enumerated binding provisions |
| **Confidentiality** | BINDING — scope, permitted disclosures (advisors, lenders), duration, injunctive relief remedy |
| **Exclusivity** | BINDING — no-shop period (typically 30–90 days), termination triggers, breach consequences |
| **Acceptance** | Deadline (date + time), delivery method, auto-expiration language |
| **Signature Blocks** | Buyer execution block; Seller "AGREED AND ACCEPTED" block; name, title, date lines |

### Due Diligence Scope Checklist

Include in the due diligence section:

- [ ] Physical/structural inspection
- [ ] Phase I (and Phase II if warranted) environmental
- [ ] Title commitment and exception review
- [ ] ALTA survey
- [ ] Lease and tenant file review
- [ ] Operating statements and financial records (3 years minimum)
- [ ] Zoning/land use/entitlement verification
- [ ] Service contracts and vendor agreements
- [ ] Property tax and assessment history
- [ ] Insurance loss history (CLUE report)

## Guidelines

- **Binding vs. non-binding** — The LOI must clearly distinguish non-binding business terms from binding provisions (confidentiality, exclusivity, good faith negotiation, governing law). Use bold headers or a separate "Binding Provisions" section.
- **Assignment clause** — Include buyer's right to assign to affiliates/SPEs; this is standard in CRE transactions.
- **Bracketed placeholders** — Use `[___]` for all amounts, dates, and party-specific details not yet provided.
- **Tone** — Professional, collaborative, deal-facilitating. Avoid adversarial framing.
- **Jurisdiction** — Drafted for U.S. transactions. Note that transfer tax allocation, title insurance customs, and closing mechanics vary by state. Flag state-specific items with `[Confirm per local custom]`.
- **No legal advice** — Include standard disclaimer that the LOI does not constitute legal advice and parties should consult their own counsel.
- **Exclusivity consideration** — Tie exclusivity to buyer's commitment of time and resources in conducting due diligence to support enforceability.
- **FRE awareness** — The LOI may become an exhibit; avoid admissions or concessions that could be problematic if the deal fails.
