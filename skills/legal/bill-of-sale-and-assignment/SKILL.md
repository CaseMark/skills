# Bill of Sale and Assignment Agreement

Drafts the primary closing deliverable transferring purchased assets and memorializing liability assumption pursuant to an underlying Asset Purchase Agreement (APA).

## Prerequisites

1. **Asset Purchase Agreement** — executed or execution-form APA with party names, effective date, and defined terms for Purchased Assets, Assumed Liabilities, and Excluded Assets
2. **Asset schedule/exhibit** — complete list of tangible and intangible assets being transferred
3. **Liability schedule** — definition of Assumed Liabilities (and Excluded Liabilities if separately scheduled)
4. **Party information** — full legal names, organizational form (corp/LLC/partnership), and jurisdiction of formation for each party
5. **Jurisdiction requirements** — governing law, any state-specific transfer requirements (bulk sales, transfer tax, real property)

## Output Structure

### 1. Header & Recitals

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Document title | Bill of Sale, Assignment and Assumption Agreement |
| Parties | Full legal names, organizational form, jurisdiction of formation |
| Effective date | Transfer date (may differ from execution date) |
| APA reference | Identify APA by date and parties |
| Defined terms | Incorporate Purchased Assets, Assumed Liabilities, Excluded Assets from APA |
| Purpose recital | "This instrument is delivered pursuant to the APA to effectuate the transfers contemplated therein" |

### 2. Bill of Sale (Tangible Assets)

Use present-tense conveyancing language:
> Seller **does hereby sell, transfer, assign, convey, and deliver** to Buyer all of Seller's right, title, and interest in and to the tangible personal property constituting Purchased Assets...

**Title warranty**: Seller has good and marketable title, free and clear of all liens, claims, security interests, and encumbrances, except as disclosed in the APA.

### 3. Assignment (Intangible Assets)

Separate section with equivalent conveyancing language. Enumerate:
- [ ] Intellectual property (patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets)
- [ ] Contract rights (subject to required third-party consents)
- [ ] Permits and licenses (to the extent assignable)
- [ ] Customer lists and data
- [ ] Goodwill
- [ ] Books and records
- [ ] Other intangibles per APA schedule

Note any consent requirements and whether assignment is conditioned on obtaining them.

### 4. Assumption of Liabilities

> Buyer **hereby assumes and agrees to pay, perform, and discharge when due** the Assumed Liabilities as defined in Section [X] of the APA.

Include express carve-out:
> Buyer is not assuming any liability other than the Assumed Liabilities. All Excluded Liabilities remain solely the responsibility of Seller.

### 5. General Provisions

| Clause | Key Language |
|---|---|
| Subordination to APA | "Nothing herein supersedes, modifies, or replaces the representations, warranties, covenants, indemnities, or other provisions of the APA, all of which remain in full force and effect." |
| Further assurances | Seller obligated to execute additional instruments of conveyance reasonably necessary to transfer Purchased Assets or put Buyer in actual possession and operating control |
| Governing law | Match APA governing law unless specific reason to diverge |
| Boilerplate | Successors and assigns, counterparts, severability |

### 6. Signature Blocks

Both parties execute with authorized officer signature, printed name, and title. Buyer block: "Accepted and Agreed."

## Guidelines

- **Non-novation**: Confirm Bill of Sale does not constitute a novation or replacement of the APA
- **Consistency check**: All defined terms must match APA definitions exactly; verify party names, dates, and section cross-references
- **Special transfer instruments**: Identify assets requiring separate documentation beyond this instrument:
  - IP assignments for USPTO or Copyright Office recordation
  - Vehicle certificate of title transfers
  - Real estate deeds (if any real property included)
- **Jurisdiction-specific requirements**:
  - Transfer tax affidavits (state-dependent)
  - Bulk sales compliance [VERIFY applicable state law]
  - Notarization if required for specific asset types
- **Consent-conditioned assignments**: Flag any contracts or licenses where assignment requires third-party consent not yet obtained at closing
- **Effective date**: Confirm whether effective date aligns with or differs from APA closing date and document accordingly
