# Non-Profit Board Meeting Minutes

Drafts formal, governance-ready minutes that serve as the official corporate record of board actions and can withstand IRS, auditor, and regulatory scrutiny.

## Prerequisites

1. **Meeting logistics** — date, start time, location (in-person / virtual / hybrid), presiding officer name and title
2. **Attendance roster** — all board members present, absent (excused/unexcused), and any staff or guests
3. **Bylaws quorum requirement** — or applicable state non-profit corporation law default (typically majority of board)
4. **Prior meeting minutes** — for approval, including any corrections to be noted
5. **Agenda and supporting documents** — officer/committee reports, financial statements, proposed resolutions
6. **Vote tallies** — exact counts for each motion (ayes / nays / abstentions)
7. **Conflict disclosures** — any recusals, who left the room, and on which items

---

## Output Structure

### 1. Header Block

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Organization name | Full legal name |
| Meeting type | Regular / Special / Emergency |
| Date | Month DD, YYYY |
| Time | Start time and time zone |
| Location | Address or platform (e.g., Zoom video conference) |
| Presiding officer | Name, Title |
| Recording officer | Name, Title (typically Secretary) |

---

### 2. Attendance & Quorum

```
BOARD MEMBERS PRESENT: [Name, Name, Name...]
BOARD MEMBERS ABSENT (Excused): [Name...]
BOARD MEMBERS ABSENT (Unexcused): [Name...]
STAFF/GUESTS PRESENT: [Name, Title...]

QUORUM: A quorum of [N] of [Total] directors was present as required by
Article [X], Section [X] of the Bylaws. The meeting was duly constituted
for the transaction of business.
```

---

### 3. Approval of Prior Minutes

- Motion to approve minutes of [prior meeting date]
- Moved by: ___ | Seconded by: ___
- Corrections noted (if any): [describe specifically]
- Vote: Carried [unanimously / X-X-X]

---

### 4. Officer & Committee Reports

For each report, capture:

- **Presenter** and role
- **Key metrics or findings** material to board oversight (not verbatim)
- **Action items or recommendations** referred to full board
- **Documents distributed** (note retained in records; do not reproduce)

Mandatory for Treasurer's Report:
- [ ] Current cash/bank position
- [ ] Revenue vs. budget YTD
- [ ] Significant variances explained
- [ ] Overall financial health assessment

---

### 5. Old Business

For each item:

```
ITEM: [Descriptive title]
Background: [Brief context — enough for future reader to understand]
Discussion: [Summary of substantive points; do not attribute to individuals
             unless material]
Motion: [Exact resolution language]
Moved by: ___  |  Seconded by: ___
Vote: Carried/Failed [X ayes – X nays – X abstentions]
```

---

### 6. New Business

Same format as Old Business. Flag with extra precision for:

| Decision Type | Required in Minutes |
|---|---|
| Annual budget approval | Total amount, fiscal year |
| Major transaction / contract | Counterparty, amount, scope, authorization limit |
| Officer election | Name, title, term |
| Bylaw amendment | Exact amended language |
| Conflict of interest approval | Conflicted party, nature of conflict, recusal notation |
| Delegation of authority | Scope, dollar threshold, duration |
| Legal counsel consulted | Note consultation only; do not disclose privileged content unless privilege waived |

**Recusal template:**
> "[Name] disclosed a potential conflict of interest regarding [general description]. [Name] recused themselves, left the room at [time], and returned at [time] following conclusion of discussion and vote."

---

### 7. Executive Session *(if applicable)*

```
The Board entered Executive Session at [time] to discuss [general category:
e.g., personnel matter / pending litigation / sensitive negotiation].

Present during Executive Session: [Names]

The Board returned to open session at [time].

[Any actions taken post-executive session are recorded below under New/Old
Business. Deliberative content remains confidential.]
```

---

### 8. Action Item Summary

| Item | Responsible Party | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| [Task] | [Name / Committee] | [Date or "Next meeting"] |

---

### 9. Adjournment & Certification

```
There being no further business, a motion to adjourn was made by [Name],
seconded by [Name]. The meeting adjourned at [time].

Next meeting: [Date, Time, Location — if established]

CERTIFICATION

I certify that these minutes accurately reflect the actions taken and
decisions made at the [Organization Name] Board of Directors meeting held
on [Date].

___________________________________     Date: ____________
[Secretary Name], Secretary

___________________________________     Date: ____________
[Chair Name], Board Chair
```

## Guidelines

- **Tense & tone**: Past tense throughout; formal corporate terminology (`moved`, `seconded`, `carried`, `tabled`, `resolved`)
- **Precision on resolutions**: Copy exact motion language — minutes serve as official authorization for actions taken
- **No editorial content**: Omit subjective characterizations, personal opinions, or verbatim debate transcripts
- **Conflict of interest**: Always document recusals with room-exit notation; critical for IRS Form 990 and duty-of-loyalty compliance
- **Quorum**: If bylaws are silent, apply state default — majority of authorized directors [VERIFY for specific state]
- **IRS/audit readiness**: Minutes may be reviewed on Form 990 audit; ensure budget approvals, compensation decisions, and related-party transactions are fully documented
- **Executive session**: Record category only — never document privileged legal advice or confidential personnel deliberations in the main minutes
- **Retention**: Flag that supporting documents (financials, reports, contracts) are incorporated by reference and retained in the corporate records book
