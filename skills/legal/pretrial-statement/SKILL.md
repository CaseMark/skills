# Pre-Trial Statement / Report

Create a court-compliant pre-trial statement/report that narrows issues and provides the trial roadmap.

## Prerequisites

1. **Local rules and judge order** for pre-trial statements and formatting.
2. **Case posture summary** with claims, defenses, and key rulings.
3. **Stipulated facts** agreed with opposing counsel (if joint).
4. **Contested issues list** (law and fact) aligned to trial themes.
5. **Witness disclosures** and expert reports.
6. **Exhibit inventory** with identifiers (Bates, exhibit numbers).
7. **Trial management inputs** (trial length, jury/bench, motions in limine status).

## Output Structure / Process

1. **Caption + Title**
2. **Introductory paragraph** describing filing type (joint/individual) and governing order.
3. **Stipulated Facts**
4. **Contested Issues of Law**
5. **Contested Issues of Fact**
6. **Witness List**
7. **Exhibit List**
8. **Procedural / Trial Management Matters**
9. **Signature block(s)** per local rule

### Stipulated Facts (numbered, non-argumentative)

Use verbatim agreed wording where possible. One fact per number.

| No. | Stipulated Fact (verbatim) | Source / Record Cite | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|

### Contested Issues of Law (questions for decision)

Frame each as a discrete question the court must decide.

| No. | Legal Issue (question) | Authority (statute/case/rule) | Impact on Trial |
|---|---|---|---|

Template phrasing:
- “Whether [legal standard] applies to [claim/defense] where [key fact]…”
- “Whether [doctrine] bars [claim] given [contract/statute]…”

### Contested Issues of Fact (neutral, material disputes)

State neutrally, at meaningful granularity.

| No. | Factual Issue (neutral) | Key Evidence Sources | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|

Template phrasing:
- “Whether [party] represented [fact] on [date].”
- “Whether [event] caused [harm/damages].”

### Witness List

Include only properly disclosed witnesses. Flag any proposed additions.

| Order | Witness Name | Type (Fact/Expert) | City/State | Subject of Testimony | Disclosure / Report |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

### Exhibit List

Follow local numbering convention and joint numbering rules.

| Exh No. | Description | Bates / ID | Offered By | Objection / Stipulation |
|---|---|---|---|---|

### Procedural / Trial Management Matters

Include only items required by local rule or judge order.

Checklist:
- Trial type: jury or bench.
- Estimated trial length (days).
- Damages categories and amounts (if required).
- Motions in limine list and status.
- Deposition designations and counter-designations.
- Technology / courtroom needs.
- Proposed jury instructions or verdict form issues (if required).
- Bifurcation / sequencing proposals.
- ADR history or settlement status (if allowed by rule).

### Signature Block

- Include counsel signatures per local rule.
- If joint, include signatures for all parties.

## Guidelines

- Use local rules and judge orders as controlling authority for required sections and formatting.
- Stipulated facts must be purely factual, non-argumentative, and actually agreed.
- Do not concede contested elements in stipulated facts or issue framing.
- Align issues with claims/defenses and anticipated jury instructions or pretrial rulings.
- Ensure every witness and exhibit is already disclosed; flag any gaps.
- Use consistent numbering and labels across the document.
- If the court requires citations or record references, include them.
- Confirm page limits, font, spacing, and header/footer requirements.
- Flag missing inputs or unresolved joint wording for attorney review.
