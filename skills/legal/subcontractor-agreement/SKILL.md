# Subcontractor Agreement

Drafts a comprehensive subcontractor agreement that defines scope, allocates risk, and ensures compliance with applicable construction and contract law.

## Prerequisites

1. **Prime contract** — underlying owner-contractor agreement, including flow-down provisions
2. **Scope documents** — plans, specifications, drawings, or SOW defining the subcontracted work
3. **Project details** — location (state/county), timeline, milestones, project type (construction vs. service)
4. **Party information** — legal names, entity types, states of formation, authorized signatories
5. **Insurance requirements** — Contractor's minimum coverage expectations
6. **Payment structure** — lump sum, unit price, T&M, or cost-plus; retainage percentage

## Output Structure

### Section Outline

| # | Section | Key Contents |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Parties | Legal names, entity types, state of formation, DBAs, authorized reps |
| 2 | Recitals | Prime contract reference, project description, subcontract purpose |
| 3 | Scope of Work | Included/excluded work, specs, materials/equipment responsibility, permits, coordination, change order process |
| 4 | Contract Price & Payment | Price structure, invoice requirements, payment timing, retainage (typically 5-10%), lien waivers, disputed amounts, late payment interest |
| 5 | Schedule | Start/completion dates, milestones, delay notice requirements, liquidated damages, force majeure, time extensions |
| 6 | Insurance & Bonding | CGL, workers' comp, auto, professional liability minimums; additional insured; waiver of subrogation; bond requirements |
| 7 | Indemnification | Defense/indemnity/hold-harmless obligations; compliance with state anti-indemnity statutes |
| 8 | Confidentiality | Definition, obligations, exceptions, survival period (2-5 years), marketing restrictions |
| 9 | Termination | For cause (with cure period), for convenience (payment for work performed + demobilization), post-termination duties |
| 10 | Dispute Resolution | Governing law, tiered process (negotiation → mediation → arbitration/litigation), fee allocation, jury waiver |
| 11 | General Provisions | Independent contractor status, compliance with laws, assignment restrictions, severability, integration, amendments |
| 12 | Execution | Signature blocks with name, title, signature, date; counterparts; e-signature authorization |

### Payment Clause Template

```
Section _: COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT

_.1 Contract Price. Contractor shall pay Subcontractor [LUMP SUM $_____ / 
unit prices per Exhibit _ / T&M per rate schedule Exhibit _] for complete 
performance of the Work.

_.2 Invoicing. Subcontractor shall submit invoices [monthly by the ___ day / 
upon milestone completion] with: (a) itemized work completed; (b) supporting 
documentation; (c) conditional lien waivers for current payment; 
(d) unconditional lien waivers for prior payment.

_.3 Payment. Contractor shall pay undisputed amounts within [30] days of 
receipt of a proper invoice, less retainage of [___]%.

_.4 Retainage Release. Retainage shall be released within [30] days after: 
(a) final completion and acceptance; (b) receipt of unconditional final lien 
waiver; and (c) delivery of all close-out documents.

_.5 Disputed Amounts. Contractor may withhold payment for disputed or 
defective work but shall pay all undisputed amounts per §_.3.

_.6 Late Payment. Overdue undisputed amounts bear interest at [___]% per 
annum [or the maximum rate permitted under [State] law].
```

### Insurance Requirements Table

| Coverage | Minimum Limits | Requirements |
|----------|---------------|--------------|
| Commercial General Liability | $1-2M per occurrence / $2-4M aggregate | Name Contractor + Owner as additional insureds |
| Workers' Compensation | Statutory limits | Employer's liability $1M |
| Auto Liability | $1M combined single limit | If vehicles used on project |
| Professional Liability | $1M per claim | If design or professional services involved |
| Builder's Risk / Installation Floater | As appropriate | If applicable to work type |

**All policies**: 30-day cancellation notice to Contractor; waiver of subrogation; certificates due before work begins.

### Indemnification Checklist

- [ ] Subcontractor indemnifies for claims arising from its work, negligence, or breach
- [ ] Verify state anti-indemnity statute compliance:
  - **Type I states** (e.g., CA, TX, FL): Void if indemnifies for indemnitee's sole negligence [VERIFY]
  - **Type II states** (e.g., NY, IL): Void if indemnifies for indemnitee's any negligence [VERIFY]
  - **No restriction states**: Broad indemnity permitted [VERIFY]
- [ ] Consider mutual indemnification for respective negligence
- [ ] Include duty to defend (not just indemnify)
- [ ] Confirm indemnity survives termination

### Termination Framework

| Type | Notice | Cure Period | Payment Owed |
|------|--------|-------------|-------------|
| For Cause — curable breach | Written | 5-10 days | Work performed less damages |
| For Cause — incurable/safety | Written | None | Work performed less damages |
| For Cause — insolvency/bankruptcy | Written | None | Work performed less damages |
| For Convenience | Written, [__] days | N/A | Work performed + demobilization; no lost profit |

## Guidelines

- **State-specific compliance**: Construction contract laws vary significantly — check retainage caps, prompt payment statutes, mechanic's lien requirements, and anti-indemnity statutes for the project state
- **Flow-down provisions**: Incorporate prime contract obligations by reference; ensure subcontract terms don't conflict with prime contract
- **Liquidated damages**: Must be a reasonable pre-estimate of actual harm, not a penalty — document the basis
- **OSHA compliance**: Include explicit safety obligations and right-to-stop-work for violations
- **Prevailing wage**: If public project or Davis-Bacon applies, include wage compliance and certified payroll requirements
- **Defined terms**: Use initial caps consistently; avoid undefined uses of "reasonable" or "promptly"
- **Change orders**: All scope/price/time changes require written authorization — no oral modifications
- **Lien waivers**: Match waiver type (conditional/unconditional, progress/final) to state statutory forms where required
- **Independent contractor**: Explicitly disclaim employment relationship; Subcontractor responsible for its own taxes, benefits, and workers' comp
- **Do not** include arbitration clauses without confirming client preference — some clients prefer court litigation
- **Do not** draft indemnification broader than state law permits
