# Commercial Real Estate Purchase LOI

Sets proposed deal terms and a negotiation framework for a commercial property acquisition while limiting binding effect.

## Prerequisites

1. **Parties** — legal names, entity types, authority, notice addresses.
2. **Property ID** — street address, county/state, APN(s), included/excluded assets.
3. **Economic terms** — price, consideration type, deposit, financing posture.
4. **Timeline** — due diligence length, PSA target date, closing target.
5. **Binding scope** — confidentiality, exclusivity, expense reimbursement, governing law.

## Output Structure / Process

### Document Skeleton

1. **Header**
   - Date.
   - Seller name and address.
   - Subject line: "Letter of Intent – [Property Address/Name]".

2. **Intro Paragraph**
   - Identify buyer and seller.
   - Identify property and intended transaction.
   - State non-binding nature except specified sections.

| Term | Drafting Instruction | Placeholder |
| --- | --- | --- |

### Due Diligence Scope Checklist

- Physical inspections and access rules.
- Environmental review and reports.
- Title commitment, exceptions, and survey.
- Leases, rent roll, tenant files, estoppels.
- Operating statements and expense history.
- Zoning, permitted use, compliance records.
- Service contracts and vendor agreements.

### Binding Provisions Block

Include a discrete section titled "Binding Provisions" that expressly states these are binding and all other terms are non-binding.

| Binding Item | Required Elements |
| --- | --- |
| Confidentiality | Scope, permitted disclosures, duration, exclusions, remedies |
| Exclusivity | Start date, duration, prohibited activities, consideration, breach consequences |
| Expenses | Each side bears its own, or seller reimburses buyer for diligence on breach |
| Governing Law | State law and venue for binding provisions only |

### Acceptance Block

- Acceptance deadline with date/time and delivery method.
- Buyer signature block with name/title/date.
- Seller acceptance block labeled "Agreed and Accepted" with name/title/date.

### Optional Terms (Include Only If Provided)

| Optional Clause | Use When |
| --- | --- |
| Tenant Estoppels/SNDA | Multi-tenant or credit-sensitive deals |
| PSA Milestones | Need staged diligence or deposit increases |
| Risk Allocation | Casualty/condemnation between LOI and closing |
| Broker Commission | Broker involved or allocation disputes anticipated |

## Guidelines

- State non-binding status in the intro and again before signature blocks.
- Isolate binding sections and add a severability sentence for those sections only.
- Avoid "agreement to agree" language unless expressly intended.
- Do not include good-faith negotiation obligations unless client wants that exposure.
- Use specific dates and dollar amounts; bracket unknowns.
- Confirm compliance with local transfer tax and escrow customs.
- If seller is not record owner, require evidence of authority to sell.
