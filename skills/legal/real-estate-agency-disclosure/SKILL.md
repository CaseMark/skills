# Real Estate Agency Disclosure

Drafts a jurisdiction-compliant agency disclosure establishing representation relationships and fiduciary duties before parties commit to a residential real estate transaction.

## Prerequisites

1. **Jurisdiction** — state where the property is located (drives all statutory requirements)
2. **Party identification** — full legal names of buyer(s), seller(s), and licensee(s); brokerage names; license numbers
3. **Transaction details** — property address and transaction type (purchase or listing)
4. **Intended agency model** — buyer's agent, seller's agent, dual agency, designated agency, or transaction brokerage

## Output Structure

### 1. Header

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Document title | "Real Estate Agency Disclosure" or jurisdiction-mandated title |
| Disclosure date | Date of delivery/execution |
| Parties | Full legal names of all buyers, sellers, and licensees; brokerage; license numbers |

### 2. Statutory Basis
- Cite the specific state statute or regulation requiring this disclosure
- State the trigger: delivery required at **first substantive contact** (verify jurisdiction-specific timing)
- Note any state-specific timing variations or alternative trigger points

### 3. Agency Relationship Definitions

| Role | Represents | Core Duties |
|---|---|---|
| **Seller's Agent** | Seller exclusively | Loyalty, confidentiality, disclosure re: buyers, best price/terms for seller |
| **Buyer's Agent** | Buyer exclusively | Loyalty, confidentiality, disclosure re: property/seller, best price/terms for buyer |
| **Dual Agent** | Both parties — same transaction | Neutrality; cannot advocate for either party or cross-disclose confidential information |
| **Designated Agent** | Respective client (separate agents within same brokerage) | Full fiduciary to each client; supervising broker is dual agent |
| **Transaction Broker / Facilitator** | Neither party — assists transaction only | Honesty, material fact disclosure, accounting, ministerial skill and care |

**Client vs. Customer**: Client receives full fiduciary representation; customer receives honest treatment and factual disclosure only — no advocacy or confidential advice.

**Core fiduciary duties** (where full agency applies): loyalty · confidentiality · disclosure · obedience · reasonable care and diligence · accounting

### 4. Dual Agency / Alternative Models — Jurisdiction Checklist

- [ ] Does the state **permit** dual agency? (Some states have abolished it — [VERIFY current state list])
- [ ] Does the state require **informed written consent** before dual agency may proceed?
- [ ] Does the state offer a statutory **transaction broker** or **facilitator** role as an alternative?
- [ ] Does the state require **designated agency** within a brokerage in lieu of dual agency?

If dual agency is **prohibited**: state this explicitly and identify the required alternative arrangement.

If dual agency is **permitted**: include a conspicuous warning that the agent cannot fully advocate for either party and that consent is required beyond this disclosure.

### 5. Confirmation and Acknowledgment Language

The disclosure must state:
- Receipt of this disclosure does **not** create an agency relationship
- Agency is formalized only through a **separate written agreement**
- Parties have the right to choose any available representation type or to proceed unrepresented
- Parties have the right to seek independent legal counsel

Suggested acknowledgment:
> "I acknowledge that I have received, read, and understand this Real Estate Agency Disclosure and the types of agency relationships described herein. I understand that I have the right to choose the representation type that best serves my interests."

### 6. Agency Election (if jurisdiction requires on-form selection)

- [ ] Seller's Agency
- [ ] Buyer's Agency
- [ ] Dual Agency *(where permitted)*
- [ ] Designated Agency
- [ ] Transaction Brokerage / Facilitator

### 7. Signature Blocks

Provide a separate block for each buyer, seller, and licensee:

```
Printed Name:  _________________________________
Signature:     _________________________________
Date:          _________________________________
License No.:   _________________________________  (licensees only)
Brokerage:     _________________________________  (licensees only)
```

Caption above each block:
> "By signing, I acknowledge receipt of this disclosure on the date indicated and confirm my understanding of the agency relationships described herein."

- Verify whether the jurisdiction requires **witness signatures** or **notarization**
- If electronically executed: confirm compliance with state e-signature law (UETA / E-SIGN [VERIFY])

## Guidelines

- **Jurisdiction first** — agency models, permissible relationships, mandatory verbatim language, and execution formalities vary significantly by state; always verify current code and real estate commission regulations before drafting
- **Mandatory verbatim language** — many states require exact statutory text; do not paraphrase without confirming flexibility is permitted
- **Timing** — most states require delivery at first substantive contact; cite the applicable statutory trigger explicitly
- **No recommendation on choice** — the disclosure informs parties of their options; it does not advise which representation type to select
- **Dual agency warnings** — if permitted, conspicuous warnings are required; inadequate disclosure of limitations can void consent and create liability
- **NAR alignment** — follow current NAR guidelines but prioritize state statutory requirements where they diverge
- **[VERIFY]** all statutory citations against the current edition of the applicable state code and real estate commission regulations before use in an actual transaction
