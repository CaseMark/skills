# Commercial Triple Net Lease Agreement

Drafts an enforceable triple-net lease that converts transaction facts into a complete execution-ready agreement.

## Prerequisites

1. **Deal package**: term sheet/LOI, rent schedule, concessions, TI allowance, pre-close conditions.
2. **Party information**: legal names, entity type, state of formation, authority to sign, guarantor details.
3. **Property package**: legal description, parcel ID, site map, title evidence, zoning code, occupancy and code-status.
4. **Risk package**: insurance binders/baselines, required coverage levels, indemnity expectations.
5. **Operational package**: permitted use, hours, hours-of-operation constraints, signage/parking/access needs.
6. **Jurisdiction research**: governing-state landlord-tenant statute, fee-shifting rules, anti-indemnification limits, assignment restrictions, acceleration/enforcement caps.

## Output Structure / Process

### 1) Intake and validation matrix

```text
PARTIES | PREMISES | RENT/TERM | EXPENSES | USE/COMPLIANCE | DEFAULTS | MORTGAGE | EXECUTION
```

### 2) Drafting framework

| Module | Mandatory content | Required output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Definitions | Consistent defined terms for all recurring concepts | Cross-reference-safe definitions block |
| Premises and title rights | Description, legal parcel ties, use areas, common-area rights | Clear scope + boundaries + access rights |
| Term structure | Start trigger, delivery/possession milestones, renewal mechanics | Option windows, notice format, rent formula |
| Base rent | Base rent, due dates, proration, late charges | Payment timetable + method + return-to-tenant accounting |
| Triple-net expenses | Taxes, insurance, CAM, utilities, major repair reserves | Pass-through formula + audit rights + billing timing |
| Insurance | CGL, property, excess, WC, umbrella | Additional insured, primary/non-contributory, lapse cure process |
| Maintenance/repairs | Structural vs non-structural split, workmanship standard | Repair obligations + capital replacement treatment |
| Use and compliance | Permitted/prohibited use, permits, ADA, environmental | Zoning/codes alignment + change-of-use triggers |
| Alterations | Consent standards, approvals, quality standards, ownership at end | Buildout obligations + surrender handling |
| Transfer rights | Assignment/subletting/transfer of control | Consent gate, criteria, deemed consent, continued liability |
| Defaults | Monetized + non-monetary default list | Notice and cure windows + cure exceptions |
| Remedies | Reentry, reletting, acceleration, damages, mitigation | Jurisdiction-compliant remedy ladder |
| Risk allocation | Indemnity, mutual waiver of subrogation, liability split | Negligence carve-outs + public policy guardrails |
| Mortgage relations | SNDA/attornment and mortgage priority | Sale/foreclosure continuity language |
| Final provisions | Notice, force majeure, estoppel, integration, jury trial | State-compliant boilerplate and severability |
| Execution kit | Signature blocks, exhibits, delivery checklist | Execution-ready blanks marked clearly |

### 4) Compliance controls (must be applied before finalize)

1. Validate all defined terms appear in order and are used consistently.
2. Ensure each variable and rate (rent, escalation, CAM, taxes) references a computable source.
3. Insert required exhibits and mark unresolved items `[____]`.
4. Add jurisdiction-specific caveats where rule uncertainty exists: mark `[VERIFY]`.
5. Include closing checklist: insurance binders, estoppel, deposits, corporate authority, first payment and proration.

### 5) Jurisdiction and risk checks

| Check | Why | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| State anti-indemnity/neglect limits | Many states ban indemnity for own negligence | Add strict carve-outs and caps |
| Enforcement of holdover/penalty rent | Could be deemed punitive outside market range | Set defensible standard for escalation |
| Personal guaranty enforceability | Varies by signature authority, drafting, and wording | Confirm guarantor language and notice terms |
| Notice service validity | State-specific form and receipt rules differ | Use state-appropriate service matrix |
| Attorney-fee recovery symmetry | Mutuality often required in some states | Mirror or justify unilateral fee recovery |

## Guidelines

- Do preserve the full triple-net economics: taxes, insurance, CAM, utilities, common-area methodology.
- Do separate structural repair from ordinary maintenance and allocate responsibility unambiguously.
- Do include measurable consent standards for transfers and subletting to prevent unconstrained waiver.
- Do include tenant and landlord remedies with statutory cure and mitigation controls.
- Do include SNDA/attornment linkage where financing and foreclosure risk exists.
- Don't assume statutory defaults; always localize assignment, cure, acceleration, and indemnity clauses.
- Don't leave unresolved blanks unmarked.
- If any legal rule is uncertain, tag it as `[VERIFY]` in the draft.
