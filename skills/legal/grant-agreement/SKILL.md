# Grant Agreement

Drafts a legally enforceable U.S. grant agreement transferring funds with conditions, protecting grantor oversight and accountability while giving grantees clear operational parameters.

## Prerequisites

1. **Grantor identity** — full legal name, state of formation, EIN, entity type (private foundation, corporate entity, public charity, government agency), authorized signatory and authority source (bylaws, board resolution, delegation)
2. **Grantee identity** — full legal name, DBA names, jurisdiction, EIN, tax classification (501(c)(3), governmental entity, fiscal sponsor, foreign org), authorized signatory
3. **Grant terms** — total amount, currency, disbursement type (lump sum / installment / milestone-based), grant period start and end dates
4. **Project details** — purpose, measurable objectives, deliverables, timeline, geographic scope, approved budget broken down by expense category
5. **Prior materials** — application/proposal, board resolutions, prior correspondence, matching fund commitments
6. **Special flags** — private foundation grantor (triggers IRC § 4945); foreign grantee (equivalency determination or expenditure responsibility required); government funds involved (assess Uniform Guidance, 2 CFR Part 200)

## Output Structure

### 1. Parties & Recitals

**Grantor block:** Legal name, state of formation, principal address, EIN, entity type, authorized representative + title + authority source.

**Grantee block:** Legal name, DBA, jurisdiction, EIN, tax status (cite 501(c)(3) determination letter date), principal address. If fiscal sponsor: identify all three parties and assign obligations explicitly. If foreign org: note equivalency determination election or expenditure responsibility.

**Recitals:** Establish (a) grantor's exempt purpose or CSR rationale; (b) grantee's qualifications and track record; (c) selection process (competitive, invitation, board approval); (d) nature of relationship — grant, not loan, contract, joint venture, or exchange for services; (e) legal authority for grant (payout compliance, board approval, government authorization).

### 2. Grant Amount & Payment

| Element | Terms |
|---|---|
| Total amount | State in numerals and words; specify currency |
| Disbursement type | Lump sum / scheduled installments / milestone tranches |
| Conditions precedent per payment | Reports approved, deliverables met, continued compliance, matching funds evidenced |
| Budget modification threshold | ≤10% line-item variance: grantee discretion; >10% or category change: prior written approval required |
| Unexpended funds | Return to grantor within [30] days of term end, or carry-forward with written approval |

---

### 3. Permitted & Prohibited Uses

**Permitted:**
- Direct program costs: salaries/benefits (grant-allocated FTE %), equipment, supplies, travel, consultants, participant costs
- Indirect/overhead: [specify % of direct costs or fixed dollar cap]
- Subgrants or contracts: only with prior written approval; key agreement provisions must flow down

**Prohibited:**
- Political campaign activity (IRC § 501(c)(3) absolute prohibition)
- Lobbying beyond permissible limits (track against IRC § 501(h) election if applicable)
- Private benefit or inurement to insiders or related parties
- Capital campaigns or endowment contributions (unless expressly authorized)
- Activities that would jeopardize either party's tax-exempt status
- Illegal activities under applicable federal, state, or local law

---

### 4. Conditions Precedent & Ongoing Obligations

**Pre-disbursement checklist:**
- [ ] Current IRS determination letter (501(c)(3)) or equivalent
- [ ] Certificate of good standing in jurisdiction of formation
- [ ] Detailed budget with narrative justification by category
- [ ] Work plan and project timeline with key milestones
- [ ] Evidence of required insurance (GL and D&O — specify minimum limits)
- [ ] Executed conflict of interest policy signed by board members and key staff
- [ ] Board resolution authorizing acceptance of grant
- [ ] Expenditure responsibility agreement if grantor is a private foundation (IRC § 4945(d)(4))

**Ongoing covenants:**
- Maintain grant funds in segregated account or accounting system that tracks grant expenditures separately
- GAAP-compliant financial records; retain all grant-related records [3–7] years post-term
- Maintain all licenses, permits, and accreditations required for funded activities
- Comply with all applicable federal, state, and local law (employment, environmental, health/safety, sector-specific)
- Maintain required insurance; name grantor as additional insured where appropriate
- Acknowledge grantor support in publications and public communications per grantor's recognition guidelines (acknowledgment ≠ endorsement of findings)
- Obtain prior written approval before: key personnel changes, scope modifications, subgranting, budget reallocations above threshold

---

### 5. Reporting & Monitoring

| Report | Frequency | Due Date | Required Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| Financial progress | Quarterly or semi-annual | [X] days after period end | Expenditures by budget category, cumulative totals, variance explanation (>10–15% triggers narrative), officer certification of compliance |
| Programmatic / narrative | Same as financial | Same | Activities completed, progress vs. objectives, challenges encountered, outcomes (quantitative + qualitative), plan modifications with justification |
| Final report | Once | [30–90] days post-term | Full financial reconciliation, outcomes vs. original objectives, lessons learned, sustainability plan, publications/IP inventory |
| Independent audit | Annual (if required) | [120–180] days after fiscal year end | CPA audit per GAAS; required if grant exceeds [$50k–$100k] or org annual revenue exceeds [$750k] [VERIFY OMB Single Audit threshold applicability at 2 CFR § 200.501] |

**Grantor monitoring rights:**
- Site visits with [X] business days' notice (no notice required if fraud suspected)
- On-demand inspection of books, records, and facilities
- Reporting delinquency consequences: suspension of future payments, return demand, or termination for material breach

---

### 6. Intellectual Property & Confidentiality

- Specify ownership of IP created with grant funds: grantee-owned / grantor-owned / jointly owned / public domain
- If grantee retains ownership: grant grantor a royalty-free license for specified purposes
- Confidentiality: each party protects the other's proprietary information; obligation survives termination

---

### 7. Termination

**Grantor termination for cause** (with [15–30]-day written cure period unless breach is incurable):

| Incurable (immediate) | Curable (notice + cure period) |
|---|---|
| Fraud or intentional misuse of funds | Reporting delinquency |
| Criminal conviction of key personnel | Failure to maintain insurance |
| Loss of 501(c)(3) or required legal status | Budget overrun without approval |
| Bankruptcy or dissolution | Key personnel departure without notice |
| OFAC/sanctions violation | Minor scope deviation |

**Automatic termination triggers:** Bankruptcy filing, dissolution, loss of tax-exempt status, prohibited change of control or mission.

**Termination for convenience:** [30]-day written notice; grantee retains funds properly expended before effective date.

**Grantee withdrawal:** Good-faith written notice if performance becomes impossible or would violate mission; return unexpended funds.

**Post-termination obligations:**
- Return unexpended funds within [30] days + final accounting
- For-cause termination: grantor may demand return of all funds, including amounts already expended
- Surviving provisions: record retention, audit cooperation, confidentiality, IP, indemnification

**Tangible assets purchased with grant funds:** [Return to grantor / Transfer to grantor-designated nonprofit / Grantee retains for continued charitable use — specify]

### 8. Standard Provisions

| Provision | Terms |
|---|---|
| Governing law | [Grantor's state]; without conflict-of-laws principles |
| Venue | [County/District], [State] — exclusive jurisdiction |
| Dispute resolution | (1) Good-faith negotiation [30 days] → (2) Mediation, costs shared equally → (3) Litigation or binding AAA Commercial Arbitration |
| Indemnification | Grantee indemnifies grantor against third-party claims arising from grantee's acts, omissions, or breach |
| Assignment | Grantee: prohibited without prior written consent. Grantor: may assign to successor or co-funder assuming all obligations |
| Relationship of parties | Independent contractor; no partnership, JV, agency, or employment relationship created |
| Amendment | Written, signed by authorized representatives of both parties |
| Integration | Entire agreement; supersedes all prior negotiations, correspondence, and understandings |
| Severability | Invalid provisions modified to minimum extent necessary; remainder unaffected |
| Waiver | Must be written and signed; failure to enforce is not a waiver |
| Counterparts | Electronic and facsimile signatures valid; multiple originals constitute one instrument |
| Notices | Certified mail return receipt / overnight courier with tracking / email with confirmed receipt to designated addresses |

## Guidelines

- **Private foundation grantors**: IRC § 4945 expenditure responsibility is mandatory if grantee is not a U.S. public charity; requires pre-grant inquiry documentation, written expenditure responsibility agreement, and grantee reports to IRS
- **Foreign grantees**: Elect expenditure responsibility or obtain equivalency determination; comply with OFAC sanctions screening and anti-terrorism financing laws (USA PATRIOT Act)
- **Government funds**: If grantor is passing through federal awards, Uniform Guidance (2 CFR Part 200) applies and specific required award terms must be included [VERIFY applicable pass-through requirements with agency]
- **Lobbying**: Grant funds cannot support lobbying without express tracking; grantee's IRC § 501(h) election determines permissible limits
- **OMB Single Audit threshold**: Currently $750,000 in federal expenditures annually [VERIFY — threshold subject to regulatory update]
- **Proportionality**: Scale reporting and audit requirements to grant size; imposing full audit obligations on small grants is disproportionate and may deter qualified grantees
- **State law**: Some states impose charitable solicitation registration requirements on organizations receiving grants for activities within those states; advise grantee to confirm compliance
