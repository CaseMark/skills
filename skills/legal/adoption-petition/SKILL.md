# Petition for Adoption

Drafts a court-ready Petition for Adoption that satisfies statutory requirements and presents a best-interests case for finalizing the adoption.

## Prerequisites

1. **Adoption type classified** — stepparent, relative, agency, or independent (drives procedural path)
2. **Party information** — full legal names, DOBs, addresses for petitioner(s), child, and biological parents
3. **Governing statute identified** — state adoption code sections, local court rules, mandatory judicial council forms
4. **Consent / TPR status** — executed consents with dates, any termination orders (case number, court, date, grounds), or facts supporting statutory exceptions
5. **Home study report** — completed by licensed agency or qualified social worker
6. **Background checks** — criminal history and child abuse registry clearances for all adult household members
7. **Supporting documents** — child's birth certificate, marriage/divorce decrees, financial affidavits, medical/educational records

## Output Structure

### Document Skeleton

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| **Caption** | Court name (county + judicial district), case number placeholder, "In the Matter of the Adoption of [Child's Full Legal Name]" or jurisdiction-required style |
| **Document Title** | "PETITION FOR ADOPTION" |
| **Introduction** | Petitioner(s) identified by name; nature of petition; adoption type |
| **Party Identification** | Numbered paragraphs per party (see below) |
| **Jurisdictional Allegations** | Residency facts, statutory authority, personal jurisdiction |
| **Relationship & Caregiving History** | Placement date, circumstances, bond narrative |
| **Eligibility & Fitness** | Statutory criteria, background checks, home study, financial adequacy |
| **Consent / TPR Status** | Consent details or termination orders or exception facts |
| **Best Interests Analysis** | Evidence-based argument (see framework below) |
| **Prayer for Relief** | Specific requests |
| **Verification & Execution** | Oath, signature blocks, notary acknowledgment |
| **Exhibit List** | Labeled attachments referenced in body |

### Party Identification Checklist

**Each Petitioner:**
- [ ] Full legal name, DOB, current address
- [ ] Marital status, relationship to child
- [ ] Duration of residency in jurisdiction

**Child (Adoptee):**
- [ ] Current legal name, DOB, current residence
- [ ] Duration in petitioners' care
- [ ] Proposed new name (if any) — state both current and proposed

**Biological Parents / Legal Guardians:**
- [ ] Full legal names, last known addresses
- [ ] Status of parental rights (intact / voluntarily relinquished / involuntarily terminated)

### Jurisdictional Allegations

1. Cite exact state adoption code sections for this adoption type
2. Allege residency with specific dates showing petitioner or child meets statutory minimum (typically 6–12 months)
3. If Uniform Adoption Act applies, cite those provisions
4. Address personal jurisdiction over any party whose consent or TPR is at issue

### Consent & Termination Framework

| Scenario | Required Allegations |
|---|---|
| **Written consent obtained** | Date executed, identity of consenting party, manner of execution (witnessed / acknowledged before judicial officer), statutory sufficiency |
| **Prior TPR order** | Case number, issuing court, date, grounds for termination |
| **Consent exception claimed** | Specific statutory provision + particularized facts for each element (abandonment period, failure to support, unfitness) |
| **TPR pending** | Case number, court, current status, anticipated timeline |
| **Waiting period** | Allege elapsed time since consent/TPR meets statutory minimum |

### Best Interests Analysis Framework

Address each factor with specific, concrete facts:

- [ ] Emotional and psychological benefits of permanency
- [ ] Stability of home environment
- [ ] Educational opportunities and progress
- [ ] Medical/developmental needs and petitioners' capacity to meet them
- [ ] Quality of attachment (reference professional observations if available)
- [ ] Sibling relationships — preservation plan
- [ ] Child's preference (if age-appropriate; typically 12+ but varies by state)
- [ ] Integration into extended family and community
- [ ] Home study conclusions and professional recommendations

### Prayer for Relief

Request each item explicitly:

1. Grant petition; enter final decree of adoption establishing legal parent-child relationship
2. Order name change from [current name] to [proposed name] (if applicable)
3. Direct state registrar to issue new birth certificate reflecting adoptive parents
4. Seal adoption records per state law
5. Such other relief as the court deems just and proper

## Guidelines

- **Adoption type controls procedure** — verify which consent, waiting period, and eligibility rules apply to the specific adoption type before drafting
- **Residency requirements vary significantly** — confirm exact statutory minimum for the filing jurisdiction; do not assume 6 months
- **Consent execution defects are fatal** — verify witnessing/acknowledgment requirements precisely; a technically deficient consent can void proceedings
- **ICPC compliance** — if child is crossing state lines, Interstate Compact on the Placement of Children requirements must be satisfied before placement; flag if applicable
- **ICWA applicability** — if the child is or may be a member of or eligible for membership in an Indian tribe, the Indian Child Welfare Act (25 U.S.C. §§ 1901–1963) imposes heightened requirements for notice, active efforts, and qualified expert testimony; screen and flag early [VERIFY specific notice requirements with current ICWA regulations]
- **Use bracketed placeholders** for missing information: `[INSERT CASE NUMBER WHEN ASSIGNED]`, `[ATTACH EXHIBIT _]`
- **Do not fabricate** consent dates, court order numbers, or statutory citations; use `[VERIFY]` for any uncertain citation
- **Format per local rules** — margins, line numbering, font, pagination, and abbreviated caption on continuation pages as required by jurisdiction
