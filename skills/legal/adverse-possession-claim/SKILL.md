# Adverse Possession Claim

Drafts a litigation-ready adverse possession complaint seeking judicial recognition of ownership through long-term possession against the record title holder.

## Prerequisites

1. **Jurisdiction identified** — state-specific adverse possession statute located, statutory period confirmed (ranges 5–30 years by state), tax payment prerequisite determined
2. **Party information** — full legal names, addresses for claimant (adverse possessor) and respondent (record title holder)
3. **Property documents** — recorded deeds, surveys, title reports, plats, assessor's parcel number
4. **Possession evidence** — photographs (time-stamped across statutory period), utility bills, tax payment records, maintenance records, correspondence with record owner
5. **Witness information** — neighbors or others who observed possession over the statutory period

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption & Jurisdictional Foundation

| Component | Requirements |
|-----------|-------------|
| Court | Research whether jurisdiction uses general civil court, land court, or specialized property division |
| Subject matter jurisdiction | Cite specific statutory authority governing adverse possession |
| Venue | Establish based on property location |
| Parties | Claimant as plaintiff/petitioner; record title holder as defendant/respondent |
| Statutory period | State exact period with statutory citation |

### 2. Property Description

Include ALL of the following:

- [ ] Complete legal description from chain of title (lot/block, metes and bounds, or government survey)
- [ ] Street address and assessor's parcel number
- [ ] If partial parcel: delineate specific area with measurements, physical markers, survey references
- [ ] Description of structures, fences, improvements on claimed property
- [ ] Confirm description meets jurisdictional standards sufficient for court decree affecting title

### 3. Possessory Elements

Draft element-by-element proof with specific factual support:

| Element | What to Establish | Evidence Types |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Actual possession** | Physical occupancy and use — structures built/maintained, land cultivated/improved, activities conducted | Photos, building permits, receipts |
| **Open & notorious** | Visible to anyone including true owner making reasonable inspection — fencing, buildings, landscaping | Photos over time, neighbor testimony |
| **Continuous** | Unbroken throughout entire statutory period; explain any absences and why continuity preserved under state law | Timeline of occupancy, utility bills, tax records |
| **Exclusive** | Possessed as owner would, excluding record owner and general public | Testimony, locked gates/fences, no shared use |
| **Hostile/adverse** | Without owner's permission, under claim of right | Determine jurisdiction's standard: good faith belief vs. intentional trespass; no license or lease agreements |

### 4. Legal Framework

- [ ] Quote applicable statutory language with full citation
- [ ] Cite controlling case law interpreting the statute, prioritizing similar fact patterns
- [ ] Identify claim category: color of title vs. no color of title; good faith vs. bad faith (per jurisdiction)
- [ ] Preemptively address affirmative defenses:

| Defense | Rebuttal Approach |
|---------|-------------------|
| Permission/license | Show no agreement; hostile entry or expired permission |
| Owner disability | Confirm no tolling applies (minority, incapacity, imprisonment) |
| Interruption of possession | Timeline showing no meaningful break |
| Tax payment failure | Show payment records or that jurisdiction does not require it |

### 5. Evidentiary Support

**Exhibit list** — organize chronologically:

```
Exhibit A — Recorded deed(s) showing respondent's record title
Exhibit B — Survey/plat of claimed property
Exhibit C — Tax payment records (claimant), Years [X]–[Y]
Exhibit D — Utility bills in claimant's name, Years [X]–[Y]
Exhibit E — Photographs documenting possession [dated series]
Exhibit F — Correspondence with record owner (if any)
Exhibit G — Affidavit of Claimant
Exhibit H — Affidavit(s) of Witness(es)
```

**Affidavit requirements:**
- Personal knowledge basis stated
- Specific observations tied to possessory elements with concrete dates
- No legal conclusions — facts only
- Notarized
- Each witness establishes their opportunity to observe

### 6. Prayer for Relief

Request all of the following:

1. Declaratory judgment that claimant acquired title through adverse possession
2. Order quieting title in claimant's name, extinguishing respondent's record title
3. Order directing respondent to execute necessary deeds/documents to perfect title
4. Costs and attorney's fees (if permitted by statute or equity)
5. Injunctive relief against interference with possession pending resolution
6. Such other equitable relief as the court deems just

## Guidelines

- **Verify statutory period and elements** for the specific state — do not assume uniform requirements across jurisdictions
- **Tax payment** is a statutory prerequisite in some states (e.g., California requires 5 years of tax payment under CCP § 325 [VERIFY]); confirm before drafting
- **Color of title** claims may have shorter statutory periods — identify and apply if available
- **Tacking** — if claimant relies on predecessor's possession, establish privity between successive possessors
- **Government land** — most jurisdictions prohibit adverse possession against government entities; confirm before proceeding
- **All legal citations** must conform to Bluebook or local citation standards; flag any unverified citations with [VERIFY]
- **Tone**: acknowledge seriousness of divesting record title while confidently asserting legal and factual basis
- Every factual assertion must be tied to a specific exhibit or witness; every legal conclusion grounded in cited authority
