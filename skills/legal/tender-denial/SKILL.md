# Tender Letter Denial

Drafts a formal denial of a tendered defense/indemnification demand, building a defensible record for potential coverage litigation.

## Prerequisites

1. **Tender letter** — the demand being denied, with date and sender identified
2. **Governing agreement** — contract, policy, lease, or other document containing the indemnification provision
3. **Underlying complaint or claim** — allegations, parties, damages sought, legal theories
4. **Supporting evidence** — correspondence, inspection reports, maintenance records, expert opinions, photos bearing on scope of indemnity obligation
5. **Client identification** — full legal name of denying party and counsel information

## Output Structure

### Letter Format

| Element | Requirements |
|---------|-------------|
| Letterhead | Firm name, address, contact info |
| Date | Current date |
| Delivery method | Per contractual notice requirements (certified mail, email to designated addresses) |
| Addressee | Person who signed tender letter or appropriate representative, full title and address |
| Re: line | Case caption, claim number, contract/policy number, date of loss |

### Section 1: Denial Statement

- Identify counsel and client
- Acknowledge tender letter receipt by specific date
- State denial unambiguously: *"This letter constitutes [Client]'s formal denial of your tender of defense and indemnification dated [date] in the matter of [caption]."*
- Tone: firm, measured, professional

### Section 2: Substantive Analysis

Build layered grounds for denial. Quote the exact indemnification language, then address applicable grounds:

| Ground | Analysis Required |
|--------|------------------|
| **Scope exclusion** | Claim falls outside "arising out of," "caused by," or "in connection with" language as interpreted in the jurisdiction |
| **Indemnitee's own negligence** | Sole/active negligence or willful misconduct excluded by provision or public policy |
| **Causal break** | Indemnitee's independent actions/decisions/omissions sever causal chain |
| **Specific exclusions** | Match complaint allegations to contractual carve-outs (IP, employment, environmental, professional E&O, law violations, third-party breach) |
| **Anti-indemnity statutes** | Provision unenforceable under applicable state anti-indemnity statute [VERIFY specific statute for jurisdiction] |
| **Control/responsibility** | Evidence showing indemnitee had exclusive control, prior notice, or made independent modifications |

Reference specific factual evidence for each ground: complaint allegations, communications, reports, expert opinions.

Apply jurisdiction-specific contract interpretation principles:
- Construe ambiguities against drafter
- Give effect to parties' reasonable expectations
- Read contract as a whole; give meaning to every provision

### Section 3: Procedural Deficiencies

Address as independent denial grounds where applicable:

- [ ] Untimely notice (specify contractual deadline and actual notice date)
- [ ] Missing required documentation (complaint, demand, proof of loss)
- [ ] Wrong recipient or delivery method
- [ ] Failure to cooperate with investigation/defense
- [ ] Failure to satisfy conditions precedent

### Section 4: Conditional Reconsideration

> "Should you possess additional information, documentation, or legal authority that materially alters this analysis, please submit it to this office within [14–21] days. Unless and until such information demonstrates the claim falls within the scope of the indemnification provision under [Agreement], [Client] maintains its denial and will not assume defense or indemnification obligations."

### Section 5: Reservation of Rights

Explicitly reserve:
- All defenses (late notice, failure to cooperate, breach, failure to mitigate, unauthorized settlement)
- Affirmative claims (reverse indemnification, contribution, breach of contract)
- Enforceability challenges (lack of clear/unequivocal language for own-negligence indemnity, anti-indemnity statutes, unconscionability)

### Section 6: Closing

- Professional signature block (name, title, bar admissions, firm, phone, email)
- cc: notation only if strategically appropriate — consider privilege, work product, and settlement implications before copying plaintiff's counsel or co-defendants

## Guidelines

- **Length**: 2–4 pages depending on complexity
- **Tone**: Professional, confident, non-inflammatory. This letter may become an exhibit in coverage litigation.
- **Do not** use hostile, sarcastic, or dismissive language
- **Do** cite specific contract provisions verbatim
- **Do** reference concrete evidence (dates, documents, communications) rather than conclusory assertions
- **Do** build multiple independent grounds for denial so the position survives if one argument fails
- **Verify** all statutory citations and jurisdictional interpretation rules before finalizing
- **Proofread** for consistency in party names, dates, document titles, and contract references throughout
- **Privilege**: Consider whether broader distribution (cc list) may waive attorney-client privilege or work product protection
