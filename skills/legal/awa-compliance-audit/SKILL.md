# AWA Compliance Audit (USDA/APHIS)

## Why This Skill Exists

USDA/APHIS inspection reports create a discoverable paper trail that drives enforcement actions, license revocations, and reputational damage. Facilities often treat citations in isolation rather than identifying the systemic patterns (recurring housing deficiencies, lapsed veterinary oversight, record gaps) that trigger escalation from Indirect to Direct to Critical findings. Without a structured audit that maps each citation to verified CFR text, analyzes recurrence, and produces a corrective action plan with proof artifacts, facilities remain reactive — and counsel cannot accurately assess enforcement exposure. This skill produces an enforcement-aware audit tied to the actual regulatory text.

---

## Checkpoint A: Pre-Draft Intake (Mandatory)

Ask every time unless the user says "use defaults" or "just draft." Gather:

1. **Facility status** — USDA license/registration class and number, regulated activities, covered species, site locations
2. **Inspection/enforcement record** — all Form 7002 reports (3–5 years), warnings, investigations, settlements, ALJ decisions
3. **Operating evidence** — PVC signed by Attending Veterinarian, SOPs, treatment logs, A&D records (Forms 7001/7020), ID system, environmental/sanitation logs, training records, staffing, incident reports, maintenance logs, enclosure specs
4. **Research materials** (if applicable) — IACUC minutes, protocols, Search for Alternatives documentation
5. **Governing authorities** — AWA statute (7 U.S.C. § 2131 et seq.) [VERIFY], 9 C.F.R. Parts 1–3 (species subparts) [VERIFY], APHIS guidance if cited [VERIFY]

**If the user doesn't respond**, apply and clearly label these defaults: exhibitor/dealer facility class; 3-year inspection window; general compliance audit scope; all species present at facility. Flag missing items in an "Open Items / Needed Inputs" section.

### Documents to Request

If any of these are missing, flag explicitly and proceed with labeled assumptions:

- All Form 7002 inspection reports (minimum 3 years)
- Current PVC with AV signature
- Acquisition and disposition records
- Current SOPs for husbandry, sanitation, veterinary care
- Enclosure specifications and maintenance records

Map every cited item from Form 7002 reports to verified CFR text and facility evidence.

## Step 3: Severity and Pattern Analysis

Classify each issue by USDA NCI level and identify systemic patterns.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|

- **NCI levels**: Indirect, Direct, Critical [VERIFY source if not in report]
- **Internal risk tiers**: Low, Medium, High (explicitly internal, not USDA classifications)
- Identify recurrence by CFR section and time window
- Flag patterns that suggest escalation risk (same section cited 3+ times, progression from Indirect to Direct)

## Step 4: Program Deep-Dive

Evaluate each core compliance program against CFR requirements:

- **PVC/AV Oversight**: verify authority, signatures, visit cadence, treatment documentation [VERIFY 9 C.F.R. § 2.40]
- **Species Subparts**: confirm standards for each species; avoid applying general standards to species-specific rules
- **Housing/Sanitation/Environment**: structural integrity, drainage, ventilation, water, feed, pest control
- **Records/ID/Traceability**: acquisition/disposition, identification, health certifications [VERIFY 9 C.F.R. §§ 2.75, 2.78]
- **IACUC** (research): protocol review, semiannual inspection, alternatives search [VERIFY 9 C.F.R. § 2.31]
- **Transport/Carriers**: handling, containment, carrier obligations [VERIFY]

For each program area, note: compliant elements, deficiencies, documentation gaps, and recommended improvements.

## Step 5: Corrective Action Plan (CAP)

### Required Deliverable: CAP Table

Prioritize by:
1. Animal welfare risk (immediate harm potential first)
2. Enforcement escalation risk (recurring Direct/Critical findings)
3. Documentation gaps that could become adverse findings

Every corrective action must include a specific proof artifact (photo, log entry, signed SOP, training record) that demonstrates compliance.

## Quality Audit

- Every legal requirement tied to verified eCFR text with CFR cite
- Every factual statement tied to a report, record, or interview (labeled)
- Gaps in records explicitly stated — never papered over
- CAP includes owners, dates, and proof artifacts for every finding
- Language avoids admissions unless instructed (use "USDA cited noncompliance with…" not "in violation of")
- NCI severity classifications sourced or labeled [VERIFY]
- Pattern analysis identifies recurrence by CFR section and time window
- Species-specific standards not conflated with general standards
- State/local overlay noted where stricter than AWA
- No invented USDA guidance, penalty ranges, or enforcement thresholds

---

## Guidelines

- Verify all CFR citations at `https://www.ecfr.gov/`; mark any uncertain cite as `[VERIFY]`
- Do not invent USDA guidance, penalty ranges, or enforcement thresholds; label as `[VERIFY]` if referenced
- Treat inspection reports as discoverable; use precise, non-conclusory language
- Avoid stating "in violation" unless directed; use "USDA cited noncompliance with…"
- If mixed operations create ambiguity, state the ambiguity and its compliance impact
- If animal welfare risk is imminent, flag for immediate action and expert consultation
- Distinguish AWA scope from state/local overlays; note that stricter state laws may apply
- Preserve privilege: label as attorney work product when appropriate; limit distribution
- Output requires attorney review before external use

**Required disclaimer on every output:**

> THIS AUDIT REQUIRES INDEPENDENT ATTORNEY VERIFICATION OF ALL CFR CITATIONS, REGULATORY INTERPRETATIONS, AND FACTUAL ASSERTIONS, AND DOES NOT CONSTITUTE LEGAL ADVICE.
