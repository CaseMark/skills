# Technology Tutorial for Court

Drafts a neutral, non-argumentative slide-deck tutorial that gives the court foundational technical knowledge for a patent case.

## Prerequisites

1. **Patent specification(s)** — full text including claims, figures, and prosecution history
2. **Case caption** — parties, case number, court, judge
3. **Technical expert reports** (if available) — for accuracy cross-reference
4. **Uploaded case documents** — prior art references, technical literature, deposition excerpts on technology

## Output Structure

Generate a structured document suitable for slide-deck conversion. Each section maps to a slide group. Keep slides to ≤5–6 bullet phrases with dominant visuals; use speaker notes for verbal elaboration.

### Slide Sequence

| # | Section | Content |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Title Slide** | Full case caption, "Technology Tutorial," presenting party, date |
| 2 | **Purpose & Agenda** | State neutral/educational purpose; list patent number(s); outline topic roadmap |
| 3–5 | **The Problem & Prior Art** | State of the art before the invention; prior attempted solutions and their shortcomings; at least one everyday analogy; simplified diagram of the problem |
| 6–9 | **How the Invention Works** | Break invention into fundamental components; step-by-step operational walkthrough; extract/simplify patent figures; include flowcharts or block diagrams with annotations |
| 10–11 | **Key Technical Concepts** | Define each essential term: technical term → plain-language definition → analogy (where helpful); build from foundational to complex |
| 12 | **Glossary** | Alphabetized quick-reference table of all terms used in the tutorial |
| 13 | **Closing** | Invite questions; reinforce educational (non-adversarial) purpose |

### Slide Formatting Rules

- Each slide: clear title, ≤6 short bullet phrases, ≥1 visual element
- Mark diagram placements as `[DIAGRAM: description of what to depict]`
- Include `[SPEAKER NOTE: ...]` blocks for verbal guidance on complex concepts
- Target total presentation length: 30–60 minutes

## Analogy & Diagram Requirements

- Provide at least **one everyday analogy** per major technical concept (e.g., data compression → folding clothes to fit a suitcase)
- Simplified diagrams must include labels and legends — avoid raw patent schematics without annotation
- Build concepts incrementally; each new idea must rest on previously explained material

## Glossary Format

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| {Technical term} | {Plain-language definition, 1–2 sentences} |

Alphabetize. Include every specialized term that appears in the tutorial.

## Guidelines

- **Strict neutrality** — never characterize the invention as "innovative," "superior," or "groundbreaking"; never disparage prior art
- **No claim language** — use functional descriptions, not patent claim terms or legal jargon
- **No advocacy** — this is education, not argument; do not preview infringement or validity positions
- **Assume no technical background** — but respect judicial intelligence; explain, don't condescend
- **Cross-reference accuracy** — verify every technical assertion against patent specification and case file source materials
- **Trace all assertions** — every technical statement must be traceable to the patent spec or an authoritative technical source in the record
- **Comply with local rules** — format title slide and case caption per applicable court rules
