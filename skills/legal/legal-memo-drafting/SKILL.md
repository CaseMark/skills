# Legal Memo & Brief Drafting

Drafts structured legal documents using research, analysis, and persuasive reasoning.

## Prerequisites

1. **Assignment** — legal question, audience (memo vs. filing), format constraints
2. **Research** — completed research with relevant authorities
3. **Facts** — relevant facts organized chronologically

## Workflow

### 1. Structure
**Memos:** Question → Short Answer → Facts → Discussion → Conclusion.
**Briefs:** Introduction → Facts → Argument (by issue) → Conclusion.

### 2. Draft
Facts: neutral, chronological, cite record. Analysis: IRAC per issue. Citations: proper format, pin cites.

### 3. Strengthen
Lead with strongest arguments. Address adverse authority. Use conclusory topic sentences. Ensure logical flow.

### 4. Polish
Verify citations. Check court rule compliance. Consistent terminology. Store in vault.

## Pitfalls
- State your conclusion upfront, then support it
- Cite everything — unsupported assertions destroy credibility
- Address counterarguments — ignoring them doesn't make them go away
- Match tone to audience — memos are objective, briefs advocate
