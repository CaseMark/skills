# Motion for Conditional Certification (FLSA)

Drafts a persuasive Motion for Conditional Certification under 29 U.S.C. § 216(b), seeking court-authorized notice to similarly situated employees in a wage and hour collective action.

## Prerequisites

1. **Named plaintiff declarations** — attesting to job duties, pay practices, and knowledge of similarly situated coworkers
2. **Proposed collective definition** — specific job titles, time period, geographic scope
3. **Evidence of common policy/practice** — handbooks, job descriptions, pay records, org charts, centralized decision-making proof
4. **Proposed notice and consent forms** — drafted as exhibits
5. **Local rules review** — page limits, formatting, caption requirements, and any judge-specific conditional certification preferences

## Output Structure

### Caption & Introduction

- Full court name, case number, party designations, motion title per local rules
- Identify named plaintiff(s) by name and job title
- Define proposed collective with specificity: role, employer, date range
- Frame violations as collective in nature (company-wide policies, uniform practices)
- State this seeks **first-stage conditional certification only** — modest factual showing

### Factual Background

Present facts establishing **systematic, uniform violations** across the proposed collective:

| Element | What to Include |
|---|---|
| Business operations | Defendant's structure, locations, relevant departments |
| Common policies | Handbook provisions, pay structures, classification decisions |
| Specific violations | Misclassification, off-the-clock work, overtime failures, rate miscalculation |
| Uniformity indicators | Standardized job descriptions, centralized pay decisions, common working conditions |
| Plaintiff knowledge | Declaration testimony re: personal experience AND observation of coworkers |

Emphasize pattern over isolated incidents. Cite to declarations, complaint allegations, and any available discovery.

### Legal Standard

Present the two-stage certification framework:

1. **Stage 1 (Conditional Certification)** — lenient standard; modest factual showing that plaintiffs are similarly situated. Court authorizes notice. Cite *Hoffmann-La Roche Inc. v. Sperling*, 493 U.S. 165 (1989).
2. **Stage 2 (Decertification)** — rigorous scrutiny after discovery; court may decertify if record shows plaintiffs are not similarly situated.

**Circuit-specific standards** — identify and apply the controlling test:

| Circuit | Standard | Key Case |
|---|---|---|
| Most circuits | "Modest factual showing" / "reasonable basis" | Various |
| Fifth Circuit | Heightened: whether members are identified, common policy exists, others desire to opt in | *Swales v. KLLM Transport Services*, 985 F.3d 430 (5th Cir. 2021) |

Stress that conditional certification is liberally granted because it merely permits notice — the court retains full decertification authority.

### Argument: Similarly Situated

Organize around jurisdiction's factors, typically:

- [ ] Same or similar job titles and descriptions
- [ ] Same or similar pay policies and practices
- [ ] Similar job duties and working conditions
- [ ] Violations stem from common policy, plan, or practice

For each factor: apply specific evidence to legal standard. Preempt defenses:

| Common Defense | Response |
|---|---|
| Different supervisors/locations | Immaterial where centralized policies control compensation |
| Variation in specific duties | Minor differences don't defeat similarity at Stage 1 |
| Individual circumstances differ | Stage 1 does not require identical situations — common nucleus of tasks suffices |

Argue conditional certification promotes judicial efficiency over numerous individual suits.

### Relief Requested

Request the following specific relief:

1. Conditional certification of the defined collective
2. Approval of proposed notice and consent forms (attached as exhibits)
3. Order compelling defendant to produce contact information (names, addresses, emails, phone numbers) within a specified timeframe
4. Opt-in deadline of **60–90 days** from notice distribution
5. **Equitable tolling** of the statute of limitations from filing date through notice distribution
6. Notice methods: first-class mail + email; follow-up for returned mail

Describe proposed notice as neutral in tone, accurately describing the litigation, explaining opt-in process, and advising right to independent counsel.

### Conclusion

Concise restatement: modest factual showing met, collective members similarly situated under common FLSA violations, certification promotes judicial efficiency and FLSA's remedial purpose.

### Signature Block & Certificate of Service

- Attorney name, bar number, firm, address, phone, fax, email
- Comply with local e-filing signature rules
- Certificate of service with date, method, and counsel served

## Guidelines

- **Standard of proof**: Emphasize the deliberately low threshold — this is notice-stage, not merits
- **Collective definition**: Neither overbroad (encompassing dissimilar roles) nor unduly narrow (excluding proper members) — courts may modify
- **All factual assertions** must cite to record evidence (declarations, complaint, exhibits, discovery)
- **Citation format**: Bluebook unless local rules specify otherwise
- **Exhibits**: Attach proposed notice form, consent form, and plaintiff declarations
- **Judge research**: Review assigned judge's prior conditional certification rulings for preferences on collective scope, notice periods, and tolling
- **Comply with** local page limits, margin/font rules, and any meet-and-confer requirements before filing
- Party role: Plaintiff-side motion
