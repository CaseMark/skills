# Shareholder Rights Plan

Produce an execution-ready rights plan package with board-ready rationale and compliance steps.

## Prerequisites

1. Charter and bylaws (including blank check preferred stock authority and vote thresholds)
2. Cap table and outstanding equity/derivative positions
3. Board process and resolution template
4. Proposed Rights Agent and fee terms
5. Trading price range and peer rights plan comparables
6. Incorporation jurisdiction and listed exchange rules
7. Known large holders and current activism or inbound bid context

## Output Structure / Process

### 1) Fact Intake Checklist

- [ ] Incorporation state corporate law standards (e.g., Unocal/Revlon for Delaware)
- [ ] Anti-takeover statute applicability (e.g., DGCL 203)
- [ ] Charter/bylaw constraints on rights or preferred issuance
- [ ] Exchange rules and proxy advisor policies [VERIFY]
- [ ] SEC reporting obligations and timeline (Form 8-K within 4 business days) [VERIFY]
- [ ] Existing defensive measures and their thresholds
- [ ] Comparable rights plan terms in industry peers

### 2) Key Plan Terms Table

| Term | Default Range | Decision Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Trigger threshold (Acquiring Person) | 10% to 20% | Adjust for ownership concentration and activism risk |
| Distribution Date | 10 business days after trigger or tender offer commencement | Board can extend/shorten by amendment |
| Exercise Price | 2x to 3x current market price | Drives dilution severity |
| Plan duration | 1 to 10 years | Align with governance expectations |
| Redemption price | $0.001 per Right | Nominal; confirm economics |
| Exchange ratio | 1 share per Right | Subject to adjustments |

### 3) Rights Agreement Drafting Outline

- Preamble and parties: company legal name, state, principal office; Rights Agent identity
- Recitals: board fiduciary rationale, shareholder value protection, fair value objective
- Definitions section:
  - Acquiring Person: threshold, affiliates/associates aggregation, exceptions
  - Beneficial Ownership: reference SEC Rule 13d-3
  - Distribution Date, Flip-In Event, Flip-Over Event, Expiration Date
  - Business Day, Close of Business, Common Stock, Exercise Price
- Rights issuance:
  - One Right per outstanding share, record date
  - Rights attach to common stock until Distribution Date
- Preferred stock terms (Series A Junior Participating):
  - Economic equivalence to common stock
  - Dividend, voting, liquidation parity
- Triggering mechanics:
  - 10 business days after public announcement of threshold ownership
  - 10 business days after tender/exchange offer commencement
- Exceptions:
  - Inadvertent acquirer cure period
  - Passive investor exception with certification
  - Company repurchase-induced ownership increase
- Flip-In provisions:
  - Rights (other than Acquiring Person) buy common at 50% discount
  - Acquiring Person Rights become void
  - Exercise procedures and payment methods
  - Fractional share cash-in-lieu
- Flip-Over provisions:
  - Post-Flip-In merger/asset sale triggers right to buy acquirer shares at 50% discount
  - Acquirer assumption or supplemental agreement required
  - Authorized shares requirement and covenant to increase if needed
- Redemption and exchange:
  - Pre-Distribution redemption at nominal price
  - Post-Flip-In exchange for common at set ratio
- Amendments:
  - Broad pre-trigger amendment authority
  - Post-trigger no adverse amendments to non-Acquiring holders
- Termination and expiration:
  - Fixed expiry date, redemption, exchange, or board-approved merger
  - Optional annual review covenant
- Rights Agent:
  - Limited duties, reliance on company instructions
  - Standard of care, indemnification, resignation/termination, successor
- Governing law and forum:
  - State of incorporation law
  - Exclusive forum clause for internal corporate claims

### 4) Certificate of Designations Outline

- Class/series name and number of shares
- Dividend parity with common
- Voting parity with common
- Liquidation preference formula
- Conversion/exchange mechanics tied to Rights
- Anti-dilution adjustments

### 5) Board Memo Outline

- Fiduciary duty framework (Unocal/Revlon)
- Threat analysis and proportionality
- Market comparables and governance considerations
- Recommended plan terms and alternatives

### 6) Disclosure Package Outline

- Form 8-K summary and exhibit list [VERIFY]
- Press release key points and FAQ
- Stock exchange notice checklist [VERIFY]

### 7) Post-Adoption Implementation Checklist

- [ ] File Certificate of Designations with Secretary of State
- [ ] File Form 8-K with rights agreement and exhibits [VERIFY]
- [ ] Notify exchange and transfer agent [VERIFY]
- [ ] Issue press release and update investor materials
- [ ] Train internal contacts and Rights Agent on exercise/redemption workflows

## Guidelines

- Build a fiduciary record: board rationale and proportionality are essential (Unocal/Revlon).
- Align thresholds with ownership concentration and existing charter defenses.
- Ensure authorized shares are sufficient for flip-in/exchange and flip-over scenarios.
- Confirm no charter/bylaw constraints on rights or preferred issuance.
- Avoid adopting or extending plans contrary to exchange or proxy advisor policies [VERIFY].
- Use bracketed placeholders for client-specific inputs: [COMPANY NAME], [STATE], [THRESHOLD], [EXERCISE PRICE], [EXPIRATION DATE], [RIGHTS AGENT].
