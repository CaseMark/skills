# Debtor-in-Possession Financing Motion

Drafts a court-ready motion seeking approval of post-petition financing under the Bankruptcy Code.

## Prerequisites

1. Case caption data: court, district, case number, debtor legal name(s), chapter, judge, hearing date/time, and notice type.
2. DIP facility terms: lender identity, facility size, pricing, maturity, draw conditions, covenants, milestones, defaults.
3. Budget and cash flow: 13-week forecast and sources/uses tied to the requested facility.
4. Capital structure: existing secured debt, lien priorities, collateral description, cash collateral status.
5. Adequate protection package: proposed replacement liens, cash payments, equity cushion, reporting.
6. Financing process: marketing efforts, alternative proposals, and reasons unsecured credit is unavailable.
7. Exhibits: term sheet or credit agreement, budget, declaration(s), proposed order.
8. Local rules and judge procedures: emergency relief standards, notice and service requirements.

## Output Structure / Process

Use the following structure and fill with case-specific facts and exhibit citations.

```text
I. Preliminary Statement / Relief Sought
II. Jurisdiction, Venue, and Statutory Authority
III. Background and Need for Financing
IV. Terms of Proposed DIP Facility
V. Adequate Protection for Prepetition Secured Parties
VI. Legal Standard and Argument
VII. Notice and Hearing
VIII. Conclusion
```

### Relief Sought Matrix

| Requested Relief | Statute | Required Showing | Evidence / Exhibit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Superpriority administrative expense | 11 U.S.C. § 364(c)(1) | Unsecured credit unavailable under § 503(b)(1); terms reasonable | Financing process declaration; term sheet |
| Lien on unencumbered property | 11 U.S.C. § 364(c)(2) | Same as above; identification of unencumbered collateral | Collateral schedule; lien analysis |
| Junior lien on encumbered property | 11 U.S.C. § 364(c)(3) | Same as above; priority structure | UCC/lien summary; intercreditor terms |
| Priming lien | 11 U.S.C. § 364(d) | Inability to obtain credit otherwise; adequate protection of existing lienholders | Adequate protection package; valuation data |

### Required Findings Checklist

- Debtor cannot obtain unsecured credit allowable as an administrative expense under 11 U.S.C. § 503(b)(1).
- DIP terms are fair, reasonable, negotiated at arm's length, and in the best interests of the estate.
- Adequate protection of existing secured creditors (if their liens are subordinated or primed).
- Financing is necessary to preserve going concern value and maximize recoveries.

### Adequate Protection Menu

| Tool | Typical Use | Data Needed |
|---|---|---|
| Replacement liens | Collateral base stability | Collateral description; lien order |
| Periodic cash payments | Cash collateral use or priming | Budget line items; payment schedule |
| Equity cushion | Oversecured collateral | Valuation support |
| Reporting package | Ongoing monitoring | KPI and variance reporting format |
| Carve-out | Professional fees | Carve-out amount; beneficiaries |

### Fact Sections to Include

- Business overview and filing context.
- Liquidity crisis and immediate cash needs.
- Financing marketing process and alternatives.
- Summary of key DIP terms and conditions precedent.
- Impact on existing secured parties and protections offered.

### Exhibit List

- DIP credit agreement or term sheet.
- Budget and 13-week cash flow forecast.
- Declaration(s) of CFO/CRO/financial advisor.
- Proposed order.
- Lien and capital structure summary (if not included in declarations).

### Proposed Order Checklist

- Approved facility size and use of proceeds.
- Authorized liens and priorities.
- Adequate protection terms.
- Reporting, milestones, and default remedies.
- Final hearing scheduling and interim relief, if applicable.
- Service and notice findings.

## Guidelines

- Confirm local bankruptcy rules and judge-specific procedures; adjust notice, emergency relief, and hearing mechanics accordingly.
- Do not request priming liens unless the adequate protection record is concrete and documented.
- If roll-ups or cross-collateralization are requested, flag explicitly and provide statutory and case authority; mark uncertain citations with `[VERIFY]`.
- Use precise statutory references for all relief requests; mark uncertain citations with `[VERIFY]`.
- Keep all factual assertions tied to declarations or exhibits.
