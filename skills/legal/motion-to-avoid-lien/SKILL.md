# Motion to Avoid Lien Under 11 U.S.C. § 522(f)

Drafts a motion to avoid a lien that impairs a debtor's exemptions in bankruptcy, with full statutory analysis and impairment calculation.

## Prerequisites

1. **Bankruptcy case information** — case number, chapter (7/11/13), filing date, court district/division, assigned judge
2. **Property details** — description (legal description or address), type (real/personal), current fair market value, appraisals if available
3. **Lien information** — lienholder name, lien type (judicial or nonpossessory NPMSI), originating judgment/agreement, recording date, lien amount
4. **Senior encumbrances** — all liens senior to the challenged lien with amounts
5. **Exemption claimed** — specific state or federal exemption statute, dollar amount claimed, as listed in Schedule C
6. **Local rules** — court-specific formatting, notice periods, hearing requirements

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Court | Full bankruptcy court name with district and division |
| Case number | Complete case number |
| Chapter | 7, 11, or 13 designation |
| Debtor name | As it appears on the petition |
| Title | "Motion to Avoid Lien Under 11 U.S.C. § 522(f)" |
| Judge | If required by local rules |

Format per local rules (font, spacing, additional fields).

### 2. Introduction

State in 1–2 paragraphs:
- Moving party (debtor or DIP)
- Specific lien challenged and lienholder (full legal name)
- Property subject to lien (with legal description or address)
- Statutory basis: § 522(f)(1) (judicial liens) **or** § 522(f)(2) (nonpossessory NPMSI)
- Core premise: lien impairs an exemption to which debtor is entitled

### 3. Factual Background

Present chronologically:

1. Bankruptcy filing date and chapter
2. Property description, type, and fair market value
3. Origin of the lien — when/how it attached (judgment date, security agreement date, recording)
4. Debtor's claimed exemption — cite specific statute, dollar amount from Schedule C
5. **Impairment calculation:**

```
  Lien to be avoided:                    $________
+ All other liens on property:           $________
+ Debtor's claimed exemption:            $________
= Total:                                 $________
- Fair market value of property:         $________
= Impairment amount:                     $________
```

If Total > FMV, the lien impairs the exemption. See § 522(f)(2)(A).

### 4. Legal Argument

**For judicial liens — § 522(f)(1):**

Establish all three elements:

| Element | Standard |
|---------|----------|
| Judicial lien | Meets definition under 11 U.S.C. § 101(36) — lien obtained by judgment, levy, sequestration, or other legal/equitable process |
| Impairment | Sum of (lien + all other liens + exemption) exceeds FMV per § 522(f)(2)(A) |
| Valid exemption | Debtor entitled to exemption under § 522(b) (state or federal) |

**For nonpossessory NPMSI — § 522(f)(2):**

Lien must encumber one of:
- Household furnishings/goods, wearing apparel, appliances, books, animals, crops, musical instruments, or jewelry held primarily for personal/family/household use
- Tools of the trade (implements, professional books, or tools of the debtor or a dependent)
- Professionally prescribed health aids

**Case law support:**
- Cite binding circuit and district precedent on impairment calculation, property valuation method, and treatment of the specific lien type
- Address likely counterarguments (exemption validity challenges, valuation disputes, lien classification)

### 5. Prayer for Relief

Request the court to:
1. Avoid the lien in its entirety (or to the extent it impairs the exemption)
2. Declare the lien void and of no further force or effect
3. Grant other relief as the court deems just and proper
4. If appropriate: request expedited consideration or specific hearing date

### 6. Exhibits and Supporting Documentation

Attach and reference:
- [ ] Judgment or security agreement creating the lien
- [ ] Evidence of lien recording/perfection
- [ ] Debtor's Schedule C (claimed exemptions)
- [ ] Property appraisal or valuation evidence
- [ ] Debtor's declaration/affidavit authenticating factual assertions (if applicable)

### 7. Certificate of Service

- Certify service on: lienholder, bankruptcy trustee, all parties in interest per local rules
- Specify: method (ECF, mail, personal delivery), date, names and addresses of all parties served
- Comply with FRBP 7004, 9014, and applicable local rules for contested matters
- Signed by attorney (with bar number) or pro se debtor with full contact information

## Guidelines

- Verify the impairment calculation is arithmetically correct — courts reject motions with math errors
- Confirm the exemption is properly claimed on Schedule C before filing; if not, amend schedules first
- Check whether the jurisdiction uses state or federal exemptions (opt-out states) — this determines which exemption statute to cite
- For partially impaired liens, specify the exact dollar amount to be avoided vs. the amount that survives
- All citations in Bluebook format; mark any unverified citations with [VERIFY]
- Format per local court rules (margins, font, line spacing, page limits)
- Ensure consistency between the motion's factual assertions and the bankruptcy schedules
- Notice periods vary by district — verify local rule requirements before filing
