# Cross-Jurisdictional Compliance Review

Analyzes agreements against the legal requirements of multiple jurisdictions to identify conflicts and recommend region-specific amendments.

## Prerequisites

- Contract or policy document to review
- List of applicable jurisdictions (countries, states, or regions)
- Reference materials for jurisdiction-specific requirements (data privacy, employment, consumer protection)

## Workflow

### 1. Map Applicable Jurisdictions

Identify every jurisdiction where the contract will be performed, enforced, or where affected parties reside. Include sub-national requirements (e.g., state-level privacy laws, provincial employment standards).

### 2. Extract Jurisdiction-Sensitive Provisions

Flag clauses that vary by jurisdiction: governing law, dispute resolution, data processing and transfer, employment terms, limitation of liability caps, indemnification scope, and consumer protection disclosures.

### 3. Run Compliance Analysis Per Jurisdiction

For each jurisdiction, check flagged provisions against local requirements. Note: mandatory provisions that cannot be contracted around, prohibited clauses, required disclosures, and registration or filing obligations.

### 4. Generate Conflict Matrix

Produce a matrix showing each provision against each jurisdiction with status: Compliant, Requires Amendment, or Prohibited. Include the specific local law reference for each conflict.

### 5. Draft Jurisdiction-Specific Riders

For each conflict, draft amendment language or jurisdiction-specific addenda that bring the agreement into compliance while preserving commercial intent.

## Pitfalls

- Assuming a governing law clause overrides mandatory local law protections
- Missing sub-national requirements (CCPA vs federal, provincial vs national)
- Treating EU member states as having identical requirements under GDPR
