## AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES

### First Affirmative Defense — Comparative Fault
Plaintiff's own negligence, including failure to observe reasonably apparent conditions on the premises, is a proximate cause of her alleged injuries and bars or reduces recovery under applicable state comparative-fault principles.

### Second Affirmative Defense — Open and Obvious Condition
The condition, if any, was open and obvious, such that an invitee exercising reasonable care would have avoided it.

### Third Affirmative Defense — No Notice
Defendant had no actual or constructive notice of the alleged condition prior to the incident.

### Fourth Affirmative Defense — Pre-Existing Condition
Plaintiff's claimed injuries are attributable in whole or in part to pre-existing conditions unrelated to the alleged incident.

### Fifth Affirmative Defense — Failure to Mitigate
Plaintiff has failed to mitigate damages by failing to follow prescribed medical treatment and by seeking excessive or unnecessary care.

### Sixth Affirmative Defense — Collateral Source
Defendant reserves its right to offset recovery by collateral-source payments to the extent permitted by law.

### Seventh Affirmative Defense — Reservation
Defendant reserves the right to assert additional affirmative defenses as discovery proceeds.
