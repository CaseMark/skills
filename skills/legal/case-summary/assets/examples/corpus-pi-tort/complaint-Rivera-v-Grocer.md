Plaintiff Maria Rivera, by and through counsel, alleges as follows:

## I. PARTIES AND JURISDICTION

1. Plaintiff Maria Rivera is an adult resident of Middle County, State of Example. Date of birth: March 3, 1978. Occupation: licensed hairstylist.

2. Defendant BestValue Grocer, Inc. is a Delaware corporation doing business at 1847 Main Avenue, Middletown, Example State, operating Store No. 0147. BestValue is a business invitee operator under state premises law.

3. This Court has jurisdiction pursuant to state long-arm statute and general jurisdiction over BestValue by virtue of continuous business operations.

## II. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

4. On February 15, 2024, at approximately 2:38 PM, Plaintiff entered BestValue Store No. 0147 as a customer/business invitee.

5. Near the produce section's rear cooler, a transparent liquid had accumulated on the tile floor. The spill spanned approximately 18 inches in diameter.

6. No warning cone, signage, or mat had been placed around the spill.

7. Plaintiff, while approaching the cooler, slipped on the liquid, fell backward, and struck the floor with her right hip, lower back, and head.

8. BestValue employees — including the store manager on duty, Victor Chen — responded within approximately two minutes. EMS was called at 2:43 PM.

9. Store surveillance video captured the incident. Surveillance footage preserved by BestValue pursuant to Plaintiff's February 16, 2024 preservation letter.

10. The spill had been present for at least 27 minutes prior to Plaintiff's fall, based on timestamp review of surveillance footage provided in initial disclosures.

## III. CLAIMS

### Count I — Negligence (Premises Liability)

11. Plaintiff incorporates paragraphs 1–10.

12. Defendant owed Plaintiff, as a business invitee, a duty of reasonable care to inspect, maintain, and warn of hazardous conditions on the premises.

13. Defendant breached that duty by (a) failing to inspect the area in accordance with its own written policies, (b) failing to promptly remediate the spill, and (c) failing to warn invitees of the hazard.

14. Defendant had actual or constructive notice of the spill, evidenced by surveillance footage showing the spill present for 27+ minutes before Plaintiff's fall, and by BestValue's written 15-minute inspection-sweep policy.

15. Defendant's breach proximately caused Plaintiff's injuries.

16. Plaintiff has suffered past medical specials, anticipated future medical care, lost income, pain and suffering, and loss of enjoyment of life.

## IV. PRAYER FOR RELIEF

C. General damages for pain and suffering, loss of enjoyment, and emotional distress;
D. Costs of suit;
E. Prejudgment interest where authorized; and
F. Such other relief as the Court deems just.

Plaintiff demands a jury trial on all triable issues.
