## I. Executive Summary

This is a premises-liability slip-and-fall with strong notice evidence and a moderate-impact medical picture. Plaintiff Maria Rivera, 45, slipped on a clear-liquid spill near a known-leaking produce cooler at BestValue Store No. 0147 on 2024-02-15, sustaining a right hip contusion, lumbar strain with imaging consistent with disc bulge at L4–L5, and an occipital contusion. Past medical specials are $19,748 billed / $13,047 paid with a $6,701 outstanding balance [OBJ:Billing]. Liability is strong: defendant's own 15-minute sweep policy shows a 41-minute gap before the fall [OBJ:ChenDepo 35:14–15]; the store manager admitted two prior leaks from the same cooler with no preventive action after the second [OBJ:ChenDepo 37:1–21]; the cone was placed 5–6 feet from the spill rather than between the hazard and customer traffic [OBJ:ChenDepo 38:9–24]. Principal weaknesses: pre-existing lumbar findings at L4–L5 visible on imaging [OBJ:ER §5; OBJ:Billing §6], and defendant has pled comparative-fault and open-and-obvious defenses [OBJ:Answer §§1–2 Aff. Def.]. Preliminary economic exposure $25,000–$35,000; total settlement range methodology-estimated at $60,000–$110,000, subject to attorney judgment on venue and insurance posture.
