# Corporate Practice of Medicine Compliance Structure

Drafts a regulatory compliance framework ensuring lawful separation of corporate ownership from medical practice under the CPOM doctrine.

## Prerequisites

1. **Entity details** — business structure, state(s) of operation, healthcare sectors (medical, dental, optometry, telemedicine, PT)
2. **Existing agreements** — current MSA/MSO arrangements, physician employment contracts, governance documents
3. **Jurisdictional scope** — all states where entity operates or plans to operate
4. **Business model** — revenue structure, physician compensation methodology, administrative service arrangements

## Output Structure

### 1. Introduction & Scope

- Define CPOM doctrine and its application to the entity
- State the jurisdictions covered
- Identify violation consequences: license revocation, contract voidability, criminal prosecution, civil fines

### 2. State-Specific CPOM Analysis

Produce a jurisdictional matrix:

| State | Prohibition Level | Key Statute | Exemptions | Enforcement Pattern |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|------------|---------------------|
| CA | Strict | Bus. & Prof. Code §2400 [VERIFY] | Licensed clinics (§1204) | Active — medical board | 
| TX | Strict | Tex. Occ. Code §164.052 [VERIFY] | Limited (5.01(a) entities) | Active |
| NY | Moderate | Educ. Law §6512 [VERIFY] | Article 28 facilities | Moderate |
| FL | Permissive | — | Broad corporate employment | Minimal |

- Flag states relevant to entity's operations
- Note sector-specific variations (telemedicine, dental, optometry, PT face different scrutiny levels)
- Reference recent legislative changes affecting compliance strategy

### 3. Compliant Organizational Structures

Compare available models:

| Structure | Description | Physician Control | Risk Level | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|------------|----------|
| **PC/PA** | Physician-owned professional entity | Full ownership + governance | Low | Single-state practices |
| **MSO + PC** | MSO provides admin services; PC retains clinical control | PC controls all clinical decisions | Low-Medium | Multi-state platforms |
| **Friendly PC** | Physician nominally owns PC; MSO holds economic control | Nominal only — high regulatory scrutiny | High | Avoid unless carefully structured |
| **PPM** | Practice management with equity participation | Shared governance | Medium | Large physician groups |

For each structure, specify:
- Governance requirements and ownership restrictions
- Operational boundaries between entities
- Courts' and regulators' scrutiny factors for de facto control
- Selection criteria based on business model, geography, risk tolerance

### 4. Management Services Agreement (MSA) Architecture

**MSO-permissible functions:**
- Billing, collections, revenue cycle
- HR administration (non-physician staff)
- Facility management, IT, marketing
- Financial reporting and accounting

**Reserved to physician entity (non-delegable):**
- Clinical decision-making and patient care protocols
- Physician hiring, credentialing, supervision, termination
- Fee-setting for medical services
- Patient acceptance, treatment, referral, discharge decisions
- Quality standards and peer review

**Required MSA provisions:**

| Provision | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Compensation | FMV for services; no clinical-outcome-based incentives; physician fees set by physicians |
| Termination | Physicians may exit without losing patient relationships or essential practice assets |
| Governance | MSO advises; physicians hold final authority on all clinical and professional matters |
| Compliance | Mutual CPOM compliance obligations; immediate remediation protocols |
| Financial structure | FMV documentation; defensible under AKS and Stark analysis |
| Audit rights | Both parties retain independent audit rights |

### 5. Operational Compliance Checklist

| Control Point | Requirement | Responsible Party | Frequency |
|---------------|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Physician hiring/credentialing | PC makes all final decisions | Medical Director | Each hire |
| Clinical protocols | Developed by physicians without MSO interference | Clinical Committee | Annual review |
| Fee setting | PC independently determines charges | PC Board | Annual/as needed |
| Patient care decisions | No MSO influence on acceptance, treatment, referral, discharge | Treating physician | Ongoing |
| Marketing materials | Accurately represent physician-owned nature of practice | Compliance Officer | Each campaign |
| Equipment/supply procurement | Clinical purchasing decisions by PC | Medical Director | Each purchase |
| Board minutes | Separate PC and MSO minutes documenting independent decision-making | Corporate Secretary | Each meeting |
| Approval workflows | Clear audit trail showing physician authority | Compliance Officer | Quarterly audit |

### 6. Training, Monitoring & Enforcement

**Training matrix:**

| Audience | Content Focus | Timing |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| Physicians | Governance responsibilities; independent judgment obligations | Onboarding + annual |
| Admin staff | Authority boundaries; when to defer to physician decision-makers | Onboarding + annual |
| Executives | Legal risks; compliance requirements; structural constraints | Onboarding + annual |
| All personnel | Updated training upon organizational changes affecting CPOM | As needed |

**Monitoring program:**
- Quarterly MSA compliance audits
- Annual review of decision-making documentation
- Annual assessment of financial arrangements for FMV compliance
- Governance practice evaluation
- Anonymous reporting channel for CPOM concerns (whistleblower-protected)

**Enforcement escalation:**
1. Corrective action plan with defined timeline
2. Structural modification
3. Relationship termination if non-remediable

### 7. Risk Assessment & Mitigation

| Risk Category | Consequence | Mitigation |
|---------------|-------------|------------|
| Regulatory penalties | Medical board discipline; corporate sanctions; criminal liability | Robust compliance program; external legal opinions |
| Contract voidability | Unwinding of business relationships and financial arrangements | Proper MSA structure; periodic legal review |
| Qui tam / FCA exposure | CPOM violations supporting false claims allegations | AKS/Stark compliance layered with CPOM compliance |
| Reputational harm | Impact on recruitment, patient trust, payer relationships | Proactive compliance posture; documentation |
| Discovery of violations | Regulatory investigation; financial exposure | Voluntary disclosure protocol; D&O / regulatory insurance |

Include self-disclosure decision framework: weigh severity, duration, patient harm, cooperation credit, and jurisdiction-specific voluntary disclosure programs.

### 8. Conclusion & Authorization

- Reaffirm CPOM compliance as an ongoing obligation requiring annual review
- Specify review triggers: operational changes, new jurisdictions, legislative updates, enforcement actions in sector

**Signature blocks:**
- Business entity authorized representative
- Physician entity leadership (Medical Director / PC President)
- Legal counsel (confirming review and approval)
- Effective date and next scheduled review date

## Guidelines

- All legal citations must be verified for current validity; mark uncertain citations with [VERIFY]
- Address federal overlay (Anti-Kickback Statute, Stark Law, OIG guidance) alongside state CPOM analysis
- Customize state analysis to entity's actual operational footprint — do not produce generic 50-state surveys unless requested
- Flag "friendly PC" arrangements for heightened scrutiny; never present as low-risk
- Structure document for dual audience: legal/regulatory reviewers and operational implementers
- Reference OIG compliance program guidance and MGMA best practices where applicable
- Treat CPOM compliance as interdependent with AKS/Stark compliance — never analyze in isolation
