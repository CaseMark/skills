# Lead-Based Paint Disclosure

Prepare a compliant federal lead-based paint disclosure for pre-1978 residential sales or leases.

## Prerequisites

1. Property address, legal description, and year built confirming pre-1978 status
2. Transaction type (sale or lease) and execution timeline
3. Full legal names and contact info for seller/landlord and buyer/tenant
4. All known lead-based paint or lead hazard details, locations, dates, and remediation history
5. All available records/reports (inspection, risk assessment, abatement, clearance, correspondence)
6. Agent/broker names, brokerage, and license numbers (if any)
7. For sales: inspection period choice (default 10 days or negotiated alternative) and any inspection contingency terms

## Output Structure / Process

| Section | Required Content |
| --- | --- |
| Title/Header | Document title, property address, legal description, transaction type, date, party names/addresses, agent details |
| Seller/Landlord Disclosure | Choose one statement: known lead-based paint/hazards with specifics; no knowledge after reasonable inquiry; or previously known hazards remediated with documentation attached |
| Hazard Definition | Include that hazards include deteriorated paint, friction/impact surfaces, and lead-contaminated dust or soil |
| Records/Reports | Attach all available records; if none, state "No records available" |
| Pamphlet Delivery | Confirm delivery of EPA pamphlet "Protect Your Family from Lead in Your Home" or EPA-approved state equivalent |
| Inspection Opportunity (sale only) | Buyer 10-day inspection/risk assessment opportunity or negotiated period; record waiver or exercise; note any contingency terms |
| Acknowledgments | Separate initials or signatures for each required acknowledgment |
| Certifications & Signatures | Seller/landlord, buyer/tenant, and agents (if any), each with printed name, signature, date |

Checklist (must include):
- [ ] Pre-1978 applicability confirmed
- [ ] Seller/landlord disclosure statement selected and completed
- [ ] Lead hazard definition included
- [ ] Records/reports attached or "none" stated
- [ ] EPA pamphlet delivery acknowledged
- [ ] Buyer inspection option recorded (sale only)
- [ ] All acknowledgments separately initialed/signed
- [ ] All certifications and signature blocks included

## Guidelines

- Provide the disclosure before the buyer/tenant becomes obligated under the contract.
- Do not omit the lead hazard definition or the pamphlet delivery acknowledgment.
- List every available record/report; if none, explicitly state "No records available."
- For sales, include the inspection opportunity and a clear waiver or election.
- Use plain English; avoid extra assurances beyond actual knowledge.
- Add state or local lead disclosure addenda if required by jurisdiction.
- Retain signed copies for at least 3 years [VERIFY].
- For leases, provide a disclosure for each new tenant or renewal as required [VERIFY].
