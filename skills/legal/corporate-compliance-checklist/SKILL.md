# Corporate Compliance Checklist

Drafts an assessment-ready compliance program checklist grounded in DOJ, FSG, and SEC frameworks, covering all major program pillars from board oversight through domain-specific controls.

## Prerequisites

1. Company name, industry sector, and primary business activities
2. Organizational structure (public/private, size, geographic footprint)
3. Known regulatory profile (industry-specific regulators, prior enforcement history)
4. Any existing compliance program materials, audit findings, or regulatory correspondence
5. Specific compliance domains to prioritize (or confirm full-spectrum coverage)

## Output Structure

Generate a professionally formatted checklist with the following sections. Use ☐ checkboxes for each line item, grouped under bold subheadings.

---

### 1. Governance & Oversight

**Board/Committee**
| Element | Standard |
|---|---|
| Board compliance oversight charter | Caremark duties (In re Caremark Int'l, Del. Ch. 1996) |
| Audit or compliance committee with direct CCO access | FSG §8B2.1(b)(2) |
| Regular board-level compliance reporting (≥ quarterly) | DOJ ECCP (2023) §I |
| Board training on compliance red flags and regulatory trends | DOJ ECCP §I |

**Chief Compliance Officer**
- Organizational independence from revenue-generating functions
- Direct reporting line to CEO and board/audit committee
- Adequate budget, staffing, and authority
- Documented mandate and delegated authority matrix

**Policy Framework** — each policy requires: approval authority, effective date, version control, distribution log, employee acknowledgment, and review cycle (≤ 3 years):

| Policy | Key Requirements |
|---|---|
| Code of Conduct | Values, escalation paths, annual certification |
| Anti-Corruption / Anti-Bribery | FCPA compliance, foreign official interactions |
| Gift & Entertainment | Monetary thresholds, pre-approval for government officials |
| Conflict of Interest | Disclosure form, recusal process, committee review |
| Insider Trading | Trading windows, pre-clearance, MNPI handling |
| Related Party Transactions | Arm's-length standard, board approval thresholds |
| Whistleblower / Non-Retaliation | SOX §301, Dodd-Frank §922 requirements |

### 2. Compliance Risk Assessment

- Annual enterprise-wide risk assessment (refresh triggered by: M&A, new markets, new regulations, significant incidents)
- Inherent vs. residual risk scoring on likelihood × impact matrix
- Risk inventory by: business unit, product line, geography, functional area
- Third-party risk tiering: enhanced due diligence for high-risk vendors/agents (government interaction, high-risk jurisdictions)
- Assessment methodology documented and board-reported

**Frameworks to reference:** COSO ERM (2017), ISO 31000, DOJ ECCP §II

---

### 3. Training & Culture

**Curriculum by Audience**

| Audience | Content | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Board members | Oversight duties, regulatory trends, red flags | Annual |
| Executives/Senior management | Tone-from-top, accountability, culture indicators | Annual |
| All employees | Code of conduct, reporting channels, key policies | Annual + onboarding |
| High-risk roles (sales, procurement, finance, HR) | Role-specific scenarios (FCPA, SOX, antitrust, FLSA) | Annual + role-change |

**Documentation Requirements**
- Completion records with timestamps
- Assessment scores (minimum passing threshold defined)
- Records retained ≥ 7 years (align with SOX audit work paper standard)

**Culture Indicators to Track**
- Helpline utilization rate
- Anonymous vs. identified report ratio
- Post-training assessment pass rates
- Policy acknowledgment completion rate (target: 100%)

---

### 4. Monitoring, Testing & Audit

**Continuous Monitoring**
- Automated transaction monitoring with defined alert thresholds
- Expense report analytics (anomaly detection)
- Vendor screening alerts (sanctions, adverse media)
- Access and privilege reviews (quarterly)
- Policy exception tracking

**Periodic Testing**
- Annual compliance audit across all material domains
- Targeted audits of highest-risk areas from risk assessment
- Transaction sample testing
- Control operating effectiveness testing
- Follow-up reviews confirming remediation within agreed timelines

**Independence Requirements**
- Internal audit reports to audit committee, not management
- Compliance testing independent from business units under review
- Work papers meet IIA International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing

---

### 5. Reporting Infrastructure & Investigations

**Reporting Channels** (SOX §301 / Dodd-Frank §922)
- Third-party anonymous hotline (24/7, multilingual)
- Web-based anonymous reporting portal
- Designated compliance officer intake
- Direct audit committee channel for accounting/audit concerns

**Investigation Protocol**
1. Intake → triage within 5 business days
2. Assign investigator based on expertise and independence
3. Issue litigation hold if legal exposure identified
4. Document: interview notes, evidence log, timeline, findings memo
5. Remediation plan with owner and deadline
6. Closed-loop reporter notification (where permissible)

**Escalation Triggers** (immediate CCO/General Counsel/Board notification)
- Potential criminal conduct
- Regulatory self-disclosure considerations
- C-suite or board member involvement
- Material financial impact

**Anti-Retaliation Controls**
- Track employment actions affecting reporters (12-month lookback)
- Follow-up interviews with reporters at 60/120 days
- Zero-tolerance policy with defined disciplinary matrix

---

### 6. Domain-Specific Compliance

**Employment Law**
- FLSA: exempt/non-exempt classification, overtime, minimum wage
- Title VII / ADA / ADEA: anti-discrimination policies, manager training, complaint investigation SLA
- OSHA: hazard programs, injury recordkeeping (Form 300), safety training
- FMLA / state leave: leave administration procedures
- FCRA: background check authorization, adverse action process
- Independent contractor classification (IRS 20-factor test; state ABC tests where applicable)

**Data Privacy & Cybersecurity**
- CCPA/CPRA (CA), VCDPA (VA), CPA (CO) + other applicable state laws
- Privacy notice, consumer rights workflow (access, deletion, opt-out)
- Data minimization and retention schedules
- Vendor DPA execution for service providers
- Breach notification: state law matrix (most require 30–72 hours)
- HIPAA (if PHI), GLBA (if financial data), GDPR (if EU data subjects)
- NIST CSF or equivalent security framework adoption

**Financial Reporting & Internal Controls**
- SOX §302/§404: disclosure controls, ICFR documentation and testing
- Segregation of duties matrix for financial processes
- Revenue recognition policy (ASC 606)
- Financial close procedures with defined review/approval hierarchy
- Anti-fraud program (ACFE framework)

**Contracts & Procurement**
- Contract review thresholds and approval matrix
- Standard template library with legal-approved fallbacks
- Vendor due diligence: background check, financial screen, compliance questionnaire
- Government contract compliance (FAR/DFARS where applicable)
- Contract management system tracking obligations and renewals

**Environmental**
- Permit inventory and compliance calendar
- Air (CAA), water (CWA), hazardous waste (RCRA), chemicals (TSCA)
- Spill prevention (SPCC) and emergency response plan
- State environmental requirements overlay
- Annual environmental audit

**Antitrust**
- HSR filing thresholds for M&A (current: $119.5M [VERIFY])
- Competitor interaction policy: no price-fixing, market allocation, bid-rigging
- Distribution practices: resale price maintenance guardrails
- Pre-clearance for trade association participation
- Training for sales, marketing, business development annually

---

### 7. Documentation & Recordkeeping

| Record Type | Minimum Retention |
|---|---|
| Compliance policies (all versions) | Perpetual |
| Training completion records | 7 years |
| Audit work papers | 7 years (SOX requirement) |
| Investigation files | Statute of limitations + 3 years |
| Risk assessments | 7 years |
| Board/committee compliance minutes | Perpetual |
| Employment records | 3–7 years (varies by law) |
| Environmental permits and monitoring | Duration of permit + 5 years |

- Litigation hold procedures documented and tested annually
- Privilege protection: sensitive investigations directed by counsel; privileged materials clearly marked
- Centralized compliance management system with access controls and audit trail

---

### 8. Implementation Roadmap & Metrics

**Phase 1 – Assessment (0–60 days)**
- Gap analysis against this checklist
- Compliance risk assessment or refresh
- Secure executive/board commitment and budget

**Phase 2 – Foundation (60–180 days)**
- Appoint/confirm CCO with appropriate authority
- Draft/update Code of Conduct and priority policies
- Launch hotline and investigation procedures
- Deploy onboarding and high-risk role training

**Phase 3 – Expansion (180–365 days)**
- Full employee training rollout
- Monitoring system configuration
- First annual compliance audit
- Metrics dashboard operational

**Phase 4 – Optimization (Ongoing)**
- Annual DOJ ECCP self-assessment (well-designed? earnestly applied? works in practice?)
- Benchmarking against peer organizations and consent decrees
- Regulatory development monitoring (DOJ, SEC, FTC, DOL, EPA, state AGs)

**Key Performance Indicators**

| Leading Indicators | Lagging Indicators |
|---|---|
| Training completion rate (target: 100%) | Violations and incidents count |
| Policy acknowledgment rate | Regulatory findings / citations |
| Hotline utilization rate | Audit deficiencies |
| Risk assessment coverage (% of BUs) | Investigation cycle time |
| Third-party due diligence completion | Repeat findings rate |

---

## Guidelines

- Tailor domain-specific sections to the company's actual regulatory profile; not all domains apply equally
- FSG Chapter 8 and DOJ ECCP (2023) are the primary evaluative frameworks for U.S. enforcement — reference both explicitly in the checklist preamble
- Privilege: recommend all sensitive investigation work be conducted under attorney direction
- Self-disclosure considerations require separate legal analysis; flag but do not resolve in the checklist
- Verify current HSR thresholds and state-specific privacy law applicability at time of use — these change frequently
- For public companies, SOX §302/§404 obligations are non-negotiable; note private company analogues where useful
- GDPR applicability depends on whether the company processes EU resident data — confirm before including
