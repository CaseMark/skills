# Data Subject Access Request (DSAR) Form

Drafts a legally compliant DSAR intake form enabling individuals to exercise statutory privacy rights under GDPR (Arts. 15–22) and CCPA (Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.100 et seq.).

## Prerequisites

1. **Jurisdiction scope** — confirm whether GDPR, CCPA, both, or additional laws apply (e.g., UK GDPR, CPRA)
2. **Organization identity** — legal name, Data Protection Officer/Privacy Officer contact, submission channels
3. **Supervisory authority** — relevant regulator (e.g., ICO for UK, lead SA for EU, state AG for California)
4. **Submission infrastructure** — secure email, upload portal, or postal address for receiving completed forms
5. **Retention/fee policy** — whether the org charges fees for manifestly unfounded/excessive requests

## Output Structure / Process

### Section 1 — Introduction & Purpose

Include the following in plain language:
- Legal basis: GDPR Arts. 15–22; CCPA § 1798.100 et seq.
- Rights covered (see request types below)
- Response timelines (see Guidelines)
- Fee conditions for excessive/manifestly unfounded requests
- Right to complain to supervisory authority if dissatisfied

### Section 3 — Request Details

**Type of request** (checkbox — select all that apply):

- [ ] Access — copy of all personal data held
- [ ] Access — specific categories only (describe below)
- [ ] Rectification — correct inaccurate/incomplete data
- [ ] Erasure — "right to be forgotten"
- [ ] Restriction of processing
- [ ] Data portability — structured, machine-readable format (GDPR only)
- [ ] Object to processing
- [ ] Opt-out of sale/sharing of personal data (CCPA)

**Scope fields:**

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Description of data / categories sought | Encourage specificity; overly broad requests may require clarification |
| Relevant time period | Date range if applicable |
| Preferred response format | PDF / CSV / Secure portal / Encrypted email |
| Additional context | Any details to help locate records |

Include a note that rights may be limited where: data is legally privileged, retention is required by law, or disclosure would adversely affect third-party rights.

---

### Section 4 — Identity Verification

Specify accepted documents (proportionate to data sensitivity per ICO guidance):

**Tier 1 — Standard requests:**
- Government-issued photo ID (passport, driver's license, national ID card)
- One proof of address (utility bill, bank statement — dated within 3 months)

**Tier 2 — Sensitive data categories (GDPR Art. 9):**
- Two forms of photo ID, or
- Photo ID + video verification via secure portal

**Submission of verification documents:**
- Via encrypted email / secure upload portal (preferred)
- In-person (by appointment)
- Verification documents used solely for DSAR processing and securely destroyed upon completion

Organization reserves the right to request additional verification if identity is reasonably uncertain.

---

### Section 5 — Declaration & Consent

Draft declaration language including:

1. Requester certifies they are the data subject or duly authorized representative
2. All information provided is true and accurate
3. Understands that false statements may result in denial and potential legal consequences
4. Consents to processing of personal data submitted in this form for the purpose of identity verification and request fulfillment only

Include signature line, printed name, and date.

## Guidelines

**Response timelines:**

| Regulation | Standard | Extension | Extension Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| GDPR | 30 days | +60 days (90 total) | Complex or numerous requests |
| CCPA/CPRA | 45 days | +45 days (90 total) | Reasonably necessary |
| UK GDPR | 30 days | +60 days | Same as GDPR |

**Fees:**
- GDPR: Free as a rule; reasonable fee or refusal permitted for manifestly unfounded or excessive (especially repetitive) requests (Art. 12(5))
- CCPA: Free for up to 2 disclosures per 12-month period; reasonable fee for excessive requests

**Do's:**
- Minimize data collected on the form to what is strictly necessary for processing
- Clearly mark mandatory vs. optional fields
- Adapt jurisdiction-specific language based on where the data subject and organization are located
- Reference ICO Subject Access Code of Practice for UK/EU deployments

**Don'ts:**
- Do not create unnecessary barriers to submission (e.g., requiring notarized documents for routine requests)
- Do not use verification data for any purpose other than DSAR processing
- Do not delay acknowledgment pending full response

**Jurisdiction notes:**
- CCPA rights apply to California residents; CPRA (effective 2023) added right to correct and limit use of sensitive PI
- GDPR applies to EU/EEA residents regardless of where the organization is located
- UK GDPR applies post-Brexit; substantively mirrors EU GDPR [VERIFY current divergences]
- Additional state laws (Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, etc.) may require separate form variants
