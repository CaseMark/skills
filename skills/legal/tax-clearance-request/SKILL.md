# Request for Tax Clearance Certificate

Drafts a submission-ready letter requesting a Tax Clearance Certificate from the appropriate state tax authority, confirming all corporate tax obligations are satisfied.

## Prerequisites

1. **Corporate formation documents** — Articles of Incorporation / Certificate of Formation with exact legal name
2. **Tax identification numbers** — FEIN and all state tax account numbers (income, sales/use, withholding, unemployment)
3. **Filed tax returns** — all periods within clearance scope, with filing confirmations
4. **Payment records** — cancelled checks, EFT confirmations, or bank statements for all tax remittances
5. **Transaction documents** — purchase agreement, merger docs, dissolution resolution, or license renewal notice establishing purpose and deadline
6. **Prior clearance certificates or tax authority correspondence** — audits, assessments, payment plans, disputes

## Output Structure

### Document Format

Formal business letter on corporate letterhead. 1–2 pages. Single-spaced, double between paragraphs. Professional tone appropriate for government agency correspondence.

### Required Sections

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| **Header** | Entity letterhead (legal name, address, phone, email), date, recipient agency (specific clearance division) |
| **Subject Line** | "Request for Tax Clearance Certificate — [Entity Legal Name] — [Primary Tax ID]" |
| **Purpose Statement** | What is requested, who is requesting, why (transaction type + anticipated closing date or statutory basis) |
| **Entity Identification** | Full legal name (as registered with SOS), FEIN, all state tax IDs by category, principal + additional addresses, formation date, any prior names with effective dates |
| **Tax Account Inventory** | All accounts: corporate income, sales/use, employer withholding, unemployment insurance, excise, special assessments. Note closed/transferred accounts with dates. |
| **Clearance Scope** | Comprehensive vs. limited (which tax types); time period (formation date through current/closing date); validity period needed |
| **Timeline & Expediting** | Transaction deadline, expedited processing request if needed, willingness to pay expedited fees |
| **Supporting Documentation** | Numbered enclosure list: filed returns, payment proofs, formation docs, good standing certificate, prior clearances, assessment correspondence |
| **Proactive Disclosures** | Pending audits, disputed assessments, payment plans, recent large payments (with dates, amounts, confirmation numbers) |
| **Closing & Contact** | Primary contact (name, title, phone, email), counsel/CPA contact if applicable with scope of authorization, signature block with authorized officer |

## Guidelines

- **Verify exact legal name** against SOS records — mismatches cause automatic rejection
- **Cross-reference all tax IDs** against source documents; never estimate or assume account numbers
- **Check jurisdiction-specific requirements** — some states mandate official forms (not letter requests), notarization, or corporate seal
- **Distinguish clearance types** — some states issue separate tax clearance certificates vs. certificates of good standing; confirm which is needed
- **Multi-state operations** — flag if clearances may be needed from multiple jurisdictions (each state where nexus exists)
- **Multi-agency clearance** — some states require separate clearance from Department of Labor (unemployment/workers' comp) in addition to tax authority
- **Recent payments** — highlight payments made within 30 days with confirmation numbers; request verification of posting before clearance determination
- **FRE 408 is not applicable** — this is administrative correspondence, not settlement negotiation
- **Processing times vary** — range from days to weeks by jurisdiction; factor into transaction planning
- **Validity windows** — typically 30/60/90 days; ensure certificate will remain valid through closing
- **Flag missing information** — if critical data (tax IDs, filed returns, payment records) is unavailable, identify gaps explicitly and request from the user
