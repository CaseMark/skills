# Quality Agreement for Contract Manufacturing

Drafts a legally enforceable Quality Agreement defining quality roles, responsibilities, and cGMP compliance obligations between a product owner and a contract manufacturing organization (CMO).

## Prerequisites

1. **Underlying manufacturing/supply agreement** — including commercial terms, product scope, facility details
2. **Regulatory identifiers** — FDA Establishment IDs, DUNS numbers, DEA registrations (if controlled substances), ISO certifications
3. **Product details** — dosage form, API/finished product, regulatory classification (Rx, OTC, biologic), therapeutic category
4. **Existing quality documents** — prior quality agreements, SOPs, regulatory correspondence, inspection history (483s, warning letters)
5. **Specifications and methods** — approved specs, analytical methods, stability protocols

## Output Structure

### Document Framework

| Section | Key Contents |
|---------|-------------|
| **Parties & Scope** | Legal names, regulatory IDs, product scope (finished dose/API/intermediate), exclusions, quality-over-commercial supremacy clause |
| **Definitions** | Regulatory terms, party-specific roles, defined terms used throughout |
| **Quality Unit Authority** | Responsibilities matrix for both QUs, independence provisions, escalation paths |
| **Manufacturing & Documentation** | Batch record requirements, documentation standards, retention periods, 21 CFR Part 11 compliance |
| **Change Control** | Change categories (minor/major), approval workflows, review timelines, emergency change provisions |
| **Audit & Inspection** | Audit rights (routine/for-cause/unannounced), inspection notification, 483 response coordination |
| **Quality Events & CAPA** | Deviation reporting timelines, investigation requirements, CAPA approval, recall authority |
| **Specifications & Release** | Method validation (ICH Q2), testing responsibilities, OOS investigations, stability programs, reference standards |
| **Personnel & Training** | Qualification requirements, training programs, key personnel change notification |
| **Term & Termination** | Duration, termination rights, transition obligations, survival clauses |
| **Appendices** | Change control forms, deviation templates, quality metrics dashboards, contact lists |

### Notification Timelines

| Event Type | Notification Deadline |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Critical quality event (safety/regulatory) | 24 hours |
| Significant quality event | 72 hours |
| Routine quality event | 5 business days |
| Unannounced regulatory inspection | 4 hours |
| Announced regulatory inspection | 24 hours |
| Key personnel change | 30 days advance |

### Change Control Framework

| Change Category | Examples | Approval Authority | Review Period |
|----------------|----------|-------------------|--------------|
| Minor | Administrative SOP updates, like-for-like equipment | CMO QU (notify product owner) | 15 business days |
| Major | Critical process parameters, alt. suppliers, method revisions, packaging changes | Product owner QU prior written approval | 30 business days |
| Emergency | Immediate safety/compliance issues | Expedited CMO implementation → retrospective product owner approval | Immediate |

Change proposals must include: description, scientific justification, quality risk assessment (FMEA/ICH Q9), validation protocols if applicable, regulatory impact analysis, implementation timeline.

### Audit Rights Structure

- **Pre-approval audits** — before manufacturing initiation
- **Routine audits** — not exceeding 24-month intervals, 30 days advance notice
- **For-cause audits** — triggered by quality events or regulatory concerns
- **Unannounced audits** — preserved right for serious quality/regulatory concerns
- **Scope** — unrestricted access to batch records, validation reports, deviations, training records, personnel interviews, facility/equipment inspection
- **Post-audit** — report issued within 30 days; CMO CAPA response within 15 business days

### Batch Record & Documentation Requirements

- Contemporaneous recording (indelible ink or validated electronic systems)
- Complete material genealogy and traceability
- CMO initial review: 5–10 business days post-batch completion
- Product owner QU review: additional 10–15 business days before disposition
- Retention per 21 CFR 211.180: ≥1 year past expiry or 3 years post-distribution (whichever longer)
- 21 CFR Part 11 compliance for electronic records: system validation, audit trails, data integrity controls

### Termination & Transition

- **Non-renewal notice**: ≥180 days before term expiry
- **Material breach cure period**: 60 days from written notice
- **Termination for convenience**: 180–360 days advance notice
- **Post-termination obligations**: CMO must support product through end of shelf life (stability testing, reserve samples, complaints, inspections)
- **Document transfer**: Complete batch records and quality documentation within 90 days
- **Survival**: Confidentiality (indefinite), record retention (regulatory periods), regulatory support (while product in distribution)

## Guidelines

- Use mandatory language ("shall," "must") — not aspirational ("should," "will")
- Quality decisions must be independent from commercial considerations; include explicit supremacy clause
- Align with FDA 2016 Guidance for Industry: Contract Manufacturing Arrangements for Drugs: Quality Agreements [VERIFY]
- Address data integrity per FDA 2018 Data Integrity and Compliance with Drug CGMP guidance [VERIFY]
- For international markets, incorporate ICH Q7, EU GMP Annex 11, WHO guidelines as applicable
- Include cybersecurity provisions for manufacturing systems and confidential regulatory data
- Ensure no conflict with underlying supply agreement; quality agreement governs all quality matters
- Structure with clear hierarchical headings for easy reference during inspections and audits
- Use appendices for operational templates (change control forms, deviation notifications, audit report formats)
- If user provides 483s or warning letters, incorporate preventive measures addressing cited deficiencies
- Search user-uploaded documents for existing quality agreements, specs, and regulatory correspondence before drafting
