# Shareholder Voting Agreement

Drafts a binding voting agreement coordinating shareholder voting rights on specified corporate matters, integrating with related governance documents and jurisdiction-specific requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Corporate details** — jurisdiction of incorporation, capitalization table, share classes and voting rights
2. **Parties and holdings** — legal names, entity type/state of formation, share counts and percentages for each party
3. **Transaction context** — financing round, merger, founder arrangement, or standalone governance
4. **Related agreements** — investors' rights agreement, ROFR/co-sale agreement, stockholders' agreement (for consistency review)
5. **Voting scope** — specific matters covered: board composition, fundamental transactions, charter/bylaw amendments, equity plan approvals
6. **Term preference** — duration tied to IPO/liquidity event, continued employment, fixed period, or perpetual

## Output Structure

### 1. Recitals
- Total shares outstanding; parties' aggregate voting percentage
- Transaction or event precipitating the agreement
- Cross-references to related agreements by name and date

### 2. Party Identification

| Field | Individual | Entity |
|---|---|---|
| Name | Full legal name | Legal entity name + state of formation |
| Address | Residential | Principal place of business |
| Share ownership | Record + beneficial if nominee-held | Record holder + beneficial owner |
| Execution capacity | Personal | Signatory name, title, authorization |

### 3. Voting Commitments

For each covered matter, specify commitment type and mechanics:

| Matter | Commitment Type | Key Mechanics |
|---|---|---|
| Director elections | Vote for named individuals or group designees | Nomination process, qualifications, replacement on resignation/removal |
| Fundamental transactions | Affirmative / negative / board-directed | Criteria if conditional approval |
| Charter/bylaw amendments | As specified per amendment type | Class vote applicability |
| Authorized share increases | As specified | Which class(es) subject |
| Equity incentive plans | As specified | Board-approval prerequisite |

- **After-acquired shares:** Specify whether shares from options, warrants, stock dividends, or open-market purchases are automatically subject or require amendment
- **Quorum covenant:** Parties agree to attend or deliver proxies to ensure quorum at all relevant meetings
- **Written consent:** Obligation to execute when board-approved matters proceed by consent in lieu of meeting

### 4. Irrevocable Proxy

- Explicitly irrevocable; coupled with an interest [Delaware: DGCL §212(e) — verify equivalent for non-Delaware jurisdictions]
- Triggered only upon breach or failure to vote as required — self-executing enforcement, not routine voting mechanism
- Survives: bankruptcy, death, incapacity, and transfer of underlying shares
- Scope: vote shares, execute written consents, take all actions necessary to effectuate commitments
- Appoints proxy holder as attorney-in-fact with full authority

### 5. Enforcement

- Acknowledgment that voting rights are unique and breach causes irreparable harm not compensable by money damages
- Non-breaching parties entitled to specific performance and injunctive relief **without posting bond**
- Equitable remedies are cumulative and in addition to all other available remedies

### 6. Term and Termination

**Term:** Align with transaction purpose; DGCL §218 imposes no statutory duration cap on voting agreements (voting trusts capped at 10 years — distinct instrument). [VERIFY duration limits for non-Delaware jurisdictions.]

**Automatic termination triggers:**
- Qualified IPO meeting specified size/exchange criteria
- Change of control or acquisition of the corporation
- Dissolution or winding-up
- Parties' collective ownership falls below [___]% threshold

**Voluntary termination:** Unanimous written consent, or [supermajority] of shares subject to agreement — distinguish core voting provisions (unanimous) from administrative provisions (supermajority)

**Partial termination:** Specify whether agreement terminates in full or only as to departing parties upon triggering event

### 7. Transfer Provisions

Select one approach and draft accordingly:

| Approach | Mechanics |
|---|---|
| **Obligation runs with shares** | Transferee executes joinder as condition precedent to transfer; corporation refuses to record transfer without executed joinder |
| **Personal obligation only** | Transfer releases voting obligation as to transferred shares; specify whether this triggers adjustment rights or termination rights for remaining parties |

- Transferring party must provide advance notice to other parties
- Include Form of Transferee Joinder Agreement as exhibit

### 8. Representations and Warranties (Each Party)

- Sole legal and beneficial owner of listed shares, or disclose nominee/trustee arrangement
- Shares free and clear of liens, pledges, encumbrances, and conflicting voting arrangements or proxies
- Full power and authority to execute; no required third-party consents
- Execution and performance do not violate any other agreement or obligation
- **Entity parties add:** due organization, good standing, duly authorized signatory

### 9. Covenants

- Vote all subject shares in accordance with agreement at meetings and by written consent
- No conflicting proxies, voting trusts, or other voting arrangements inconsistent with commitments
- Execute all documents reasonably necessary to effectuate voting obligations
- Provide timely notice of any proposed transfer
- Cooperate in amending agreement to address changes in applicable law or corporate structure

### 10. Governance Integration

- Expressly designate controlling document in event of conflict with related agreements
- **Multi-class stock:** Identify which classes are subject; address how class-specific votes interact with commitments
- **Voting instruction procedure:** If a designee directs votes — specify notice method, timing, and default rule if instructions not timely received

### 11. Standard Provisions

| Provision | Key Drafting Points |
|---|---|
| Governing law | State of incorporation |
| Amendments | Unanimous / supermajority written consent; distinguish core vs. administrative provisions |
| Notices | Physical + email addresses per party; deemed-received rules for each delivery method |
| Severability | Invalidity of one voting commitment does not invalidate remaining commitments |
| Integration | Supersedes all prior voting arrangements on subject matter |
| Counterparts | Electronic signatures enforceable; counterparts constitute single instrument |

### 12. Schedules and Exhibits

| Document | Content |
|---|---|
| Schedule A — Shareholdings | Party name, share count, class, certificate numbers, % of outstanding |
| Exhibit A — Form of Irrevocable Proxy | Self-executing proxy for use upon breach |
| Exhibit B — Form of Transferee Joinder | Agreement by transferee to be bound by voting commitments |

## Guidelines

- **DGCL §218:** Governs Delaware voting agreements; no statutory duration cap (voting trusts are the capped instrument). Confirm analogous statute for non-Delaware corporations [VERIFY].
- Irrevocable proxy must be "coupled with an interest" — the voting agreement itself typically supplies the interest; include explicit recitation to that effect in the proxy.
- Enumerate specific covered matters rather than using broad language — vague commitments create enforcement ambiguity in contested situations.
- Reconcile with preferred stock protective provisions in VC financings — preferred holders may hold separate class vote rights outside this agreement that could override or duplicate commitments.
- If parties collectively hold less than a majority of voting power, flag that the agreement may not achieve stated control objectives.
- Ensure no conflicts with concurrent investors' rights, ROFR/co-sale, or stockholders' agreements — conflicting governance documents undermine enforceability of all instruments.
