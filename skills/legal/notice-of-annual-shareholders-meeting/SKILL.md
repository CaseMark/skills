# Notice of Annual Shareholders Meeting

Produces a legally defensible annual-meeting notice that protects shareholder voting rights and supports the validity of meeting actions.

## Prerequisites

1. State of incorporation and all governing documents: charter/articles, bylaws, and any shareholder agreements.
2. Corporate identity details, including legal name, stock classes, and voting rights.
3. Confirmed annual meeting date/time, format (in-person, virtual, or hybrid), and venue/platform details.
4. Confirmed record date (COB cutoff) and transfer-agent/stock ledger process.
5. Full agenda and proposal package with proposed resolutions and required board recommendations.
6. Current quorum rules and vote thresholds (per class, ordinary, and special resolutions).
7. For public companies: latest proxy materials, filing references, and e-delivery settings.
8. Authorized signatory and approved notice/distribution method.

## Output Structure / Process

### 1) Intake and compliance matrix

| Item | Required input | Validation rule |
|---|---|---|
| Jurisdictional base | State and filing regime | Use the longer of statute and bylaws notice rule. |
| Record date | Date/time/cutoff | Only holders of record as of this point are eligible. |
| Notice recipients | Shareholder list source | Match transfer agent/stock ledger as of record date. |
| Meeting mechanics | Venue/platform, registration, access | No ambiguity on where/how to participate. |
| Voting mechanics | Class rights and vote standards | Tie each proposal to its exact threshold. |
| Proxy process | Deadline and revocation rules | Ensure counting rules are explicit and verifiable. |
| Accessibility/disclaimer | Accommodation contacts | Include request method and timeline. |

### 2) Mandatory notice sections

1. Header: exact phrase “NOTICE OF ANNUAL MEETING OF SHAREHOLDERS.”
2. Corporate identity and legal authority (bylaws + applicable state act).
3. Notice of meeting date, time, timezone, place/website.
4. Record-date statement and voting eligibility rules.
5. Detailed agenda with short description of each item.
6. Quorum and voting section by proposal type.
7. Proxy issuance, delivery, revocation, and late-proxy handling.
8. Shareholder support contacts and material-access instructions.
9. Signature block with date, officer name, title, and office contact.

### 3) Draft template

```text
NOTICE OF ANNUAL MEETING OF SHAREHOLDERS
[Corporation Legal Name], a [State of Incorporation] corporation

To: Shareholders of record as of [Record Date] at close of business.

Notice is hereby given that a meeting of shareholders will be held on [Meeting Date], at [Time] [Time Zone], at [Location / Virtual Platform].

This meeting will consider:
1. [Agenda Item]
2. [Agenda Item]
3. [Agenda Item]

Record Date: [Record Date] (COB)
Eligible Voters: [Class-by-class voting rights and any class-action rules]
Quorum: [Quorum standard]
Proposal Vote Requirements: [Majority / supermajority / class vote requirements]

Proxy Instructions:
- Deadline: [Deadline]
- Delivery methods: [Mail/telephone/electronic]
- Revocation: [Method/recipient/last-minute rule]

[Contact for materials, accommodations, and inquiries]

Sincerely,
[Name], [Title]
[Date]
```

### 4) Final quality gates

- Notice timing tested against statute + bylaws minimums.
- Distribution method and proof-of-service documented.
- Proposal descriptions accurate and consistent with resolutions.
- Public-company filing/disclosure references included and reconciled [VERIFY].
- SEC proxy rule references verified before final output [VERIFY].

## Guidelines

- Use plain-language instructions where possible, preserve legal precision.
- Keep statutory and bylaw requirements consistent across title, body, and signature block.
- Include contingencies for location/platform changes and communication path updates.
- Do not include unnotified substantive business in “other business.”
- Do not mix notice period for annual meetings with special meeting timing rules.
- Jurisdictional caveat: conflicting federal reporting obligations override internal defaults for public companies.
