# Request for Order Modification

Drafts a post-judgment motion to modify an existing family law court order based on material changes in circumstances.

## Prerequisites

1. **Original order** — full text with entry date, issuing judge, specific provisions at issue
2. **Evidence of changed circumstances** — financial records, medical records, correspondence, school records, employment docs, incident documentation
3. **Prior declarations/financial disclosures** — baseline conditions at time of original order
4. **Jurisdiction identification** — state/county, applicable modification statute, local court rules
5. **Prior modification history** — any earlier modification attempts and outcomes

## Process

### Phase 1: Jurisdiction & Standard Research

Identify the governing legal standard:

| Modification Type | Common Standards | Typical Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Child support | Material change in circumstances; income change threshold | State family code; child support guidelines |
| Spousal support | Changed circumstances; cohabitation; retirement | State family code; controlling appellate law |
| Custody/visitation | Best interests of child + changed circumstances | State family code; custody factors statute |
| Property division | Fraud, mistake, or newly discovered assets (rarely modifiable) | State family code; FRCP 60(b) analogs |

Verify:
- [ ] Specific statutory authority for this modification type
- [ ] Burden of proof (moving party's burden)
- [ ] Whether jurisdiction requires threshold showing before evidentiary hearing
- [ ] Meet-and-confer or ADR prerequisite
- [ ] Filing deadlines or waiting periods post-original order

### Phase 2: Caption & Formatting

- [ ] Full court name with department/division
- [ ] Exact case number from court records
- [ ] Party names with correct designations (Petitioner/Respondent)
- [ ] Document title per local rules ("Request for Order," "Motion to Modify," "Order to Show Cause")
- [ ] Comply with local formatting rules (font, margins, line spacing, page numbering)
- [ ] Identify required companion filings (proposed order, income/expense declaration, notice of motion)

### Phase 3: Draft Structure

```
1. INTRODUCTION
   - Identity of moving party and relationship to case
   - Original order: title, date, issuing judicial officer
   - Specific provisions sought to be modified (cite paragraph/section numbers)
   - Summary of relief requested
   - One-sentence statement of changed circumstances

2. PROCEDURAL HISTORY
   - Timeline: original order → key developments → present motion
   - Prior modifications or attempts (distinguish current facts)
   - Compliance history with existing order

3. STATEMENT OF FACTS
   Chronological narrative of changed circumstances:
   - Baseline: conditions at time of original order
   - Changes: specific dates, quantified data, concrete details
   - Each fact tied to admissible evidence (cite exhibit)
   - Address foreseeability — explain why changes were unanticipated
   - Preempt counterarguments (temporary vs. enduring; foreseeable vs. new)

4. LEGAL ARGUMENT
   - Cite governing statute with quoted language
   - State elements/factors court must consider
   - Apply each element to specific facts
   - Cite controlling appellate authority [VERIFY all citations]
   - Distinguish unfavorable authority if applicable

5. REQUESTED RELIEF
   - Specific modifications in adoptable order language:
     * Dollar amounts, effective dates, payment mechanisms
     * Detailed schedules (days, times, holidays, vacations)
     * Step-down/termination provisions if applicable
   - Alternative relief if primary request denied
   - "Such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper"

6. CONCLUSION
   - Restate each modification concisely
   - Prayer for relief
```

### Phase 4: Supporting Documents

**Moving Party Declaration:**
- First person, paragraph-per-fact structure
- Establish personal knowledge for each assertion
- Authenticate all referenced exhibits
- Include quantified financial data or specific incidents (not conclusory statements)

## Guidelines

- **Accuracy over advocacy** — every factual assertion must be verifiable from the record; credibility is dispositive in discretionary rulings
- **Quantify everything** — dollar amounts, dates, percentages, specific schedule times; avoid vague language
- **Distinguish prior attempts** — if earlier modifications were denied, explicitly differentiate current facts
- **FRE 408 awareness** — do not reference settlement discussions or mediation offers unless exception applies
- **Child-focused framing** — for custody/visitation modifications, anchor every argument to the child's best interests, not parental preference
- **Jurisdiction-specific compliance** — local rules vary significantly; always verify required forms, filing fees, and hearing reservation procedures
- **Mark unverified citations** — use [VERIFY] for any statutory section or case citation not confirmed against source material
