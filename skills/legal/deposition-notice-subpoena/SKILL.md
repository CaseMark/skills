# Deposition Notice and Subpoena

Produce enforceable federal deposition notices and Rule 45 subpoenas with defensible scope and timing.

## Prerequisites

1. Case caption, court, civil action number, judge or magistrate.
2. Deponent identity and status: party individual, party entity, current employee, former employee, nonparty.
3. Scheduling order: discovery cutoff, deposition limits, sequencing, any stipulations.
4. Proposed date, time, time zone, location or remote platform and access plan.
5. Recording method: stenographic only or stenographic plus audiovisual.
6. 30(b)(6) topic list with defined terms and time ranges if entity deposition.
7. Document requests (Schedule A) and desired production format.
8. Existing protective order or confidentiality stipulation.
9. Rule 45 compliance details: service address, compliance location, witness fee and mileage (28 U.S.C. § 1821).
10. Prior discovery tie-in: pleadings, interrogatory responses, key productions.

## Output Structure / Process

**1. Select the instrument**

| Deponent type | Instrument | Service | Key rules |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Party individual | Rule 30(b)(1) notice | Serve counsel under Rule 5 | Rule 30(b)(3) recording method; Rule 30(d)(1) 7-hour limit; Rule 30(a)(2)(A)(i) deposition limits |
| Party entity | Rule 30(b)(6) notice | Serve counsel under Rule 5 | Topics must be reasonably particular; entity must designate and prepare |
| Nonparty | Rule 45 subpoena | Personal service + fees | Rule 45(b)(1) tender fees; Rule 45(c) compliance limits; Rule 45(a)(4) pre-service notice to parties |
| Former employee | Rule 45 subpoena | Personal service + fees | Treat as nonparty |

**2. Build the timeline**

1. If requesting documents with a notice, serve at least 30 days before deposition to satisfy Rule 34 response period.
2. If subpoenaing documents, allow 14 days for objections under Rule 45(d)(2)(B).
3. Reserve time for meet-and-confer and motion practice before the discovery cutoff.

**3. Draft Rule 30(b)(1) notice (template)**

```text
[Caption]

NOTICE OF DEPOSITION OF [NAME]

PLEASE TAKE NOTICE that, pursuant to Fed. R. Civ. P. 30(b)(1), [Noticing Party] will take the deposition upon oral examination of [Deponent], on [Date], at [Time] [Time Zone], at [Address] or via [Remote Platform] (access details to follow). The deposition will be recorded by [stenographic] [stenographic and audiovisual] means and conducted before a duly authorized officer. The deposition will proceed from day to day until completed.
```

**4. Draft Rule 30(b)(6) topics**

Use a topic table and avoid open-ended phrasing. Tie each topic to a claim or defense, add time limits, and define key terms.

| Topic No. | Subject | Time range | Limits/definitions | Source anchors |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [Business function or transaction] | [Start-End] | [Define Product, Incident, Project] | [Pleadings, RFPs, Interrogatory responses] |

Rules:
- No "all facts" or "entire complaint" topics.
- Avoid "including but not limited to" as scope expansion.
- Aim for prepared testimony from documents and interviews, not legal conclusions.

**5. Draft Rule 45 subpoena**

Use AO 88A (testimony) or AO 88B (documents). Include Rule 45(d) and (e) text as required by Rule 45(a)(1)(A)(iv). Ensure issuing court is the court where the action is pending under Rule 45(a)(2).

**Deposition subpoena addendum (if testimony commanded):**
- Deposition date, time, time zone.
- Place of compliance within Rule 45(c) limits.
- Remote appearance language if permitted.

**6. Schedule A request template**

```text
Schedule A - Document Requests

1. Documents sufficient to show [specific fact], limited to [date range].
2. Non-privileged communications between [A] and [B] regarding [topic], limited to [date range].
3. Records of [payments/shipments/transactions] for [Project], limited to [date range].
```

**ESI and format table**

| Item | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Format | Native for spreadsheets; PDF/TIFF for images |
| Metadata | Include standard email metadata fields |
| Deduplication | Allowed; explain method if used |
| Attachments | Produce with parent emails |
| Privilege | Log withheld material; use Rule 26(b)(5)(A) format |

**7. Service packet checklist**

- AO 88A/88B subpoena with Schedule A.
- Notice of deposition (if testimony).
- Pre-service notice to parties for document subpoenas (Rule 45(a)(4)).
- Witness fee and mileage tender (28 U.S.C. § 1821).
- Proof of service form for process server.
- Cover letter offering burden-reduction and confidentiality handling.

**8. Quality control checklist**

- Caption, court, case number match docket.
- Recording method stated; "audiovisual" included if video intended.
- Compliance location fits Rule 45(c) 100-mile limit.
- Document requests tie to claims/defenses and have time limits.
- Rule 30 limits complied with or leave obtained.
- Rule 45(d)(1) undue burden analysis documented.
- Protective order referenced or flagged if needed.

## Guidelines

- Do not notice nonparties under Rule 30; use Rule 45.
- Do not subpoena represented parties directly; serve counsel.
- Do not guess local-rule notice periods; flag for attorney verification.
- Rule 34 applies to document requests in notices; plan for 30-day response.
- Rule 45 objections are due within 14 days; build into the schedule.
- Avoid legal conclusions and privileged subject matter in 30(b)(6) topics.
- State court matters require new rules: stop and request state and court level.
- State examples: California CCP § 2025.220 [VERIFY], New York CPLR 3107 and 22 NYCRR Part 221 [VERIFY], Texas R. Civ. P. 199.2 [VERIFY].
- Ethics: comply with Model Rules 1.1, 1.3, 3.4, 4.2, 4.3, 4.4, and 1.6.
- Attorney review required before use; confirm local rules, judge-specific procedures, and subpoena form requirements.
