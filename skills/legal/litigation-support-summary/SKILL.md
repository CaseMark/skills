# Litigation Support Summary

Produces a strategic overview of an active litigation matter, synthesizing case materials into a structured management document for legal teams and clients.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case file access** — pleadings, court orders, docket sheet, case caption/number, presiding judge
2. **Discovery materials** — requests, responses, production logs, deposition transcripts/summaries
3. **Correspondence and memoranda** — strategic memos, client communications, opposing counsel letters
4. **Scheduling orders** — court-imposed deadlines, trial date, expert disclosure dates
5. **Budget/staffing info** (if available) — litigation budget, team assignments, cost tracking

## Output Structure

Produce the summary using the following sections. Target 5–10 pages depending on complexity.

### 1. Case Overview & Procedural Posture

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Caption & Docket | Full case caption, docket number |
| Court & Judge | Court, jurisdiction, presiding judge |
| Parties | All parties with roles (plaintiff/defendant/third-party/intervenor) |
| Claims & Defenses | Each cause of action and counterclaim with current status |
| Procedural Stage | Current phase (pleadings / discovery / pre-trial / trial) |
| Significant Rulings | Key motions filed, hearings held, orders issued |

### 2. Strategy & Theory of Case

- **Primary legal theories** and key factual arguments
- **Desired outcome** (damages amount, injunctive relief, dismissal, etc.)
- **Strategy evolution** — how discovery, rulings, or new facts have shifted approach
- **Strengths/weaknesses assessment** — candid, for both sides

| Factor | Our Position | Opposing Position |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Strongest argument | | |
| Key vulnerability | | |
| Critical unresolved issue | | |

- **ADR/Settlement posture** — current settlement status, demand/offer history, mediation prospects

### 3. Timeline & Critical Deadlines

Present as a chronological table:

| Date | Event/Deadline | Status | Action Required |
|------|---------------|--------|-----------------|
| _past dates_ | Significant events | Complete | — |
| _upcoming_ | Deadline description | Pending | Responsible party + prep needed |

Flag any deadline requiring **immediate attention** with urgency notation.

Include: discovery cutoffs, expert disclosures, dispositive motion deadlines, pretrial conferences, trial date, and internal work-product targets.

### 6. Risks, Opportunities & Recommendations

**Risks:**
- Adverse outcome exposure (liability + damages range)
- Cost escalation triggers
- Reputational or business disruption concerns

**Opportunities:**
- Favorable legal developments or opponent weaknesses
- Settlement leverage points
- Dispositive motion prospects

**Recommendations:**
Numbered, actionable next steps with priority and responsible party:

1. _[Action]_ — Priority: High/Medium/Low — Owner: ___
2. ...

## Guidelines

- **Audience-appropriate language** — accessible to non-lawyers while legally precise; define terms of art on first use
- **Cite to the record** — reference specific documents by name and page/paragraph when asserting facts
- **Distinguish fact from argument** — clearly separate established facts, disputed facts, and legal contentions
- **Candid assessment** — provide realistic evaluations, not advocacy; flag information gaps
- **Visual aids** — use tables and timelines for complex procedural histories or multi-party matters
- **Living document framing** — note the summary date and indicate it should be updated as the case progresses
- **Confidentiality** — mark as attorney-client privileged / work product as appropriate
