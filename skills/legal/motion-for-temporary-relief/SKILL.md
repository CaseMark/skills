# Motion for Temporary Relief (Pendente Lite)

Drafts a comprehensive, evidence-anchored pendente lite motion seeking interim orders for custody, support, property use, and fees while the family law matter proceeds to final resolution.

## Prerequisites

1. **Initiating pleading** — petition/complaint with case number, department/division, and party names as filed
2. **Financial affidavits** — completed income/expense/asset/debt disclosures for both parties
3. **Income documentation** — recent pay stubs, last 2 years' tax returns, business records if self-employed
4. **Parenting facts** — children's names, DOBs, current living arrangements, existing informal schedules
5. **Property inventory** — marital residence address, vehicle list, any existing temporary agreements or orders
6. **Client declaration** — signed declaration with personal-knowledge support for each relief category

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption & Introduction
- Court name (including dept./division), case number, party names exactly as in initiating pleading
- Title: *Motion for Temporary Relief* or *Motion for Pendente Lite Orders* per local usage
- Opening paragraph: moving party's status, statutory basis for temporary-order jurisdiction, brief overview of relief categories, framing as preservation of status quo / prevention of irreparable harm

### 2. Factual Background
| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Marriage/relationship dates | Date of marriage, date of separation |
| Children | Name, DOB, current residence, school |
| Historical roles | Primary caregiver, breadwinner, shared duties |
| Current arrangements | Who is in marital home, informal custody in place |
| Material disputes | Specific incidents with dates, tied to declarations/exhibits |

### 3. Child Custody & Parenting Time
- **Legal/physical custody** requested with statutory basis (cite state family code section)
- **Detailed parenting schedule**: weekday/weekend time, exchange times and locations, transportation responsibility
- **Holiday/vacation calendar** for upcoming 12 months
- **Decision-making authority**: education, healthcare, extracurriculars
- **Best interest analysis** — apply each statutory factor with specific factual support:
  - Child's age, health, developmental needs
  - Each parent's historical involvement in daily care
  - Continuity and stability of environment
  - Child's ties to school, community, siblings
  - Each parent's ability to cooperate and facilitate relationship with other parent
  - Any DV, substance abuse, or mental health concerns (cite supporting evidence)
  - Child's preference if age-appropriate and documented

### 4. Child Support
```
Guideline calculation inputs (cite state guidelines statute):
  - Parent A gross monthly income: $______  (source: [Exhibit __])
  - Parent B gross monthly income: $______  (source: [Exhibit __])
  - Adjustable deductions (other support obligations, health insurance premiums): $______
  - Parenting time split (overnights per year each parent): ______
  - Guideline monthly support: $______

Health insurance:
  - Coverage provider: Parent [A/B], monthly premium attributable to children: $______
  - Proposed allocation of unreimbursed medical/dental/vision: [__% / __%] per income ratio

Deviations (if any): identify statutory deviation factor and factual basis
```
Attach or reference completed child support worksheet.

### 5. Spousal / Partner Support
**Need:** Moving party's gross monthly income vs. documented monthly expenses (itemized: housing, utilities, food, transportation, insurance, medical, debt service) — show monthly deficit.

**Ability to pay:** Other party's gross income, own expenses, available liquid assets.

Specify: amount requested, pendente lite duration (defined period or until further order).

### 6. Exclusive Use & Possession of Property

**Marital residence:**
- Basis: children's stability, primary caregiver needs, safety concerns (DV history), inability to cohabitate
- Allocation of carrying costs: mortgage, taxes, insurance, utilities, routine maintenance (who pays what)

**Vehicles** (list by year/make/model):

| Vehicle | Current Possessor | Proposed Assignment | Payments / Insurance Responsibility |
|---|---|---|---|

### 7. Attorney's Fees & Costs
- Fees incurred to date: $______ (attach counsel's billing summary or supporting declaration)
- Projected fees through temporary orders hearing: $______
- Financial disparity: moving party's liquid assets and income vs. other party's
- Statutory basis for fee contribution [cite state family code section — VERIFY]
- If other party's conduct increased costs: document specific obstructive acts with dates
- Requested contribution amount: $______

### 8. Prayer for Relief
Enumerate each order requested in precise, enforceable language suitable for direct incorporation into a court order:
1. Temporary [legal/physical] custody to [Party] per the schedule attached as Exhibit __
2. Child support of $______ per month payable by [Party] to [Party] beginning [date]
3. Temporary spousal support of $______ per month payable by [Party] to [Party]
4. Exclusive use and possession of the marital residence at [address] to [Party]
5. Exclusive use of [vehicle] to [Party]
6. Attorney's fees contribution of $______ within __ days
7. Hearing date set on an expedited basis with [X] days' notice to opposing counsel
8. Such other and further relief as the court deems just and proper

Include request for a proposed order if required by local rules.

## Guidelines

- **Cite precisely**: every factual assertion must reference a declaration paragraph or exhibit (e.g., "See Decl. of [Client], ¶ 12"; "Exh. C to Financial Affidavit")
- **Use jurisdiction's statutes**: temporary-order authority, best interest factors, support guidelines, and fee-shifting statutes vary by state — insert correct citations and mark any uncertain ones `[VERIFY]`
- **No unsupported allegations**: avoid hearsay that cannot be authenticated; DV/substance abuse claims must be tied to police reports, medical records, or sworn declarations
- **Local rules compliance**: verify page limits, font/margin requirements, meet-and-confer certifications, certificates of service, and whether a separate proposed order must accompany the motion
- **Tone**: professional and zealous — factual, no inflammatory language or personal attacks; acknowledge adverse facts with context rather than omitting them
- **Domestic violence**: if DV is alleged, assess whether an emergency protective order or ex parte application is more appropriate than a noticed motion
