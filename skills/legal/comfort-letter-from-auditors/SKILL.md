# Comfort Letter from Auditors

Drafts a PCAOB AS 6101-compliant comfort letter providing negative assurance on unaudited financial information for underwriters in U.S. securities offerings.

## Prerequisites

1. **Offering details** — type (IPO, secondary), SEC file number (e.g., No. 333-XXXXX), form type (S-1, S-3), pricing/closing date
2. **Audited financial statements** — fiscal periods covered, audit report date
3. **Unaudited interim financials** — periods subject to review procedures; pro forma data if applicable
4. **Underwriting agreement** — date, full legal names of all addressees (underwriters/placement agents), specific comfort items requested
5. **Cut-off date** — date within 5 business days of comfort letter date through which procedures are performed
6. **Engagement authorization** — engagement letter authorizing comfort letter preparation

## Output Structure

### 1. Header & Salutation
- Auditing firm letterhead: full firm name, address, PCAOB registration number
- Date: issuance date (coincides with pricing or closing)
- Addressee: underwriters/placement agents by full legal name
- Reference: underwriting agreement date; engagement letter

### 2. Introduction

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Purpose | Comfort letter in connection with [offering type] of [company name] |
| Registration Statement | No. 333-XXXXX on Form [S-1 / S-3] |
| Audited periods | Fiscal years ended [dates] per audit report dated [date] |
| Governing standard | PCAOB AS 6101 |
| Authorization | Issued pursuant to underwriting agreement dated [date] |

### 3. Scope of Review & Procedures Performed

**Required disclaimer:** Procedures performed do **not** constitute an audit or review conducted in accordance with PCAOB standards and are **substantially less in scope** than an examination, the objective of which would be the expression of an opinion.

Standard procedures checklist:
- [ ] Read minutes of meetings of stockholders, board of directors, and relevant committees through cut-off date
- [ ] Inquire of company officials re: changes in capital stock, increases in long-term debt, decreases in specified financial statement items
- [ ] Apply analytical procedures to interim financial data (comparison to prior periods)
- [ ] Agree interim figures to company accounting records
- [ ] Read unaudited interim financial statements included or incorporated in the registration statement
- [ ] [If applicable] Perform agreed-upon procedures on pro forma financial information

**Cut-off period:** From the date of the latest audited balance sheet through [cut-off date].

### 4. Negative Assurance Statements

Standard formulation — use consistently; **never** imply positive assurance:

> *"Nothing came to our attention as a result of the foregoing procedures that caused us to believe that..."*

Address each item requested in the underwriting agreement:

| Topic | Completing Clause |
|---|---|
| Specified financial statement items | ...there were any material decreases in [item] compared to [audited period] |
| Long-term debt | ...long-term debt increased by more than [threshold / any material amount] |
| Stockholders' equity | ...stockholders' equity decreased materially from [date] to the cut-off date |
| Interim financial statements | ...the unaudited interim financial statements were not prepared, in all material respects, in conformity with GAAP applied on a basis substantially consistent with the audited financial statements |
| Pro forma financials (if applicable) | ...the pro forma adjustments were not applied on a basis consistent with [applicable SEC rules / GAAP] [VERIFY: Regulation S-X Rule 11-02] |

### 5. Limitations & Disclaimers

- [ ] Letter is solely for the information and use of the named addressees to assist them in their investigation
- [ ] No reliance permitted by any other party without auditors' prior written consent
- [ ] No opinion on the sufficiency of procedures for the underwriters' purposes
- [ ] No responsibility for the adequacy of disclosures in the registration statement or prospectus
- [ ] No assurance on legal matters, litigation outcomes, or regulatory compliance beyond financial statement presentation
- [ ] No obligation to update beyond the stated cut-off date
- [ ] No responsibility for events or circumstances arising after the cut-off date

### 6. Closing & Signature Block

- Reaffirm limited nature of assurance and permissible reliance
- State restrictions on inclusion in transaction documents and further distribution
- Signature block: full legal firm name | authorized partner/principal signature | "Certified Public Accountants" | city and state of signing office
- If required by engagement: confirm PCAOB registration and compliance with applicable independence standards

## Guidelines

- **Negative assurance only** — "nothing came to our attention" is the ceiling of comfort; never draft language that implies positive assurance or a guarantee
- **PCAOB AS 6101** — all procedures and formulations must conform; cite in introduction [VERIFY current standard designation as AS 6101]
- **Addressee specificity** — letter is non-transferable; name all relying parties explicitly in salutation; comfort does not extend to unnamed parties
- **Cut-off discipline** — describe procedures with precision as to time period covered; do not extend assurance beyond stated cut-off date
- **Independence** — confirm PCAOB and SEC independence requirements are satisfied before issuance; note in closing if required by engagement
- **Pro forma** — if registration statement includes pro forma financials, address separately under applicable Regulation S-X rules [VERIFY]
- **Securities Act** — U.S. offerings under Securities Act of 1933; underwriters typically rely on comfort letter as part of due diligence defense under Section 11
- **Senior complexity** — requires PCAOB-registered audit firm and experienced securities counsel; flag for engagement partner review before issuance; do not finalize without attorney sign-off on limitations language
