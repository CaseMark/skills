# Advertising Fund Contribution Agreement

Drafts a franchise advertising fund contribution agreement or addendum establishing franchisee obligations, fund administration framework, and dispute-minimizing transparency provisions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Franchise Agreement** — executed copy with date, amendment numbers, notice clauses, and defined terms (especially "Gross Sales")
2. **Fund structure decision** — standalone agreement vs. addendum; national vs. regional fund; separate account vs. accounting mechanism
3. **Contribution rate** — percentage of Gross Sales, payment frequency, and method (typically EFT aligned with royalty schedule)
4. **State franchise law requirements** — disclosure obligations, separate-account mandates, reporting requirements for the relevant jurisdiction(s)
5. **FDD representations** — current Franchise Disclosure Document language on advertising fund to ensure consistency

## Output Structure

### Document Sections

| # | Section | Key Content |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Header & Parties | Title (standalone or addendum), full legal names, effective date, reference to underlying Franchise Agreement by execution date, incorporation language |
| 2 | Recitals | Business purpose of fund, mutual benefit acknowledgment, commencement date of obligations |
| 3 | Fund Establishment | Fund scope (national/regional/system-wide), legal structure (separate entity, designated account, or accounting mechanism), stated purpose |
| 4 | Contribution Obligation | Exact percentage of Gross Sales, comprehensive Gross Sales definition (see below), payment frequency, due dates, remittance method, late payment consequences (interest, fees) |
| 5 | Fund Administration | Franchisor discretion and control, explicit non-fiduciary disclaimer, permissible uses (enumerated list), right to modify structure/rates with notice, carry-forward and borrowing provisions |
| 6 | Franchisee Rights & Limitations | No proportional-benefit guarantee, no geographic-targeting guarantee, separation from local advertising obligations, franchisor approval rights over local ads, limited audit/inspection rights |
| 7 | Financial Reporting | Reporting frequency (annual minimum), content (receipts, disbursements by category, ending balance), audit provisions, FDD integration, surplus/deficit treatment |
| 8 | Term & Termination | Coterminous with franchise agreement, modification authority and notice periods, post-termination payment obligations, surviving provisions (confidentiality), fund dissolution and asset disposition |
| 9 | Legal Compliance | Authority representations, state franchise law compliance, FDD consistency, governing law, dispute resolution, integration clause |
| 10 | Execution | Signature blocks (authorized representatives), printed names/titles/dates, personal guarantee language if entity franchisee, exhibit/schedule references |

### Gross Sales Definition Checklist

Include or expressly exclude each item:

- [ ] Sales taxes collected
- [ ] Customer returns and refunds
- [ ] Employee discounts
- [ ] Inter-company/affiliate transactions
- [ ] Gift card sales vs. redemptions
- [ ] Delivery fees and surcharges
- [ ] Online/e-commerce revenue
- [ ] Catering or wholesale revenue

### Permissible Fund Uses

Draft an enumerated list covering at minimum:

- TV, radio, print, digital, social media advertising production and placement
- Public relations and market research
- Website and app development/maintenance
- Promotional materials and point-of-sale displays
- Reasonable administrative costs (internal staff, external agencies, franchisor management fee)

### Non-Fiduciary Disclaimer Template

```
Franchisor administers the Fund in its sole business judgment and does not act
as a fiduciary to Franchisee or any other contributor with respect to Fund
assets, expenditures, or strategic allocation decisions.
```

### Post-Termination Obligations Template

```
Upon termination or expiration of the Franchise Agreement, Franchisee shall
remain liable for all accrued but unpaid contributions through the effective
date of termination. Franchisee shall have no ownership interest in Fund
assets and no right to refund of any contributions previously made.
```

## Guidelines

- **FDD consistency is mandatory** — all terms must align with Item 6 and Item 7 disclosures; contradictions create regulatory exposure
- **State-specific requirements vary** — some states mandate separate trust accounts, specific reporting intervals, or restrict permissible uses; verify for each jurisdiction [VERIFY]
- **Non-fiduciary language is essential** — without it, franchisor risks fiduciary duty claims over allocation decisions
- **Separate local ad obligations clearly** — franchisees frequently conflate fund contributions with local spend requirements; use explicit disclaimer language
- **Cap modification authority where possible** — consider maximum contribution rate increases per period to reduce franchisee pushback and regulatory scrutiny
- **Align defined terms** — import Gross Sales definition from the Franchise Agreement or redefine with express override language; do not leave ambiguous
- **No refund on termination** — standard practice but must be clearly stated to be enforceable
- **Format consistently** — match typography, numbering, and defined-term conventions of the underlying Franchise Agreement if this is an addendum
