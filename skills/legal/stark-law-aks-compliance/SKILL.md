# Stark Law & Anti-Kickback Statute Compliance Plan

Drafts a defensible compliance plan covering physician self-referral prohibitions (Stark) and anti-kickback requirements (AKS) tailored to a healthcare organization's operations and risk profile.

## Prerequisites

1. **Organizational documents** — corporate structure, service lines, covered entities
2. **Physician arrangement inventory** — employment agreements, PSAs, medical directorships, leases, JV interests, recruitment packages
3. **Referral data** — volume by physician, service type, and payer
4. **Prior compliance history** — audit findings, OIG/CMS correspondence, prior self-disclosures
5. **Current compensation data** — physician pay, FMV assessments, survey benchmarks

## Output Structure

### I. Scope & Authority

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Covered entities | All legal entities in the health system |
| Covered personnel | Employees, contractors, medical staff, board members |
| Covered relationships | All financial arrangements with referral sources |
| DHS trigger | Any designated health service under 42 U.S.C. § 1395nn |
| Authority basis | Board resolution with date, amendments |

### II. Regulatory Framework

**Stark Law (42 U.S.C. § 1395nn; 42 C.F.R. § 411.350–.389)**
- Prohibits physician self-referrals for DHS when financial relationship exists
- "Financial relationship" = ownership/investment interest OR compensation arrangement
- Strict liability — no intent requirement
- Penalties: claim denial, refund obligations, CMPs up to $100k/arrangement, False Claims Act exposure

**DHS Categories** — map to organization's actual service lines:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Clinical lab | Blood work, pathology |
| PT/OT/speech | Rehab services |
| Radiology/imaging | MRI, CT, X-ray, ultrasound |
| Radiation therapy | Oncology radiation |
| DME | Equipment supplies |
| Home health | Visiting nurse, home therapy |
| Outpatient Rx | Drugs administered in clinic |
| Inpatient/outpatient hospital | Facility services |

**Anti-Kickback Statute (42 U.S.C. § 1320a-7b(b); 42 C.F.R. § 1001.952)**
- Criminal prohibition: offering/paying/soliciting/receiving remuneration to induce referrals
- "One purpose" test — any intent to induce suffices
- Penalties: felony (up to 5 years), $100k CMP/violation, treble damages, exclusion

**Key Safe Harbors (42 C.F.R. § 1001.952)** — detail requirements for each applicable safe harbor:

| Safe Harbor | Critical Elements |
|-------------|------------------|
| Employment | ≥1 yr, FMV, not volume/value-based, commercially reasonable |
| Personal services | Written agreement, specific services, ≥1 yr term, FMV, aggregate compensation set in advance |
| Space rental | Written agreement, specific space, ≥1 yr term, FMV, not volume/value-based |
| Equipment rental | Written agreement, specific equipment, ≥1 yr term, FMV, not volume/value-based |
| Practitioner recruitment | Written agreement, benefits to underserved area, net cost to practice |

### III. Risk Assessment

**Arrangement Inventory Checklist:**
- [ ] Employed physicians — compensation structures
- [ ] Independent contractor physicians (PSAs)
- [ ] Medical directorships / consultancies
- [ ] Physician ownership / JV interests
- [ ] Space and equipment leases
- [ ] Recruitment incentives / income guarantees
- [ ] Call coverage arrangements
- [ ] Research collaborations with referral overlap

**Risk Scoring Matrix:**

| Factor | High (3) | Medium (2) | Low (1) |
|--------|----------|------------|---------|
| Referral volume | Top decile | Average | Minimal |
| DHS dollar value | >$500k/yr | $100k–$500k | <$100k |
| Compensation variability | Volume-linked components | Partially variable | Fixed only |
| FMV verification | >24 months stale | 12–24 months | <12 months current |
| Written agreement | Missing/expired | Gaps in terms | Fully compliant |

**Referral Pattern Analysis:**
- Compare per-physician referral volumes against peer benchmarks
- Flag post-arrangement referral spikes (>20% increase)
- Identify exclusive/near-exclusive referral patterns
- Validate utilization rates against clinical norms for patient population

### IV. Operational Policies

**Arrangement Lifecycle Requirements:**

| Phase | Requirement |
|-------|------------|
| Pre-execution | Submit to compliance ≥30 days before effective date |
| Agreement content | Parties, services, time commitment, compensation (specific $), term (start/end dates), termination provisions |
| FMV threshold | Third-party valuation required if >$50k/yr OR high referral volume |
| FMV sources | National/regional surveys, local market data, physician qualifications, time commitment, commercial reasonableness independent of referrals |
| Execution | Signed before services begin or compensation paid |
| Annual review | Verify continued compliance, FMV currency, services rendered |
| Renewal | Re-assess ≥60 days before expiration |

**Prohibited Practices:**
- [ ] Productivity bonuses tied to DHS volume ordered
- [ ] Per-click lease payments varying with referred-patient equipment use
- [ ] Medical director stipends that fluctuate with referrals
- [ ] Rental rates above/below FMV
- [ ] Free/discounted services to referring physicians (lab, records, billing)
- [ ] Recruitment guarantees without proper documentation and time limits

### V. Training Program

| Audience | Timing | Content Focus |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| All employees | Within 30 days of hire + annual | Basic framework, reporting obligations |
| Physician contracting staff | Quarterly | Arrangement structuring, exception/safe harbor elements |
| Business development | Quarterly | Relationship identification, prohibited inducements |
| Billing/coding | Semi-annual | DHS claim requirements, documentation |
| Physicians | Annual + onboarding | Personal liability, disclosure obligations, referral restrictions |
| Compliance staff | Ongoing | Regulatory updates, enforcement trends, audit techniques |

Requirements: scenario-based assessments, passing score threshold, documented attendance, materials updated annually for regulatory changes and enforcement actions.

### VI. Monitoring & Audit

**Continuous Monitoring:**
- Automated tracking: referral volumes by physician/service, compensation payments, utilization rates, contract expiration dates
- Alert triggers: volume threshold breaches, pattern deviations >20% from baseline, approaching expirations

**Annual Internal Audit:**
- Risk-based sampling — weight toward high-risk arrangements
- Per-arrangement verification checklist:
  - [ ] Written agreement with all required elements
  - [ ] Applicable exception/safe harbor satisfied
  - [ ] FMV supported by current documentation
  - [ ] Services actually rendered (time records, deliverables)
  - [ ] Referral patterns within normal parameters
  - [ ] Billing for DHS compliant

**External Review:** Every 3 years minimum (or after significant events: mergers, enforcement actions, major regulatory changes).

**Reporting:** Compliance dashboard to board/senior management — finding count/severity, corrective action status, training completion rates, hotline volume.

### VII. Reporting & Non-Retaliation

**Channels:**
- Third-party hotline (24/7, multilingual)
- Dedicated compliance email
- Physical mail to Compliance Officer
- Online portal (intranet)
- In-person to compliance or HR

**Non-retaliation policy** — covers termination, demotion, pay reduction, schedule changes, hostile environment. Violations subject to discipline up to termination.

**Report triage:**

| Severity | Criteria | Response |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Critical | Ongoing violation, significant financial/patient safety risk | Immediate investigation, interim suspension of arrangement |
| High | Systemic compliance failure | Expedited investigation (30 days) |
| Standard | Isolated potential violation | Standard investigation (60 days) |
| Referral | Non-compliance matter | Route to HR/operations |

### VIII. Investigation & Remediation

**Investigation Protocol:**
1. Immediate assessment — interim protective measures (suspend arrangement, restrict access)
2. Engage external counsel for potential criminal violations or significant exposure
3. Document: scope, evidence gathered, interviews, timeline, findings, regulatory citations
4. Present findings to Compliance Officer / Committee / Board per severity

**Corrective Actions:**
- Terminate or restructure non-compliant arrangements
- Recover overpayments / improper remuneration
- Discipline responsible individuals
- Enhance controls to prevent recurrence
- Self-disclose when required or strategically advisable (OIG Self-Disclosure Protocol / CMS Voluntary Refund process)
- Follow-up verification of implementation

### IX. Documentation & Retention

**Per-Arrangement File:**
- Executed agreement + all amendments
- FMV analysis with methodology and data sources
- Board/committee approval (minutes)
- Compliance review certification
- Evidence of services rendered
- Compensation payment records
- Correspondence and annual review documentation

**Retention:** Minimum 10 years from last service/payment (longer if litigation/investigation pending or anticipated). Secure storage with role-based access controls.

### X. Governance

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| Compliance Officer | Day-to-day oversight, direct access to board, authority to challenge business decisions |
| Compliance Committee | Quarterly meetings: review findings, assess corrective actions, evaluate regulatory developments, recommend enhancements |
| Board of Directors | Ultimate oversight, annual program effectiveness review, resource allocation |

**Annual Program Review:**
- Policy currency against current regulations
- Monitoring/audit effectiveness
- Training outcome assessment
- Reporting mechanism utilization analysis
- Benchmark against OIG compliance guidance and HCCA standards

**Regulatory Change Protocol:** Monitor CMS/OIG final rules, advisory opinions, enforcement actions; amend plan through formal process (draft → legal review → Committee approval → Board approval → communication/training).

## Guidelines

- Cite specific statutory/regulatory provisions throughout the plan (42 U.S.C. § 1395nn, 42 U.S.C. § 1320a-7b(b), 42 C.F.R. § 411.357, 42 C.F.R. § 1001.952)
- Use directive language ("must," "shall") not aspirational ("should generally")
- Cross-reference between sections (e.g., risk assessment → monitoring procedures)
- Verify all regulatory citations are current before finalizing [VERIFY]
- Tailor DHS categories, safe harbors, and examples to the organization's actual service lines
- Address state-level self-referral and anti-kickback statutes where applicable
- Include CMS Voluntary Self-Referral Disclosure Protocol (SRDP) and OIG Self-Disclosure Protocol procedures
- Note that AKS safe harbors are voluntary — failure to meet one doesn't automatically establish a violation, but arrangement must still lack improper intent
- Stark exceptions are mandatory — if no exception applies, the arrangement violates the statute regardless of intent
