# Oil and Gas Lease

Drafts an enforceable oil and gas lease conveying subsurface mineral rights from lessor to lessee while protecting surface rights, royalty interests, and environmental obligations under applicable state law.

## Prerequisites

1. **Property deed** — full legal description (metes and bounds, govt survey, or lot and block), recording information, acreage
2. **Mineral ownership verification** — fractional interest, existing reservations, encumbrances, co-owners
3. **Entity/capacity documents** — board resolutions, operating agreements, trust instruments, spousal joinder requirements
4. **Commercial terms** — primary term length, royalty fraction, delay rental rate, bonus payment, shut-in royalty amount
5. **State-specific requirements** — mandatory minimum royalty rates, prohibited provisions, recording format rules
6. **Environmental/regulatory context** — applicable federal and state environmental regulations, permit requirements

## Output Structure

### Section Checklist

| # | Section | Key Elements |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Parties & Capacity | Full legal names, addresses, TINs, entity type/jurisdiction, authority confirmation, co-owner inclusion, trust/community property disclosure |
| 2 | Property & Mineral Rights | Legal description per jurisdiction survey system, acreage (2 decimal places), minerals covered (oil, gas, CBM, helium, CO₂), depth limitations, fractional interest, recording references |
| 3 | Grant Clause | Exclusive rights: geological/geophysical testing, drilling/production, facilities, surface/groundwater use, pooling/unitization; address implied covenants (reasonable development, drainage protection, marketing, prudent operator) |
| 4 | Lease Term | Primary term (typically 3-5 years) with exact dates; habendum clause ("thereafter" so long as producing in paying quantities); cessation-of-production window (60-90 days); continuous drilling provisions; shut-in royalty clause |
| 5 | Financial Terms | See Royalty Structure table below |
| 6 | Operations & Surface Use | Good and workmanlike standard; setback distances; surface restoration; pipeline burial depth; agricultural interference restrictions; environmental compliance; groundwater protection (casing/cementing); produced water disposal; insurance minimums; permit obligations |
| 7 | Warranties & Indemnification | Lessor ownership warranty; proportionate reduction clause; reserved surface rights; lessee indemnification (injury, property damage, environmental, regulatory); survival post-termination |
| 8 | Assignment & Pooling | Assignment rights (notice/consent requirements); pooling/unitization provisions; proportionate royalty allocation by acreage ratio; lessor consent requirements; unit size limits; multi-formation units |
| 9 | Default & Termination | Notice-and-cure period (30-60 days); remedies (termination, damages, specific performance); voluntary surrender procedure; automatic termination triggers; post-termination obligations (plugging, removal, restoration, release) within 90-180 days |
| 10 | General Provisions | Governing law (situs state); notice method (certified mail); severability; merger clause; amendment requirements; force majeure; waiver; successors/assigns; counterparts; recordation |
| 11 | Execution | Signature blocks per party type; spousal joinder (community property states); notarial acknowledgment per situs state; corporate authority certificates; homestead waiver compliance |

### Royalty Structure

| Component | Standard Terms | Drafting Notes |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Delay rentals | $/acre/year during primary term | Specify due date, payment method, auto-termination on non-payment |
| Oil royalty | 1/8 – 1/4 of production | Market value at wellhead or posted price; state rules on deductions |
| Gas royalty | 1/8 – 1/4 of proceeds | Proceeds received or index pricing; specify deduction treatment |
| NGL/condensate | Same fraction as gas | Clarify allocation from wellstream |
| Post-production costs | Deductible or non-deductible | **Jurisdiction-critical**: some states prohibit deductions (e.g., WV, KS, CO marketable condition rule); others allow (TX, OK) [VERIFY current state law] |
| Payment timing | Monthly, within 30-60 days | Include statutory interest on late payments; minimum accumulation threshold |
| Shut-in royalty | $/acre/year | Maintains lease when well capable but not producing (no market, pipeline capacity) |

### Grant Clause Template

```
Lessor hereby grants, leases, and lets exclusively unto Lessee the
following described land in [County], [State], to wit:

[LEGAL DESCRIPTION]

containing [___] acres, more or less (the "Leased Premises"), for the
purpose and with the exclusive right of exploring, drilling for,
producing, and marketing oil, gas, and all associated hydrocarbons,
together with the right to:

(a) conduct geological and geophysical surveys, including seismic;
(b) drill, complete, maintain, and operate wells;
(c) construct roads, pipelines, tanks, and facilities reasonably
    necessary for operations;
(d) use water from the Leased Premises reasonably necessary for
    operations; and
(e) pool or unitize the Leased Premises as provided herein.
```

### Habendum Clause Template

```
This lease shall remain in force for a primary term of [___] years
from the Effective Date ("Primary Term") and as long thereafter as
oil or gas is produced in paying quantities from the Leased Premises
or lands pooled therewith, or as long as drilling or reworking
operations are conducted thereon with no cessation of more than
[60/90] consecutive days.
```

## Guidelines

- **State law governs**: Oil and gas law varies dramatically by state. Verify royalty calculation rules, implied covenant standards, and recording requirements for the situs jurisdiction
- **"Paying quantities" definition**: Clarify in the lease whether this means revenue exceeding operating costs (objective test) or whether a prudent operator would continue (subjective test) — states differ
- **Post-production cost deductions**: This is the most litigated royalty issue. Draft the royalty clause to explicitly state whether transportation, processing, compression, dehydration, and marketing costs are deductible. Do not rely on ambiguous language
- **Pooling authority**: Distinguish between voluntary pooling (lessee discretion) and forced pooling (regulatory). Specify lessor consent requirements and maximum unit acreage
- **Environmental provisions**: Require compliance with RCRA, Clean Water Act, Safe Drinking Water Act (UIC program), and state equivalents. Mandate baseline water testing where applicable
- **Surface damage**: Many states have surface damage acts requiring separate compensation to surface owners — include or cross-reference as needed [VERIFY applicability by state]
- **Recording format**: Most counties require specific margins, font sizes, and notarial forms. Verify before finalizing
- **Proportionate reduction**: Always include a proportionate reduction clause when lessor's ownership is uncertain or fractional
- **Do not** draft provisions that waive statutory minimum royalty rates where state law sets a floor
- **Do not** omit plugging and abandonment obligations — these survive lease termination and may trigger bonding requirements
