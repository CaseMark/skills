# Motion for Final Approval of Class Action Settlement

Drafts a court-ready motion demonstrating the settlement is fair, reasonable, and adequate under the applicable multi-factor test, with full procedural compliance documentation.

## Prerequisites

1. **Preliminary approval order** — date, docket number, any court directives or conditions
2. **Executed settlement agreement** — all material terms, release provisions, distribution methodology
3. **Notice materials** — proof of dissemination, publication records, claims administrator reports
4. **Objection/opt-out data** — number, substance of objections; opt-out count and rate
5. **Claims data** — submission rate, total eligible class members, estimated recovery per claimant
6. **Fee records** — time entries with hours/rates (lodestar) or fund percentage basis; litigation costs
7. **Case docket** — key filings, rulings, discovery milestones with dates and docket numbers
8. **Declarations** — class counsel, class representative, experts (if applicable), claims administrator

## Output Structure

### I. Caption & Introduction

- Full caption matching court records: court, case number, all parties
- Title: "Motion for Final Approval of Settlement and Memorandum of Points and Authorities in Support Thereof"
- Opening paragraph: reference preliminary approval order (date, docket #), summarize key settlement benefits, state the request

### II. Procedural & Litigation History

Construct a chronological narrative from uploaded pleadings and docket entries:

| Element | Detail Required |
|---|---|
| Filing date & claims | Original complaint, amendments, class allegations |
| Class certification | Date, class definition, any modifications |
| Discovery scope | Depositions, document production volume, expert work |
| Dispositive motions | Summary judgment rulings, Daubert challenges |
| Settlement negotiations | Mediation sessions, mediator identity, negotiation timeline |
| Preliminary approval | Date, conditions imposed, notice plan approved |

Purpose: demonstrate informed decision-making and litigation risk awareness.

### III. Settlement Terms

Extract from the settlement agreement and present:

- **Monetary relief** — total fund, per-claimant estimates, payment structure
- **Non-monetary relief** — injunctive terms, practice changes, compliance monitoring
- **Claims process** — eligibility criteria, submission deadlines, distribution plan
- **Release scope** — claims released, carve-outs, future claims treatment
- **Reversion/cy pres** — unclaimed fund disposition

### IV. Notice Compliance

Document constitutional due process and Rule 23(c)(2) / state equivalent satisfaction:

| Notice Method | Reach/Metric |
|---|---|
| Direct mail/email | Number sent, delivery rate |
| Publication | Outlets, dates, circulation |
| Digital/social media | Impressions, click-through |
| Settlement website | Unique visitors, page views |
| Toll-free hotline | Call volume |

Report: opt-out count and percentage, objection count and substance, claims filed vs. eligible.

### V. Fairness Analysis — Multi-Factor Test

Apply the jurisdiction's controlling test. For federal courts, address each factor under the governing circuit standard:

| Factor | Analysis Required |
|---|---|
| Strength of plaintiff's case on merits | Assess liability risks with record citations |
| Complexity, expense, duration of further litigation | Estimate remaining costs, trial length, appeal risk |
| Amount of discovery completed | Show sufficient information for informed settlement |
| Stage of proceedings | Demonstrate maturity of case |
| Risk of establishing liability | Identify specific legal/factual vulnerabilities |
| Risk of establishing damages | Class-wide damages methodology challenges |
| Risk of maintaining class through trial | Predominance/superiority concerns at trial stage |
| Ability of defendant to withstand greater judgment | Financial condition if discoverable |
| Reasonableness relative to best possible recovery | Compare settlement to realistic trial outcome range |
| Reaction of the class | Opt-out rate, objection rate, claims rate as endorsement |

Cite binding circuit/state precedent for each factor. Include persuasive authority from other jurisdictions where helpful.

### VI. Attorneys' Fees & Costs

**Percentage method:**
- State requested percentage and dollar amount
- Cite approved percentages in comparable settlements in-circuit
- Cross-check with lodestar for reasonableness

**Lodestar method:**
- Total hours × reasonable rates = lodestar
- Justify any multiplier with case-specific factors
- Cite governing fee-award standards

**Costs:** Itemize major categories (experts, e-discovery, mediation, travel). Demonstrate necessity and reasonableness.

### VII. Prayer for Relief

Request each item specifically:
1. Final approval of the settlement
2. Approval of the plan of allocation
3. Approval of attorneys' fees, costs, and service awards
4. Entry of final judgment dismissing with prejudice
5. Retention of jurisdiction for settlement enforcement

### VIII. Supporting Exhibits

Attach and reference:

| Exhibit | Content |
|---|---|
| A | Settlement Agreement |
| B | Preliminary Approval Order |
| C | Declaration of Class Counsel |
| D | Declaration of Class Representative |
| E | Claims Administrator Declaration & Report |
| F | Notice materials (all forms) |
| G | Proof of notice dissemination |
| H | List of objections with responses |
| I | Expert declarations (if applicable) |
| J | Fee/cost documentation |

Check local rules for exhibit formatting, declaration requirements, and page/word limits.
