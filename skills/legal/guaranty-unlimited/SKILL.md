# Unlimited Guaranty

Drafts a commercial-grade Unlimited Guaranty providing lenders with direct, uncapped recourse against a guarantor for all present and future borrower obligations.

## Prerequisites

1. **Underlying credit documents** — loan agreement, promissory note, or credit facility defining the Obligations being guaranteed
2. **Party identification** — full legal names of Guarantor, Borrower, and Lender
3. **Guarantor entity type** — individual or entity (determines signature block and authority requirements)
4. **Financial reporting requirements** — whether Lender requires ongoing financial statements or covenants from Guarantor

## Output Structure

Generate a complete Unlimited Guaranty with the following numbered sections:

| Section | Key Provisions |
|---|---|
| **1. Definitions** | Obligations (all present/future amounts), Credit Documents, Guarantor, Borrower, Lender |
| **2. Guaranty of Payment** | Absolute, unconditional guaranty of prompt payment and performance; payment not collection |
| **3. Unlimited Liability** | No cap on guaranteed amount; covers principal, interest, fees, costs, expenses, indemnities |
| **4. Waivers** | See checklist below |
| **5. Subrogation & Reimbursement** | Subordination/waiver until indefeasible payment in full |
| **6. Continuing Guaranty** | Covers future obligations; survives modifications, renewals, extensions |
| **7. Financial Covenants** *(if required)* | Annual financials, notice of material adverse change, other reporting |
| **8. Governing Law & Jurisdiction** | Match underlying loan documents; submission to jurisdiction |
| **9. Jury Trial Waiver** | Standard commercial waiver language |
| **10. Signature Block** | Guarantor name, title, date; entity: officer title + authority recital |

### Section 4 — Waivers Checklist

Guarantor must waive all of the following:

- [ ] Presentment and demand for payment
- [ ] Protest and notice of dishonor
- [ ] Notice of acceptance of this Guaranty
- [ ] Notice of existence, creation, or incurrence of new or additional Obligations
- [ ] Notice of any action or non-action by Borrower or Lender
- [ ] Requirement that Lender proceed first against Borrower or exhaust collateral (anti-exhaustion)
- [ ] Defense based on modification, extension, renewal, or amendment of Obligations
- [ ] Suretyship defenses (impairment of collateral, release of co-guarantors, etc.)
- [ ] Any right to require Lender to marshal assets

### Section 5 — Subrogation Language (Core)

> Until all Obligations are indefeasibly paid in full and all commitments of Lender are terminated, Guarantor waives and subordinates: (i) any right of subrogation to Lender's rights against Borrower; (ii) any right of reimbursement or indemnity from Borrower; and (iii) any right to participate in any security held by Lender.

## Guidelines

- **Guaranty of payment, not collection** — confirm Section 2 explicitly states Lender may proceed directly against Guarantor without exhausting remedies against Borrower or collateral
- **Governing law alignment** — match the governing law and forum selection clause to the underlying loan documents
- **Entity guarantors** — verify signatory has authority; include officer certificate or board resolution recital if required by Lender
- **Indefeasible payment** — use "indefeasibly paid in full" (not merely "paid in full") to protect against preference/clawback in bankruptcy
- **Future obligations** — confirm "continuing guaranty" language covers obligations arising after execution
- **Jurisdiction** — US only; state-specific suretyship statutes may impose additional requirements [VERIFY applicable state law before finalizing]
- Flag for attorney review: anti-deficiency statutes in CA, AZ, and other states may limit enforceability of certain waiver provisions [VERIFY]
