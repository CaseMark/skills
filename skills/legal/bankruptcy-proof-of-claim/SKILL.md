# Proof of Claim (Bankruptcy)

Prepares a complete proof of claim for filing with the U.S. Bankruptcy Court, compliant with FRBP 3001 and Official Form B 410.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case information** — debtor name, case number, court, chapter, bar date
2. **Creditor information** — legal name, address, authorized agent
3. **Claim basis** — contract, promissory note, invoice, judgment, statutory, tort
4. **Claim amount** — principal, interest (rate and calculation), fees, costs
5. **Security interest** — if secured: collateral description, lien perfection documents, valuation
6. **Priority status** — if priority: statutory basis (§ 507(a) category)
7. **Supporting documents** — contracts, invoices, account statements, UCC filings, judgments

## Output Structure

### Official Form B 410 — Part 1: Claim Information

- Debtor name and case number
- Creditor name and mailing address
- Amount of claim as of petition date
- Basis for claim (select and describe)
- Whether claim includes interest or charges after petition date
- Whether claim is subject to setoff

### Part 2: Secured Claim

If secured:
- Value of collateral (real property, personal property, or other)
- Basis for perfection (mortgage, deed of trust, UCC filing, judicial lien)
- Amount of secured claim (lesser of claim amount or collateral value)
- Amount of unsecured deficiency (if undersecured)
- Attach evidence of perfection

### Part 3: Priority Claim

If claiming priority under § 507(a):
- Domestic support obligations (§ 507(a)(1))
- Wages/salaries within 180 days, up to statutory cap (§ 507(a)(4))
- Employee benefit plan contributions (§ 507(a)(5))
- Tax claims (§ 507(a)(8))
- Amount entitled to priority

### Part 4: Supporting Documentation

Attach per FRBP 3001(c):
- For claims based on a writing: the writing itself (contract, note, invoice)
- For secured claims: evidence of perfection (recorded mortgage, UCC filing)
- For claims based on an open-end or revolving credit agreement: account statement
- For claims by assignee: documentation of assignment chain

### Signature and Declaration

- Signed under penalty of perjury
- Authorized representative information
- Filing agent information (if applicable)

## Claim Calculation

| Component | Amount | Basis |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Principal | $ | Contract/invoice amount |
| Pre-petition interest | $ | Rate × period (cite contractual or statutory rate) |
| Late fees/charges | $ | Per contract terms |
| Attorney's fees | $ | If contractually authorized |
| **Total claim** | $ | As of petition date |

**Post-petition amounts**: Note separately — not included in filed claim amount but may be recoverable for oversecured creditors (§ 506(b)).

## Guidelines

- **Bar date is jurisdictional** — miss it and the claim is typically disallowed
- File even if claim amount is disputed — protect the creditor's position
- Claim amount is calculated as of the petition date, not filing date
- For secured claims, the bifurcation under § 506(a) splits into secured (up to collateral value) and unsecured deficiency
- Attach all supporting documents — courts can reduce or disallow claims without documentation
- If filing on behalf of creditor, include power of attorney or authorization
- Amended claims may be filed before distribution — but monitor deadlines
- Check local rules for electronic filing requirements (most courts use CM/ECF)
