# Digital Media Law Summary

Produces a citation-verified summary of current digital media law across copyright/IP, privacy, and content liability — with practical implications for platforms, creators, and compliance teams.

## Prerequisites

1. **Time window** — specify lookback period (default: 12–18 months)
2. **Pillar focus** — all three pillars (default) or specify subset: copyright/IP, privacy, content liability
3. **Jurisdiction** — default US + significant EU/UK developments with US compliance impact
4. **Audience** — legal specialists, business/compliance teams, or mixed (affects technical depth)

## Output Structure

### 1. Executive Overview
- Top 3–5 cross-pillar developments (2–3 sentences each)
- Dominant trends and directional signals
- Highest-risk areas requiring immediate compliance attention

## Guidelines

- Bluebook format for all citations; mark uncertain citations `[VERIFY]`
- Distinguish binding precedent from persuasive authority; state jurisdictional scope for each development
- Note whether agency guidance carries force of law vs. interpretive/non-binding status
- Present circuit splits and doctrinal conflicts neutrally; identify majority or trending view
- Flag all areas where law is actively unsettled or under appellate review
- Include international developments only where they create US compliance obligations or meaningfully influence US doctrine
