# Pre-Hearing Statement of Proof

Drafts a structured prehearing submission that presents the evidentiary foundation, witness summaries, and legal arguments for a personal injury matter before a court or administrative agency.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case identifiers** — case name, number, forum/agency, hearing date, submitting party
2. **Chronological fact summary** — key dates, events, parties, and relationships
3. **Witness list** — names, contact info, relationship to case, expected testimony
4. **Documentary evidence** — medical records, photos, correspondence, reports
5. **Medical records** — providers, diagnoses, treatment history, causation opinions
6. **Damages calculation** — itemized breakdown by category and time period

## Output Structure

Draft the statement as a professional legal document with numbered paragraphs and the following sections:

### I. Introduction

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Caption | Case name, number, forum/agency |
| Hearing date | Date and location |
| Submitting party | Name, role (plaintiff/claimant) |
| Nature of claim | Concise description of dispute |
| Legal basis | Statutes, regulations, or common law grounds |
| Relief sought | Specific remedy or damages requested |

### II. Statement of Facts

- Present facts in **strict chronological order**
- Identify all parties and their relationships
- Every factual assertion must be tied to evidence that will be presented
- Maintain objective tone while building the factual foundation for the legal position

### III. Issues for Determination

- Frame each disputed question of law or fact as a precise issue statement
- Order issues logically to guide the hearing officer's analysis
- Distinguish legal questions from factual disputes

### IV. Witness Summary

For each witness, provide:

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Name | Full name |
| Contact | Address, phone |
| Relationship | Connection to case |
| Expected testimony | Key facts/opinions witness will establish |
| Purpose | How testimony supports the legal position |

For **expert witnesses**, add: qualifications, area of expertise, and substance of expected opinions.

### V. Exhibit List

| Exhibit | Description | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| A, B, C... | Document type (e.g., chiropractic records, photos, bills) | What it proves |

Organize exhibits chronologically or by category. Assign identification letters/numbers.

### VI. Medical Proof

- Identify all treating/evaluating healthcare providers
- Summarize diagnoses and treatment history
- State medical opinions on **causation** and **extent of impairment/disability**
- Connect medical evidence to applicable legal standards
- Tone: persuasive but accurate in favor of submitting party

### VII. Legal Argument

- Cite applicable statutes, regulations, and case law
- Show how evidence satisfies each legal element
- Anticipate and rebut likely opposing arguments
- Mark any uncertain citations with `[VERIFY]`

### VIII. Damages / Relief Sought

- Itemize all monetary damages by category (medical, lost wages, pain/suffering, etc.)
- Show calculation methodology and time periods covered
- For non-monetary relief, specify exact action requested and legal basis

### IX. Stipulations

- List all facts agreed to by both parties
- Identify exhibits admitted without objection
- Note any narrowing of issues

### X. Procedural History

- Summarize prior hearings or proceedings
- Note relevant discovery conducted
- **Do NOT reference settlement discussions** (inadmissible)

## Guidelines

- Use numbered paragraphs throughout for easy reference
- Every factual statement must be supported by identified evidence — no speculation
- Maintain professional, persuasive tone favoring the submitting party
- Use clear section headers to guide the reader
- Comply with applicable local rules for prehearing submissions
- Exclude any settlement communications per FRE 408 / state equivalents
- If jurisdiction-specific procedural requirements are known, incorporate them
