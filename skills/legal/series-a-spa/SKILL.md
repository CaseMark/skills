# Series A Stock Purchase Agreement

Drafts a litigation-ready, market-standard Series A SPA governing the sale of preferred stock in early-stage venture financings.

## Prerequisites

1. **Term sheet** — executed or agreed-upon term sheet with economic and governance terms
2. **Cap table** — current capitalization table showing all outstanding equity, options, warrants, convertibles
3. **Organizational documents** — Certificate of Incorporation, Bylaws, any prior financing docs
4. **Investor details** — full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions, addresses for all purchasers
5. **Transaction specifics** — share count, per-share price, aggregate raise, single vs. multiple closings
6. **Ancillary agreements** — drafts or terms for Amended & Restated COI, IRA, Voting Agreement, ROFR/Co-Sale
7. **Due diligence materials** — financials, IP portfolio, material contracts, litigation status, employment agreements

## Research Phase

Before drafting, search the client repository for:

| Item | Extract |
|------|---------|
| Term sheet | Price, shares, investors, special terms, side letters |
| Cap table | Authorized shares by class, outstanding, option pool, convertibles |
| Prior financing docs | Existing investor rights, anti-dilution provisions, protective provisions |
| Correspondence | Negotiated carve-outs, open issues, secondary sale components |
| Org docs | Notice clauses, approval requirements, existing transfer restrictions |

Determine: (a) primary-only vs. primary + secondary structure; (b) single vs. multiple closings; (c) all concurrent ancillary agreements.

## Output Structure

### Article I — Definitions & Preamble

- Full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions for Company and each Purchaser
- If secondary sales: separately identify each Selling Stockholder
- Recitals stating transaction purpose
- Schedule of Purchasers: investor name, share count, individual purchase price
- Verify all figures reconcile with aggregate totals
- Cross-reference all Transaction Documents being executed concurrently

### Article II — Purchase & Sale

| Provision | Key Terms |
|-----------|-----------|
| Issuance | Company issues; Purchasers severally (not jointly) purchase |
| Payment | Wire transfer of immediately available funds; specify account or notice mechanism (2 business days prior) |
| Delivery | Stock certificates or book-entry confirmations; registered in Purchaser names |
| Closing mechanics | Simultaneous vs. deferred closing; if multiple closings: deadline, min/max amounts, Company discretion to extend/terminate, price parity |
| Share status | Validly issued, fully paid, non-assessable, free of preemptive rights except per Transaction Documents |
| Secondary sales (if applicable) | Parallel transfer mechanics, seller delivery obligations, price allocation |

### Article III — Company Representations & Warranties

Draft the following reps, using disclosure schedules where appropriate:

- [ ] **Organization & Standing** — duly organized, validly existing, good standing (unqualified)
- [ ] **Authorization** — board approval, stockholder approval if required, binding obligation (unqualified)
- [ ] **Capitalization** — authorized shares by class, issued/outstanding, option pool detail, no undisclosed equity rights (unqualified; attach cap table schedule)
- [ ] **No Conflicts** — no violation of COI/Bylaws, contracts, laws; no liens created; required consents obtained
- [ ] **Financial Statements** — GAAP-compliant, fairly present financial condition
- [ ] **Absence of Undisclosed Liabilities** — none except per financials, ordinary course, or disclosed
- [ ] **IP** — ownership/adequate licenses, no infringement claims, reasonable protection measures, employee/contractor IP assignments
- [ ] **Material Contracts** — disclosure of contracts above threshold, affiliate transactions, exclusivity/non-competes; all in full force, no defaults
- [ ] **Litigation** — no pending/threatened actions
- [ ] **Compliance with Laws** — general + employment, environmental, export, anti-corruption
- [ ] **Tax** — timely filed returns, taxes paid, no audits/disputes
- [ ] **Employee Matters** — disclosed agreements, wage/hour compliance, no labor disputes, IP/confidentiality assignments, work authorizations
- [ ] **Data Privacy & Security** (tech companies) — compliance with privacy laws, reasonable security, no material breaches

Qualification rules:
- Fundamental reps (organization, authorization, capitalization): **NO** knowledge or materiality qualifiers
- Other reps: knowledge qualifier limited to actual knowledge of named officers after reasonable inquiry
- Define "Material Adverse Effect" excluding general economic/industry conditions and transaction-related changes

### Article IV — Seller Representations (Secondary Only)

- [ ] Good and marketable title, free of liens except Transaction Documents and securities laws
- [ ] Authority and capacity (individual or entity)
- [ ] No conflicting agreements or all restrictions waived/terminated
- [ ] Securities law compliance
- [ ] No finder's fees payable by Company or Purchasers
- [ ] Independent evaluation; reliance on own advisors
- [ ] If representative capacity: authority, no fiduciary conflicts

### Article V — Purchaser Representations

- [ ] Authority, organization, binding obligation
- [ ] No conflicts with org docs, contracts, laws
- [ ] All necessary consents obtained (investment committees, LPs, regulators)
- [ ] Investment intent — acquiring for own account, no distribution intent
- [ ] Accredited investor status per Rule 501(a) of Reg D — specify basis
- [ ] For non-U.S. purchasers: Reg S or other applicable exemption
- [ ] Adequate information received; opportunity to ask questions
- [ ] Risk acknowledgment — speculative nature, illiquidity, potential total loss
- [ ] Transfer restriction acknowledgment — restrictive legends, no registration obligation
- [ ] Disclaimer of reliance on Company/other Purchasers for advice
- [ ] ERISA compliance (fund purchasers)

### Article VI — Covenants

**Pre-closing covenants (Company):**
- Operate in ordinary course consistent with past practice
- Prohibited actions without Purchaser consent (specify % threshold):
  - Amend COI/Bylaws
  - Issue equity (except ordinary course option grants)
  - Incur debt above threshold
  - Create liens, make acquisitions, dispose of material assets
  - Declare dividends, enter affiliate transactions
  - Change accounting methods, settle litigation above threshold

**Ongoing covenants:**
- Confidentiality — all parties; survives 2-3 years post-termination; standard exceptions (legal requirement, advisors, capital sources)
- Information rights — annual audited financials, quarterly unaudited, annual budget, notice of material developments
- Share reservation — maintain authorized unissued Common Stock for full conversion
- Corporate maintenance — existence, good standing, insurance, legal compliance
- Rule 144 cooperation — maintain current information, assist holders
- Transaction-specific operational covenants as negotiated

### Article VII — Conditions Precedent

**Conditions to Purchasers' obligations:**
- [ ] Company/Seller reps true and correct in all material respects at closing
- [ ] Company/Seller covenants performed in all material respects
- [ ] No Material Adverse Change since latest financials
- [ ] Delivery of: Amended & Restated COI (certified by SOS), Amended Bylaws, executed IRA/Voting/ROFR agreements, stock certificates, legal opinion, officer certificates, good standing certificates, board/stockholder resolutions
- [ ] All regulatory approvals obtained (HSR if applicable, state securities filings)
- [ ] No governmental prohibition on transaction

**Conditions to Company's obligations:**
- [ ] Purchaser reps true and correct
- [ ] Purchaser covenants performed
- [ ] Delivery of purchase price, executed Transaction Documents, accredited investor documentation

Specify: which conditions benefit which parties; waiver requires written consent of benefited party only.

### Article VIII — Indemnification

| Element | Company/Sellers → Purchasers | Purchasers → Company/Sellers |
|---------|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Scope | Breach of reps/warranties/covenants; misrepresentation; third-party claims | Breach of own reps/warranties/covenants (several only) |
| Losses | Direct damages, attorneys' fees, investigation costs, expert fees, court costs | Same |
| Excluded damages | Consequential, punitive, special, lost profits (except fraud/willful breach) | Same |
| Survival — general reps | 12-24 months | Same |
| Survival — fundamental reps | Indefinite or statute of limitations | N/A |
| Survival — tax reps | SOL + 60 days | N/A |
| Basket/deductible | $50K-$250K or ___% of purchase price (tipping or true deductible) | N/A |
| Cap | Purchase price or ___% thereof | Individual Purchaser's investment amount |
| Carve-outs from limits | Fundamental reps, fraud, willful breach | Same |
| Seller cap | Proceeds received by such Seller | N/A |

Procedural requirements:
1. Prompt written notice specifying claim, basis, estimated losses (failure to notify relieves only to extent of material prejudice)
2. Third-party claims: indemnifying party may assume defense if it acknowledges obligation in writing and claim seeks only monetary damages
3. No settlement without indemnified party consent if it imposes non-monetary obligations, includes liability admission, or lacks full release
4. Set-off rights: notice + opportunity to dispute; exercisable only after final determination
5. Insurance/tax benefit offset: reduce losses by actual recoveries; reimburse if received post-indemnification

### Article IX — Termination

| Trigger | Who May Terminate |
|---------|-------------------|
| Outside date (60-90 days) not met | Either party (if not in material breach) |
| Governmental prohibition (final, non-appealable) | Either party |
| Other party's uncured material breach (10-20 business day cure period) | Non-breaching party |
| Material Adverse Change | Purchasers |
| Board fiduciary duty determination (with counsel advice) | Company |

Effects of termination:
- Agreement void except: confidentiality, expense allocation, governing law, dispute resolution, pre-termination breach liability
- No relief from liability for willful breach or fraud
- Each party bears own expenses unless termination caused by willful breach (breaching party reimburses)

### Article X — Miscellaneous

- **Governing law**: Delaware (or Company's incorporation state); no conflicts-of-law principles
- **Jurisdiction**: exclusive in Delaware Chancery / U.S. District Court for District of Delaware
- **Jury waiver**: knowing, voluntary, intentional
- **Notices**: writing required; effective on personal delivery, email with confirmation, 1 business day after overnight courier, 3 business days after certified mail
- **Amendment**: written instrument signed by Company + Purchasers holding [majority/⅔] of purchased shares
- **Waiver**: written, signed by waiving party; no waiver by delay or partial exercise
- **Severability**: modify to minimum extent; sever if modification impossible
- **Entire agreement**: supersedes all prior negotiations; no oral agreements
- **Counterparts**: electronic signatures valid
- **Assignment**: no assignment without consent; permitted transfers to affiliates or in M&A
- **No third-party beneficiaries** (except indemnified parties)
- **Expenses**: each party bears own; Company pays filing fees and transfer taxes
- **Further assurances**: execute additional documents as reasonably necessary
- **Publicity**: no announcements without mutual consent except as legally required
- **No partnership/agency**: independent contractors

## Guidelines

1. **Securities law compliance** — Verify Reg D exemption requirements (Rule 506(b) or 506(c)), state blue sky filings, Form D filing obligations
2. **Coordinate with ancillary docs** — Ensure consistent cross-references with Amended COI, IRA, Voting Agreement, ROFR/Co-Sale Agreement
3. **Disclosure schedules** — Flag every rep requiring a disclosure schedule; do not leave placeholder schedules empty without noting "to be completed by Company"
4. **Secondary sales** — If included, maintain parallel but separate provisions; do not commingle Company and Seller obligations
5. **Multiple closings** — If contemplated, specify whether subsequent closing investors get identical terms and whether initial investors have pro rata rights
6. **Defined terms** — Capitalize consistently; define on first use or in definitions article; verify every defined term is used and every usage is defined
7. **Internal consistency** — Cross-check all share numbers, dollar amounts, percentages, and dates across body, schedules, and recitals
8. **Mark uncertain citations** — Use [VERIFY] for any statutory reference not confirmed against current law
9. **NVCA alignment** — Follow NVCA model document conventions where the term sheet does not specify otherwise
10. **Jurisdiction-specific requirements** — Flag if Company is incorporated outside Delaware, as certain provisions may need adjustment
