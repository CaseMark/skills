# Contract Clause Checklist

Produces a structured review checklist that maps every material clause in an incoming contract against your organization's preferred positions.

## Prerequisites

- Incoming contract (counterparty draft or redline)
- Organization's clause playbook or standard positions document
- Fallback positions for key terms (if available)

## Workflow

### 1. Extract Clause Inventory

Parse the incoming contract and identify every material clause. Categorize by type: limitation of liability, indemnification, IP ownership, termination, confidentiality, governing law, assignment, force majeure, warranty, and representations.

### 2. Compare Against Standards

For each clause, compare the proposed language against the organization's preferred position. Classify as: Acceptable, Non-Standard (needs review), Unacceptable (requires change), or Missing (should be added).

### 3. Cross-Reference Related Provisions

Check for clauses that reference each other across sections. A limitation of liability on page 3 may be modified by an IP clause on page 8. Capture these cross-references in the checklist.

### 4. Generate Position-Level Summary

For each flagged clause, produce:
- **Current language**: What the counterparty proposes
- **Standard position**: What your organization prefers
- **Fallback position**: Maximum acceptable deviation
- **Recommended markup**: Suggested replacement language

### 5. Prioritize and Sequence

Order the checklist by business impact. Deal-breakers at top, negotiable items in middle, minor deviations at bottom.

## Pitfalls

- Reviewing clauses in isolation misses cross-references that change their effect
- Accepting "substantially similar" language without verifying the legal distinction
- Skipping boilerplate sections where counterparties often embed material deviations
