# Non-Compete Agreement with Litigation Provisions

Drafts a jurisdiction-tailored, enforcement-ready non-compete agreement balancing employer protection against employee mobility rights.

## Prerequisites

1. **Governing jurisdiction** — state law controlling enforceability
2. **Employee details** — name, title, department, hire date, role description, access level to confidential information
3. **Employer details** — legal entity name, formation jurisdiction, principal place of business
4. **Business interests to protect** — trade secrets, customer relationships, specialized training, proprietary methods
5. **Existing agreements** — prior employment contracts, NDAs, or restrictive covenants
6. **Desired scope** — geographic territory, duration, restricted activities

## Jurisdictional Analysis

Before drafting, determine and document:

| Factor | Research Required |
|---|---|
| **Reformation approach** | Blue-pencil (strike unenforceable parts), reformation (court modifies), or red-pencil (voids entire provision) |
| **Consideration requirements** | At-will employment sufficient, or independent consideration required for existing employees |
| **Income/role thresholds** | Minimum salary or categorical prohibitions (e.g., low-wage workers, physicians) |
| **Notice requirements** | Mandatory advance notice periods before enforcement |
| **Statutory restrictions** | State-specific statutes limiting scope, duration, or applicability |
| **FTC guidance** | Current federal regulatory posture on non-compete enforceability |
| **Pending legislation** | Any bills that could alter enforceability landscape |

## Output Structure

### 1. Title and Parties

- Full agreement title: "Non-Compete, Non-Solicitation, and Confidentiality Agreement"
- Employer: legal name (as registered with secretary of state), formation jurisdiction, principal address, DBAs
- Employee: full name, address, title, department, reporting structure, hire/promotion date
- Detail sufficient for service of process and personal jurisdiction

### 2. Recitals

Articulate **specific** legitimate business interests — not generic boilerplate:

- Proprietary systems, methodologies, and trade secrets employee will access
- Customer relationships and goodwill at stake
- Employer's investment in training and development
- Geographic markets and competitive landscape
- Sales cycle / customer retention periods relevant to measuring harm

### 3. Definitions

Draft precise definitions for:

| Term | Scope |
|---|---|
| **Confidential Information** | Specific categories (customer lists, pricing, strategies, technical specs, supplier terms) with exclusions for public/independently developed information |
| **Restricted Territory** | Based on actual market presence — named counties/MSAs, radius from work locations, or territories of customer contact |
| **Restricted Period** | 6–24 months calibrated to seniority and access level |
| **Competitive Activities** | Specific prohibited conduct: employment with identified competitors in similar roles, competing ventures, consulting using employer's confidential information |

### 4. Restrictive Covenants

Draft each with three-dimensional reasonableness (geographic + temporal + activity):

**Non-Compete:**
- Geographic: no broader than employer's actual business footprint or employee's service area
- Temporal: justified by information decay period and industry norms
- Activity: only activities that would harm protectable interests

**Non-Solicitation of Customers:**
- Limited to customers with whom employee had material contact during lookback period (typically 12–24 months)
- Cover solicitation, servicing, and acceptance of business

**Non-Solicitation of Employees:**
- Prohibit recruiting, soliciting, or hiring employer's employees, contractors, consultants

**Confidentiality:**
- Perpetual for trade secrets (per applicable trade secret law)
- Time-limited for other proprietary information
- Return/destruction of materials obligation upon termination

### 5. Consideration

Document with specificity based on timing:

| Scenario | Required Consideration |
|---|---|
| **At hire** | Employment itself + access to confidential information + training |
| **During employment** | Promotion, raise, signing bonus, access to new confidential information, or guaranteed continued employment for specified period |

State the specific value/nature to demonstrate bargained-for exchange.

### 6. Employee Acknowledgments

Employee affirms:
- [ ] Restrictions are reasonable and necessary
- [ ] Adequate consideration received
- [ ] Will not prevent earning a livelihood in non-competitive work
- [ ] Restrictions are ancillary to enforceable employment relationship
- [ ] Opportunity to consult independent legal counsel

### 7. Enforcement and Remedies

**Injunctive Relief:**
- Stipulation that monetary damages are inadequate
- Right to TRO, preliminary and permanent injunction
- Bond waiver language (where enforceable)
- Preserve right to compensatory damages, lost profits, and punitive damages where permitted

**Reformation/Severability:**
- Authorize court to modify overbroad restrictions to maximum enforceable extent
- Tailored to jurisdiction's approach (blue-pencil vs. reformation)

**Attorneys' Fees:**
- Prevailing party recovery of fees, expert costs, and litigation expenses (or mutual fee-shifting — choose based on enforcement posture)

**Tolling:**
- Restricted period extended by duration of any breach

### 8. Dispute Resolution

Specify one framework:

| Option | Key Terms to Include |
|---|---|
| **Litigation** | Exclusive venue, consent to personal jurisdiction, forum non conveniens waiver |
| **Arbitration** | Administering org, number of arbitrators, location, scope of judicial review |
| **Hybrid** | Mandatory mediation → arbitration/litigation; carve-out for injunctive relief in court |

Select forum considering favorability of non-compete law and practical enforceability.

### 9. Governing Law and Standard Provisions

- Choice of law with conflict-of-laws waiver (jurisdiction must have reasonable nexus to parties)
- Amendment: written, signed by both parties
- Non-waiver: failure to enforce ≠ waiver of future rights
- Notice: addresses + permitted delivery methods
- Assignment: binding on successors and assigns
- Entire agreement / integration clause
- Severability (working with reformation clause)

### 10. Execution

- Employer signature block: authorized representative with title, date, authority representation
- Employee signature block: printed name, signature, date
- Separate acknowledgment section for employee's voluntary execution
- Notarization lines where appropriate

## Guidelines

- **Always jurisdiction-specific** — generic agreements are unenforceable; tailor every scope dimension to applicable state law
- **Narrow > broad** — courts enforce narrow restrictions; overbroad provisions risk voiding the entire agreement in red-pencil jurisdictions
- **Recitals do heavy lifting** — detailed recitals establish reasonableness and serve as enforcement evidence
- **No bracketed placeholders** — output must be execution-ready drafted language
- **Mark uncertain citations** with `[VERIFY]` — state non-compete statutes change frequently
- **FTC compliance** — note current federal posture and flag if proposed rule would affect enforceability
- **Table of contents** for agreements exceeding 5 pages
- **Include exhibits/schedules** as needed for customer lists, competitor lists, or territory maps
- **California, Minnesota, North Dakota, Oklahoma** — near-categorical bans on non-competes; pivot to non-solicitation and NDA-only approach in these jurisdictions
