# Formal Tender Letter

Drafts a litigation-grade tender letter that serves as official notice of payment or performance, creating a protective legal record while demonstrating good faith.

## Prerequisites

1. **Governing contract/agreement** — execution date, parties, contract number, payment/performance provisions
2. **Financial documentation** — invoices, payment schedules, purchase orders, statements of work with amounts and terms
3. **Correspondence history** — payment demands, default notices, cure notifications, resolution attempts (chronological)
4. **Tender logistics** — wire instructions, bank details, delivery addresses, account numbers
5. **Party identification** — full legal names, capacities, contact information, authorized representatives

## Output Structure

### 1. Header & Party Identification

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Sender | Full legal name, capacity (individual/entity/agent), address, email, phone |
| Recipient | Same specificity as sender |
| Agreement reference | Contract number, execution date, parties, specific obligation addressed |

### 2. Formal Tender Statement

- [ ] Explicitly state this constitutes a **formal legal tender under applicable law**
- [ ] Specify exact amount, currency, and obligation satisfied
- [ ] Reference invoice numbers, payment schedules, or contractual milestones from source docs
- [ ] Detail method of tender (wire transfer, certified bank draft, cashier's check, etc.)
- [ ] Confirm unconditional readiness to perform per agreement terms
- [ ] Include **without-prejudice language** preserving all rights, defenses, and remedies

### 3. Factual & Chronological Context

Narrate the obligation history using specific dates and document citations:

- Inception of obligation
- Prior payment demands and correspondence (quote relevant passages)
- Default/breach notices and cure periods
- Resolution/negotiation attempts
- Document sender's compliance efforts
- Note any recipient failures or delays

### 4. Legal Foundation

- [ ] Cite specific contract provisions by section number
- [ ] Quote pertinent contractual language
- [ ] Reference applicable statutory requirements or common law principles
- [ ] Assert tender constitutes **complete, timely, and lawful fulfillment**
- [ ] State acceptance will satisfy and discharge sender's liability in full

### 5. Consequences of Refusal

Formally notify recipient that wrongful rejection may result in:

| Consequence | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Interest cessation | Interest stops accruing from date of tender |
| Cost shifting | Costs and attorney's fees shift to recipient |
| Loss of remedies | Recipient loses remedies for non-performance |
| Damages liability | Liability for damages from wrongful rejection |
| Breach defense | Establishes sender's defense to breach/default claims |

### 6. Reservation of Rights

Expressly reserve all rights, remedies, claims, and defenses including:
- Declaratory relief confirming tender sufficiency
- Damages for breach
- Reimbursement of costs
- Any other relief at law or in equity

### 7. Acceptance Instructions

- [ ] Specify how, where, and when payment/performance will be delivered
- [ ] Provide bank account details, delivery address, or transfer logistics
- [ ] Set acknowledgment deadline: **7–14 business days** from letter date
- [ ] Request written confirmation of acceptance

### 8. Signature Block

```
[Full Legal Name]
[Title/Capacity]
[Entity Name, if applicable]
Date: [Execution Date]
```

Consider notarization or witness signatures based on amount at stake and party relationship.

## Guidelines

- **Tone**: Formal, professional, legally authoritative — not adversarial. Demonstrate good faith while creating a protective record.
- **Specificity**: Use exact dates, amounts, contract sections, and document references throughout. Never use approximate or vague language for financial figures.
- **Dual purpose**: Every sentence should advance both settlement likelihood and litigation readiness.
- **Flow**: Identification → Context → Legal Basis → Notice → Acceptance Instructions → Close.
- **Jurisdiction**: Default U.S. law; flag state-specific tender statutes where relevant (e.g., UCC § 2-511 for goods, state-specific payment tender rules).
- **Evidentiary weight**: The letter must stand alone as a complete legal document supported by specific facts from the matter file.
- **Do not** include language that could be construed as an admission of liability beyond the specific obligation being tendered.
