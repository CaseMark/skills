# CAFA Notice of Removal

Drafts a procedurally compliant Notice of Removal invoking federal jurisdiction under CAFA for class actions filed in state court.

## Prerequisites

1. **State court complaint** — complete with all exhibits and the original filing date
2. **Service documents** — exact date and method of service on each defendant
3. **State court docket sheet** — all filings and proceedings to date
4. **Party citizenship information** — state of incorporation and principal place of business for each corporate party; domicile for individuals
5. **Class size evidence** — complaint allegations or other basis supporting 100+ putative members
6. **AIC basis** — complaint's prayer for relief, damages model, or other record support for aggregate amount exceeding $5,000,000
7. **Co-defendant consents** — written consent from all other properly served defendants (if not co-signing)

## Output Structure

### 1. Caption & Title
- Federal district court (correct division); full case style mirroring the state court caption
- State court case number
- Title: `NOTICE OF REMOVAL PURSUANT TO 28 U.S.C. §§ 1332(d), 1441, AND 1446`

### 2. Introductory Statement
- Defendant(s) removing from [State Court, County] to this Court
- Statutory basis: 28 U.S.C. §§ 1441(a), 1446, and CAFA (§ 1332(d))
- Date complaint filed; date defendant served; affirmative statement of timely filing within 30 days

### 3. CAFA Jurisdictional Elements

| Element | Threshold | What to Show |
|---|---|---|
| Minimal diversity | ≥1 plaintiff class member diverse from ≥1 defendant | Named plaintiff + defendant citizenships; corporate: state of incorp. + principal place of business |
| Amount in controversy | >$5,000,000 aggregate (excl. interest & costs) | Calculation tied to complaint allegations, estimated class size, and per-member damages/relief |
| Class size | ≥100 putative class members | Cite complaint's class definition and size allegations |

**AIC calculation note:** Show arithmetic — (estimated class size) × (per-member damages) + statutory penalties, multipliers, or attorneys' fees where recoverable. A good-faith plausible allegation is sufficient in the notice itself; no evidentiary submission required unless contested. *Dart Cherokee Basin Operating Co. v. Owens*, 574 U.S. 81 (2014).

### 4. CAFA Exceptions Analysis

Affirmatively address each exception if any risk of application exists:

| Exception | Statute | Trigger | Counter-argument to develop |
|---|---|---|---|
| Home state | § 1332(d)(4)(B) | Primary defendants + ≥2/3 of class are citizens of forum state | Show defendant citizenship outside forum or class spread across multiple states |
| Local controversy | § 1332(d)(4)(A) | ≥2/3 of class are forum citizens + significant local defendant + no similar prior class action within 3 yrs | Show class is multi-state or no significant local defendant qualifies |
| Discretionary decline | § 1332(d)(3) | Between 1/3 and 2/3 of class are forum citizens | Apply multi-factor analysis; argue federal interest predominates |

### 5. Procedural History
Chronological narrative: complaint filed → claims asserted → service on each defendant → any prior state court motions or filings.

If removal is triggered by an amended pleading or other paper rather than the initial complaint: identify the document, the date received, and the specific information that first made removability ascertainable.

### 6. Timeliness

| Trigger | Deadline | Statute |
|---|---|---|
| Initial pleading | 30 days from service of summons/complaint | § 1446(b)(1) |
| Amended pleading or other paper | 30 days from receipt | § 1446(b)(3) |
| Outer limit | 1 year from commencement (absent bad faith) | § 1446(c)(1) |

State the specific service date and filing date; show the day count explicitly.

### 7. Unanimity of Defendants
- All properly joined and served defendants must consent: § 1446(b)(2)(A)
- Satisfy by: all defendants co-sign this notice, or attach written consent from each non-signing served defendant as an exhibit
- Identify any unserved defendants; state that the unanimity requirement does not extend to them

### 8. Exhibits

| Exhibit | Contents |
|---|---|
| A | State court complaint + all attachments |
| B | Summons and proof of service for each defendant |
| C | State court docket sheet |
| D | Written consents from non-signing co-defendants (if applicable) |
| E+ | Amended complaints or other papers triggering removal (if applicable) |

### 9. Prayer for Relief
Request the Court: (1) accept this Notice as properly filed; (2) exercise CAFA jurisdiction over this action; (3) deny any motion to remand; (4) grant such further relief as is just and proper.

Include post-filing compliance statement: removing defendant will promptly file a copy of this Notice with the state court clerk and serve all parties per § 1446(d).

### 10. Signature Block
Attorney name, bar number, firm, address, phone, fax, email, and party represented. FRCP 11 certification. Date = actual filing date. Confirm compliance with local rules for e-filing signatures.

## Guidelines

- Removal statutes are strictly construed; ambiguities resolved against removal — every jurisdictional assertion must be grounded in the record or a reasonable inference therefrom
- Burden of establishing federal jurisdiction rests on the removing party
- Corporate citizenship = state of incorporation + principal place of business (nerve center test, *Hertz Corp. v. Friend*, 559 U.S. 77 (2010))
- Do not assert federal question jurisdiction under § 1331 unless independently supported; state clearly that CAFA is the sole jurisdictional basis
- If removing solely on CAFA grounds, distinguish cases where federal preemption or embedded federal questions might otherwise apply
- Check local rules for font, margins, page limits, and e-filing requirements before finalizing
- Verify whether the district requires a Civil Cover Sheet (JS-44) and/or a CAFA disclosure statement at filing
