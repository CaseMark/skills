# Form 10-K Annual Report

Drafts a complete Form 10-K Annual Report pursuant to Section 13 or 15(d) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, compliant with Regulation S-K and GAAP.

## Prerequisites

1. **Prior SEC filings** — most recent 10-K, 10-Qs, 8-Ks, and any SEC comment letters
2. **Audited financial statements** — balance sheets (2 years), income/cash flow/equity statements (3 years), and auditor's report
3. **Company records** — certificate of incorporation, bylaws, material contracts, board/committee minutes
4. **Compensation data** — payroll records, equity award agreements, employment agreements, benefit plan documents
5. **Risk and compliance materials** — ERM assessments, audit committee reports, litigation files, insurance policies
6. **Stock and ownership data** — transfer records, Schedule 13D/13G filings, Section 16 reports, equity plan details

## Output Structure

### Cover Page

| Field | Source |
|---|---|
| Registrant legal name | Charter documents |
| Fiscal year end date | Company records |
| Commission File Number | Prior filings |
| State of incorporation | Formation docs |
| IRS EIN | Tax records |
| Principal office address + phone | Corporate records |
| Filer status (LAF/AF/NAF/SRC/EGC) | Public float + revenue calculation |
| Shell company status | Checkbox disclosure |
| Aggregate market value of non-affiliate equity | Closing price, last business day of most recent completed Q2 |

### PART I

**Item 1 — Business.** Draft in this order:

1. Corporate history (formation date, state, name changes, reorganizations)
2. Products/services by segment — what, who buys, distribution, revenue %
3. Market size, share, geographic/customer segments
4. Competitive landscape — name competitors, basis of competition, advantages/disadvantages
5. Strategy and growth plans (from board materials, strategic plans)
6. Raw materials and supply chain — sole-source dependencies, contracts, alternatives
7. IP portfolio — patent count, jurisdictions, expirations, material IP litigation
8. Seasonality — quarterly revenue distribution with historical data
9. Customer concentration — disclose any customer ≥10% of revenue
10. Regulatory environment — federal/state/local/foreign; environmental compliance costs
11. Human capital — headcount (FT/PT), functions, unions, CBA expirations, labor relations

**Item 1A — Risk Factors.** Organize by category, most significant first within each:

| Category | Key Disclosures |
|---|---|
| Business/Industry | Competition, market shifts, customer concentration, supply chain |
| Technology/Cyber | Obsolescence, IT failures, cybersecurity threats, data breach impact |
| Financial | Debt covenants, liquidity constraints, interest rate/currency/commodity exposure |
| Legal/Regulatory | Pending litigation, enforcement risk, regulatory changes |
| Key Personnel | Dependency, succession planning, employment agreements |
| IP | Patent challenges, infringement risk (offensive and defensive) |
| Capital/Securities | Stock volatility, dilution, delisting risk |

For each risk: specific heading → circumstances → why exposed → trigger events → quantified consequences where possible. Update from prior year: flag new, escalated, diminished, or removed risks.

**Item 1B — Unresolved Staff Comments.** Disclose any SEC staff comments issued ≥180 days before fiscal year-end that remain unresolved. State substance, issue, company position, resolution steps. If none: affirmative negative statement.

**Item 2 — Properties.** For each material property:
- Location, character/use, approximate size, owned vs. leased
- Lease terms: expiration, renewals, escalations, purchase options; related-party lessors
- Owned: mortgages, liens, encumbrances
- Capacity utilization; expansion plans; suitability for current/future operations

**Item 3 — Legal Proceedings.** For each material proceeding (exclude ordinary routine litigation):
- Court/agency, date instituted, parties, factual basis, claims, relief sought
- Company position on merits, estimated loss/range (or explain why not estimable), insurance coverage
- Current status (discovery, pending motions, trial date, settlement talks)
- Environmental proceedings: disclose if sanctions could exceed lesser of $1M or 1% of current assets
- Q4 terminations: resolution terms, amounts, financial statement impact

**Item 4 — Mine Safety.** Disclose per Dodd-Frank §1503(a) if applicable; otherwise state "Not applicable."

### PART II

**Item 5 — Market/Equity/Dividends.**
- Exchange, ticker symbol
- Quarterly high/low prices (2 years, tabular)
- Holders of record (recent date) + estimated beneficial owners
- Dividend history (2 years, tabular); policy, payout ratio, restrictions
- Q4 share repurchases: monthly table (shares, avg price, program details, remaining authorization)

**Item 6 — Selected Financial Data.** Five-year comparative table:
- Revenue, income from continuing operations (total + per share basic/diluted), total assets, long-term obligations, redeemable preferred, dividends per share
- Footnote: acquisitions, restatements, accounting changes, unusual items affecting comparability

**Item 7 — MD&A.** Structure as follows:

1. **Executive Overview** — 1-2 pages: YoY performance summary, key metrics (revenue growth, operating margin, EPS, ROE), major events
2. **Results of Operations** — YoY and prior-year comparisons for each income statement line; quantify drivers (volume, price, acquisitions, FX); discuss trends and forward outlook
3. **Liquidity** — Operating/investing/financing cash flows; explain YoY changes; assess adequacy for short- and long-term needs
4. **Capital Resources** — Credit facilities (size, rate, maturity, drawn, available, covenants, compliance); capex plans and funding
5. **Contractual Obligations** — Table with columns: <1yr, 1-3yr, 3-5yr, >5yr for debt, capital leases, operating leases, purchase obligations, other long-term liabilities
6. **Off-Balance Sheet Arrangements** — Guarantees, indemnifications, unconsolidated entities; or affirmative negative statement
7. **Critical Accounting Estimates** — For each: nature, methodology, key assumptions, sensitivity to change, YoY changes

**Item 7A — Market Risk.** Qualitative: primary exposures (interest rate, FX, commodity, equity), risk management policies, hedging strategies, derivative use. Quantitative: choose one of tabular presentation, sensitivity analysis, or VaR per Item 305 of Reg S-K. Disclose limitations and YoY changes.

**Item 8 — Financial Statements.** Include:
- Consolidated balance sheets (2 years)
- Consolidated statements of income, comprehensive income, cash flows, stockholders' equity (3 years)
- Notes to financial statements (significant accounting policies + detailed caption notes)
- Auditor's report (GAAP opinion + ICFR opinion per SOX §404)
- Supplementary quarterly data (revenue, gross profit, net income, EPS by quarter, 2 years)

**Item 9 — Accountant Changes/Disagreements.** If change occurred: date, former/successor firms, audit committee approval, prior opinions, disagreements, reportable events, former accountant's letter (filed as exhibit). If none: affirmative negative statement.

**Item 9A — Controls and Procedures.**
- CEO/CFO evaluation of disclosure controls effectiveness as of fiscal year-end
- Management's ICFR report per SOX §404: responsibility statement, framework (COSO), effectiveness assessment, auditor attestation reference
- Material weaknesses: disclose each, explain, describe remediation
- Q4 changes to ICFR: describe or state none

**Item 9B — Other Information.** Disclose any Q4 8-K-reportable events not previously reported; Rule 10b5-1 trading arrangements adopted/modified/terminated by directors/officers. If none: "None."

### PART III

May incorporate by reference from definitive proxy statement (must be filed ≤120 days after fiscal year-end). For each item, cite specific proxy sections.

**Item 10 — Directors/Officers/Governance.**
- Directors: name, age, position, year first elected, 5-year business experience, other public boards, qualifications
- Executive officers (non-directors): name, age, position, 5-year experience
- Family relationships
- Committee membership (audit, compensation, nominating/governance) + meeting counts
- Audit committee: independence determinations, financial expert identification
- Code of ethics: access method, amendment/waiver disclosure procedure
- Director nomination process and shareholder recommendation procedures

**Item 11 — Executive Compensation.**
- CD&A: objectives, each compensation element (why, how determined, how fits overall), say-on-pay response, consultants, peer benchmarking, performance metrics, equity grant practices, ownership requirements, hedging/pledging policies, clawback policies, tax/accounting implications
- Summary Compensation Table (3 years, all NEOs)
- Grants of Plan-Based Awards table
- Outstanding Equity Awards at FYE table
- Option Exercises and Stock Vested table
- Pension Benefits table (if applicable)
- Nonqualified Deferred Compensation table (if applicable)
- Employment agreements, severance, change-in-control terms
- Director Compensation table + program narrative

**Item 12 — Security Ownership/Equity Plans.**
- Beneficial ownership table: >5% holders, each director/NEO, all directors+officers as group; footnote basis (direct, family, trust, options exercisable within 60 days)
- Change-in-control arrangements
- Equity compensation plan table: outstanding awards, weighted-average exercise price, shares available; separate approved vs. non-approved plans

**Item 13 — Related Transactions/Director Independence.**
- Related person transactions exceeding lesser of $120K or 1% of avg total assets (2 years): parties, terms, dollar amounts, arm's-length comparison, business purpose
- Review/approval policies and procedures
- Director independence determinations under applicable listing standards

**Item 14 — Principal Accounting Fees.**
- Fee table (2 years): audit, audit-related, tax, all other — with descriptions
- Pre-approval policies; de minimis exception usage %
- Audit committee independence compatibility conclusion

### PART IV

**Item 15 — Exhibits/Schedules.**

Exhibit index per Item 601 of Reg S-K. Required exhibits include:

| Exhibit | Description |
|---|---|
| 3.1, 3.2 | Certificate of incorporation + bylaws (with amendments) |
| 4.x | Instruments defining security holder rights |
| 10.x | Material contracts, employment agreements, compensation plans |
| 14.1 | Code of ethics |
| 21.1 | List of subsidiaries |
| 23.1 | Auditor consent |
| 31.1, 31.2 | SOX §302 certifications (CEO, CFO) |
| 32.1, 32.2 | SOX §906 certifications (CEO, CFO) |
| 101.x | XBRL instance + taxonomy documents |

For incorporated exhibits: cite filing type, date, and exhibit number.

**Signatures.** Two blocks:
1. "Pursuant to Section 13 or 15(d)..." — registrant name, authorized officer signature, title, date
2. "Pursuant to the Securities Exchange Act of 1934..." — PEO, PFO, PAO/controller, majority of directors; each with /s/ conformed signature, printed name, title, date

## Guidelines

- Write in **plain English** per SEC guidance; avoid boilerplate and unnecessary jargon
- Every assertion must be supported by specific facts, figures, or data from company records
- Quantify trends with percentages and dollar amounts; do not use vague qualitative language
- Ensure **internal consistency** across all sections (financial data, cross-references, exhibit citations)
- All financial statements must comply with **GAAP**; all narrative disclosure must comply with **Regulation S-K**
- Flag any area where source data is incomplete with `[DATA NEEDED]`
- Mark uncertain legal citations with `[VERIFY]`

### Filing Deadlines

| Filer Status | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Large Accelerated Filer | 60 days after FYE |
| Accelerated Filer | 75 days after FYE |
| Non-Accelerated Filer | 90 days after FYE |

Form 12b-25 available for 15-day extension (requires good-cause showing).

### Compliance Checklist

- [ ] All Items 1–15 addressed or properly cross-referenced
- [ ] All exhibits filed or incorporated by reference with complete citations
- [ ] Financial data consistent across cover page, selected data, MD&A, and financial statements
- [ ] All required signatures obtained and dated on or before filing date
- [ ] XBRL tagging complete and accurate
- [ ] SOX §302 and §906 certifications executed
- [ ] EDGAR formatting verified; submission accepted without errors
