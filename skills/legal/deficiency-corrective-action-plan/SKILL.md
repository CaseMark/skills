# Corrective Action Plan (Healthcare)

Create a regulator-ready CAP that is specific, measurable, and aligned to cited healthcare compliance deficiencies.

## Prerequisites

1. Statement of deficiencies/survey report with citations, scope/severity, dates, and cited evidence.
2. Prior regulator correspondence, exit interview notes, and any prior plans of correction.
3. Relevant policies, procedures, training curricula, and competency records.
4. RCA inputs: incident reports, audit data, QA/QAPI logs, staffing metrics, and workflow artifacts.
5. Organizational chart and role owners for each corrective action.
6. Submission deadline requirements and format requirements from the regulator/accreditor.

## Output Structure / Process

1. Document header: facility name, survey/audit date(s), regulator/accreditor, CAP version/date, primary contact, submission deadline.
2. Deficiency-by-deficiency section: include verbatim finding text, citation/standard, scope/severity, affected unit/population, date(s) of occurrence, and whether repeat or condition-level.
3. Root cause analysis: use Five Whys or fishbone; identify systemic vs. isolated causes; tie each cause to evidence from records.
4. Corrective actions: separate immediate correction from systemic prevention; include policy updates, training, staffing, workflow redesign, technology, and resource commitments.
5. Accountability: identify CAP coordinator and named owners by title; specify authority to allocate resources and enforce compliance.
6. Timeline: absolute dates for each action and milestone; align with regulator deadlines.
7. Monitoring: define process metrics, outcome metrics, sampling method, frequency, and reporting line.
8. Validation: define success thresholds, duration of sustained compliance, and objective evidence required.
9. Sustainability: integrate into routine QAPI, orientation, and periodic re-audits.
10. Consistency check: reconcile with prior statements to regulators; flag and resolve conflicts.
11. Attachments list: policies, training materials, audit tools, and evidence artifacts.

CAP Submission Checklist:

- [ ] All citations and standards verified against the survey report.
- [ ] Actions are specific, measurable, and directly mapped to root causes.
- [ ] Absolute dates provided for every action and milestone.
- [ ] Monitoring and validation thresholds are defined and objective.
- [ ] Responsibilities align with actual authority and reporting lines.
- [ ] PHI removed or de-identified; HIPAA-compliant references used.
- [ ] Consistency checked against prior regulator communications.
- [ ] Executive approvals and signatures obtained.

## Guidelines

- Use the regulator’s language for findings; do not minimize or dispute in the CAP.
- Avoid legal admissions beyond the cited findings; stay factual and precise.
- If prior corrective actions failed, state why and how this plan differs.
- Use absolute dates, not relative timeframes.
- Tie every corrective action to a root cause and a monitoring metric.
- Ensure resource commitments are realistic and budgeted.
- Redact or de-identify PHI; refer to cases by internal ID only.
- If multiple deficiencies share a cause, document linkage and one integrated fix.
- Align timelines to regulator deadlines; escalate conflicts to leadership.
- Keep tone professional, accountable, and non-defensive.
