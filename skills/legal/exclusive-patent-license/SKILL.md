# Exclusive Patent License Agreement

Drafts an arm's-length Exclusive Patent License Agreement balancing Licensor IP protection with Licensee commercialization rights.

## Prerequisites

1. **Licensed Patent portfolio** — patent numbers, application numbers, filing dates, issue dates, jurisdictions, titles (for Exhibit A)
2. **Party information** — full legal names, jurisdictions of organization, principal places of business for both Licensor and Licensee
3. **Business terms** — Territory, Field of Use, financial terms (upfront fee, royalty rate, minimums, milestones), sublicense policy
4. **Prosecution/maintenance allocation** — which party controls patent prosecution and bears costs
5. **Governing law and dispute resolution preferences**

## Output Structure

### Document Skeleton

| Section | Key Contents |
|---|---|
| Title + Preamble | "EXCLUSIVE PATENT LICENSE AGREEMENT", effective date, party identification, recitals |
| Definitions | Licensed Patents, Licensed Products, Territory, Field of Use, Net Sales, Affiliate |
| Grant of License | Exclusive license grant, scope of rights, sublicense terms |
| Financial Terms | Upfront fee, running royalties, minimum annuals, milestones, payment mechanics |
| Reporting & Audit | Quarterly reports, audit rights, underpayment threshold |
| Diligence Obligations | Commercialization milestones, cure provisions, conversion to non-exclusive |
| Patent Prosecution | Control allocation, information sharing, abandonment notice + step-in rights |
| Infringement Enforcement | Notice obligations, first right to sue, recovery allocation |
| Representations & Warranties | Authority, ownership, no conflicting grants, IP disclaimers |
| Indemnification | Reciprocal obligations, product liability, insurance requirements |
| Confidentiality | Scope, exceptions, survival period |
| Term & Termination | Duration, breach cure periods, patent challenge termination, sell-off |
| General Provisions | Governing law, dispute resolution, entire agreement, assignment, severability |
| Signatures + Exhibits | Signature blocks, Exhibit A (patent schedule) |

### Definitions — Required Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Licensed Patents** | Patents/applications in Exhibit A + continuations, CIPs, divisionals, reissues, reexaminations, extensions, foreign equivalents |
| **Licensed Products** | Products/processes/services that would infringe a valid claim of Licensed Patents absent this license |
| **Net Sales** | Gross invoiced amounts less: returns, credits, sales taxes, customs duties, freight; exclude inter-affiliate transfers except at FMV |
| **Territory** | Geographic scope (worldwide or specified countries) |
| **Field of Use** | Application/industry limitations on exclusivity (if any) |

### Grant Clause — Required Elements

- [ ] Exclusive license (Licensor excluded from practicing in licensed field unless specifically reserved)
- [ ] Rights: make, have made, use, sell, offer for sale, import, otherwise exploit
- [ ] Scope limited to Territory + Field of Use
- [ ] Sublicense rights: specify if permitted, conditions, whether Licensor consent required
- [ ] Coverage of improvements, continuations, divisionals, reissues, foreign counterparts

### Financial Terms — Structure

```
Upfront Fee:        $[AMOUNT] due upon execution
Running Royalty:    [X]% of Net Sales (Licensee + sublicensees)
Minimum Annuals:    Year 1: $[___]  Year 2: $[___]  Year 3+: $[___]
                    (running royalties credited against minimums)
Milestone Payments: [Development/regulatory milestones if applicable]
Currency:           [USD/other]
Withholding Tax:    [Allocation]
```

### Reporting & Audit

- Quarterly reports due within **30 days** of quarter-end
- Report contents: units sold, gross sales, deductions, Net Sales, royalty calculation, sublicensee list
- Royalty payment accompanies each report
- Audit: independent CPA, once/year max, reasonable notice, normal business hours
- **Underpayment >5%** → Licensee bears audit cost + pays deficiency + interest

### Diligence Framework

- Commercially reasonable efforts to develop + commercialize throughout Territory
- Milestone schedule with target dates (e.g., clinical trials, regulatory filing, first commercial sale, sales thresholds)
- Failure to meet milestone = material breach
- Cure options: (a) achieve milestone within cure period, OR (b) license converts to non-exclusive

### Patent Prosecution Allocation

| Scenario | Controlling Party | Obligations |
|---|---|---|
| Licensor controls | Licensor | Keep Licensee informed, share substantive correspondence, allow comment before filing |
| Licensee controls | Licensee | Same information-sharing obligations to Licensor |
| Abandonment | Controlling party | Advance written notice → other party may assume responsibility + costs |

Prosecution costs may **not** offset royalties unless specifically agreed.

### Infringement Enforcement

1. Prompt written notice of suspected infringement by either party
2. **Licensee has first right to sue** (own expense, own counsel)
3. Decision deadline: **90 days** from notice
4. If Licensee declines/fails to act → right reverts to Licensor
5. Non-suing party cooperates, joins if needed for standing
6. Recovery allocation: suing party recoups costs first → remainder split per agreed formula

### Warranties & Disclaimers

**Licensor represents:**
- Owns/controls Licensed Patents; right to grant license
- Licensed Patents subsisting; to Licensor's knowledge valid and enforceable
- No conflicting grants

**Critical disclaimer (caps required):**
> LICENSOR MAKES NO WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING ANY IMPLIED WARRANTY OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, OR ANY WARRANTY THAT THE LICENSED PATENTS ARE VALID OR THAT LICENSED PRODUCTS WILL NOT INFRINGE THIRD-PARTY RIGHTS.

### Indemnification

| Party | Covers |
|---|---|
| Licensee indemnifies Licensor | Product liability, regulatory violations, third-party infringement from commercialization |
| Licensor indemnifies Licensee | Lack of authority to grant license, breach of third-party rights by the grant itself |

- Standard procedures: prompt notice, indemnitor controls defense, cooperation required
- Licensee maintains product liability insurance (minimum limits specified), Licensor named as additional insured

### Term & Termination

| Trigger | Notice/Cure |
|---|---|
| Expiration | Last-to-expire valid claim of Licensed Patents |
| Material breach | 30 days written notice to cure (90 days for payment breaches) |
| Patent challenge by Licensee | Immediate termination by Licensor |
| Licensee convenience | 90 days written notice |

**Post-termination effects:**
- All licenses terminate; Licensee ceases making/selling (reasonable sell-off period for existing inventory)
- All unpaid royalties immediately due
- Surviving provisions: confidentiality, indemnification, dispute resolution

### Exhibits

- **Exhibit A**: Licensed Patent schedule (patent numbers, application numbers, filing dates, issue dates, jurisdictions, titles)
- **Exhibit B** (optional): Field of Use definition if complex
- **Exhibit C** (optional): Pre-approved sublicense form
- **Exhibit D** (optional): Detailed development milestones

## Guidelines

- Draft exclusivity grant precisely — confirm whether Licensor retains any rights (e.g., research use, government obligations)
- Net Sales deductions must be **specifically enumerated**; avoid open-ended deduction categories
- Minimum annual royalties protect against shelving; calibrate to reasonable commercialization timeline
- Patent challenge termination clauses face enforceability scrutiny in some jurisdictions — flag for review [VERIFY: Lear v. Ashcroft implications on licensee estoppel post-MedImmune v. Genentech, 549 U.S. 118 (2007)]
- For life sciences/pharma Licensed Products, expand diligence milestones to cover clinical trial phases and regulatory approval stages
- Sublicense terms should address what happens to sublicenses upon termination of the head license
- If Territory includes multiple countries, address foreign filing and maintenance obligations per jurisdiction
- Use defined terms consistently (capitalized); include table of contents if agreement exceeds 10 pages
- All bracketed placeholders must be completed based on negotiated business terms
