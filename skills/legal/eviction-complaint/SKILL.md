# Unlawful Detainer — Eviction Complaint

Drafts a jurisdiction-compliant Unlawful Detainer complaint that establishes grounds for possession recovery and withstands demurrer.

## Prerequisites

1. **Lease agreement** — written or description of oral terms (rent amount, due date, duration, material provisions)
2. **Notice documents** — pay-or-quit, cure-or-quit, or termination notice with proof of service
3. **Rent ledger** — payment history showing arrearage amounts and dates
4. **Property identification** — street address, unit number, APN or legal description
5. **Party information** — full legal names of all plaintiffs (as title is held) and all tenant-defendants
6. **Jurisdiction** — state, county, and court where filing

Flag any deficiency — improper notice is the #1 defense.

### Phase 2: Draft Complaint

#### Caption
- Court's full official name, department/division if required
- Plaintiff name exactly as title is held (individual, LLC, trust, etc.)
- All lease-named tenants as defendants; include "DOES 1–10" for unknown occupants
- Title: "COMPLAINT FOR UNLAWFUL DETAINER"

#### Jurisdictional Allegations
```
1. This court has jurisdiction under [State Code §___].
2. Venue is proper because the subject property is located at
   [address], [city], [county], [state] [APN if available],
   within this court's territorial jurisdiction.
3. [If required: Amount in controversy / jurisdictional classification]
```

#### Factual Allegations Checklist

- [ ] Formation of tenancy (date, parties, written/oral, material terms)
- [ ] Property description (address, unit, legal description)
- [ ] Rent terms (amount, due date, payment method)
- [ ] Specific ground for eviction:
  - **Non-payment**: each missed payment date + amount; total arrearage through filing date
  - **Lease violation**: specific provision breached, date(s), description of conduct
  - **Holdover**: lease expiration date, refusal to vacate
- [ ] Notice: type, date served, method, statutory authority, expiration date
- [ ] Attach notice + proof of service as Exhibit A
- [ ] Tenant failed to comply/vacate within notice period
- [ ] Tenant remains in possession without right

#### Prayer for Relief

```
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff prays for judgment as follows:
1. Possession and restitution of the premises at [address];
2. Past-due rent of $[amount] through [date], plus $[daily rate]/day
   thereafter until possession is restored;
3. Late fees of $[amount] per [lease provision §__];
4. Reasonable attorney's fees per [lease §__ / statute §__];
5. Costs of suit;
6. Such other relief as the court deems just.
```

Include daily holdover rate = monthly rent ÷ 30.

### Phase 3: Formatting & Exhibits

- Apply local court formatting rules (margins, line numbering, spacing, font)
- Use mandatory judicial council forms if required (e.g., CA Form UD-100)
- Attach and label exhibits:
  - Exhibit A: Notice to tenant + proof of service
  - Exhibit B: Lease agreement (relevant pages)
  - Exhibit C: Rent ledger (if non-payment)

## Guidelines

- **Notice is dispositive** — if notice is defective, advise re-service before filing
- **Name matching** — plaintiff must match title holder; defendants must match lease signatories
- **Monetary calculations** — itemize all amounts; courts reject lump-sum allegations
- **Do not include** FED/FRE arguments — unlawful detainer is summary proceeding
- **Rent control / just-cause jurisdictions** — check for additional requirements (e.g., SF, LA, NYC, Portland) and add required allegations
- **COVID-era protections** — verify any remaining moratorium or phase-out provisions in jurisdiction [VERIFY]
- **Anti-retaliation** — do not file if facts suggest retaliatory eviction risk; flag to attorney
- **Habitability** — if tenant has raised habitability complaints, flag as potential defense
- **All statutory citations must be verified** against current code for the filing jurisdiction
