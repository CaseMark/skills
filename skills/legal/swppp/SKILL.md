# Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP)

Drafts a construction SWPPP compliant with 40 CFR Part 122, EPA Construction General Permit (CGP), and applicable state NPDES requirements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Project details** — name, location (coordinates or legal description), total disturbance acreage, project timeline
2. **Operator identification** — entity with day-to-day operational control of construction activities
3. **Site characterization data** — soil types, topography, drainage patterns, existing vegetation
4. **Receiving waters** — all water bodies receiving discharge, including distance and direction; any impaired waters (303(d) listed) or TMDL watersheds
5. **Environmental features** — wetlands, endangered species habitat, historical properties within or adjacent to site
6. **Existing permits** — NPDES permit number, state construction general permit, any MS4 requirements
7. **Site plans / grading plans** — if available

## Output Structure

### 1. Cover Page & Certifications

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Project name & NPDES permit # | Required |
| Operator name, address, contact | Required |
| Preparer name & qualifications | Required |
| PE seal | If required by state |
| Signature block | Operator or authorized representative — name, title, date |
| Certification language | "I certify under penalty of law that this document and all attachments were prepared under my direction or supervision in accordance with a system designed to assure that qualified personnel properly gathered and evaluated the information submitted..." [use full 40 CFR 122.22 language] |

### 2. Site Description

- Project narrative (scope, phases, timeline)
- Total site area vs. area of disturbance
- Soil types (reference NRCS Web Soil Survey data)
- Pre- and post-construction drainage patterns with flow arrows
- Receiving waters table:

| Receiving Water | Classification | 303(d) Listed? | TMDL? | Distance from Site |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| {name} | {class} | Y/N | Y/N | {ft/mi, direction} |

- Endangered species / historic preservation eligibility determination

### 3. Best Management Practices (BMPs)

Organize BMPs into these categories with a table for each:

**Erosion & Sediment Controls**

| BMP | Location | Install Timing | Design Spec | Maintenance Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Silt fence | Downslope perimeter | Before grading | DOT/state std detail | Inspect 7-day / post-storm; repair within 24 hrs |
| Sediment basin | Low point of drainage area | Before grading | Size per CGP (3,600 cf/acre or state requirement) | Dewater & clean at 50% capacity |
| Inlet protection | All active storm inlets | Before grading | Per manufacturer / state detail | Clean when sediment reaches 50% capacity |
| Stabilized entrance | Each construction access point | Before traffic | Min 50 ft × 20 ft, 6" stone | Replenish stone as needed; sweep tracked sediment daily |
| Temporary seeding | Inactive areas >14 days | Within 14 days of last disturbance | State seed mix; apply per agronomic rates | Re-seed bare areas; maintain until permanent stabilization |

**Good Housekeeping & Pollution Prevention**

| Practice | Standard |
|---|---|
| Material storage | Secondary containment for liquids; covered storage for erodible materials |
| Concrete washout | Lined, bermed area ≥10 ft from drainage; no direct discharge |
| Spill prevention | Spill kits within 50 ft of fuel/chemical storage; report spills per state thresholds |
| Waste management | Dumpsters covered; no burying or burning debris; hazmat manifested per RCRA |
| Sanitary waste | Licensed portable facilities; serviced weekly minimum |
| Vehicle maintenance | Drip pans required; no on-site fluid changes unless over containment |

**Stabilization Practices**

| Condition | Deadline | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Areas inactive <14 days | 14 days from last disturbance (7 days if discharging to impaired water) | Temporary seed, mulch, or erosion blanket |
| Final grade reached | Per CGP timeline (typically 14 days) | Permanent seed + mulch or sod |
| Steep slopes (>3:1) | Immediately after grading | Erosion control blanket + seed |

### 4. Inspection & Monitoring Program

**Inspection Schedule**

| Trigger | Frequency | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Routine | Every 7 calendar days | During active construction |
| Storm event (≥0.5 in.) | Within 24 hours after discharge ends | Document rainfall amount |
| Reduced frequency option | Once per month | Only if site is temporarily stabilized and CGP allows |

**Inspector Qualifications**: Qualified person with knowledge of SWPPP, BMPs, and CGP requirements. States may require Certified Professional in Erosion and Sediment Control (CPESC) or equivalent.

**Inspection Checklist** — each inspection must document:

- [ ] All BMPs operational and maintained
- [ ] No evidence of off-site sediment discharge
- [ ] All slopes and disturbed areas assessed
- [ ] Sediment accumulation levels at basins/traps
- [ ] Construction entrances functioning
- [ ] Material storage areas properly contained
- [ ] New stormwater discharge points identified
- [ ] Corrective actions from prior inspection completed

**Corrective Actions**: Initiate within 24 hours of identification; complete before next storm event or within 7 days, whichever is sooner. Document all corrections in SWPPP log.

### 5. Recordkeeping

| Record | Retention | Location |
|---|---|---|
| SWPPP (current version) | Duration of permit + 3 years | On-site or readily accessible |
| Inspection reports | 3 years after NOT filed | On-site or central office |
| Rainfall data | 3 years after NOT filed | Attached to inspection reports |
| BMP maintenance logs | 3 years after NOT filed | SWPPP appendix |
| SWPPP amendments | All versions retained 3 years | SWPPP appendix |
| Training records | 3 years after NOT filed | SWPPP appendix |
| Agency correspondence | 3 years after NOT filed | SWPPP appendix |

NOT = Notice of Termination.

### 6. SWPPP Amendments

The SWPPP is a living document. Amend when:
- BMPs are ineffective and must be replaced
- Site conditions change (new phases, redesign)
- Inspection reveals new pollutant sources
- Regulatory agency directs changes
- Operator changes

Log all amendments with date, description, and responsible party.

### 7. Personnel & Training

- Identify SWPPP administrator by name and contact
- All personnel involved in BMP installation, inspection, or maintenance must receive initial training + annual refresher
- Training topics: SWPPP requirements, BMP installation/maintenance, spill response, reporting obligations

### 8. Appendices

- A: Site maps (existing conditions, grading plan, BMP locations, drainage areas)
- B: BMP detail drawings
- C: NPDES permit / state CGP authorization
- D: NOT (when filed)
- E: Inspection report forms (blank + completed)
- F: Subcontractor certifications
- G: Endangered species / historic preservation documentation

## Guidelines

- **Tailor to state requirements** — CGP varies by state; always confirm state-specific BMP design standards, inspection frequencies, stabilization deadlines, and reporting thresholds
- **303(d) / TMDL waters** — trigger enhanced BMPs and potentially numeric effluent limits; flag prominently
- **MS4 permits** — check if the local MS4 imposes additional requirements beyond the CGP
- **Multi-operator sites** — each operator needs their own SWPPP or clearly defined responsibilities within a shared plan
- **Do not certify** — include signature blocks but note that the operator (not the drafter) must sign
- **Federal citations**: 40 CFR § 122.26, 40 CFR § 450.21, CWA § 402 [VERIFY specific CGP version year against current EPA issuance]
- **Mark all state-specific citations** with `[VERIFY]` unless the user has provided the applicable state CGP
- **Linear projects** (roads, utilities) — use phased SWPPP approach with separate BMP plans per active segment
