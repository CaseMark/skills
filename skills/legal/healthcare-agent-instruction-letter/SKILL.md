# Healthcare Agent Instruction Letter

Drafts a concise, scenario-based, non-binding values letter in the client’s voice to empower the health care agent and reduce ambiguity at the bedside.

## Prerequisites

1. Confirm client identity, preferred name, and jurisdiction.
2. Collect executed legal documents (name/date/type): health care power of attorney or proxy, advance directive, POLST/MOLST if present, HIPAA release, and any related estate documents.
3. Identify primary agent and alternates (full names, roles, and communication preferences).
4. Capture high-signal values: identity, core goals, acceptable quality-of-life levels, tolerance for cognition loss, dependence, pain, and institutional care.
5. Capture key clinical preference domains: CPR/intubation/ventilation, artificial nutrition/hydration, antibiotics, surgery/recovery expectations, time-limited trials, hospice, comfort care emphasis.
6. Capture spiritual/cultural commitments that affect care, rituals, diet, modesty, clergy contact, and decision-making frameworks.
7. Identify likely conflict vectors and family communication plan.
8. Capture preferred tone, distribution timing, and where originals/keys are stored.
9. Ask for missing facts explicitly; never invent names, dates, diagnoses, beliefs, or legal dates.

## Output Structure / Process

| Phase | Required Action | Expected Output |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Validate non-binding status | Short opening statement that this is guidance, not a substitute for operative directives |
| 2 | Harmonize documents | Exact title(s) and dates of governing documents; explicit instruction hierarchy note |
| 3 | Elicit decision framework | Clear Big-Three thresholds: cognition, independence, suffering |
| 4 | Draft scenario logic | Decision sections by treatment category with conditional examples |
| 5 | Add faith/culture mechanics | Practical care requests tied to beliefs and limits (without legal overstatement) |
| 6 | Address conflict protocol | Script for disagreements, consults, and clinician-facing communication |
| 7 | Add logistics and distribution | Where to share, with whom, now vs. incapacity only |
| 8 | Run legal/quality controls | Mismatch flags, ambiguity reduction, attorney-review gate |

### Required writing constraints

1. Use client voice, plain language, short headings, and one-to-three page length.
2. Use conditional framing: “if/when…then…” and scenario examples.
3. Never include medical orders or statutory language that should belong in POLST/MOLST/clinic forms.
4. Every section must be usable under stress by one reader in minutes.

## Required Content Checks (run after drafting)

1. Non-binding statement appears in first paragraph and near ending.
2. No contradiction with known operative legal documents; all discrepancies are flagged in a standalone section.
3. No invented facts; bracketed placeholders remain where missing.
4. Treatment guidance is scenario-based, not pure slogans.
5. Conflict instructions explicitly protect the agent’s authority consistent with legal appointment.
6. No absolute commands that imply standalone legal effect of this letter.
7. Includes attorney review gate and confidentiality note for disclosure scope.

## Jurisdictional Guardrails

| Topic | Rule |
|---|---|
| Terminology | Match client documents’ terminology: health care proxy, medical power of attorney, advance health care directive, or equivalent. |
| POLST/MOLST | Do not draft as an order form; recommend separate legal/clinical workflow if appropriate [VERIFY]. |
| New York evidentiary burden | Use heightened specificity because some jurisdictions reportedly require clear-and-convincing evidence in life-sustaining-treatment disputes (example often cited: *In re Storar*, 52 N.Y.2d 363 (1981)) [VERIFY]. |
| MAID | Do not imply the agent can request aid-in-dying on the principal’s behalf; this is separate statutory process [VERIFY]. |
| Default surrogacy | Mention only client-selected legal chain; do not summarize statutes without attorney verification. |

## Guidelines

- Do keep language operational: who, when, what threshold, what to do.
- Do preserve the client’s style (intimate, spiritual, direct, or concise).
- Do include placeholders for absent details instead of fabricating.
- Do instruct attorney review before final use and prior to document execution workflow.

- Do not treat this as an enforceable legal order or substitute advance directive.
- Do not include medical advice or treatment-level guarantees.
- Do not create contradictions with signed legal documents.
- Do not include inflammatory family language that can weaponize disputes.

- Confidentiality: confirm client control over disclosure scope before advising distribution to clinicians or health systems.
- Attorney review is mandatory; draft output is informational assist only and does not itself constitute legal advice.
