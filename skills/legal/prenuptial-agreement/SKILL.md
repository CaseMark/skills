# Prenuptial / Postnuptial Agreement

Drafts an enforceable marital agreement with full financial disclosure, property classification, support provisions, and procedural safeguards tailored to the governing US jurisdiction.

## Prerequisites

1. **Agreement type** — prenuptial (pre-marriage) or postnuptial (during marriage)
2. **Governing jurisdiction** — state of execution and residence of each party
3. **Financial disclosures** — asset inventories, account statements, business valuations, appraisals, income documentation for both parties
4. **Governing contract framework** — identify whether jurisdiction follows UPAA, UPMAA, or its own statutory scheme
5. **Existing documents** — prior marital agreements, estate plans, business operating agreements, correspondence between parties

## Output Structure

### 1. Jurisdictional Analysis

Determine before drafting:

| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| Framework | UPAA / UPMAA / state-specific statute |
| Disclosure standard | Full & fair / reasonable / statutory schedule |
| Spousal support waiver | Permitted / limited / unconscionability review at enforcement |
| Waiting period | State-required minimum before wedding (e.g., 30 days) |
| Independent counsel | Required / recommended / waivable |
| Execution formalities | Notarization, witnesses, self-proving affidavit |

### 2. Financial Disclosure Schedules (Exhibit A / B)

Each party must disclose:

- Real property — legal descriptions, FMV, encumbrances
- Financial accounts — institution, type, approximate balance
- Retirement accounts — plan type, vested amount, beneficiary
- Business interests — entity, ownership %, valuation methodology
- Intellectual property — description, estimated value
- Vehicles and tangible personal property above threshold
- All liabilities — secured, unsecured, contingent, tax
- Income from all sources — employment, business, investment, rental
- Monthly expenses and regular financial obligations

Include sworn representations under penalty of perjury affirming completeness. State that material misrepresentation or concealment may render agreement voidable.

### 3. Property Classification

Draft provisions covering:

- **Separate property** — pre-marital assets (reference disclosure schedules), gifts, inheritances received individually
- **Marital property** — assets acquired through earnings/joint effort during marriage
- **Appreciation** — distinguish passive appreciation (remains separate) vs. active appreciation from marital effort (specify treatment)
- **Commingling rules** — when separate property loses character; tracing methodology
- **Marital residence** — separate property of purchaser / joint property / division formula; occupancy rights, buyout options, refinancing obligations
- **Business interests** — business remains separate; specify compensation method for marital contribution to growth (percentage interest / formula payment / explicit waiver)

### 4. Debt Allocation

- Pre-marital debts: sole responsibility of incurring party with cross-indemnification and hold-harmless
- Marital debts: allocate by incurring party / equal division / proportional to income
- Joint debts: designate primary responsibility and contribution obligations
- New debt controls: spousal consent threshold for debts above $[amount]; prohibition on pledging other party's separate property
- Tax liabilities: joint return allocation, separate business income liability, audit procedures
- Dissolution: assumption/indemnification, refinancing deadlines, asset sale waterfall

### 5. Spousal Support

**If full waiver:**
> Each party forever and irrevocably waives any right to seek temporary or permanent spousal support, maintenance, or alimony regardless of marriage duration, circumstances of dissolution, financial condition, or changed circumstances.

Include acknowledgments that each party:
- Understands the significance of the waiver
- Has evaluated current and future earning capacity
- Has considered ability to be self-supporting
- Makes the waiver voluntarily

**If limited support preserved:** specify triggering conditions, duration caps, amount caps, termination events, and whether modifiable.

**Jurisdiction check:** If state requires independent counsel for support waivers or applies unconscionability review at enforcement, add appropriate safeguards and fairness-at-enforcement language.

### 6. Estate and Death Provisions

- Waiver of elective share / statutory spousal share / community property rights (if agreed)
- Life insurance: maintenance requirements, minimum coverage, ownership, beneficiary designation, survival of obligations post-divorce
- Retirement accounts: waiver of survivor benefits, QDRO rights, ERISA protections
- Estate planning coordination: agreement supersedes contrary will/trust provisions; commitment to execute consistent documents
- Prior-relationship children: carve-outs protecting inheritance rights
- Delineation of assets subject to agreement vs. freely disposable by will/trust

### 7. Procedural Fairness Recitals

Include all of the following:

- [ ] Each party advised of right to independent counsel
- [ ] Adequate opportunity to retain and consult separate attorneys
- [ ] If represented: attorney acknowledgment sections confirming review, explanation, and belief in voluntary consent
- [ ] If counsel waived: knowing and voluntary waiver after explanation of significance
- [ ] Prenuptial timing: executed ≥30 days before wedding (or per jurisdiction)
- [ ] No duress, coercion, fraud, or undue influence
- [ ] Complete financial disclosures provided and reviewed
- [ ] Adequate time to review, consider, and negotiate terms
- [ ] If sophistication disparity: extended review period, mandatory counsel, explicit acknowledgments

### 8. Modification, Sunset, and Review

- Amendments: written, signed by both parties, same execution formalities as original
- Sunset clause (if desired): automatic termination after [X] years or upon specified event (e.g., birth of children); specify fallback legal regime
- Periodic review: every [X] years or upon significant life changes; process for review
- No oral modifications; no waiver by course of conduct

### 9. Dispute Resolution

- **Governing law:** [State] without regard to conflict-of-law principles
- **Venue:** [County/District]
- **Process:** Mandatory mediation → litigation (or binding arbitration if preferred)
- **Fees:** prevailing party / proportional to resources / each bears own
- **Severability:** invalid provisions severed; remainder enforceable; reform to parties' intent

### 10. General Provisions

- Integration/merger clause superseding all prior negotiations
- Representations: each party has read, understands, signs voluntarily
- Relationship to prior agreements: revokes / supersedes / supplements
- Confidentiality of terms
- Notice: method, addresses, effective date
- Binding on heirs, executors, administrators, assigns
- Covenant to execute additional instruments (deeds, beneficiary designations, QDROs)
- Headings for convenience only

### 11. Execution Block

Assemble per jurisdiction:

| Element | Required? |
|---|---|
| Party signatures with printed name and date | Always |
| Witness attestation (2 disinterested witnesses) | Check jurisdiction |
| Notarial acknowledgment with commission expiration, county, seal | Check jurisdiction |
| Attorney certificates of independent legal advice | Check jurisdiction |
| Self-proving affidavit | If permitted |

## Guidelines

- Verify whether jurisdiction follows UPAA, UPMAA, or independent framework before drafting — this controls disclosure requirements, support waiver enforceability, and execution formalities
- For prenuptial agreements, confirm execution timing satisfies any state-mandated waiting period before the wedding
- Never draft spousal support waivers without checking whether the jurisdiction applies unconscionability review at the time of enforcement (not just execution)
- Flag any provision that may be unenforceable in the identified jurisdiction with `[JURISDICTION NOTE]`
- Mark uncertain statutory citations with `[VERIFY]`
- If either party lacks independent counsel, strengthen procedural safeguards and include explicit waiver-of-counsel acknowledgments
- Financial disclosures must be specific enough for meaningful evaluation — approximate balances, not vague ranges
- Address ERISA preemption issues when waiving retirement account survivor benefits
- Include execution instructions specifying signing sequence, number of copies, and any documents that must be executed simultaneously
