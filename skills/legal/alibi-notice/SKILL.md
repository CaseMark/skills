# Notice of Alibi Defense

Drafts a Rule 12.1-compliant alibi notice that satisfies disclosure obligations while preserving defense strategy and trial flexibility.

## Prerequisites

1. **Charging documents** — indictment or information with exact dates, times, and location of alleged offense
2. **Alibi details** — defendant's actual location(s) during the relevant time period with full addresses
3. **Alibi witnesses** — full legal names, current addresses, phone numbers, and expected testimony
4. **Corroborating evidence** — receipts, surveillance footage, GPS data, access logs, employment records, timestamped communications, or other documentary proof
5. **Jurisdictional rules** — confirm whether Fed. R. Crim. P. 12.1 or state equivalent applies; determine filing deadline (typically calculated from arraignment, indictment, or court order)
6. **Local court formatting rules** — font, spacing, margins, e-filing requirements

## Output Structure

### 1. Court Caption

Standard criminal case caption per local rules:
- Full court name with jurisdictional designator
- Case number as assigned by clerk
- Defendant's full legal name (matching charging documents)
- Prosecuting entity name
- Document title: "Notice of Alibi Defense" or "Defendant's Notice of Alibi"

### 2. Introductory Statement

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Defendant ID | Full legal name |
| Purpose | Express intent to present alibi defense at trial |
| Rule citation | Fed. R. Crim. P. 12.1 or state equivalent, by exact designation |
| Offense timing | Date and time as charged |
| Core assertion | Defendant was not present at alleged crime scene during relevant period |
| Compliance statement | Good faith filing within prescribed deadline |

### 3. Alibi Specification

For each alibi location, provide:

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Date/time range | Must encompass full period alleged in charging document, with margins |
| Location | Full street address, building/business name, suite/unit, city, state, ZIP |
| Activities | Nature and sequence of defendant's activities at location |
| Corroborating evidence | Timestamped receipts, card transactions, surveillance, GPS, access logs, social media check-ins, parking records |

> Specificity standard: "Riverside Bistro, 847 Waterfront Drive, Suite 101, Portland, OR 97204, reservation at 7:30 PM, departed approximately 9:45 PM" — not "a restaurant."

### 4. Alibi Witness Disclosure

For each witness:

```
Name:               [Full legal name per official ID]
Address:            [Current residential address]
Phone/Email:        [If appropriate under local practice]
Relationship:       [To defendant or alibi location]
Expected testimony: [Concise summary of facts witness will establish]
```

Organize witnesses by chronological sequence of events or by alibi location.

Include supplementation reservation:
> "The defendant reserves the right to call additional alibi witnesses whose identities may become known through continuing investigation and will supplement this notice within the time permitted by rule."

### 5. Reciprocal Discovery Demand

Demand under Fed. R. Crim. P. 12.1(b) (or state equivalent) that prosecution disclose within the rule-specified timeframe (14 days under federal rule):

- [ ] Names, addresses, and expected testimony of rebuttal witnesses
- [ ] Physical/documentary evidence placing defendant at crime scene
- [ ] Surveillance footage, electronic data, or expert testimony contradicting alibi

Use mandatory language reflecting prosecution's legal obligation.

### 6. Legal Authority

- Cite controlling procedural rule with full citation
- Cite jurisdiction-specific case law on alibi notice specificity requirements [VERIFY applicable case law for jurisdiction]
- Distinguish any exclusion cases by showing this notice satisfies articulated requirements

### 7. Attorney Certification

```
I hereby certify that the foregoing notice is made in good faith,
based upon information and belief formed after reasonable inquiry
and investigation, and in compliance with [Rule citation] and all
applicable local rules.

[Attorney Name]
[Bar Number], [Jurisdiction]
[Firm Name]
[Address]
[Phone] | [Email]
Attorney for Defendant [Full Legal Name]
```

If local rules require defendant's signature, add signature line with printed name and accuracy affirmation.

### 8. Certificate of Service

- Date of service
- Name and address of prosecutor/office served
- Method of service (ECF, mail, personal delivery)
- Compliance with e-filing requirements

## Guidelines

- **Filing deadline**: Calculate precisely from triggering event (arraignment, indictment, court order). Document compliance in the notice itself.
- **Disclose only what is required**: Do not include work product, mental impressions, legal theories, or trial strategy beyond mandatory disclosure elements.
- **Privacy-sensitive alibis**: If alibi involves medical treatment, counseling, or other private matters, consider requesting protective order or in camera review before filing.
- **Accuracy is mandatory**: All facts must be verifiable. False statements risk sanctions, ethical violations, or criminal liability.
- **Balance specificity with strategy**: Satisfy disclosure requirements without unnecessarily constraining trial presentation or enabling prosecution rebuttal preparation.
- **Preserve rights**: Include language preserving defendant's right to testify or remain silent, right to present a defense, and right to supplement the notice.
- **Cross-check consistency**: Verify all dates, times, locations, and witness information are internally consistent and match case file materials.
- **Format**: Typically 2-5 pages. Follow local rules for font (usually 12pt Times New Roman), double-spaced body, single-spaced caption/signature block, consecutive page numbering with case identifier in footer.
