# Trademark Cease-and-Desist Letter

Generate a formal, evidentiary demand letter that requires immediate trademark-use cessation, requires corrective action, and preserves all U.S. legal remedies.

## Prerequisites

1. Confirm the jurisdiction is U.S. and state the intended forum if litigation is possible.
2. Confirm sender authority: trademark owner, principal, and legal representative (if applicable).
3. Gather trademark rights evidence: registration number/class/use details OR common-law first use, continuity, market presence, and secondary meaning.
4. Gather infringement evidence with provenance: first-seen date, locations, URLs, photos/screenshots, catalogs, ads, SKUs, and social posts.
5. Gather claimed harm: consumer confusion indicators, reputational harm, diversion, dilution indicators, and competitive injury.
6. Gather requested relief: cease window, removal scope, inventory destruction, accounting period, and proof format.
7. Confirm delivery preferences: email + certified mail/return receipt requested.

## Output Structure / Process

### Required sections (always include)

| Section | Include | Mandatory logic |
|---|---|---|
| Header | Sender/recipient identities, address, date, "Re: Cease and Desist Regarding Trademark Infringement" | If represented by counsel, include firm and bar contact block. |
| Rights basis | Registered or common-law rights assertion | Registered: mark, USPTO number, filing/registration date, goods/services/classes, channels and territory. Common-law: first use, continuous use, promotion, goodwill, distinctiveness. |
| Infringing acts | Fact-based timeline of unauthorized use | Tie each example to date/location/evidence and remove ambiguity. |
| Legal risk analysis | Likelihood of confusion and potential dilution claim | Address similarity, relatedness, channels, consumers, intent, actual confusion where available; dilution only if mark is famous [VERIFY]. |
| Demands | Clear cure demands and strict compliance checklist | Include 10–15 business-day default window unless user sets alternative. |
| Remedies warning | Concrete remedies and rights reservation | Mention likely litigation path and available remedies under Lanham Act [VERIFY]. |
| Response mechanics | Deadline, contact method, documents required | Require written confirmation of full cure, destruction/removal proof, and sales/accounting response. |

### Trademark rights fact sheet (insert before drafting)

| Field | Required | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Mark identification | Exact mark, spelling, stylization | Registration or business filings |
| Ownership proof | Certificate, assignment, entity authority | USPTO records / assignment docs |
| Scope of protection | Goods/services, class, use territory | Registration + portfolio records |
| Infringement instances | 3+ specific examples minimum | Screenshots, archive links, invoices, samples |
| Prejudice evidence | Confusion complaints / business impact | Supportive correspondence and metrics |
| Harm quantification | Sales data needed | Internal bookkeeping or market proof |

### Core drafting template

```text
[Sender Letterhead]
[Date]

VIA CERTIFIED MAIL, RETURN RECEIPT REQUESTED
[Recipient Name]
[Recipient Address]

Re: Cease and Desist Regarding Trademark Infringement of "[MARK]"

1. Statement of authority and ownership
2. Summary of infringement with exhibit references
3. Why the use is actionable:
   - Likelihood of confusion (15 U.S.C. §1125(a)) [VERIFY]
   - Optional dilution if famous: 15 U.S.C. §1125(c) [VERIFY]
4. Demands (all permanent, not temporary)
5. Deadline and cure mechanics
6. Litigation and remedy notice:
   - Injunctive relief (15 U.S.C. §1116) [VERIFY]
   - Damages/profits/fees (15 U.S.C. §1117(a)) [VERIFY]
7. Reservation of rights and no waiver
8. Compliance response instructions
```

## Guidelines

1. Use only verified facts; avoid vague threats and unsupported legal conclusions.
2. Keep tone firm and professional; do not include coercive, extortionate, or personally inflammatory language.
3. For registered marks, include constructive notice/presumption implications and class-specific scope [VERIFY].
4. For unregistered marks, prove secondary meaning, source identification, and geographic continuity.
5. Explicitly reject apparent defenses when present (descriptive or nominative use, prior use, fair use, geographic limitations) with brief, evidence-based rebuttal.
6. Demand complete cessation across all channels: packaging, packaging, web, social, domains, marketplaces, email, advertising.
7. Require written accounting of revenues, unit sales, and profit impact tied to infringing use period.
8. Add a compliance package list: removal screenshots, destruction certificate, distributor notices, and profit ledger.
9. Add governing-law language for U.S. matters; flag cross-border takedown complexity and coordinate with local counsel for foreign use [VERIFY].
