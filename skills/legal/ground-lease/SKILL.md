# Ground Lease Agreement

Drafts a financeable, senior-level ground lease governing long-term land use where the tenant constructs improvements and the landlord retains fee ownership, with improvements reverting to the landlord upon expiration.

## Prerequisites

1. **Parties** — full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions of formation, and principal addresses for landlord and tenant (plus any guarantor or parent)
2. **Property** — complete legal description (metes and bounds, lot/block, plat reference), street address, county/state, and any title or survey exceptions
3. **Economic terms** — initial base rent, escalation methodology, rent commencement trigger, and any percentage or participation rent
4. **Development plan** — intended use, improvement scope, construction timeline, and target certificate of occupancy date
5. **Financing structure** — whether leasehold is subordinate or unsubordinate to tenant's financing; identity of anticipated lender(s) if known
6. **Special provisions** — any negotiated deal points from LOI or term sheet, governmental approvals required, or institutional/public-entity constraints

---

## Output Structure

### 1. Parties, Recitals & Property

- Full entity details for landlord and tenant; identify any SPE and whether parent guaranty is contemplated
- Recitals: landlord's fee ownership rationale (estate planning, long-term hold, institutional mandate), tenant's development intent, project scope
- Legal description: incorporate exact metes-and-bounds; note that legal description controls over street address
- Appurtenant rights: access, utility, parking, and signage easements included; reservations (mineral rights, prior easements) explicitly carved out
- Three-dimensional scope: address air rights / subsurface rights if bifurcated

### 2. Lease Term

| Element | Typical Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Initial term | 49–99 years | Select based on financing requirements and improvement useful life |
| Renewal options | 1–3 options, 10–25 years each | Specify notice period (12–24 months prior), conditions (no uncured default), and rent reset method |
| Commencement trigger | Execution date or conditions precedent | CP examples: zoning approval, financing commitment, landlord delivery |
| Early termination | Rarely granted to tenant | If included: specify notice, fee, and improvement treatment |

### 3. Rent & Escalation

**Base rent** — Typically 5–8% of appraised land value (state appraised value, appraisal date, and methodology used).

| Escalation Method | Key Drafting Points |
|---|---|
| Fixed percentage steps | Specify interval (e.g., every 5 years), step-up percentage, and whether cumulative |
| CPI adjustment | Identify index (CPI-U, All Urban Consumers), base period, measurement interval, cap (e.g., 10% per period) and floor (e.g., 0%) |
| FMV reset | Appraisal procedure: dual appraisers, averaging or baseball arbitration, notice timing (12–18 months prior), costs allocation |
| Percentage rent | Define "Gross Revenues," reporting periods, audit rights, and breakpoint |

**Payment mechanics:** specify due dates, payee, late charge (typically 5% of overdue amount after 10–15 day grace), and default interest (prime + 3–5%, non-compounding unless specified). Verify usury compliance.

**Tax obligations:** tenant pays all real property taxes and assessments (land + improvements) directly to taxing authority; provide evidence of payment annually; tenant may contest assessments but must prevent liens on landlord's fee interest.

### 4. Use, Development & Improvements

**Permitted use:** reference current zoning; define broadly enough for business evolution but restrict environmental-risk uses (dry cleaning, gas stations, industrial hazmat operations) and uses violating existing covenants.

**Construction obligations:**

| Milestone | Typical Timeframe |
|---|---|
| Commence construction | Within 6–12 months of commencement date |
| Complete construction | Within 12–36 months of commencement (scope-dependent) |
| Completion standard | Certificate of occupancy or substantial completion per AIA standard |
| Failure consequence | Landlord termination right or rent acceleration |

**Improvement ownership:**
- During term: improvements owned by tenant; tenant may depreciate for tax purposes and encumber via leasehold mortgage
- Upon expiration/termination: improvements revert to landlord without compensation (unless otherwise negotiated)
- Tenant's trade fixtures and equipment: removable upon surrender if not affixed

### 5. Maintenance, Compliance & Insurance

**Maintenance:** tenant responsible for all structural and non-structural maintenance, entire premises including land; standards = first-class comparable properties in market.

**Insurance requirements:**

| Coverage | Minimum Limits | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| Commercial general liability (occurrence) | $5M–$10M+ (per occurrence/aggregate) | Landlord as additional insured; contractual liability endorsement |
| Umbrella/excess | As needed above primary | Follow-form |
| Property (all-risk, replacement cost) | Full replacement cost of improvements | Landlord as loss payee; no depreciation deduction; include business income/rent loss |
| Builder's risk | Full value of work in progress | During construction periods only |
| Workers' compensation | Statutory limits | If tenant has employees on-site |
| Pollution liability | As appropriate to use | Required for operations involving hazardous materials |

Carrier requirements: A.M. Best A- or better. Certificates: prior to possession, annually thereafter; 30-day cancellation notice; primary and non-contributory; subrogation waived in favor of landlord.

### 6. Casualty & Condemnation

**Casualty:**
- Tenant obligation to restore; commence within 60–90 days of casualty; complete within 12–24 months
- Insurance proceeds held in trust/controlled disbursement; released against architect certifications
- Tenant funds any shortfall; landlord's consent not required for restoration plans unless materially different from original
- Termination rights (either party): damage in final [X] years of term; damage > [Y]% of replacement cost; proceeds insufficient and tenant declines to fund shortfall
- Rent abatement: proportional to untenantable area; continues through restoration or termination

**Condemnation:**
- Total taking: both parties may terminate; awards allocated — landlord receives land value, tenant receives improvement value, leasehold bonus value, business damages, relocation
- Partial taking: lease continues with rent adjusted proportionally; restoration obligations apply
- Parties cooperate on joint condemnation proceeding or pursue separate claims per agreement

### 7. Assignment & Transfer

**Consent standard:** landlord consent required (not unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed); 30–60 day response period; deemed consent if no response.

**Evaluation criteria (objective):**
- Net worth ≥ [X × annual base rent] or fixed minimum
- Demonstrated experience operating comparable properties
- No prohibited use intended
- Creditworthiness evidenced by audited financials

**Permitted transfers (no consent required):**
- Affiliate transfers (control, controlled by, common control) if original tenant remains liable or transferee has comparable financial strength
- Merger, consolidation, or asset sale to entity with equal or greater financial strength
- Foreclosure by leasehold mortgagee or transfer pursuant to foreclosure sale

**No recapture right** (inappropriate given tenant's capital investment in improvements).

**Profit sharing:** specify whether tenant retains all transfer profits or shares a portion with landlord (negotiate based on transaction economics).

### 8. Leasehold Mortgage Provisions

Critical for institutional financing. Include all of the following:

- **Right to mortgage:** tenant may encumber leasehold and improvements without consent; leasehold mortgage subordinate to ground lease
- **Lender notice:** tenant to provide landlord with name/address of each leasehold lender; landlord to simultaneously send all default notices to registered lenders
- **Lender cure rights:** lender has tenant's full cure period plus an additional 30–60 days to cure monetary defaults; additional reasonable time for non-monetary defaults requiring possession
- **New lease right:** if ground lease terminates for tenant default, registered lender may elect (within 30–60 days of termination notice) to receive a new ground lease on identical terms, provided lender cures all defaults and pays all arrears
- **Landlord non-disturbance:** landlord will not disturb lender's security so long as lender performs tenant's obligations
- **Foreclosure recognition:** landlord agrees to recognize purchaser at leasehold foreclosure sale as tenant under lease upon assumption of obligations and cure of defaults

### 9. Environmental

- **Landlord representations:** no known contamination or violations as of commencement (subject to disclosed Phase I exceptions)
- **Tenant obligations:** comply with all environmental laws; no contamination; obtain required permits; maintain hazmat records; provide access for inspection
- **Remediation:** tenant remediates all contamination caused during lease term to regulatory closure standards; landlord right to perform and charge costs if tenant fails
- **Indemnification:** tenant indemnifies landlord for all environmental claims/costs arising from tenant's operations; **survives lease expiration**
- **Surrender condition:** deliver property free of tenant-caused contamination; provide Phase I or regulatory closure evidence

### 10. Default & Remedies

| Default Type | Notice Required | Cure Period |
|---|---|---|
| Monetary (rent, taxes, insurance) | Written notice | 10–15 days |
| Non-monetary (maintenance, use, compliance) | Written notice | 30–60 days + reasonable extension if cure commenced and diligently pursued |
| Non-curable defaults | Written notice | N/A — immediate termination right |

**Landlord remedies:** termination and repossession; rent acceleration (subject to mitigation); self-help cure with cost reimbursement as additional rent; specific performance; damages.

**Tenant remedies:** damages; offset rights (perform landlord's obligation and deduct); specific performance; termination only for material landlord breach (e.g., failure to deliver possession, interference with quiet enjoyment).

### 11. General Provisions

- **Governing law:** state where property is located
- **Dispute resolution:** senior-executive negotiation (30 days) → optional mediation (AAA/JAMS) → litigation or arbitration; carve out termination disputes and injunctive relief from mandatory ADR
- **Jury waiver:** include with required conspicuousness; confirm enforceability in applicable jurisdiction [VERIFY state-specific requirements]
- **Notices:** simultaneous delivery to lenders for default/termination notices; certified mail + overnight courier + email
- **Recording:** record memorandum/short-form lease (preserving economic term confidentiality) rather than full lease; include term, options, and leasehold mortgage rights in memorandum

## Guidelines

- **Financing review:** before finalizing, confirm all leasehold mortgage provisions satisfy requirements of anticipated institutional lender (CMBS, life company, bank) — lender counsel typically requires specific language review
- **Subordination decision:** whether ground lease is subordinate or unsubordinate to tenant's financing fundamentally changes risk allocation; confirm parties' intent early and draft accordingly
- **Term selection:** terms of 49 or 99 years are most common; avoid terms that cross a century without careful consideration of renewal mechanics and rent reset methodology
- **FMV reset risk:** if FMV rent resets apply at renewal, model scenarios to ensure reset methodology is clear and dispute resolution procedure (baseball arbitration preferred) is functional
- **Improvement reversion value:** if landlord is an institution or governmental entity, confirm that improvement reversion is consistent with its accounting, tax-exempt status, or regulatory obligations
- **Jurisdiction caveats:** landlord-tenant statutes, usury caps, jury waiver enforceability, and recording requirements vary by state — flag for local counsel review
- **No tenant recapture:** do not include landlord recapture rights; ground lease tenants have invested substantial capital and recapture would impair financing and marketability
- **Mark [VERIFY]** on any statutory citation, index reference, or local law requirement that has not been confirmed against current sources
