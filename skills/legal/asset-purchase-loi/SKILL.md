# Asset Purchase Letter of Intent

Drafts an asset purchase LOI that aligns deal terms, diligence, and binding protections while preserving non-binding status.

## Prerequisites

1. Buyer and seller legal names, entity types, formation states, and addresses.
2. Business description and scope of assets to be acquired.
3. Proposed purchase price structure and any adjustments.
4. Liability assumption position and any specific assumed liabilities.
5. Diligence scope, access parameters, and target timeline.
6. Exclusivity/no-shop intent and duration.
7. Confidentiality status and existing NDA details, if any.
8. Governing law and dispute forum preference.
9. Key conditions precedent and regulatory/consent requirements.
10. Non-compete/non-solicit intent, duration, and scope parameters.

| Term | Required Inputs | Drafting Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |

| Assets | Included assets, excluded assets | Use schedules or bullet lists. |
| Liabilities | Assumed vs excluded | State buyer assumes only listed liabilities. |
| Price | Cash, seller note, earnout, adjustments | Specify mechanics and timing. |
| Allocation | IRC 1060 allocation intent | Note Form 8594 consistency. |
| Diligence | Start date, duration, access limits | Buyer discretion for satisfaction. |
| Closing | Target date or window | Tie to definitive agreement and conditions. |
| Conditions | Consents, financing, MAC, approvals | List critical items. |
| Exclusivity | Duration, scope, notice | Make binding. |
| Confidentiality | NDA reference or LOI clause | Make binding. |
| Costs | Each party bears own costs | Make binding. |
| Law/Forum | State law, courts/arbitration | Make binding. |
| Non-compete | Duration, geography, scope | Reference in LOI; detail later. |

**Core Letter Sections (template skeleton)**
```text
[Date]

[Seller Name]
[Seller Address]

Re: Letter of Intent for Asset Purchase of [Business/Assets]

1. Parties and Transaction Overview (Non-Binding)
2. Assets to Be Acquired (Non-Binding)
3. Excluded Assets (Non-Binding)
4. Assumed Liabilities (Non-Binding)
5. Excluded Liabilities (Non-Binding)
6. Purchase Price and Payment Structure (Non-Binding)
7. Allocation of Purchase Price; IRC 1060; Form 8594 (Non-Binding)
8. Due Diligence Period and Access (Non-Binding)
9. Conditions to Closing (Non-Binding)
10. Timeline and Definitive Documentation (Non-Binding)
11. Non-Competition / Non-Solicitation Framework (Non-Binding)
12. Binding Provisions
   a. Confidentiality
   b. Exclusivity / No-Shop
   c. Costs and Expenses
   d. Governing Law; Dispute Resolution
   e. Public Announcements (if applicable)
13. Non-Binding Effect; Termination

Signature Blocks
```

**Binding vs Non-Binding (table)**
| Provision | Binding? | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Confidentiality | Yes | Reference existing NDA or include clause. |
| Exclusivity / No-Shop | Yes | Include duration, notice, injunctive relief. |
| Costs and Expenses | Yes | Each party bears own costs unless stated. |
| Governing Law / Forum | Yes | Specify state law and venue or arbitration. |
| Deal Terms | No | Explicit non-binding statement. |
| Diligence / Conditions | No | Non-binding until definitive agreement. |

**Key Clause Requirements (checklist)**
- Explicit non-binding statement for all business terms.
- Binding list is enumerated and exclusive.
- Buyer may terminate discussions at any time absent definitive agreement.
- Diligence conditioned on buyer satisfaction in sole discretion.
- Exclusivity start date, end date, and early termination triggers.
- Confidentiality survival period and return/destroy obligations.
- Tax allocation intent under IRC 1060 and Form 8594 consistency.
- Non-compete described at high level; details deferred to definitive docs.

**Ancillary Documents (list)**
- Asset Purchase Agreement.
- Bill of Sale.
- Assignment and Assumption Agreements.
- IP Assignment.
- Non-Compete and Non-Solicit Agreements.
- Employment or Consulting Agreements (if needed).
- Consents and Regulatory Approvals.
- Closing Certificates and Legal Opinions (if required).

## Guidelines

- Keep all commercial terms expressly non-binding unless specifically labeled binding.
- Do not promise closing; use "intend" and "subject to definitive agreement."
- Tailor non-compete duration and geography to state law enforceability.
- If HSR or other regulatory approvals are possible, flag as a condition precedent.
- Avoid overly broad asset or liability descriptions; use schedules where possible.
- Ensure confidentiality and exclusivity obligations are enforceable and time-limited.
- Include a "no obligation to proceed" clause for both parties.
- Align tax allocation language with IRC 1060 and Form 8594 requirements.
- Identify any deal-critical third-party consents early.
- If an NDA exists, incorporate by reference and confirm it remains in effect.
