# Exporter of Record (EOR) Agreement

Drafts a legally enforceable EOR Agreement that appoints a qualified third party as USPPI for international export transactions, allocates compliance obligations, and manages liability exposure under U.S. export control law.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party details** — full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions of organization, principal places of business, IRS/EIN numbers, CBP importer/exporter of record numbers
2. **Goods/technology description** — technical specifications, existing ECCN classifications, USML categories if defense articles involved, commodity jurisdiction determinations (if any)
3. **Destination countries and end-users** — all anticipated destinations, identified consignees, end-use certifications
4. **Scope of EOR services** — compliance-only vs. combined logistics/freight forwarding; exclusive vs. non-exclusive appointment
5. **Existing licenses or authorizations** — any BIS/DDTC licenses, license exception determinations, OFAC authorizations
6. **Business terms** — fee structure, term length, insurance minimums, preferred governing law/venue

## Output Structure

### 1. Parties & Recitals
- Full party identification including CBP registration numbers
- Recitals establishing: principal's export need → regulatory complexity → EOR's specialized expertise
- Acknowledgment of penalty exposure under EAR, ITAR, OFAC, and CBP

### 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Exporter of Record / USPPI** | Party appearing on export documentation as legally responsible for the transaction; assumes all attendant regulatory obligations |
| **Export Controls** | EAR (15 C.F.R. §§ 730–774), ITAR (22 C.F.R. §§ 120–130), OFAC sanctions programs, anti-boycott regulations (15 C.F.R. Part 760), and all other applicable federal export laws |
| **EEI/AES Filing** | Electronic Export Information submitted through the Automated Export System per 15 C.F.R. Part 30 |
| **Controlled Items** | Goods, software, or technology subject to EAR, ITAR, or OFAC restrictions |
| **Denied Parties Screening** | Pre-shipment check against: Denied Persons List, Entity List, Unverified List, SDN List, Foreign Sanctions Evaders List, Debarred Parties List (ITAR), and any applicable non-U.S. lists |
| **Export Documentation** | Commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading/AWB, export license/exception, EEI, certificate of origin, destination control statement (DCS) |

### 3. Appointment & Scope
- **Appointment type**: exclusive/non-exclusive; specify product categories, destinations, and any carve-outs
- **Authority limits**: compliance and regulatory acts only vs. authority to bind on commercial terms
- Escalation procedure for novel classification questions, sensitive end-users, or denied-party hits

### 4. EOR Obligations

- [ ] Classify all items under EAR (ECCN) or ITAR (USML category); document classification rationale
- [ ] Determine licensing requirements; apply applicable license exceptions or obtain BIS/DDTC licenses
- [ ] Prepare and submit accurate EEI via AES for all shipments meeting filing thresholds (15 C.F.R. Part 30)
- [ ] Place required Destination Control Statements on commercial invoices and bills of lading
- [ ] Conduct denied-party screening of all transaction participants **before each shipment**; document results
- [ ] Prepare complete export documentation package per transaction
- [ ] Maintain export records for **5 years** from date of export, license expiration, or enforcement action (whichever is later), in promptly retrievable format (15 C.F.R. § 762.2)
- [ ] Notify Principal within **[X] business days** of: compliance issues, license denials, government inquiries, regulatory changes affecting covered exports
- [ ] Maintain: E&O insurance ($[X]M min.), customs bond if applicable, general liability ($[X]M min.)

### 5. Principal Obligations

- [ ] Provide complete, accurate technical specifications and end-use/end-user information before each shipment
- [ ] Disclose all prior commodity jurisdiction determinations, classification requests, or advisory opinions
- [ ] Notify EOR immediately of: product changes, new destinations/end-users, changes in ownership or control
- [ ] Warrant IP rights and manufacturing authorizations are in place; no liens restricting export
- [ ] Bear all financial obligations: EOR fees, government filing fees, duties, taxes, license application fees
- [ ] Cooperate with government audits, investigations, and enforcement actions
- [ ] Comply with FCPA (15 U.S.C. § 78dd-1 et seq.) and anti-boycott regulations (15 C.F.R. Part 760)

### 6. Representations & Warranties (Both Parties)
- Not listed on any restricted party list; no pending disqualifying government investigations
- Authority to execute; performance does not violate other agreements or court orders
- **EOR adds**: holds all required licenses/registrations; maintains adequate compliance systems and trained personnel
- **Principal adds**: all information provided is accurate and complete; items have no undisclosed restrictions
- Ongoing covenant: notify counterparty immediately upon any change in restricted-party status or material compliance issue

### 7. Indemnification

| Indemnifying Party | Covered Claims |
|---|---|
| **Principal** | Claims arising from inaccurate/incomplete information; undisclosed classifications; principal-caused violations; unauthorized destination/product changes; principal's breach of reps/warranties |
| **EOR** | Claims arising from EOR's negligence, gross negligence, or willful misconduct; filing/documentation errors attributable to EOR; unauthorized acts beyond scope |

- Indemnification procedure: prompt written notice → indemnifying party controls defense with counsel of its choice → indemnified party cooperates
- Unlimited liability carve-outs: gross negligence, willful misconduct, fraud, intentional export control violations, confidentiality breaches

### 8. Limitation of Liability
- Cap: total aggregate liability not to exceed $[X] or [X] months of fees paid, whichever is greater
- Mutual exclusion: no consequential, incidental, special, or punitive damages (except as awarded to third parties in indemnified claims or for willful misconduct)

### 9. Term & Termination

| Event | Notice / Cure Period |
|---|---|
| Termination for convenience | [30–90] days written notice |
| Material breach | [15–30] days written notice + cure period |
| Immediate termination (no cure) | Bankruptcy/insolvency; restricted-party listing; license revocation essential to performance; imminent export control violation risk |

**Post-termination obligations:**
- EOR completes in-process shipments unless directed otherwise
- EOR transfers export licenses per applicable agency rules; withdraws or assigns pending applications
- Return of principal's documents and confidential information within [X] days
- Prorated refund of prepaid, unearned fees
- Surviving provisions: indemnification, confidentiality, record retention, payment obligations

### 10. Governing Law & Disputes
- Governing law: [State] (federal export control law supersedes on regulatory matters)
- Tiered dispute resolution:
  1. Senior executive negotiation — [30] days
  2. Mediation (JAMS/AAA) — [60] days; costs shared equally
  3. Binding arbitration (AAA Commercial Rules) or litigation in [forum]; [1 or 3] arbitrators
- Equitable relief carve-out: either party may seek injunctive relief in court of competent jurisdiction without exhausting other tiers (confidentiality breaches, imminent export control violations)
- Jury trial waiver (if litigating)

### 11. General Provisions
- Integration clause; amendments in writing signed by authorized representatives
- Counterparts; electronic signatures acceptable
- Schedules: product/ECCN list, fee schedule, approved destinations, compliance procedures

## Guidelines

- **ITAR trigger**: If any defense articles (USML Categories I–XXI) are involved, ensure separate ITAR-specific representations, DDTC license/TAA references, and registration requirements (22 C.F.R. § 122) are addressed; consider a standalone ITAR addendum
- **AES filing threshold**: EEI required for most shipments >$2,500 or requiring a license (15 C.F.R. § 30.2); confirm current thresholds [VERIFY]
- **OFAC**: Even absent ITAR/EAR license requirements, screen for SDN and sectoral sanctions (50 U.S.C. § 1701 et seq.); include explicit OFAC representation
- **Anti-boycott**: Required if goods destined for or transiting Arab League countries; EOR and Principal must each covenant compliance (15 C.F.R. Part 760)
- **Insurance minimums**: Confirm E&O, customs bond, and GL minimums against industry standards and the specific risk profile of the exports
- **State law variation**: Indemnification and limitation of liability provisions may be subject to state-specific restrictions; confirm enforceability in chosen governing-law jurisdiction
- **License transferability**: Export licenses generally are not transferable to a successor EOR without BIS/DDTC approval; flag this risk in transition planning
