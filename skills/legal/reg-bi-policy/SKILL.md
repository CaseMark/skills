# Suitability and Best Interest Policy (Reg BI)

Drafts a comprehensive, board-ready compliance policy governing recommendations to retail customers under FINRA Rule 2111 and SEC Regulation Best Interest (17 CFR 240.15l-1).

## Prerequisites

1. **Firm-specific details** — business model, product offerings, compensation structures, organizational chart
2. **Existing compliance materials** — current policies, compliance manuals, supervisory procedures
3. **Product inventory** — list of securities, strategies, and product types offered to customers
4. **Compensation arrangements** — all compensation structures, revenue-sharing, third-party payments

## Output Structure

Draft a formal compliance policy with hierarchical section numbering, suitable for board approval and regulatory presentation. Include the following sections in order:

### 1. Policy Statement

- Commitment to FINRA Rule 2111 and Reg BI compliance
- Integration with firm's broader compliance framework
- Effective date and review cycle provisions

### 2. FINRA Rule 2111 Suitability Framework

| Component | Scope | Key Requirements |
|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| **Reasonable-Basis** | Product/strategy level | Due diligence on risks, rewards, costs, complexity, liquidity, volatility; written approval records; enhanced review for complex/novel products |
| **Customer-Specific** | Individual customer | Match recommendation to investment profile: age, finances, tax status, objectives, experience, time horizon, liquidity needs, risk tolerance |
| **Quantitative** | Pattern of transactions | Monitor turnover ratios, cost-to-equity ratios, trading frequency; distinguish customer-initiated vs. recommended trades; establish review thresholds |

For customer-specific suitability, include:
- Minimum information requirements before any recommendation
- Periodic profile update intervals and life-event triggers
- Protocols when customers decline to provide information

### 3. Reg BI Four-Obligation Framework

#### 3a. Disclosure Obligation
- Written disclosure before or at time of recommendation
- Plain English, clear and concise
- Required disclosures:

| Category | Content |
|----------|---------|
| Relationship | Capacity (broker-dealer vs. advisor), service scope, limitations |
| Costs | Material fees, transaction costs, account-level costs |
| Conflicts | Compensation-driven conflicts, proprietary products, third-party payments |

- Procedures for keeping disclosures current

#### 3b. Care Obligation
- Three-part test:
  1. Understand potential risks, rewards, and costs of the recommendation
  2. Reasonable basis it could benefit at least some retail customers
  3. Reasonable basis it is in THIS customer's best interest given their profile
- **Reasonably available alternatives analysis** — evaluate less costly, less complex, or lower-risk alternatives that meet the same objectives
- Document the analytical framework for each recommendation

#### 3c. Conflict of Interest Obligation
- Systematic conflict identification across:
  - [ ] Differential compensation by product
  - [ ] Sales contests, bonuses, special compensation
  - [ ] Proprietary/affiliate products
  - [ ] Third-party payments and revenue sharing
  - [ ] Principal trading
- For each conflict: disclose + mitigate, or eliminate
- Mitigation measures: compensation restructuring, supervisory review layers, product limitations

#### 3d. Compliance Obligation
- CCO or designated senior officer ownership
- Training program:
  - Initial training before making any recommendations
  - Ongoing CE covering regulatory updates, new products, enforcement lessons
- Supervisory review processes:
  - Pre-approval requirements (where applicable)
  - Post-trade surveillance
  - Periodic account audits
  - Disclosure compliance testing
- Escalation and disciplinary procedures

### 4. Form CRS Integration

- Delivery timing: before or at earliest of recommendation, account type recommendation, or account opening
- Required content per 17 CFR 240.17a-14 [VERIFY] and 17 CFR 279.2 [VERIFY]:
  - Relationships and services offered
  - Fees, costs, conflicts, standard of conduct
  - Disciplinary history
  - How to obtain additional information
- Filing amendments within 30 days of material inaccuracy; annual review minimum
- Form CRS supplements but does not replace Reg BI Disclosure Obligation detail

### 5. Documentation and Recordkeeping

Per-recommendation documentation checklist:
- [ ] Date, substance, and basis of recommendation
- [ ] Associated person identity
- [ ] Customer investment profile (current)
- [ ] Reasonable-basis and customer-specific analysis
- [ ] Alternatives considered
- [ ] Disclosures provided
- [ ] Conflicts identified and addressed
- [ ] Supervisory review/approval

Retention: minimum 6 years per SEC Rule 17a-4; first 2 years in easily accessible location. Organize and index for regulatory examination retrieval.

### 6. Supervision and Enforcement

- Define roles: line supervisors, compliance department, senior management, board
- Violation consequences scaled to severity, intent, customer harm, pattern, and history
- Remediation: enhanced supervision, retraining, restitution, policy revision
- Customer complaint and regulatory inquiry handling procedures

## Guidelines

- All regulatory citations must be verified against current rule text — use `[VERIFY]` for any uncertain citation
- Tailor thresholds, committees, and governance structures to the specific firm's size and complexity
- Do not present as legal advice; frame as a compliance policy template requiring firm counsel review
- Include definitions section for key terms (retail customer, investment profile, associated person, etc.)
- Use hierarchical section numbering (1.1, 1.2, etc.) for cross-referencing
- Address both dual-registrant and standalone broker-dealer scenarios where applicable
- Jurisdiction: U.S. federal (SEC/FINRA); note state-level blue sky requirements may apply
