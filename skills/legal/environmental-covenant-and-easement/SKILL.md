# Environmental Covenant and Easement

Drafts a recordable, perpetual environmental covenant and easement restricting land use to protect public health following contamination, remediation, or as a condition of regulatory settlement.

## Prerequisites

1. **Property records** — legal description, tax parcel number, deed, title report
2. **Environmental reports** — Phase I/II ESAs, remediation action plans, site investigation reports
3. **Regulatory instruments** — consent decrees, administrative orders, voluntary cleanup agreements, agency correspondence specifying required institutional controls
4. **Parties** — grantor's full legal name and authority to encumber; grantee identity (agency, land trust, or designated holder) and statutory authority to hold covenants under applicable state law
5. **Contamination specifics** — hazardous substances present, remedial measures completed, ongoing monitoring obligations, engineered controls in place

## Output Structure

### 1. Parties
| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Grantor | Current property owner conveying restrictions; confirm legal authority to encumber |
| Grantee | Governmental environmental agency or authorized holder empowered under UECA or state law |
| Additional | Subordinating lienholders, regulatory agencies with oversight, third-party beneficiaries |

Include: full legal names, addresses, official capacities.

### 2. Recitals
- Complete legal description (with parcel number and recording info)
- Nature/extent of contamination and hazardous substances identified
- Summary of remediation completed and any ongoing obligations
- Reference to triggering regulatory action (EPA order, state cleanup agreement, CERCLA/RCRA program)
- Public health and environmental protection purpose
- Statutory authority (UECA, state environmental law, CERCLA § 120(h) if applicable)

### 3. Definitions
Define precisely: Property, Restricted Activities, Contamination, Remedial Action, Institutional Controls, Environmental Agency, and all site-specific terms. Align with UECA definitions and underlying regulatory documents.

### 4. Grant of Covenant and Easement

**Prohibited Activities (enumerate with specificity):**
- Residential use or occupancy
- Installation of groundwater wells for potable purposes
- Excavation, grading, or soil disturbance below [specified depth] without agency approval
- Activities disturbing or damaging remedial systems, monitoring wells, or engineered caps/barriers
- Any use increasing exposure to contaminated media

**Affirmative Obligations:**
- Maintain vegetative caps and engineered barriers
- Conduct periodic groundwater monitoring per agency schedule
- Submit annual compliance reports
- Provide access for agency inspections
- Notify agency before property transfer or proposed use change

**Run-with-the-land clause**: Restrictions bind grantor, successors, assigns, lessees, and all future occupants.

**Easement grant**: Access rights to grantee to inspect, monitor, maintain remedial systems, and enforce covenant terms.

### 5. Duration and Termination
- Covenant is **perpetual** unless agency issues formal written release
- Termination requires: owner petition → evidence of remediation to unrestricted-use standards → agency written approval → UECA-compliant statutory procedure
- No modification or release without written agency consent
- Subordination: covenant has priority over subsequently recorded interests; mortgagees/lienholders must subordinate

### 6. Enforcement and Remedies
- Agency right of entry at reasonable times for inspection, monitoring, and verification
- Violations enforceable via: injunctive relief, specific performance, civil penalties, cost recovery, attorneys' fees
- Agency may perform corrective actions upon owner default; costs recoverable from owner
- Covenant preserves independent agency enforcement authority under environmental statutes
- Third-party beneficiary rights for regulatory agencies expressly stated

### 7. Miscellaneous Provisions
- **Notices**: Certified mail or comparable method; addresses for all parties
- **Governing law**: State where property is located
- **Severability**, **amendment** (written consent of all parties + UECA procedure)
- **Recording**: Grantor must record in county land records and provide proof to grantee

### 8. Execution Block
- Signature lines: name, title, date for all parties
- Agency acceptance block
- Notarial acknowledgments compliant with state real property recording requirements (confirm: corporate seal, witness, specific notarial language if required)

### Exhibits
- [ ] Legal description of property
- [ ] Survey or map showing restricted areas/contaminated zones
- [ ] Referenced environmental reports or regulatory documents

## Guidelines

- **UECA adoption**: Verify whether the property's state has adopted UECA [VERIFY state-specific version]; non-adopting states may use common law easement or state-specific institutional control statutes
- **CERCLA § 120(h)**: Required disclosures apply to federal facility transfers [VERIFY applicability]
- **RCRA corrective action**: Covenants may be required as postclosure permit conditions [VERIFY]
- **Priority and subordination**: Obtain subordination agreements from all existing lienholders before execution to ensure enforceability against future interests
- **Recording**: Failure to record defeats constructive notice to future purchasers; confirm county-specific recording requirements
- **Do not**: Omit the run-with-the-land clause, agency termination consent requirement, or access easement — these are core enforceability elements
- Flag all site-specific depth thresholds, monitoring frequencies, and restricted activity lists for attorney and agency review before execution
