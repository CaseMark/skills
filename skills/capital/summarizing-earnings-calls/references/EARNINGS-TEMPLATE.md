# Earnings Call Structure Reference

## Standard Earnings Call Format

### 1. Operator Introduction (1-2 min)
- Conference call logistics
- Forward-looking statements disclaimer (SEC safe harbor)
- Introduction of speakers

### 2. Prepared Remarks — CEO (5-10 min)
**Listen for**:
- Strategic narrative and tone
- Key accomplishments highlighted
- Market/competitive commentary
- Forward guidance themes
- Personnel or organizational changes

### 3. Prepared Remarks — CFO (5-10 min)
**Extract**:
- Revenue (total, by segment, by geography)
- Gross margin and operating margin
- EPS (GAAP and non-GAAP, with reconciliation items)
- Cash flow and balance sheet highlights
- Updated guidance (specific numbers, range changes)

### 4. Q&A Session (20-40 min)
**Analyze**:
- Which topics analysts ask about repeatedly (signals market concern)
- Questions management deflects or gives vague answers to
- New disclosures that weren't in prepared remarks
- Analyst tone (challenging vs. supportive)

## Key Metrics by Sector

| Sector | Revenue Metrics | Profitability | Operational KPIs |
|--------|----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **SaaS** | ARR, NRR, bookings | Non-GAAP operating margin, Rule of 40 | DAU/MAU, churn, LTV/CAC |
| **E-commerce** | GMV, take rate, same-store | Contribution margin, fulfillment cost | Orders, AOV, repeat rate |
| **Banking** | NII, fee income, NIM | Efficiency ratio, ROE, ROTCE | Loan growth, NPL ratio, CET1 |
| **Pharma** | Product revenue, pipeline | Gross margin, R&D % revenue | Pipeline stages, FDA dates |
| **Industrials** | Organic growth, backlog | Segment margins, FCF conversion | Book-to-bill, utilization |
| **Retail** | Same-store sales, e-comm % | Gross margin, SG&A leverage | Traffic, conversion, inventory turns |
| **Telecom** | ARPU, subscriber count | EBITDA margin, CapEx intensity | Churn, subscriber adds, FTTH |

## Guidance Interpretation

| Language | Translation |
|----------|-------------|
| "We are raising guidance" | Increasing numbers — bullish signal |
| "Narrowing the range" | Reducing uncertainty; direction depends on which end narrows |
| "Maintaining guidance" | No change — can be neutral or slightly negative if beat expected |
| "Prudent to take a cautious view" | Likely lowering or widening range |
| "We expect headwinds in the near term" | Warning that next quarter may disappoint |
| "Visibility remains limited" | Uncertain environment; may pull guidance entirely |

## Non-GAAP Reconciliation Items to Track

Common adjustments that inflate non-GAAP vs. GAAP earnings:

| Adjustment | Legitimate? | Watch For |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Stock-based compensation | Debatable — real cost diluting shareholders | If growing as % of revenue, it's masking true cost structure |
| Restructuring charges | Usually legitimate if truly one-time | Recurring "one-time" charges over multiple quarters |
| Acquisition-related costs | Legitimate for amortization | If company is serial acquirer, these are ongoing costs |
| Litigation settlements | Usually legitimate | If frequent, may indicate systemic issues |
| Asset impairments | Depends on frequency | Repeated impairments suggest poor capital allocation |
