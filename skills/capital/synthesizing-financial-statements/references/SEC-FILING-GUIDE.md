# SEC Filing Navigation Guide

## Filing Types

| Filing | Full Name | Frequency | Key Content |
|--------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **10-K** | Annual Report | Annual (within 60-90 days of fiscal year-end) | Full financial statements, MD&A, risk factors, auditor opinion |
| **10-Q** | Quarterly Report | Quarterly (within 40-45 days of quarter-end) | Unaudited quarterly financials, updated MD&A |
| **8-K** | Current Report | Event-driven (within 4 business days) | Material events: earnings, M&A, leadership changes, etc. |
| **DEF 14A** | Proxy Statement | Annual (before shareholder meeting) | Executive comp, board nominees, shareholder proposals |
| **S-1** | Registration Statement | IPO or new security offering | Full company disclosure for public offering |
| **13-F** | Institutional Holdings | Quarterly (within 45 days) | Holdings of institutional investors (>$100M AUM) |
| **SC 13D** | Beneficial Ownership | Within 10 days of acquiring >5% | Activist/large holder intentions |
| **Form 4** | Insider Transactions | Within 2 business days | Insider buys, sells, option exercises |

## 10-K Structure (Key Sections)

| Item | Section | What to Extract |
|------|---------|----------------|
| 1 | Business | Company description, segments, competitive position |
| 1A | Risk Factors | Material risks; changes from prior year are most telling |
| 2 | Properties | Facilities, real estate |
| 3 | Legal Proceedings | Pending/threatened litigation |
| 6 | Selected Financial Data | Multi-year financial summary (being phased out) |
| 7 | MD&A | Management's analysis of results; most valuable narrative section |
| 7A | Market Risk | Interest rate, FX, commodity exposure |
| 8 | Financial Statements | Full audited financials with notes |
| 9A | Controls and Procedures | SOX 302/404 assessment |

## Reading MD&A Effectively

MD&A is where management explains the "why" behind the numbers.

**Look for**:
- Explanations for revenue changes (volume vs. price vs. mix vs. FX)
- Margin drivers and headwinds
- Cash flow changes and capital allocation plans
- Known trends or uncertainties
- Off-balance-sheet arrangements

**Red flags in MD&A**:
- Vague language replacing previously specific disclosures
- New risk factors appearing without discussion
- Changes in accounting estimates or policies
- Related-party transactions buried in footnotes
- Revenue recognition policy changes

## Financial Statement Footnotes (Key Notes)

| Note | Why It Matters |
|------|---------------|
| Revenue Recognition | How/when revenue is recognized; changes signal aggression |
| Segment Reporting | Profitability by segment; can reveal subsidizing business units |
| Debt and Credit Facilities | Maturity schedule, covenants, variable vs. fixed rate |
| Leases | Operating vs. finance lease obligations |
| Stock-Based Compensation | Dilution impact, expense methodology |
| Income Taxes | Effective vs. statutory rate, deferred tax assets/liabilities |
| Commitments and Contingencies | Legal exposure, purchase obligations, guarantees |
| Related Party Transactions | Transactions with insiders — always flag these |
| Subsequent Events | Material events after balance sheet date |
