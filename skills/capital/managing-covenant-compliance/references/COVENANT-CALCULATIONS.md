# Covenant Calculation Conventions

## EBITDA Definitions in Credit Agreements

**Standard EBITDA**: Net Income + Interest Expense + Income Tax + Depreciation + Amortization

### Common Permitted Add-Backs

| Add-Back | Typical Cap | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Non-cash charges | Unlimited | Stock comp, impairments, unrealized losses |
| Restructuring/severance | 10-15% of EBITDA | Must be truly non-recurring |
| Transaction costs | Capped amount or % | M&A, refinancing fees |
| Non-recurring legal/settlement | Varies | Must not recur for 2+ years |
| Pro forma adjustments (acquisitions) | Full-year run-rate | As if acquisition occurred day 1 of period |
| Pro forma adjustments (cost savings) | 50-100% of identified savings | Must be realized within 12-24 months |
| Management fees to owners | Varies | Common in PE-owned companies |

**Key warning**: Always read the specific credit agreement definition. "EBITDA" in the agreement may differ substantially from standard EBITDA.

## Common Financial Covenants

### Maximum Leverage Ratio

```
Leverage Ratio = Total Funded Debt / EBITDA (as defined in credit agreement)

Typical thresholds:
- Investment grade: 3.0-3.5x
- Leveraged loans: 4.0-6.0x (with step-downs)
- Real estate: Debt/Total Assets < 60%
```

**Testing**: Usually quarterly, using trailing twelve months (TTM) EBITDA

### Minimum Interest Coverage / Fixed Charge Coverage

```
Interest Coverage = EBITDA / Cash Interest Expense

Fixed Charge Coverage = (EBITDA - CapEx - Taxes) / (Interest + Scheduled Principal + Distributions)

Typical minimum: 1.25x - 2.00x
```

### Minimum Debt Service Coverage

```
DSCR = (EBITDA - CapEx - Taxes) / (Principal + Interest on all debt)

Typical minimum: 1.15x - 1.50x
```

### Maximum Capital Expenditure

```
Typically expressed as: CapEx shall not exceed $[X] per fiscal year

May include carryforward: unused portion of prior year cap carries forward (50-100%)
```

### Minimum Net Worth / Tangible Net Worth

```
Net Worth = Total Assets - Total Liabilities
Tangible Net Worth = Net Worth - Intangible Assets - Goodwill

Floor typically set at: 75-80% of net worth at closing, increasing by 50% of positive net income each year
```

## Calculation Timing

| Convention | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| TTM / LTM | Trailing/Last twelve months — sum of most recent 4 quarters |
| Run-rate | Annualized based on partial period |
| Pro forma | Adjusted as if events (acquisitions/dispositions) occurred at start of period |
| Most Recent Quarter Annualized (MRQA) | Current quarter × 4 — less common, more volatile |

## Cure Rights

Many credit agreements include equity cure provisions:

| Feature | Typical Terms |
|---------|-------------|
| Cure mechanism | Equity injection counted as EBITDA or debt reduction |
| Frequency limit | No more than 2 times in any 4 consecutive quarters |
| Lifetime limit | 3-5 cures total |
| Timing | Must be made within 10-15 business days of compliance certificate |
| Amount | Minimum amount needed to cure; excess not counted |

## Compliance Certificate Requirements

Standard compliance certificate includes:
1. Officer certification of covenant compliance
2. Detailed calculations with line-item support
3. Reference to specific credit agreement sections
4. Identification of any defaults or events that could become defaults
5. Financial statements for the period (attached or incorporated by reference)

**Delivery deadline**: Usually 45-60 days after quarter-end; 90-120 days after fiscal year-end
